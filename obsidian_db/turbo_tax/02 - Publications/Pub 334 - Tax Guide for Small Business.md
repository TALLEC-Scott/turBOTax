# Publication 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business

## Summary

Publication 334 provides comprehensive federal tax information for self-employed individuals and statutory employees who use [[Schedule C (Form 1040)]]. It covers business income, expenses, and tax credits for small business owners filing their income tax returns.

**Target Audience:**
- Sole proprietors
- Independent contractors
- Statutory employees
- Single-member LLC owners
- Qualified joint ventures (spouses)

**Not Covered:** Corporations, partnerships, S corporations, farming, rental activities, or passive activities.

## What's New for 2025

- **Maximum net earnings subject to Social Security:** $176,100 (up from 2024)
- **Standard mileage rate:** 70 cents per mile for business use
- **Section 179 deduction limit:** $2.5 million maximum, reduced when property exceeds $4 million
- **Bonus depreciation:** 100% additional first-year deduction for certain qualified property acquired after January 19, 2025, or elect 40% (60% for long-production property)
- **No tax on qualified overtime:** Deduction up to $12,500 ($25,000 MFJ) on Schedule 1-A
- **No tax on qualified tips:** Deduction up to $25,000 on Schedule 1-A
- **No tax on car loan interest:** New deduction for qualified passenger vehicle loan interest
- **Commercial clean vehicle credit:** Eliminated for vehicles acquired after September 30, 2025

## Important Rules

1. **Self-employment tax threshold:** Must file Schedule SE if net earnings are $400 or more
2. **Estimated tax requirement:** Must pay estimated tax if expected to owe $1,000 or more
3. **Filing deadline:** April 15 for calendar-year taxpayers (or 15th day of 4th month after fiscal year ends)
4. **EIN requirement:** Required if you pay wages to employees or file pension/excise tax returns
5. **Business expense test:** Must be both ordinary and necessary
6. **Home office deduction:** Regular and exclusive use requirement for principal place of business
