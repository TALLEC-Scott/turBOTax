# Pub 530 - Tax Information for Homeowners

## Summary

Publication 530 provides comprehensive tax information for homeowners, covering deductions for real estate taxes and mortgage interest, tax credits, and basis calculations. This publication is essential for understanding the tax benefits and recordkeeping requirements associated with owning a home.

## What's New for 2025

- **SALT Deduction Limit Increased**: The overall limit on state and local income, sales, and property taxes increased to $40,000 ($20,000 if married filing separately). The limit is reduced if modified AGI exceeds $500,000 ($250,000 if MFS) but not below $10,000 ($5,000 if MFS).
- **Energy Credits Terminating**: Residential clean energy credits and energy efficient home improvement credits cannot be claimed for expenditures/property after December 31, 2025.

## Important Limits

### Home Mortgage Interest Limits

- Loans after December 15, 2017: $750,000 limit ($375,000 MFS)
- Loans on or before December 15, 2017: $1,000,000 limit ($500,000 MFS)
- Interest only deductible on loan proceeds used to buy, build, or substantially improve the home

### SALT Deduction Limit

- $40,000 ($20,000 MFS) for state/local income, sales, and property taxes combined
- Reduced if modified AGI exceeds $500,000 ($250,000 MFS)

## Recordkeeping Requirements

Keep records for:
- Purchase contract and settlement papers
- Receipts for improvements
- Form 1098 statements
- Property tax bills
- Records affecting basis

Retain records for at least 3 years after filing, or longer if needed to establish basis.
