# Publication 936 - Home Mortgage Interest Deduction

## Summary

Publication 936 explains the rules for deducting home mortgage interest on your federal income tax return. It covers what qualifies as home mortgage interest, the limits on deductions, and how to report the deduction on Schedule A (Form 1040).

**Tax Year:** 2025

## Key Topics Covered

- [[Home Mortgage Interest]] - Core rules for deducting interest on loans secured by your home
- [[Home Acquisition Debt]] - Mortgages taken out to buy, build, or substantially improve a qualified home
- [[Grandfathered Debt]] - Mortgages taken out before October 14, 1987
- [[Points (Mortgage)|Points]] - Deduction rules for loan origination fees and discount points
- [[Qualified Home]] - Definition of main home and second home for deduction purposes
- [[Form 1098]] - Mortgage Interest Statement reporting requirements

## Important Rules

### Mortgage Debt Limits (2025)

| Debt Type | Limit (MFJ/QSS) | Limit (MFS) |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| Grandfathered debt | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Home acquisition debt (pre-12/16/2017) | $1,000,000 | $500,000 |
| Home acquisition debt (post-12/15/2017) | $750,000 | $375,000 |

### Expired Deductions

1. **Mortgage insurance premiums** - The itemized deduction for mortgage insurance premiums has expired
2. **Home equity loan interest** - No longer deductible unless proceeds used to buy, build, or substantially improve the home

### Fully Deductible Interest Conditions

Your home mortgage interest is fully deductible if all mortgages fit into one of these categories at all times during the year:

1. **Grandfathered debt** - Mortgages taken out on or before October 13, 1987
2. **Home acquisition debt (pre-2017)** - Mortgages after October 13, 1987 and before December 16, 2017, to buy, build, or substantially improve your home (limit: $1M/$500K)
3. **Home acquisition debt (post-2017)** - Mortgages after December 15, 2017, to buy, build, or substantially improve your home (limit: $750K/$375K)

### Binding Contract Exception

A taxpayer who entered into a written binding contract before December 15, 2017, to close on the purchase of a principal residence before January 1, 2018, and who purchases such residence before April 1, 2018, is considered to have incurred the home acquisition debt prior to December 16, 2017.

## Points Deduction Rules

### When Points Are Fully Deductible in Year Paid

Points are fully deductible in the year paid if ALL these tests are met:

1. Loan is secured by your main home
2. Paying points is an established business practice in the area
3. Points paid weren't more than generally charged in that area
4. You use the cash method of accounting
5. Points weren't paid in place of amounts stated separately (appraisal fees, title fees, etc.)
6. Funds provided at closing were at least as much as points charged
7. You use the loan to buy or build your main home
8. Points were figured as a percentage of principal
9. Amount is clearly shown on settlement statement as points

### Second Home and Refinancing Rules

- **Second home** - Points can only be deducted over the life of the loan, not in year paid
- **Refinancing** - Points generally deducted over the life of the loan; exception for portion used to substantially improve main home

## Reporting on Tax Return

| Interest Type | Schedule A Line |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Interest reported on Form 1098 | Line 8a |
| Interest NOT reported on Form 1098 | Line 8b |
| Points NOT reported on Form 1098 | Line 8c |

## Special Situations

- **Late payment charges** - Deductible as home mortgage interest if not for specific services
- **Mortgage prepayment penalty** - Deductible as home mortgage interest
- **Sale of home** - Deduct interest up to (but not including) date of sale
- **Prepaid interest** - Must be spread over tax years to which it applies
- **Mortgage interest credit** - Available if issued a Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC); must reduce deduction by credit amount
- **Reverse mortgages** - Interest accrued is not deductible
