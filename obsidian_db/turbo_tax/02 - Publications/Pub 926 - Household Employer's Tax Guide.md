# Pub 926 - Household Employer's Tax Guide

## Summary

Publication 926 provides comprehensive guidance for individuals who hire household employees. It explains how to determine whether you have a household employee, calculate and pay employment taxes (Social Security, Medicare, FUTA), and file required forms.

## Who Needs This Publication

This publication applies if you hire someone to do household work in your home and you control not only what work is done, but how it is done. Examples include:
- Babysitters, nannies
- Housekeepers, maids
- Health aides, private nurses
- Cooks, butlers
- Drivers, yard workers

## Key Thresholds for 2026

| Tax Type | Threshold | Rate |
|----------|-----------|------|
| Social Security & Medicare | $3,000+ cash wages to one employee | 15.3% (7.65% each) |
| FUTA | $1,000+ total cash wages in any calendar quarter | 6% (first $7,000/employee) |
| Social Security Wage Base | Maximum wages subject to SS tax | $184,500 |

## Employment Tax Checklist

### When You Hire a Household Employee
- Verify employment eligibility using Form I-9
- Determine if you need to withhold and pay federal taxes
- Determine if you need to withhold and pay state taxes

### When You Pay Your Household Employee
- Withhold Social Security and Medicare taxes
- Withhold federal income tax (if requested)
- Decide how you will make tax payments
- Keep records

### Key Filing Deadlines
- **February 1, 2027**: Provide Form W-2 to employee; file Form W-2 and W-3 with SSA
- **April 15, 2027**: File Schedule H with federal income tax return

## What's New for 2026

### Social Security and Medicare Taxes
- Social security tax rate: 6.2% each for employee and employer
- Social security wage base limit: $184,500
- Medicare tax rate: 1.45% each for employee and employer (no wage base limit)
- Threshold for household workers: $3,000 or more in cash wages

### Qualified Overtime Compensation Deduction
For tax years 2025-2028, employees can deduct up to $12,500 ($25,000 if married filing jointly) of qualified overtime compensation. Employers should use updated Form W-4 and Pub. 15-T withholding procedures.

### Transportation Benefits
- Qualified parking monthly exclusion: $340
- Commuter highway vehicle/transit passes: $340/month

### Bicycle Commuting Reimbursements
P.L. 119-21 permanently eliminates the exclusion of qualified bicycle commuting reimbursements from employee income for tax years beginning after 2025.

## Forms You Must File

| Form | Purpose | Due Date |
|------|---------|----------|
| Form I-9 | Employment eligibility verification | First day of work |
| Form W-2 | Report wages and withholding | February 1, 2027 |
| Form W-3 | Transmittal of W-2s | February 1, 2027 |
| Schedule H | Report household employment taxes | April 15, 2027 |
| Form SS-4 | Apply for EIN | Before filing |

## Wages Not Counted

Don't count wages paid to:
- Your spouse
- Your child under age 21
- Your parent (with exceptions for childcare situations)
- Employee under age 18 (unless household services is their principal occupation)

## Payment Options

1. **With income tax return**: Pay with Form 1040 using Schedule H
2. **Estimated tax payments**: Use Form 1040-ES (due Apr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Jan 15)
3. **Withholding increase**: Increase withholding from pension/wages via Form W-4 or W-4P
4. **Business employer option**: Include with business employment tax deposits (Form 941/943/944)
