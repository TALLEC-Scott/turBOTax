# Publication 504 - Divorced or Separated Individuals

## Summary

Publication 504 explains tax rules that apply if you are divorced or separated from your spouse. It covers filing status options, dependent claims, alimony treatment, property settlements, and special rules for community property states.

## Filing Status After Divorce

### Unmarried Persons
You are unmarried for the whole year if:
- You obtained a final decree of divorce or separate maintenance by the last day of your tax year
- You obtained a decree of annulment (must file amended returns for affected years)

### Married Persons
You are married for the whole year if separated but without a final decree of divorce or separate maintenance by year-end.

### Head of Household
You may be considered unmarried and file as head of household if:
- You file a separate return
- You paid more than half the cost of keeping up your home
- Your spouse didn't live in your home during the last 6 months of the tax year
- Your home was the main home of your child for more than half the year

## Alimony Rules

### Post-2018 Instruments
For divorce or separation instruments executed after December 31, 2018:
- Alimony is **not deductible** by the payer
- Alimony is **not includible** in the recipient's income

### Pre-2019 Instruments
For instruments executed on or before December 31, 2018:
- Alimony is deductible by the payer
- Alimony is includible in the recipient's income
- Must report recipient's SSN or ITIN on tax return

### Alimony Requirements (Pre-2019)
- Payment must be in cash
- Instrument doesn't designate payment as not alimony
- Spouses aren't members of the same household
- No liability for payments after recipient's death
- Payment isn't treated as child support

## Relief from Joint Liability

### Innocent Spouse Relief
Available when your spouse (or former spouse) improperly reported or omitted items on a joint return. File [[Form 8857]] to request relief.

### Separation of Liability
Available to divorced, legally separated, or spouses who haven't lived together for 12 months before filing.

### Equitable Relief
Available when it would be unfair to hold you liable for understated or unpaid tax.

### Injured Spouse
File [[Form 8379]] to recover your share of a joint refund applied to your spouse's past-due debts (child support, student loans, etc.).

## Community Property States

Community property states include: Arizona, California, Idaho, Louisiana, Nevada, New Mexico, Texas, Washington, and Wisconsin.

### Relief from Community Income Liability
You may not be responsible for tax on community income if:
- You didn't file a joint return
- You didn't include the community income item
- The income belongs to your spouse under community property law
- You didn't know and had no reason to know of the income
- It would be unfair to include it in your income
