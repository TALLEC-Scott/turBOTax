# Publication 559 - Survivors, Executors, and Administrators

## Summary

IRS Publication 559 provides comprehensive guidance for personal representatives (executors, administrators) handling the tax responsibilities of a deceased individual's estate. It covers filing requirements for final income tax returns, estate income tax returns (Form 1041), and estate/gift tax returns (Forms 706 and 709). The publication explains how to report income in respect of a decedent, calculate estate tax deductions, and distribute assets to beneficiaries.

## Key Topics Covered

### Personal Representative Duties
- Applying for an Employer Identification Number (EIN) for the estate
- Filing all required tax returns (income, estate, gift)
- Paying taxes determined up to discharge from duties
- Filing [[Form 56]] to notify IRS of fiduciary relationship

### Final Income Tax Return (Form 1040/1040-SR)
- Due by April 15 of the year following death
- Must report "DECEASED" across the top with date of death
- Income included up to date of death
- Deductions and credits available to decedent

### Estate Income Tax Return (Form 1041)
- Required for estates with gross income ≥ $600
- Must file within 15th day of 4th month after end of tax year
- Exemption amount and income distribution deduction
- Separate shares rule for multiple beneficiaries

### Estate and Gift Taxes
- Basic exclusion amount for 2025: $13,990,000
- Annual gift exclusion for 2025: $19,000
- Portability of Deceased Spousal Unused Exclusion (DSUE)
- Generation-skipping transfer tax rules

## Key Rules and Exceptions

### Income in Respect of Decedent (IRD)
Income the decedent was entitled to but didn't receive before death. IRD is:
- Included in the decedent's gross estate for estate tax
- Taxable to the recipient (estate or beneficiary) when received
- Subject to estate tax deduction for estate tax paid on the income

### Basis of Inherited Property
Generally fair market value (FMV) on date of death (or alternate valuation date):
- Step-up in basis to FMV at death
- Special rules for community property states
- Qualified joint interest rules for spouses

### Tax Forgiveness
Available for members of Armed Forces who died in combat zone, victims of terrorism, and astronauts.
