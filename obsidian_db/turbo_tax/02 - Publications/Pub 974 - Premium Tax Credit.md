# Publication 974 - Premium Tax Credit

## Summary

Publication 974 provides comprehensive guidance on the Premium Tax Credit (PTC), a refundable tax credit that helps eligible individuals and families pay for health insurance coverage purchased through the Health Insurance Marketplace. This publication covers eligibility requirements, how to calculate the credit, and special situations including self-employed taxpayers, victims of domestic abuse, and those with employer-sponsored coverage.

## What's New for 2025

### Coverage Month Definition Changes
As of January 1, 2025, a month may be considered a coverage month (and PTC allowed) if a portion of the enrollment premium is unpaid, but the amount paid (including APTC) is sufficient to avoid termination of coverage under specific scenarios.

### MEC Reporting Changes
Reporting entities are no longer required to automatically send Forms 1095-B and 1095-C unless requested. If requested, the form must be provided by January 31 or 30 days after the request date, whichever is later.

### Employer Coverage Affordability for Family Members
For tax years beginning after 2022, affordability of employer coverage for an employee's spouse or dependent is based on the premium for the employee AND family members, not just the employee-only premium.

### Federal Poverty Line Percentages
For tax year 2025, taxpayers with household income exceeding 400% of the federal poverty line may be allowed a PTC.

## Who Must File Form 8962

You must file Form 8962 with your income tax return if:

1. You are taking the PTC
2. APTC was paid for you or another individual in your tax family
3. APTC was paid for an individual you told the Marketplace would be in your tax family, and neither you nor anyone else included that individual in a tax family

## Eligibility Requirements

To take the PTC for 2025, you must meet ALL of the following:

1. **Coverage Requirement**: An individual in your tax family was enrolled in a qualified health plan through the Marketplace on the first day of at least one month

2. **MEC Ineligibility**: That individual was not eligible for Minimum Essential Coverage (MEC) for the month, other than individual market coverage

3. **Premium Payment**: The enrollment premiums were paid by the due date of your tax return, covered entirely by APTC, or sufficient to avoid coverage termination

4. **Dependent Status**: No one can claim you as a dependent for the year

5. **Applicable Taxpayer Status**: Your household income is at least 100% of the federal poverty line (with exceptions), and if married, you generally must file jointly

## Special Situations

### Married Taxpayers
Married taxpayers must generally file jointly to claim PTC. Exceptions exist for:
- Certain married persons living apart (Exception 1)
- Victims of domestic abuse or spousal abandonment (Exception 2)

### Self-Employed Taxpayers
Self-employed taxpayers may claim both the PTC and the self-employed health insurance deduction, but must follow specific rules to coordinate the two benefits. See the illustrated example in the publication for the calculation method.

### Individuals Not Lawfully Present
Individuals not lawfully present in the United States are not eligible for PTC for their own coverage but may claim PTC for lawfully present family members.

### QSEHRA Recipients
If you were provided a QSEHRA, special rules apply to determine if it's affordable and how it affects your PTC. Use Worksheet N and Worksheet Q to calculate the impact.

## Important Definitions

- **Tax Family**: You, your spouse (if filing jointly), and your dependents
- **Coverage Family**: Individuals in your tax family enrolled in a qualified health plan and not eligible for other MEC
- **Household Income**: Modified AGI of you and your spouse plus modified AGI of dependents required to file
- **Monthly Contribution Amount**: Your share of premiums based on household income percentage
- **Applicable SLCSP Premium**: The second lowest cost silver plan premium for your coverage family

## Report Changes in Circumstances

Report these changes to the Marketplace promptly:
- Changes in household income
- Gaining or losing eligibility for other health coverage
- Moving to a different address
- Changes in employment
- Marriage or divorce
- Birth or adoption
- Other changes affecting tax family composition
