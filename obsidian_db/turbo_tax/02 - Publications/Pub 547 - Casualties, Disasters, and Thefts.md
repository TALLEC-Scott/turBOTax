# Publication 547 - Casualties, Disasters, and Thefts

## Summary

IRS Publication 547 explains the tax treatment of casualties, thefts, and losses on deposits. It covers how to determine if you have a deductible loss, how to figure the amount of your gain or loss, deduction limits, and special rules for disaster area losses.

## Key Topics Covered

- **[[Casualty Losses]]** - Damage, destruction, or loss of property from sudden, unexpected, or unusual events
- **[[Theft Losses]]** - Losses from the taking of money or property with intent to deprive
- **[[Disaster Area Losses]]** - Special rules for federally declared disaster areas
- **[[Qualified Disaster Loss]]** - Enhanced benefits for specific major disasters
- **[[Federally Declared Disasters]]** - Definition and requirements for federal disaster declarations
- **[[Insurance Reimbursements]]** - How reimbursements affect casualty and theft loss deductions
- **[[Casualty Loss Deduction Limits]]** - $100 rule and 10% AGI reduction rules
- **[[Replacement Property]]** - Postponing gain by purchasing replacement property
- **[[Loss on Deposits]]** - Losses from insolvent or bankrupt financial institutions

## Key Rules

### Personal-Use Property Limitations (Post-2017)

For tax years beginning after 2017, casualty and theft losses of personal-use property are deductible **only if attributable to a federally declared disaster**. Exception: Losses may offset personal casualty gains.

### Deduction Limits

1. **$100 Rule**: Reduce each casualty or theft loss by $100 ($500 for qualified disaster losses)
2. **10% AGI Rule**: Reduce total federal disaster losses by 10% of AGI (does not apply to qualified disaster losses)

### Qualified Disaster Loss Benefits

- $500 reduction instead of $100
- No 10% AGI reduction
- Can be claimed without itemizing (increased standard deduction)
- Can elect to claim loss in preceding tax year

## Safe Harbor Methods (Revenue Procedure 2018-08)

For determining casualty loss amounts:

1. **Estimated Repair Cost Method** - For losses $20,000 or less
2. **De Minimis Method** - For losses $5,000 or less
3. **Insurance Method** - Based on insurance company loss reports
4. **Contractor Safe Harbor** - For federally declared disasters
5. **Disaster Loan Appraisal** - Using federal loan appraisals
