# Publication 560 - Retirement Plans for Small Business

## Summary

Publication 560 provides comprehensive guidance on retirement plans for small businesses, covering SEP (Simplified Employee Pension) plans, SIMPLE (Savings Incentive Match Plan for Employees) plans, and Qualified Plans (including 401(k) plans). These plans offer tax-favored ways for employers and self-employed individuals to save for retirement.

## Key Topics Covered

- **SEP Plans**: Simplified method for making retirement contributions without complex qualified plan requirements
- **SIMPLE Plans**: Retirement plans for employers with 100 or fewer employees
- **Qualified Plans**: More complex plans with greater flexibility and higher contribution limits
- **401(k) Plans**: Cash or deferred arrangements allowing employee salary deferrals

## 2025 Contribution Limits

| Plan Type | Employee/Elective Deferral | Employer Contribution | Annual Addition Limit |
|-----------|---------------------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| SEP | N/A | 25% of compensation | $70,000 |
| SIMPLE IRA | $16,500 ($20,000 if 50+) | 3% matching or 2% nonelective | N/A |
| 401(k) | $23,500 ($31,000 if 50+) | 25% of compensation | $70,000 |
| Defined Benefit | N/A | Actuarially determined | $280,000 annual benefit |

## 2026 Contribution Limits

| Plan Type | Employee/Elective Deferral | Employer Contribution | Annual Addition Limit |
|-----------|---------------------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| SEP | N/A | 25% of compensation | $72,000 |
| SIMPLE IRA | $17,000 ($21,000 if 50+) | 3% matching or 2% nonelective | N/A |
| 401(k) | $24,500 ($32,500 if 50+) | 25% of compensation | $72,000 |
| Defined Benefit | N/A | Actuarially determined | $290,000 annual benefit |

## Compensation Limits

- **2025**: $350,000 maximum compensation for contribution calculations
- **2026**: $360,000 maximum compensation for contribution calculations

## Key SECURE 2.0 Act Changes

- **Automatic Enrollment**: Required for 401(k) and 403(b) plans established after December 29, 2022
- **Higher Catch-up Contributions (Ages 60-63)**: $11,250 for 401(k) plans, $5,250 for SIMPLE plans
- **Roth SEP and SIMPLE IRAs**: Contributions may be made to Roth IRAs under these arrangements
- **Starter 401(k)**: New deferral-only arrangement for certain employers
- **Student Loan Matching**: Employers can make matching contributions based on student loan payments

## Tax Credits for Employers

- **Small Employer Pension Plan Startup Credit**: Up to 100% of startup costs for employers with 1-50 employees
- **Automatic Enrollment Credit**: $500 per year for 3 years
- **Military Spouse Participation Credit**: Available for eligible small employers
