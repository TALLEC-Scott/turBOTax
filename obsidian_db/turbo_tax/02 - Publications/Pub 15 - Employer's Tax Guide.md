# Publication 15 - Employer's Tax Guide (Circular E)

## Summary

IRS Publication 15 (Circular E) is the comprehensive employer's tax guide explaining employer responsibilities for withholding, depositing, reporting, and paying employment taxes. It covers all employers, including agricultural employers and employers in U.S. territories.

**Tax Year:** 2026

## What's New for 2026

### Social Security and Medicare Tax Changes
- **Social security wage base limit:** $184,500 (up from previous year)
- **Social security tax rate:** 6.2% each for employer and employee
- **Medicare tax rate:** 1.45% each for employer and employee (no wage limit)
- **Household workers threshold:** $3,000 or more in cash wages
- **Election workers threshold:** $2,500 or more in cash or equivalent compensation

### Legislative Changes (P.L. 119-21 - One Big Beautiful Bill Act)
- **Individual tax rates:** Permanently extended from TCJA
- **Supplemental wage withholding rate:** 22% (37% if over $1 million)
- **Backup withholding rate:** 24%
- **Information reporting threshold:** Increased from $600 to $2,000

### New Deductions
- **Qualified tips deduction:** Up to $25,000 for tax years 2025-2029
- **Qualified overtime deduction:** Up to $12,500 ($25,000 if married filing jointly) for tax years 2025-2029
- **Moving expense reimbursements:** No longer excluded from income (except for military and intelligence community)

### Other Changes
- **Trump accounts:** New IRA type for children under 18; employers may contribute up to $2,500/year
- **Direct deposit refunds:** Now available for employment tax returns
- **Employment tax return transcripts:** Available electronically for 2023 and later

## Key Employer Responsibilities

### For New Employees
- Verify work eligibility using Form I-9
- Record employee name and SSN from social security card
- Request completed Form W-4
- Report new hires to state registry

### Each Payday
- Withhold federal income tax based on Form W-4
- Withhold employee's share of social security and Medicare taxes
- Deposit withheld taxes according to schedule

### Quarterly (Form 941 Filers)
- File Form 941 by April 30, July 31, October 31, January 31
- Deposit FUTA tax if undeposited amount exceeds $500

### Annually
- File Form 940 (FUTA) by January 31
- File Form W-2 with SSA by January 31
- Furnish Form W-2 to employees by January 31
- File Forms 1099-NEC by January 31
