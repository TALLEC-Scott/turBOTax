# Publication 225 - Farmer's Tax Guide

## Overview

Publication 225 explains how federal tax laws apply to farming businesses. A farm includes livestock, dairy, poultry, fish, fruit, and truck farms, as well as plantations, ranches, ranges, orchards, and groves. This guide covers farm income, expenses, depreciation, credits, and special provisions unique to agricultural operations.

## Key Topics Covered

### Recordkeeping Requirements
Farmers must maintain records to prepare accurate tax returns, monitor business progress, prepare financial statements, and support items reported on returns. Records should include gross income, expenses, deductions, and credits.

### Accounting Methods
Farmers can use cash, accrual, or special crop methods. The cash method is most common. Certain farming corporations and partnerships may be required to use accrual accounting.

### Farm Income
- **Schedule F (Form 1040)**: Primary form for reporting farm income and expenses
- **Sales of farm products**: Raised or purchased for resale
- **Crop shares**: Income recognition when converted to money
- **Agricultural program payments**: Most government payments are taxable
- **Market Assistance Loans (MALs)**: Special reporting rules available
- **Income averaging**: Available to farmers to smooth tax burden

### Farm Business Expenses
Deductible expenses include feed, seed, fertilizer, labor, repairs, insurance, rent, depreciation, and other ordinary business costs. Special rules apply to:
- **Prepaid farm supplies**: Limited to 50% of deductible farm expenses
- **Soil and water conservation**: Deductible subject to 25% of gross farm income limit
- **Depreciation**: MACRS system with special recovery periods for farm assets

### Depreciation and Section 179
- **Section 179 deduction**: Up to $2,500,000 for 2025
- **Bonus depreciation**: 100% special allowance restored for qualified property placed in service after January 19, 2025
- **Farm property recovery periods**: Vary by asset type (5-20 years under GDS)

### Self-Employment Tax
Farmers pay self-employment tax on net farm earnings. Maximum net earnings subject to Social Security tax is $176,100 for 2025. Optional methods available for low-income farmers.

### Employment Taxes
Farm employers must withhold income tax, pay Social Security and Medicare taxes, and file Form 943 annually. Special thresholds apply for agricultural employees.

### Fuel Tax Credits
Farmers may claim credits or refunds for federal excise taxes paid on fuel used for farming purposes.

## What's New for 2025

- **Standard mileage rate**: 70 cents per business mile
- **Section 179 limit**: $2,500,000 maximum deduction
- **Social Security wage base**: $176,100
- **100% bonus depreciation**: Restored for property placed in service after January 19, 2025
- **Qualified overtime compensation**: Deduction available for tax years 2025-2028
