# Publication 3 - Armed Forces' Tax Guide

## Summary

IRS Publication 3 covers the special tax situations of active members of the U.S. Armed Forces. It provides guidance on combat zone exclusions, military pay and allowances, filing extensions, and tax forgiveness for deceased servicemembers.

**Tax Year:** 2025
**Due Date:** April 15, 2026

## Key Topics Covered

### Gross Income
- **Taxable military pay** includes basic pay, bonus pay (career sea, diving duty, foreign duty, hardship duty, hostile fire/imminent danger), incentive pay (submarine, flight, hazardous duty), and accrued leave
- **Excluded from income:** Combat zone pay, BAH (Basic Allowance for Housing), BAS (Basic Allowance for Subsistence), death allowances, family allowances, moving allowances, travel allowances, and in-kind military benefits

### Combat Zone Benefits
- Combat zone pay exclusion for enlisted personnel (unlimited) and commissioned officers (limited to the highest enlisted rate + hostile fire pay)
- Filing deadline extensions (180 days + time remaining)
- Tax forgiveness for deaths in combat zones

### Credits
- [[Child Tax Credit]] - Maximum $2,200 per qualifying child for 2025
- [[Earned Income Credit]] - Special rules for nontaxable combat pay election
- Credit for excess Social Security tax withheld

### Filing Extensions
- Automatic 2-month extension for those outside the U.S.
- Combat zone service extensions (180 days after last day in zone)
- Spouse extensions (with limitations)

## Income Inclusions and Exclusions

### Included in Gross Income (Table 1)
- Basic pay (active duty, service school attendance, back wages)
- Bonus pay (reenlistment, career sea, diving, foreign duty, hardship, hostile fire)
- Incentive pay (submarine, flight, hazardous duty, HALO)
- Accrued leave, CONUS COLA, student loan repayments

### Excluded from Gross Income (Table 2)
- Combat zone pay (with officer limits)
- BAH, BAS, OHA (Overseas Housing Allowance)
- Death gratuities, burial services
- Moving allowances (dislocation, storage, temporary lodging)
- Medical/dental care, commissary discounts

## Tax Forgiveness for Deceased Servicemembers

Tax forgiveness applies when a member dies:
1. While in active service in a combat zone
2. From wounds, disease, or injury received in a combat zone
3. From wounds or injury incurred in a terrorist or military action

**Claim Process:**
- File Form 1040 or 1040-X with "Iraqi Freedom-KIA," "Enduring Freedom-KIA," etc. written on page 1
- Include Form 1310 (unless surviving spouse filing joint return)
- Attach death certification (DD Form 1300 for military)

## Filing Deadline Extensions

### Combat Zone Extensions
Deadline extended by:
1. 180 days after the last day in the combat zone (or last day of continuous qualified hospitalization), plus
2. The number of days remaining in the original filing period when you entered the zone

**Example:** If you had 105 days left in the filing period when you entered a combat zone, and leave on March 31, 2025, your deadline is extended 285 days (180 + 105) from that date.

### Spouse Extensions
Spouses receive the same extensions with two exceptions:
- Doesn't apply for tax years beginning more than 2 years after the area ceases to be a combat zone
- Doesn't apply during periods the servicemember is hospitalized in the U.S. for combat zone injuries
