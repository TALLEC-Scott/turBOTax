# Publication 554 - Tax Guide for Seniors

## Summary

IRS Publication 554 provides a comprehensive tax guide for older taxpayers (age 65 and older). It covers special tax provisions that apply to seniors, including higher filing thresholds, enhanced deductions, retirement income taxation, and credits available to elderly or disabled taxpayers.

## Key Topics

- **Filing Requirements** - Higher gross income thresholds for taxpayers age 65 or older
- **Standard Deduction** - Increased amounts for seniors with additional amounts for age 65+
- **Enhanced Deduction for Seniors** - New for 2025: up to $6,000 ($12,000 MFJ) deduction for eligible seniors
- **Social Security Benefits** - Taxation rules and how to determine taxable portion
- **Retirement Plan Distributions** - IRAs, pensions, annuities, and RMD rules
- **Sale of Home** - Exclusion of gain up to $250,000 ($500,000 MFJ)
- **Credits** - Credit for the elderly or disabled, EIC, child and dependent care credit

## What's New for 2025

### Enhanced Deduction for Seniors
Beginning in 2025, taxpayers age 65 or older may be eligible for an enhanced deduction:
- **Maximum amount**: $6,000 per person ($12,000 if married filing jointly and both spouses eligible)
- **Income limit**: Phased out if modified AGI exceeds $75,000 ($150,000 MFJ)

### Standard Deduction Amounts Increased (2025)
| Filing Status | Standard Deduction |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Single or Married filing separately | $15,750 |
| Married filing jointly or Qualifying surviving spouse | $31,500 |
| Head of household | $23,625 |

### AMT Exemption Increased
- $88,100 ($137,000 if MFJ or qualifying surviving spouse; $68,500 if MFS)

### Earned Income Credit Thresholds
Maximum income to qualify:
- No qualifying child: $19,104 ($26,214 if MFJ)
- One qualifying child: $50,434 ($57,554 if MFJ)
- Two qualifying children: $57,310 ($64,430 if MFJ)
- Three or more qualifying children: $61,555 ($68,675 if MFJ)

## Special Provisions for Seniors

### Higher Gross Income Threshold for Filing
Taxpayers age 65 or older get a higher filing threshold. You are considered age 65 on the day before your 65th birthday.

### Higher Standard Deduction
Additional standard deduction amount if age 65 or older at end of tax year.

### Credit for the Elderly or Disabled
Available to taxpayers age 65+ or those retired on permanent and total disability with taxable disability income.

## Filing Requirements for Seniors (2025)

| Filing Status | Age | File if Gross Income ≥ |
|---------------|-----|----------------------|
| Single | Under 65 | $15,750 |
| Single | 65 or older | $17,750 |
| Head of household | Under 65 | $23,625 |
| Head of household | 65 or older | $25,625 |
| Married filing jointly | Both under 65 | $31,500 |
| Married filing jointly | One spouse 65+ | $33,100 |
| Married filing jointly | Both 65+ | $34,700 |
| Married filing separately | Any age | $5 |
| Qualifying surviving spouse | Under 65 | $31,500 |
| Qualifying surviving spouse | 65 or older | $33,100 |
