# Publication 517 - Social Security and Other Information for Members of the Clergy and Religious Workers

## Summary

IRS Publication 517 provides comprehensive tax guidance for members of the clergy, religious workers, and members of recognized religious sects. It covers Social Security coverage rules, self-employment tax requirements, income exclusions for housing allowances, and retirement plan options specific to religious workers.

## Who Should Use This Publication

- [[Ministers]]
- [[Members of Religious Orders]]
- [[Religious Workers]] (church employees)
- Christian Science practitioners and readers
- [[SE Tax Exemption|Members of recognized religious sects seeking tax exemptions]]

## Key Topics

### Social Security Coverage

Clergy and religious workers are covered under either FICA or SECA depending on their classification:

| Class | FICA Coverage | SECA Coverage |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Minister | No (exempt) | Yes, unless exemption approved |
| Religious order member (no vow of poverty) | No (exempt) | Yes, unless exemption approved |
| Religious order member (vow of poverty) | Only if order elected coverage | No (exempt) |
| Religious worker (church employee) | Yes, unless employer elected exclusion | Yes, if employer elected exclusion |
| Member of recognized religious sect | Yes, unless exemption approved | Yes, unless exemption approved |

See [[Social Security Coverage for Clergy]] for detailed rules.

### Ministerial Services

Services performed in the exercise of ministry include:
- Conducting religious worship
- Performing sacerdotal functions
- Controlling, conducting, and maintaining religious organizations
- Teaching at theological seminaries

See [[Ministerial Services]] for complete definitions and special rules.

### Self-Employment Tax

Members of the clergy are generally treated as self-employed for Social Security tax purposes, even when employed by a church. The SE tax rate is 15.3% (12.4% for Social Security + 2.9% for Medicare).

For 2025, the maximum earnings subject to Social Security tax is $176,100.

See [[Self-Employment Tax for Clergy]] for calculation methods.

### Parsonage and Housing Allowance

Ministers may exclude from income:
- Fair rental value of a parsonage provided by the church
- Rental allowance used to provide a home (limited to fair rental value plus utilities)

This exclusion applies only for income tax, not for SE tax.

See [[Parsonage Allowance]] for detailed rules and examples.
