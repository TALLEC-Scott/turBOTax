# Publication 526 - Charitable Contributions

**Comprehensive guide to deducting charitable contributions for individuals.**

## Overview

Publication 526 explains how individuals claim deductions for charitable contributions. It covers:
- Types of organizations that qualify to receive deductible contributions
- Types of contributions you can deduct
- How much you can deduct
- Records you must keep
- How to report charitable contributions

A **charitable contribution** is a donation or gift to, or for the use of, a qualified organization. It is voluntary and made without getting, or expecting to get, anything of equal value.

## What's New for 2025

### Qualified Charitable Distribution One-Time Election
You can elect to make a one-time distribution up to **$54,000** from an IRA to charities through:
- Charitable remainder annuity trust
- Charitable remainder unitrust
- Charitable gift annuity (if funded only by QCDs)

See [[Pub 590-B]] for more information.

### Armed Forces Veteran Organizations
Beginning in 2025, contributions to **federally chartered veteran service organizations** under section 501(c)(19) are deductible even if membership is not limited primarily to wartime veterans.

## Key Topics

### Qualified Organizations

Only contributions to qualified organizations are deductible. See [[Qualified Organizations]] for detailed criteria.

**Types include:**
- Churches, synagogues, temples, mosques
- Nonprofit charitable organizations (Red Cross, United Way)
- Nonprofit educational organizations (colleges, museums, Scouting organizations)
- Nonprofit hospitals and medical research organizations
- War veterans' organizations
- Federal, state, and local governments (for public purposes only)

**Canadian, Mexican, and Israeli charities** may qualify under tax treaties.

### Types of Contributions

| Type | Deduction Limit | Key Requirements |
|------|-----------------|------------------|
| Cash | 60% of AGI | Bank record or written acknowledgment |
| Noncash (ordinary income property) | FMV or basis (lesser) | Receipt, Form 8283 if >$500 |
| Noncash (capital gain property) | 30% of AGI (FMV) | Qualified appraisal if >$5,000 |
| Qualified Conservation Contribution | 50% of AGI (100% for farmers/ranchers) | Special requirements apply |
| Vehicles | Varies | Form 1098-C from organization |

See [[Noncash Contributions]] and [[Vehicle Donations]] for details.

### Contribution Limits

Your deduction for charitable contributions generally cannot exceed **60% of your AGI**. Lower limits (20%, 30%, or 50%) may apply depending on:
- Type of property contributed
- Type of organization receiving the contribution
- Whether contribution is "for the use of" the organization

See [[Charitable Contribution Limits]] for detailed calculations.

### Contributions From Which You Benefit

If you receive a benefit from a contribution, deduct only the amount exceeding the benefit's value.

**Examples:**
- Charity dinner: Ticket price minus fair market value of meal
- Auction items: Payment minus fair market value received
- Memberships: Dues minus value of benefits received

**Token items** and certain membership benefits ($75 or less) can be disregarded.

### Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs)

A QCD is a distribution from your IRA directly to a qualified organization.

**Requirements:**
- Must be age 70½ or older
- Maximum $108,000 per year
- Must be from traditional or Roth IRA (not SEP or SIMPLE IRA during ongoing contributions)

**Tax Treatment:**
- Not included in taxable income
- Cannot also claim as charitable deduction
- Counts toward Required Minimum Distribution

See [[Qualified Charitable Distributions]] for full details.

### Volunteer Expenses

You cannot deduct the value of your time or services, but may deduct unreimbursed out-of-pocket expenses:

**Deductible:**
- 14 cents per mile for charitable use of car (standard rate)
- Actual gas and oil costs (if not using standard rate)
- Parking fees and tolls
- Uniforms required and not suitable for everyday use
- Travel expenses while away overnight performing services

**Not Deductible:**
- Value of your time or services
- Childcare expenses while volunteering
- Personal expenses

See [[Volunteer Expense Deductions]] for comprehensive rules.

### Substantiation Requirements

**Cash Contributions:**
- Bank record (canceled check, statement) showing organization name, date, and amount
- Written acknowledgment from organization
- Payroll deduction records plus pledge card

**Contributions of $250 or More:**
- Contemporaneous written acknowledgment (CWA) required
- Must include: amount, description of goods/services provided, good faith estimate of value
- Must receive before filing return or due date (whichever is earlier)

**Noncash Contributions:**
- Less than $250: Receipt from organization
- $250-$500: Written acknowledgment
- $500-$5,000: Form 8283, Section A
- Over $5,000: Form 8283, Section B + qualified appraisal

### Reporting Charitable Contributions

Report on **Schedule A (Form 1040)**:
- Line 11: Cash contributions and out-of-pocket expenses
- Line 12: Noncash contributions
- Lines 13-14: Carryovers from prior years

**Form 8283** required for noncash contributions over $500.

## Contributions You Cannot Deduct

- Contributions to specific individuals
- Contributions to nonqualified organizations (civic leagues, social clubs, labor unions, chambers of commerce)
- Part of contribution from which you benefit
- Value of your time or services
- Appraisal fees
- Certain contributions to donor-advised funds
- Contributions of partial interests in property (with exceptions)
- Raffle, bingo, or lottery tickets
- Tuition payments
