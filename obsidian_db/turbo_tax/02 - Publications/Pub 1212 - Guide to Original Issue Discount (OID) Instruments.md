# Publication 1212 - Guide to Original Issue Discount (OID) Instruments

## Summary

Publication 1212 serves two primary purposes:
1. Help brokers and middlemen identify publicly offered OID debt instruments held as nominees to file Forms 1099-OID or 1099-INT
2. Help owners of OID debt instruments determine how much OID to report on income tax returns

## Important Definitions

### Original Issue Discount (OID)
OID is a form of interest representing the excess of a debt instrument's stated redemption price at maturity over its issue price. Zero coupon bonds and debt instruments paying no stated interest until maturity are examples.

### Accrual Period
An interval of time used to measure OID, typically 6 months or 1 year depending on when the debt instrument was issued.

### Qualified Stated Interest
Stated interest unconditionally payable in cash or property at least annually over the debt instrument term at a single fixed rate.

### Yield to Maturity (YTM)
The discount rate that produces an amount equal to the issue price when used to figure present value of all principal and interest payments.

## Debt Instruments NOT in OID Tables

- U.S. savings bonds
- Certificates of deposit issued at discount
- Tax-exempt organization obligations
- Mortgage-backed securities
- REMIC regular interests and CDOs
- Commercial paper and banker's acceptances
- Foreign obligations not traded in the United States

## Key Rules

### De Minimis Rule
OID can be treated as zero if total OID is less than 0.25% (0.0025) of stated redemption price multiplied by number of full years from issue to maturity.

### Including OID in Income
Generally, OID must be included in income as it accrues each year, whether or not payments are received from the issuer.

### Exceptions to OID Accrual Rules
- U.S. savings bonds
- Tax-exempt obligations
- Loans of $10,000 or less between individuals not in lending business

## Reporting OID

Report OID and taxable interest on Schedule B (Form 1040). Adjustments may be needed for:
- Acquisition premium
- Market discount
- Premium amortization
