# Publication 570 - Tax Guide for Individuals With Income From U.S. Territories

## Summary

Publication 570 provides comprehensive guidance for individuals with income from U.S. territories, including American Samoa, the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands (CNMI), Guam, Puerto Rico, and the U.S. Virgin Islands (USVI). It covers residency requirements, income sourcing rules, and filing obligations for both U.S. and territory tax returns.

## Important Rules

### Bona Fide Residence Requirements

To qualify as a bona fide resident of a U.S. territory, you must:
1. Meet the presence test (183 days in territory, or 549 days over 3-year period with 60 days minimum per year, or other alternatives)
2. Not have a tax home outside the relevant territory
3. Not have a closer connection to the United States or a foreign country than to the territory

### Income Sourcing

Same general rules as U.S. source income apply, with exceptions:
- Compensation for labor/services: Sourced where services are performed
- Interest: Residence of payer
- Dividends: Where corporation is organized
- Rental income: Location of property
- Real property sales: Location of property
- Personal property sales: Seller's tax home

### Filing Obligations by Territory

| Territory | Bona Fide Resident Files With | Non-Resident Files With |
|-----------|-------------------------------|-------------------------|
| American Samoa | AS + U.S. (Form 4563) | AS + U.S. (credit for AS tax) |
| CNMI | CNMI only | U.S. + Form 5074 |
| Guam | Guam only | U.S. + Form 5074 |
| Puerto Rico | PR + U.S. (if U.S. income) | U.S. (credit for PR tax) |
| USVI | USVI only | U.S. + Form 8689 |

### Special Tax Rules

- **Self-Employment Tax**: Must be paid to U.S. even if no income tax filing required
- **Additional Medicare Tax**: Applies to territory residents with earned income above thresholds
- **Net Investment Income Tax**: May apply to bona fide residents of Puerto Rico and American Samoa with non-territory income
- **Earned Income Credit**: Generally not available to territory residents on U.S. returns

## Deductions if Territory Income Is Excluded

When territory income is excluded from U.S. tax:
- Deductions specifically related to excluded income are not allowable
- Other deductions must be allocated between excluded and taxable income
- Standard deduction is prorated based on ratio of taxable to total income

Formula: (Gross income subject to U.S. tax ÷ Gross income from all sources) × Deduction
