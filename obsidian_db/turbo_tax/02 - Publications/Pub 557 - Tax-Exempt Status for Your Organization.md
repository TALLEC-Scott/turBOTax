# Pub 557 - Tax-Exempt Status for Your Organization

## Summary

IRS Publication 557 provides comprehensive guidance on tax-exempt status for organizations. It covers the application procedures, approval processes, and ongoing compliance requirements for various types of exempt organizations under IRC Section 501(c).

## Key Topics

### Chapter 1: Application, Approval, and Appeal Procedures
- **Application Procedures** - How to apply for recognition of tax-exempt status
- **Forms Required** - Form 1023, Form 1024, Form 1024-A, and Form 8718
- **Determination Letters** - IRS responses to exemption applications
- **Appeal Procedures** - Process for appealing adverse determinations

### Chapter 2: Filing Requirements and Disclosures
- **Annual Information Returns** - Form 990 series filing requirements
- **Public Inspection Requirements** - Making returns available for public review
- **Disclosure Requirements** - Quid pro quo contributions, acknowledgments

### Chapter 3: Section 501(c)(3) Organizations
- **Charitable Purposes** - Religious, educational, scientific, literary purposes
- **Application Process** - Form 1023 and Form 1023-EZ requirements
- **Private Foundations vs. Public Charities** - Classification and support tests
- **Lobbying and Political Activity Restrictions**

### Chapter 4: Other Exempt Organizations
- **501(c)(4) - Social Welfare Organizations**
- **501(c)(5) - Labor, Agricultural, and Horticultural Organizations**
- **501(c)(6) - Business Leagues, Chambers of Commerce**
- **501(c)(7) - Social and Recreation Clubs**
- **501(c)(8)/(10) - Fraternal Organizations**
- **501(c)(9) - Voluntary Employees' Beneficiary Associations**
- **501(c)(12) - Benevolent Life Insurance Associations**

### Chapter 5: Private Foundations
- **Private Foundation Status** - Definition and classification
- **Excise Taxes** - Net investment income tax, self-dealing restrictions
- **Distribution Requirements** - Minimum distribution rules
- **Excess Business Holdings** - Restrictions on business ownership

## Important Rules

1. Organizations must be organized and operated exclusively for exempt purposes
2. No part of net earnings may inure to private shareholders or individuals
3. Section 501(c)(3) organizations cannot participate in political campaigns
4. Private foundations face additional excise taxes and restrictions
5. Annual information returns (Form 990 series) are generally required

## Application Deadlines

| Organization Type | Application Form | Deadline |
|-------------------|------------------|----------|
| 501(c)(3) Organizations | Form 1023/1023-EZ | Within 27 months of formation |
| Other 501(c) Organizations | Form 1024 | Within 27 months of formation |
| Group Exemption | Central Organization Request | Annual updates required |
