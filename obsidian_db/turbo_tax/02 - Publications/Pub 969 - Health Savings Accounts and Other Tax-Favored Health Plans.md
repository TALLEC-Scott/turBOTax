# Publication 969 - Health Savings Accounts and Other Tax-Favored Health Plans

## Summary

Publication 969 explains tax-advantaged programs designed to help individuals offset health care costs. It covers four main programs: Health Savings Accounts (HSAs), Medical Savings Accounts (Archer MSAs and Medicare Advantage MSAs), Health Flexible Spending Arrangements (FSAs), and Health Reimbursement Arrangements (HRAs).

## Key Topics Covered

- [[Health Savings Accounts]] - Tax-exempt accounts for eligible individuals with HDHPs
- [[High Deductible Health Plans]] - Requirements and limits for HDHPs
- [[Archer MSAs]] - Medical savings accounts for self-employed and small employer employees
- [[Medicare Advantage MSAs]] - MSAs designated by Medicare for qualified individuals
- [[Health FSAs]] - Employer-established flexible spending arrangements
- [[Health Reimbursement Arrangements]] - Employer-funded reimbursement arrangements
- [[Qualified Medical Expenses]] - Definition and eligible expenses for all account types
- [[HSA Contribution Limits]] - Annual limits and eligibility rules

## What's New for 2025

### Telehealth and Remote Care Services
Public Law 119-21 (July 4, 2025) amended Code section 223 to provide that:
1. An HSA-eligible individual may have disregarded coverage for telehealth and other remote care
2. A plan will not fail to be treated as an HDHP by reason of failing to have a deductible for telehealth services

These amendments apply to plan years beginning after 2024.

### Health FSA Contribution and Carryover for 2025
- Contribution limit: **$3,300** (Revenue Procedure 2024-40)
- Maximum carryover: **$660**

### Preventive Care (Notice 2024-75)
Expanded preventive care benefits for HDHPs include:
- Over-the-counter oral contraceptives (including emergency contraceptives)
- Male condoms
- All types of breast cancer screening for undiagnosed individuals
- Continuous glucose monitors for diabetics

## Comparison of Tax-Favored Health Plans

| Feature | HSA | Archer MSA | Health FSA | HRA |
|---------|-----|------------|------------|-----|
| Who can contribute | Individual, employer, others | Individual OR employer (not both) | Employee, employer | Employer only |
| Self-employed eligible | Yes | Yes | No | No |
| Contributions deductible | Yes | Yes | Pre-tax salary reduction | Not taxable |
| Earnings tax-free | Yes | Yes | N/A | N/A |
| Distributions tax-free | For qualified medical expenses | For qualified medical expenses | For qualified medical expenses | For qualified medical expenses |
| Portable | Yes | Yes | No | No |
| Rollover allowed | Yes | Yes | Limited carryover/grace period | Yes |

## Important Rules

1. **Eligibility Requirements** - Must be covered under an HDHP, have no other health coverage (with exceptions), not enrolled in Medicare, and not claimed as a dependent

2. **Last-Month Rule** - If eligible on December 1, treated as eligible for entire year, subject to testing period

3. **Catch-up Contributions** - Individuals age 55+ may contribute an additional $1,000 per year

4. **Excess Contributions** - Subject to 6% excise tax per year remaining in account

5. **Non-qualified Distributions** - Subject to income tax plus 20% additional tax (exceptions for disability, age 65+, or death)

6. **Prohibited Transactions** - Account ceases to be HSA; fair market value included in income
