# Publication 925 - Passive Activity and At-Risk Rules

## Summary

Publication 925 explains two sets of rules that limit deductible losses from trades, businesses, rental activities, and other income-producing activities:

1. **Passive Activity Rules** - Limit losses from activities in which you don't materially participate
2. **At-Risk Rules** - Limit losses to the amount you have "at risk" in the activity

**Critical:** Apply at-risk rules BEFORE passive activity rules when figuring allowable losses.

## Who Must Use These Rules

The passive activity rules apply to:
- Individuals
- Trusts (other than grantor trusts)
- Estates
- Personal service corporations
- Closely held corporations

Even though the rules don't apply directly to grantor trusts, partnerships, and S corporations, they do apply to the owners of these entities.

- [[Passive Activity Loss]] - Generally disallowed, with exceptions

### At-Risk Rules

- [[At-Risk Rules]] - Limit losses to amounts you have at risk

### Other Topics

- Publicly traded partnerships

## Special $25,000 Allowance

Taxpayers who **actively participate** in rental real estate activities may deduct up to $25,000 of loss from nonpassive income, subject to phaseout:

| Modified AGI | Allowance |
|--------------|-----------|
| $100,000 or less | Full $25,000 |
| $100,001 - $150,000 | Phased out 50¢ per dollar |
| $150,000 or more | $0 |

Married filing separately with lived apart all year: Maximum $12,500, phaseout $50,000-$75,000.

## Key Concepts

### Types of Passive Activities

1. **Rental activities** - Even if you materially participate (unless you're a real estate professional)
2. **Trade or business activities** - In which you don't materially participate

### Order of Loss Limitations

1. Basis limitations
2. At-risk limitations (Form 6198)
3. Passive loss limitations (Form 8582)
4. Excess business loss limitation (Form 461)
