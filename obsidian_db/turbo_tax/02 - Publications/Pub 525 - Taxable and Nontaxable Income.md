# Publication 525 - Taxable and Nontaxable Income

## Summary

Publication 525 provides comprehensive guidance on what types of income are taxable and nontaxable for federal income tax purposes. It covers employee compensation, fringe benefits, business and investment income, sickness and injury benefits, and various types of miscellaneous income.

- [[Stock Options]] - Nonstatutory and statutory stock options, ISOs, employee stock purchase plans

## What's New for 2025

- **Overtime compensation** - May be able to claim deduction for pay exceeding regular rate
- **Tips** - May be able to claim deduction for qualified tips
- **Deferred contribution limit increased** - $23,500 standard, $31,000 if 50+, $34,750 if ages 60-63
- **Health FSA limit** - $3,300 for salary reduction contributions
- **Rollovers from 529 plans to Roth IRAs** - Now permitted under certain restrictions

## Core Principles

### Constructively Received Income

Income is taxable when it is made available to you, regardless of whether actually in your possession. A valid check received or made available before year-end is constructively received in that year.

### Assignment of Income

Income received by an agent for you is constructively received when the agent receives it. If you agree that a third party receives income for you, you must include it when the third party receives it.

### Fair Market Value (FMV)

The price at which property would change hands between a willing buyer and seller, neither being required to buy or sell and both having reasonable knowledge of relevant facts.

## Major Sections

### Employee Compensation

All compensation for personal services is taxable including:
- Wages, salaries, commissions, fees, tips
- Bonuses and awards (cash, goods, services)
- Back pay awards
- Severance pay
- Differential wage payments
- Sick pay from employer

Related: [[Form W-2]], [[Self-Employment Tax]], [[Tips and Tip Reporting]]

### Fringe Benefits

Most fringe benefits are taxable unless specifically excluded:
- Accident or health plans (generally nontaxable)
- Adoption assistance (exclusion up to limits)
- Athletic facilities (nontaxable if on employer premises)
- De minimis benefits (nontaxable)
- Dependent care benefits (exclusion up to $5,000)
- Educational assistance (exclusion up to $5,250)
- Employee discounts (limited exclusion)
- Group-term life insurance (first $50,000 nontaxable)
- Meals and lodging (nontaxable under conditions)
- Transportation benefits (limited exclusion)
- Working condition benefits (nontaxable)

Related: [[Health Savings Account]], [[Dependent Care Benefits]], [[Cafeteria Plans]]

### Stock Options

Two main types with different tax treatment:

**Nonstatutory Stock Options:**
- Taxable upon exercise (spread between FMV and exercise price)
- Reported on Form W-2, box 12, code V

**Statutory Stock Options:**
- Incentive Stock Options (ISOs) - No regular tax at exercise, but AMT adjustment may apply
- Employee Stock Purchase Plans - Special holding period rules

Related: [[Alternative Minimum Tax]], [[Capital Gains]], [[Form 3921]], [[Form 3922]]

### Retirement Plan Contributions

- Employer contributions to qualified plans generally not taxed until distribution
- Elective deferrals: $23,500 limit for 2025 (plus catch-up contributions)
- Excess deferrals must be included in income

Related: [[IRAs]], [[401k Plans]], [[Retirement Plans]], [[Pub 590]]

### Disability and Injury Benefits

**Taxable:**
- Disability pensions from employer-paid plans
- Workers' compensation that reduces Social Security benefits
- Sick pay from employer

**Generally Nontaxable:**
- Workers' compensation for occupational injury/illness
- Benefits from policy you paid premiums for
- Compensatory damages for physical injury
- Combat-related disability payments

Related: [[Military Pay and Allowances]], [[VA Disability Benefits]], [[Pub 907]]

### Miscellaneous Income

- **Bartering** - FMV of goods/services received is taxable
- **Canceled debt** - Generally taxable unless exception applies
- **Court awards and damages** - Taxability depends on nature
- **Gambling winnings** - Fully taxable
- **Prizes and awards** - FMV is taxable

Related: [[Gambling Income]], [[Court Awards and Damages]]
