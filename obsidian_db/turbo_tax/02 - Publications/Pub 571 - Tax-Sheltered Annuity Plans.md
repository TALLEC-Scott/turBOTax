# Publication 571 - Tax-Sheltered Annuity Plans (403(b) Plans)

## Summary

IRS Publication 571 provides comprehensive guidance on 403(b) plans, also known as tax-sheltered annuity (TSA) plans. These retirement plans are available to employees of public schools, certain tax-exempt organizations, and ministers.

## Key Updates for 2026

- **Limit on elective deferrals** increased from $23,500 to **$24,500**
- **Limit on annual additions** increased from $70,000 to **$72,000**
- **Retirement savings contributions credit** AGI limits increased:
  - Married filing jointly: $80,500 (up from $79,000)
  - Head of household: $60,375 (up from $59,250)
  - Single/MFS/QSS: $40,250 (up from $39,500)

## Key Updates for 2025

- **Enhanced catch-up contributions** for ages 60-63: Greater of $10,000 or 150% of standard catch-up limit ($7,500), resulting in up to $11,250

## Who Can Participate

Eligible employees include:
- Employees of tax-exempt organizations under section 501(c)(3)
- Employees of public school systems involved in day-to-day operations
- Employees of cooperative hospital service organizations
- Civilian faculty and staff of Uniformed Services University
- Certain ministers (including self-employed ministers and chaplains)

## Types of Contributions

1. **Elective deferrals** - Contributions under salary reduction agreements
2. **Nonelective contributions** - Employer contributions not under salary reduction
3. **After-tax contributions** - Contributions made with already-taxed income

## Maximum Amount Contributable (MAC)

Your MAC is the lesser of:
- Your [[Annual Additions Limit]]
- Your [[Elective Deferrals|Limit on Elective Deferrals]]
