# Publication 1040 - Tax and Earned Income Credit Tables (2025)

## Summary

This publication contains the **Tax Table** and **Earned Income Credit (EIC) Table** from the Instructions for Form 1040 and 1040-SR for tax year 2025. These tables are used to determine tax liability and calculate the Earned Income Credit for qualifying taxpayers.

## Purpose

The Tax Table provides tax amounts based on:
- **Taxable income** (Form 1040, line 15)
- **Filing status** (Single, Married Filing Jointly, Married Filing Separately, Head of Household, Qualifying Surviving Spouse)

The EIC Table provides credit amounts based on:
- **Earned income**
- **Filing status**
- **Number of qualifying children**

## Key Components

### Tax Table

The Tax Table is used when taxable income is under $100,000. For incomes of $100,000 or more, taxpayers must use the **Tax Computation Worksheet**.

**How to use the Tax Table:**
1. Find the row with your taxable income range (At least... But less than...)
2. Find the column for your filing status
3. Read down the column to find your tax amount

**Example:** A married couple filing jointly with taxable income of $25,300:
- Find the $25,300-$25,350 row
- Find the "Married filing jointly" column
- Tax amount: $2,562

### Filing Status Categories

| Column | Who Uses It |
|--------|-------------|
| Single | Unmarried taxpayers |
| Married filing jointly | Married couples filing together |
| Married filing separately | Married couples filing separately |
| Head of household | Unmarried taxpayers with dependents |
| Qualifying surviving spouse | Widows/widowers with dependent children (uses MFJ column) |

### 2025 Tax Computation Worksheet

For taxable income of $100,000 or more, use the Tax Computation Worksheet instead of the Tax Table.

### 2025 EIC Table

The Earned Income Credit table helps low-to-moderate income working taxpayers calculate their credit. The amount depends on:
- Earned income
- Filing status
- Number of qualifying children (0, 1, 2, or 3+)
