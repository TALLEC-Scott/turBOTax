# Pub 4681 - Canceled Debts, Foreclosures, Repossessions, and Abandonments

## Summary

Publication 4681 explains the federal tax treatment of canceled debts, foreclosures, repossessions, and abandonments for individuals. Generally, if a debt is canceled or forgiven for less than the full amount owed, the canceled amount is treated as taxable income. However, several exclusions may apply to make the canceled debt nontaxable.

## Key Topics Covered

### Chapter 1: Canceled Debts

- **General Rule**: Canceled debt is generally taxable as ordinary income
- **Form 1099-C**: Lenders report canceled debts of $600 or more
- **Identifiable Event Codes**: Box 6 codes (A-H) indicate the reason for cancellation

### Exclusions from Income

1. **Bankruptcy Exclusion** - Debt canceled in a Title 11 bankruptcy case
2. **Insolvency Exclusion** - Debt canceled when liabilities exceed assets
3. **Qualified Farm Indebtedness** - Debt canceled for qualifying farmers
4. **Qualified Real Property Business Indebtedness** - Election available for business real property debt
5. **Qualified Principal Residence Indebtedness** - Mortgage debt on main home (expires after 2025)

### Chapter 2: Foreclosures and Repossessions

- **Recourse Debt**: Amount realized is the smaller of outstanding debt or FMV of property
- **Nonrecourse Debt**: Amount realized is the full outstanding debt
- **Gain/Loss Calculation**: Compare amount realized to adjusted basis

### Chapter 3: Abandonments

- **Recourse Debt Abandonment**: No gain/loss until foreclosure completes
- **Nonrecourse Debt Abandonment**: Treated as sale or exchange immediately

## Important Rules

1. Report taxable canceled debt as ordinary income on Schedule 1 (Form 1040), line 8c for nonbusiness debt
2. Business debt may be reported on Schedule C, E, or F depending on the type
3. File [[Form 982]] to claim exclusions and reduce tax attributes
4. Bankruptcy and insolvency exclusions must be applied before other exclusions
5. Qualified principal residence indebtedness exclusion expires after December 31, 2025

## Key Forms

| Form | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| [[Form 982]] | Reduction of Tax Attributes Due to Discharge of Indebtedness |
| Form 1099-C | Cancellation of Debt (received from lender) |
| Form 1099-A | Acquisition or Abandonment of Secured Property |

## Tax Attribute Reduction

When excluding canceled debt from income, you must reduce certain tax attributes in the following order:

1. Net operating losses (NOLs)
2. General business credit carryovers
3. Minimum tax credit
4. Capital loss carryovers
5. Basis of property
6. Passive activity loss and credit carryovers
7. Foreign tax credit carryovers

## What's New for 2025

- **Qualified principal residence indebtedness**: Cannot be excluded from income for discharges completed after December 31, 2025
- **Student loan discharge**: SSN required on tax return for discharge after 2025 due to death or disability
