# Publication 17 - Your Federal Income Tax

**Comprehensive guide to federal income tax for individuals.**

## Overview

Publication 17 is the IRS's primary guide for individual taxpayers, covering the general rules for filing a federal income tax return. It follows Form 1040 and Form 1040-SR structure, explaining tax law to help taxpayers pay only what they owe.

## What's New for 2025

### Standard Deduction Amounts Increased
- **Single or Married filing separately:** $15,750
- **Married filing jointly or Qualifying surviving spouse:** $31,500
- **Head of household:** $23,625

See [[Standard Deduction]] for details.

### New Deductions (Schedule 1-A)
- **No tax on tips:** Deduction up to $25,000 (phaseout above $150,000 MAGI)
- **No tax on overtime:** Deduction up to $12,500 ($25,000 MFJ)
- **No tax on car loan interest:** Deduction up to $10,000 for vehicles purchased in 2025
- **Enhanced senior deduction:** Up to $6,000 for those born before January 2, 1961

### Other Changes
- **SALT deduction limit increased:** $40,000 ($20,000 MFS), reduced above $500,000 MAGI
- **Trump accounts:** New retirement account type for children (Form 4547)
- **Adoption credit:** Up to $5,000 refundable; $17,280 maximum per child
- **Deferred compensation limits:** $23,500 standard; $31,000 if 50+; $37,750 if ages 60-63
- **AMT exemption:** $88,100 ($137,000 MFJ)

## Key Topics Covered

### Filing Requirements
Must file if gross income exceeds threshold based on:
- Filing status
- Age
- Whether claimed as dependent

### Electronic Filing
- Free File available for eligible taxpayers
- E-file provides faster processing and refunds
- Direct deposit recommended for refunds

### Extensions
- Automatic 6-month extension available (Form 4868)
- Extension to file ≠ extension to pay

### Penalties
- Accuracy-related penalty: 20% of underpayment
- Fraud penalty: 75% of underpayment due to fraud
- Frivolous tax submission: $5,000 penalty
- Failure to file/pay penalties may apply
