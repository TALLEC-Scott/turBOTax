# Publication 970 - Tax Benefits for Education

## Summary

Publication 970 explains tax benefits available for saving for or paying education costs for yourself or family members. Most benefits apply to higher education, with some extending to elementary and secondary education expenses.

## Key Topics Covered

### Education Credits
- [[American Opportunity Credit]] - Chapter 2 - Up to $2,500 per eligible student
- [[Lifetime Learning Credit]] - Chapter 3 - Up to $2,000 per tax return

### Deductions
- [[Student Loan Interest Deduction]] - Chapter 4 - Up to $2,500 deduction
- [[Business Deduction for Work-Related Education]] - Chapter 11

### Education Savings Accounts
- [[Coverdell ESA]] - Chapter 6 - Tax-advantaged education savings
- [[Qualified Tuition Program]] - Chapter 7 - 529 plans
- [[Education Savings Bond Program]] - Chapter 9 - Tax-free interest for education

### Tax-Free Benefits
- [[Scholarships and Fellowship Grants]] - Chapter 1 - Tax-free treatment rules
- [[Employer-Provided Educational Assistance]] - Chapter 10 - Up to $5,250 exclusion
- [[Student Loan Cancellations]] - Chapter 5 - Tax-free loan forgiveness
- [[Education Exception to Early IRA Distribution Tax]] - Chapter 8 - Avoid 10% penalty

## Important Rules

1. **No Double Benefit** - You generally cannot claim more than one tax benefit for the same education expense
2. **MAGI Limits** - Many benefits phase out at higher income levels
3. **Eligible Educational Institution** - Must be accredited and eligible for federal student aid
4. **Qualified Education Expenses** - Vary by benefit type; some include room and board, others do not

## What's New for Tax Year 2025

- **Trump Accounts** - New Form 4547 for establishing Trump accounts for children born 2025-2028
- **Student Loan Interest Deduction** - Phase-out range $85,000-$100,000 ($170,000-$200,000 MFJ)
- **Education Savings Bond Program** - Phase-out range $99,500-$114,500 ($149,250-$179,250 MFJ)
- **Standard Mileage Rate** - 70 cents per mile for work-related education

## What's New for Tax Year 2026

- **SSN Requirement** - American Opportunity and Lifetime Learning credits will require valid SSN for work
