# Publication 575 - Pension and Annuity Income

## Summary

Publication 575 explains the tax treatment of distributions from pension and annuity plans. It covers how to report pension and annuity income, including periodic payments (annuities) and nonperiodic payments (lump-sum distributions). The publication also addresses rollovers, early distribution penalties, and special rules for specific situations.

## Key Topics

### Types of Retirement Income

- **Pension Payments** - Series of payments made after retirement based on years of service and compensation
- **Annuity Payments** - Payments under a contract at regular intervals over more than 1 year
- **Variable Annuities** - Payments that vary based on investment performance
- **Disability Pensions** - Payments received before minimum retirement age due to disability

### Taxation Methods

- **Simplified Method** - Worksheet for figuring tax-free part of pension payments
- **General Rule** - Used for nonqualified plans and certain older contracts
- **Cost Recovery** - How to recover your investment in the contract tax-free

### Distribution Types

- **Periodic Payments** - Regular payments over time (annuity)
- **Nonperiodic Payments** - Lump-sum distributions and withdrawals
- **Rollovers** - Tax-free transfers between retirement plans
- **Loans** - When loans from plans are treated as distributions

### Special Taxes

- **Early Distribution Tax** - 10% additional tax on distributions before age 59½
- **Excess Accumulation Tax** - Tax on insufficient distributions after age 73
- **Exceptions** - Various exceptions to early distribution penalties

### Special Situations

- **Railroad Retirement Benefits** - Tier 1 (SSEB and NSSEB) and Tier 2 benefits
- **Section 457 Plans** - Deferred compensation plans for government and tax-exempt employees
- **Public Safety Officers** - Exclusion for health insurance premiums
- **Disaster Relief** - Special rules for qualified disaster distributions

## Key Rules

1. **Cost Recovery** - You recover your investment in the contract tax-free over time
2. **Rollover Rules** - Generally must complete rollover within 60 days
3. **Withholding** - Distributions are subject to federal income tax withholding
4. **Early Distribution Penalty** - 10% tax applies to distributions before age 59½ unless exception applies
5. **Required Minimum Distributions** - Must begin after age 73 to avoid excess accumulation tax
