# Pub 503 - Child and Dependent Care Expenses

Covers the rules for claiming the [[Child and Dependent Care Credit]] for taxpayers who pay someone to care for a qualifying person so they can work or look for work. Also covers employment tax rules for household employers.

## Summary

The Child and Dependent Care Credit can be worth up to 35% of employment-related expenses. To qualify, you must pay expenses so you (and your spouse if filing jointly) can work or look for work. The care must be for a [[Qualifying Person]].

## Tests to Claim the Credit

You must meet ALL of the following tests:

1. **Qualifying Person Test** - Care must be for one or more qualifying persons
2. **Earned Income Test** - You (and spouse if filing jointly) must have earned income
3. **Work-Related Expense Test** - Expenses must allow you to work or look for work
4. **Payment Test** - Payments can't be to your spouse, your dependent, or your child's other parent (if child under 13)
5. **Joint Return Test** - If married, must generally file jointly (exceptions for legally separated or living apart)
6. **Provider Identification Test** - Must identify the care provider on your tax return

## Dollar Limits

| Qualifying Persons | Maximum Expenses |
|--------------------|------------------|
| One | $3,000 |
| Two or more | $6,000 |

**Note:** Dollar limit is reduced by any [[Dependent Care Benefits]] excluded from income.

## Credit Percentage

The credit percentage ranges from **20% to 35%** based on your adjusted gross income (AGI):

| AGI Range | Credit Percentage |
|-----------|-------------------|
| $0 - $15,000 | 35% |
| $15,000 - $43,000 | Decreases 1% per $2,000 |
| Over $43,000 | 20% (minimum) |

## What's New for 2025

**Trump Account and Form 4547** - Parents, guardians, and authorized individuals can establish a Trump account for children born after 2024 and before 2029. A $1,000 pilot program contribution may be available. See Form 4547 for details.

## Reminders

- Taxpayer identification number (SSN, ITIN, or ATIN) required for each qualifying person
- May have to pay employment taxes if you pay someone to provide care in your home
- Use Form W-10 to get provider's identification information
