# Publication 505 - Tax Withholding and Estimated Tax

## Summary

IRS Publication 505 provides comprehensive guidance on the pay-as-you-go federal income tax system. It covers two methods for paying taxes throughout the year: **withholding** (from wages, pensions, and other income) and **estimated tax payments** (for income not subject to withholding).

## Key Concepts

### Pay-As-You-Go System
Federal income tax must be paid as income is earned or received. There are two payment methods:
1. **Withholding** - Tax automatically withheld from wages, pensions, bonuses, commissions, gambling winnings
2. **Estimated Tax** - Quarterly payments for self-employment, interest, dividends, rents, royalties

### Who Must Pay Estimated Tax
Generally required if you expect to owe **$1,000 or more** at tax time and:
- Have self-employment income
- Receive interest, dividends, alimony, or rental income
- Have capital gains
- Have income not subject to withholding

- Maximum: $17,280

## Tax Withholding (Chapter 1)

### Form W-4 - Employee's Withholding Certificate
Four-step process for determining withholding:
- **Step 1:** Personal information and filing status
- **Step 2:** Multiple jobs or working spouse
- **Step 3:** Dependents and tax credits
- **Step 4:** Other adjustments (deductions, other income, additional withholding)

### Form W-4P - Pension/Annuity Withholding
For retirees receiving pension or annuity payments. Similar four-step process as Form W-4.

### Special Withholding Situations
- **Part-Year Method:** Available if employed 245 days or less
- **Cumulative Wage Method:** Adjusts for mid-year withholding changes
- **Exemption from Withholding:** Available if no tax liability expected

### Supplemental Wages
Bonuses, commissions, and overtime may be withheld at:
- **22% flat rate** if paid separately from regular wages
- **Aggregate method** if combined with regular wages

### Tips
- Report tips of $20+ monthly to employer
- Employer withholds based on reported tips

## Estimated Tax (Chapter 2)

### 2025 Quarterly Due Dates
| Period | Due Date |
|--------|----------|
| Jan 1 - Mar 31 | April 15, 2025 |
| Apr 1 - May 31 | June 16, 2025 |
| Jun 1 - Aug 31 | September 15, 2025 |
| Sep 1 - Dec 31 | January 15, 2026 |

### Safe Harbor Rules
Avoid underpayment penalty by paying the **lesser of:**
1. **90%** of current year's tax liability, OR
2. **100%** of prior year's tax (110% if AGI > $150,000)

### Farmers and Fishermen
- Special rules: Only one payment due January 15
- Lower safe harbor: 66.67% of current year tax

## Additional Taxes

### Additional Medicare Tax
- **Rate:** 0.9% on wages/SE income over threshold
- **Thresholds:** $200,000 (Single/HOH/QSS), $250,000 (MFJ), $125,000 (MFS)

### Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)
- **Rate:** 3.8% on lesser of:
  - Net investment income, OR
  - MAGI over threshold ($200,000 Single, $250,000 MFJ, $125,000 MFS)

## Credits Affecting Withholding

### Child Tax Credit
- Up to $2,000 per qualifying child
- Enter in Step 3 of Form W-4

### Earned Income Credit
- Refundable credit for low-income workers
- Can receive advance payments through employer

### Premium Tax Credit
- For health insurance through Marketplace
- Advance payments affect refund/balance due

## Penalty for Underpayment

### Penalty Triggers
Owe penalty if:
- Required payments < 90% of current year tax, AND
- Required payments < 100%/110% of prior year tax

### Penalty Exceptions
- Tax due < $1,000
- Casualty, disaster, or unusual circumstance
- Retired after age 62 or became disabled
- Reasonable cause (not willful neglect)
