# Pub 939 - General Rule for Pensions and Annuities

## Summary

Publication 939 explains how to determine the tax treatment of pension and annuity income under the **General Rule**. The General Rule is used to figure the tax-free part of each annuity payment based on the ratio of your investment in the contract to the total expected return.

## Who Must Use the General Rule

Use this publication if you receive pension or annuity payments from:

1. **Nonqualified plans** (private annuities, purchased commercial annuities, nonqualified employee plans)
2. **Qualified plans** if:
   - Annuity starting date is before November 19, 1996 (and after July 1, 1986), and you don't qualify for or didn't choose the Simplified Method
   - Annuity starting date is after November 18, 1996, and you are age 75 or over with payments guaranteed for at least 5 years

## Key Concepts

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| [[Investment in the Contract]] | Your net cost in the contract, adjusted for refund features |
| [[Expected Return]] | Total amount you can expect to receive under the contract |
| [[Exclusion Percentage]] | Investment in contract divided by expected return |
| [[Annuity Starting Date]] | The date used to figure investment and expected return |

## Types of Annuities Covered

- **Fixed period annuities** - Payments for a specified length of time
- **Single-life annuities** - Payments for life, ending at death
- **Joint and survivor annuities** - Payments for life, then to survivor
- [[Variable Annuities]] - Payments that may vary in amount
- **Disability pensions** - Payments due to disability retirement

## Computation Steps

1. Figure your [[Investment in the Contract]] (including adjustments for refund feature and death benefit exclusion)
2. Figure your [[Expected Return]]
3. Divide Step 1 by Step 2 to get the [[Exclusion Percentage]]
4. Multiply exclusion percentage by the first regular payment to get the tax-free part
5. Multiply tax-free part by number of payments received during the year
6. Subtract tax-free part from total payment to get taxable amount

## Refund Feature

A [[Refund Feature]] exists when:
- Expected return depends on life of one or more individuals
- Contract provides payments to beneficiary if specified amount hasn't been paid before death
- Payments are a refund of amount paid for the contract

You must reduce your net cost by the value of the refund feature (using Tables III or VII).

## Exclusion Limits

- **After 1986**: Total tax-free amount cannot exceed your net cost
- Any unrecovered net cost at death is allowed as an other itemized deduction on the final return
