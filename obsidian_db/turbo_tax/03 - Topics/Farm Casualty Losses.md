# Farm Casualty Losses

## Overview

Farmers may deduct losses from casualties, thefts, and condemnations. A casualty is damage or destruction of property from a sudden, unexpected event. Special rules apply to farm property and federally declared disasters.

## What Qualifies as a Casualty

### Deductible Casualties
- Fires, storms, floods, earthquakes
- Lightning, tornadoes, hurricanes
- Car accidents (not willful negligence)
- Vandalism, terrorist attacks
- Government-ordered demolition due to disaster

### Nondeductible Losses
- Progressive deterioration (rust, termites)
- Damage by family pet
- Accidental breakage under normal conditions
- Willfully set fires

## Figuring Farm Casualty Loss

### Personal-Use Property
For tax years after 2017, personal casualty losses are deductible only if attributable to a **federally declared disaster**.

### Business Property
Farm business property casualty losses are fully deductible:
- **Complete destruction**: Adjusted basis minus salvage value minus insurance
- **Partial damage**: Lesser of basis or decrease in FMV, minus insurance

## Reporting Requirements

### Timing
- Deduct loss in year casualty occurs or theft discovered
- Special rules if reimbursement claim pending

### Forms
- Farm business property: Form 4797 or Schedule F
- Personal-use property: Schedule A (if from federal disaster)

## Federally Declared Disasters

### Enhanced Benefits
- No $100 per casualty reduction
- No 10% AGI floor for personal losses
- Extended replacement period for involuntary conversions

### Extended Deadlines
- Mandatory 120-day extension for certain tax deadlines (disasters after July 24, 2025)
- Additional time to file and pay

## Involuntary Conversions

### Postponing Gain
If property is destroyed, stolen, or condemned:
- May postpone gain by purchasing replacement property
- Replacement period: 2 years (4 years for federal disasters)
- Must be similar or related in service or use

### Livestock Sales Due to Weather
Special rules for weather-related livestock sales:
- Postpone gain under Section 451(g) or Section 1033(e)
- May use income in next year
