# Federal Poverty Line

## Overview

The Federal Poverty Line (FPL) is a measure of income issued annually by the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) that determines eligibility for various federal programs, including the Premium Tax Credit. Your household income as a percentage of the FPL determines your PTC eligibility and contribution amounts.

## Why It Matters for PTC

The FPL is used to determine:

1. **PTC Eligibility**: Generally need household income at least 100% of FPL (exceptions exist)
2. **Contribution Percentage**: How much of your income goes toward premiums
3. **Repayment Limitations**: Caps on excess APTC you must repay
4. **Cost-Sharing Reductions**: Eligibility for reduced deductibles and copays (Silver plans)

## Determining Your FPL Percentage

### Calculation

```
FPL Percentage = (Household Income ÷ FPL for Family Size) × 100
```

### Example

A family of 4 with household income of $75,000:
- 2025 FPL for family of 4: $31,200
- Percentage: $75,000 ÷ $31,200 = 2.404 = 240% of FPL

## Impact on PTC Contribution Rates

Your FPL percentage determines your required contribution:

| FPL Range | Required Contribution |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Up to 150% | 0% - 4.0% of income |
| 150% - 200% | 4.0% - 6.3% of income |
| 200% - 250% | 6.3% - 8.1% of income |
| 250% - 300% | 8.1% - 9.1% of income |
| 300% - 400% | 9.1% - 9.8% of income |
| Over 400% | No cap (may still qualify for 2025) |

## Special Rules for 2025

### Income Above 400% FPL

For tax year 2025, taxpayers with household income exceeding 400% of FPL **may still be allowed a PTC**. This is a temporary expansion of eligibility.

### Income Below 100% FPL

If household income is below 100% FPL, you may still qualify for PTC if:
1. You were enrolled in a Marketplace plan
2. You received APTC during the year
3. Your actual income is different from what you projected

**Exception for Intentional Misrepresentation**: The exception for estimated household income does NOT apply if you provided incorrect information to the Marketplace with intentional or reckless disregard for the facts.

## Family Size Determination

Your family size equals the number of individuals in your tax family:
- You (if filing a return and not a dependent)
- Your spouse (if filing jointly and not a dependent)
- Your dependents claimed on your return

**Note for Form 1040-NR filers**: Include dependents only if you're a U.S. national, resident of Canada/Mexico/South Korea, or resident of India who was a student or business apprentice.

## Repayment Limitations by FPL

If your APTC exceeds your PTC, repayment is capped based on FPL percentage:

| FPL Range | Single | Other Filing Statuses |
|-----------|--------|----------------------|
| Under 200% | $375 | $750 |
| 200% - 300% | $975 | $1,950 |
| 300% - 400% | $1,625 | $3,250 |
| Over 400% | Full amount | Full amount |

**Exception**: Individuals not lawfully present in the U.S. are not eligible for repayment limitations.
