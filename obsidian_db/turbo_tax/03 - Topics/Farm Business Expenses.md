# Farm Business Expenses

## Overview

Farm business expenses are ordinary and necessary costs incurred in operating a farming business. These expenses are deductible against farm income on Schedule F (Form 1040). Expenses must be paid (cash method) or incurred (accrual method) during the tax year.

## Common Deductible Expenses

### Operating Expenses
- Feed, seed, fertilizer, and chemicals
- Livestock purchased for resale
- Repairs and maintenance
- Veterinary fees and medicine
- Fuel and lubricants
- Insurance premiums
- Rent paid for farmland or equipment
- Utilities and telephone (business portion)

### Labor Costs
- Wages paid to farm workers
- Employer's share of employment taxes
- Employee benefits
- See [[Farm Employment Taxes]] for withholding requirements

### Professional Services
- Accounting and legal fees
- Consultant fees
- Farm management services

### Travel and Transportation
- Standard mileage rate: 70 cents per mile (2025)
- Actual expense method also available
- Business use percentage required
- See [[Vehicle and Travel Expenses]] for details

## Special Expense Rules

### Prepaid Farm Supplies
Limited to 50% of other deductible farm expenses. Includes feed, seed, fertilizer, and similar supplies paid in advance.

### Soil and Water Conservation
Deductible up to 25% of gross farm income. Excess carries forward. See [[Soil and Water Conservation Deduction]].

### Depreciation
Recover cost of assets over time. See [[Depreciation for Farmers]] and [[Section 179 Deduction]].

### Business Use of Home
Deductible if used regularly and exclusively for farm business administration. See [[Home Office Deduction]].

## Non-Deductible Expenses

- Personal living expenses
- Fines and penalties
- Lobbying expenses
- Club membership dues
- Capital expenses (must depreciate)
