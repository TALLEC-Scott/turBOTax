# Agricultural Program Payments

## Overview

Most government payments to farmers are taxable income. This includes payments from agricultural programs, conservation programs, disaster assistance, and commodity programs. Report these payments on Schedule F (Form 1040).

## Types of Taxable Payments

### Commodity Programs
- Price Loss Coverage (PLC) payments
- Agricultural Risk Coverage (ARC) payments
- Marketing loan gains
- Certificate exchange gains

### Conservation Programs
- Conservation Reserve Program (CRP) payments
- Environmental Quality Incentives Program (EQIP)
- Conservation Stewardship Program (CSP)

### Disaster Assistance
- Livestock Indemnity Program (LIP) payments
- Livestock Forage Program (LFP) payments
- Emergency Assistance for Livestock, Honeybeles, and Farm-Raised Fish (ELAP)
- Tree Assistance Program (TAP)
- Wildfire and Hurricane Indemnity Program (WHIP)

## Cost-Sharing Exclusion

### What's Excludable
Payments under certain conservation programs may be excluded from income if:
- Payment is for conservation practice
- Program is approved by Secretary of Agriculture
- Payment does not exceed actual cost

### Basis Reduction
If exclusion is claimed, reduce basis of affected property by amount excluded.

## Market Assistance Loans (MALs)

### Tax Treatment Options
1. **Loan Proceeds**: Report as income when forfeited or repaid
2. **Election to Report as Income**: Include in income when received

### Market Gain
- Difference between loan rate and repayment rate
- Taxable unless MAL proceeds were already included in income
- Report on Schedule F

## Reporting Requirements

### Where to Report
- Schedule F, Part I for most payments
- May need separate reporting for different programs

### Form 1099
- Form 1099-G for government payments
- Form 1099-MISC for other program payments

## CRP Payments

### Self-Employment Tax
- CRP payments generally subject to self-employment tax
- Exception for certain individuals receiving Social Security benefits

### Rental Arrangements
- CRP payments treated as rental income if not materially participating
- Subject to passive activity rules
