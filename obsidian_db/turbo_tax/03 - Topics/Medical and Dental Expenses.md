# Medical and Dental Expenses

## Overview

Medical and dental expenses are costs for the diagnosis, cure, mitigation, treatment, or prevention of disease, and for affecting any structure or function of the body. These expenses can be deducted as [[Itemized Deductions]] on [[Schedule A (Form 1040)|Schedule A]] if they exceed 7.5% of your adjusted gross income (AGI).

## Key Rules

### AGI Floor

Only medical expenses exceeding **7.5% of AGI** are deductible. This threshold applies to the total of all medical expenses, not each expense individually.

**Example:** If your AGI is $60,000, only expenses exceeding $4,500 (7.5% × $60,000) are deductible.

### Definition of Medical Care

Expenses must be primarily to alleviate or prevent a physical or mental disability or illness. Expenses that are merely beneficial to general health (vitamins, vacations) do not qualify.

### Whose Expenses Qualify

You can include expenses paid for:
- **Yourself** - Any qualifying medical expense you pay
- **Spouse** - If married when services provided or when paid
- **Dependents** - If a qualifying child or qualifying relative when services provided or when paid
  - See [[Qualifying Child]] and [[Qualifying Relative]] for definitions

### Timing of Payment

- Include expenses in the year paid (not when services received)
- Credit card charges count when charged, not when paid
- Check payments count when mailed or delivered

## Types of Deductible Expenses

### Professional Services
- Physicians, surgeons, specialists
- Dentists and orthodontists
- Psychiatrists and psychologists
- Chiropractors and osteopaths
- Optometrists

### Treatment and Procedures
- Hospital care (includes meals and lodging)
- Nursing home care (if primarily for medical care)
- Surgery (non-cosmetic)
- Psychiatric care
- Drug and alcohol addiction treatment

### Medical Equipment and Supplies
- Artificial limbs and teeth
- Crutches and wheelchairs
- Hearing aids and batteries
- Eyeglasses and contact lenses
- Diagnostic devices (blood sugar monitors)
- Bandages and medical supplies

### Insurance
- Health insurance premiums
- Medicare Part B and Part D premiums
- Long-term care insurance (subject to limits)
- See [[Health Insurance Premiums]] for detailed rules

### Transportation
- Ambulance service
- Standard mileage rate: 21 cents/mile (2025)
- Actual out-of-pocket costs (gas, oil)
- Parking fees and tolls
- Public transportation fares

### Home Modifications
- See [[Capital Medical Expenses]] for rules on home improvements for medical purposes

## Expenses NOT Deductible

| Expense | Reason |
|---------|--------|
| Cosmetic surgery | Not medically necessary (exceptions apply) |
| Health club dues | General health improvement |
| Nonprescription drugs | Must be prescribed (insulin excepted) |
| Funeral expenses | Not medical care |
| Maternity clothes | Personal expense |
| Teeth whitening | Cosmetic |
| Weight-loss for general health | Must treat specific disease |
| Vitamins and supplements | General health unless prescribed |

## Calculating the Deduction

1. **Total all qualifying medical expenses** paid during the tax year
2. **Calculate 7.5% of your AGI**
3. **Subtract** the AGI threshold from total expenses
4. **Report** the remainder on [[Schedule A (Form 1040)|Schedule A]], line 1

### Worksheet Example

| Step | Calculation |
|------|-------------|
| 1. Total medical expenses | $12,000 |
| 2. AGI | $80,000 |
| 3. 7.5% of AGI | $6,000 |
| 4. Deductible amount | $6,000 |

## Reimbursements

Reduce your total medical expenses by any reimbursements received:
- Insurance payments
- Medicare payments
- HSA distributions
- Employer-provided reimbursements

If you deducted medical expenses in a prior year and receive reimbursement later, you may need to include the reimbursement in income.
