# Canceled Debt

## Overview

When a debt you owe is canceled or forgiven (other than as a gift or bequest), you generally must include the canceled amount in your income. However, several important exceptions may allow you to exclude canceled debt from income.

## General Rule

Canceled debt is taxable income in the year it is forgiven. The amount is reported to you on Form 1099-C.

### Reporting

| Debt Type | Where to Report |
|-----------|----------------|
| Nonbusiness debt | Schedule 1 (Form 1040), line 8c |
| Business debt | Schedule C (Form 1040) |
| Farm debt | Schedule F (Form 1040) |

## Form 1099-C

Issued by federal agencies, financial institutions, and credit unions when debt of $600 or more is canceled.

- **Box 2**: Amount of debt discharged
- **Box 3**: Interest amount (if included in box 2)

### Interest Portion

- **Deductible interest** (e.g., business loan): Report net amount (box 2 minus box 3)
- **Nondeductible interest** (e.g., personal loan): Report full amount from box 2

## Exceptions to Inclusion

### Student Loan Cancellation

**Work-Related Exclusion:**
Tax-free if loan is canceled because you work:
- In certain professions
- For a certain period
- For any of a broad class of employers

**Special Rule for 2021-2025:**
American Rescue Plan Act makes certain student loan discharges tax-free:
- Loans for postsecondary educational expenses
- Private education loans
- Loans from educational organizations
- Refinancing loans from tax-exempt organizations

**Student Loan Repayment Assistance:**
Tax-free payments from:
- National Health Service Corps (NHSC) Loan Repayment Program
- State education loan repayment programs under Public Health Service Act
- State programs for health services in underserved areas

### Deductible Debt

No income from cancellation if payment of debt would have been deductible. Applies only to cash-method taxpayers.

### Price Reduction After Purchase

If seller reduces debt owed for property purchased, treat as purchase price adjustment reducing your basis.

### Gift or Bequest

Cancellation intended as a gift is not income.

## Exclusions from Gross Income

### Bankruptcy

Debt canceled in a Title 11 bankruptcy case is excluded from income. See [[Pub 908 - Bankruptcy Tax Guide]].

### Insolvency

Debt canceled when you're insolvent is excluded up to the amount of insolvency. Insolvency means liabilities exceed FMV of assets.

### Qualified Farm Debt

Debt canceled by qualified person if at least 50% of gross income from farming in past 3 years. See [[Pub 225 - Farmer's Tax Guide]].

### Qualified Real Property Business Debt

Exclusion for certain business real property debt. See [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]].

### Qualified Principal Residence Indebtedness (QPRI)

Debt secured by principal residence used to buy, build, or substantially improve the home. Limited exclusion amounts apply.

## Special Situations

### Discounted Mortgage Payoff

If lender offers discount for early mortgage payoff, the discount is canceled debt income.

### Mortgage Relief Upon Sale/Foreclosure

**Recourse Debt:**
- Gain or loss up to property FMV
- Mortgage discharge exceeding FMV is canceled debt income (unless excluded)

**Nonrecourse Debt:**
- Entire relief included in amount realized
- May have taxable gain if exceeds basis
- Not treated as canceled debt

### Stockholder Debt

**Corporation cancels debt you owe:**
- Constructive dividend income

**You cancel debt corporation owes you:**
- Generally no income
- Treated as capital contribution

## Recourse vs. Nonrecourse Debt

| Recourse Debt | Nonrecourse Debt |
|---------------|------------------|
| Personally liable | Not personally liable |
| May have COD income on foreclosure | No COD income on foreclosure |
| Report gain up to FMV | Entire amount relief is proceeds |
| Excess relief = COD income | Compare to basis for gain/loss |

## Reporting Requirements

### Form 982

Use Form 982 to report:
- Exclusion for bankruptcy
- Exclusion for insolvency
- Exclusion for qualified farm debt
- Exclusion for QPRI
- Reduction of tax attributes

### Claim for Refund

If you included canceled debt in income and later pay the debt, you may file Form 1040-X for refund within the statute of limitations (generally 3 years from return due date).

## Common Questions

### Q: I received Form 1099-C but the debt was canceled as a gift. Is it taxable?
**A:** No, if the cancellation was intended as a gift, it's not taxable income. Keep documentation.

### Q: How do I know if I was insolvent?
**A:** Compare your total liabilities to the FMV of your assets immediately before the debt cancellation. If liabilities exceeded assets, you were insolvent.

### Q: What if I disagree with Form 1099-C?
**A:** Contact the creditor to correct the form. If they refuse, attach an explanation to your return with the correct amount.

### Q: Is credit card debt forgiveness taxable?
**A:** Yes, unless you qualify for an exception (bankruptcy, insolvency, etc.).
