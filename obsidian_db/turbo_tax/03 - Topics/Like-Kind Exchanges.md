# Like-Kind Exchanges

## Overview

A like-kind exchange under Section 1031 allows you to exchange real property held for business or investment purposes for other real property of like kind without recognizing gain or loss. This is a nontaxable exchange.

## What Qualifies as Like-Kind

### Real Property Only
After the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017, **only real property** qualifies for like-kind exchange treatment. Personal property no longer qualifies.

### Like-Kind Definition
Real property is "like-kind" to other real property regardless of differences in:
- Grade or quality
- Improvement status (improved vs. unimproved)
- Specific use

**Examples of qualifying exchanges:**
- Apartment building for office building
- Land for rental house
- Retail space for warehouse

**Not like-kind:**
- Real property in the U.S. for real property outside the U.S.
- Property held primarily for sale (dealer property)

## Holding Requirement

Both properties must be held for:
- Business use, OR
- Investment purposes

**Does NOT qualify:**
- Property held primarily for sale (inventory)
- Property used as your personal residence

## Basis of Property Received

### General Rule
Basis of property received = Basis of property given up (carryover basis)

### Adjustments to Basis

| Adjustment | Effect on Basis |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Plus: Gain recognized | Increase basis |
| Plus: Additional money paid | Increase basis |
| Plus: Liabilities assumed | Increase basis |
| Minus: Money received | Decrease basis |
| Minus: Liabilities relieved | Decrease basis |
| Minus: Loss recognized | Decrease basis |

## Exchange Expenses

Exchange expenses are added to the basis of the replacement property:
- Brokerage commissions
- Attorney fees
- Deed preparation costs
- Title insurance
- Escrow fees
- Qualified intermediary fees

## Boot (Money or Non-Like-Kind Property)

If you receive boot, you must recognize gain (but not loss) to the extent of the boot received.

### Types of Boot
- Cash received
- Non-like-kind property
- Net relief from liabilities (liabilities assumed by other party minus liabilities you assume)

### Example

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Adjusted basis of property given up | $60,000 |
| Cash received | $10,000 |
| Gain recognized | $2,000 |

**Basis of new property:**
| | Amount |
|---|--------|
| Adjusted basis old property | $60,000 |
| Minus: Cash received | ($10,000) |
| Plus: Gain recognized | $2,000 |
| **Basis of new property** | **$52,000** |

## Multiple Properties Received

If you receive multiple properties in a like-kind exchange:

1. **Allocate total basis** among properties based on their respective FMVs
2. **Allocate excess basis** (if any) proportionally

## Partially Nontaxable Exchanges

If the exchange involves both like-kind and non-like-kind property:

### If Gain
- Recognize gain to extent of non-like-kind property received
- Carryover basis for like-kind property received

### If Loss
- Recognize loss on non-like-kind portion
- Adjust basis of like-kind property

## Reporting Requirements

- Report the exchange on Form 8824
- File for the year the exchange is completed
- May need to report in multiple years for deferred exchanges
