# Marketplace (Health Insurance)

## Overview

The Health Insurance Marketplace (also called the "Exchange") is a service that helps people shop for and enroll in health insurance. The Marketplace is established under the Affordable Care Act and is where you can purchase Qualified Health Plans eligible for Premium Tax Credit subsidies.

## Types of Marketplaces

### Federal Marketplace
- Operated by the federal government
- Website: HealthCare.gov
- Available in states without their own marketplace

### State-Based Marketplaces
- Operated by individual states
- Each has its own website and enrollment process
- Examples: Covered California, NY State of Health, Washington Healthplanfinder

### State-Partnership Marketplaces
- Jointly operated by state and federal government
- Uses HealthCare.gov platform with state oversight

## What the Marketplace Does

### Enrollment Services
- Compare qualified health plans side-by-side
- Enroll in coverage during Open Enrollment or Special Enrollment
- Renew existing coverage

### Financial Assistance
- Determine eligibility for Premium Tax Credit (PTC)
- Estimate and authorize Advance Payments of PTC (APTC)
- Determine eligibility for Cost-Sharing Reductions
- Check eligibility for Medicaid and CHIP

### Eligibility Determinations
- Verify citizenship/lawful presence
- Confirm income projections
- Assess eligibility for exemptions

## Forms and Documentation

### Form 1095-A
The Marketplace provides Form 1095-A (Health Insurance Marketplace Statement) by early February each year. This form shows:
- Your enrollment premiums (Column A)
- Your applicable SLCSP premium (Column B)
- APTC paid on your behalf (Column C)

**What to do if Form 1095-A is missing or incorrect**:
- Contact your Marketplace immediately
- You don't need a corrected form to file taxes - you can determine correct SLCSP using tools

### Written Notices
For QSEHRA recipients, employers provide written notices showing permitted benefit amounts, which must be used when calculating PTC.

## Reporting Changes in Circumstances

Report these changes to the Marketplace promptly to adjust your APTC:

| Change | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Income changes | Affects PTC amount |
| Family size changes | Affects poverty line percentage |
| Address change | May affect available plans and SLCSP |
| Employment change | May affect employer coverage eligibility |
| Marriage/divorce | Affects filing status and household |
| Birth/adoption | Affects family size and coverage needs |
| Gaining/losing other coverage | Affects PTC eligibility |

## Open Enrollment Period

- Typically November 1 through January 15 (varies by state)
- This is when anyone can enroll or change plans
- Coverage begins January 1 if enrolled by mid-December

## Special Enrollment Periods

Available outside Open Enrollment for qualifying life events:

- Loss of minimum essential coverage
- Marriage
- Birth, adoption, or foster care placement
- Move to a new coverage area
- Income change affecting subsidy eligibility
- Gaining status as member of federally recognized tribe

## Marketplace Appeals

If you disagree with a Marketplace eligibility determination:

1. Request an appeal within 90 days of the determination
2. Provide documentation supporting your position
3. If successful, you may be able to retroactively enroll in a plan

**Note**: If you become eligible for APTC through a successful appeal and retroactively enroll, you must pay your share of premiums within 120 days of the appeals decision.
