# Form W-4P - Withholding Certificate for Pension or Annuity Payments

## Overview

Form W-4P is used by retirees and beneficiaries to instruct pension payers, annuity providers, and certain other plan administrators on how much federal income tax to withhold from periodic payments.

## Who Uses Form W-4P

### Applicable Payments
- Pensions (including military retirement)
- Annuities
- Certain periodic distributions from deferred compensation plans
- Profit-sharing plans
- IRAs (traditional, SEP, SIMPLE)

### NOT Used For
- Wages from employment (use [[Form W-4]])
- Nonperiodic payments (use Form W-4R)
- Eligible rollover distributions (use Form W-4R)
- Social Security benefits (use Form W-4V)

## Four Steps of Form W-4P

### Step 1: Personal Information
- Enter name, address, SSN
- Select anticipated **filing status**
- Determines standard deduction and tax rates for withholding

### Step 2: Multiple Income Sources
Complete if you:
1. Have income from a job or multiple pensions/annuities, OR
2. Are married filing jointly and spouse has income from job or pension

**Important:**
- If you have a job, complete Steps 3-4(b) on Form W-4 (not W-4P)
- If no job, complete Steps 3-4(b) on W-4P for highest-paying pension only

### Step 3: Dependents and Credits
- Calculate [[Child Tax Credit]] for qualifying children
- Calculate [[Credit for Other Dependents]]
- Include other tax credits:
  - Education credits
  - [[Foreign Tax Credit]]
  - [[Retirement Savings Contributions Credit]]

### Step 4: Other Adjustments
- **4(a):** Other income (not from pensions)
- **4(b):** Deductions (itemized or above-the-line)
- **4(c):** Extra withholding per payment

## Default Withholding Rules

### If No Form W-4P Submitted
Payer withholds as if:
- Filing status: **Single**
- No adjustments in Steps 2-4
- Standard deduction only

### Exceptions
- For payments that began before 2025: Current election remains in effect
- Must submit new form to change existing withholding

## Determining Withholding Amount

### Percentage Method
Payers may offer percentage withholding options:
- Common options: 0%, 10%, 15%, 20%, 25%, 30%, 35%
- Some payers allow specific dollar amounts

### Withholding Tables
Payers use IRS withholding tables based on:
- Payment amount
- Payment frequency
- Filing status
- Adjustments on Form W-4P

## When to Submit Form W-4P

### Initial Enrollment
- Submit when payments begin
- Provides withholding instructions to payer

### Changes in Circumstances
Submit new form when:
- Marital status changes
- Spouse starts/stops working
- Income from other sources changes significantly
- Deductions or credits change
- Want to adjust withholding amount

## Coordination with Form W-4

### If Also Employed
When receiving pension AND working:
- Submit Form W-4 to employer
- Submit Form W-4P to pension payer
- Coordinate withholding across both income sources

### Example: Combined Income
- Receiving $30,000 annual pension
- Working part-time earning $25,000
- Complete Steps 3-4(b) on Form W-4 (for employment)
- Leave Steps 3-4(b) blank on Form W-4P
- Or adjust both forms to split withholding

## Special Situations

### Military Retirees
- Military retirement pay uses Form W-4 (not W-4P)
- Withholding rules same as regular wages
- Treated as pension for other tax purposes

### Nonperiodic Payments
For lump-sum or irregular distributions:
- Use Form W-4R (not W-4P)
- Default withholding: 10%
- Can elect higher rate or to not withhold

### Eligible Rollover Distributions
- Use Form W-4R
- Default withholding: 20%
- Can elect higher rate

### Periodic vs Nonperiodic

| Payment Type | Form | Default Rate |
|--------------|------|--------------|
| Regular pension payments | W-4P | Based on tables |
| Lump-sum distributions | W-4R | 10% |
| Rollover distributions | W-4R | 20% |

## Estimated Tax Considerations

### May Need Estimated Payments If:
- Pension withholding insufficient
- Multiple income sources
- Other taxable income (investments, rental, etc.)

### Coordination Strategy
1. Calculate total expected tax liability
2. Determine pension withholding amount
3. Calculate additional withholding or estimated payments needed
4. Adjust Form W-4P or make quarterly payments

## Penalties for Insufficient Withholding

### Underpayment Penalty
May owe penalty if:
- Withholding + estimated payments < 90% of current year tax
- Withholding + estimated payments < 100%/110% of prior year tax

See [[Underpayment Penalty]] for details.

## State Tax Withholding

### State Requirements Vary
- Some states conform to federal Form W-4P
- Some states have separate withholding forms
- Some states do not tax pension income
- Check state-specific requirements

## Amending Withholding

### Anytime During Year
- Can submit new Form W-4P at any time
- Payer implements by first payment period ending 30+ days after receipt
- Review withholding annually

### Year-End Planning
- Review withholding before year-end
- Adjust if under/over withheld
- Cannot adjust prior-year withholding
