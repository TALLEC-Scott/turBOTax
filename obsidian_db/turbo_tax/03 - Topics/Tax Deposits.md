# Tax Deposits

## Overview

Employers must deposit federal income tax withheld and both employer and employee shares of social security and Medicare taxes. These are trust fund taxes that must be deposited on schedule.

## Deposit Methods

### Electronic Deposit Required (EFT)
All federal tax deposits must be made electronically using:

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| EFTPS | Electronic Federal Tax Payment System (free) |
| IRS Direct Pay | Free, no enrollment required |
| Business Tax Account | IRS.gov/BusinessAccount |
| Same-day wire | Through financial institution |
| Third-party payer | Payroll service, reporting agent |

### EFTPS Benefits
- Free service from U.S. Treasury
- Schedule payments up to 365 days in advance
- View payment history
- Receive immediate confirmation

## Deposit Schedules

Two deposit schedules determine when deposits are due:

### Monthly Schedule Depositor
- **Criteria:** Reported $50,000 or less in taxes during lookback period
- **Due date:** 15th of the following month
- Example: March taxes due April 15

### Semiweekly Schedule Depositor
- **Criteria:** Reported more than $50,000 in taxes during lookback period
- **Due date:** Based on payday

| Payday Falls On | Deposit Due By |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Wednesday, Thursday, Friday | Following Wednesday |
| Saturday, Sunday, Monday, Tuesday | Following Friday |

## Lookback Period

### For Form 941 Filers
Lookback period is the 4 quarters ending June 30:

| Tax Year | Lookback Period |
|----------|-----------------|
| 2026 | July 1, 2024 - June 30, 2025 |
| 2027 | July 1, 2025 - June 30, 2026 |

### For Form 943/944/945 Filers
Lookback period is the second preceding calendar year:

| Tax Year | Lookback Period |
|----------|-----------------|
| 2026 | Calendar year 2024 |
| 2027 | Calendar year 2025 |

## $100,000 Next-Day Deposit Rule

If you accumulate **$100,000 or more** in taxes on any day:
1. Deposit by the **next business day**
2. You become a **semiweekly depositor** for rest of year and next year

### How to Calculate
- Accumulate taxes for each deposit period
- Reset to zero after each deposit period
- Don't combine across periods

### Example
- Monday: $110,000 accumulated → Deposit Tuesday
- Tuesday: $30,000 accumulated → Deposit by Friday (semiweekly schedule)

## Payment with Return

You may pay with your return instead of depositing if:

| Return | Condition |
|--------|-----------|
| Form 941 | Quarterly liability less than $2,500 |
| Form 943 | Annual liability less than $2,500 |
| Form 944 | Annual liability less than $2,500 |
| Form 945 | Annual liability less than $2,500 |

**Note:** Monthly schedule depositors may also pay shortfall with return.

## Accuracy of Deposits Rule

Penalties are waived if:
1. Deposit shortfall is ≤ the greater of $100 or 2%
2. Shortfall is paid by makeup date:
   - Monthly depositor: Due date of return
   - Semiweekly depositor: By first Wednesday or Friday after shortfall

## Separate Deposit Requirements

### Don't Combine
- Form 941/943/944 taxes with Form 945 taxes
- Farm (Form 943) and non-farm (Form 941) wages

### Do Make Separate Deposits
- Use separate transactions for each type
- Deposit on the same day if both are due

## FUTA Tax Deposits

- Different rules apply (see [[Federal Unemployment Tax (FUTA)]])
- Deposit only if undeposited amount exceeds $500
- Deposit by end of month following quarter

## Business Day Rules

### Legal Holidays Extend Deadlines
If due date falls on Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday:
- Deposit is timely if made next business day

### Semiweekly Depositors
- Have at least 3 business days after deposit period ends
- Each legal holiday adds one day

### 2026 Legal Holidays (D.C.)
- January 1, January 19, February 16
- April 16 (D.C. Emancipation Day)
- May 25, June 19, July 3 (observed)
- September 7, October 12, November 11, November 26, December 25

## Trust Fund Recovery Penalty

If trust fund taxes (income tax withheld + employee FICA) aren't deposited:
- **Responsible persons** may be personally liable
- Penalty equals 100% of unpaid trust fund taxes
- Can apply to multiple individuals
