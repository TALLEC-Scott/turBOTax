# Credit for Other Dependents

The Credit for Other Dependents (ODC) is a nonrefundable credit for dependents who do not qualify for the Child Tax Credit.

## Overview

- **Amount:** $500 per qualifying dependent
- **Nonrefundable:** Can only reduce tax to zero
- **Phaseout:** Begins at $200,000 ($400,000 MFJ)

## 2025 Changes

Beginning in 2025, to claim the credit for other dependents:
- You (or your spouse if filing jointly) must have a valid SSN or ITIN
- Must be issued on or before the due date of your return (including extensions)

## Qualifying Person

A qualifying person for ODC must be:

### Qualifying Relative
- Your qualifying relative (as defined in [[Dependents]])
- Must be a U.S. citizen, national, or resident alien

### Qualifying Child Who Doesn't Meet CTC Age Test
- A qualifying child who is 17 or older
- Meets all other qualifying child tests except age

## Who Cannot Be Claimed for ODC

- The taxpayer or spouse
- A dependent who qualifies for the Child Tax Credit
- A nonresident alien
- A person who is not a U.S. citizen, national, or resident alien

## Phaseout Calculation

The credit is reduced by $50 for each $1,000 (or fraction thereof) that your MAGI exceeds the threshold:

| Filing Status | Phaseout Begins | Credit Eliminated At |
|---------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Single | $200,000 | $212,000 |
| MFJ | $400,000 | $412,000 |
| MFS | $200,000 | $212,000 |
| HOH | $200,000 | $212,000 |

### Example
A single taxpayer with MAGI of $205,000 and one qualifying dependent:
- Credit before phaseout: $500
- Reduction: ($205,000 - $200,000) / $1,000 × $50 = $250
- **Credit allowed: $250**

## Claiming the Credit

1. List the dependent in the Dependents section of Form 1040
2. Check the appropriate box for credit for other dependents
3. The credit is calculated automatically on your return

## Relationship to Other Credits

A dependent can qualify for only one of the following:
- [[Child Tax Credit]] - For children under 17
- Credit for Other Dependents - For other dependents

If a dependent qualifies for both, you must claim the Child Tax Credit.
