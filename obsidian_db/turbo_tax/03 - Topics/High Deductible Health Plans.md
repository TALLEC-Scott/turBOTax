# High Deductible Health Plans (HDHPs)

## Overview

A High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) is a health insurance plan with higher annual deductibles than typical health plans and a maximum limit on out-of-pocket expenses. HDHP coverage is required to establish and contribute to a [[Health Savings Accounts|Health Savings Account (HSA)]] or Archer MSA. See [[Pub 969 - Health Savings Accounts and Other Tax-Favored Health Plans]].

## HDHP Requirements

An HDHP must have:
1. A higher annual deductible than typical health plans
2. A maximum limit on the sum of annual deductible and out-of-pocket medical expenses

Out-of-pocket expenses include copayments and other amounts, but NOT premiums.

## HDHP Limits for 2025

| Requirement | Self-Only Coverage | Family Coverage |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Minimum Annual Deductible | $1,650 | $3,300 |
| Maximum Annual Deductible and Out-of-Pocket | $8,300 | $16,600 |

## HDHP Limits for 2026

| Requirement | Self-Only Coverage | Family Coverage |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Minimum Annual Deductible | $1,700 | $3,400 |
| Maximum Annual Deductible and Out-of-Pocket | $8,500 | $17,000 |

**Note:** The out-of-pocket limit does not apply to deductibles and expenses for out-of-network services if the plan uses a network of providers.

## Coverage Types

### Self-Only HDHP Coverage
Coverage for only an eligible individual.

### Family HDHP Coverage
Coverage for an eligible individual and at least one other individual (whether or not that other individual is eligible).

**Special Rule for Married People:** If either spouse has family HDHP coverage, both spouses are treated as having family HDHP coverage for HSA contribution limit purposes.

## Family Plans That Don't Qualify as HDHPs

Some family plans have deductibles for both the family as a whole AND for individual family members. The plan does NOT qualify as an HDHP if:
- The deductible for the family as a whole is less than the minimum annual deductible for family coverage, OR
- The deductible for an individual family member is less than the minimum annual deductible for family coverage

## Preventive Care Safe Harbor

An HDHP may provide preventive care benefits without a deductible or with a deductible below the minimum annual deductible.

### Preventive Care Includes:
1. Periodic health evaluations (annual physicals, tests, diagnostic procedures)
2. Routine prenatal and well-child care
3. Child and adult immunizations
4. Tobacco cessation programs
5. Obesity weight-loss programs
6. Screening services for:
   - Cancer
   - Heart and vascular diseases
   - Infectious diseases
   - Mental health conditions
   - Substance abuse
   - Metabolic, nutritional, and endocrine conditions
   - Musculoskeletal disorders
   - Obstetric and gynecological conditions
   - Pediatric conditions
   - Vision and hearing disorders

### Notice 2024-75 Additions (Effective 2025):
- Over-the-counter oral contraceptives (including emergency contraceptives)
- Male condoms
- All types of breast cancer screening for undiagnosed individuals
- Continuous glucose monitors for diabetics

### Chronic Condition Preventive Care (Notice 2019-45):
Treatment for individuals with certain chronic conditions listed in Notice 2019-45 may be treated as preventive care if services were received or items incurred on or after July 17, 2019.

## Insulin Products

Public Law 117-169 (August 16, 2022) permits an HDHP to have a $0 deductible for selected insulin products. Applies to plan years beginning after 2022.

## Surprise Billing

Public Law 116-260 (December 27, 2020) permits an HDHP to provide benefits under federal and state anti-"surprise billing" laws with a $0 deductible. An eligible individual remains HSA-eligible even if receiving anti-surprise billing benefits outside the HDHP.

## Telehealth and Remote Care (2025)

Public Law 119-21 (July 4, 2025) provides:
1. An HSA-eligible individual may have disregarded coverage for telehealth and other remote care
2. A plan will not fail to be an HDHP for lacking a deductible for telehealth services

Applies to plan years beginning after 2024.

## Permitted Other Coverage

An individual with HDHP coverage can have additional coverage for:
- Workers' compensation
- Specific disease or illness
- Fixed amount per day of hospitalization
- Accidents
- Dental care
- Disability
- Vision care
- Long-term care
- Telehealth and remote care

**Caution:** Plans where substantially all coverage is for these items are NOT HDHPs.

## Prescription Drug Plans

A prescription drug plan (separate from HDHP or as rider) is permitted if:
- The plan doesn't provide benefits until the minimum annual deductible of the HDHP has been met

If benefits are available before the deductible is met, the individual is NOT HSA-eligible.
