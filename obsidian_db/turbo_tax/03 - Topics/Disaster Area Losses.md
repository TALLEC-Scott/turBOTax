# Disaster Area Losses

## Overview

A disaster loss is a loss attributable to a [[Federally Declared Disasters|federally declared disaster]] that occurs in an area eligible for assistance under the Stafford Act. Special rules apply for timing, reporting, and calculation of these losses.

## Eligibility

The loss must occur in an area designated by FEMA for:
- Public assistance, OR
- Individual assistance, OR
- Both

Check eligibility at **FEMA.gov/Disaster**

## Key Benefits

### 1. Elect to Deduct in Prior Year

You can elect to deduct the loss on your return (or amended return) for the **tax year immediately preceding the disaster year**.

**Deadline**: 6 months after the regular due date for filing the disaster year return (October 15 of the following year for calendar year taxpayers).

### 2. Extended Replacement Period

For main homes in disaster areas, the replacement period is **4 years** (instead of 2 years) after the close of the first tax year in which gain is realized.

### 3. Special Reporting Rules

- Use Section D of Form 4684
- Include FEMA disaster declaration number (DR-XXXX or EM-XXXX)
- May result in faster refund

## Qualified Disaster Relief Payments

Payments received for expenses incurred due to a federally declared disaster are **not taxable income**. This includes:

- Amounts to repair/replace personal property
- Medical expenses
- Temporary housing assistance
- Transportation costs

**Exception**: Disaster unemployment assistance is taxable.

## Federal Disaster Relief Grants

Grants under the Stafford Act are generally **not included in income**.

### Qualified Wildfire Relief Payments (2020-2025)

Certain relief payments following wildfire disasters are not taxable. File amended return if previously reported.

### East Palestine Disaster Relief Payments

Relief payments for the February 2023 train derailment in East Palestine, Ohio are not taxable.

## Qualified Disaster Mitigation Payments

Payments from state/local governments for hazard mitigation measures are excluded from income.

**Cannot** increase basis or deduct as business expense for qualified disaster mitigation payments.

## Postponed Tax Deadlines

### Mandatory 120-Day Extension

For disasters declared after July 24, 2025:
- Automatic 120-day extension for certain tax deadlines
- Applies to both federal disasters and qualified state-declared disasters
- Covers filing, paying income/excise/employment taxes, and IRA contributions

### Deadline Postponement Rules

Disaster-related postponements are treated as filing extensions for calculating lookback periods for credit/refund claims (effective December 26, 2025).

## How to Claim Prior Year Deduction

1. File Form 1040-X for the preceding year
2. Attach Form 4684 (Section D, Part I) for that year
3. Include FEMA disaster declaration number
4. File by October 15 of year after disaster (for calendar year filers)

### Revoking the Election

File Form 1040-X for the preceding year with Form 4684, Section D, Part II. Must be filed within 90 days after the election deadline.

## Main Home in Disaster Area

### Home Made Unsafe by Disaster

If government orders demolition/relocation within 120 days:
- Treated as casualty loss from disaster
- FMV after casualty = value before demolition/relocation

### Special Rules for Insurance Proceeds

1. **No gain recognized** on insurance proceeds for unscheduled personal property
2. Other proceeds treated as for single item of property
3. 4-year replacement period applies
