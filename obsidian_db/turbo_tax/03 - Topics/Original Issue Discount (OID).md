# Original Issue Discount (OID)

## Overview

Original Issue Discount (OID) is a form of interest representing the excess of a debt instrument's stated redemption price at maturity over its issue price. OID accrues over the life of the debt instrument and must generally be included in income as it accrues each year.

## Key Rules

### Including OID in Income
- OID must be included in income as it accrues each year, whether or not payments are received
- For long-term debt instruments, use either the OID tables or income tax regulations to figure OID
- Basis in the debt instrument increases by OID included in income

### De Minimis Rule
OID can be treated as zero if total OID is less than 0.25% (0.0025) of the stated redemption price multiplied by the number of full years from issue to maturity.

**Example:** A 10-year $1,000 bond issued at $980 has $20 OID. The de minimis threshold is $25 (0.0025 × $1,000 × 10 years). Since $20 < $25, OID can be treated as zero.

### Exceptions to Accrual Rules
OID accrual rules generally do not apply to:
- U.S. savings bonds
- Tax-exempt obligations
- Loans of $10,000 or less between individuals not in the lending business

## Types of OID Debt Instruments

1. **Zero coupon bonds** - Pay no stated interest until maturity
2. **Discount bonds** - Issued at price below stated redemption price
3. **Stripped bonds/coupons** - Separated interest and principal components

## Figuring OID

### For Debt Instruments Issued After 1984
Use the **constant yield method**:
1. Multiply adjusted issue price at beginning of accrual period by YTM divided by number of accrual periods per year
2. Subtract qualified stated interest allocable to the accrual period

### Daily OID Calculation
Daily OID = (Adjusted Issue Price × YTM ÷ n - Qualified Stated Interest) ÷ Days in Accrual Period

Where n = number of accrual periods per year

## Adjustments to OID

- [[Acquisition Premium]] - Reduces OID when purchased above adjusted issue price
- [[Market Discount]] - Additional income when purchased below issue price plus accrued OID
- Bond premium - Eliminates future OID reporting

## Sale or Redemption

- Gain/loss is generally capital if held as capital asset
- Basis = Cost + OID included in income
- For covered securities, broker reports adjusted basis on [[Form 1099-B]]
