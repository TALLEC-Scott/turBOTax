# Bankruptcy Tax Filing Requirements

## Overview

The Bankruptcy Code requires debtors filing under Chapters 7, 11, 12, and 13 to file all applicable federal, state, and local tax returns that become due after the case commences. Failure to file returns timely or obtain extensions can cause dismissal or conversion of the case.

## Pre-Petition Returns (Chapter 13)

For [[Chapter 13 Bankruptcy Tax|Chapter 13]] cases, debtors must file all required tax returns for tax periods ending during the **4-year period prior** to the bankruptcy filing date.

| Requirement | Deadline |
|-------------|----------|
| File federal returns | Before first creditors' meeting |
| Request extension | Trustee may hold meeting open 120 days |
| Court extension | Up to additional 30 days after notice/hearing |

**Caution:** Failure to timely file returns can prevent plan confirmation and result in dismissal or conversion to Chapter 7.

## Post-Petition Returns

For all chapters (7, 11, 12, and 13), debtors must file tax returns that become due after the case commences or obtain extensions before the due date.

If the debtor fails to file:
1. Taxing authority may request dismissal or conversion
2. Court must dismiss or convert if return not filed within 90 days of request

### Chapter 11 Specific Rules

Failure to file tax returns timely and pay taxes owed after the order for relief is cause for:
- Dismissal of the Chapter 11 case
- Conversion to Chapter 7
- Appointment of a Chapter 11 trustee

## Filing Threshold for Bankruptcy Estates (2025)

A bankruptcy estate must file Form 1041 if gross income meets or exceeds **$15,750** (equal to the standard deduction for married filing separately). This amount is adjusted annually.

## Return Filing by Entity Type

### Individual Debtors

| Chapter | Debtor Files | Estate Files |
|---------|--------------|--------------|
| Chapter 7 | Form 1040/1040-SR | Form 1041 (trustee) |
| Chapter 11 | Form 1040/1040-SR | Form 1041 (DIP/trustee) |
| Chapter 12 | Form 1040/1040-SR | None |
| Chapter 13 | Form 1040/1040-SR | None |

**Note:** In Chapter 11, the debtor-in-possession files both individual and estate returns.

### Partnerships

Filing requirements don't change—file Form 1065. The trustee or debtor-in-possession assumes responsibility for filing.

### Corporations

Filing requirements don't change—file Form 1120 or 1120-S. The trustee or debtor-in-possession assumes responsibility.

## Extension for Bankruptcy Estate

Bankruptcy estates are eligible for an **automatic 6-month extension** to file Form 1041 upon filing Form 7004.

| Return Type | Regular Due Date | Extended Due Date |
|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Calendar-year estate | April 15 | October 15 |
| Fiscal-year estate | 15th day of 4th month after year-end | 6 months later |

## Change of Accounting Period

A bankruptcy estate may change its tax year **once without IRS approval**, allowing the trustee to close the estate's tax year early before expected termination.

## Ordering Tax Transcripts

Debtors can request free transcripts:
- **Online:** IRS.gov/Transcripts
- **Phone:** 800-908-9946
- **Form:** Form 4506-T

Transcripts are normally mailed within 10 business days.

For copies of actual returns, file Form 4506 with $30 fee per return (up to 75 calendar days for processing).

## Disclosure of Return Information

### To Trustee

| Case Type | Disclosure Rules |
|-----------|------------------|
| Individual Chapter 7/11 | Returns open to trustee upon written request |
| Other cases | Only if trustee has material interest |

### To Debtor

The bankruptcy estate's return is open to the individual debtor in Chapter 7 or 11 upon written request (needed to determine tax attributes the debtor assumes at termination).
