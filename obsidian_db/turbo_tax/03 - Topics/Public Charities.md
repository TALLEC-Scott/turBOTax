# Public Charities

## Overview

Public charities are Section 501(c)(3) organizations that receive a substantial portion of their support from the general public or government. They enjoy more favorable tax treatment than private foundations, including higher contribution limits for donors and fewer restrictions on activities.

## Classification Under Section 509(a)

### Section 509(a)(1) Organizations

Organizations described in Section 170(b)(1)(A):
- Churches and religious orders
- Educational organizations (schools, colleges, universities)
- Hospitals and medical research organizations
- Organizations supporting government units
- Publicly supported organizations meeting the one-third support test

### Section 509(a)(2) Organizations

Organizations that:
- Receive more than one-third of support from gifts, grants, contributions, membership fees, and gross receipts from related activities
- Receive no more than one-third of support from investment income and unrelated business income

### Section 509(a)(3) Supporting Organizations

Organizations that support one or more public charities:
- Must be operated, supervised, or controlled by the supported organization (Type I)
- Must be supervised or controlled in connection with the supported organization (Type II)
- Must be operated in connection with the supported organization (Type III)

## Public Support Tests

### One-Third Support Test (509(a)(1))
Support from the public must exceed one-third of total support:

| Source | Treatment |
|--------|-----------|
| General public | Count fully |
| Government units | Count fully |
| Public charities | Count fully |
| Disqualified persons | Limited to 2% of total support |

### Facts and Circumstances Test
If organization fails one-third test but achieves at least 10% public support, it may still qualify if it:
- Maintains a continuous program for attracting public support
- Has a governing body representing public interests
- Makes services available to the public

### Section 509(a)(2) Support Tests
1. **One-third test**: More than one-third from permitted sources
2. **Not-more-than-one-third test**: No more than one-third from investment income and UBTI

## Gross Receipts Test (509(a)(2))

For receipts from related activities:
- Gross receipts from any person limited to greater of $5,000 or 1% of total support
- Receipts above these limits are excluded from support calculation

## Community Trusts

Community trusts may be treated as single entities for public support purposes if they:
- Are known as community trusts or similar names
- Have common governing instruments
- Have common governing bodies with specified powers
- Prepare consolidated financial reports

## Support Calculation Period

Support is computed over a 5-year period:
- Current year plus 4 preceding years
- New organizations: reasonable expectation test for first 5 years
- Must maintain status annually after initial period

## Advantages Over Private Foundations

| Feature | Public Charities | Private Foundations |
|---------|------------------|---------------------|
| Cash contribution limit | 60% of AGI | 30% of AGI |
| Excise tax on investment income | None | 1.39% |
| Distribution requirement | None | 5% annually |
| Self-dealing rules | Excess benefit rules only | Strict prohibitions |
| Excess business holdings | No limit | 20% limit |

## Schedule A Requirements

Public charities must complete Schedule A (Form 990) annually to:
- Document public support calculations
- Identify type of public charity
- Report supporting organization information

See [[Form 990 Filing Requirements]] for filing details.
