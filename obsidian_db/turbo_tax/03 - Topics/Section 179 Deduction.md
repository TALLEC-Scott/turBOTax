# Section 179 Deduction

## Overview

Section 179 allows farmers to deduct the full cost of qualifying property in the year it is placed in service, rather than depreciating it over multiple years. This provides immediate tax savings for farm equipment purchases.

## 2025 Limits

### Maximum Deduction
- **$2,500,000** maximum Section 179 deduction for 2025

### Phase-Out Threshold
- Deduction reduced dollar-for-dollar when qualified property exceeds **$4,000,000**
- Complete phase-out at $6,500,000 of qualified property

### Sport Utility Vehicle Limit
- **$31,300** maximum for SUVs placed in service in 2025

## Qualified Property

### Eligible Property
- Tangible personal property (machinery, equipment, furniture)
- Off-the-shelf computer software
- Qualified real property (see below)
- Property used in farming business

### Qualified Real Property
- Qualified improvement property (interior improvements to nonresidential buildings)
- Roofs, HVAC, fire protection, alarm systems, security systems

### Ineligible Property
- Land
- Buildings (except qualified improvement property)
- Investment property
- Property used outside the United States

## Farm-Specific Considerations

### Livestock
- Livestock held for draft, dairy, breeding, or sporting purposes may qualify
- Must be placed in service (acquired and ready for intended use)

### Farm Machinery
- Tractors, combines, irrigation equipment
- Grain bins and fencing
- Must have determinable useful life of more than one year

## Business Use Requirement

- Property must be used more than 50% in business
- Business use percentage multiplied by cost to determine deduction
- If business use drops to 50% or less, recapture rules apply

## Making the Election

- Claim deduction on [[Form 4562]]
- Attach to timely filed return (including extensions)
- Specific identification required for assets elected

## Recapture Rules

If business use falls to 50% or less during recovery period:
- Include recaptured amount as ordinary income
- Report on Form 4797
- Recapture equals difference between Section 179 taken and depreciation that would have been allowed
