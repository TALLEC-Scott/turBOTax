# Rollovers and Transfers

## Overview

[[403(b) Plans]] allow various types of transfers and rollovers to move funds between accounts while maintaining tax-deferred status.

## Types of Transfers

### Contract Exchanges (90-24 Transfers)

A contract exchange transfers your interest from one 403(b) contract to another 403(b) contract held in the **same plan**.

**Requirements**:
- Tax-free if properly executed
- Requires employer involvement (after September 24, 2007)
- Plan must provide for exchanges
- Distribution restrictions must be same or stricter
- Accumulated benefit must be equal after exchange

### Plan-to-Plan Transfers

Transfer part or all of your interest from one 403(b) plan to another 403(b) plan.

**Requirements**:
- Must be employee/former employee of receiving plan's employer
- Both plans must allow transfers
- Accumulated benefit must be at least equal
- Distribution restrictions same or stricter

### Direct Trustee-to-Trustee Transfers

From governmental 403(b) account to defined benefit governmental plan for:
- **Permissive service credits** - Credit for periods of service
- **Repayment of refunded contributions** - Restore previously forfeited service credit

**Note**: After-tax contributions can be rolled over between 403(b) plans, IRAs, and defined contribution plans through direct trustee-to-trustee transfer.

## Tax-Free Rollovers

### Rollover to Traditional IRA or Eligible Plan

You can roll over all or part of a distribution from a 403(b) plan to:
- Traditional IRA
- Non-Roth eligible retirement plan

**Exception**: Nonqualifying distributions cannot be rolled over.

### Rollover to Roth IRA

You may convert a distribution from a 403(b) plan by direct rollover to a Roth IRA.

**Tax Impact**: Conversion amounts are generally includible in taxable income in the year of distribution.

### 60-Day Rollover Rule

Distribution amounts must be rolled over within **60 calendar days** to qualify as nontaxable.

**Exceptions**:
- Automatic waiver (financial institution error)
- Self-certification waiver
- Private letter ruling waiver

### Hardship Waiver

The IRS may waive the 60-day requirement if failure would be against equity or good conscience, including:
- Casualty
- Disaster
- Events beyond reasonable control

## Special Transfer Situations

### Tax-Free Transfers for Cash Distributions

Applies to cash distributions from insurance companies under:
- Rehabilitation
- Conservatorship
- Insolvency
- Similar state proceedings

**Requirements**:
1. Withdraw all cash entitled to (or maximum permitted by state)
2. Reinvest in single policy/custodial account within 60 days
3. Assign future distribution rights to new contract

**Documentation**: Must provide written statement to new insurer with:
- Gross cash amount distributed
- Cash reinvested in new contract
- Investment in old contract on first distribution date

**Tax Return**: Attach statement with "ELECTION UNDER REV. PROC. 92-44" to timely filed return.

## Qualified Domestic Relations Orders (QDROs)

Distributions under a QDRO are taxable to the alternate payee, not the participant. See [[Qualified Domestic Relations Order]].

## Deceased Employees

If you're a beneficiary of a deceased 403(b) participant, special rollover rules apply. See [[Distributions to Beneficiaries]].
