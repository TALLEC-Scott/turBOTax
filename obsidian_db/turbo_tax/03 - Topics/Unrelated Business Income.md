# Unrelated Business Income

## Overview

Unrelated business income (UBI) is income from a trade or business that is regularly carried on by a tax-exempt organization and is not substantially related to the organization's exempt purpose. Tax-exempt organizations may be subject to unrelated business income tax (UBIT) on this income.

## Three Requirements for UBI

All three conditions must be met:

### 1. Trade or Business
- Any activity carried on for the production of income
- Includes selling goods or performing services
- Does not require profit motive

### 2. Regularly Carried On
- Frequency and continuity similar to comparable commercial activities
- Not isolated or sporadic activities
- Part-time or seasonal activities may qualify

### 3. Not Substantially Related
- Activity does not contribute importantly to exempt purpose
- Business activity is not causally connected to exempt purpose
- Income use (for exempt purposes) does not create relatedness

## Exceptions to UBI

### Statutory Exclusions

| Exclusion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Volunteer labor | Substantially all work performed by volunteers |
| Convenience of members | Activities for convenience of members, their families, or guests |
| Thrift shop sales | Selling donated merchandise |
| Dividends and interest | Investment income from securities |
| Royalties | Royalties from intellectual property |
| Rental income | Real property rental (with exceptions) |
| Research income | Research for government or educational purposes |

### Passive Income Exception
Investment income generally excluded:
- Dividends from corporate stock
- Interest from loans and deposits
- Annuities
- Royalties from licensing
- Rents from real property (generally)

## Debt-Financed Property

Income from debt-financed property may be taxable:
- Rental income from mortgaged property
- Investment income from leveraged investments
- Exception for substantially related activities

## Controlled Organizations

Income from controlled organizations may be taxable:
- Subsidiary corporations
- Partnerships where exempt organization is partner
- Income from controlled foreign corporations

## Form 990-T

Organizations with $1,000 or more of unrelated business income must file Form 990-T:

### Filing Requirements
- Due date: 15th day of 5th month after tax year
- Estimated tax payments required if tax exceeds $500
- May use same extensions as Form 990

### Deductions
- Directly connected expenses
- Charitable contribution deduction (for 501(c)(3) organizations)
- Specific deduction of $1,000

## Specific Organizations

### 501(c)(3) Organizations
- Subject to UBIT on unrelated business income
- May claim charitable contribution deduction
- Lower tax rates available

### Social Clubs (501(c)(7))
- Nonmember income generally taxable
- Investment income included
- Exception for 35% of gross receipts from outside sources

### Trade Associations (501(c)(6))
- Nonmember income may be taxable
- Political activity income separately taxed

## Penalty for Underpayment

- Underpayment penalty may apply
- Use Form 2220 to calculate
- Same rates as corporate underpayment penalties

## Reporting

Unrelated business income reported on:
- Form 990, Part I and Schedule I
- Form 990-T for tax calculation

See [[Form 990 Filing Requirements]] for annual reporting details.
