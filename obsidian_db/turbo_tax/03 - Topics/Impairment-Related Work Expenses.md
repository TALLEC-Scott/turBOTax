# Impairment-Related Work Expenses

## Overview

Impairment-related work expenses are a special category of business deductions available to disabled individuals. Unlike medical expenses, these expenses are **not subject to the 7.5% AGI floor** that limits medical expense deductions. They are deducted as business expenses on the appropriate schedule.

## Who Qualifies

An individual qualifies as disabled if they have either:

1. A **physical or mental disability** (e.g., blindness or deafness) that functionally limits their being employed; OR
2. A **physical or mental impairment** that substantially limits one or more major life activities:
   - Performing manual tasks
   - Walking
   - Speaking
   - Breathing
   - Learning
   - Working

## Definition of Impairment-Related Expenses

Expenses must meet ALL three criteria:

1. **Necessary for work** - Required to do the work satisfactorily
2. **Not for personal use** - Goods and services not required or used (other than incidentally) in personal activities
3. **Not covered elsewhere** - Not specifically covered under other income tax laws

## Key Advantage Over Medical Expense Deduction

| Aspect | Impairment-Related Work Expenses | Medical Expenses |
|--------|----------------------------------|------------------|
| Deduction type | Business deduction | Itemized deduction |
| AGI floor | None | 7.5% of AGI |
| Where to claim | Schedule C, E, F, or Form 2106 | Schedule A |
| Limitation | Must be work-related | Must exceed AGI floor |

## Examples of Qualifying Expenses

- Prosthetic limbs needed for work
- Special telephone equipment for hearing-impaired persons
- Wheelchair or mobility devices for work
- Guide dog or service animal costs for work
- Special transportation equipment
- Attendant care services at work
- Adaptive computer equipment
- Sign language interpreter services

## Where to Claim

| Filing Situation | Form/Schedule |
|------------------|---------------|
| Self-employed | Schedule C (line 14), Schedule E (line 19 or 28), or Schedule F (line 15) |
| Employee | Form 2106 (Employee Business Expenses) - subject to 2% AGI floor for miscellaneous itemized deductions |

**Note:** Employee business expenses are currently suspended (2018-2025) as miscellaneous itemized deductions subject to 2% AGI floor, unless an exception applies (e.g., Armed Forces reservists, qualified performing artists, fee-basis state or local government officials, or employees with impairment-related work expenses).

## Documentation Requirements

Keep records of:
- Nature of disability
- How expenses enable work
- Cost and date of expenses
- Connection between expense and work duties
