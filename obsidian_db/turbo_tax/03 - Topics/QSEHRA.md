# QSEHRA - Qualified Small Employer Health Reimbursement Arrangement

## Overview

A Qualified Small Employer Health Reimbursement Arrangement (QSEHRA) allows small employers (fewer than 50 full-time employees) to reimburse employees tax-free for medical expenses, including premiums for Marketplace health insurance. If you receive QSEHRA benefits, special rules apply to your Premium Tax Credit calculation.

## How QSEHRA Works

### For Employers
- Must not offer a group health plan to any employee
- Must offer QSEHRA to all full-time employees
- Annual permitted benefit is capped (adjusted yearly)
- Must provide written notice to employees

### For Employees
- Reimbursements are tax-free up to the permitted benefit amount
- Reported on Form W-2, Box 12, with code FF
- May affect eligibility for Premium Tax Credit

## Impact on Premium Tax Credit

### Key Rule

If the QSEHRA is **affordable**, you cannot receive PTC for that month. If the QSEHRA is **not affordable**, you can receive PTC but must reduce it by the monthly permitted benefit amount.

### Determining Affordability

The QSEHRA is considered **affordable** if the self-only SLCSP premium minus the monthly permitted benefit is less than or equal to a percentage of your household income.

**Formula**:
```
Self-only SLCSP premium - Monthly permitted benefit ≤ Affordability threshold × Household income ÷ 12
```

Use **Worksheet N** in Publication 974 to determine affordability.

## Worksheet N - Affordability Calculation

### Key Inputs

| Input | Source |
|-------|--------|
| Household income | From Form 8962 calculations |
| Affordability percentage | Updated annually by IRS |
| Self-only SLCSP premium | From Marketplace or tools |
| Self-only permitted benefit | From employer written notice |
| Number of months provided | From employer or W-2 |

### Worksheet N Process

1. Calculate monthly household income (annual ÷ 12)
2. Multiply by affordability percentage
3. Get self-only SLCSP premium
4. Calculate monthly permitted benefit (annual ÷ months provided)
5. Compare: If SLCSP - permitted benefit ≤ threshold, QSEHRA is affordable

## Worksheet Q - PTC Calculation with QSEHRA

If the QSEHRA is not affordable (or affordable for some months only), use Worksheet Q to calculate your adjusted PTC.

### Part I - Monthly Permitted Benefit
Calculate the monthly permitted benefit amount from your W-2, Box 12, code FF.

### Part II - Annual Calculation
If you had the same SLCSP all year and QSEHRA was unaffordable all months, complete Part II to reduce your annual PTC.

### Part III - Monthly Calculation
If conditions varied during the year, complete monthly calculations for each month.

## Form W-2 Reporting

Your employer reports QSEHRA benefits in:
- **Box 12, Code FF**: Annual permitted benefit amount
- This amount may be for self-only or family coverage

### Written Notice Requirement

Employers must provide written notice showing:
- Self-only permitted benefit amount
- Family permitted benefit amount (if applicable)
- Which months the QSEHRA was provided

**Important**: If your notice doesn't include this information, contact your employer.

## Part-Year and Changing Situations

### If You Changed Jobs
Complete separate Worksheet N for each employer and each period with different SLCSP premiums.

### If You Changed Marketplace Plans
If your SLCSP changed mid-year, complete separate worksheets for each period.

### Self-Only vs. Family Coverage
If you received self-only for part of the year and family for another part:
- Use the appropriate permitted benefit for each period
- Divide annual amounts by 12 for monthly rates
- Complete Part III of Worksheet Q

## Example Scenario

Bob was employed all of 2025 by an employer providing QSEHRA. He changed Marketplace policies in May due to a move, resulting in different SLCSP premiums for January-May vs. June-December.

**Result**: Bob must complete separate Worksheet N for each period to determine affordability.

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
