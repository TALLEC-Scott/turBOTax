# Bargain Sales

## Overview

A bargain sale occurs when property is sold or exchanged for less than fair market value (FMV) with the intent of making a gift. The transaction is partly a sale or exchange and partly a gift.

## General Rules

### Gain Recognition
- Gain recognized if amount realized > adjusted basis
- Gain = Amount realized - Adjusted basis

### Loss Treatment
- **No loss deduction** if amount realized < adjusted basis
- The gift element prevents loss recognition

## Bargain Sales to Charity

When property is sold to a charitable organization below FMV:

### Allocation of Basis
If a charitable deduction is allowable:

```
Basis of part sold = (Adjusted basis × Amount realized) / FMV of entire property
```

### Example
- FMV of property: $10,000
- Sale price to charity: $2,000
- Adjusted basis: $4,000
- Basis of part sold: $4,000 × ($2,000/$10,000) = $800
- **Gain: $2,000 - $800 = $1,200**

### Charitable Deduction
- Deduction = FMV - Amount realized
- Subject to charitable contribution limits
- See [[Pub 526 - Charitable Contributions]]
