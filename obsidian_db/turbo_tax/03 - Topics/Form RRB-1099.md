# Form RRB-1099

## Overview

Form RRB-1099 (Payments by the Railroad Retirement Board) reports the Social Security Equivalent Benefit (SSEB) portion of Tier 1 railroad retirement benefits. This form is for citizens and residents of the United States and is used to determine the taxable portion of your benefits.

## Who Receives This Form

Railroad retirees and their families who received, repaid, or had tax withheld from SSEB benefits or special guaranty benefits during the tax year.

**Note**: Each beneficiary receives their own Form RRB-1099. If you receive benefits on more than one railroad retirement record, you may receive more than one form.

## Related Forms

| Form | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| RRB-1099 | SSEB portion of Tier 1 (like Social Security) |
| RRB-1099-R | Tier 2, NSSEB, VDB, Supplemental Annuity (like pension) |
| SSA-1099 | Social Security benefits paid through SSA |

## Key Boxes Explained

### Box 1 - Claim Number and Payee Code
Your RRB claim number (alphabetical prefix + 6 or 9 digits) and payee code. Use this for all RRB correspondence.

### Box 2 - Recipient's Identification Number
Your SSN, ITIN, or EIN.

### Box 3 - Gross SSEB Paid in 2025
**Gross Social Security Equivalent Benefit portion of Tier 1 paid in 2025.**

This is the amount BEFORE deductions for:
- Federal income tax withholding
- Legal process garnishment
- Medicare premiums
- Overall minimum assignment payments
- Overpayment recovery
- Workers' compensation offset

This is the amount AFTER deductions for:
- Social security benefits
- Public service pensions
- Age reduction
- Dual railroad retirement entitlement
- Work deductions
- Annuity waiver
- Actuarial adjustment

**Includes** lump-sum payments for prior years (see Boxes 7, 8, 9).

### Box 4 - SSEB Repaid to RRB in 2025
**Total SSEB benefits repaid in 2025.**

Includes:
- Returned payments
- Cash refunds
- Amounts withheld to recover overpayments
- Amounts withheld to recover overpayments incurred by others under your claim

### Box 5 - Net SSEB Paid in 2025
**The figure to use for tax calculations.**
- Box 3 minus Box 4
- Use this amount to determine if benefits are taxable
- If negative (in parentheses), see [[Benefits Repayment Rules]]

### Box 6 - Workers' Compensation Offset
For information only. Shows workers' compensation benefits used to offset your Tier 1 payments. This amount is already included in Boxes 3 and 5.

### Boxes 7, 8, 9 - Benefits Paid for Prior Years

| Box | Year |
|-----|------|
| 7 | Benefits paid in 2025 for 2024 |
| 8 | Benefits paid in 2025 for 2023 |
| 9 | Benefits paid in 2025 for years before 2023 |

These amounts are already included in Box 3. If you have amounts in these boxes, consider the [[Lump-Sum Election]] method.

### Box 10 - Federal Income Tax Withheld
Total federal income tax withheld based on Form W-4V. Include this on your tax return as tax withheld.

### Box 11 - Medicare Premium Total
**For information only - not reported to IRS.**

Total Part B, Part C, and Part D Medicare premiums deducted from your railroad retirement annuity in 2025.

## Examples

### Example 1: Railroad Unemployment Insurance Offset
From January-March 2025, you received $300 in Railroad Unemployment Insurance. Your SSEB benefits of $509/month started in January, but first payment came in April.

- April payment reduced by $300 (unemployment offset)
- Box 3 shows $5,599 ($509 × 11 months gross)
- Even though you received less, Box 3 shows the gross amount

### Example 2: Medicare Premium Deduction
Tier 1 benefits of $600/month consist of:
- SSEB portion: $250/month
- NSSEB portion: $350/month

Starting July, $185/month Medicare premium deducted.

- Box 3 shows $3,000 ($250 × 12 months gross SSEB)
- Box 11 shows $1,111 ($185 × 6 months Medicare)
- NSSEB shown on Form RRB-1099-R

## Using Form RRB-1099 for Tax Filing

### Step 1: Gather All Forms
Add amounts from all RRB-1099 forms received:
- Total all Box 5 amounts for net SSEB
- Total all Box 10 amounts for tax withheld

### Step 2: Report on Form 1040
| Form 1040 Line | Amount to Enter |
|----------------|-----------------|
| 6a | Total from all Box 5 (plus SSA-1099 Box 5) |
| 6b | Taxable amount from worksheet |
| 6c | Check if using lump-sum election |
| 6d | Check if MFS and lived apart all year |

### Step 3: Claim Withholding
Include Box 10 amount on Form 1040 as federal tax withheld.

## Repayment Situations

### Returned to Railroad Work
If you return to work for a railroad employer, you must repay benefits for those months:
- Example: Returned to work June-August 2025
- SSEB was $450/month
- Box 4 shows $1,350 repayment

### Overpayment Recovery
If you were overpaid, RRB withholds from future payments:
- Example: Overpaid $800 January-April
- $200/month withheld May-August
- Box 4 shows $800 repayment

### SSEB Overpayment Offset by Social Security
If SSA begins paying benefits that RRB was paying:
- RRB may keep SSA payments to offset SSEB overpayment
- Example: $900 kept to offset overpayment
- Box 4 shows $900 repayment
- You also receive SSA-1099 showing the $900 from SSA
