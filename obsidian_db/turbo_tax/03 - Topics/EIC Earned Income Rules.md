# EIC Earned Income Rules

The Earned Income Credit requires that you have [[Earned Income Credit|earned income]]. This topic explains what counts as earned income for EIC purposes under [[Pub 596 - Earned Income Credit|Publication 596, Rule 7]].

## What Is Earned Income?

The credit is called the "earned income" credit because you must work and have earned income to qualify. If married filing jointly, at least one spouse must have earned income.

## Types of Earned Income

### Wages, Salaries, and Tips
- Amounts reported in **Box 1 of Form W-2**
- Report on Form 1040, line 1a
- Includes:
  - Regular wages and salaries
  - Tips reported to employer
  - Bonuses and commissions

### Other Taxable Employee Pay
Report on Form 1040, lines 1b-1h:
- Household employee wages
- Tip income not reported to employer
- Taxable dependent care benefits
- Employer-provided adoption benefits (Form 8839)
- Wages from Form 8919
- Other earned income

### Self-Employment Income
- Net earnings from self-employment
- Report on [[Schedule C (Form 1040)|Schedule C]] or Schedule F
- Calculate earned income on **EIC Worksheet B** in Form 1040 instructions

### Statutory Employee Income
- You are a statutory employee if Box 13 of Form W-2 is checked "Statutory employee"
- Report on Schedule C

### Strike and Lockout Benefits
- Cash benefits are generally earned income
- Fair market value of non-cash benefits (other than bona fide gifts)
- Report on Form 1040, line 1h

### Disability Benefits
- Taxable disability benefits count as earned income **until minimum retirement age**
- After reaching minimum retirement age, benefits are pensions (not earned income)
- Report pre-retirement age disability benefits on Form 1040, line 1h

### Nontaxable Combat Pay
- You may **elect** to include nontaxable combat pay in earned income
- Shown on Form W-2, Box 12, code Q
- Election may increase or decrease your EIC
- Calculate both ways to determine benefit

## Special Rules for Clergy and Religious Workers

### Ministers
- Housing allowance and rental value of parsonage are included in earned income for EIC (unless Form 4361 approved)
- See [[Ministers and Church Employees]] and [[Self-Employment Tax for Clergy]]

### Form 4361 (Ministers)
If you have an approved Form 4361:
- Employee compensation for ministerial duties = earned income
- Nontaxable housing allowance = NOT earned income
- Self-employment income from ministerial duties = NOT earned income

### Form 4029 (Religious Exemption)
If you have an approved Form 4029:
- Wages, salaries, tips = earned income
- Self-employment income = NOT earned income

## What Does NOT Count as Earned Income

### Investment Income
- Interest and dividends
- Capital gains
- See [[Investment Income for EIC]]

### Retirement Income
- Pensions and annuities
- Social Security benefits (including disability benefits after minimum retirement age)
- Railroad retirement benefits

### Government Benefits
- Welfare benefits
- Workers' compensation
- Unemployment compensation
- Veterans' benefits including VA rehabilitation payments

### Other Nontaxable Income
- Alimony (for EIC purposes)
- Child support
- Nontaxable foster care payments
- Nontaxable workfare payments

### Earnings While Incarcerated
- Amounts received for services performed while an inmate in a penal institution are NOT earned income for EIC

### Nontaxable Military Pay (Except Combat Pay)
- Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH)
- Basic Allowance for Subsistence (BAS)
- See [[Pub 3 - Armed Forces Tax Guide]]

## Conservation Reserve Program (CRP) Payments

CRP payments are NOT earned income if you were receiving Social Security retirement or disability benefits when you received the payments.

## Community Property States

In community property states:
- Your earned income for EIC does NOT include spouse's earnings treated as yours under community property laws
- Your earned income includes entire amount YOU earned, even if part belongs to spouse

See [[Community Property]] for details.

## Calculating Earned Income

### For Employees
Use earned income from Form W-2, Box 1, plus any other taxable employee compensation.

### For Self-Employed
Use **EIC Worksheet B** in the Form 1040 instructions to calculate:
- Start with net self-employment income
- Add back certain deductions
- Subtract losses from other businesses

### For Statutory Employees
Use net profit from Schedule C.
