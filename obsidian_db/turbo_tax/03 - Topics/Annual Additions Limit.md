# Annual Additions Limit

## Overview

The annual additions limit is the first component of your Maximum Amount Contributable (MAC) for [[403(b) Plans]]. It limits the total contributions that can be made to your 403(b) account each year.

## Definition

Annual additions include all of the following:
- [[Elective Deferrals]] (including Roth contributions)
- [[Nonelective Contributions]] from employer
- After-tax contributions
- Forfeitures allocated to your account

## The Limit

The annual additions limit is the **lesser of**:

| Limit | 2025 | 2026 |
|-------|------|------|
| Dollar maximum | $70,000 | $72,000 |
| Percentage limit | 100% of includible compensation | 100% of includible compensation |

## Includible Compensation

Your [[Includible Compensation]] for your most recent year of service is used to determine the percentage-based limit. This generally includes:

- Wages, salaries, and fees for personal services
- Elective deferrals (pre-tax)
- Section 125 cafeteria plan amounts
- Section 457 plan deferrals
- Pre-tax transportation fringe benefit contributions
- Foreign earned income (if otherwise excludable)

### What's NOT Included

- Employer contributions to your 403(b) account
- Compensation earned while employer wasn't eligible
- Employer contributions to qualified plans (excludable from income)
- Cost of incidental life insurance

## Multiple 403(b) Accounts

If you have more than one 403(b) account maintained by the **same employer**, you must combine all contributions to determine the annual additions limit.

**Exception**: If you participate in 403(b) plans maintained by **different employers**, you don't combine amounts for annual addition limits.

## Participation in Qualified Plans

If you participate in both a 403(b) plan and a qualified plan, you must combine contributions to both plans for the annual additions limit if you have more than 50% control of the corporation, partnership, or sole proprietorship maintaining the plans.

## Special Rules

### Ministers and Church Employees
See [[Ministers and Church Employees]] for special rules regarding:
- Alternative $10,000 annual limit (lifetime max $40,000)
- Different includible compensation calculations

### Foreign Missionaries
May have different includible compensation rules. See [[Ministers and Church Employees]].

## Figuring Your Limit

Use Worksheet 1, Part I in [[Pub 571 - Tax-Sheltered Annuity Plans|Publication 571]] to calculate your annual additions limit.
