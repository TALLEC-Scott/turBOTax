# Material Participation

## Overview

Material participation determines whether a trade or business activity is treated as passive or nonpassive. If you **materially participate** in an activity, it is NOT a passive activity, and losses can be deducted without limitation under the passive activity rules.

This is a critical test for taxpayers with business interests, particularly those in partnerships, S corporations, or sole proprietorships.

## Seven Tests for Material Participation

You materially participate in a trade or business activity for a tax year if you satisfy **any one** of these tests:

### Test 1: 500+ Hours
You participated in the activity for **more than 500 hours** during the tax year.

### Test 2: Substantially All Participation
Your participation was **substantially all** the participation in the activity of all individuals for the tax year, including individuals who didn't own any interest.

### Test 3: 100+ Hours and Most Participation
You participated for **more than 100 hours** during the tax year AND you participated at least as much as any other individual (including non-owners).

### Test 4: Significant Participation Activities
The activity is a significant participation activity AND you participated in all significant participation activities combined for more than 500 hours.

A **significant participation activity** is one in which you participated for more than 100 hours but didn't materially participate under any other test.

### Test 5: Five of Ten Years
You materially participated in the activity (other than by meeting this fifth test) for any **5 of the 10 immediately preceding tax years** (consecutive or not).

### Test 6: Personal Service Activity
The activity is a personal service activity in which you materially participated for any **3 preceding tax years** (consecutive or not).

**Personal service activities** involve services in:
- Health (including veterinary)
- Law
- Engineering
- Architecture
- Accounting
- Actuarial science
- Performing arts
- Consulting
- Any trade or business where capital isn't a material income-producing factor

### Test 7: Facts and Circumstances
Based on all facts and circumstances, you participated on a **regular, continuous, and substantial basis** during the year.

**Limitations on Test 7:**
- Doesn't apply if you participated 100 hours or less
- Your management participation doesn't count if anyone else:
  - Received compensation for managing the activity, OR
  - Spent more hours managing than you did

## What Counts as Participation

### Included Activities
Any work you do in connection with an activity in which you own an interest counts as participation.

### Excluded: Work Not Usually Performed by Owners
Work doesn't count if BOTH:
1. The work isn't customarily done by owners of that type of activity, AND
2. One of your main reasons is to avoid disallowance of losses under passive activity rules

### Excluded: Investor Activities
Work as an investor doesn't count unless you're directly involved in day-to-day management or operations. Investor activities include:
- Studying and reviewing financial statements or reports
- Preparing summaries or analyses for your own use
- Monitoring finances or operations in a nonmanagerial capacity

### Spouse's Participation
Your participation includes your spouse's participation, even if:
- Your spouse didn't own any interest in the activity
- You don't file a joint return for the year

## Proof of Participation

You can use any reasonable method to prove participation. You don't need contemporaneous time reports if you can establish participation another way:
- Appointment books
- Calendars
- Narrative summaries

## Special Rules

### Limited Partners
Limited partners generally aren't treated as materially participating UNLESS they meet:
- Test 1 (500+ hours)
- Test 5 (5 of 10 years)
- Test 6 (Personal service activity, 3 years)

Exception: You're not treated as a limited partner if you were also a general partner at all times during the partnership's tax year.

### Retired or Disabled Farmers
Treated as materially participating if you materially participated for **5 or more of the 8 years** before retirement or disability.

### Surviving Spouse of Farmer
Treated as materially participating if the real property meets estate tax rules for special valuation of farm property AND you actively manage the farm.

### Corporations
A closely held corporation or personal service corporation is treated as materially participating only if shareholders holding **more than 50% by value** of outstanding stock materially participate.
