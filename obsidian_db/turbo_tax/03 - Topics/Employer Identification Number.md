# Employer Identification Number (EIN)

## Overview

An Employer Identification Number (EIN) is a nine-digit number assigned by the IRS to identify employers and other entities for tax purposes. It's also known as a Federal Tax Identification Number (TIN).

## EIN Format

- **Format:** XX-XXXXXXX (two digits, hyphen, seven digits)
- Example: 12-3456789

## Who Needs an EIN

You need an EIN if you:
- Have employees
- Operate your business as a corporation or partnership
- File employment tax returns
- File certain excise tax returns
- Withhold taxes on income paid to nonresident aliens
- Have a Keogh (self-employed) retirement plan
- Are involved with certain organizations (nonprofits, trusts, estates)

## How to Apply for an EIN

### Online Application (Preferred)
- **Website:** IRS.gov/EIN
- **Availability:** Monday-Friday, 7:00 AM - 10:00 PM EST
- **Speed:** Immediate issuance

### Form SS-4 Application
Submit by:
- **Fax:** Fax completed Form SS-4 to appropriate fax number
- **Processing time:** 4 business days
- **Mail:** Mail to appropriate IRS center
- **Processing time:** 4-6 weeks

### International Applicants
If principal business is outside the U.S.:
- Call 267-941-1099 (toll call)
- Available Monday-Friday, 6:00 AM - 11:00 PM EST

## EIN Usage Rules

### Do:
- Use your EIN on all tax returns, payments, and correspondence
- Provide EIN to employees for their Form W-2
- Use EIN when filing Forms 1099

### Don't:
- Use your SSN in place of an EIN
- Use another business's EIN
- Have multiple EINs for the same business
- Use a predecessor's EIN (for successor employers)

## When You Need a New EIN

You may need a new EIN if:
- Business structure changes (sole proprietorship to corporation)
- New business is formed
- You become a sole proprietor and hire employees
- Corporation receives new charter from state
- Partnership changes and all partners are replaced

### Usually No New EIN Needed For:
- Change in business name or location
- Adding/ending a partnership
- Filing bankruptcy as same entity
- Corporate reorganization without changing structure

## Changing Business Information

### Business Name Change
- Notify IRS immediately
- Write to IRS at filing address (without payment address)
- See [[Pub 1635]] for guidance

### Business Address or Responsible Party Change
- File [[Form 8822-B]] - Change of Address or Responsible Party

## Third-Party Payers and EINs

| Entity Type | Uses Whose EIN |
|-------------|----------------|
| Payroll Service Provider | Employer's EIN |
| Reporting Agent | Employer's EIN |
| Agent with Form 2678 | Agent's own EIN |
| CPEO | CPEO's EIN for wages it pays |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using wrong EIN** - Ensure EIN matches IRS assignment
2. **EIN mismatch on Form W-3** - Forms 941 and W-3 must show same EIN
3. **Using SSN instead of EIN** - Never substitute SSN for EIN on employment forms
4. **Not updating responsible party** - Must report changes via Form 8822-B
