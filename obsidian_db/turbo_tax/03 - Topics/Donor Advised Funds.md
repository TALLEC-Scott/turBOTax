# Donor Advised Funds

## Overview

A donor advised fund (DAF) is a fund or account separately identified by reference to contributions of a donor or donors that is owned and controlled by a sponsoring organization and for which the donor has or expects to have advisory privileges concerning the distribution or investment of the funds.

## Definition Requirements

A donor advised fund must meet three criteria:
1. Separately identified by reference to contributions of a donor or donors
2. Owned and controlled by a sponsoring organization
3. Donor has or expects to have advisory privileges

## Sponsoring Organization

A sponsoring organization is:
- A Section 170(c) organization
- Neither a government organization nor a private foundation
- Typically a community foundation or other public charity

## Exceptions

The following are NOT donor advised funds:
1. Funds that make distributions only to a single identified organization or governmental entity
2. Certain funds for individual grants for travel, study, or similar purposes if:
   - Advisory privileges are performed exclusively in committee member capacity
   - No combination of advisors control the committee
   - Grants are awarded on objective and nondiscriminatory basis

## Taxable Distributions (Section 4966)

### Definition
A taxable distribution is any distribution from a DAF:
- To any natural person, OR
- To any other person if:
  - Not for Section 170(c)(2)(B) purposes, OR
  - Sponsoring organization doesn't exercise expenditure responsibility

### Exceptions
Distributions to the following are NOT taxable:
- The sponsoring organization
- Section 170(b)(1)(A) organizations (other than disqualified supporting organizations)
- Other donor advised funds

### Tax Rates
| Taxpayer | Rate |
|----------|------|
| Sponsoring organization | 20% of taxable distribution |
| Fund manager (if knowing) | 5% of distribution (max $10,000) |

## Prohibited Benefits (Section 4967)

### Definition
A prohibited benefit occurs when a donor, donor advisor, or related party receives a more than incidental benefit from a distribution.

### Tax Rates
| Taxpayer | Rate |
|----------|------|
| Advisor/recipient | 125% of the benefit |
| Fund manager (if knowing) | 10% of benefit (max $10,000) |

### Exception
If the transaction is also an excess benefit under Section 4958, only Section 4958 applies.

## Disqualified Supporting Organizations

Organizations that are NOT eligible to receive DAF distributions without expenditure responsibility:
1. Type III supporting organizations that are not functionally integrated
2. Supporting organizations where the donor controls a supported organization

## Recordkeeping Requirements

### Sponsoring Organizations Must Track
- Each donor's contributions
- Advisory privileges granted
- Distributions made on donor advice
- Compliance with distribution rules

## Reporting

### Form 990 Requirements
- Report DAF activity on Form 990
- Complete Schedule D for donor advised funds
- List number of DAF accounts and aggregate assets
