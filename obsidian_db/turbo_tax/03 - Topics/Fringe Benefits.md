# Fringe Benefits

## Overview

Fringe benefits are compensation provided in connection with the performance of services. Most fringe benefits are included in taxable income unless specifically excluded by law or you pay fair market value for them.

## Key Principles

### Recipient of Benefit

You're the recipient if you perform services for which the benefit is provided, even if given to a family member. Partners, directors, and independent contractors can also be recipients.

### Provider of Benefit

Your employer or person for whom you perform services is the provider, regardless of who actually provides the benefit.

### Valuation Rule

Taxable amount is generally FMV minus any amount you paid and any statutory exclusion.

## Excludable Fringe Benefits

### Accident or Health Plans

Employer-provided health coverage is generally excluded from income. Benefits received may be taxable depending on who paid premiums.

Related: [[Health Savings Account]], [[Long-Term Care]]

### Adoption Assistance

Up to specified limits, employer payments for qualified adoption expenses are excluded from income. Reported on Form W-2, box 12, code T. File Form 8839.

Related: [[Form 8839]]

### Athletic Facilities

Value of free/low-cost use of employer-operated gym on employer premises is excluded if used primarily by employees and families.

### De Minimis Benefits

Benefits so small that accounting for them is unreasonable are excluded:
- Occasional personal use of copier (85%+ business use)
- Company picnics
- Holiday turkeys or hams (not cash/gift cards)
- Cab fare home when working overtime

### Dependent Care Benefits

Up to $5,000 ($2,500 if MFS) excluded from income for dependent care assistance. Reported on Form W-2, box 10. Must file Form 2441.

Related: [[Child and Dependent Care Credit]], [[Form 2441]], [[Dependent Care Benefits]]

### Educational Assistance

Up to $5,250 of qualified employer-provided educational assistance excluded from income.

Related: [[Pub 970 - Tax Benefits for Education]]

### Employee Discounts

Excluded from income up to:
- **Services**: 20% of price charged customers
- **Property**: Employer's gross profit percentage

Must be in your line of business. Doesn't apply to real estate or investment property.

### Group-Term Life Insurance

Cost of first $50,000 coverage excluded from income. Excess coverage taxable based on IRS table rates.

| Age | Monthly Cost per $1,000 |

| Under 25 | $0.05 |
| 25-29 | $0.06 |

Reported on Form W-2, box 12, code C.

### Meals and Lodging

Excluded from income if:
1. **Meals**: Furnished on business premises for employer's convenience
2. **Lodging**: On business premises, for employer's convenience, and condition of employment

### No-Additional-Cost Services

Services provided free/reduced cost excluded if employer has no substantial additional cost and offers same services to customers.

Example: Free flights for airline employees on standby.

### Retirement Planning Services

Qualified retirement planning services from employer excluded from income.

### Transportation Benefits

| Benefit | 2025 Monthly Limit |
|---------|-------------------|
| Transit pass | $325 |
| Commuter highway vehicle | $325 |
| Qualified parking | $325 |

### Tuition Reduction

Qualified tuition reduction for education below graduate level at educational institution where employed. Also applies to graduate teaching/research assistants.

### Working Condition Benefits

Property or services excluded if cost would have been deductible business expense if you paid for it yourself.

## Taxable Fringe Benefits

- Financial counseling fees (except retirement planning)
- Health FSA contributions above limit
- Life insurance above $50,000 coverage
- Meals not meeting exclusion requirements
- Personal use of employer vehicle

## Form W-2 Reporting

Taxable fringe benefits included in:
- Box 1: Wages, tips, other compensation
- Boxes 3 and 5: Social Security and Medicare wages
- Box 14: May show total fringe benefit values

## Common Questions

### Q: Is employer-paid gym membership taxable?
**A:** Yes, unless the gym is on employer premises and operated by employer primarily for employees.

### Q: Are holiday bonuses taxable?
**A:** Yes, cash bonuses and gift certificates are always taxable. Turkeys or hams are de minimis benefits.

### Q: How do I value personal use of company car?
**A:** Use general valuation rule (what it would cost to lease) or special valuation rules (cents-per-mile, commuting rule, etc.).
