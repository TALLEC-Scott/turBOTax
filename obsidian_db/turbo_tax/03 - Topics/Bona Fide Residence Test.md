# Bona Fide Residence Test

## Overview

The Bona Fide Residence Test is one of two tests that can qualify you for the [[Foreign Earned Income Exclusion]] and [[Foreign Housing Exclusion and Deduction]]. It requires establishing genuine residence in a foreign country for an entire tax year.

## Eligibility Requirements

To qualify under the bona fide residence test, you must:

### 1. Be a U.S. Citizen OR a Qualifying Resident Alien

**U.S. Citizens:** Automatically eligible to use this test.

**Resident Aliens:** Must be a citizen or national of a country with which the United States has an income tax treaty in effect.

### 2. Be a Bona Fide Resident of a Foreign Country

You must be a bona fide resident of a foreign country (or countries) for an **uninterrupted period that includes an entire tax year**.

**For calendar year taxpayers:** January 1 through December 31 (or longer).

## What is "Bona Fide Residence"?

Bona fide residence means you have established your tax home in a foreign country with the intention of remaining there indefinitely. It is NOT automatically acquired by merely living abroad for one year.

### Factors the IRS Considers

| Factor | Indicates Bona Fide Residence |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| **Intention** | You intend to stay indefinitely, not for a specific period |
| **Employment** | Permanent or indefinite employment vs. fixed-term contract |
| **Home** | You've established a permanent home |
| **Family** | Family accompanies you abroad |
| **Economic ties** | Banking, investments, business connections in foreign country |
| **Social ties** | Community involvement, memberships, friendships |
| **Duration** | Typically longer stays, though not determinative |
| **Documentation** | Residence permits, local driver's license, voter registration |

### What Does NOT Automatically Establish Residence

- Living abroad for exactly one year
- Having a temporary work assignment
- Maintaining a home in the United States
- Planning to return to the U.S. after a specific period

## Time Requirements

### Full Tax Year
The bona fide residence period must include an **entire tax year**. This means:

- **Calendar year filers:** January 1 - December 31 (or longer)
- **Fiscal year filers:** First day through last day of fiscal year

### Uninterrupted Period
The residence must be continuous. Brief trips outside the foreign country do not break the period, but moving between countries may.

### Example Scenarios

**Qualifies:**
- You move to Germany on October 1, 2024, and establish bona fide residence through all of 2025. You qualify for 2025.

**Does NOT Qualify:**
- You live in France from July 1, 2024, through June 30, 2025. This period does not include an entire calendar year, so you don't qualify for 2024 or 2025 under this test.

## Statement Required

You must attach a statement to your tax return explaining:
- That you are a bona fide resident of a foreign country
- The date you established residence
- Facts supporting your bona fide residence claim

**Form 2555** includes the necessary questions and space for this information.

## Exceptions for Adverse Conditions

If you leave a foreign country because of war, civil unrest, or similar adverse conditions, the minimum time requirements may be waived.

**Requirements:**
- You were a bona fide resident or physically present in the country
- You left during a specified period designated by the Treasury Secretary
- You can establish that but for those conditions, you would have met the requirements

The IRS publishes an annual list of qualifying countries in the Internal Revenue Bulletin.

## When to Use This Test

**Best for:**
- Long-term expatriates with indefinite stays
- Those who have established community ties abroad
- Families living abroad
- Those with permanent employment abroad

**Consider Physical Presence Test instead if:**
- You are on a fixed-term assignment
- You travel frequently between countries
- You cannot commit to a full tax year abroad
- You are a resident alien without treaty country citizenship

## Common Mistakes

1. **Assuming one year abroad automatically qualifies** - Intent matters
2. **Not maintaining foreign residence throughout the year**
3. **Moving between countries during the tax year**
4. **Being a resident alien without treaty country citizenship**
5. **Planning to return to U.S. for a specific reason** (indicates temporary intent)
