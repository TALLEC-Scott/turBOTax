# Section 501(c)(3) Organizations

## Overview

Section 501(c)(3) organizations are the most common type of tax-exempt organizations. They include organizations organized and operated exclusively for religious, charitable, scientific, testing for public safety, literary, or educational purposes, or to foster national or international amateur sports competition, or for the prevention of cruelty to children or animals.

## Exempt Purposes

### Charitable Purposes
The term "charitable" includes:
- Relief of the poor, distressed, or underprivileged
- Advancement of religion, science, or education
- Erection or maintenance of public buildings or monuments
- Lessening the burdens of government
- Elimination of prejudice and discrimination
- Defense of human and civil rights

### Other Exempt Purposes
- **Religious** - Churches, synagogues, mosques, and other religious organizations
- **Educational** - Schools, colleges, museums, and educational programs
- **Scientific** - Research organizations
- **Literary** - Libraries and literary societies
- **Prevention of cruelty** - Animal welfare and child protection organizations

## Organizational Requirements

### Articles of Organization
Must include:
1. Purpose limited to exempt purposes
2. Prohibition on private inurement
3. Dedication of assets to exempt purposes upon dissolution
4. For private foundations: specific distribution and lobbying restrictions

### Operational Requirements
1. Must be operated exclusively for exempt purposes
2. No more than insubstantial part of activities for non-exempt purposes
3. Cannot engage in political campaign activity
4. Limited lobbying activity allowed (with section 501(h) election)

## Application for Recognition

### Form 1023 vs. Form 1023-EZ
| Feature | Form 1023 | Form 1023-EZ |
|---------|-----------|--------------|
| Eligibility | All 501(c)(3) organizations | Small organizations meeting criteria |
| Filing Fee | Higher | Lower |
| Complexity | Full application | Streamlined |
| Processing Time | Longer | Faster |

### 27-Month Rule
Organizations that file Form 1023 within 27 months of formation receive retroactive exemption from the date of formation. Late filers may request discretionary extension.

See [[Pub 557 - Tax-Exempt Status for Your Organization]] for detailed application procedures.

## Private Foundation vs. Public Charity

### Private Foundations
- Receive substantial support from limited sources
- Subject to excise taxes on net investment income
- Must meet minimum distribution requirements
- Restricted on self-dealing and excess business holdings

See [[Private Foundations]] for detailed rules.

### Public Charities
- Receive substantial public support (at least one-third)
- Include churches, schools, hospitals, and publicly supported organizations
- More favorable tax treatment than private foundations

See [[Public Charities]] for classification rules and support tests.

## Political Activity Restrictions

### Absolute Prohibition
Section 501(c)(3) organizations cannot:
- Participate or intervene in political campaigns
- Endorse or oppose candidates for public office
- Make campaign contributions

### Lobbying Limitations
- No substantial part of activities can be lobbying
- May make section 501(h) election for measurable lobbying limits
- Private foundations generally cannot lobby

## Contribution Deductibility

Contributions to 501(c)(3) organizations are generally deductible by donors under IRC Section 170, except for organizations testing for public safety.

See [[Charitable Contributions]] for substantiation requirements.
