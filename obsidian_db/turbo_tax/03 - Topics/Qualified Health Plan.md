# Qualified Health Plan

## Overview

A Qualified Health Plan (QHP) is a health insurance plan that meets all Affordable Care Act (ACA) requirements and is certified by a Health Insurance Marketplace. These plans are eligible for Premium Tax Credit subsidies.

## Plan Metal Levels

QHPs are categorized by "metal" levels, which indicate how costs are shared between you and the plan:

| Metal Level | Plan Pays | You Pay |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| Bronze | 60% | 40% |
| Silver | 70% | 30% |
| Gold | 80% | 20% |
| Platinum | 90% | 10% |

**Note**: The metal level affects your out-of-pocket costs, not the quality of care.

## Required Benefits (Essential Health Benefits)

All QHPs must cover these 10 essential health benefits:

1. **Ambulatory patient services** - Outpatient care
2. **Emergency services** - Emergency room visits
3. **Hospitalization** - Inpatient care
4. **Maternity and newborn care** - Pregnancy and delivery
5. **Mental health and substance use disorder services** - Behavioral health
6. **Prescription drugs** - Medications
7. **Rehabilitative services and devices** - Therapy and equipment
8. **Laboratory services** - Tests and diagnostics
9. **Preventive and wellness services** - Screenings and immunizations
10. **Pediatric services** - Children's care including dental and vision

## Key Features

### No Pre-existing Condition Exclusions
QHPs cannot deny coverage or charge more based on health status.

### No Annual/Lifetime Limits
QHPs cannot impose dollar limits on essential health benefits.

### Guaranteed Issue
QHPs must be available to all eligible individuals regardless of health history.

### Essential Health Benefits
All QHPs must cover the 10 essential health benefit categories.

## Relationship to PTC

QHPs are the **only** type of coverage eligible for the Premium Tax Credit:

- You must be enrolled in a QHP through the Marketplace to claim PTC
- The PTC helps pay premiums for QHPs at any metal level
- APTC is paid directly to QHP insurers

## What is NOT a Qualified Health Plan

The following are NOT QHPs for PTC purposes:

| Plan Type | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| Catastrophic plans | Available only to those under 30 or with hardship exemptions |
| Stand-alone dental plans | Can be purchased with QHP but not eligible for PTC |
| SHOP plans | Small Business Health Options Program plans |
| Grandfathered plans | Plans existing before March 23, 2010 |
| Short-term health insurance | Limited duration, not ACA-compliant |

## Enrollment Periods

### Open Enrollment
- Typically November 1 - January 15 (dates vary by state)
- You can enroll, renew, or change plans

### Special Enrollment Periods
You can enroll outside Open Enrollment if you have a qualifying life event:
- Loss of other health coverage
- Marriage
- Birth or adoption
- Move to a new coverage area
- Income change affecting subsidy eligibility

## QHP Selection Considerations

When choosing a QHP, consider:

1. **Premium**: Monthly cost (before and after PTC)
2. **Deductible**: Amount you pay before plan starts paying
3. **Copays/Coinsurance**: Your share of service costs
4. **Network**: Which doctors and hospitals are in-network
5. **Drug formulary**: Coverage for your medications
6. **Metal level**: Balance of premium vs. out-of-pocket costs

## Silver Plans and Cost-Sharing Reductions

Silver plans have special importance:

- Cost-sharing reductions (lower deductibles, copays) are available ONLY with Silver plans
- Eligible if household income is 100-250% of federal poverty line
- The [[SLCSP (Second Lowest Cost Silver Plan)]] is used as the benchmark for PTC calculations
