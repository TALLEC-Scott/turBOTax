# Condominium Rental Rules

## Overview

A condominium is a multi-unit structure where units are individually owned. When you rent out a condominium, you follow the same basic rules as other residential rental property, but with some special considerations for association dues and shared expenses.

## Tax Treatment

### Same as Other Rental Property
- Report rental income on [[Schedule E (Form 1040)]]
- Deduct ordinary and necessary rental expenses
- Depreciate the unit over 27.5 years under [[MACRS Depreciation]]

### Deductible Expenses Include
- Real estate taxes on the unit
- Mortgage interest paid on the unit
- **Association dues** - Generally deductible as rental expense
- Repairs to the interior of the unit
- Insurance for the unit
- Depreciation on the unit

## Association Dues

Monthly condo association fees are generally deductible rental expenses. They cover:
- Building maintenance and repairs
- Insurance for common areas
- Landscaping and grounds maintenance
- Security
- Management fees
- Reserve fund contributions

**Note**: Special assessments may be treated differently (see below).

## Special Assessments

### Deductible Assessments
Assessments for **maintenance, repairs, or operations** are deductible as rental expenses.

### Capital Assessments
Assessments for **capital improvements** (new roof, parking lot, structural repairs) must be:
- Added to your basis in the unit
- Not deducted currently
- Considered when calculating gain/loss on sale

## Depreciation Rules

### What to Depreciate
- The condominium unit itself (including interior walls, fixtures)
- Appliances and furniture used in the rental

### What NOT to Depreciate
- Land (allocated portion of purchase price)
- Common areas (owned by association)

### Basis Allocation
When purchasing a condo:
1. Determine total purchase price
2. Allocate between land and building based on FMV
3. Only depreciate the building portion

## Renting vs. Personal Use

If you use the condominium personally AND rent it out:
- See [[Vacation Home Rules]] for expense allocation
- Divide expenses between rental and personal use
- Personal use limits may restrict deductions

### 15-Day Rule
If you rent the condo for **less than 15 days** per year:
- Rental income is **not taxable**
- Rental expenses are **not deductible**
- Mortgage interest and taxes may be deductible on Schedule A
