# Backup Withholding

## Overview

Backup withholding is a 24% flat-rate withholding that applies when a payer is required to withhold tax on certain payments because the payee failed to provide a correct Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN) or otherwise failed to certify their status properly.

## Withholding Rate

**24% flat rate** on reportable payments

## When Backup Withholding Applies

### Missing or Incorrect TIN
Backup withholding is required when:
1. Payee fails to provide TIN
2. TIN provided is incorrect
3. IRS notifies payer that TIN is incorrect
4. Payee fails to certify they are not subject to backup withholding

### B-Notices and IRS Notifications
- **First B-Notice:** Payee must provide TIN and certification
- **Second B-Notice:** Payee must provide TIN from IRS (Form 14815)
- **IRS Notice CP2100:** Notifies payer of incorrect TINs

## Payments Subject to Backup Withholding

### Interest and Dividends
- Interest payments (Form 1099-INT)
- Dividend payments (Form 1099-DIV)
- Original issue discount (Form 1099-OID)

### Other Reportable Payments
- Patronage dividends (Form 1099-PATR)
- Royalties (Form 1099-MISC)
- Rents (Form 1099-MISC)
- Nonemployee compensation (Form 1099-NEC)
- Certain government payments (Form 1099-G)
- Third-party network transactions (Form 1099-K)
- Gross proceeds to attorneys (Form 1099-MISC)

### Broker Transactions
- Proceeds from broker transactions (Form 1099-B)
- Barter exchange transactions (Form 1099-B)

## Payments NOT Subject to Backup Withholding

### Exempt Payments
- **Wages** - Regular income tax withholding applies
- **Pensions and annuities** - Subject to different rules
- **IRAs** - Including SEP and SIMPLE IRAs
- **HSA distributions** - Health Savings Account withdrawals
- **MSA distributions** - Medical Savings Account withdrawals
- **Real estate transactions** - Reportable but exempt
- **Disaster relief payments**
- **Scholarship/fellowship payments** (unless for services)

### Exempt Recipients
Backup withholding generally does not apply to:
- Corporations
- Tax-exempt organizations
- IRAs
- U.S. government agencies
- States and political subdivisions
- International organizations
- Foreign governments
- Registered securities dealers

## Compliance Process

### Initial Certification
Payees must certify on Form W-9:
1. Correct TIN
2. Not subject to backup withholding
3. U.S. person status
4. FATCA status

### When TIN Problems Arise

#### First B-Notice (from payer)
- Send to payee within 15 business days
- Payee returns with correct TIN
- Stop backup withholding when valid TIN provided

#### Second B-Notice (within 3 calendar years)
- Requires TIN from IRS (not self-certification)
- Payee must contact IRS for verification
- Continue backup withholding until resolved

#### IRS Notice CP2100/CP2100A
- Lists payees with incorrect TINs
- Compare against your records
- Send B-notices as appropriate
- Begin backup withholding if not corrected

## How to Begin Backup Withholding

### Timing
Begin backup withholding:
- Immediately if no TIN provided
- 30 days after first B-notice if no response
- 7 days after second B-notice if no response (or per IRS notice)

### Calculating Withholding
- 24% of gross reportable payments
- Withhold from each payment until issue resolved

### Reporting
- Report on Form 945 (Annual Return of Withheld Federal Income Tax)
- Report withheld amount on Form 1099 (Box 4)

## Stopping Backup Withholding

When backup withholding stops:
1. Valid TIN received from payee
2. IRS confirms TIN is correct
3. Continue normal reporting without withholding

## Forms and Documentation

| Form | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Form W-9 | Request for Taxpayer Identification Number and Certification |
| Form 945 | Annual Return of Withheld Federal Income Tax |
| Form 1099 | Report payments and withheld amounts |
| Form 945-A | Annual Record of Federal Tax Liability |

## Recordkeeping Requirements

Maintain records for at least 4 years showing:
- TINs received
- Dates of B-notices sent
- Responses received
- Amounts withheld
- Dates withholding began/stopped
