# Private Foundations

## Overview

Private foundations are Section 501(c)(3) organizations that do not qualify as public charities. They are typically funded by a single source (an individual, family, or corporation) and are subject to more restrictive rules and excise taxes than public charities.

## Definition

A private foundation is a 501(c)(3) organization that:
- Does not qualify as a public charity under Section 509(a)(1), (2), or (3)
- Is not an operating foundation
- Has not elected to be treated as a private foundation

## Key Differences from Public Charities

| Feature | Private Foundations | Public Charities |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Public Support | Limited sources | At least one-third from public |
| Excise Tax | 1.39% on net investment income | Generally none |
| Distribution Requirement | 5% minimum annually | None |
| Self-Dealing Rules | Strict prohibitions | Less restrictive |
| Excess Business Holdings | 20% limit | No limit |
| Contribution Limits | 30% AGI for cash | 60% AGI for cash |

## Excise Taxes

### Tax on Net Investment Income
- Rate: 1.39% of net investment income
- Includes interest, dividends, rents, royalties, capital gains
- Reported on Form 990-PF

### Taxes on Prohibited Activities
1. **Self-Dealing Tax** (Section 4941)
   - 10% tax on amount involved (25% for second-tier)
   - Applies to transactions with disqualified persons

2. **Failure to Distribute Income** (Section 4942)
   - 15% tax on undistributed income
   - 100% additional tax if not corrected

3. **Excess Business Holdings** (Section 4943)
   - 10% tax on excess holdings (5% for pre-2006 years)
   - Generally limited to 20% ownership

4. **Jeopardizing Investments** (Section 4944)
   - 10% tax on jeopardizing investments
   - 25% additional tax for failure to remove

5. **Taxable Expenditures** (Section 4945)
   - 20% tax on certain prohibited expenditures
   - 25% tax on agreement to make expenditure

## Distribution Requirements

### Minimum Distribution
Private foundations must distribute:
- Approximately 5% of net investment assets annually
- Includes qualifying distributions and set-asides
- Must be paid by end of following tax year

### Qualifying Distributions
- Grants to public charities
- Grants to individuals for scholarships (with approved procedures)
- Direct charitable activities
- Reasonable administrative expenses

## Self-Dealing Prohibitions

### Prohibited Transactions
- Sale, exchange, or lease of property
- Lending money or extending credit
- Furnishing goods, services, or facilities
- Paying compensation (unless reasonable)
- Transfer to, or use by, disqualified person

### Disqualified Persons
- Substantial contributors
- Foundation managers
- Family members of above
- Corporations controlled by above
- Partnerships with substantial disqualified ownership

See [[Excess Benefit Transactions]] for related rules applicable to all exempt organizations.

## Excess Business Holdings

Private foundations generally cannot hold more than 20% of a business enterprise, reduced by the holdings of all disqualified persons. See Section 4943(g) exception for independently operated businesses.

## Form 990-PF

Private foundations must file Form 990-PF annually, regardless of revenue:
- Reports income, expenses, and distributions
- Calculates excise taxes
- Lists grants and contributions
- Available for public inspection

See [[Form 990 Filing Requirements]] for more information.
