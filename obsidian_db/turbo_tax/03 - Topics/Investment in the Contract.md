# Investment in the Contract

## Overview

Your **investment in the contract** is the amount you use to calculate the tax-free portion of your annuity payments under the [[General Rule]]. It represents your net cost in the contract as of the [[Annuity Starting Date]].

## How to Calculate

### Step 1: Find Total Cost

Start with the total premiums, contributions, or other amounts paid, including:

- Amounts you actually contributed
- Amounts your employer contributed (if you were required to include them in income)
- Amounts paid for the contract purchase

### Step 2: Subtract Recovered Amounts

From total cost, subtract:

1. **Refunded premiums, rebates, dividends, or unrepaid loans** (not included in income) received by the later of:
   - The annuity starting date, or
   - The date you received your first payment

2. **Additional premiums paid for double indemnity or disability benefits**

3. **Any other tax-free amounts** received under the contract before the later of the dates in (1)

## Adjustments to Cost

### Foreign Employment Contributions

Special rules apply if you worked abroad:

- Contributions before 1963 by your employer
- Contributions after 1962 if excludable from gross income
- Contributions after 1996 for foreign missionary services

### Death Benefit Exclusion

If you are the beneficiary of a deceased employee who died **before August 21, 1996**, you may qualify for a death benefit exclusion of up to $5,000. Add this to your cost.

Note: No death benefit exclusion for deaths after August 20, 1996.

### Refund Feature Adjustment

If your annuity has a [[Refund Feature]], you must reduce your net cost by the value of the refund feature:

- Use Table III or VII from [[Pub 939 - General Rule for Pensions and Annuities]]
- The value is a percentage of the guaranteed amount

## Net Cost

Your total cost plus additions minus subtractions equals your **net cost**. This is your unrecovered investment in the contract as of the annuity starting date.

For annuity starting dates after 1986, this is the maximum amount you may recover tax-free under the contract.

## Relationship to Exclusion

```
Investment in Contract
──────────────────────── = Exclusion Percentage
    Expected Return
```

The higher your investment relative to expected return, the larger your tax-free portion.

## Examples

**Example 1**: At age 65, you bought an annuity for $21,053 with a refund feature. The value of the refund feature (from Table VII) is 15% of $21,053 = $3,158. Your investment in the contract is $17,895 ($21,053 - $3,158).

**Example 2**: Surviving spouse receives annuity. Deceased spouse contributed $25,576 and died before August 21, 1996. Add $5,000 death benefit exclusion. Adjusted investment = $30,576.
