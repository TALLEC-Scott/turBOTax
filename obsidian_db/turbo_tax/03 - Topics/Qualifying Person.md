# Qualifying Person

A person for whom you can claim the [[Child and Dependent Care Credit]] by paying for their care so you can work or look for work. Defined in [[Pub 503 - Child and Dependent Care Expenses]].

## Who is a Qualifying Person?

A qualifying person is one of the following:

### 1. Qualifying Child Under Age 13

Your [[Qualifying Child]] who:
- Is your dependent
- Was under age 13 when the care was provided

**Special rule for divorced/separated parents:** Even if you cannot claim your child as a dependent, they are treated as your qualifying person if you are the custodial parent (child lived with you for more nights during the year).

### 2. Spouse Physically or Mentally Unable to Care for Self

Your spouse who:
- Was not physically or mentally able to care for themselves
- Lived with you for more than half the year

### 3. Disabled Dependent or Would-Be Dependent

A person who:
- Was not physically or mentally able to care for themselves
- Lived with you for more than half the year
- AND either:
  - Was your dependent, OR
  - Would have been your dependent except that:
    - They had gross income of $5,200 or more (2025), OR
    - They filed a joint return, OR
    - You or your spouse could be claimed as a dependent on someone else's return

## Definition of "Not Able to Care for Self"

Persons who cannot dress, clean, or feed themselves due to physical or mental disabilities are considered not able to care for themselves. This also includes persons who must have constant attention to prevent them from injuring themselves or others.

## Part-Year Qualification

You determine a person's qualifying status **each day**.

### Child Turning 13
If your child turns 13 on September 16, only expenses through September 15 qualify.

### Birth or Death
A person born or who died during 2025 is treated as having lived with you for more than half the year if your home was their home more than half the time they were alive.

## Taxpayer Identification Number Required

You must include on your return the name and TIN (generally SSN) of each qualifying person. If information is incorrect, the credit may be reduced or disallowed.

### ITIN for Aliens
If your qualifying person is a nonresident or resident alien without an SSN, use their ITIN. Apply using Form W-7.

### ATIN for Adoptions
For a child placed with you for adoption without an SSN, get an ATIN using Form W-7A.

## Special Rules

### Child of Divorced or Separated Parents

Even if you cannot claim your child as a dependent, they are treated as your qualifying person if ALL of the following apply:

1. Child was under 13 or was not able to care for themselves
2. Child received over half their support from one or both parents who are:
   - Divorced or legally separated under decree of divorce/separate maintenance, OR
   - Separated under written separation agreement, OR
   - Lived apart at all times during the last 6 months of the year
3. Child was in custody of one or both parents for more than half the year
4. You were the **custodial parent** (the parent the child lived with for more nights)

**The noncustodial parent** cannot treat the child as a qualifying person even if they can claim the child as a dependent.

### Equal Nights Rule
If the child was with each parent for an equal number of nights, the custodial parent is the parent with the higher AGI.

## Key Differences from Other Dependent Rules

| For Child and Dependent Care Credit | For Other Tax Benefits |
|-------------------------------------|------------------------|
| Child must be under 13 | Child can be under 19 (or 24 if student) |
| Disabled spouse qualifies | Spouse never counts as a dependent |
| Person who "would be dependent" can qualify | Must actually be your dependent |
| Custodial parent always qualifies | Noncustodial parent can claim dependent |
