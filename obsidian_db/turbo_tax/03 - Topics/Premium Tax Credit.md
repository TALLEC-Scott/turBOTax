# Premium Tax Credit

## Overview

The Premium Tax Credit (PTC) is a refundable credit for eligible individuals and families who purchase health insurance through a Health Insurance Marketplace. The credit helps make coverage more affordable by subsidizing monthly premiums.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Must Meet ALL of the Following
1. **Purchase coverage** through a Health Insurance Marketplace
2. **Not eligible for other coverage:**
   - Employer-sponsored coverage (unless unaffordable)
   - Medicare, Medicaid, CHIP, TRICARE
   - Veterans health care
3. **Income within limits** (generally 100%-400% of federal poverty line)
4. **Cannot be claimed as a dependent** by another taxpayer
5. **Filing status** cannot be Married Filing Separately (exceptions exist)
6. **U.S. citizen or lawfully present** in the United States

### Income Limits (2025)
Premium tax credit is available for households with income:
- At least **100%** of federal poverty line (FPL)
- Up to **400%** of FPL (special rules may apply above 400%)

## How the Credit Works

### Two Options
1. **Advance Payments** - Credit paid monthly to insurance company
2. **Lump Sum** - Claim full credit when filing tax return

### Calculating the Credit
Based on:
- Household income as percentage of FPL
- Cost of "benchmark plan" (second-lowest-cost Silver plan)
- Required contribution percentage (based on income level)

### Required Contribution Percentages (2025)
Income as % of FPL determines required contribution:
- Lower income (100-150% FPL): Lower percentage
- Higher income (300-400% FPL): Higher percentage
- Above 400% FPL: May still qualify if benchmark plan exceeds percentage

## Advance Premium Tax Credit (APTC)

### How It Works
- Marketplace estimates credit based on projected income
- Payments sent directly to insurance company monthly
- Reduces monthly premium amount you pay
- Can be taken in any amount (partial or full)

### Reporting Changes
Must report changes to Marketplace within 30 days:
- Income increase or decrease
- Change in household size
- Change in marital status
- Change in eligibility for other coverage

### Year-End Reconciliation
- File Form 8962 with tax return
- Compare actual income to estimated income
- May owe money if received too much advance credit
- May receive additional refund if received too little

## Reconciling the Credit

### Form 8962
Use Form 8962 (Premium Tax Credit) to:
1. Calculate actual premium tax credit
2. Compare to advance payments received
3. Report excess advance payments or additional credit

### Possible Outcomes

#### Received Less Than Entitled
- Claim additional credit on tax return
- Increases refund or reduces tax owed

#### Received More Than Entitled
- Must repay excess advance payments
- May be subject to repayment caps

### Repayment Caps (2025)
Limits on repayment if income is below 400% FPL:
- Lower income: Lower cap
- Higher income: Higher cap
- Above 400% FPL: Must repay full excess amount

## Withholding Considerations

### Form W-4 Adjustments
If receiving advance payments:
- May want to increase withholding to cover potential repayment
- Use Form W-4 Step 4(c) for additional withholding
- Consider using IRS Tax Withholding Estimator

### Estimated Tax Payments
- May need to make estimated payments if:
  - Repayment is likely
  - Not enough withholding from wages
- Include potential PTC repayment in calculations

## Affordability Safe Harbor

### Employer Coverage Exception
Can claim PTC if employer coverage is "unaffordable":
- Employee share of premium exceeds **9.02%** (2025) of household income
- For family coverage: Use employee-only cost for this calculation

### Minimum Value
Employer plan must provide minimum value:
- At least 60% of total allowed costs
- Substantial coverage for physician and inpatient services

## Special Situations

### Marriage or Divorce
- Report change to Marketplace
- May affect credit calculation
- File Form 8962 with proper allocation

### Moving to Different State
- Report change to Marketplace
- Different states have different plans and premiums
- May affect credit amount

### Change in Immigration Status
- Report change to Marketplace
- May affect eligibility

## Filing Requirements

### Must File Tax Return
If you received advance payments, you MUST:
- File a tax return
- File Form 8962
- Reconcile advance payments with actual credit

### Failure to File
- May lose eligibility for future advance payments
- Marketplace may adjust future payments
- Must repay any excess advance payments

## Forms and Documentation

| Form | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Form 8962 | Premium Tax Credit calculation |
| Form 1095-A | Health Insurance Marketplace Statement |

- Received from Marketplace by late January
- Shows coverage months, premiums, and advance payments
- Required to file Form 8962
