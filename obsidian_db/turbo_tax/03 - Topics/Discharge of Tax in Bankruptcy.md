# Discharge of Tax in Bankruptcy

## Overview

The bankruptcy discharge is a permanent court injunction prohibiting collection of certain debts as personal liability of the debtor. However, many tax debts are **excepted from discharge**—the scope depends on the bankruptcy chapter and nature of the debt.

## Chapter 7 Discharge (Individuals Only)

Only individuals may receive a discharge in Chapter 7; corporations and other entities cannot.

### Non-Dischargeable Taxes (Chapter 7)

The following taxes (including interest) are **not dischargeable**:

| Tax Type | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Eighth priority taxes | Income taxes meeting priority rules |
| Late-filed returns | Return filed after 2 years before bankruptcy petition |
| No return filed | Taxes for which no return was ever filed |
| Fraudulent return | Taxes where fraudulent return was filed |
| Willful evasion | Taxes the debtor willfully attempted to evade or defeat |

### Penalties

Penalties are dischargeable unless:
- Event giving rise to penalty occurred within 3 years of bankruptcy
- Penalty relates to a non-dischargeable tax

## Chapter 11 Discharge

### Individuals

Same exceptions to discharge as Chapter 7 (see above).

### Corporations

Corporations may receive a broad discharge when the reorganization plan is confirmed, but:
- Secured and priority claims must be satisfied under the plan
- Exception: Taxes where debtor filed fraudulent return or willfully attempted to evade

## Chapter 13 Discharge

Debtors completing all payments under the Chapter 13 plan receive a broad discharge of all debts provided by the plan. However, priority tax claims must be paid in full.

### Non-Dischargeable Taxes (Chapter 13)

| Tax Type | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Withholding taxes | Liable in any capacity |
| No return filed | Must have filed return |
| Late-filed returns | Filed after 2 years before petition |
| Fraudulent return | Fraud issues |
| Willful evasion | Evasion issues |

### Hardship Discharge

If failure to complete payments is due to circumstances beyond debtor's control, court may grant hardship discharge:
- Unsecured claims must receive at least what they would in Chapter 7
- Chapter 7 non-dischargeable debts are also excepted

## Chapter 12 Discharge

Same exceptions as Chapter 7 for individuals. Exceptions don't apply to Chapter 12 cases of non-individuals (family farmers that are corporations or partnerships).

## Eighth Priority Tax Claims

Understanding priority status is critical because eighth priority taxes are generally non-dischargeable.

### What Qualifies as Eighth Priority?

1. **Income taxes** for tax year ending on or before petition date, with return last due (including extensions) within 3 years of petition
2. **Income taxes** assessed within 240 days before petition (exclusive of offer in compromise time and stay periods)
3. **Income taxes** not assessed but assessable as of petition date
4. **Withholding taxes** incurred in any capacity
5. **Employer's share of employment taxes** on priority wages or returns due within 3 years of petition
6. **Excise taxes** on transactions before petition, with return due within 3 years of petition

## Federal Tax Liens After Discharge

Even if tax is discharged, it may still be collectible from pre-bankruptcy property:

| Situation | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| NFTL filed before bankruptcy | Lien passes through bankruptcy; property remains subject to lien |
| No NFTL filed, property exempted | Tax lien removed from exempted property |
| No NFTL, property excluded/abandoned | Tax lien may remain on property |

## Payment of Priority Tax Claims

### Chapter 7

Eighth priority taxes paid from estate assets after secured creditors and higher-priority claims.

### Chapter 11

Plan may provide payment over 5 years from order for relief, with post-confirmation interest, in manner not less favorable than most favored non-priority claims.

### Chapter 12

Deferred cash payments over time. Exception: Unsecured priority tax from sale of farm assets treated as non-priority unsecured claim.

### Chapter 13

Payment over 3 years (or 5 years with court approval).
