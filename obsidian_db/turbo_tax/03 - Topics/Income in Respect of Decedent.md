# Income in Respect of Decedent

## Overview

Income in Respect of a Decedent (IRD) is income that the decedent was entitled to receive but did not receive before death. This income is included in the decedent's gross estate for estate tax purposes and is taxable to the recipient when received.

## What Qualifies as IRD

### Examples of IRD
- Unpaid wages, salaries, and commissions
- Uncollected accounts receivable (cash method)
- Accrued but unpaid interest
- Deferred compensation
- Traditional IRA distributions
- Pension payments due but not received
- Installment sale payments due after death
- Partnership income not yet distributed

### Not IRD
- Property that generates income (only the income itself is IRD)
- Income properly reported on decedent's final return

## Tax Treatment

### Double Taxation Issue
IRD is subject to two taxes:
1. **Estate Tax**: Included in gross estate
2. **Income Tax**: Taxable to recipient when received

### Estate Tax Deduction
To prevent double taxation, recipients can deduct:
- Estate tax attributable to IRD
- Deductible on income tax return for same year IRD is reported
- Individuals: Itemized deduction on Schedule A, line 16
- Estates: Deduction on Form 1041, line 19

## Calculation of Estate Tax Deduction

### Formula
```
IRD Value     Total Estate Tax
─────────── × ────────────────── = Estate Tax Deduction
Total IRD     Qualifying for Deduction
```

### Steps
1. Determine total IRD in estate
2. Calculate estate tax with and without IRD
3. Difference = estate tax attributable to IRD
4. Allocate proportionally to each IRD item

## Who Reports IRD

### Estate Receives IRD
- Report on Form 1041
- Deduct if distributed to beneficiaries (income distribution deduction)

### Beneficiary Receives IRD
- Report on beneficiary's income tax return
- Beneficiary claims estate tax deduction

### Surviving Annuity Recipient
- Annuity payments to survivor are IRD
- Special computation required for estate tax deduction
- See Regulations section 1.691(d)-1

## Specific Types of IRD

### IRAs and Retirement Plans
- Traditional IRA balances are IRD
- Roth IRAs are generally NOT IRD (after-tax contributions)
- Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) not taken before death

### Installment Obligations
- Remaining payments on installment sales are IRD
- Beneficiary continues reporting as decedent would have

### Partnership and S Corporation Income
- Distributive share through date of death on final return
- Post-death income may be IRD to estate or beneficiary

## Deductions in Respect of Decedent

Expenses the decedent owed but weren't deductible on final return:
- Business expenses
- Income-producing expenses
- Interest and taxes owed

These are deductible by:
- The estate, OR
- The person who acquired the interest subject to the obligation
