# Religious Workers

## Overview

Religious workers (church employees) are individuals who work for churches or qualified church-controlled organizations but are not ministers, members of religious orders, or Christian Science practitioners. They have unique Social Security coverage rules that differ from other clergy classifications.

## Definition

Religious workers are church employees who are not in one of the clergy classifications:
- Not a minister (ordained, commissioned, or licensed)
- Not a member of a religious order
- Not a Christian Science practitioner or reader

Examples include:
- Church administrative staff
- Church music directors (who are not ordained/commissioned ministers)
- Church maintenance workers
- Other church support staff

## Social Security Coverage

Religious workers may be covered under either FICA or SECA depending on employer elections.

### Default Coverage (FICA)

Generally, religious workers are covered under FICA:
- Employer withholds employee's share (7.65%)
- Employer pays employer's share (7.65%)
- Total: 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare)

### Election to Exclude from FICA

Churches and qualified church-controlled organizations can elect to exclude their employees from FICA coverage by filing [[Form 8274]].

**Reasons for election:**
- Religious opposition to payment of Social Security and Medicare taxes

**Effect of election:**
- Employer doesn't pay employer's portion of FICA
- Employer doesn't withhold employee's portion of FICA
- Employee's wages become subject to SECA instead

## When Church Elects Exclusion

If employer files [[Form 8274]]:

| Aspect | Treatment |
|--------|-----------|
| FICA | Not applicable |
| SECA | Employee must pay SE tax on wages |
| Threshold | Wages over $108.28 during tax year |

### Employee SE Tax Obligations

1. File [[Schedule SE]] with tax return
2. Pay full 15.3% SE tax on church wages
3. May need to make estimated tax payments

### Exemption for Religious Sect Members

If you are a member of a recognized religious sect:
- May qualify for exemption from both FICA and SECA
- File [[Form 4029]]
- See [[SE Tax Exemption]] for details

## Filing Requirements

Even if income is below normal filing thresholds, you MUST file if:

| Situation | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Not exempt from SE tax | Net earnings from self-employment of $400+ |
| Church wages from electing employer | Wages of $108.28+ |
| SE tax exempt but have other SE income | Other net earnings of $400+ |

## Income Tax Withholding

Unlike ministers, religious workers generally have income tax withheld from wages under normal FICA rules.

If employer elected FICA exclusion:
- Income tax withholding may still apply
- Can arrange voluntary withholding agreement with church
- May need to make estimated tax payments

## Retirement Plan Participation

Religious workers who are common-law employees may participate in:
- Church retirement plans
- Tax-sheltered annuity (403(b)) plans
- SEP or SIMPLE plans (if self-employed for other work)

See [[Retirement Plans for Clergy]] for details.

## Employee vs. Self-Employed Status

Use common-law rules to determine status:
- **Employee**: Church controls what you do AND how you do it
- **Self-employed**: You control your own work methods

If uncertain, file [[Form SS-8]] for IRS determination.

## Comparison Table

| Classification | FICA | SECA | Can Claim Exemption |
|----------------|------|------|---------------------|
| Religious worker (normal) | Yes | No | No (unless religious sect member) |
| Religious worker (employer elected out) | No | Yes | Yes (if religious sect member via Form 4029) |
| Minister | No | Yes | Yes (via Form 4361) |
| Religious order member | Varies | Varies | Yes (via Form 4361) |
