# Health Savings Accounts (HSAs)

## Overview

A Health Savings Account (HSA) is a tax-exempt trust or custodial account established with a qualified trustee to pay or reimburse qualified medical expenses. HSAs offer a triple tax advantage: contributions are tax-deductible, earnings grow tax-free, and distributions for qualified medical expenses are tax-free. See [[Pub 969 - Health Savings Accounts and Other Tax-Favored Health Plans]].

## Eligibility Requirements

To be an eligible individual and qualify for HSA contributions, you must meet ALL of the following requirements:

1. **HDHP Coverage** - Covered under a [[High Deductible Health Plans|High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP)]] on the first day of the month
2. **No Other Coverage** - Have no other health coverage except permitted coverage (see Other Health Coverage below)
3. **Not Enrolled in Medicare** - Cannot be enrolled in Medicare
4. **Not a Dependent** - Cannot be claimed as a dependent on someone else's tax return

## Benefits of an HSA

- Tax deduction for contributions (even if not itemizing)
- Employer contributions excluded from gross income
- Contributions remain in account until used
- Tax-free earnings on assets
- Tax-free distributions for qualified medical expenses
- Portable - stays with you if you change employers

## Contribution Limits (2025)

| Coverage Type | Under Age 55 | Age 55+ (Catch-up) |
|--------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Self-only | $4,300 | $5,300 |
| Family | $8,550 | $9,550 |

See [[HSA Contribution Limits]] for detailed rules and calculations.

## High Deductible Health Plan Requirements

An HDHP must meet the following for 2025:

| Requirement | Self-Only | Family |
|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Minimum Annual Deductible | $1,650 | $3,300 |
| Maximum Out-of-Pocket | $8,300 | $16,600 |

See [[High Deductible Health Plans]] for complete HDHP rules.

## Other Health Coverage

You can still be eligible for an HSA if you have:

**Permitted Insurance:**
- Workers' compensation
- Specific disease or illness coverage
- Fixed amount per day of hospitalization

**Permitted Coverage:**
- Accidents
- Dental care
- Disability
- Vision care
- Telehealth and other remote care (effective for plan years after 2024)
- Long-term care

## Special Rules

### Last-Month Rule
If you are eligible on December 1, you are treated as eligible for the entire year. Subject to testing period through December 31 of the following year. See [[Last-Month Rule]].

### Catch-up Contributions
Individuals age 55 or older may contribute an additional $1,000 per year. Each spouse must have their own HSA to make catch-up contributions.

### Qualified HSA Funding Distribution
One-time tax-free transfer from an IRA to an HSA, limited to the contribution limit. Cannot be from an ongoing SEP IRA or SIMPLE IRA.

### Rollovers
- Can roll over from Archer MSAs and other HSAs
- Must complete within 60 days
- Only one rollover per 1-year period
- Trustee-to-trustee transfers are not rollovers (unlimited)

## Distributions

### Qualified Medical Expenses
Tax-free distributions for [[Qualified Medical Expenses]] incurred after establishing the HSA. Expenses for you, your spouse, and dependents qualify.

### Insurance Premiums
HSA funds can be used for:
- Long-term care insurance (subject to age-based limits)
- Health care continuation coverage (COBRA)
- Health coverage while receiving unemployment compensation
- Medicare and other health coverage if 65 or older (not Medigap)

### Non-Qualified Distributions
Subject to:
- Income tax on distribution amount
- 20% additional tax (exceptions: disability, age 65+, death)

### Recordkeeping Requirements
Must maintain records showing:
- Distributions were for qualified medical expenses
- Expenses were not previously reimbursed
- Medical expenses were not deducted on Schedule A

## Excess Contributions

Contributions exceeding limits:
- 6% excise tax per year excess remains in account
- Can withdraw excess by due date (including extensions) to avoid tax
- Must withdraw earnings on excess and report as income

## Death of HSA Holder

- **Spouse beneficiary:** HSA becomes spouse's HSA
- **Non-spouse beneficiary:** Account ceases to be HSA; FMV taxable to beneficiary
- **Estate beneficiary:** FMV included on final income tax return
