# Real Property Basis

## Overview

Real property (real estate) includes land and generally anything built on or attached to it. Special rules apply when determining the basis of real property for tax purposes.

## Settlement Costs

### Costs to Include in Basis
Your basis includes settlement fees and closing costs for buying property:

- Abstract fees (abstract of title fees)
- Legal fees (title search, sales contract, deed preparation)
- Charges for installing utility services
- Recording fees
- Transfer taxes
- Surveys
- Owner's title insurance
- Amounts seller owes that you agree to pay (back taxes, interest, recording fees, improvements, sales commissions)

### Costs NOT to Include in Basis

**Pre-occupancy costs (deductible as business expenses if business property):**
1. Casualty insurance premiums
2. Rent for occupancy before closing
3. Charges for utilities or services before closing

**Loan-related costs (capitalize and deduct over loan term):**
4a. Points (discount points, loan origination fees)
4b. Mortgage insurance premiums
4c. Loan assumption fees
4d. Credit report costs
4e. Appraisal fees required by lender
5. Refinancing fees

## Real Estate Taxes

### Taxes Seller Owed
If you pay real estate taxes the seller owed and weren't reimbursed, include in your basis. You can't deduct them as taxes.

### Taxes Seller Paid for You
If seller paid your taxes and you didn't reimburse them, reduce your basis by that amount. If you reimbursed the seller, deduct as expense in year of purchase.

## Points

### General Rule
Don't add points to basis of related property. Generally deduct points over the term of the loan.

### Home Mortgage Points
Special rules may allow full deduction in year paid if certain requirements are met. Reduce home basis by any seller-paid points.

## Mortgage Assumption

If you buy property and assume an existing mortgage, your basis includes:
- Amount paid for the property, PLUS
- Amount to be paid on the mortgage

**Example:** Buy building for $20,000 cash, assume $80,000 mortgage → Basis = $100,000

## Allocating Land and Buildings

When you buy buildings and land together for a lump sum, allocate basis between them:

**Formula:**
- Basis of each asset = Lump sum × (FMV of that asset ÷ FMV of whole property)

If FMV is uncertain, use assessed values for real estate tax purposes.

## Demolition of Building

- Add demolition costs and losses to basis of the land
- Don't claim as current deduction

**Exception - Modification vs. Demolition:**
If both conditions are met, costs are added to building basis:
- 75%+ of internal structural framework retained
- 75%+ of external walls retained (as internal or external walls)

For certified historic structures, modification must be part of certified rehabilitation.

## Subdivided Lots

If you buy land and subdivide it:
- Determine basis of each lot separately
- Basis per lot = Total cost × (FMV of lot ÷ FMV of entire tract)
- Gain/loss figured separately for each lot sold
- Can't correct basis errors for closed tax years

## Constructing Real Property

Basis includes:
- Land costs
- Architect's fees
- Labor and materials
- Building permit charges
- Equipment rental
- Contractor payments
- Inspection fees
- Employee wages for construction work
- Depreciation on equipment used in construction

**Don't include:** Value of your own unpaid labor
