# Tax Computation Worksheet

The Tax Computation Worksheet is used to calculate tax liability when taxable income is $100,000 or more. Unlike the [[Tax Tables]], which list tax amounts directly, the worksheet applies marginal tax rates to compute tax.

## When to Use

Use the Tax Computation Worksheet when:
- Taxable income (Form 1040, line 15) is **$100,000 or more**
- You cannot or choose not to use the [[Tax Tables]]

## How It Works

The worksheet applies the marginal tax rate system:
1. Divide taxable income into portions based on tax brackets
2. Apply the appropriate rate to each portion
3. Sum the results to get total tax

### 2025 Tax Brackets by Filing Status

| Rate | Single | MFJ | MFS | Head of Household |
|------|--------|-----|-----|-------------------|
| 10% | $0 - $11,925 | $0 - $23,850 | $0 - $11,925 | $0 - $17,000 |
| 12% | $11,925 - $48,475 | $23,850 - $96,950 | $11,925 - $48,475 | $17,000 - $64,850 |
| 22% | $48,475 - $103,350 | $96,950 - $206,700 | $48,475 - $103,350 | $64,850 - $137,700 |
| 24% | $103,350 - $209,425 | $206,700 - $418,850 | $103,350 - $209,425 | $137,700 - $279,450 |
| 32% | $209,425 - $397,275 | $418,850 - $462,500 | $209,425 - $231,250 | $279,450 - $439,300 |
| 35% | $397,275 - $631,075 | $462,500 - $693,750 | $231,250 - $346,875 | $439,300 - $525,900 |
| 37% | Over $631,075 | Over $693,750 | Over $346,875 | Over $525,900 |

## Worksheet Steps

The worksheet typically follows this structure:

1. **Enter taxable income** from Form 1040, line 15
2. **Find your bracket** based on filing status
3. **Calculate tax** using the appropriate formula:
   - For each bracket portion, multiply by the rate
   - Add the base tax from lower brackets

### Example Calculation

Single filer with $150,000 taxable income (2025):
- 10% on first $11,925: $1,193
- 12% on $11,925 to $48,475: $4,386
- 22% on $48,475 to $103,350: $12,072
- 24% on $103,350 to $150,000: $11,196
- **Total tax**: $28,847

## Special Situations

### Qualified Dividends and Capital Gains

If you have qualified dividends or capital gains, use the **Qualified Dividends and Capital Gain Tax Worksheet** instead, as these are taxed at preferential rates (0%, 15%, or 20%).

### Alternative Minimum Tax

High-income taxpayers may need to calculate AMT using Form 6251. The AMT system has different rates and brackets.

### Net Investment Income Tax

Taxpayers with modified AGI above threshold amounts may owe an additional 3.8% NIIT on net investment income.
