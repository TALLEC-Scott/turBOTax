# Trump Accounts

Trump Accounts are a new type of Individual Retirement Account established by recent legislation (2025) for the benefit of certain children. This topic covers eligibility, contributions, and reporting requirements.

## Overview

Trump Accounts are a new type of IRA that parents, guardians, and other authorized individuals can establish for children. The program includes a government pilot contribution of $1,000.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Child Requirements
To be eligible for a Trump Account, the child must:
1. **Born after 2024 and before 2029** (years 2025-2028)
2. **U.S. citizen**
3. Meet any other requirements specified in regulations

### Who Can Establish
- Parents
- Guardians
- Other authorized individuals

## Pilot Program Contribution

### $1,000 Government Contribution
Eligible children can receive a **$1,000 pilot program contribution** to their Trump Account.

### How to Claim
- File **Form 4547** to make the election
- Can be filed with the authorized individual's 2025 income tax return
- Must meet all eligibility requirements

### Timing
- The election can be made when filing the 2025 return
- Both the account establishment election and pilot contribution election can be made on the same Form 4547

## Establishing a Trump Account

### Required Form
Use **Form 4547** to:
1. Elect to establish a Trump Account for an eligible child
2. Elect to receive the $1,000 pilot program contribution

### Filing Options
- File Form 4547 with your income tax return, OR
- File Form 4547 separately (follow Form 4547 instructions)

## Tax Treatment

As a type of IRA, Trump Accounts follow many standard IRA rules:
- Earnings grow tax-deferred
- Distributions may be tax-free if qualified
- Early distribution penalties may apply

**Note:** Specific distribution and taxation rules for Trump Accounts are detailed in Form 4547 instructions and forthcoming IRS guidance.

## Key Differences from Traditional/Roth IRAs

| Feature | Trump Account | Traditional/Roth IRA |
|---------|---------------|---------------------|
| Who can establish | Parents/guardians for children | Individuals for themselves |
| Age eligibility | Children born 2025-2028 | Any age with compensation |
| Government contribution | $1,000 pilot program | None |
| Contribution source | Government + authorized individuals | Individual's compensation |
| Required form | Form 4547 | Standard IRA application |

## Filing Requirements

### Form 4547
The primary form for Trump Accounts:
- Elect to establish the account
- Elect to receive pilot contribution
- Report required information about the child

### Timing
- Can be filed with the 2025 income tax return
- Check Form 4547 instructions for specific deadlines

## Related Legislation

Trump Accounts were established by recent legislation (P.L. 119-21, the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act") enacted July 4, 2025. The legislation created:
- The Trump Account as a new IRA type
- The $1,000 pilot program contribution for eligible children
- Form 4547 for making elections

## Future Years

The program currently covers children born:
- 2025
- 2026
- 2027
- 2028

Check IRS.gov/OBBB for updates on program extensions or modifications.

## Planning Considerations

### For Parents/Guardians
1. **File Form 4547** with your 2025 return for children born in 2025
2. **Keep records** of the account establishment
3. **Monitor for updates** as IRS issues additional guidance
4. **Consider other savings vehicles** like 529 plans alongside Trump Accounts

### Coordination with Other Accounts
- Trump Accounts are separate from 529 education savings plans
- May complement education savings strategies
- Consider overall college and retirement savings plan
