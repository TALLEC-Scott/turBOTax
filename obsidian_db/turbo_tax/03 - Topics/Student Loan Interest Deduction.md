# Student Loan Interest Deduction

## Overview

The Student Loan Interest Deduction allows you to deduct up to **$2,500** of interest paid on a qualified student loan. This is an **above-the-line deduction**, meaning you can claim it even if you don't itemize deductions.

**Authority:** [[Pub 970 - Tax Benefits for Education#Chapter 4]]

## Key Requirements

### Loan Requirements
- Taken out solely to pay qualified education expenses
- For you, your spouse, or your dependent at the time borrowed
- Cannot be from a related person or qualified employer plan

### Student Requirements
- Enrolled at least half-time in a program leading to a degree, certificate, or recognized credential
- At an eligible educational institution

### Taxpayer Requirements
- MAGI below $100,000 ($200,000 if married filing jointly)
- Cannot be claimed as a dependent on another's return
- If married filing separately, cannot claim deduction

## Income Limits (2025)

| Filing Status | Phase-Out Begins | Phase-Out Ends |
|---------------|------------------|----------------|
| Single, HoH, QSS | $85,000 | $100,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $170,000 | $200,000 |

## Maximum Deduction

| MAGI Range | Maximum Deduction |
|------------|-------------------|
| Below phase-out | $2,500 |
| Within phase-out | Reduced proportionally |
| Above phase-out | $0 |

## Qualified Education Expenses

For purposes of this deduction, qualified expenses include:
- Tuition and fees
- Room and board
- Books, supplies, and equipment
- Other necessary expenses (transportation, etc.)

## What is Student Loan Interest?

- Interest paid during the tax year on a qualified student loan
- Includes both required and voluntary interest payments
- Includes loan origination fees (deductible over loan term)
- Includes capitalized interest

## Loan Origination Fees

Treated as interest and deducted ratably over the term of the loan.

## Exceptions to Deduction

### No Double Benefit
Cannot deduct interest paid by your employer after March 27, 2020, under an educational assistance program.

### Related Person Loans
Cannot deduct interest on loans from:
- Spouse
- Siblings (including half-siblings)
- Ancestors (parents, grandparents)
- Lineal descendants (children, grandchildren)
- Certain corporations, partnerships, and trusts

### Employer Plan Loans
Cannot deduct interest on loans from qualified employer plans (401(k), etc.)

## Form Required

- [[Form 1040]] - Schedule 1, Line 13
- No separate form needed (no itemizing required)
- [[Form 1098-E]] - Student Loan Interest Statement (received from lender)

## Common Questions

### Q: Can I deduct interest on a loan for my child's education?
**A:** Yes, if you are legally obligated to pay the loan, or if you are the borrower and your child was your dependent when the loan was taken out.

### Q: What if my employer pays my student loan?
**A:** Employer payments up to $5,250 under an educational assistance program are tax-free to you, but you cannot deduct them as interest.

### Q: What if I refinance my student loan?
**A:** The refinanced loan qualifies if used solely to pay education expenses of the original qualified loan.
