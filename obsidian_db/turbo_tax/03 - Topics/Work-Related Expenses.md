# Work-Related Expenses

Expenses paid for the care of a [[Qualifying Person]] that allow you (and your spouse if filing jointly) to work or look for work. These expenses qualify for the [[Child and Dependent Care Credit]]. Covered in [[Pub 503 - Child and Dependent Care Expenses]].

## Requirements

Expenses are work-related only if BOTH are true:

1. They allow you (or your spouse if filing jointly) to work or look for work
2. They are for a [[Qualifying Person]]'s care

## Working or Looking for Work

### What Counts as Work
- Employment for others or in your own business/partnership
- Full-time or part-time work
- Work in or out of your home
- **Actively looking for work** (but if you don't find a job and have no earned income, you can't take the credit)

### What Doesn't Count as Work
- Unpaid volunteer work
- Work for a nominal salary

### Temporary Absence
Short absences (2 weeks or less) from work for vacation or minor illness don't require daily expense allocation if you must still pay for care.

**Longer absences** (like 4 months for illness) generally mean expenses are NOT work-related.

### Part-Time Work
If you work part-time, you generally must figure expenses for each day worked. However, if you're required to pay for care on days you don't work (e.g., weekly rate), you can include those expenses.

**Any day you work at least 1 hour counts as a work day.**

## Types of Qualifying Expenses

### Care Expenses

| Type | Qualifies? | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| Babysitter/nanny | Yes | For care during work hours |
| Daycare center | Yes | Must comply with state/local regulations |
| Dependent care center | Yes | Must comply with regulations if cares for 6+ persons |
| Nursery school/preschool | Yes | Below kindergarten level |
| Before/after-school care | Yes | For kindergarten and above |
| Day camp | Yes | Even if specialized (computers, sports) |
| Overnight camp | No | Not considered work-related |
| Summer school/tutoring | No | Not for care |
| Kindergarten and above | No | Education, not care |

### Household Services

Services that are partly for the care of a qualifying person qualify, including:
- Cook, maid, babysitter, housekeeper, cleaning person

**Don't qualify:** Chauffeur, bartender, gardener

**Housekeeper lodging/meals:** You can count extra costs for providing lodging and meals to a housekeeper whose services include care of a qualifying person.

### Transportation

- Transportation by care provider for qualifying person: **Yes**
- Your transportation costs: **No**
- Transportation costs for provider to come to your home: **No**

### Fees and Deposits

| Type | Qualifies? |
|------|------------|
| Agency fees to get a provider | Yes |
| Preschool deposits | Yes |
| Application fees | Yes |
| Forfeited deposits | No |

### Taxes on Wages

Taxes you pay on wages for qualifying child and dependent care services ARE work-related expenses.

## Expenses That Don't Qualify

- Food, lodging, clothing, education, entertainment (unless incidental and inseparable from care)
- Child support payments
- Kindergarten tuition and above
- Summer school and tutoring programs

## Care Outside Your Home

You can count care outside your home if it's for:
- Your dependent under age 13, OR
- Any qualifying person who regularly spends at least 8 hours each day in your home

## Payments to Relatives

| Paid To | Can Count? |
|---------|------------|
| Relative who is NOT your dependent | Yes |
| Person you CAN claim as dependent | No |
| Your child under age 19 | No (even if not your dependent) |
| Your spouse | No |
| Other parent of your qualifying child (if child under 13) | No |

## Allocating Expenses

When expenses are partly work-related and partly for other purposes, you must divide the expense. Count only the work-related portion.

**Exception:** You don't have to divide if only a small part is for other purposes.

### Example
A housekeeper cares for children and also spends 30 minutes daily driving you to work. The driving is minimal, so the entire expense can be work-related.

## Timing of Expenses

| When Paid | When Counted |
|-----------|--------------|
| In 2025 for 2025 care | 2025 credit |
| In 2025 for 2024 care | 2025 credit (may be additional credit) |
| In 2024 for 2025 care | 2025 credit (when care received) |
| Prepaid before services | Year care is actually received |

## Reimbursed Expenses

If expenses are reimbursed (by employer or state social services agency), you cannot count the reimbursed portion.

**Medical expenses:** Expenses that qualify for both the credit AND medical expense deduction can be used either way, but not both.
