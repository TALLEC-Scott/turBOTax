# Foreign Housing Exclusion and Deduction

## Overview

In addition to the [[Foreign Earned Income Exclusion]], U.S. citizens and resident aliens living abroad may exclude or deduct certain housing costs. This benefit recognizes that housing expenses in foreign locations can significantly exceed costs in the United States.

**Two Types of Benefits:**
- **Housing Exclusion** - For employer-provided amounts (employees)
- **Housing Deduction** - For self-employment earnings (self-employed)

## Eligibility Requirements

You must meet ALL of the following:

1. Have a [[Tax Home]] in a foreign country
2. Qualify under either the [[Bona Fide Residence Test]] OR [[Physical Presence Test]]
3. Have foreign earned income
4. File Form 2555 with your tax return

## Housing Amount Calculation

Your housing amount is calculated as:

```
Housing Amount = Housing Expenses - Base Housing Amount
```

### Base Housing Amount

The base housing amount is **16% of the maximum foreign earned income exclusion**, calculated on a daily basis.

**Formula:**
```
Base Amount = (Maximum FEIE ÷ 365 days) × Days in Qualifying Period
```

This represents the portion of housing costs the IRS expects you to pay from your own income.

## Housing Expenses

### Eligible Expenses
- Rent
- Utilities (electricity, gas, water)
- Real and personal property insurance
- Occupancy taxes
- Nonrefundable fees for securing a lease
- Residential parking
- Household repairs
- Furniture rental

### NOT Eligible
- Purchase price of property
- Capital improvements
- Mortgage principal payments
- Depreciation
- Value of meals/lodging provided by employer
- Expenses reimbursed by employer
- Expenses for U.S. residence

## Limit on Housing Expenses

### Standard Limit
Maximum eligible housing expenses are generally **30% of the maximum FEIE** (calculated on a daily basis), multiplied by the number of qualifying days.

### High-Cost Localities
Higher limits apply for designated high-cost locations. Limits are listed in the Instructions for Form 2555.

**Examples of High-Cost Locations:**
- Hong Kong
- Singapore
- Tokyo
- London
- Geneva
- Various cities in the Middle East

## Foreign Housing Exclusion (Employees)

If you are an employee receiving employer-provided amounts, you may **exclude** your housing amount from income.

### Employer-Provided Amounts Include
- Salary
- Housing allowances
- Reimbursements for housing expenses
- Fair rental value of employer-provided housing
- Tax equalization payments
- Education allowances for dependents

### Limitation
Your exclusion is limited to the lesser of:
1. Your housing amount, OR
2. Your foreign earned income

## Foreign Housing Deduction (Self-Employed)

If you are self-employed, you may **deduct** your housing amount on Schedule 1 (Form 1040), line 24j.

### Mixed Income Situations
If you have both employer-provided and self-employment income:
1. Calculate the portion related to employer-provided amounts (exclusion)
2. Calculate the portion related to self-employment income (deduction)

**Formula for Split:**
```
Excludable Amount = Housing Amount × (Employer Income ÷ Total Foreign Earned Income)
Deductible Amount = Housing Amount - Excludable Amount
```

## Special Situations

### Second Foreign Household
If you maintain a second household for your spouse/dependents because conditions near your tax home are dangerous or unhealthful, those expenses may be included.

**Limitations:**
- Only one second foreign household at a time
- Must be outside the United States
- Must be due to adverse conditions

### Meals and Lodging Exclusion
If your employer provides meals and lodging that meet specific requirements, you can exclude their value from income. These amounts are NOT considered housing expenses.

**Requirements:**
- Meals provided on business premises for employer's convenience
- Lodging provided on business premises for employer's convenience, as condition of employment

## Claiming the Benefit

### Form Required
File **Form 2555** with your Form 1040.

**Note:** You must calculate the housing exclusion/deduction BEFORE calculating your foreign earned income exclusion.

### Carryover
Unused housing deductions may be carried forward to the next tax year.

## Interaction with Other Benefits

### Cannot Claim Both Exclusion and Credit
You cannot take a foreign tax credit or deduction for foreign taxes on income excluded as foreign housing amounts.

### Excluded Income Deductions
If you claim the housing exclusion:
- Deductions related to excluded income are not allowed
- Itemized deductions must be reduced proportionally
- Use Form 2555, line 44, to report deductions attributable to excluded income

## Married Couples

If both spouses qualify:
- Each can claim the housing benefit separately
- Each needs their own qualifying income
- Cannot double-count the same housing expenses

If only one spouse qualifies, that spouse can claim the housing benefit for household expenses.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Including ineligible expenses** (purchase price, improvements)
2. **Not reducing for employer reimbursements**
3. **Forgetting to calculate housing exclusion first** before FEIE
4. **Missing the Form 2555 filing requirement**
5. **Not using high-cost locality limits** when available
