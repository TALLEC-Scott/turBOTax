# Third-Party Payer Arrangements

## Overview

Employers may outsource payroll and tax duties to third-party payers. Understanding the different arrangements and their implications for tax liability is critical.

## Types of Third-Party Payers

### 1. Payroll Service Provider (PSP)
- Processes payroll and calculates taxes
- Files returns using **employer's EIN**
- Makes deposits on employer's behalf
- **Employer remains fully liable** for taxes

### 2. Reporting Agent
- Type of PSP authorized by Form 8655
- Can electronically sign and file certain forms
- Makes deposits using **employer's EIN**
- **Employer remains liable** for all taxes
- Not liable as employer for employment taxes

### 3. Section 3504 Agent (Form 2678)
- Authorized to perform employer functions
- Files returns using **agent's own EIN**
- Files Schedule R to allocate wages/taxes to each employer
- **Agent and employer are jointly liable** for employment taxes
- Must deposit taxes using EFTPS

### 4. Certified Professional Employer Organization (CPEO)
- IRS-certified PEO under section 7705
- Treated as **employer for wages it pays**
- Files aggregate Forms 940, 941, 943 using CPEO's EIN
- Files Schedule R to allocate to each customer
- CPEO liability for wages it pays; customer may also be liable for certain employees

## CPEO Requirements

### To Become Certified
- Apply through IRS Online Registration System
- Meet financial assurance requirements
- Meet bond and liability requirements
- File Form 8973 for contract start/end notifications

### To Remain Certified
- Meet all requirements of sections 3511 and 7705
- File required returns electronically
- Maintain certification status

## Employer Responsibilities with Third Parties

### Before Hiring a Third Party
- Verify their credentials and history
- Check for IRS or state compliance issues
- Review service agreement carefully
- Understand who is liable for errors

### Ongoing Oversight
- Review tax deposits made on your behalf
- Check EFTPS for deposit confirmations
- Review filed returns for accuracy
- Monitor for timely filings

### Key Point
> **You remain responsible** for ensuring taxes are filed and deposited correctly, even if you hire a third party. If the third party fails, the IRS will come to you for payment.

## Protecting Yourself

### EFTPS Access
- Enroll in EFTPS to monitor deposits
- Third parties can't prevent you from checking
- Confirm deposits match payroll amounts

### Separate Accounts
- Consider requiring separate accounts for your taxes
- Prevents third party from commingling funds

### Regular Review
- Check deposits within 1-2 days after payroll
- Review filed returns quarterly
- Verify W-2 accuracy before distribution

## Reporting Requirements

### Aggregate Filers
CPEOs and Section 3504 agents must file:
- [[Schedule R (Form 940)]] for aggregate Form 940
- [[Schedule R (Form 941)]] for aggregate Form 941
- [[Schedule R (Form 943)]] for aggregate Form 943

### Form 8973 (CPEOs Only)
- File within 60 days of contract start
- File within 60 days of contract end
- Notify IRS of service agreement changes

## Liability Comparison

| Arrangement | Whose EIN | Who's Liable |
|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| PSP/Reporting Agent | Employer's | Employer only |
| Section 3504 Agent | Agent's | Agent + Employer (joint) |
| CPEO | CPEO's | CPEO (wages paid); Customer may be liable |
