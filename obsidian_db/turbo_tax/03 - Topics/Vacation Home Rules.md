# Vacation Home Rules

## Overview

When you rent a dwelling unit (vacation home, beach cottage, ski condo) that you also use personally, special rules determine how much of your expenses are deductible. The rules depend on whether the property is considered a "home" and how many days it's rented.

## Key Threshold: Is It a "Home"?

You use a dwelling unit as a **home** during the tax year if personal use exceeds the greater of:

1. **14 days**, OR
2. **10% of total days rented at fair rental price**

This classification affects what expenses are deductible.

## Days of Personal Use

A day of personal use includes any day the unit is used by:

1. **You or any owner** (unless rented to another owner as their main home under shared equity financing)
2. **Family member of any owner** (unless it's their main home and they pay fair rent)
   - Family includes: spouse, siblings, half-siblings, parents, grandparents, children, grandchildren
3. **Anyone under an arrangement** letting you use another dwelling unit
4. **Anyone at less than fair rental price**

### Donation of Use
Days when you donate the use to charity and the charity sells the use at a fundraiser count as personal use.

## Days of Rental Use

- Any day rented at **fair rental price** counts as a rental day
- Days available but not actually rented **don't count**
- A day rented at fair price but also used personally = **rental day** for expense division, but **personal day** for "home" determination

## Three Tax Situations

### Situation 1: Rented 15 Days or Less

If you rent for **15 days or less** and use personally for more than 14 days:

| Treatment | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Rental income | **Not reported** (tax-free) |
| Rental expenses | **Not deductible** |
| Mortgage interest/taxes | Deduct on Schedule A if itemizing |

### Situation 2: Rented More Than 15 Days, Personal Use ≤ 14 Days (or 10% of Rental)

Property is **NOT** considered a home:

| Treatment | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Rental income | Report on Schedule E |
| Rental expenses | Fully deductible (excess carries forward) |
| Personal portion | Not deductible |

### Situation 3: Rented More Than 15 Days, Personal Use Exceeds Limits

Property **IS** considered a home → **Expense limits apply**

## Dividing Expenses

When property is used for both rental and personal purposes:

### Formula
```
Rental expense = Total expense × (Rental days ÷ Total use days)
```

### Order of Allocation
Allocate expenses in this order:

1. **Mortgage interest and real estate taxes** (may be fully deductible)
2. **Operating expenses** (maintenance, utilities, etc.)
3. **Depreciation**

### Expense Limitation Rules

When property is a home and rental expenses exceed rental income:

1. **Interest and taxes** - Allocate between rental and personal; rental portion deductible
2. **Operating expenses** - Deductible only to extent of remaining rental income
3. **Depreciation** - Deductible only to extent of remaining rental income after (1) and (2)

**Excess deductions carry forward** to future years.

## Example Calculation

**Facts**:
- Beach cottage available June 1 - August 31 (92 days)
- Rented 85 days at fair rental price
- Not rented 7 days (available but no tenant)
- Family used 14 days in May
- You used 2 days in July while rented

**Determination**:
- Rental days: 85 + 2 = 87 days (for expense division)
- Personal days: 14 + 2 = 16 days (for home determination)
- Total use: 101 days

**Is it a home?**
- Personal days (16) > 14 days ✓
- Personal days (16) > 10% of rental days (8.7) ✓
- **Yes, it's a home** → expense limits apply

**Expense allocation**:
- Rental portion: 87/101 = 86%

## Fair Rental Price

The rent you charge should be comparable to similar properties in the area. Consider:
- Same purpose?
- Similar condition?
- Similar size?
- Similar furnishings?
- Similar location?

If substantially below market, it's not a fair rental price.
