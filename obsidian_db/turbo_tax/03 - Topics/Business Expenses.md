# Business Expenses

## Overview

Business expenses are the costs of operating a trade or business. To be deductible, an expense must be both **ordinary** and **necessary** per IRC Section 162.

- **Ordinary:** Common and accepted in your trade or business
- **Necessary:** Helpful and appropriate for your business (need not be indispensable)

These expenses are reported on [[Schedule C (Form 1040)]] and deducted from gross income to arrive at net profit.

## Deductible Expense Categories

### Advertising (Line 8)
Business cards, online ads, print advertisements, website costs, promotional materials

### Car and Truck Expenses (Line 9)
See [[Car and Truck Expenses]] for standard mileage rate (70 cents/mile for 2025) vs. actual expense method

### Commissions and Fees (Line 10)
Payments to non-employees for services, sales commissions

### Contract Labor (Line 11)
Payments to independent contractors for business services (may require filing [[Form 1099-NEC]])

### Depletion (Line 12)
Deduction for timber, minerals, or natural resources

### Depreciation (Line 13)
See [[Depreciation for Farmers|Depreciation]] and [[Section 179 Deduction]]
- Claimed on [[Form 4562]]
- MACRS for most business property
- Section 179 limit: $2.5 million for 2025

### Employee Benefit Programs (Line 14)
Accident and health plans, group-term life insurance, dependent care assistance

### Insurance (Line 15)
Liability, malpractice, fire, theft, flood, credit insurance, workers' compensation
- **Not deductible:** Life insurance if you are a beneficiary

### Interest (Line 16)
- Business loan interest
- Mortgage interest on business property
- [[Interest Income|Investment interest]] limited to net investment income

### Legal and Professional Fees (Line 17)
Accountant fees, attorney fees for business matters
- **Note:** Legal fees to acquire business assets are added to basis, not deducted

### Office Expense (Line 18)
Office supplies, postage, small equipment not subject to depreciation

### Pension and Profit-Sharing Plans (Line 19)
Contributions to [[IRAs|SEP, SIMPLE, or qualified plans]]
- See [[Pub 560 - Retirement Plans for Small Business]]

### Rent or Lease (Line 20)
- Business real estate rent
- Equipment rentals
- Vehicle lease payments

### Repairs and Maintenance (Line 21)
Repairs that don't add to property value or prolong its life

### Supplies (Line 22)
Materials used in business operations (not for resale)

### Taxes and Licenses (Line 23)
- State and local business taxes
- Real estate and personal property taxes on business assets
- **Not deductible:** Federal income taxes, estate/gift taxes

### Travel and Meals (Line 24)
- Travel expenses: 100% deductible
- Meals: 50% deductible (generally)
- See [[Pub 463 - Travel, Gift, and Car Expenses]]

### Utilities (Line 25)
Telephone, electricity, gas, water, internet for business use

### Wages (Line 26)
Payments to employees (not your own salary as sole proprietor)
- Must file employment tax returns

## Expenses Requiring Special Treatment

### Bad Debts
See [[Bad Debts]] - Must be business-related and wholly or partially worthless

### Business Use of Home
See [[Business Use of Home]] - Requires regular and exclusive use; calculated on [[Form 8829]]

### Startup Costs
- Up to $5,000 deductible in first year (reduced by amount over $50,000)
- Remaining costs amortized over 180 months

### Organizational Costs
- Up to $5,000 deductible (reduced by amount over $50,000)
- Remaining amortized over 180 months

## Nondeductible Expenses (Line 47)

Per [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]], the following are NOT deductible:

1. **Bribes and kickbacks** - Illegal payments
2. **Fines and penalties** - Violations of law
3. **Lobbying expenses** - Influencing legislation
4. **Political contributions** - Campaign donations
5. **Commuting costs** - Travel between home and regular workplace
6. **Personal expenses** - Not related to business
7. **Capital expenses** - Must be depreciated or amortized
8. **Club membership dues** - Social, athletic, luncheon, airline, hotel clubs

## Mixed Business and Personal Expenses

If an expense is partly business and partly personal:
- Separate and deduct only the business portion
- Example: Car used 60% business = deduct 60% of expenses

### Q: Can I deduct my own salary as a sole proprietor?
**A:** No. You are not an employee of your sole proprietorship. You report net profit and pay income and self-employment tax on that amount.

### Q: Are meals with clients deductible?
**A:** Generally 50% deductible if the meal is directly related to business or associated with a substantial business discussion.

### Q: What records do I need to keep?
**A:** Keep receipts, canceled checks, invoices, and other documentation showing the amount, date, payee, and business purpose of each expense.
