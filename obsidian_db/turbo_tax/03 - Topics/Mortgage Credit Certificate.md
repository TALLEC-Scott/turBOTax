# Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC)

## Overview

A Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC) is a certificate issued by a state or local government that allows certain homebuyers to claim a tax credit for a portion of the mortgage interest they pay. The MCC program is designed to help lower-income individuals afford home ownership.

## How It Works

1. **Apply before purchase**: Contact your state or local housing finance agency before getting a mortgage
2. **Receive the MCC**: If approved, you'll receive a certificate showing:
   - Certificate credit rate
   - Certified indebtedness amount
3. **Claim the credit**: File [[Form 8396]] with your tax return each year

## Key Certificate Information

### Certificate Credit Rate

The percentage used to calculate your credit. If the rate is **higher than 20%**, your credit is capped at **$2,000** per year.

### Certified Indebtedness Amount

The maximum loan amount on which you can claim the credit. If your actual mortgage exceeds this amount, you'll need to prorate the interest.

## Eligibility

MCCs are generally available to:
- First-time homebuyers (in many programs)
- Lower-to-moderate income buyers
- Buyers meeting specific income and purchase price limits

**Contact your state or local housing finance agency** for specific eligibility requirements.

## Calculating the Credit

### If Mortgage ≤ Certified Indebtedness

Credit = Interest paid × Certificate credit rate

### If Mortgage > Certified Indebtedness

```
Credit = Interest × (Certified amount ÷ Mortgage) × Credit rate
```

See [[Mortgage Interest Credit]] for detailed calculation examples.

## Credit Limits

1. **Rate-based limit**: If credit rate > 20%, credit capped at $2,000
2. **Tax liability limit**: Credit cannot exceed your tax liability

Unused credit (due to tax limit) can be carried forward for up to **3 years**.

## Important Rules

### Reduce Interest Deduction

If you itemize deductions, you must reduce your [[Home Mortgage Interest]] deduction by the amount of credit claimed.

### Recapture on Early Sale

If you sell the home within **9 years** of purchase, you may have to repay (recapture) part of the credit benefit.

### Refinancing

You must obtain a **new MCC** when refinancing. The new MCC cannot increase your credit beyond what the old MCC would have provided.

## How to Apply

1. **Before buying**: Contact your state or local housing finance agency
2. **Get pre-approved**: Many programs require MCC approval before closing
3. **At closing**: Ensure proper documentation

## State and Local Programs

MCC availability and terms vary by jurisdiction. Contact:
- State housing finance agency
- Local housing authority
- Participating lenders
