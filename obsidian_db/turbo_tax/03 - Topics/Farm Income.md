# Farm Income

## Overview

Farm income includes amounts received from cultivating, operating, or managing a farm for gain or profit, either as owner or tenant. This encompasses income from stock, dairy, poultry, fish, fruit, and truck farms, as well as plantations, ranches, ranges, orchards, and groves.

## Types of Farm Income

### Sales of Farm Products
- Products raised on farm for sale (livestock, produce, grains)
- Products bought for resale
- Reported on [[Schedule F (Form 1040)]]

### Crop Shares
- Rent received in form of crop shares included in income when converted to money
- Subject to self-employment tax if landlord materially participates
- See [[Farm Recordkeeping]] for tracking requirements

### Agricultural Program Payments
- Most government payments are taxable income
- Conservation payments may have special treatment
- Report on Schedule F, Part I

### Market Assistance Loans (MALs)
- Can elect to report loan proceeds as income in year received
- Market gain may be taxable depending on reporting method

### Custom Hire Income
- Income from contract work or custom work performed off your farm
- Report on Schedule F unless activity rises to level of separate business

## Reporting Farm Income

- [[Schedule F (Form 1040)]]: Primary form for reporting farm income
- [[Form 4797]]: Sales of business property including livestock held for draft, breeding, dairy, or sport
