# Volunteer Expense Deductions

While you cannot deduct the value of your time or services, you may be able to deduct unreimbursed out-of-pocket expenses incurred while volunteering for a qualified organization.

## What You Cannot Deduct

| Item | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Value of your time | No deduction for services |
| Value of your skills | No deduction for expertise |
| Blood donations | Not deductible |
| Income lost while volunteering | Opportunity cost not deductible |
| Childcare while volunteering | Personal expense |
| General household expenses | Not directly related to services |

## What You Can Deduct

### Requirements

To deduct volunteer expenses, all of these must be true:

1. **Unreimbursed** - You were not paid back by the organization
2. **Directly connected** - Expenses were incurred because of your volunteer services
3. **Not personal** - Not living or family expenses
4. **Only because of services** - You would not have had the expense otherwise

### Car Expenses

**Standard Mileage Rate:** 14 cents per mile (2025)

**Or actual expenses:**
- Gas and oil directly related to charitable use
- Parking fees and tolls

**Not Deductible:**
- General repairs and maintenance
- Depreciation
- Registration fees
- Insurance
- Tires

**Records Required:**
- Date of each trip
- Organization name
- Purpose of trip
- Miles driven
- Expenses incurred

### Travel Expenses

**Deductible if:**
- You are away from home overnight
- There is no significant element of personal pleasure, recreation, or vacation
- You are on duty in a genuine and substantial sense throughout the trip

**Include:**
- Air, rail, and bus transportation
- Taxi and other local transportation
- Lodging costs
- Meal costs
- Out-of-pocket car expenses

**Not Deductible:**
- Personal sightseeing
- Theater or entertainment
- Spouse or children's expenses
- Expenses when duties are nominal

**Example:**
- You're a scout leader on a camping trip, responsible for setup and supervision throughout → Travel expenses deductible
- You count whales 8 hours/day on a cruise with 16 hours/day free → Travel expenses NOT deductible

### Uniforms

**Deductible if:**
- Required to be worn while volunteering
- Not suitable for everyday use
- You must pay for them yourself

**Includes:**
- Cost of purchase
- Cost of cleaning and maintenance

**Examples:**
- Nurse's aide uniform with organization logo → Deductible
- Suit worn to volunteer at office → NOT deductible (suit suitable for everyday use)

### Conventions

If a qualified organization selects you as its representative:

**Deductible:**
- Travel, meals, and lodging while away overnight
- Registration fees
- Transportation

**Not Deductible:**
- Attendance as regular member (not representative)
- Personal entertainment
- Family member expenses

### Student Living With You

You may deduct up to **$50 per month** for hosting a student if:

**Requirements:**
1. Written agreement with qualified organization
2. Student is in 12th grade or lower, full-time
3. Student is not your relative or dependent
4. Lives in your home

**Deductible Expenses:**
- Books and tuition
- Food
- Clothing
- Transportation
- Medical and dental care
- Entertainment
- Other amounts for student's well-being

**Not Deductible:**
- Depreciation on home
- Fair market value of lodging
- General household expenses (taxes, insurance, repairs)

**Mutual Exchange Program:** NOT deductible (your child lives abroad in exchange)

### Foster Parents

**Deductible if:**
- No profit motive in providing foster care
- Qualified organization selects individuals placed in your home
- Expenses primarily benefit the organization

**Deductible:**
- Unreimbursed expenses for food, clothing, care
- Must be primarily to benefit organization

**NOT Deductible:**
- Expenses when your motive is adoption

### Church Deacon

If you're in a permanent diaconate program:

**Deductible:**
- Vestments
- Books
- Transportation required for program

### Whaling Captains

Special deduction for Alaska Eskimo Whaling Commission recognized captains:

- Up to **$10,000 per year**
- Reasonable and necessary whaling expenses
- Includes: boats, weapons, gear, food, supplies, catch distribution

## Documentation Requirements

### For Expenses Under $250

Keep records of:
- Date and nature of expense
- Amount paid
- Organization name
- Purpose of expense

### For Expenses of $250 or More

Must have **contemporaneous written acknowledgment** from organization including:

1. Description of services you provided
2. Statement whether organization provided goods/services in return
3. Description and value of any goods/services provided
4. Date of contribution

**Get acknowledgment by:**
- Date you file return, OR
- Due date of return (including extensions) - whichever is earlier

### Car Expense Records

If using standard mileage rate:
- Miles driven for charitable purpose
- Dates of trips
- Organizations served

If using actual expenses:
- Gas and oil receipts
- Parking and toll receipts
- Record of which expenses are charitable vs. personal

## Reporting

Report on **Schedule A, line 11** (cash contributions and out-of-pocket expenses).

Include with other charitable contributions; subject to same AGI limits.
