# Defined Benefit Plans

## Overview

A defined benefit plan is a qualified retirement plan that provides a definite, determinable benefit at retirement. Unlike defined contribution plans, benefits are determined by a formula rather than contributions. These plans require actuarial assumptions and ongoing professional administration.

## Key Characteristics

- Provides a specific retirement benefit amount
- Benefits calculated using a formula (typically based on salary and years of service)
- Employer bears investment risk
- Requires actuary to determine contributions
- Subject to minimum funding requirements

## Annual Benefit Limits (2025-2026)

| Year | Maximum Annual Benefit |
|------|----------------------|
| 2025 | Lesser of $280,000 or 100% of average compensation |
| 2026 | Lesser of $290,000 or 100% of average compensation |

**Note**: Compensation is averaged over the highest 3 consecutive calendar years.

## Minimum Funding Requirements

### Quarterly Installments
- Due dates: 15 days after end of each quarter (April 15, July 15, October 15, January 15)
- Each installment: 25% of required annual contribution
- Interest charged on underpayments

### Extended Contribution Period
- Additional contributions to satisfy minimum funding can be made up to 8½ months after plan year end

## Deduction Limits

- Based on actuarial assumptions and computations
- Must use an actuary to figure deduction limit
- Cannot take into account contributions exceeding benefit limits

## Benefit Form Requirements

### Qualified Joint and Survivor Annuity
- Required for vested participants with a spouse
- Surviving spouse receives at least 50% of participant's benefit

### Qualified Pre-retirement Survivor Annuity
- Required if vested participant dies before annuity starting date
- Surviving spouse receives survivor annuity

## Participation Requirements

- Must allow participation at age 21 with 1 year of service
- Cannot exclude employees based on age
- Leased employees may need to be included

## Vesting Requirements

| Vesting Schedule Type | Requirement |
|----------------------|-------------|
| 3-Year Cliff | 100% after 3 years |
| 6-Year Graded | 20% after 2 years, 40% after 3 years, 60% after 4 years, 80% after 5 years, 100% after 6 years |

**Note**: Plans may offer faster vesting schedules.

## Top-Heavy Plan Rules

A plan is top-heavy if key employees own more than 60% of total benefits. Additional requirements include:
- Minimum benefits for non-key employees
- Faster vesting schedules
- Special contribution rules

## Setting Up a Defined Benefit Plan

1. Adopt a written plan document
2. Establish a trust for plan assets
3. Obtain actuarial services
4. Apply for determination letter (optional)

### Plan Documents
- IRS pre-approved plans available
- Individually designed plans permitted
- Professional assistance strongly recommended

## Survivor Benefit Requirements

- Automatic survivor benefits required for married participants
- Spouse must consent to waive survivor benefits
- Consent must be witnessed by plan representative or notary public

## When to Consider a Defined Benefit Plan

- Older business owners seeking to accelerate retirement savings
- Businesses with stable, predictable cash flow
- Employers wanting to provide guaranteed retirement income
- Companies looking for larger tax deductions
