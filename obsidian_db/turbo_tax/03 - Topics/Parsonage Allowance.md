# Parsonage Allowance

## Overview

Ordained, commissioned, or licensed ministers may exclude from income tax the rental allowance or fair rental value of a parsonage provided as pay for ministerial services. This is one of the most significant tax benefits available to clergy.

## Important Limitation

**The parsonage exclusion applies ONLY for income tax purposes.** For [[Self-Employment Tax for Clergy|SE tax purposes]], the fair rental value of a parsonage and any rental allowance MUST be included in gross income.

## What Can Be Excluded

### Fair Rental Value of Parsonage
- The fair rental value of a home provided by the church
- Includes cost of utilities if furnished by the church
- Must not exceed reasonable pay for services

### Rental Allowance
- Amount officially designated as housing allowance
- Amount actually used to provide or rent a home
- Limited to fair rental value of home (including furnishings) plus utilities

## Designation Requirements

The church must:
1. **Officially designate** the payment as a housing allowance BEFORE making payment
2. Designate a **definite amount** (cannot determine amount later)
3. Designation must be in:
   - Employment contract
   - Minutes of church or organization meeting
   - Budget
   - Other official action taken in advance

**Note:** Informal discussions do NOT constitute official designation.

### Local vs. National Designation

If employed by a local congregation:
- A national church agency resolution does NOT effectively designate your housing allowance
- The LOCAL congregation must officially designate the allowance

If employed directly by a national agency:
- A national church agency resolution CAN designate your allowance

## Exclusion Limits

The amount excluded cannot be more than the **smallest of**:

1. Amount officially designated as rental allowance
2. Amount actually used to provide a home
3. Fair rental value of home (including furnishings, utilities, garage, etc.)

### Example

Rev. Baker receives:
- Parsonage with fair rental value: $24,000/year
- Salary: $67,000 (including $7,500 utility allowance)
- Actual utility costs: $7,000

**Income tax exclusion:** $31,000 ($24,000 FRV + $7,000 utilities)
**SE tax income:** $91,000 ($67,000 salary + $24,000 FRV)

## Home Ownership

If you own your home and receive a housing allowance, exclude the smallest of:
- The officially designated allowance
- Amount actually used to provide a home
- Fair rental value of the home (including furnishings, utilities, garage)

## Excess Rental Allowance

Include in gross income any rental allowance exceeding the limits. Report on:
- Form 1040 or 1040-SR, line 1h
- Write "Excess allowance" and the amount on the dotted line

## Deductible Expenses

Even when paying expenses with tax-free allowance, you may deduct:
- **Home mortgage interest** on [[Schedule A]]
- **Real estate taxes** on [[Schedule A]]

## Special Situations

### Retired Ministers
May exclude from income:
- Rental value of home provided by church for past services
- Part of pension designated as rental allowance

**Note:** Surviving spouse cannot exclude rental value unless for services they perform.

### Teachers and Administrators
- **Church school employees**: Housing exclusion applies
- **Non-church college faculty**: Housing exclusion does NOT apply

### Ministers of Music/Education
If NOT authorized to perform substantially all religious duties of an ordained minister, the housing exclusion does NOT apply, even if commissioned.

### Theological Students
Cannot exclude parsonage or rental allowance unless ordained, commissioned, or licensed.

### Traveling Evangelists
May exclude rental allowance if:
- Ordained minister
- Perform services at churches away from community
- Use allowance to maintain permanent home

### Cantors
May exclude rental allowance if:
- Have bona fide commission
- Employed full-time by congregation
- Perform substantially all religious functions of Jewish faith

## Expenses Allocable to Tax-Free Income

You cannot deduct expenses allocable to your tax-free parsonage allowance. Calculate the nondeductible portion using:

```
Tax-free allowance ÷ Total ministerial income × Deductible expenses
```

**Exception:** Home mortgage interest and real estate taxes remain deductible.

## Required Statement

If you receive tax-free parsonage allowance and have ministerial expenses, attach a statement to your return with:
- List of each tax-free income item (parsonage allowance) with amounts
- List of each taxable income item (wages, weddings, etc.) with amounts
- List of each deductible expense with amounts
- Statement that other deductions aren't allocable to tax-free income
- Explanation of how you calculated the nondeductible portion
