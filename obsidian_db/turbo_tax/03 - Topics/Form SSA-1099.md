# Form SSA-1099

## Overview

Form SSA-1099 (Social Security Benefit Statement) is issued by the Social Security Administration to report Social Security benefits paid to you during the tax year. It shows the gross benefits, repayments, and net benefits you need to report on your tax return.

## Who Receives This Form

Every person who received Social Security benefits receives Form SSA-1099. If you receive benefits on more than one Social Security record, you may receive more than one form.

## Key Boxes Explained

### Box 1 - Name
The person for whom the Social Security benefits were paid.

### Box 2 - Beneficiary's Social Security Number
The SSN of the person named in Box 1.

### Box 3 - Benefits Paid in 2025
**Gross benefits paid to you in 2025.** This includes:
- Regular monthly benefits
- Lump-sum payments for prior years (indicated by asterisk *)
- Any adjustments or additions

### Box 4 - Benefits Repaid to SSA in 2025
**Total benefits you repaid during 2025.** This includes:
- Checks returned to SSA
- Deductions for work or other adjustments
- Other repayments by direct remittance

### Box 5 - Net Benefits for 2025
**The figure to use for tax calculations.**
- Box 3 minus Box 4
- Use this amount on Form 1040, Line 6a
- If negative (in parentheses), see [[Benefits Repayment Rules]]

### Box 6 - Voluntary Federal Income Tax Withheld
Federal income tax you elected to have withheld using Form W-4V.

### Box 8 - Claim Number
Use this number for all correspondence with SSA.

## Description of Amount in Box 3

This section details the items included in Box 3:

### Additions (Increase Gross Benefits)

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Paid by check or direct deposit | Amount you actually received |
| Medicare premiums deducted | Basic premium ($185.00/month for 2025) |
| Workers' compensation offset | Reduction for WC benefits |
| Disability payments | SSDI payments (may be excludable for terrorist attack victims) |
| Paid to another family member | Amounts withheld for child support/alimony |
| Deductions for work | Amounts withheld due to work or overpayment recovery |
| Attorney fees | Fees withheld and paid directly to attorney |
| Voluntary federal income tax withheld | Tax withheld at your request |
| Treasury offset/garnishment/levy | Amounts withheld for debts |

### Subtractions (Decrease Gross Benefits)

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Payments for months before December 1983 | NOT taxable regardless of when paid |
| Lump-sum death payment | NOT taxable |
| Amounts refunded to you | Medicare premium refunds, excess attorney fees |

## Description of Amount in Box 4

Details the repayments included in Box 4:
- Checks returned to SSA
- Deductions for work or other adjustments
- Other repayments by direct remittance

## Asterisk (*) After Box 3 Amount

An asterisk indicates that Box 3 includes benefits received in 2025 for one or more earlier years. This means you may want to consider the [[Lump-Sum Election]] method.

## Using Form SSA-1099 for Tax Filing

### Step 1: Gather All Forms
If you received multiple SSA-1099 forms, add the amounts from each:
- Total all Box 5 amounts
- Total all Box 6 amounts

### Step 2: Report on Form 1040
| Form 1040 Line | Amount to Enter |
|----------------|-----------------|
| 6a | Total from all Box 5 |
| 6b | Taxable amount from worksheet |
| 6d | Check if MFS and lived apart all year |

### Step 3: Claim Withholding
Include Box 6 amount on Form 1040 as federal tax withheld.

## Form SSA-1042S (Nonresident Aliens)

Nonresident aliens receive Form SSA-1042S instead, which includes additional boxes:

| Box | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| 6 | Rate of Tax (usually 30%) |
| 7 | Amount of Tax Withheld |
| 8 | Amount of Tax Refunded |
| 9 | Net Tax Withheld |

For treaty exemptions, see [[Nonresident Alien Social Security Taxation]].

## Common Issues

### Medicare Premiums
- NOT deducted from Box 5
- Already included in gross benefits
- May be deductible on Schedule A as medical expense

### Workers' Compensation Offset
- Shown in description section
- Already factored into Box 3 and Box 5
- Do NOT adjust for this amount

### Negative Amount in Box 5
If Box 5 shows a negative amount (in parentheses):
- You repaid more than you received
- None of your benefits are taxable
- See [[Benefits Repayment Rules]]
