# Installment Obligation

## Overview

An **installment obligation** is the buyer's debt instrument representing their promise to make future payments to the seller. It can take the form of a deed of trust, note, land contract, mortgage, or other evidence of the buyer's debt.

## Nature of the Obligation

The installment obligation is:
- Created when property is sold on the installment method
- The buyer's promise to pay the remaining purchase price
- The seller's asset (receivable) during the installment period

## Determining Basis in the Obligation

**Basis = Unpaid Balance − (Unpaid Balance × Gross Profit Percentage)**

Or equivalently:
**Basis = Unpaid Balance × (1 − Gross Profit Percentage)**

The basis represents the seller's remaining investment in the property that has not yet been recovered.

## Example

You sold property with:
- Unpaid balance on obligation: $50,000
- Gross profit percentage: 60%

**Basis calculation:**
- Unrealized profit = $50,000 × 60% = $30,000
- Basis in obligation = $50,000 − $30,000 = $20,000

## Disposition of Installment Obligation

When you dispose of an installment obligation, you may recognize gain or loss:

### Sale or Exchange

**Gain/Loss = Amount Realized − Basis in Obligation**

### Gift

**Gain/Loss = FMV at Time of Gift − Basis in Obligation**

The donee takes your basis and gross profit percentage for future payments.

### Cancellation

**Gain/Loss = FMV at Cancellation − Basis in Obligation**

Between related persons, FMV is presumed to be at least face value.

### Transfer at Death

**Not a disposition** - The beneficiary steps into the seller's shoes and reports installment income as payments are received.

## Third-Party Notes

If the buyer gives you a third-party note (someone else's obligation):
- **FMV of note** = Payment to you in year received
- **Excess of face over FMV** = Market discount (subject to Sections 1276, 1278)

### Exception

If the third-party note is payable on demand or readily tradable:
- Cash method: FMV on date received = payment
- Accrual method: Face amount or stated redemption price

## Transfer Between Spouses

No gain or loss is recognized on transfer of an installment obligation:
- Between spouses, or
- Between former spouses incident to divorce

The transferee takes the transferor's basis and continues reporting under the same terms.

**Exception:** Nonresident alien spouse or former spouse—recognition required.

## Pledge Rule

If you use an installment obligation to secure debt:
- Net debt proceeds may be treated as a payment
- Applies when selling price exceeds $150,000
- Exceptions for farm property, timeshares, personal-use property

See [[Pledge Rule]] for details.

## Common Questions

### Q: What happens to the obligation if I die?
**A:** The obligation passes to your heirs or beneficiaries. They report installment income as they receive payments, using your gross profit percentage.

### Q: Can I sell my installment note to someone else?
**A:** Yes. The sale of the note is a disposition. Calculate gain/loss as the difference between the amount received and your basis in the obligation.

### Q: What if the buyer pays off the note early?
**A:** Early payoff is treated as a payment. Apply the gross profit percentage to determine the gain.
