# 15-Year Rule

## Overview

The 15-year rule (also called the "special section 403(b) catch-up" or "years-of-service catch-up") allows certain long-service employees to contribute more to their [[403(b) Plans]] than the standard [[Elective Deferrals]] limit.

## Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for the 15-year rule increase, you must:

1. Have **at least 15 years of service** with a qualifying organization
2. Have a plan document that permits the 15-year rule increase

## Qualifying Organizations

The 15-year rule applies to service with:
- Educational organizations (public or private schools)
- Hospitals
- Home health service agencies
- Health and welfare service agencies
- Churches or conventions/associations of churches
- Associated organizations

## How Much Can You Contribute?

The increase is the **least of** these three amounts:

| Limit | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| $3,000 | Flat dollar amount per year |
| $15,000 | Lifetime maximum, reduced by prior years' increases |
| $5,000 × years of service | Minus total prior elective deferrals |

## Maximum Deferral Limits with 15-Year Rule

| Year | Standard Limit | With 15-Year Rule |
|------|----------------|-------------------|
| 2025 | $23,500 | Up to $26,500 |
| 2026 | $24,500 | Up to $27,500 |

## Determining Years of Service

### Definition

Years of service are the total number of years you worked as a full-time employee for the employer maintaining your 403(b) account as of the end of the year.

### Key Rules

1. **Status of employer** - Only count periods when employer was eligible
2. **Same employer rule** - Generally can't count service with other employers
3. **Church employees** - Treat all service with related church organizations as same employer
4. **Self-employed ministers** - Include full and part years of self-employment

### Figuring Years of Service

#### Full-Time for Full Year
Count each full year as 1 year of service.

#### Part-Time or Partial Year
Your year of service is a fraction:
- **Numerator**: Time you actually worked
- **Denominator**: Normal annual work period for the position

**Note**: You cannot accumulate more than 1 year of service in a 12-month period.

### Example Calculation

A teacher started in September 2021 at ABC Public Schools (academic year = Sept-Dec and Feb-May):

| Year | Period Worked | Years of Service |
|------|---------------|------------------|
| 2021 | Sept-Dec | 0.5 year |
| 2022 | Feb-May, Sept-Dec | 1.0 year |
| 2023 | Feb-May, Sept-Dec | 1.0 year |
| 2024 | Feb-May, Sept-Dec | 1.0 year |
| 2025 | Feb-May, Sept-Dec | 1.0 year |
| **Total** | | **4.5 years** |

## Interaction with Catch-up Contributions

If eligible for BOTH the 15-year rule and age 50+ [[Catch-up Contributions]]:

1. **Apply 15-year rule first** - Allocate amounts under this provision
2. **Then apply catch-up** - Add age 50+ catch-up contributions

This ordering is mandatory.

## Prior Year Tracking

Keep records of:
- Additional pre-tax elective deferrals made under 15-year rule in prior years
- Designated Roth contributions permitted under 15-year rule in prior years

These reduce your lifetime $15,000 limit.
