# Debt Cancellation in Bankruptcy

## Overview

Generally, when debt is canceled or forgiven, the amount canceled is taxable income to the debtor. However, debt canceled in a bankruptcy case under Title 11 is **excluded from gross income**.

This exclusion is one of several exclusions available for canceled debt income under IRC Section 108.

## Exclusions from Income

Don't include canceled debt in gross income if:

1. **Bankruptcy** - Cancellation takes place in a bankruptcy case under Title 11
2. **Insolvency** - Debtor is insolvent (liabilities exceed FMV of assets)
3. **Qualified farm debt** - Debt incurred in operating a farm
4. **Qualified real property business indebtedness** - Debt connected to business real property
5. **Qualified principal residence indebtedness** - For discharges before January 1, 2026

## Order of Exclusions

| Priority | Exclusion Type |
|----------|----------------|
| 1st | Bankruptcy exclusion (takes precedence over all others) |
| 2nd | Insolvency exclusion (over farm/real property exclusions) |
| 3rd | Qualified principal residence indebtedness (over insolvency unless taxpayer elects otherwise) |

## Bankruptcy Case Exclusion Requirements

For the bankruptcy exclusion to apply:
- The debtor must be under the jurisdiction of the court
- The cancellation must be granted by the court OR result from a court-approved plan

**Result:** None of the debt canceled in bankruptcy is included in the debtor's gross income in the year canceled.

## Insolvency Exclusion

A debtor is insolvent when liabilities exceed the FMV of assets immediately before cancellation.

**Rule:** Exclude debt canceled up to the amount of insolvency.

### Example

Simpson Corporation has $21,000 in liabilities and $17,500 in assets (insolvent by $3,500). If $4,000 of debt is canceled outside bankruptcy:
- $3,500 can be excluded (amount of insolvency)
- $500 must be included in income
- Tax attributes must be reduced by the $3,500 excluded

## Tax Attribute Reduction

When debt is excluded under bankruptcy or insolvency, the debtor must reduce certain tax attributes by the amount excluded. This postpones rather than eliminates the tax on canceled debt.

See [[Tax Attribute Reduction]] for detailed rules on:
- Order of reduction
- Basis reduction
- Credit carryover reduction
- Election to reduce basis first

## Form 1099-C

If debt forgiveness is $600 or more, the debtor should receive Form 1099-C (Cancellation of Debt) from the lender.

## Partnerships

If partnership debt is canceled due to bankruptcy or insolvency:
- Exclusion rules apply at the **individual partner level**
- Each partner's share must be reported unless partner qualifies for exclusion
- Elections (basis reduction, short tax year) must be made by individual partners
- Partner treats partnership interest as depreciable property for basis reduction purposes

## Corporations

Corporations follow the same rules as individuals for debt cancellation and attribute reduction in bankruptcy or insolvency.

### Stock for Debt Exchange

If a corporation transfers stock in satisfaction of debt and the FMV is less than the indebtedness:
- Corporation has income from the difference
- Can exclude if in bankruptcy or insolvent
- Must reduce tax attributes by excluded amount

### Earnings and Profits

Earnings and profits don't include discharge income applied to reduce basis of property. Otherwise, discharge income (including excluded amounts) increases E&P.

## S Corporations

For S corporations, the bankruptcy and insolvency exclusions apply at the **corporate level** (unlike partnerships where they apply at partner level).
