# Self-Employment Tax for Clergy

## Overview

Members of the clergy are generally treated as self-employed for Social Security and Medicare tax purposes, even when employed by a church. This means they pay the full 15.3% self-employment (SE) tax on their ministerial earnings, rather than splitting it with an employer.

## SE Tax Rates for 2025

| Component | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Social Security (OASDI) | 12.4% |
| Medicare (HI) | 2.9% |
| **Total SE Tax** | **15.3%** |

## Earnings Limits for 2025

| Limit | Amount |
|-------|--------|
| Maximum earnings subject to Social Security tax | $176,100 |
| Minimum net earnings requiring SE tax filing | $400 |
| Minimum church wages requiring SE tax filing | $108.28 |

## Figuring Net Earnings

### Regular Method

1. Total gross income from ministerial services
2. Subtract allowable business deductions
3. Multiply by 92.35% (0.9235)

Use [[Schedule SE]] to calculate net earnings and SE tax.

### Nonfarm Optional Method

May be used if:
1. Self-employed on a regular basis (net earnings of $400+ in 2 of prior 3 years)
2. Used this method fewer than 5 prior years
3. Net nonfarm profits were less than $7,840 AND less than 72.189% of gross nonfarm income

| Gross Nonfarm Income | Net Earnings |
|----------------------|--------------|
| $10,860 or less | 2/3 of gross income |
| More than $10,860 | $7,240 |

## Amounts Included in Gross Income

Include when figuring net earnings for SE tax:

1. **Salaries and fees** for ministerial services
2. **Offerings** received for marriages, baptisms, funerals, masses, etc.
3. **Meals and lodging** provided for employer's convenience
4. **Fair rental value of parsonage** (including utilities)
5. **Rental allowance** (including utility allowance)
6. **Church payments toward your income or SE tax** (also subject to income tax)

### Critical Distinction

For SE tax purposes, the parsonage allowance and fair rental value of a parsonage MUST be included in gross income, even though they may be excluded from income tax.

## Amounts NOT Included in Gross Income

- Church contributions to tax-sheltered annuity plans
- Offerings made to the church (not to you personally)
- Pension payments for past ministerial services
- Post-retirement parsonage rental value or allowance

## Allowable Deductions

### Self-Employed Ministerial Expenses
Deduct on [[Schedule C]] and carry net amount to [[Schedule SE]] line 2:
- Expenses for performing marriages, baptisms
- Expenses for delivering speeches
- Other self-employed ministerial expenses

### Common-Law Employee Expenses
- Subtract allowable expenses from wages
- Report net amount on [[Schedule SE]] line 2
- Attach an explanation
- Do NOT complete [[Schedule C]]

## SE Tax Deduction

You may deduct one-half of your SE tax when calculating AGI. Claim this deduction on [[Schedule 1]] line 15.

**Note:** This deduction does NOT reduce net earnings for SE tax purposes.

## Additional Medicare Tax

A 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax applies when Medicare wages and self-employment income exceed:
- $250,000 if married filing jointly
- $125,000 if married filing separately
- $200,000 for any other filing status

Use [[Form 8959]] to calculate and report this tax.

## Exemption from SE Tax

Clergy may request exemption from SE tax by filing [[Form 4361]]. See [[SE Tax Exemption]] for requirements.

## Estimated Tax Payments

Ministers must generally make estimated tax payments if they expect to owe $1,000 or more in taxes (including SE tax). Use:
- [[Form 1040-ES]] for payment
- IRS Withholding Estimator at IRS.gov/W4App
