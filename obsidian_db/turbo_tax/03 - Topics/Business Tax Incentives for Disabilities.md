# Business Tax Incentives for Disabilities

## Overview

Business owners can benefit from several tax incentives designed to help persons with disabilities. These incentives encourage businesses to make facilities accessible, hire individuals with disabilities, and remove barriers to employment.

## Three Main Incentives

### 1. Deduction for Removing Barriers (Architectural and Transportation Barrier Removal)

Businesses can deduct costs for making facilities or transportation vehicles more accessible to persons with disabilities or the elderly.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Maximum deduction** | $15,000 per year |
| **Eligible expenses** | Ramps, accessible parking, elevators, accessible restrooms, wider doorways, etc. |
| **Where to claim** | Deduct as business expense on appropriate form |
| **ADA compliance** | Must meet Americans with Disabilities Act standards as of November 5, 1990 |

**Eligible improvements include:**
- Entrance and exit ramps
- Accessible parking spaces
- Accessible restrooms
- Elevators and lifts
- Door widening and automatic doors
- Accessible drinking fountains
- Accessible public transportation vehicles

### 2. Disabled Access Credit

A **nonrefundable tax credit** for eligible small businesses that incur expenses to comply with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Eligibility** | Small businesses with $1 million or less in gross receipts OR 30 or fewer full-time employees |
| **Credit rate** | 50% of eligible access expenditures |
| **Maximum credit** | $5,000 per year |
| **Credit form** | Form 8826, Disabled Access Credit |
| **Base amount** | First $250 is not counted; credit applies to amounts over $250 up to $10,250 |

**Eligible expenditures:**
- Removing architectural, communication, physical, or transportation barriers
- Providing qualified interpreters or other effective methods for hearing-impaired individuals
- Providing qualified readers, taped texts, or other effective methods for visually impaired individuals
- Acquiring or modifying equipment or devices for persons with disabilities

### 3. Work Opportunity Credit

A credit for businesses that hire individuals from targeted groups with high unemployment rates or special employment needs.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Credit type** | Nonrefundable |
| **Credit rate** | Up to 40% of first-year wages |
| **Maximum wages** | $6,000 per employee (maximum credit $2,400) |
| **Credit form** | Form 5884, Work Opportunity Credit |
| **Certification** | Must obtain certification from state workforce agency |

**Vocational Rehabilitation Referrals:**
Individuals with physical or mental disabilities resulting in substantial handicap to employment who have been referred upon completion of (or while receiving) rehabilitative services.

**Other targeted groups include:**
- Qualified IV-A recipients (TANF)
- Qualified veterans
- Ex-felons
- Designated community residents
- Supplemental Security Income (SSI) recipients
- Summer youth employees
- Qualified long-term unemployment recipients

## Coordination of Benefits

### Barrier Removal: Deduction vs. Credit

You can use **both** the deduction and credit for different accessibility expenses, but not for the same expense:

1. **Deduction** (up to $15,000): For removing architectural and transportation barriers
2. **Credit** (up to $5,000): For other ADA compliance expenses

**Strategy:** Allocate expenses between the deduction and credit to maximize tax benefit.

### Example

A small business spends $20,000 on accessibility improvements:
- $15,000 for barrier removal (deduct as business expense)
- $5,000 for providing sign language interpreters (50% credit = $2,500 credit)

**Total tax benefit:** $15,000 deduction + $2,500 credit

## Eligibility Requirements Summary

| Incentive | Business Size Requirement | Annual Limit |
|-----------|--------------------------|--------------|
| Barrier Removal Deduction | Any business | $15,000 deduction |
| Disabled Access Credit | ≤$1M revenue OR ≤30 employees | $5,000 credit |
| Work Opportunity Credit | Any business | Varies by targeted group |
