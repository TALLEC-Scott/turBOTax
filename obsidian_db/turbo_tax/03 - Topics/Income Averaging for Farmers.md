# Income Averaging for Farmers

## Overview

Income averaging allows farmers to smooth out the tax burden from fluctuating farm income. By using tax rates from the three prior years (base years), farmers may reduce their tax liability in years when farm income is unusually high.

## Who Can Use Income Averaging?

### Eligible Taxpayers
- Individuals engaged in a farming business
- Partners in a farming partnership
- Shareholders in an S corporation engaged in farming

### Not Eligible
- C corporations
- Partnerships
- S corporations
- Estates and trusts

## Elected Farm Income (EFI)

EFI is the amount of farm income you elect to average over the three base years:

### How to Calculate
1. Determine total taxable income
2. Subtract non-farm income
3. The result is electable farm income

### What's Included
- Net profit from Schedule F
- Farm wages
- Distributive share of partnership farm income
- S corporation farm income

## How Income Averaging Works

### Process
1. Calculate tax on current year income without averaging
2. Calculate tax using base year rates on elected farm income
3. Compare results - use method that results in lower tax

### Base Years
- Three years immediately before the current tax year
- Example for 2025: Use 2022, 2023, and 2024 tax rates

## When Averaging Helps

Income averaging is beneficial when:
- Current year farm income is significantly higher than base years
- Base years had lower marginal tax rates
- Income fluctuates substantially year to year

## Making the Election

- File [[Schedule J (Form 1040)]]
- Attach to Form 1040 for the current tax year
- Can change election by filing amended return within 3 years
