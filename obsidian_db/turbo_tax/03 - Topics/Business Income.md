# Business Income

## Overview

Business income is any income received in connection with a trade or business. If there is a connection between income and your business (the payment would not have been made without the business), it is business income per [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]].

Business income is reported on [[Schedule C (Form 1040)]], Line 1 (gross receipts) or Line 6 (other income).

## Types of Business Income

### Sales Revenue
Income from selling products or merchandise
- Report gross sales, then subtract returns and allowances
- Subtract [[Cost of Goods Sold]] to determine gross profit

### Service Income
Fees for professional services, consulting, repairs, etc.
- No inventory or COGS typically required
- Gross profit equals net receipts

### Bartering Income
Fair market value of goods or services received in exchange for business goods or services
- Report FMV at time received
- May need to file [[Form 1099-B]] for barter exchange transactions

### Real Estate Rents (as Business Income)
Report on Schedule C (not Schedule E) if you are:
- A real estate dealer selling property for profit
- Hotel/motel owner providing substantial services (maid service, etc.)
- Trailer park owner providing substantial services

### Personal Property Rents
Income from renting equipment, vehicles, formal wear, etc.
- Report on Schedule C
- Include prepaid rent when received

### Interest Income
Business interest includes:
- Interest on notes receivable from customers
- Interest on business loans made
- Interest on business bank accounts

### Dividend Income
Generally nonbusiness income, except for:
- Securities dealers
- Dividends from business insurance policies (recovery of deducted premiums)

### Canceled Debt
May be taxable income if:
- Debt was incurred in business
- You were not bankrupt or insolvent
- Not qualified principal residence debt

See [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]] Chapter 5 for exclusions.

### Other Income (Line 6)
- Scrap sales
- Bad debt recoveries
- Fuel tax credits and refunds
- Interest from business bank accounts

## Nonemployee Compensation (Form 1099-NEC)

If you receive [[Form 1099-NEC]] showing nonemployee compensation:
- This is self-employment income
- Report on Schedule C
- Subject to [[Self-Employment Tax]]

### NIL Income (Name, Image, Likeness)
NIL opportunities may be classified as self-employment income:
- Report income from Form 1099-NEC or 1099-MISC
- May be subject to SE tax if engaged in trade or business

## Payment Card Transactions (Form 1099-K)

If you receive [[Form 1099-K]]:
- Report gross receipts on Schedule C
- May need adjustments for:
  - Sales tax collected (not income)
  - Amounts not related to your business
  - Personal transactions

## Income Not Reported on Schedule C

Report elsewhere if NOT connected to your sole proprietorship:

| Income Type | Report On |
|------------|-----------|
| Capital gains/losses | [[Schedule D (Form 1040)]] |
| Rental income (not as business) | Schedule E |
| Farm income | [[Schedule F (Form 1040)]] |
| Wages as employee | Form 1040, Line 1 |

## Accounting for Income

### Cash Method
Report income when actually or constructively received:
- Money received
- Property received (at fair market value)
- Services received (at fair market value)

### Accrual Method
Report income when:
- All events have occurred fixing the right to receive income
- Amount can be determined with reasonable accuracy

## Items NOT Considered Income

Per [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]]:
- **Loans** - Money borrowed must be repaid
- **Sales tax collected** - Held in trust for state/local government
- **Security deposits** - Held for return to tenant
- **Casualty insurance proceeds** - May reduce basis or create gain

## Common Questions

### Q: Do I report all income even without a 1099?
**A:** Yes. You must report all business income received, whether or not you received a Form 1099. The IRS requires reporting income from all sources.

### Q: How do I report bartering income?
**A:** Report the fair market value of goods or services received on Schedule C, Line 1 (gross receipts) or Line 6 (other income).

### Q: What if my business didn't make a profit?
**A:** You still report all income and expenses. A net loss may be deductible against other income (subject to limitations). See Net Operating Loss rules.
