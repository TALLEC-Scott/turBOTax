# Related Party Sales

## Overview

Special rules apply to sales or exchanges of property between related persons. Gains may be treated as ordinary income, and losses may not be deductible.

## Gain Is Ordinary Income

Gain on sale or exchange between related persons may be ordinary income if:

### Depreciable Property Transaction
Between:
- Person and their controlled entity (>50% ownership)
- Taxpayer and trust where they are beneficiary
- Executor and estate beneficiary
- Employer and welfare benefit fund

### Controlled Partnership Transaction
Between:
- Partnership and person owning >50% interest
- Two partnerships with same >50% owners

## Nondeductible Loss Rules

Loss on sale between related persons is **NOT deductible**.

### Related Persons Include

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Family members | Siblings, half-siblings, spouse, ancestors, descendants |
| Individual + Corporation | Individual owns >50% of stock |
| Controlled group members | Corporations in same controlled group |
| Trust relationships | Grantor-fiduciary, fiduciary-beneficiary |
| Two trusts | Same grantor for both |
| Exempt organization | Organization and controlling person |
| Corporation + Partnership | Same persons own >50% of both |
| S Corporations | Same persons own >50% of each |
| Partnerships | Same persons own >50% of each |
| Person + Partnership | Person owns >50% interest |
| Executor + Beneficiary | Unless pecuniary bequest |

### Exceptions
- Partnership interest transactions (categories 12-13)
- Corporate liquidations
- Controlled group losses are deferred, not denied

## Property Received from Related Person

If you receive property from related person who had disallowed loss:
- Recognize gain only to extent it exceeds disallowed loss
- Applies only to original transferee
- Does not apply to wash sales

### Example
- Brother sells stock to you for $7,600 (his basis $10,000)
- His $2,400 loss is disallowed
- You later sell for $10,500
- Your recognized gain: $500 (gain $2,900 - disallowed $2,400)

## Constructive Ownership Rules

1. Stock/partnership interest owned by entity = owned proportionately by owners
2. Individual deemed to own family member's interests
3. Family = siblings, half-siblings, spouse, ancestors, descendants
4. Constructive ownership not reapplied for family attributions

## Indirect Transactions

Loss not deductible if:
- You sell stock through broker
- Related person/entity buys same stock
- Under prearranged plan
