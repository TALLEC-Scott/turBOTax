# Qualified Disaster Loss

## Overview

A qualified disaster loss is an individual's casualty or theft loss of personal-use property attributable to specific major disasters. These losses receive **enhanced deduction benefits** beyond standard [[Disaster Area Losses|disaster losses]].

## Qualifying Disasters

### 2016 Disasters
- Hurricane Harvey
- Tropical Storm Harvey
- Hurricane Irma
- Hurricane Maria
- Major disasters declared under Stafford Act section 401 in 2016

### 2017-2018 Disasters
- California wildfires (2017 and January 2018)
- Major disasters declared in 2018 through December 21, 2019 (incident period ending by January 19, 2020)

### 2020-2025 Disasters
Major disasters declared by the President during **January 1, 2020 - September 2, 2025** with:
- Incident period began December 28, 2019 - July 4, 2025
- Incident period ended by August 3, 2025

**Important**: Does NOT include disasters declared only due to COVID-19.

Check declarations at **IRS.gov/DisasterTaxRelief**

## Enhanced Benefits

### 1. Increased Dollar Reduction
- **$500** per casualty reduction (instead of $100)
- Applies to each qualified disaster loss

### 2. No 10% AGI Reduction
The 10% of AGI reduction **does not apply** to qualified disaster losses.

### 3. Increased Standard Deduction Option
Can claim the loss **without itemizing** other deductions:

1. Enter Form 4684, line 15 amount on Schedule A, line 16 dotted line
2. Label as "Net Qualified Disaster Loss"
3. Add standard deduction amount with label "Standard Deduction Claimed With Qualified Disaster Loss"
4. Report combined amount on Schedule A, line 16

### 4. Prior Year Election
Can elect to claim the loss in the preceding tax year (same as [[Disaster Area Losses|regular disaster losses]]).

## Comparison: Regular vs. Qualified Disaster Loss

| Feature | Regular Disaster Loss | Qualified Disaster Loss |
|---------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Dollar reduction | $100 | $500 |
| 10% AGI reduction | Yes | No |
| Increased standard deduction | No | Yes |
| Prior year election | Yes | Yes |

## Example Calculation

**Regular Disaster Loss:**
- Loss: $10,000
- Less $100: $9,900
- Less 10% AGI ($50,000 AGI = $5,000): $4,900 deductible

**Qualified Disaster Loss:**
- Loss: $10,000
- Less $500: $9,500
- No 10% AGI reduction
- **$9,500 deductible** (or increased standard deduction)

## Reporting Requirements

1. Complete Form 4684, Section D
2. Check qualified disaster loss box
3. Enter FEMA disaster declaration number
4. If not itemizing, follow increased standard deduction procedure above

## AMT Adjustment

The alternative minimum tax adjustment for the standard deduction is **retroactively inapplicable** to net qualified disaster losses.
