# Rental Activities

## Overview

Rental activities are generally treated as **passive activities** under IRC Section 469, regardless of whether you materially participate. This means losses from rental activities are generally subject to the passive activity loss limitations.

The key exception is for taxpayers who qualify as a [[Real Estate Professional]].

## Definition of Rental Activity

An activity is a rental activity if:
- Tangible property (real or personal) is used by customers or held for use by customers
- Gross income (or expected gross income) represents amounts paid mainly for the use of the property
- The use is under a lease, service contract, or other arrangement

## Exceptions - Activities That Are NOT Rental Activities

Your activity is **not** a rental activity if any of the following apply:

### Exception 1: Average Period of Customer Use is 7 Days or Less
- Calculate by dividing total days in all rental periods by number of rentals
- Example: Hotel rooms, car rentals

### Exception 2: Average Use 30 Days or Less with Significant Personal Services
Requirements:
- Average period of customer use is 30 days or less, AND
- You provide significant personal services with the rentals

**Significant personal services** include only services performed by individuals, considering:
- Frequency of services
- Type and amount of labor required
- Value of services relative to amount charged

**NOT significant personal services:**
- Services needed to permit lawful use
- Repairs/improvements extending useful life longer than average rental
- Services similar to long-term rentals (cleaning common areas, routine repairs)

### Exception 3: Extraordinary Personal Services
Services are extraordinary if:
- They are performed by individuals, AND
- Customers' use of property is incidental to receipt of services
- Example: Hospital, nursing home

### Exception 4: Rental Incidental to Nonrental Activity

**Incidental to investment:**
- Main purpose is appreciation of property
- Gross rental income < 2% of lesser of unadjusted basis or FMV

**Incidental to trade or business:**
- You own interest in the trade or business
- Property was used mainly in that business during current year or 2 of 5 preceding years
- Gross rental income < 2% of lesser of unadjusted basis or FMV

**Employee lodging** is incidental if furnished for employer's convenience.

### Exception 5: Nonexclusive Use by Various Customers
- Property is customarily available during defined business hours
- For nonexclusive use by various customers
- Example: Golf course, health club

### Exception 6: Property Used in Nonrental Activity
- You provide property for use in a nonrental activity
- In your capacity as owner of interest in partnership, S corporation, or joint venture conducting that activity

## Special $25,000 Allowance

If you [[Active Participation|actively participate]] in rental real estate activities:

| Filing Status | Maximum Allowance | Phaseout Range |
|---------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Single, MFJ | $25,000 | $100,000 - $150,000 MAGI |
| MFS (lived apart all year) | $12,500 | $50,000 - $75,000 MAGI |
| MFS (lived together) | $0 | N/A |

**Active participation** is a lower standard than material participation. You may be treated as actively participating if you make management decisions such as:
- Approving new tenants
- Deciding rental terms
- Approving expenditures

**Ownership requirement:** You must own at least 10% (by value) of all interests in the activity.

## Real Estate Professional Exception

If you qualify as a [[Real Estate Professional]], rental real estate activities in which you materially participate are **not passive**.

See [[Real Estate Professional]] for qualification requirements.

## Reporting Rental Activities

| Activity Type | Form/Schedule |
|---------------|---------------|
| Rental real estate | Schedule E (Form 1040), Part I or II |
| Personal property rental | Schedule C (Form 1040) |
| Passive rental losses | Form 8582 |
| At-risk limitations | Form 6198 |

## Types of Rental Property

### Residential Rental Property
- Houses, apartments, condos, townhouses
- Vacation homes (may have personal use limitations)
- See [[Pub 527 - Residential Rental Property]] for details

### Commercial Rental Property
- Office buildings
- Retail spaces
- Industrial properties
- Mixed-use properties

### Personal Property Rental
- Equipment
- Vehicles
- Other tangible personal property
