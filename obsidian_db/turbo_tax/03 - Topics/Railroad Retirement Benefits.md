# Railroad Retirement Benefits

## Overview

Railroad Retirement benefits are paid to eligible railroad employees and their families under the Railroad Retirement Act. These benefits are divided into two tiers with different tax treatments. Understanding the distinction helps you properly report this income.

## Types of Railroad Retirement Benefits

### Tier 1 Benefits
Tier 1 benefits consist of two components:

| Component | Tax Treatment |
|-----------|---------------|
| SSEB (Social Security Equivalent) | Taxed like Social Security benefits |
| NSSEB (Non-Social Security Equivalent) | Taxed like pension from qualified plan |

**Social Security Equivalent Benefit (SSEB)**
- Equals the Social Security benefit you would have received
- Taxed under Social Security rules
- Reported on Form RRB-1099
- See [[Pub 915 - Social Security and Equivalent Railroad Retirement Benefits]] for details

**Non-Social Security Equivalent Benefit (NSSEB)**
- Portion of Tier 1 that exceeds SSEB
- Taxed as pension from qualified plan
- Allows recovery of employee contributions
- Reported on Form RRB-1099-R

### Tier 2 Benefits
- Similar to private pension
- Based on railroad earnings only
- Taxed as pension from qualified plan
- Employee contributions can be recovered tax-free
- Reported on Form RRB-1099-R

### Other Benefits
| Benefit Type | Tax Treatment |
|--------------|---------------|
| Vested Dual Benefit (VDB) | Fully taxable (non-contributory) |
| Supplemental Annuity | Fully taxable (non-contributory) |

## Form RRB-1099-R

The Railroad Retirement Board issues Form RRB-1099-R to report Tier 2, NSSEB, VDB, and supplemental annuity benefits:

| Box | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| 1 | Claim Number and Payee Code |
| 2 | Recipient's Identification Number |
| 3 | Employee Contributions |
| 4 | Contributory Amount Paid (NSSEB + Tier 2) |
| 5 | Vested Dual Benefit |
| 6 | Supplemental Annuity |
| 7 | Total Gross Paid |
| 8 | Repayments |
| 9 | Federal Income Tax Withheld |
| 10 | Rate of Tax (nonresident aliens) |
| 11 | Country (nonresident aliens) |
| 12 | Medicare Premium Total |

## Tax-Free Recovery of Employee Contributions

You can recover your railroad employee contributions tax-free from NSSEB and Tier 2 benefits:

1. Employee contributions (Box 3) represent your investment
2. Use the Simplified Method to figure the tax-free part
3. The tax-free portion is applied to the Contributory Amount Paid (Box 4)
4. VDB and Supplemental Annuity are fully taxable (no employee contributions)

## Withholding

### For Citizens and Resident Aliens
- Use Form W-4V to request voluntary withholding from SSEB
- Use Form RRB W-4P for NSSEB, Tier 2, VDB, and Supplemental Annuity

### For Nonresident Aliens
- Provide Form RRB-1001 to claim treaty benefits
- Subject to 30% withholding unless treaty exemption applies

## Repayment of Benefits

If you repaid benefits received in an earlier year:
- More than $3,000: Deduct on Schedule A or claim credit
- $3,000 or less: Not deductible for tax years after 2017

## Reporting on Tax Return

### Form 1040 Lines 5a and 5b
- Line 5a: Enter amount from Form RRB-1099-R, Box 7
- Line 5b: Enter taxable amount after applying Simplified Method

### Form RRB-1099 (SSEB)
Report SSEB portion according to Social Security taxation rules.

## Key Rules

1. **Tier 1 has two components with different tax treatments**
2. **Employee contributions are recovered tax-free from Tier 2 and NSSEB**
3. **VDB and Supplemental Annuity are fully taxable**
4. **Nonresident aliens may be subject to 30% withholding**
5. **Contact RRB at 877-772-5772 for assistance**
