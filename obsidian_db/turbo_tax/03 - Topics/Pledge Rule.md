# Pledge Rule

## Overview

The **pledge rule** under IRC Section 453A(d) treats net debt proceeds as a payment on an [[Installment Obligation]] when you use the obligation to secure debt. This prevents taxpayers from effectively converting deferred gain into borrowed funds without triggering recognition.

## When the Pledge Rule Applies

The pledge rule applies when:
1. You use an installment obligation to secure any debt
2. The original selling price of the property was **over $150,000**

## Exceptions to the Pledge Rule

The pledge rule does **not** apply to:
- Sales of property used or produced in farming
- Qualifying sales of timeshares and residential lots
- Sales of personal-use property

## How It Works

### Amount Treated as Payment

The net debt proceeds are treated as a payment on the installment obligation.

**Net Debt Proceeds = Gross Debt − Direct Expenses of Obtaining Debt**

### Timing

The payment is considered received on the **later of**:
1. The date the debt becomes secured by the obligation
2. The date you receive the debt proceeds

### Limit on Payment Amount

The amount treated as payment cannot exceed:

**Contract Price − Payments Received Before Debt Proceeds**

## What Constitutes "Secured"

A debt is secured by an installment obligation if payment of principal or interest on the debt is:
- Directly secured by any interest in the installment obligation
- Or, an arrangement allows you to satisfy the debt with the installment obligation

## Effect on Future Installment Payments

Once the pledge rule applies:
1. The pledged amount is reported as installment income immediately
2. Future actual payments are not reported until they exceed the pledged amount already reported

## Example

You sold property for $500,000:
- Contract price: $500,000
- Gross profit percentage: 60%
- Received $100,000 in payments

You then pledge the remaining installment obligation to secure a $200,000 loan:
- Net proceeds: $200,000
- Treated as payment: Lesser of $200,000 or ($500,000 − $100,000) = $200,000
- Report installment income: $200,000 × 60% = $120,000

## Grandfather Exception

The pledge rule does **not** apply to pledges made after December 17, 1987, to refinance debt if:
1. The debt was outstanding on December 17, 1987
2. The debt was secured by that installment obligation on that date
3. The debt remained secured until the refinancing

The exception only applies to the original principal amount—any excess is treated as a payment.

### Refinancing by Creditor Call

If the creditor calls the debt and another party provides refinancing, it's treated as a continuation of the original debt (within the original principal amount).

## Reporting the Payment

When the pledge rule applies:
1. Report the deemed payment on [[Form 6252]]
2. Include in installment income for that year
3. Track pledged amount against future actual payments

## Common Questions

### Q: Does the pledge rule apply if I use the property as collateral, not the installment obligation?
**A:** No. The pledge rule specifically applies when the **installment obligation** (the buyer's note) is used as security, not the underlying property.

### Q: What if the selling price was exactly $150,000?
**A:** The pledge rule only applies if the selling price exceeds $150,000. At exactly $150,000, the rule does not apply.

### Q: Can I avoid the pledge rule by using a home equity loan?
**A:** Using a home equity loan secured by your residence would not implicate the pledge rule. However, using the installment obligation itself as security would trigger the rule (if applicable).
