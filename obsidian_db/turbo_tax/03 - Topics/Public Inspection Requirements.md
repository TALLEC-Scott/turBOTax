# Public Inspection Requirements

## Overview

Tax-exempt organizations must make certain documents available for public inspection. This transparency requirement ensures public access to information about exempt organizations.

## Documents Available for Inspection

### Exemption Applications
- Form 1023, Form 1024, Form 1024-A
- All supporting documents submitted with application
- Determination letter
- Any letter or document issued by IRS regarding application

### Annual Returns
- Form 990, Form 990-EZ, Form 990-PF
- Form 990-T (unrelated business income)
- All schedules, attachments, and supporting documents
- Except Schedule B (contributor information)

### Political Organization Forms
- Form 8871 (Notice of Section 527 Status)
- Form 8872 (Report of Contributions and Expenditures)

## Documents NOT Available

The following are NOT required for public inspection:
- Schedule B (contributor information)
- Schedule B of Form 990-T (except for Section 527 organizations)
- Form 990-N (e-postcard)
- Trade secrets or proprietary information
- Donor lists and identities

## Methods of Disclosure

### In-Person Inspection
- Must make documents available at principal office
- Must make available at regional and district offices if three or more employees
- Must provide copies during regular business hours
- No advance appointment required

### Written Requests
- Must respond within 30 days
- May charge reasonable copying fees
- Must mail copies to requestor

### Website Posting
Organizations with websites should post:
- Form 990 series for most recent 3 years
- Exemption application and determination letter

## Copying Fees

### Permitted Charges
- First 100 pages: May charge reasonable fee
- After 100 pages: May charge $0.20 per page
- May also charge actual postage
- May require prepayment

### Fee Waiver
- May waive fees in appropriate circumstances
- May provide free copies to requestors

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Failure to Provide Documents
| Penalty | Amount |
|---------|--------|
| Per day penalty | $20 |
| Maximum per return | $10,500 |
| Maximum per application | $10,500 |
| Responsible person penalty | $10,500 per failure |

### No Reasonable Cause Defense
Penalties apply even if organization has reasonable cause for failure.

## Exceptions

### Church Exception
Churches are not required to make exemption applications or annual returns available for public inspection.

### Limited Exception for Form 990-T
Form 990-T generally not required for public inspection except for Section 527 political organizations.

## Practical Considerations

### Best Practices
- Maintain organized file of required documents
- Train staff on public inspection requirements
- Have copies readily available
- Consider posting documents on website

### Common Mistakes
- Failing to respond to written requests timely
- Charging excessive copying fees
- Not including all required schedules
- Requiring advance appointments

## Redaction Rules

### Permitted Redactions
- Schedule B contributor information
- Social Security numbers
- Home addresses of individuals
- Trade secrets (with IRS approval)

### NOT Permitted
- Redacting information simply because embarrassing
- Redacting compensation information
- Redacting financial data
