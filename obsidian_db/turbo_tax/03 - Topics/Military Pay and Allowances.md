# Military Pay and Allowances

## Overview

Members of the U.S. Armed Forces receive various types of pay and allowances. Understanding which items are taxable and which are excluded from gross income is essential for accurate tax reporting.

## Taxable Military Pay (Table 1)

These items are included in gross income unless earned in a combat zone:

### Basic Pay
- Active duty pay
- Attendance at designated service school
- Back wages

### Bonus Pay
- Career sea pay
- Diving duty pay
- Foreign duty pay (outside 48 contiguous states and D.C.)
- Foreign language proficiency pay
- Hardship duty pay
- Hostile fire/imminent danger pay
- Medical and dental officer special pay
- Nuclear-qualified officer pay
- Special duty assignment pay
- Reenlistment bonuses
- Enlistment bonuses
- Overseas extension bonuses

### Incentive Pay
- Submarine pay
- Flight pay
- Hazardous duty pay
- High Altitude/Low Opening (HALO) pay

### Other Taxable Pay
- Accrued leave
- CONUS COLA (Cost-of-Living Adjustment)
- High deployment per diem
- Personal money allowances (high-ranking officers)
- Student loan repayments (amounts exceeding $5,250 for qualified education expenses)

### In-Kind Military Benefits (Taxable)
- Personal use of a government-provided vehicle

## Nontaxable Military Pay (Table 2)

These items are excluded from gross income:

### Combat Zone Pay
- Compensation for active service in a combat zone
- Note: Officer exclusion is limited (see [[Combat Zone Exclusion]])

### Living Allowances
- **BAH (Basic Allowance for Housing)** - Can still deduct mortgage interest and real estate taxes paid with BAH
- **BAS (Basic Allowance for Subsistence)**
- **OHA (Overseas Housing Allowance)**
- Housing and cost-of-living allowances abroad (U.S. or foreign government)

### Death Allowances
- Burial services
- Death gratuity payments to eligible survivors
- Travel of dependents to burial site

### Family Allowances
- Educational expenses for dependents
- Emergency assistance
- Evacuation to safety
- Separation allowances

### Moving Allowances
- Dislocation allowances
- Military base realignment and closure benefits
- Move-in housing allowances
- Moving household and personal items
- Moving trailers or mobile homes
- Storage expenses
- Temporary lodging expenses

### Travel Allowances
- Annual round trip for dependent students
- Leave between consecutive overseas tours
- Reassignment travel (dependent restricted status)
- Transportation during ship overhaul
- Per diem
- Operation Hero Miles travel benefits

### In-Kind Military Benefits (Nontaxable)
- Dependent-care assistance program
- Defense counsel services
- Legal assistance
- Medical/dental care
- Commissary/exchange discounts
- Space-available travel on government aircraft
- Uniforms furnished to enlisted personnel

### Other Excluded Items
- Group-term life insurance
- Professional education
- ROTC educational and subsistence allowances
- State bonus pay for combat zone service
- Survivor and retirement protection plan premiums

## Special Items

### Differential Wage Payments
- **Taxable** - Not treated as combat zone pay
- Payments by civilian employer representing wages the servicemember would have earned
- Subject to income tax withholding

### Thrift Savings Plan (TSP)
- **Traditional TSP:** Contributions from taxable pay are pre-tax; contributions from tax-exempt combat pay remain tax-exempt in the account
- **Roth TSP:** Contributions are after-tax; qualified distributions are tax-free
- **2025 combat zone contribution limit:** Up to $70,000 for those serving in combat zones

### Qualified Reservist Distribution (QRD)
- Distribution from cafeteria plan or health FSA when called to active duty for more than 179 days
- **Taxable** as wages to the extent it exceeds after-tax contributions

### State Bonus Payments
- Treated as combat zone pay if made for service in a combat zone
- May be excluded from federal income tax

## Reporting on Tax Return

### Form W-2
| Box | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| Box 1 | Taxable wages (excludes combat zone pay, BAH, BAS) |
| Box 12, Code Q | Nontaxable combat pay (informational) |

### Deducting Expenses Paid with BAH
Even though BAH is excluded from income, you may still deduct:
- Mortgage interest on your home (Schedule A)
- Real estate taxes on your home (Schedule A)
