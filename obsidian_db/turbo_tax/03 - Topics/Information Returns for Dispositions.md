# Information Returns for Dispositions

## Overview

When you sell certain assets, you may receive information returns showing the proceeds. These are also provided to the IRS for matching with your tax return.

## Form 1099-B - Broker Proceeds

### When Received
- Stocks sold through broker
- Bonds sold through broker
- Certain commodities

### Information Reported
- Gross proceeds from sale
- Cost basis (for covered securities)
- Holding period indication
- Federal tax withheld

### Use For
- Complete [[Form 8949]]
- Report on [[Schedule D (Form 1040)]]

## Form 1099-DA - Digital Asset Proceeds

### When Received (2025)
- Digital asset sales through broker
- Cryptocurrency transactions

### Information Reported
- Gross proceeds
- Voluntary basis reporting (not required)

### Due Date
- February 2, 2026 for 2025 transactions

## Form 1099-S - Real Estate Proceeds

### When Received
- Sale of land (improved or unimproved)
- Sale of buildings (residential, commercial, industrial)
- Condominium units
- Cooperative housing stock
- Noncontingent interest in standing timber

### Information Reported
- Gross proceeds (Box 2)
- Closing date
- Property address

### Not Required For
- Sales under $250,000 (principal residence)
- Certain transfers to corporations

## Form 1099-A - Acquisition or Abandonment

### When Received
- Property abandoned with secured debt
- Lender acquires property through foreclosure

### Information Reported
- Date of lender's acquisition
- FMV of property
- Outstanding debt balance

## Form 1099-C - Cancellation of Debt

### When Received
- Debt of $600 or more canceled
- From financial institution, credit union, or government agency

### Information Reported
- Amount of debt canceled
- Date of cancellation
- FMV of property (if applicable)

## Reporting Requirements

Even if you don't receive a form, you must report:
- All taxable sales of stocks, bonds, commodities
- Real estate transactions
- Digital asset transactions
