# Defined Contribution Plans

## Overview

A defined contribution plan is a qualified retirement plan that provides an individual account for each participant. Benefits are based on the amount contributed to the account plus earnings, gains, and losses. Unlike defined benefit plans, the participant bears the investment risk.

## Types of Defined Contribution Plans

### Profit-Sharing Plan
- Employer contributions are discretionary
- Not required to be based on profits
- Flexible contribution amounts year to year
- Can include 401(k) feature

### Money Purchase Pension Plan
- Fixed contribution percentage required
- Must contribute regardless of profits
- Higher contribution limits than profit-sharing for some employers
- Less flexibility

### 401(k) Plan
- Includes cash or deferred arrangement
- Employee salary deferrals plus employer contributions
- Most popular defined contribution plan type

### Employee Stock Ownership Plan (ESOP)
- Invests primarily in employer securities
- Provides ownership interest in company
- Special tax advantages

## Annual Addition Limits (2025-2026)

| Year | Maximum Annual Addition |
|------|------------------------|
| 2025 | Lesser of $70,000 or 100% of compensation |
| 2026 | Lesser of $72,000 or 100% of compensation |

**Note**: Annual additions include employer contributions, employee contributions, and forfeitures allocated to the account. Catch-up contributions are not subject to this limit.

## Compensation Limit

| Year | Maximum Compensation |
|------|---------------------|
| 2025 | $350,000 |
| 2026 | $360,000 |

## Deduction Limits

| Plan Type | Deduction Limit |
|-----------|----------------|
| Profit-Sharing Plan | 25% of covered compensation |
| Money Purchase Pension Plan | 25% of covered compensation |
| Combined Plans | 25% of covered compensation |

**Note**: Elective deferrals are not subject to the deduction limit.

## Carryover of Excess Contributions

- Contributions exceeding deduction limit can be carried over
- Must be deducted in later years (subject to limits)
- May be subject to 10% excise tax on nondeductible contributions

## Vesting Requirements

### Profit-Sharing Plans
| Schedule Type | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| 3-Year Cliff | 100% after 3 years |
| 6-Year Graded | 20% after 2 years, increasing 20% each year |

### Money Purchase Pension Plans
| Schedule Type | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| 3-Year Cliff | 100% after 3 years |
| 6-Year Graded | Same as profit-sharing |

**Note**: Elective deferrals are always 100% immediately vested.

## Forfeitures

- Can be allocated to remaining participants' accounts
- Can be used to reduce employer contributions
- Must be allocated in nondiscriminatory manner

## Employee Contributions

- Participants may be permitted to make after-tax contributions
- Must satisfy ACP (Actual Contribution Percentage) test
- Earnings grow tax-deferred until distribution

## Investment Options

- Participant-directed investments (if plan allows)
- Self-directed brokerage accounts possible
- Fiduciary responsibilities apply to investment selection

## Loans

- Permitted if plan document allows
- Maximum: Lesser of $50,000 or 50% of vested balance
- Must be repaid within 5 years (exceptions for residence loans)

## SEP Plans as Defined Contribution Plans

- SEP plans are treated as defined contribution plans
- Subject to annual addition limits
- Special deduction rules apply for self-employed
