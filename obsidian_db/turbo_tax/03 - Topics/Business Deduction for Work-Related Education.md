# Business Deduction for Work-Related Education

## Overview

Self-employed individuals and certain employees can deduct expenses for work-related education as a business expense. However, for most employees, this deduction is no longer available for tax years 2018-2025 due to the suspension of miscellaneous itemized deductions.

**Authority:** [[Pub 970 - Tax Benefits for Education#Chapter 11]]

## Who Can Claim This Deduction

### Self-Employed Individuals
- File [[Schedule C (Form 1040)]] - Profit or Loss From Business
- File [[Schedule F (Form 1040)]] - if engaged in farming
- Deduct directly from self-employment income

### Eligible Employees (Can Deduct)
1. **Armed Forces Reservists** - Claim on Form 2106
2. **Qualified Performing Artists** - Claim on Form 2106
3. **Fee-Based State/Local Government Officials** - Claim on Form 2106
4. **Individuals with Disabilities** - Impairment-related education expenses as itemized deduction

### Other Employees
- **Cannot deduct** for tax years 2018-2025
- Miscellaneous itemized deductions suspended

## Qualifying Work-Related Education

Education qualifies if it meets **at least ONE** of these tests:

### Test 1: Required by Employer or Law
- Required to keep current salary, status, or job
- Serves a bona fide business purpose of employer
- Does NOT qualify you for a new trade or business

### Test 2: Maintains or Improves Skills
- Maintains skills needed in present work
- Improves skills needed in present work
- Does NOT qualify you for a new trade or business

## Education That Does NOT Qualify

Even if maintaining/improving skills, education is NOT qualifying if:

1. **Minimum Educational Requirements**
   - Needed to meet minimum requirements of your trade or business
   - Determined by laws, employer, or professional standards
   - Once met, new requirements can qualify

2. **Qualifies for New Trade or Business**
   - Will qualify you for a different trade or business
   - Teaching generally considered same trade as other teaching
   - Temporary absence rule may apply

## Temporary vs. Indefinite Absence

| Absence Type | Duration | Tax Treatment |
|--------------|----------|---------------|
| Temporary | 1 year or less | Education can qualify |
| Indefinite | More than 1 year | Education does NOT qualify |

## Deductible Expenses

### Direct Expenses
- Tuition, books, supplies, lab fees
- Equipment (depreciated if lasting beyond course)

### Transportation
- Standard mileage rate: **70 cents/mile** (2025)
- Actual expenses (pro-rated)
- Parking fees and tolls

### Travel (If Overnight Required)
- Airfare, lodging, meals (50% deductible)
- Local transportation

### Other Expenses
- Research and typing expenses
- Professional dues
- Certain professional publications

## Teachers' Special Rules

- Teaching in one state = same trade as teaching in another state
- Can qualify even if changing grade levels or subjects
- Moving to administration may be new trade/business

## Common Questions

### Q: I'm a regular W-2 employee taking courses. Can I deduct my education expenses?
**A:** For tax years 2018-2025, no. The deduction for employee business expenses (including education) was suspended.

### Q: I'm self-employed. Can I deduct my MBA costs?
**A:** Yes, IF the MBA maintains or improves skills needed in your current business AND does not qualify you for a new trade or business.

### Q: My employer requires me to take courses. Are they deductible?
**A:** For employees, no (2018-2025). But your employer may pay up to $5,250 tax-free under an educational assistance program.
