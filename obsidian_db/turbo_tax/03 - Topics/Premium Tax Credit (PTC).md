# Premium Tax Credit (PTC)

## Overview

The Premium Tax Credit (PTC) is a refundable tax credit that helps eligible individuals and families with low to moderate income pay for health insurance coverage purchased through the Health Insurance Marketplace. The credit reduces the amount of tax you owe and can result in a refund.

## How It Works

The PTC provides financial assistance to pay premiums for qualified health plans by:
- Reducing the amount of tax you owe
- Providing a refund or increasing your refund amount
- Being available in advance (APTC) or claimed at tax time

## Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible for PTC, you must meet ALL of the following conditions:

1. **Enrollment**: An individual in your tax family must be enrolled in a qualified health plan through the Marketplace on the first day of the month

2. **MEC Ineligible**: That individual cannot be eligible for Minimum Essential Coverage (MEC) for that month (except individual market coverage)

3. **Premium Payment**: The enrollment premium must be paid by the tax return due date, covered by APTC, or sufficient to avoid coverage termination

4. **Not a Dependent**: No one can claim you as a dependent for the year

5. **Applicable Taxpayer Status**:
   - Household income at least 100% of federal poverty line (exceptions exist)
   - If married, must generally file jointly (exceptions for domestic abuse, spousal abandonment, or living apart)

## Calculating the Credit

The monthly credit amount is the **lesser of**:

1. The enrollment premiums for the month for qualified health plans, OR
2. The applicable SLCSP premium minus your monthly contribution amount

### Monthly Contribution Amount

Your monthly contribution amount is based on your household income as a percentage of the federal poverty line:

| Household Income (% of FPL) | Contribution % of Income |
|-----------------------------|--------------------------|
| Up to 150% | 0% - 4.0% |
| 150% - 200% | 4.0% - 6.3% |
| 200% - 250% | 6.3% - 8.1% |
| 250% - 300% | 8.1% - 9.1% |
| 300% - 400% | 9.1% - 9.8% |
| Over 400% | No cap (may still qualify for 2025) |

## Key Definitions

- **Tax Family**: You, your spouse (if filing jointly), and your dependents
- **Coverage Family**: Tax family members enrolled in qualified health plans and not eligible for other MEC
- **Household Income**: Modified AGI of you, your spouse, and dependents required to file

## Special Situations

### Married Taxpayers
Must file jointly unless:
- Living apart and meeting head of household requirements
- Victim of domestic abuse or spousal abandonment

### Self-Employed
May claim both PTC and self-employed health insurance deduction with coordination rules.

### Individuals Not Lawfully Present
Not eligible for PTC for own coverage, but may claim for lawfully present family members.
