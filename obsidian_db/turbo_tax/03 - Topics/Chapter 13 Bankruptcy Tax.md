# Chapter 13 Bankruptcy Tax

## Overview

Chapter 13 is "Adjustment of Debts of an Individual with Regular Income." Only individuals may file—corporations and partnerships are ineligible.

**Key distinction:** Unlike [[Chapter 7 Bankruptcy Tax|Chapter 7]] and [[Chapter 11 Bankruptcy Tax|Chapter 11]], the bankruptcy estate is **NOT** a separate taxable entity. The debtor files Form 1040 as usual.

## No Separate Taxable Entity

The debtor continues to file the same federal income tax returns as before bankruptcy:

| Filer | Form | What to Report |
|-------|------|----------------|
| Debtor | Form 1040/1040-SR | All income for entire year |

### Reporting Rules

- Report all income received during the entire year
- Deduct all allowable expenses
- **Do NOT include** debt canceled due to bankruptcy in income
- Include any previously reduced basis, losses, or credits from canceled debt

## Pre-Petition Return Requirements

Chapter 13 debtors must file all required tax returns for tax periods ending during the **4-year period prior** to the bankruptcy filing date.

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Due date | Before first creditors' meeting |
| Trustee extension | Up to 120 days additional |
| Court extension | Up to 30 more days after hearing |

**Caution:** Failure to file can prevent plan confirmation and result in dismissal or conversion to Chapter 7.

## Interest on Trust Accounts

Interest earned on amounts held by trustee in trust accounts:
- **NOT** included in debtor's income
- **NOT** taxable to trustee
- Available only to U.S. Trustee system

## Post-Petition Returns

Debtor must file all tax returns due after case commences or obtain extensions. Failure can result in dismissal or conversion.

## Plan Payment of Priority Taxes

Priority tax claims must be paid in full under the Chapter 13 plan:

| Option | Timeframe |
|--------|-----------|
| Standard | Over 3 years |
| With court approval | Over 5 years |

## Discharge Rules

### Broad Discharge

Upon completing all plan payments, debtor receives broad discharge of all debts provided by the plan.

### Non-Dischargeable Taxes

| Tax Type | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Withholding taxes | Liable in any capacity |
| No return filed | Must have filed return |
| Late-filed returns | Filed after 2 years before petition |
| Fraudulent return | Fraud issues |
| Willful evasion | Evasion issues |
| Unnoticed debts | If IRS didn't receive notice in time to file claim |

### Hardship Discharge

If debtor cannot complete payments due to circumstances beyond their control:
- Court may grant hardship discharge
- Unsecured claims must receive at least Chapter 7 liquidation value
- Chapter 7 non-dischargeable debts also excepted

## Conversion from Chapter 11

If Chapter 11 case converts to Chapter 13:
- Chapter 13 estate is NOT a separate taxable entity
- Post-conversion services income taxed to debtor
- Income from property of estate (realized after conversion) taxed to debtor
