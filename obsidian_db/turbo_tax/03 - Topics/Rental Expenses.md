# Rental Expenses

## Overview

Most expenses of renting property can be deducted from rental income. These include maintenance, insurance, taxes, interest, and depreciation. Understanding which expenses are deductible and when to deduct them is essential for proper tax reporting.

## Common Deductible Expenses

- **Advertising** - Costs to find tenants
- **Cleaning and maintenance** - Ongoing upkeep costs
- **Auto and travel expenses** - For rental property management
- **Commissions** - Paid to property managers or agents
- **Insurance** - Property and liability coverage
- **Depreciation** - Capital cost recovery (see [[MACRS Depreciation]])
- **Interest (other)** - Other than mortgage interest
- **Local transportation expenses** - Travel to rental properties
- **Legal and professional fees** - Tax preparation, legal advice
- **Management fees** - Property management costs
- **Points** - Mortgage points (see special rules below)
- **Mortgage interest** - Paid to banks and other lenders
- **Rental payments** - For equipment or property subletting
- **Taxes** - Real estate taxes on rental property
- **Repairs** - Ordinary and necessary repairs
- **Utilities** - If paid by landlord

## Key Expense Rules

### Insurance Premiums Paid in Advance
If paying for more than 1 year in advance:
- Can't deduct total premium in year paid
- Deduct only the portion applicable to each year of coverage

### Mortgage Interest
Deduct mortgage interest paid on rental property. When refinancing for more than the previous balance:
- Interest allocable to loan proceeds not related to rental use generally can't be deducted as rental expense

**Form 1098**: If you paid $600+ in mortgage interest, you should receive Form 1098.

### Legal and Professional Fees
- Deduct legal and professional expenses for rental activity
- Can deduct tax preparation fees for Schedule E, Part I
- Can deduct expenses to resolve tax underpayment related to rental activities

### Local Benefit Taxes
- **Can't deduct** charges that increase property value (streets, sidewalks, sewer systems)
- These are capital expenditures added to property basis
- **Can deduct** taxes for maintenance, repairs, or interest charges for benefits

### Local Transportation Expenses
Deductible for:
- Collecting rental income
- Managing, conserving, or maintaining rental property

**Not deductible**: Commuting between home and rental property unless home is principal place of business. See [[Business Use of Home]].

**Standard mileage rate for 2025**: 70 cents per mile

### Pre-Rental Expenses
Deduct ordinary and necessary expenses for managing, conserving, or maintaining property from the time it's made available for rent.

### Travel Expenses
Deductible if primary purpose is to:
- Collect rental income
- Manage, conserve, or maintain rental property

Must allocate expenses between rental and non-rental activities. Travel for improvements is capitalized and depreciated.

### Vacant Rental Property
While property is vacant but held for rental purposes:
- May deduct ordinary and necessary expenses (including depreciation)
- **Can't deduct** loss of rental income for vacancy period

### Uncollected Rent
- **Cash method**: Don't deduct uncollected rent (never included in income)
- **Accrual method**: May deduct as business bad debt if unable to collect

## Points on Rental Mortgages

Points (loan origination fees, discount points) are prepaid interest:

### De Minimis OID
Points are de minimis if less than 0.25% × stated redemption price × loan term in years.

**If de minimis**, choose one of:
1. Straight-line over loan term
2. Entirety at loan maturity
3. Proportion to stated interest payments
4. Constant-yield method

### Non-De Minimis
Must use constant-yield method to determine annual deduction.

### Loan Payoff
If loan ends (refinancing, prepayment, foreclosure), may deduct remaining points in that tax year. Exception: Refinancing with same lender - points generally deducted over new loan term.
