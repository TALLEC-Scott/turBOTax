# Contingent Payment Debt Instruments

## Overview

Contingent payment debt instruments are debt instruments issued after August 12, 1996, that provide for one or more payments contingent as to timing or amount. Special rules apply for figuring OID on these instruments.

## Key Characteristics

- Payments depend on future events or performance
- Cannot use standard constant yield method without projections
- Requires use of the "noncontingent bond method"

## Noncontingent Bond Method

### Overview
Issuers and holders must use this method because actual payments cannot be known in advance. It involves:
1. Determining a "comparable yield"
2. Creating a "projected payment schedule"
3. Accruing OID on the projected schedule
4. Making adjustments when actual payments differ

### Comparable Yield
The yield at which the issuer would issue a fixed-rate debt instrument with similar terms and conditions to the contingent payment instrument. Determined as of the issue date.

### Projected Payment Schedule
Includes:
- All fixed payments due under the instrument
- A projected fixed amount for each contingent payment

## OID Accrual Process

1. **Initial Accrual**: Use constant yield method on projected payment schedule
2. **Adjustment When Actual Differs from Projected**:
   - If actual > projected: Increase OID accruals
   - If actual < projected: Decrease OID accruals

## Covered Securities

Contingent payment debt instruments acquired on or after January 1, 2016, are "covered securities."

### Reporting on Form 8949
- Dispositions reported on Form 8949
- Column (g) adjustments with code O for noncontingent bond method
- Gains typically ordinary; losses ordinary to extent of prior OID inclusions

## Form 1099-OID Considerations

The amount shown in Box 1 may not be correct because:
- Actual contingent payments differed from projected amounts
- Adjustments needed to reflect true OID

**Action Required**: If Box 1 is incorrect, figure OID under the noncontingent bond method rules and make appropriate adjustment.
