# Traditional IRA

A Traditional IRA is a personal savings plan that offers tax advantages for retirement savings. Contributions may be tax-deductible, and earnings grow tax-deferred until withdrawn.

## Eligibility Requirements

To contribute to a Traditional IRA, you must:
- Have taxable compensation (wages, salaries, tips, self-employment income, taxable alimony)
- Be under age 73 (no age limit for contributions after 2019)

**Note:** You can contribute to a Traditional IRA regardless of whether you're covered by an employer retirement plan. However, deduction limits vary based on plan coverage and income.

## Contribution Limits (2025)

| Age | Maximum Contribution |
|-----|---------------------|
| Under 50 | $7,000 |
| 50 or older | $8,000 |

**Limitations:**
- Cannot exceed your taxable compensation for the year
- Reduced by contributions to other IRAs (combined limit with Roth IRA)
- Reduced by section 501(c)(18) plan contributions

### Kay Bailey Hutchison Spousal IRA

A spouse with little or no compensation can contribute to their own Traditional IRA if:
- Filing joint return
- Combined compensation covers both contributions
- Each spouse can contribute up to the annual limit ($14,000 combined for 2025; $16,000 if both 50+)

## Deductibility Rules

### Covered by Employer Retirement Plan

| Filing Status | Full Deduction | Partial Deduction | No Deduction |
|---------------|----------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Single/HOH | MAGI ≤ $79,000 | $79,001 - $89,000 | > $89,000 |
| MFJ/QSS | MAGI ≤ $126,000 | $126,001 - $146,000 | > $146,000 |
| MFS | N/A | $0 - $10,000 | > $10,000 |

### Not Covered, But Spouse Is Covered

| Filing Status | Full Deduction | Partial Deduction | No Deduction |
|---------------|----------------|-------------------|--------------|
| MFJ | MAGI ≤ $236,000 | $236,001 - $246,000 | > $246,000 |

### Not Covered by Any Plan

- **Full deduction** regardless of income level

See [[IRA Deductions and Phaseouts]] for detailed calculation worksheets.

## Nondeductible Contributions

If you can't deduct your Traditional IRA contribution, you can still make a nondeductible contribution:

- **Form 8606 required** - Must file to track basis
- **Cost basis** - Nondeductible contributions create basis that won't be taxed when distributed
- **Recordkeeping** - Use Appendix A of Pub 590-A to track contributions

### Penalties for Failure to File Form 8606
- $50 penalty for not filing
- $100 penalty for overstating nondeductible contributions

## Tax Treatment

| Aspect | Treatment |
|--------|-----------|
| Contributions | May be deductible (see above) |
| Earnings | Tax-deferred until withdrawn |
| Distributions | Taxed as ordinary income |
| Basis portion | Not taxed when distributed |

## Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)

- Must begin by April 1 of the year after reaching age 73
- Annual distributions required thereafter
- Failure to take RMD results in 25% excise tax (reduced from 50% by SECURE 2.0)
- See [[Required Minimum Distributions]] for details

## Rollovers and Transfers

### From Employer Plans
You can roll over distributions from:
- 401(k) plans
- 403(b) plans
- 457(b) plans
- Qualified pension plans

**20% withholding rule:** If distribution is paid directly to you, 20% is withheld. Use direct rollover to avoid withholding.

### IRA-to-IRA Rollovers
- Must be completed within 60 days
- One rollover per 12-month period per IRA owner (not per IRA)
- Trustee-to-trustee transfers have no frequency limit

See [[Rollovers]] for comprehensive rules.

## Conversions to Roth IRA

You can convert a Traditional IRA to a Roth IRA:
- **Income inclusion:** Must include converted amount in gross income (except basis)
- **No income limits:** Anyone can convert regardless of income
- **10% penalty does not apply** to conversion amounts
- **Cannot recharacterize** conversions after 2017

See [[Roth Conversions]] for detailed rules.

## Excess Contributions

An excess contribution occurs when you contribute more than:
- Your contribution limit ($7,000/$8,000)
- Your taxable compensation

**Penalty:** 6% excise tax per year until corrected

**Correction methods:**
1. Withdraw by due date (including extensions) - no penalty if earnings also withdrawn
2. Apply to a future year's contribution limit
3. Withdraw after due date under specific rules

See [[Excess IRA Contributions]] for details.

## Early Distribution Penalty

The 10% additional tax applies to distributions before age 59½, with exceptions for:
- Disability
- Death
- Substantially equal periodic payments
- Unreimbursed medical expenses > 7.5% AGI
- Health insurance while unemployed
- Qualified reservist distributions
- Birth or adoption (up to $5,000)
- Qualified disaster distributions

See [[Early Distribution Tax]] and [[Pub 590-B - Distributions from IRAs]].

## Types of Traditional IRAs

1. **Individual Retirement Account** - Trust or custodial account at a bank, credit union, or approved entity
2. **Individual Retirement Annuity** - Annuity contract from a life insurance company
3. **Employer/Employee Association Trust Account** - Trust for employees/members
4. **SEP-IRA** - Receives employer contributions under SEP arrangement
5. **SIMPLE IRA** - Receives employer contributions under SIMPLE plan (different rules apply)

## When to Contribute

- **Deadline:** Due date of return (April 15, 2026 for 2025), not including extensions
- **Extensions:** Filing extension does NOT extend contribution deadline
- **Prior year contributions:** Can contribute for prior year until April 15
