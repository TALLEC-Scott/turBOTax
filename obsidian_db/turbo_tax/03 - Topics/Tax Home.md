# Tax Home

## Overview

Your "tax home" is a critical concept for the [[Foreign Earned Income Exclusion]] and [[Foreign Housing Exclusion and Deduction]]. It determines whether you qualify for these benefits and has implications for travel expense deductibility.

**Key Point:** Your tax home is NOT necessarily where your family home is located or where you maintain your mailing address.

## Definition

Your **tax home** is your **principal place of business, employment, or post of duty**—regardless of where you maintain your family home.

### For Employees
Your tax home is generally the location of your main job or post of duty.

### For Self-Employed
Your tax home is generally your principal place of business.

## Requirements for FEIE

To qualify for the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion, your tax home must be:

1. **In a foreign country** (or countries)
2. **Throughout your period of bona fide residence or physical presence**

### What is a "Foreign Country"?

A foreign country includes:
- Any territory under the sovereignty of a government other than the United States
- The airspace above foreign countries
- U.S. embassies and consulates located in foreign countries

### NOT a Foreign Country:
- International waters
- U.S. territories (Puerto Rico, Guam, USVI, CNMI, American Samoa)
- Antarctica

## Determining Your Tax Home

### Primary Factors

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| **Business location** | Where do you perform most of your work? |
| **Time spent** | Where do you spend the most working time? |
| **Income source** | Where is your income generated? |
| **Employer location** | Where is your employer based? |

### Secondary Factors

- Where you maintain bank accounts
- Where you are registered to vote
- Where you hold licenses/professional registrations
- Where your business records are maintained

## Tax Home vs. Residence

These are DIFFERENT concepts:

| Tax Home | Residence |
|----------|-----------|
| Based on where you work | Based on where you live |
| Affects FEIE eligibility | Affects voting, state taxes |
| Principal place of business | Domicile/abode |
| Objective, work-based | Intent-based |

**Example:** You live in Paris with your family but work remotely for a U.S. company. Your tax home could be in the U.S. (where your employer is based) even though you reside in France.

## Temporary vs. Indefinite Assignment

### Temporary Assignment (Generally < 1 Year)
- Your tax home typically remains where it was before the assignment
- You may be considered "away from home" for travel expense purposes
- May NOT qualify for FEIE

### Indefinite Assignment (Generally > 1 Year)
- Your tax home moves to the foreign location
- Travel expenses to/from the foreign location are generally NOT deductible
- May qualify for FEIE

## Special Situations

### Multiple Work Locations
If you work in multiple locations, your tax home is your **principal place of business**. Consider:
- Total time spent at each location
- Business activity level at each location
- Income generated at each location

### No Regular Place of Business
If you have no regular place of business (e.g., itinerant worker), your tax home is generally your regular place of abode (where you regularly live).

### Remote Work
If you work remotely for a U.S. employer from a foreign country:
- Your tax home may be in the foreign country
- You must still meet bona fide residence or physical presence test
- Your employer's location doesn't control

## Common Issues

### Maintaining U.S. Tax Home
If your tax home remains in the U.S.:
- You CANNOT claim FEIE
- You may deduct travel expenses when working abroad temporarily
- Your worldwide income is still subject to U.S. tax

### Changing Tax Home
When your tax home moves to a foreign country:
- You may become eligible for FEIE
- Travel expenses to the foreign location are no longer deductible
- The move must be for an indefinite period

## Documentation

To establish your foreign tax home, document:
- Employment contract showing foreign assignment
- Duration of foreign work
- Location of your main work activities
- Business registrations in foreign country
- Lease or property ownership abroad

## Interaction with Other Tests

Having a foreign tax home is a PREREQUISITE for:
- [[Foreign Earned Income Exclusion]]
- [[Foreign Housing Exclusion and Deduction]]
- Using the [[Bona Fide Residence Test]] or [[Physical Presence Test]]
