# Federally Declared Disasters

## Overview

A federally declared disaster is a disaster determined by the **President of the United States** to warrant assistance by the federal government under the **Stafford Act**.

## Types of Declarations

### 1. Major Disaster Declaration
Declared under section 401 of the Stafford Act. Provides the broadest range of assistance.

### 2. Emergency Declaration
Provides more limited federal assistance than major disaster declarations.

## Eligible Areas

Disaster areas include counties designated as eligible for:
- **Public Assistance** - For state/local government and certain nonprofits
- **Individual Assistance** - For individuals and households
- **Both** public and individual assistance

Check eligible areas at **FEMA.gov/Disaster**

## FEMA Declaration Numbers

When reporting casualty/theft losses attributable to federally declared disasters:

1. Check the box on Form 4684
2. Enter the FEMA declaration number (DR-XXXX or EM-XXXX)

**Example**: DR-4865 for Arkansas Severe Storms and Tornadoes

## Significance for Tax Purposes

### Personal-Use Property (Post-2017)

Casualty and theft losses of personal-use property are **only deductible if** attributable to a federally declared disaster.

**Exception**: Personal casualty losses not from federal disasters may offset personal casualty gains.

### Business/Income-Producing Property

Federally declared disaster status is **not required** for deduction, but provides additional benefits:
- Prior year election to accelerate deduction
- Extended replacement periods
- Postponed tax deadlines

## Types of Losses Tied to Federal Disasters

| Loss Type | Description | Special Rules |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Federal Casualty Loss | Personal-use property in federally declared disaster | Standard $100/10% rules apply |
| [[Disaster Area Losses\|Disaster Loss]] | Property in eligible area | Can elect prior year deduction |
| [[Qualified Disaster Loss]] | Specific major disasters | Enhanced benefits |

## Qualified State-Declared Disasters

For disasters declared after July 24, 2025, qualified state-declared disasters may also qualify for:
- Mandatory 120-day deadline postponement
- Certain tax relief benefits

## Tax Deadline Postponements

When a federal disaster is declared, the IRS may postpone:

- Filing income tax returns
- Paying income, excise, and employment taxes
- Making IRA contributions
- Other specified deadlines

### Lookback Period Extension (December 2025)

Disaster-related postponements are treated as filing extensions for calculating the lookback period for credit/refund claims.

## How to Check Disaster Status

1. **FEMA.gov/Disaster** - List of declared disasters and eligible areas
2. **IRS.gov/Disaster** - Tax relief announcements
3. **IRS.gov/DisasterTaxRelief** - Qualified disaster loss information
