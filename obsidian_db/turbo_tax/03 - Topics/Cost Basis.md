# Cost Basis

## Overview

Cost basis is the starting point for determining the basis of most property. It represents the amount of your investment in property for tax purposes and is used to calculate depreciation, amortization, depletion, casualty losses, and gain or loss on disposition.

## What Is Included in Cost

The basis of property you buy is usually its cost, which includes:

- **Cash paid** for the property
- **Debt obligations** assumed or taken subject to
- **Sales tax** and excise taxes
- **Installation and testing** fees
- **Freight charges** for delivery
- **Legal and accounting fees** (when they must be capitalized)
- **Revenue stamps** and recording fees
- **Real estate taxes** assumed from the seller

## Special Situations

### Loans with Low or No Interest
If you buy property on a time-payment plan with little or no interest, the basis is generally the stated purchase price minus unstated interest or original issue discount. This applies when the interest rate is less than the applicable federal rate.

### Bargain Purchases
If you purchase property at less than fair market value (FMV) as compensation for services, include the difference between purchase price and FMV in income. Your basis is the FMV.

### Property Received for Services
If you receive property for services, include the FMV in income. That amount becomes your basis.

## Stocks and Bonds

- Basis = Purchase price + Commissions + Recording/transfer fees
- Must adjust basis for certain events after purchase
- If shares can't be adequately identified, use FIFO method
- Mutual fund shares may use average basis

## Real Property

For real estate, basis includes settlement fees and closing costs but NOT loan-related fees:

**Include in Basis:**
- Abstract fees
- Legal fees (title search, sales contract, deed)
- Utility installation charges
- Recording fees
- Transfer taxes
- Surveys
- Owner's title insurance
- Amounts seller owes that you agree to pay

**Don't Include:**
- Casualty insurance premiums
- Rent before closing
- Utility charges before closing
- Loan-related fees (points, mortgage insurance, credit report, appraisal)

## Constructing Assets

If you build property, basis includes:
- Land costs
- Architect's fees
- Labor and materials
- Building permit charges
- Equipment rental
- Contractor payments
- Inspection fees

**Note:** Don't include the value of your own unpaid labor.
