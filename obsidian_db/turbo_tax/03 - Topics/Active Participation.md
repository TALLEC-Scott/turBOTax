# Active Participation

## Overview

Active participation is a **lower standard** than [[Material Participation|material participation]] that allows certain taxpayers to claim the special $25,000 allowance for rental real estate losses. It applies specifically to rental real estate activities.

## Difference from Material Participation

| Standard | Requirement | Effect |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Active Participation | Make management decisions in significant and bona fide sense | Up to $25,000 loss deduction (phaseout applies) |
| Material Participation | Meet one of seven tests (500+ hours, etc.) | Activity is nonpassive (real estate professional required for rentals) |

Active participation is significantly easier to achieve than material participation.

## What Qualifies as Active Participation

You may be treated as actively participating if you make **management decisions** in a **significant and bona fide sense**.

### Examples of Management Decisions

- Approving new tenants
- Deciding on rental terms (rent amount, lease duration)
- Approving expenditures for the rental property
- Other similar management decisions

### What is NOT Required

- Day-to-day involvement in the rental activity
- Specific number of hours of participation
- Regular and continuous involvement

## Ownership Requirement

You aren't treated as actively participating in a rental real estate activity unless:

- Your interest in the activity (including your spouse's interest) was **at least 10%** (by value) of all interests in the activity
- This requirement must be met throughout the year

## Who Can Active Participate

### Individuals
Only individuals can actively participate in rental real estate activities.

### Decedent's Estate
A decedent's estate is treated as actively participating for tax years ending **less than 2 years** after the decedent's death if:
- The decedent would have satisfied the active participation requirement for the year of death

### Decedent's Qualified Revocable Trust
Can be treated as actively participating if:
- The trustee and executor (if any) elect to treat the trust as part of the estate
- The election applies to tax years ending after death and before:
  - 2 years after death (if no estate tax return required), OR
  - 6 months after estate tax liability is finally determined (if estate tax return required)
- Election is irrevocable and must be made by the due date of the estate's first income tax return (including extensions)

## Limited Partners

Limited partners are generally **not** treated as actively participating in a partnership's rental real estate activities.

## Credits Not Requiring Active Participation

Active participation is **not required** for:
- Low-income housing credit from rental real estate activities
- Rehabilitation credit from rental real estate activities

## Effect on Loss Deduction

### Special $25,000 Allowance

If you actively participate in a rental real estate activity, you may deduct up to $25,000 of loss against nonpassive income:

| Filing Status | Maximum Allowance |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Single, MFJ | $25,000 |
| MFS (lived apart all year) | $12,500 |
| MFS (lived together at any time) | $0 |

### Phaseout

The allowance is reduced by 50% of MAGI over threshold:

| Filing Status | Phaseout Begins | Phaseout Complete |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Single, MFJ | $100,000 | $150,000 |
| MFS (lived apart all year) | $50,000 | $75,000 |

**Modified AGI** excludes:
- Taxable Social Security and Tier 1 railroad retirement benefits
- Deductible IRA and section 501(c)(18) contributions
- Qualified savings bond interest exclusion
- Employer adoption assistance exclusion
- Passive activity income/loss on Form 8582
- Rental real estate loss allowed as real estate professional
- Self-employment tax deduction
- Student loan interest deduction
- FDII and GILTI

## Examples

### Example 1: Active Participation
Stacey owns a rental house. She:
- Advertised and rented the house to the current tenant
- Collected the rents
- Did repairs or hired someone to do them

**Result:** Stacey actively participated and can deduct her $4,000 rental loss against her other income (subject to phaseout rules).

### Example 2: Income and Phaseout
You are single, not a real estate professional. You have:
- $120,000 salary
- $31,000 loss from rental real estate in which you actively participated

**Calculation:**
- MAGI = $120,000
- Reduction = ($120,000 - $100,000) × 50% = $10,000
- Adjusted allowance = $25,000 - $10,000 = $15,000
- Deduction = $15,000
- Carryover = $16,000
