# Child Tax Credit

## Overview

The Child Tax Credit (CTC) provides up to **$2,000 per qualifying child** under age 17 at the end of the tax year.

## Eligibility Requirements

Per [[Pub 972]] and [[Pub 501]], a child must:

| Test | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Age | Under 17 at end of tax year |
| Relationship | Son, daughter, stepchild, foster child, sibling, or descendant |
| Residency | Lived with you for more than half the year |
| Support | Did not provide more than half of their own support |
| Citizenship | US citizen, national, or resident alien |

## 2025 Updates

Beginning in 2025, the maximum CTC amount increased to **$2,200** per qualifying child.

### SSN Requirement
To claim CTC or ACTC, you must have a valid SSN (valid for employment) issued before the due date of your return (including extensions). If filing jointly, only one spouse needs a valid SSN; the other needs either an SSN or ITIN.

### Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC)
- Refundable credit for those who can't claim full CTC
- **2025 amount:** $1,700 per qualifying child
- Refunds delayed until mid-February for returns claiming ACTC

## Military Considerations

Per [[Pub 3 - Armed Forces Tax Guide]]:
- Temporary absences for military service count as time lived with you
- Extended active duty outside the U.S. counts as living in the U.S. for EIC purposes
- Must file Form 8862 if CTC, ACTC, or ODC was previously denied
