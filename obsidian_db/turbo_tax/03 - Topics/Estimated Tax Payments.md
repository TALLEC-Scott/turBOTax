# Estimated Tax Payments

## Overview

Estimated tax is the method used to pay tax on income not subject to withholding, including self-employment income, interest, dividends, alimony, rents, and capital gains. The U.S. tax system is pay-as-you-go, meaning you must pay taxes throughout the year as income is earned.

## Who Must Pay Estimated Tax

### General Rule
Generally required if you expect to owe **$1,000 or more** at tax time AND:
- Have self-employment income
- Receive interest, dividends, or alimony
- Have rental or royalty income
- Realize capital gains
- Receive income not subject to withholding

### Not Required If
- Tax due will be less than $1,000, OR
- Had zero tax liability in prior year (certain restrictions apply), OR
- Withholding covers 90% of current year tax

## 2025 Quarterly Due Dates

| Quarter | Period Covered | Due Date |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| 1 | January 1 - March 31 | April 15, 2025 |
| 2 | April 1 - May 31 | June 16, 2025 |
| 3 | June 1 - August 31 | September 15, 2025 |
| 4 | September 1 - December 31 | January 15, 2026 |

### When Due Date Falls on Weekend/Holiday
Payment is due the next business day.

## How Much to Pay

### Safe Harbor Rules
Avoid underpayment penalty by paying the **lesser of:**

1. **90% of current year's tax liability**, OR
2. **100% of prior year's tax** (110% if AGI exceeded $150,000 on prior year return)

### Calculating Estimated Tax
Use **Form 1040-ES** worksheets:
1. Estimate expected income
2. Subtract deductions and exemptions
3. Calculate tax using rate schedules
4. Subtract credits
5. Divide by 4 for quarterly payments

### Annualized Income Installment Method
For income received unevenly throughout the year:
- Use Form 2210 Schedule AI
- Calculate tax based on actual income each period
- Must file Form 2210 with tax return

## Payment Methods

### Electronic Payments (Recommended)
1. **IRS Direct Pay** - IRS.gov/Payments (free, from bank account)
2. **EFTPS** - EFTPS.gov (free, requires enrollment)
3. **Debit/Credit Card** - Fees apply via payment processors

### By Mail
- Send check/money order with Form 1040-ES payment voucher
- Make payable to "United States Treasury"
- Include SSN, tax year, and "Form 1040-ES" on payment

### IRS2Go Mobile App
- Make payments via mobile device
- Direct Pay or debit/credit card options

## Adjusting Estimated Payments

### When to Recalculate
- Significant income change
- Major deduction/credit change
- Mid-year life event

### Amended Estimated Tax
Use Worksheet 2-10 in [[Pub 505 - Tax Withholding and Estimated Tax|Pub 505]] to figure revised payments.

## Special Rules

### Farmers and Fishermen
Special rules if farming/fishing is primary income:
- **Only one payment** due January 15 (or file by March 1 and pay in full)
- **Lower safe harbor:** 66.67% of current year tax
- At least 2/3 of gross income from farming/fishing

### Higher Income Earners
If AGI exceeded $150,000 on prior year return:
- Must pay **110% of prior year tax** for safe harbor
- Applies to both MFJ and MFS

### Household Employers
- May include household employment taxes in estimated payments
- Use Schedule H (Form 1040) to calculate

### Nonresident Aliens
- Use Form 1040-ES (NR)
- Special income rules apply
- See [[Pub 519 - U.S. Tax Guide for Aliens|Pub 519]]

## Self-Employment Tax

Self-employed individuals must pay both:
1. **Income tax** (via estimated payments)
2. **Self-Employment tax** (Social Security + Medicare)

### 2025 SE Tax Rates
- Social Security: 12.4% on first $176,100
- Medicare: 2.9% on all earnings
- Additional Medicare: 0.9% on earnings over threshold

**Deduction:** Half of SE tax is deductible on Schedule 1.

See [[Self-Employment Tax]] for detailed rules.

## Penalty for Underpayment

### Penalty Triggers
Penalty applies if required payments not met:
- Did not pay 90% of current year tax, AND
- Did not pay 100%/110% of prior year tax

### Penalty Calculation
- Based on amount underpaid × time unpaid
- Interest compounds daily
- IRS calculates on Form 2210

### Penalty Exceptions
- Tax due < $1,000
- Casualty, disaster, or unusual circumstance
- Retired after age 62 or became disabled during year
- Reasonable cause (not willful neglect)

See [[Underpayment Penalty]] for details.

## Estimated Tax Worksheet Reference

### Worksheet 2-1 (Pub 505)
Use to figure estimated tax for 2025:
1. Expected AGI
2. Adjustments to income
3. Standard or itemized deductions
4. Tax credits
5. Other taxes (SE tax, AMT, NIIT)
6. Calculate required payment

### Using Prior Year Safe Harbor
If prior year tax was $10,000:
- Regular taxpayer: Pay $2,500 per quarter ($10,000 total)
- Higher income (AGI > $150K): Pay $2,750 per quarter ($11,000 total)

## Applying Overpayment to Next Year

- Can apply prior year refund to next year's estimated tax
- Indicate on tax return (Form 1040, Line 36)
- Applies to first estimated payment (April 15)
