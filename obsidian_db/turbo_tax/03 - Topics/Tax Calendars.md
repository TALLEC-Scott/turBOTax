# Tax Calendars

## Overview

Tax calendars are 12-month calendars divided into quarters that provide specific due dates for filing tax forms, paying taxes, and taking other actions required by federal tax law. The IRS provides multiple calendars for different taxpayer types.

## Types of Tax Calendars

### 1. General Tax Calendar
The primary calendar covering deadlines for:
- **Individuals**: Form 1040, estimated tax payments
- **Partnerships**: Form 1065, Schedule K-1 distribution
- **Corporations**: Form 1120, Form 1120-S
- **Information returns**: Forms 1099, W-2, 1098

### 2. Employer's Tax Calendar
Comprehensive deadlines for employers including:
- Employment tax deposits (FICA, income tax withholding)
- Quarterly returns (Form 941)
- Annual returns (Form 940, Form 944, Form 943)
- Information returns (Form W-2, W-3)

### 3. Excise Tax Calendar
Specific calendar for excise tax obligations:
- Quarterly Form 720 filings
- Heavy highway vehicle use tax (Form 2290)
- Wagering taxes (Forms 730, 11-C)

## Calendar Year Quarters

| Quarter | Months | Common Due Dates |
|---------|--------|------------------|
| Q1 | January, February, March | April 15 (individuals), April 30 (Form 941) |
| Q2 | April, May, June | July 15 (estimated tax), July 31 (Form 941) |
| Q3 | July, August, September | October 15 (extended returns), October 31 (Form 941) |
| Q4 | October, November, December | January 15 (estimated tax), January 31 (Form 941) |

## Key Individual Deadlines

### Income Tax Filing
- **April 15**: Form 1040 due (calendar year taxpayers)
- **October 15**: Extended filing deadline (Form 4868)

### Estimated Tax Payments
- **January 15**: 4th installment for prior year
- **April 15**: 1st installment for current year
- **June 15**: 2nd installment
- **September 15**: 3rd installment

## Key Employer Deadlines

### Form 941 (Quarterly)
| Quarter | Due Date |
|---------|----------|
| Q1 (Jan-Mar) | April 30 |
| Q2 (Apr-Jun) | July 31 |
| Q3 (Jul-Sep) | October 31 |
| Q4 (Oct-Dec) | January 31 |

### Form 940 (FUTA)
- **January 31**: Annual return due
- **February 10**: Extended deadline if all taxes deposited timely

### Form W-2
- **January 31**: Furnish to employees and file with SSA

## Key Business Deadlines

### Partnerships (Form 1065)
- **March 15**: Calendar year return due
- **September 15**: Extended return due
- Furnish Schedule K-1 to partners by return due date

### S Corporations (Form 1120-S)
- **March 15**: Calendar year return due
- **September 15**: Extended return due
- Furnish Schedule K-1 to shareholders by return due date

### C Corporations (Form 1120)
- **April 15**: Calendar year return due
- **October 15**: Extended return due

## Information Return Deadlines

| Form | Furnish to Recipient | File with IRS (Paper) | File with IRS (Electronic) |
|------|---------------------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| 1099-NEC | February 2 | February 2 | February 2 |
| 1099-MISC, 1099-INT, etc. | February 2 | March 2 | March 31 |
| 1099-B, 1099-S | February 17 | March 2 | March 31 |
| W-2 | January 31 | January 31 | January 31 |
| W-2G | February 2 | March 2 | March 31 |

## Weekend and Holiday Rules

When a due date falls on:
- **Saturday or Sunday**: Deadline moves to next business day
- **Legal holiday**: Deadline moves to next business day

### Important Note
- Legal holidays are those observed in the District of Columbia
- Statewide legal holidays may affect filing deadlines for residents
- Legal holidays do NOT delay federal tax deposit deadlines

## Fiscal Year Taxpayers

If using a fiscal year instead of calendar year, adjust dates:

### Form 1040
- Due 15th day of 4th month after year-end
- Extended due date: 6 months later

### Form 1065 (Partnerships)
- Due 15th day of 3rd month after year-end

### Form 1120/1120-S (Corporations)
- **C Corps**: Due 15th day of 4th month after year-end
- **S Corps**: Due 15th day of 3rd month after year-end

## Advantages of Using Tax Calendars

1. File and pay on time to avoid penalties
2. Don't have to figure due dates yourself
3. Calendar adjusts for weekends and holidays
4. Semiweekly deposit dates pre-calculated
