# Social Security and Medicare Taxes

## Overview

Social Security and Medicare taxes (collectively known as FICA taxes) are employment taxes that fund the Social Security and Medicare programs. Both employers and employees share responsibility for paying these taxes.

## Tax Rates and Limits (2026)

### Social Security Tax (OASDI)
- **Tax rate:** 6.2% for employer + 6.2% for employee = 12.4% total
- **Wage base limit:** $184,500 (2026)
- Tax applies only to wages up to the wage base limit
- Once an employee's wages exceed the limit, no more social security tax is due for that year

### Medicare Tax (HI)
- **Tax rate:** 1.45% for employer + 1.45% for employee = 2.9% total
- **No wage base limit** - applies to all covered wages
- **Additional Medicare Tax:** 0.9% on wages over $200,000 (employee only, no employer match)

## Special Thresholds (2026)

| Worker Type | Threshold | Applies If |
|-------------|-----------|------------|
| Household workers | $3,000 | Cash wages in 2026 |
| Election workers | $2,500 | Cash or equivalent compensation |

## Employer Responsibilities

1. **Withhold** the employee's share from each wage payment
2. **Pay** the employer's share
3. **Deposit** both shares together using EFTPS or other approved methods
4. **Report** on quarterly Form 941 or annual Form 943/944

## Wages Subject to FICA

Most wages are subject to social security and Medicare taxes, including:
- Salaries, wages, bonuses, commissions
- Tips (if $20 or more per month)
- Sick pay (third-party payments)
- Certain fringe benefits

## Exemptions and Special Rules

### Exempt Payments
- Qualified disability payments from terrorist attacks
- Some religious exemptions (Form 4029)
- Nonresident alien students and scholars (F-1, J-1, M-1, Q-1 visas)
- Family employees under age 18 (parent's business)

### Special Worker Categories
- **Statutory employees:** Subject to FICA (employer pays both shares)
- **Statutory nonemployees:** Not subject to FICA
- **Independent contractors:** Not subject to FICA (self-employment tax applies)

## Additional Medicare Tax (0.9%)

Employees must pay an additional 0.9% Medicare tax on wages exceeding:
- $200,000 (Single, Head of Household, Qualifying Surviving Spouse)
- $250,000 (Married Filing Jointly)
- $125,000 (Married Filing Separately)

**Employer responsibility:** Begin withholding when wages exceed $200,000 for an employee (regardless of filing status).
