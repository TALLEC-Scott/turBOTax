# Employee Compensation

## Overview

Employee compensation includes all remuneration received for personal services. In most cases, everything received as payment for services must be included in gross income, including wages, salaries, commissions, fees, tips, and other forms of compensation like fringe benefits and stock options.

## Key Rules

### General Rule

All compensation for personal services is taxable income unless specifically exempted by law. This includes:

- **Wages and salaries** - All pay for services performed
- **Commissions and fees** - Including advance commissions
- **Bonuses and awards** - Cash, goods, or services received
- **Tips** - All tips are taxable income
- **Severance pay** - Taxable as wages

### Constructive Receipt

Income is taxable when it is made available to you, regardless of whether you actually possess it. A valid check available before year-end is income in that year.

### Assignment of Income

If you agree that a third party receives income for you, you must include it in your income when the third party receives it.

## Types of Compensation

### Wages and Salaries

Report all wages on Form 1040, line 1a. You should receive Form W-2 from your employer showing wages paid.

### Bonuses and Awards

Cash bonuses and the fair market value of non-cash awards are taxable. Employee achievement awards may be excluded up to $1,600 if certain requirements are met.

### Tips

All tips are taxable income. May be able to claim deduction for qualified tips on 2025 returns. See [[Tips and Tip Reporting]] for details.

### Severance Pay

Taxable as wages. Subject to Social Security, Medicare, and income tax withholding. See [[Pub 525 - Taxable and Nontaxable Income]].

### Back Pay

Awards for back pay are taxable, including damages and unpaid insurance premiums.

### Differential Wage Payments

Payments by employer to active duty military members representing wages they would have received. Treated as wages subject to income tax withholding but not FICA or FUTA.

### Childcare Providers

Pay received for childcare services is taxable. May be self-employed if not subject to employer's control over how work is performed.

## Special Situations

### Bankruptcy

If you filed Chapter 11 bankruptcy, you must allocate wages and withheld taxes between yourself and the bankruptcy estate.

### Sick Pay

Sick pay from employer while ill or injured is taxable salary. Also taxable if received from:
- Welfare fund
- State sickness/disability fund
- Insurance company if employer paid premiums
- Association of employers/employees

### Nonqualified Deferred Compensation

Employer reports deferrals in Form W-2, box 12, code Y. Not included in income until distributed or if plan fails requirements.

## Reporting Requirements

- **Form W-2** - Shows all wages, tips, and other compensation
- **Form 1040, Line 1a** - Report wages and salaries
- **Form 8919** - File if employer didn't withhold Social Security/Medicare taxes

### Q: What if I don't receive Form W-2?
**A:** You must still report all wages on your tax return. Contact employer for copy, or use Form 4852 as substitute.

### Q: Are gift certificates from my employer taxable?
**A:** Yes. Cash, gift certificates, or similar items are taxable regardless of amount, even holiday gifts.

### Q: Is severance pay subject to employment taxes?
**A:** Yes. Severance is subject to Social Security, Medicare, income tax withholding, and FUTA.
