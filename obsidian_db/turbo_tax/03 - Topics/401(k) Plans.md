# 401(k) Plans

## Overview

A 401(k) plan is a qualified retirement plan that includes a cash or deferred arrangement (CODA) under which participants can choose to have the employer contribute part of their before-tax compensation to the plan rather than receive it in cash. 401(k) plans offer higher contribution limits and greater flexibility than SEP or SIMPLE plans.

## Types of 401(k) Plans

### Traditional 401(k)
- Subject to ADP (Actual Deferral Percentage) and ACP (Actual Contribution Percentage) tests
- Must satisfy nondiscrimination rules
- Pre-tax elective deferrals

### Safe Harbor 401(k)
- Exempt from ADP and ACP testing
- Required employer contributions (matching or nonelective)
- Mandatory immediate vesting

### SIMPLE 401(k)
- For employers with 100 or fewer employees
- Not subject to nondiscrimination or top-heavy rules
- Lower contribution limits

## Elective Deferral Limits (2025)

| Participant Age | Deferral Limit |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Under 50 | $23,500 |
| Age 50+ | $31,000 ($23,500 + $7,500 catch-up) |
| Ages 60-63 | $34,750 ($23,500 + $11,250 enhanced catch-up) |

## Elective Deferral Limits (2026)

| Participant Age | Deferral Limit |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Under 50 | $24,500 |
| Age 50+ | $32,500 ($24,500 + $8,000 catch-up) |
| Ages 60-63 | $35,750 ($24,500 + $11,250 enhanced catch-up) |

## Annual Addition Limits

| Year | Limit |
|------|-------|
| 2025 | Lesser of $70,000 or 100% of compensation |
| 2026 | Lesser of $72,000 or 100% of compensation |

## Compensation Limit

- 2025: $350,000
- 2026: $360,000

## Employer Contributions

### Matching Contributions
- Can match employee elective deferrals
- Subject to ACP test (unless safe harbor)
- Example: 50 cents for each dollar deferred

### Nonelective Contributions
- Made regardless of employee deferrals
- Profit-sharing style contributions
- Deduction limit: 25% of covered payroll

## Safe Harbor 401(k) Requirements

### Matching Contribution Option
1. 100% match on deferrals up to 3% of compensation
2. 50% match on deferrals from 3% to 5% of compensation

### Nonelective Contribution Option
- 3% of compensation for all eligible non-highly compensated employees

**Note**: Safe harbor contributions must be 100% immediately vested.

## Automatic Enrollment (SECURE 2.0)

- Required for plans established on or after December 29, 2022 (with exceptions)
- Default deferral percentage: At least 3% (increasing to 6% over time)
- Employees can opt out or change contribution percentage

### Qualified Automatic Contribution Arrangement (QACA)
- Exempt from ADP test and top-heavy rules
- Mandatory employer contributions
- Two-year vesting requirement for employer contributions

## Participation Requirements

- Cannot require more than 1 year of service for participation
- Alternative: First period of 2 consecutive 12-month periods with 500+ hours each

## Vesting Rules

| Contribution Type | Maximum Vesting Schedule |
|-------------------|-------------------------|
| Elective Deferrals | 100% immediately vested |
| Safe Harbor Contributions | 100% immediately vested |
| QACA Employer Contributions | 2-year cliff vesting |
| Other Employer Contributions | 3-year cliff or 6-year graded |

## Distribution Rules

### Permissible Distribution Events
- Separation from service
- Death or disability
- Attainment of age 59½
- Plan termination
- Hardship (limited circumstances)
- Qualified reservist distribution

### Required Minimum Distributions
- Begin by April 1 after year participant reaches age 73
- Special rules for designated Roth accounts (no lifetime RMDs)

## Tax Treatment

### Pre-tax Deferrals
- Excluded from gross income in year of deferral
- Subject to Social Security, Medicare, FUTA taxes
- Taxable upon distribution

### Roth Contributions
- Included in gross income in year of contribution
- Qualified distributions are tax-free
- No lifetime RMD requirements

## Loans

- Permitted if plan allows
- Maximum loan: Lesser of $50,000 or 50% of vested balance
- Must be repaid within 5 years (except residence loans)

## Forms and Reporting

- **Form W-2**: Report deferrals in box 12, check retirement plan box
- **Form 5500**: Annual return/report (except one-participant plans)
- **Form 5500-EZ**: One-participant plan annual return
