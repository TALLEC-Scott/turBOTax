# Real Estate Professional

## Overview

The Real Estate Professional status is a critical exception to the general rule that rental activities are always passive. If you qualify, your rental real estate activities in which you materially participate are treated as **nonpassive**, allowing you to deduct losses without the passive activity limitations.

This is particularly valuable for taxpayers with significant rental real estate losses.

## Qualification Requirements

You qualify as a real estate professional for the year if you meet **BOTH** requirements:

### Requirement 1: More Than Half of Personal Services
More than half of the personal services you performed in all trades or businesses during the tax year were performed in **real property trades or businesses** in which you materially participated.

### Requirement 2: More Than 750 Hours
You performed **more than 750 hours of services** during the tax year in real property trades or businesses in which you materially participated.

## Employee Services

Don't count personal services performed as an employee in real property trades or businesses **unless** you were a **5% owner** of your employer.

You're a 5% owner if you owned (or are considered to have owned) more than 5% of your employer's:
- Outstanding stock
- Outstanding voting stock
- Capital or profits interest

## Joint Returns

If you file a joint return:
- **Don't count** your spouse's personal services for the qualification tests
- **Can count** your spouse's participation for determining material participation

## Real Property Trades or Businesses

A real property trade or business is any trade or business that does any of the following with real property:

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Development | Maintaining and improving raw land for subdivision, development, or construction |
| Redevelopment | Demolition and removal of existing buildings to prepare for new development |
| Acquisition | Purchasing real property |
| Construction | Building or reconstructing real property |
| Conversion | Changing property use or type |
| Operations | Day-to-day operations relating to maintenance and occupancy |
| Management | Professional management of real properties |
| Renting/Leasing | Making property available to tenants |
| Brokering | Acting as real estate broker or agent |

## Reporting

If you qualified as a real estate professional for 2025:
- Report income or losses from rental real estate activities as **nonpassive** income or losses
- Complete line 43 of Schedule E (Form 1040)

## Multiple Rental Real Estate Activities

For purposes of determining material participation in rental real estate activities:
- Each interest in rental real estate is generally a **separate activity**
- You may elect to treat all interests in rental real estate as **one activity**
- See Revenue Procedure 2011-34 for guidance on late elections

## Closely Held Corporations

A closely held corporation can qualify as a real estate professional if:
- More than **50% of gross receipts** for the tax year came from real property trades or businesses
- The corporation materially participated in those trades or businesses

## Effect on Passive Losses

If you qualify:

1. **Current year losses** - Rental real estate losses are not subject to passive activity limits if you materially participate
2. **Prior-year suspended losses** - See "Treatment of former passive activities" for using suspended losses from years you didn't qualify
3. **Net Investment Income Tax** - May also exclude rental income from NIIT

## Planning Considerations

### Documentation
Maintain detailed records of:
- Hours spent on each real property activity
- Nature of services performed
- Dates and times of activities

### Grouping Election
Consider whether to elect to treat all rental real estate as one activity:
- **Pros**: Easier to meet material participation tests
- **Cons**: Partial dispositions don't free up suspended losses
