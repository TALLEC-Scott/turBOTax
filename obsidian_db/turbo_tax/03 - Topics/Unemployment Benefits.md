# Unemployment Benefits

## Overview

Unemployment compensation is generally taxable income. You must include all unemployment benefits you receive in your gross income for the year received.

## General Rule

All unemployment compensation is taxable income. Report on Schedule 1 (Form 1040), line 7.

### What Is Unemployment Compensation

- State unemployment insurance benefits
- Benefits from Federal Unemployment Trust Fund
- Railroad unemployment compensation
- Trade readjustment allowances under Trade Act of 1974
- Unemployment assistance under Airline Deregulation Act
- Disaster Relief Act unemployment assistance
- Disability payments as substitute for unemployment compensation

## Form 1099-G

You should receive Form 1099-G showing total unemployment compensation paid in box 1.

**Note:** Many states no longer mail Form 1099-G. You may need to access it through your state's website.

## Reporting Unemployment Benefits

### Standard Reporting

Report net benefits (total received minus repayments made same year) on Schedule 1, line 7.

Write "Repaid" and the amount on the dotted line next to your entry if you repaid benefits in the same year received.

### Repayment of Benefits

**Same Year Repayment:**
Subtract repayment from total received. Report the difference.

**Prior Year Repayment (More Than $3,000):**
- Deduct on Schedule A, line 16, OR
- Take credit on Schedule 3, line 13b

**Prior Year Repayment ($3,000 or Less):**
Deduct on Schedule A if itemizing.

## Tax Withholding

You can choose to have 10% federal income tax withheld from unemployment compensation.

**To Elect Withholding:**
- Complete Form W-4V
- Submit to the paying agency

**Warning:** If you don't have tax withheld, you may need to make estimated tax payments to avoid penalties.

## Types of Unemployment Benefits

### State Unemployment Insurance

Primary unemployment benefits from your state. Fully taxable.

### Railroad Unemployment Benefits

Taxable unemployment compensation from Railroad Retirement Board.

### Trade Readjustment Allowances

Taxable benefits under Trade Act of 1974 for workers who lose jobs due to foreign trade.

### Disaster Unemployment Assistance

Taxable benefits under Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act.

## Supplemental Unemployment Benefits

Benefits from employer-financed funds (employees didn't contribute) are NOT unemployment compensation.

**Tax Treatment:**
- Taxable as wages
- Subject to income tax withholding
- May be subject to Social Security and Medicare taxes
- Report on Form 1040, line 1a

## Other Benefit Types

### Union Benefits

Benefits from union dues: Report on Schedule 1, line 8z.

Benefits from special fund with nondeductible contributions: Include only amount exceeding your contributions.

### Private Unemployment Fund

Taxable only if benefits exceed your contributions to the fund. Report on Schedule 1, line 8z.

### Guaranteed Annual Wage

Payments under union agreement guaranteeing full-year pay are taxable wages. Report on Form 1040, line 1a.

### State Employee Payments

State payments to employees not covered by state unemployment law are fully taxable but NOT reported as unemployment compensation. Report on Schedule 1, line 8z.

## Reemployment Trade Adjustment Assistance (RTAA)

RTAA payments from state agencies are taxable. Report on Schedule 1, line 8z. You should receive Form 1099-G.

## Common Questions

### Q: I didn't receive Form 1099-G. How do I report my unemployment?
**A:** Check your state unemployment agency website for electronic Form 1099-G. Report benefits even if you don't receive the form.

### Q: Can I have more than 10% withheld?
**A:** No, only 10% withholding is available for unemployment compensation. Consider making estimated tax payments for additional coverage.

### Q: What if I repaid unemployment benefits?
**A:** If repaid same year, subtract from benefits received. If repaid in later year and over $3,000, you may deduct or claim credit.

### Q: Are pandemic unemployment benefits taxable?
**A:** Yes, most pandemic-related unemployment benefits were taxable (though the American Rescue Plan excluded some 2020 benefits up to $10,200).
