# Sale of Home

## Overview

When you sell your home, you may be able to exclude up to $250,000 ($500,000 if married filing jointly) of gain from your income. Understanding the rules for calculating and excluding gain is essential for tax planning.

## Exclusion of Gain

### Maximum Exclusion

| Filing Status | Maximum Exclusion |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Single | $250,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $500,000 |
| Married Filing Separately | $250,000 each (if both meet tests) |

### Ownership and Use Tests

To qualify for the exclusion, you must meet BOTH tests:

1. **Ownership Test**: You owned the home for at least 2 of the 5 years before the sale
2. **Use Test**: You lived in the home as your main home for at least 2 of the 5 years before the sale

### Period of Ownership and Use

- The 2 years of ownership and use can be **non-consecutive**
- Temporary absences count as use (vacations, illness, education)
- Short absences are generally ignored

## Calculating Gain or Loss

### Formula

```
Amount Realized
Minus: Adjusted Basis
Equals: Gain or Loss
```

### Amount Realized

Amount realized = Selling price minus:
- Real estate commissions
- Advertising costs
- Legal fees
- Title insurance
- Transfer taxes
- Other selling expenses

### Adjusted Basis

See [[Basis of Home]] for complete rules. Generally:
- Original cost
- Plus: Cost of improvements
- Minus: Depreciation (if rental/business use)
- Minus: Energy credits claimed

## Main Home

You can have only **one main home** at a time. Your main home is where you live most of the time.

Types of homes that qualify:
- House
- Condominium
- Cooperative apartment
- Mobile home
- Houseboat
- House trailer

Must have sleeping, cooking, and toilet facilities.

## Second Home

If you have a second home, you **cannot** exclude gain from its sale using the main home exclusion. The exclusion applies only to your main home.

## Reporting the Sale

### No Reporting Required If:

- You have no gain, OR
- You exclude all of your gain

### Report on:

- [[Form 8949]] - Sales of Capital Assets
- [[Schedule D (Form 1040)]] - Capital Gains and Losses

## Mortgage Debt Forgiveness

You can exclude from income discharges of **qualified principal residence indebtedness**:
- Made after 2006 and before 2026
- Maximum exclusion: $750,000 ($375,000 MFS)
- Must reduce basis by amount excluded

See [[Pub 4681 - Canceled Debts, Foreclosures, Repossessions, and Abandonments]].

## Recapture of MCC Benefit

If you bought your home using a Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC) and sell within **9 years**, you may have to **recapture** part of the credit benefit.

See [[Mortgage Interest Credit]] and [[Pub 523 - Selling Your Home]].

## Special Situations

### Divorce or Separation

Special rules apply for transfers between spouses. See [[Pub 504 - Divorced or Separated Individuals]].

### Inherited Home

If you inherit a home, your basis is generally the fair market value at date of death. See [[Basis of Home]].

### Business or Rental Use

If you used part of your home for business or rental:
- Must **recapture depreciation** (cannot be excluded)
- May need to allocate gain between personal and business portions

See [[Pub 587 - Business Use of Your Home]].

### More Than One Home

You can only use the exclusion once every **2 years** for one main home.

## Loss on Sale

You **cannot deduct a loss** on the sale of your main home. Personal-use property losses are not deductible.

## Records to Keep

- Purchase documents and settlement statement
- Records of improvements
- Selling expenses documentation
- Form 1099-S (if received)
