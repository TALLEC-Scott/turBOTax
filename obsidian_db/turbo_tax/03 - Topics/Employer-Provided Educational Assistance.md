# Employer-Provided Educational Assistance

## Overview

Employer-provided educational assistance allows employees to receive up to **$5,250 per year** in tax-free educational benefits from their employer. This exclusion applies to both undergraduate and graduate level courses.

**Authority:** [[Pub 970 - Tax Benefits for Education#Chapter 10]]

## Maximum Exclusion

| Year | Maximum Tax-Free Amount |
|------|------------------------|
| 2025 | $5,250 |

This is an annual limit per employer.

## Types of Qualified Expenses

### Educational Assistance Includes:
- Tuition, fees, and similar payments
- Books, supplies, and equipment
- **Student loan repayment** (principal and interest) - added after March 27, 2020

### Does NOT Include:
- Courses involving sports, games, or hobbies (unless job-related or required)
- Meals, lodging, or transportation
- Tools or supplies retained after course completion

## Requirements for Tax-Free Treatment

1. **Written Plan**
   - Employer must have a written educational assistance plan
   - Plan cannot discriminate in favor of highly compensated employees

2. **Eligible Employee**
   - Cannot exclude employees under age 21
   - Cannot exclude employees with less than 1 year of service
   - Cannot exclude collective bargaining employees unless plan covers them

3. **Course Requirements**
   - Does NOT need to be job-related
   - Can be for graduate or undergraduate level
   - Can lead to a degree or be recreational (with limitations)

## Student Loan Repayment

After March 27, 2020, employers can provide:
- Up to $5,250 annually for student loan principal and interest
- Combined with other educational assistance (single $5,250 limit)
- Tax-free to employee

**Note:** Cannot deduct student loan interest paid by employer under this program.

## Coordination with Other Benefits

### With Education Credits
- Can claim credits for expenses NOT covered by employer assistance
- Employer assistance reduces qualified expenses for credits

### With Student Loan Interest Deduction
- Cannot deduct interest paid by employer
- Must reduce deductible interest by employer payments

## Form W-2 Reporting

| Amount | Reporting |
|--------|-----------|
| Up to $5,250 | Not reported on W-2 (tax-free) |
| Over $5,250 | Excess reported in Box 1 (wages) |

## Special Rules

### Job-Related Education (Not Using Section 127)
If education is job-related and employer pays:
- Can be excluded as working condition fringe benefit
- No dollar limit
- Must maintain or improve skills required in current job
- Different rules than Section 127

### Graduate Education
- Same $5,250 limit applies
- No distinction between graduate and undergraduate

## Common Questions

### Q: Does the education need to be related to my job?
**A:** No, under Section 127, the courses do not need to be job-related to qualify for tax-free treatment.

### Q: Can my employer pay for my MBA tax-free?
**A:** Yes, up to $5,250 per year can be excluded from income under an educational assistance plan.

### Q: What if my employer pays more than $5,250?
**A:** Amounts over $5,250 are taxable wages and should be included in your income.

### Q: Can I use employer assistance and education credits?
**A:** Yes, but not for the same expenses. The $5,250 reduces your qualified expenses for credit calculations.
