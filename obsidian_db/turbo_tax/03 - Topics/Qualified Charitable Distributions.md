# Qualified Charitable Distributions

A Qualified Charitable Distribution (QCD) is a distribution made directly from your IRA to a qualified charitable organization. QCDs offer tax advantages for taxpayers who are age 70½ or older.

## Overview

A QCD allows you to transfer funds directly from your IRA to a qualified charity without including the distribution in your taxable income.

**Key Benefits:**
- Not included in taxable income
- Counts toward Required Minimum Distribution (RMD)
- No itemizing required to benefit
- May reduce AGI (affects other tax calculations)

## Requirements

### Eligibility

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Age | Must be **70½ or older** at time of distribution |
| Account Type | Traditional IRA or Roth IRA |
| Exclusions | Not from ongoing SEP or SIMPLE IRA |
| Organization | Must be [[Qualified Organizations\|qualified organization]] (50% limit organization) |

### Distribution Limits

- **Maximum per year:** $108,000 (2025)
- Per IRA owner (not per account)
- Indexed for inflation

### 2025 One-Time Election

You can make a **one-time election** to distribute up to **$54,000** through:
- Charitable remainder annuity trust
- Charitable remainder unitrust
- Charitable gift annuity

**Requirements:**
- Funded only by QCDs
- One-time election (lifetime limit)

## Tax Treatment

### Not Taxable Income

- QCD amount excluded from gross income
- No charitable deduction allowed (cannot double-dip)
- Reduces AGI which may affect:
  - Medicare premium surcharges
  - Net investment income tax
  - Taxability of Social Security benefits

### Interaction with RMDs

**QCD counts toward RMD:**
- Must be made after RMD beginning date (age 73)
- Reduces amount you must withdraw
- Can satisfy RMD tax-free if transferred to charity

**Ordering Rule:**
- Distributions are treated as coming from RMD first
- Then from other amounts

## Eligible Organizations

**Qualify:**
- 50% limit organizations (churches, schools, hospitals, publicly supported charities)
- Operating foundations

**Do NOT Qualify:**
- Private nonoperating foundations
- Donor-advised funds
- Supporting organizations
- Charitable remainder trusts

## Eligible IRAs

| IRA Type | QCD Allowed? |
|----------|--------------|
| Traditional IRA | Yes |
| Roth IRA | Yes |
| SEP IRA | Yes (if not ongoing) |
| SIMPLE IRA | Yes (if not ongoing) |
| Inherited IRA | Yes (if beneficiary is 70½+) |
| 401(k), 403(b), etc. | No (must roll to IRA first) |

## How to Make a QCD

### Direct Transfer Required

**Must be:**
- Direct transfer from IRA trustee to charity
- Never in your possession
- Check payable to charity (not you)

**Process:**
1. Contact IRA trustee/custodian
2. Request direct transfer to qualified charity
3. Ensure charity provides written acknowledgment
4. Report on Form 1040

### Reporting on Tax Return

**Form 1040:**
- Report total IRA distributions on line 4a
- Enter taxable amount (minus QCD) on line 4b
- Write "QCD" next to line 4b

**No Schedule A required** - QCD not claimed as itemized deduction

## Documentation

### From Charity

Written acknowledgment must include:
- Date of contribution
- Amount of contribution
- Statement that no goods/services were provided (or description of any benefits)

### From IRA Custodian

Keep records showing:
- Date of distribution
- Amount transferred
- Name of charity receiving funds

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| Taking distribution first, then donating | Taxable distribution; no QCD treatment |
| Donating to private foundation | Does not qualify as QCD |
| Donating to donor-advised fund | Does not qualify as QCD |
| Not getting written acknowledgment | Cannot prove QCD |
| Contributing before age 70½ | Does not qualify as QCD |

## Planning Considerations

### When QCD Makes Sense

- You do not itemize deductions
- You want to reduce AGI for tax planning
- RMD pushes you into higher tax bracket
- You want to avoid increasing Medicare premiums
- Large IRA balance relative to other income

### When Standard Donation May Be Better

- You itemize deductions
- Very large charitable gift (QCD limited to $108,000)
- Want to donate appreciated securities (avoid capital gains)
