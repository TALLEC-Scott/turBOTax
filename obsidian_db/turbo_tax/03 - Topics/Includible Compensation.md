# Includible Compensation

## Overview

Includible compensation is used to determine your [[Annual Additions Limit]] for [[403(b) Plans]]. It represents the taxable wages and benefits you received from the employer maintaining your 403(b) account during your most recent year of service.

## Definition

Includible compensation for your most recent year of service is generally the amount of taxable wages and benefits you received from the employer that maintained a 403(b) account for your benefit during that period.

## What IS Included

Includible compensation includes:

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Wages, salaries, fees | Payment for personal services |
| Elective deferrals | Pre-tax contributions under salary reduction |
| Cafeteria plan amounts | Section 125 contributions/deferrals |
| Section 457 deferrals | Nonqualified deferred compensation |
| Transportation fringe benefits | Pre-tax qualified transportation contributions |
| Foreign earned income | Even if excludable under section 911 |

## What is NOT Included

The following are **excluded** from includible compensation:

1. Employer contributions to your 403(b) account
2. Compensation earned while employer wasn't an eligible employer
3. Employer contributions to qualified plans that are excludable from income
4. Cost of incidental life insurance

## Most Recent Year of Service

Your most recent year of service is your last full year of service ending on the last day of your tax year that you worked for the employer maintaining your 403(b) account.

### When Tax Year Differs from Employer's Work Period

If your tax year isn't the same as your employer's annual work period, your most recent year of service may include parts of at least two of your employer's annual work periods.

**Example**: A professor reports income on a calendar-year basis but is employed on an academic year (October through May). For 2025, the most recent year of service is January through May 2025 plus October through December 2025.

## Part-Time or Partial Year Employment

If you're part-time or employed for only part of the year, your most recent year of service includes:
- Service during the current year
- Service from preceding years until totaling 1 full year of service

## Figuring Includible Compensation

Use Worksheet B in Chapter 9 of [[Pub 571 - Tax-Sheltered Annuity Plans|Publication 571]] to calculate includible compensation.

## Special Rules

### Self-Employed Ministers
Includible compensation equals net earnings from ministry minus:
- Contributions made to retirement plan on your behalf
- Deductible portion of self-employment tax

### Foreign Missionaries
May include foreign earned income otherwise excludable under section 911. Special $3,000 contribution limit applies if AGI is $17,000 or less.

### Church Employees
See [[Ministers and Church Employees]] for alternative rules on years of service.

## Incidental Life Insurance

If your 403(b) annuity contract includes incidental life insurance:
- The cost is taxable to you in the year contributed
- Considered part of your basis when distributed
- Must be subtracted from includible compensation

Use Worksheet A in [[Pub 571 - Tax-Sheltered Annuity Plans|Publication 571]] to calculate the cost of incidental life insurance.
