# Stock Options

## Overview

Stock options are a form of equity compensation. The tax treatment depends on whether you receive a nonstatutory (nonqualified) stock option or a statutory stock option (incentive stock option or employee stock purchase plan).

## Types of Stock Options

### Nonstatutory Stock Options (NSOs)

Also called nonqualified stock options (NQSOs). The most common type with straightforward but less favorable tax treatment.

### Statutory Stock Options

Two types:
1. **Incentive Stock Options (ISOs)** - Special tax treatment if requirements met
2. **Employee Stock Purchase Plans (ESPPs)** - Special holding period rules

## Nonstatutory Stock Options

### Grant of Option

**With Readily Determinable FMV:**
- Option is treated like other property received as compensation
- Income at grant equals FMV of option

**Without Readily Determinable FMV:**
- No income at grant
- Income recognized at exercise

### Exercise of Option

**Taxable Amount:** Difference between FMV of stock and exercise price

**Reporting:** Shown on Form W-2, box 12, code V. Included in boxes 1, 3, and 5.

**Example:** Exercise price $10, FMV at exercise $25
- Spread of $15 per share is compensation income
- Basis in stock = $10 (exercise price) + $15 (income recognized) = $25

### Sale of Stock

Report on Schedule D. Holding period begins:
- Date option granted (if had readily determinable value)
- Date option exercised (if no readily determinable value)

## Incentive Stock Options (ISOs)

### Requirements

- Must be an employee when option granted and exercised (3-month rule, 1 year if disabled)
- Option not transferable except at death
- Plan approved by shareholders
- Option price ≥ FMV at grant
- No 10% shareholder rules violated

### Tax at Grant

No income recognized when ISO is granted.

### Tax at Exercise

**Regular Tax:** No income recognized when exercising ISO.

**Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT):** Must include as AMT adjustment the difference between FMV at exercise and exercise price. Report on Form 6251, line 2i.

**Example:** Exercise ISO to buy 100 shares at $10 when FMV is $25
- Regular tax: No income
- AMT: $1,500 adjustment ($25-$10) × 100 shares

### Holding Period Requirement

For favorable capital gains treatment, must hold stock until the later of:
- 1 year after exercise
- 2 years after option granted

### Disqualifying Disposition

If holding period not met:
- Ordinary income = FMV at exercise minus exercise price
- Reported as wages on Form W-2
- Remaining gain/loss is capital gain/loss

### Sale After Holding Period

Long-term capital gain = Sale price minus exercise price.

**Basis for Regular Tax:** Exercise price paid.

**Basis for AMT:** Exercise price plus AMT adjustment.

## Employee Stock Purchase Plans (ESPP)

### Requirements

- Must be employee when option granted and exercised (3-month rule)
- Option not transferable except at death
- Plan approved by shareholders
- Option price not less than 85% of FMV

### Tax at Grant and Exercise

No income recognized at grant or exercise for regular tax purposes.

### Holding Period Requirement

Same as ISOs - later of 1 year after exercise or 2 years after grant.

### Qualified Sale (Holding Period Met)

If option price ≥ 100% of FMV at grant:
- All gain is capital gain

If option price < 100% of FMV at grant (discounted):
- Ordinary income = Lesser of (FMV at grant - option price) or (FMV at sale - option price)
- Remainder is capital gain

### Disqualifying Disposition (Holding Period Not Met)

- Ordinary income = FMV at exercise minus option price
- Increase basis by ordinary income amount
- Remaining gain/loss is capital gain/loss

## Qualified Equity Grants

Section 83(i) allows qualified employees of private corporations to defer taxation for up to 5 years on qualified stock.

### Qualified Employee Requirements

Cannot be:
- 1% owner
- CEO or CFO
- Family of above
- One of four highest compensated officers

### Requirements

- Corporation has RSU or option plan covering 80%+ of U.S. employees
- Stock from option exercise or RSU settlement
- Election made similar to Section 83(b) election

## Common Questions

### Q: Should I exercise my ISOs?
**A:** Consider AMT implications. Exercise may trigger AMT even if no regular tax. Consider exercising early in year or spreading over multiple years.

### Q: What if my NSO is underwater (FMV < exercise price)?
**A:** No income at exercise, but consider whether exercise makes financial sense.

### Q: How do I adjust basis on Form 1099-B?
**A:** Form 1099-B may not reflect income recognized at exercise. Use Form 8949 to adjust basis.
