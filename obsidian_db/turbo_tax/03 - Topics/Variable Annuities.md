# Variable Annuities

## Overview

**Variable annuities** are annuity contracts where the payment amounts may vary based on factors such as investment performance, profits earned by the annuity funds, or cost-of-living indexes. They require a different calculation method under the [[General Rule]] compared to fixed annuities.

## Tax Calculation Method

Unlike fixed annuities that use an [[Exclusion Percentage]], variable annuities use a **fixed dollar amount** for the tax-free portion of each payment.

### Formula

```
                Investment in Contract
Tax-Free Amount = ────────────────────────────────────────────
                  Expected Number of Periodic Payments
```

### Determining Expected Number of Payments

- **Definite period annuity**: Multiply payments per year by number of years
- **Life annuity**: Use the multiple from the appropriate actuarial table (Table V for single life, Table VI for joint lives)

## Example

Age 65, purchased variable annuity for $12,000. Annuity starting date January 1, payments begin July 1.

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Investment in contract | $12,000 |
| Multiple from Table V (age 65) | 20 |
| Expected annual payments | 20 |
| Tax-free amount per payment | $600 |

If first payment is $920:
- Tax-free: $600
- Taxable: $320

## Refiguring the Tax-Free Amount

If a payment is **less than** the calculated tax-free amount, you may choose to refigure the tax-free portion for future payments.

### Refiguring Steps

1. Calculate the difference between the tax-free amount and the actual payment received
2. Divide by the remaining number of expected payments (from actuarial table)
3. Add the result to the previously determined tax-free amount

**Example**: Tax-free amount $600, but only received $500 in year 2. Difference = $100. Remaining payments at age 67 = 18.4. Amount to add = $100 ÷ 18.4 = $5.43. New tax-free amount = $605.43.

### Statement Required

To refigure the tax-free amount, you must file a statement with your tax return including:

1. Annuity starting date and your age on that date
2. First day of the first period for which you received a payment in the current year
3. Your investment in the contract as originally figured
4. Total amounts received tax-free from the annuity starting date through the first day of the current tax year

## Payment Variations

Variable annuity payments may depend on:

- Investment earnings from mutual funds
- Profits from pension or annuity funds
- Cost-of-living indexes
- Other specified variables
