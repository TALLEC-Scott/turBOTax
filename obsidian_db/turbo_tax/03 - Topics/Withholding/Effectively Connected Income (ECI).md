# Effectively Connected Income (ECI)

## Overview

**Effectively Connected Income (ECI)** is income from U.S. sources that is connected with a foreign person's conduct of a trade or business within the United States. ECI is taxed at graduated rates (for individuals) or regular corporate rates (for corporations) rather than the flat 30% FDAP rate.

## When Income is Effectively Connected

Income is ECI if it meets either test:

### Asset-Use Test
- The income is from assets used in, or held for use in, the conduct of a U.S. trade or business

### Business Activities Test
- The activities of the U.S. trade or business were a **material factor** in the realization of the income

## Types of ECI

### Automatically ECI
- Income from U.S. real property (if election made)
- Income from U.S. business operations

### May Be ECI (FDAP income that becomes connected)
- Interest from business accounts
- Dividends from business investments
- Royalties from business-related licenses
- Rents from business property

## Withholding Exemption

A withholding agent does **not** need to withhold tax if receiving a valid **Form W-8ECI** from the foreign payee stating:
1. The foreign payee is the beneficial owner
2. The income is effectively connected with a U.S. trade or business
3. The income is includible in the payee's gross income

### Exceptions to Exemption

The W-8ECI exemption does **not** apply to:
1. Pay for personal services performed by an individual
2. Effectively connected taxable income (ECTI) allocable to foreign partners
3. Income from disposition of U.S. real property interests (USRPI)
4. Payments to foreign corporations for personal services (if personal holding company rules apply)

## Special Rules

### Income from Securities
Special rules apply for determining whether income from securities is ECI for foreign persons engaged in banking, financing, or similar businesses.

### Notional Principal Contracts
- Certain payments are not subject to withholding regardless of Form W-8ECI
- Dividend equivalents under specified contracts are subject to withholding
- Must report ECI from notional principal contracts on Form 1042-S

### U.S. Branch of Foreign Bank/Insurance Company
- Income paid to a U.S. branch is presumed to be ECI if the branch has an EIN
- Branch must withhold if income is not actually ECI
- Form W-8BEN-E or W-8IMY can override presumption

## Partnership Withholding

Partnerships must withhold on ECTI allocable to foreign partners under Section 1446. See [[Partnership Withholding on ECTI]].

## Tax Treatment

| Type of Person | Tax Rate |
|----------------|----------|
| Individual | Graduated rates (10% - 37%) |
| Corporation | Flat 21% corporate rate |
| Foreign corporation | Form 1120-F |
