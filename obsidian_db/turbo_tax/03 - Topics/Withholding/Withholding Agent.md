# Withholding Agent

## Overview

A **withholding agent** is a U.S. or foreign person who has control, receipt, custody, disposal, or payment of an amount subject to Chapter 3 or Chapter 4 withholding. The withholding agent is personally liable for any tax required to be withheld, independent of the foreign payee's tax liability.

## Definition

A withholding agent may be:
- An individual, corporation, partnership, trust, or association
- A nominee under section 1446
- A foreign intermediary, foreign partnership, or U.S. branch of certain foreign banks and insurance companies

**Key Point:** You may be a withholding agent even if there is no requirement to withhold from a payment, or if another person has withheld the required amount.

## Responsibilities

### For Chapter 3
- Withhold 30% (or applicable treaty rate) on payments of U.S. source income to foreign persons
- Obtain and maintain valid documentation (Forms W-8 or W-9)
- File Form 1042 and Forms 1042-S annually

### For Chapter 4 (FATCA)
- Withhold 30% on withholdable payments to non-participating FFIs or passive NFFEs without proper documentation
- Determine the chapter 4 status of payees
- File Form 1042 and Forms 1042-S with appropriate codes

## Liability for Tax

- The withholding agent is **personally liable** for any tax required to be withheld
- Liability is independent of the foreign person's tax liability
- If both the withholding agent and foreign person fail, both are liable for tax, interest, and penalties
- Tax is collected only once - if the foreign person pays, the withholding agent remains liable for interest and penalties

## Determination of Amount to Withhold

1. Withhold on the **gross amount** subject to withholding
2. Cannot reduce by any deductions
3. If source or amount is unknown, withhold sufficient amount to ensure at least 30% of the taxable amount
4. May elect to hold 30% in escrow for up to 1 year while determining taxable amount

## When to Withhold

- At the time of payment to the foreign person
- "Payment" occurs when the person realizes income, whether or not cash transfers
- Includes payments to creditors, agents, or for the person's benefit
