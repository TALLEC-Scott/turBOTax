# FATCA Chapter 4 Withholding

## Overview

**Chapter 4 withholding** refers to FATCA (Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act) requirements under IRC Sections 1471-1474. It requires withholding agents to withhold 30% on certain payments to foreign financial institutions (FFIs) and non-financial foreign entities (NFFEs) that do not meet specific compliance requirements.

## Withholdable Payments

A **withholdable payment** is generally:
- U.S. source FDAP income
- Certain payments from U.S. branches of foreign banks and insurance companies

### Exceptions
Not subject to Chapter 4 withholding:
- Interest from short-term obligations
- Payments under grandfathered obligations (outstanding as of July 1, 2014)
- Certain non-financial payments (services, software licenses, transportation)

## When Chapter 4 Withholding Applies

### For FFIs
Withhold 30% if the FFI is:
- A **nonparticipating FFI** (not complying with FATCA requirements)
- A **recalcitrant account holder** of a participating FFI

### For NFFEs
Withhold 30% if the NFFE is:
- A **passive NFFE** that fails to identify its substantial U.S. owners
- Cannot certify it has no substantial U.S. owners

## FFI Status Categories

| Status | Withholding Applies? | Requirements |
|--------|---------------------|--------------|
| Participating FFI | No | Has FFI agreement with IRS |
| Registered deemed-compliant FFI | No | Registered, meets requirements |
| Reporting Model 1 FFI | No | Reports under IGA |
| Reporting Model 2 FFI | No | Reports directly to IRS |
| Nonparticipating FFI | **Yes - 30%** | No FFI agreement |
| Exempt beneficial owner | No | Governments, central banks, etc. |

## NFFE Status Categories

| Status | Withholding Applies? | Requirements |
|--------|---------------------|--------------|
| Active NFFE | No | Active business income > 50% |
| Passive NFFE (certified) | No | Certifies no substantial U.S. owners |
| Passive NFFE (uncertified) | **Yes - 30%** | Fails to provide certification |
| Excepted NFFE | No | Publicly traded, etc. |

## Intergovernmental Agreements (IGAs)

### Model 1 IGA
- FFI reports to local government
- Government exchanges information with IRS
- FFI treated as deemed-compliant

### Model 2 IGA
- FFI reports directly to IRS
- Local government may supplement information
- FFI has reporting obligations similar to participating FFI

## Documentation Requirements

To avoid Chapter 4 withholding, obtain documentation establishing:
1. The entity's chapter 4 status
2. For FFIs: GIIN (if required)
3. For passive NFFEs: Certification of substantial U.S. owners

### Acceptable Documentation
- Form W-8BEN-E (entities)
- Form W-8IMY (intermediaries)
- Documentary evidence under IGA rules

## Relationship to Chapter 3

| Situation | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| Both Chapter 3 and 4 apply | Withhold under Chapter 4 only |
| Chapter 4 applies, Chapter 3 does not | Withhold under Chapter 4 |
| Neither applies | No withholding required |

**Credit:** Amounts withheld under Chapter 4 may be credited against Chapter 3 liability.

## Key Definitions

- **FFI:** Foreign financial institution (bank, custodian, investment entity, insurance company)
- **NFFE:** Non-financial foreign entity
- **Substantial U.S. owner:** >10% direct or indirect owner (for partnerships, >10% profit interest)
- **Passive NFFE:** NFFE with >50% passive income or >50% passive assets
