# Market Discount

## Overview

Market discount occurs when a debt instrument's stated redemption price at maturity is greater than its basis after acquisition. This typically happens when purchasing debt instruments in the secondary market after interest rates have increased.

## Definition

**Market Discount** = (Issue Price + Accrued OID) - Your Adjusted Basis

A debt instrument has market discount if your adjusted basis immediately after acquisition was less than the issue price plus total OID that accrued before acquisition.

## When It Occurs

- Purchasing in the secondary market when value has decreased since issue
- Often due to interest rate increases after original issue
- Can apply to both OID and non-OID debt instruments

## Tax Treatment Options

### Default Treatment
Market discount is NOT included in income as it accrues. Instead:
- Treated as additional interest income when the instrument is sold, redeemed, or matures
- Taxed as ordinary income (not capital gain) to the extent of market discount

### Election to Accrue Currently (Section 1278(b))
You may elect to include market discount in income as it accrues:
- Must notify broker in writing
- Broker reports accrued market discount in Box 5 of [[Form 1099-OID]]
- Use constant yield method (default) or ratable method

## Accrual Methods

### Constant Yield Method (Default)
Similar to OID accrual method - uses the instrument's yield to maturity.

### Ratable Method
Market discount accrued ratably over remaining term to maturity.

## Reporting on Form 1099-OID

For covered securities with Section 1278(b) election:
- **Box 5**: Market discount accrued during the year

## Market Discount vs. OID

| Characteristic | OID | Market Discount |
|----------------|-----|-----------------|
| When Created | At original issue | After original issue, at purchase |
| Accrual | Mandatory annually | Optional (election required) |
| Character | Ordinary income | Ordinary income on disposition |
