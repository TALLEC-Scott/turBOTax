# Standard Deduction

The standard deduction is a fixed amount that reduces your taxable income. Most taxpayers claim the standard deduction rather than itemizing.

## 2025 Standard Deduction Amounts

| Filing Status | Amount |
|---------------|--------|
| Single | $15,750 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $31,500 |
| Married Filing Separately | $15,750 |
| Head of Household | $23,625 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | $31,500 |

## Additional Standard Deduction

### Age 65 or Older
Add $2,000 (per person) if born before January 2, 1961:
- Single or Head of Household: $2,000
- Married Filing Jointly: $2,000 per spouse
- Married Filing Separately: $2,000
- Qualifying Surviving Spouse: $2,000

### Blind
Add $2,000 (per condition) if legally blind.

### Example
A single taxpayer age 68 and blind:
- Base standard deduction: $15,750
- Additional for age 65+: $2,000
- Additional for blind: $2,000
- **Total: $19,750**

## Who Cannot Claim the Standard Deduction

- Married filing separately and spouse itemizes
- Nonresident alien or dual-status alien (with exceptions)
- Filing for less than a 12-month period due to change in accounting period
- Estate or trust, common trust fund, or partnership

## Standard vs. Itemized Deduction

Choose the **higher** of:
1. Standard deduction, or
2. Total [[Itemized Deductions]] (Schedule A)

### When Itemizing May Be Better
- Large unreimbursed medical expenses
- Significant state and local taxes (up to $40,000 limit)
- Large mortgage interest payments
- Substantial charitable contributions
- Casualty or theft losses from federally declared disasters

## 2025 New Deductions for Non-Itemizers

Even if claiming the standard deduction, you may deduct:

### No Tax on Tips
- Up to $25,000 of qualified tips
- Phaseout begins at $150,000 MAGI ($300,000 MFJ)
- Requires valid SSN; MFJ required if married

### No Tax on Overtime
- Up to $12,500 of qualified overtime compensation ($25,000 MFJ)
- Phaseout begins at $150,000 MAGI ($300,000 MFJ)
- Requires valid SSN; MFJ required if married

### No Tax on Car Loan Interest
- Up to $10,000 of qualified passenger vehicle loan interest
- For vehicles purchased in 2025 for personal use
- Phaseout begins at $100,000 MAGI ($200,000 MFJ)

### Enhanced Senior Deduction
- Up to $6,000 ($12,000 if both spouses eligible)
- For those born before January 2, 1961
- Phaseout begins at $75,000 MAGI ($150,000 MFJ)
- Requires valid SSN; MFJ required if married

*Claim these new deductions on Schedule 1-A (Form 1040)*

## Dependents

If claimed as a dependent by another taxpayer:
- Standard deduction is limited to the greater of:
  - $1,350, or
  - Earned income (up to $15,300) plus $450
