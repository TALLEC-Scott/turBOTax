# GILTI (Global Intangible Low-Taxed Income)

## Overview

Global Intangible Low-Taxed Income (GILTI) is a category of income that U.S. shareholders of Controlled Foreign Corporations (CFCs) must include in their gross income. Enacted by the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017, GILTI is designed to prevent U.S. taxpayers from shifting profits to low-tax foreign jurisdictions.

## Who Is Affected

GILTI applies to **U.S. shareholders** of CFCs:
- U.S. persons who own 10% or more of the total voting power OR value of all classes of stock in a foreign corporation
- Combined ownership of all U.S. shareholders must exceed 50%

See [[Controlled Foreign Corporation]] for detailed rules on CFC status.

## GILTI Calculation

A U.S. shareholder's GILTI inclusion is determined based on:

1. **Aggregate Pro Rata Share of Tested Income** - The shareholder's share of all CFCs' tested income
2. **Less: Aggregate Pro Rata Share of Tested Loss** - Offset by any tested losses
3. **Less: Net Deemed Tangible Income Return** - 10% of qualified business asset investment (QBAI)

### Tested Income
Does NOT include:
- Effectively connected income
- Subpart F income
- Foreign oil and gas income
- Certain related party payments

## Foreign Tax Credit for GILTI

### Separate Category
GILTI is classified as **Section 951A Category Income** - a separate category for FTC limitation purposes.

### Key Rules
- FTC is calculated on a **separate Form 1116** (or Form 1118 for corporate shareholders)
- **No carrybacks or carryovers** allowed for Section 951A category unused taxes
- Credit only available for foreign taxes on GILTI inclusions

### Section 962 Election
Individual shareholders may elect to be taxed at corporate rates on GILTI inclusions. This allows:
- Claiming credit for foreign taxes paid by the CFC
- Potentially more favorable tax treatment

To make the election, include a statement with Form 1118 as required under Regulations section 1.962-2.

## What's New for 2025

**Section 960(d)(4)** (P.L. 119-21) disallows a credit for:
- **10% of foreign income taxes** paid or accrued on amounts excluded from gross income under section 959(a) by reason of a GILTI inclusion under section 951A(a)
- Applies to foreign taxes paid or accrued after June 28, 2025

## Reporting Requirements

### Form 8992
U.S. shareholders must file **Form 8992** (U.S. Shareholder Calculation of Global Intangible Low-Taxed Income) to calculate their GILTI inclusion.

### Form 1116/1118
- Report GILTI on a separate Form 1116 as Section 951A category income
- Enter total inclusion in a single column; report "951A" on line i
- No country-by-country reporting required

## Interaction with Other Provisions

| Provision | Relationship to GILTI |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Subpart F Income** | Separate category; not included in tested income |
| **FDII** | Domestic counterpart incentive for U.S. corporations |
| **Section 250 Deduction** | May deduct 37.5% (2025) of GILTI for domestic corporations |
| **Foreign Earned Income Exclusion** | Not applicable to GILTI |

## Planning Considerations

1. **Section 962 Election** - Consider for individuals with significant CFC ownership
2. **QBAI Planning** - Tangible assets reduce GILTI inclusions
3. **Entity Structure** - Review ownership percentages and entity classification
4. **High-Tax Exception** - May elect out of GILTI for high-taxed income
