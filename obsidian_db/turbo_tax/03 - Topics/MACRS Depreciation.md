# MACRS Depreciation

## Overview

MACRS (Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System) is the required depreciation method for most business and investment property placed in service after 1986. Understanding MACRS is essential for properly depreciating rental property and maximizing tax benefits.

## What Property Can Be Depreciated?

Property must meet ALL requirements:
1. You **own** the property (even if subject to debt)
2. Used in **business or income-producing activity**
3. Has a **determinable useful life**
4. Expected to last **more than 1 year**

## What Can't Be Depreciated?

- **Land** - Does not wear out or become obsolete
- Property placed in service AND disposed of in same year
- Equipment used to build capital improvements (add depreciation to basis)

## When Depreciation Begins and Ends

### Placed in Service
Property is "placed in service" when **ready and available** for its specific use - even if not actually being used yet.

**Examples**:
- Dishwasher delivered in December but installed January 3 → Placed in service January
- House ready for rent July 5, rented September 1 → Placed in service July

### Conversion to Business Use
If property was used personally first, depreciation begins when use changes to income-producing.

### When Depreciation Stops
Stop depreciating when:
- Cost or basis fully recovered, OR
- Property retired from service (sold, abandoned, converted to personal use, destroyed)

## MACRS Systems

### General Depreciation System (GDS)
- **Default system** - Must use unless required to use ADS
- Generally shorter recovery periods
- Residential rental property: 27.5 years

### Alternative Depreciation System (ADS)
- Required for certain property used outside the US
- **Elective** for most property
- Longer recovery periods
- Must use straight-line method
- Residential rental property: 30 years
- **Irrevocable** once elected for residential rental property

## Property Classes Under GDS

| Property Type | Recovery Period (GDS) | Recovery Period (ADS) |
|--------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Computers & peripherals | 5 years | 5 years |
| Office machinery | 5 years | 6 years |
| Automobiles | 5 years | 5 years |
| Light trucks | 5 years | 5 years |
| Appliances, carpeting, furniture | 5 years | 9 years |
| Office furniture & equipment | 7 years | 10 years |
| Roads, fences, shrubbery | 15 years | 20 years |
| Residential rental property | 27.5 years | 30 years |

## Depreciation Conventions

### Mid-Month Convention
- **Required** for all residential rental property and nonresidential real property
- Treat property as placed in service (or disposed of) at midpoint of month

### Mid-Quarter Convention
- Required if more than **40%** of depreciable basis placed in service in last 3 months
- Treat property as placed in service at midpoint of quarter

### Half-Year Convention
- Used when neither mid-month nor mid-quarter applies
- Treat property as placed in service at midpoint of year

## Basis for Depreciation

### Cost Basis
Usually the purchase price plus:
- Sales tax
- Freight charges
- Installation and testing charges

### Real Property Basis
Also includes settlement fees:
- Abstract fees
- Legal fees
- Recording fees
- Transfer taxes
- Title insurance
- Surveys

**NOT included in basis**:
- Fire insurance premiums
- Pre-closing rent charges
- Loan-related fees (points, appraisal, credit report)

### Separating Land and Buildings
Allocate purchase price based on fair market value ratios.

**Example**: $200,000 purchase with $160,000 assessment ($136,000 house, $24,000 land):
- House basis: 85% × $200,000 = $170,000
- Land basis: 15% × $200,000 = $30,000

### Adjusted Basis
Increase basis for:
- Additions and improvements
- Casualty restoration costs
- Legal fees for title defense

Decrease basis for:
- Insurance/casualty payments received
- Depreciation claimed or allowable
- Section 179 or bonus depreciation claimed

## Special Rules

### Additions and Improvements
Treat as **separate property** for depreciation:
- Use same recovery period that would apply to original property
- Begin depreciation when placed in service

### Property Changed to Rental Use
Basis for depreciation is **lesser of**:
- FMV on date of conversion, OR
- Adjusted basis on date of conversion
