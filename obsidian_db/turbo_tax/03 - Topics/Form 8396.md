# Form 8396

## Overview

Form 8396 (Mortgage Interest Credit) is used to claim the mortgage interest credit for holders of a Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC). This credit helps lower-income individuals afford home ownership.

## Who Must File

File Form 8396 if you:
- Were issued a Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC) by a state or local government
- Paid mortgage interest on your main home during the year

## Key Lines

| Line | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | Interest paid (or prorated amount if mortgage exceeds certified indebtedness) |
| 2 | Interest × certificate credit rate |
| 3 | Credit amount (subject to limits) |

## Credit Limits

### Rate-Based Limit

If the certificate credit rate exceeds 20%, the credit cannot exceed **$2,000**.

### Tax Liability Limit

Your credit cannot exceed your tax liability. Use the Credit Limit Worksheet in the instructions.

### Carryforward

Unused credit can be carried forward for **up to 3 years**.

## Adjusting Your Deduction

If you itemize deductions, reduce your [[Home Mortgage Interest]] deduction by the credit amount on line 3.

## Refinancing

If you refinanced:
- Get a new MCC to continue claiming the credit
- The new credit cannot exceed what the old MCC would have provided
- Add interest from both loans in the year of refinancing

## When to File

File Form 8396 with your:
- Form 1040
- Form 1040-SR
- Form 1040-NR
