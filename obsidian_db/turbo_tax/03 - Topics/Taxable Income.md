# Taxable Income

Taxable income is the amount of income subject to federal income tax after subtracting deductions and exemptions. It is the starting point for calculating your tax liability using the [[Tax Tables]] or [[Tax Computation Worksheet]].

## Calculation

```
Taxable Income = Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) - Deductions
```

### On Form 1040

Taxable income appears on **line 15** of Form 1040 (2025).

## How to Calculate Taxable Income

### Step 1: Determine Gross Income

Gross income includes all income from whatever source derived:
- Wages, salaries, tips (Form W-2)
- Interest and dividends
- Business income (Schedule C)
- Capital gains (Schedule D)
- IRA distributions and pensions
- Rental real estate, royalties (Schedule E)
- Farm income (Schedule F)
- Unemployment compensation
- Other income

### Step 2: Calculate Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)

Subtract "above-the-line" deductions from gross income:
- Educator expenses
- HSA contributions
- Self-employment tax deduction
- Self-employed health insurance
- IRA contributions
- Student loan interest
- Alimony paid (pre-2019 agreements)

### Step 3: Choose Deduction Method

**Standard Deduction** (2025 amounts):
| Filing Status | Amount |
|---------------|--------|
| Single | $15,750 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $31,500 |
| Married Filing Separately | $15,750 |
| Head of Household | $23,625 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | $31,500 |

**Itemized Deductions** - Use if total exceeds standard deduction:
- Medical expenses (exceeding 7.5% of AGI)
- State and local taxes (up to $10,000/$5,000 MFS)
- Home mortgage interest
- Charitable contributions
- Casualty and theft losses

See [[Itemized Deductions]] and [[Standard Deduction]] for details.

### Step 4: Apply Qualified Business Income Deduction

If applicable, deduct up to 20% of qualified business income from pass-through entities.

## What's NOT Taxable

Some income is excluded from taxable income:
- Life insurance proceeds
- Gifts and inheritances
- Municipal bond interest
- Roth IRA qualified distributions
- Workers' compensation
- Qualified scholarships
- Combat zone pay for military

## Taxable Income and Tax Calculation

Once you have taxable income:

1. **Under $100,000**: Use the [[Tax Tables]]
2. **$100,000 or more**: Use the [[Tax Computation Worksheet]]
3. **With capital gains/dividends**: May need special worksheets

## Impact on Tax Benefits

Taxable income affects eligibility for many tax benefits:
- Phase-out of certain credits and deductions
- Eligibility for tax-advantaged accounts
- Medicare and NIIT surcharge thresholds
