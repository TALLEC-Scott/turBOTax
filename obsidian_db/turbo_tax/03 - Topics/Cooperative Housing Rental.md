# Cooperative Housing Rental

## Overview

A cooperative (co-op) is a housing corporation where you own stock that gives you the right to occupy a specific unit. When renting out a co-op apartment, special rules apply because you're depreciating stock rather than real estate.

## Unique Features

### What You Own
- **Stock** in the cooperative housing corporation
- A **proprietary lease** giving you the right to occupy a unit

### What You Don't Own
- The building or unit directly
- The land

## Depreciation Rules for Co-ops

### Depreciating Stock
Instead of depreciating real estate, you depreciate your **stock in the corporation**.

### Calculating Stock Depreciation

1. Determine the corporation's adjusted basis in the property
2. Divide by total shares outstanding
3. Multiply by your shares
4. Apply MACRS rules (27.5 years for residential rental)

**Formula**:
```
Your depreciation = (Corporation's adjusted basis ÷ Total shares) × Your shares
```

### Limits on Depreciation

Your annual depreciation deduction **cannot exceed** your adjusted basis in the stock allocable to rental property.

## Deductible Expenses

### Pass-Through Deductions
The co-op corporation can pass through to shareholders:
- **Mortgage interest** on the corporation's building mortgage
- **Real estate taxes** on the building

### How to Figure Your Share

**If corporation allocates expenses reasonably**:
- Use the allocated amounts

**If not reasonably allocated**:
1. Divide your shares by total shares outstanding
2. Multiply corporation's deductible interest by your percentage
3. Multiply corporation's deductible taxes by your percentage

### Other Deductible Expenses
- Your share of maintenance fees
- Costs specific to your unit (if paid by you)
- Interest on any loan to purchase your shares

## Capital Payments

### Added to Stock Basis
Payments that add to the capital account (not deductible):
- Special assessments for building improvements
- New roof contributions
- Parking lot paving
- Principal payments on corporation's mortgage

### Capital vs. Deductible
- **Deductible**: Operating expenses, interest, taxes
- **Capital**: Principal payments, improvements, reserves

## Example Calculation

**Facts**:
- Your shares: 100 out of 1,000 total
- Corporation's adjusted basis in building: $2,000,000
- Your stock basis: $50,000
- Corporation's mortgage interest: $80,000
- Corporation's real estate taxes: $20,000

**Your Deductions**:
- Interest: 10% × $80,000 = $8,000
- Taxes: 10% × $20,000 = $2,000
- Depreciation: ($2,000,000 ÷ 1,000) × 100 × 3.636% = $7,272

**Limited by**: Your $50,000 stock basis allocable to rental

## Converting Co-op to Rental Use

If you convert your co-op residence to rental use:
- Basis for depreciation is **lesser of** stock's FMV or adjusted basis on conversion date
- See [[Property Changed to Rental Use]] for general rules
