# Information Returns

## Overview

Information returns report payments made in the course of business to the IRS and recipients. Per [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]], these returns help the IRS verify that income is properly reported.

## Common Information Returns

### Form 1099-NEC (Nonemployee Compensation)

**Purpose:** Report payments to independent contractors

**When Required:**
- Payments of $600+ for services by nonemployees
- Cash payments for fish
- Payments to attorneys

**Due Dates:**
- Recipient copy: January 31
- IRS filing: January 31 (paper) or March 31 (electronic)

**Box 1:** Nonemployee compensation

### Form 1099-MISC (Miscellaneous Information)

**Purpose:** Report various types of payments

**When Required:**
| Payment Type | Threshold |
|--------------|-----------|
| Rents | $600+ |
| Royalties | $10+ |
| Prizes/awards | $600+ |
| Medical payments | $600+ |
| Fish purchases | $600+ |

**Due Dates:**
- Recipient copy: January 31
- IRS filing: February 28 (paper) or March 31 (electronic)

### Form W-2 (Wage and Tax Statement)

**Purpose:** Report wages paid to employees

**Required For:**
- All employees (regardless of amount)
- Include wages, tips, withheld taxes

**Due Dates:**
- Employee copy: January 31
- SSA filing: January 31 (with Form W-3)

### Form W-3 (Transmittal of Wage Statements)

**Purpose:** Transmits Forms W-2 to Social Security Administration
- Must accompany all paper W-2 filings

## Form 8300 (Cash Payments Over $10,000)

**Purpose:** Report cash payments over $10,000 received in trade or business

**What Counts as Cash:**
- U.S. and foreign currency
- Cashier's checks
- Bank drafts
- Traveler's checks
- Money orders

**NOT Cash:**
- Personal checks
- Business checks

**Due Dates:**
- Form 8300: 15 days after receiving cash
- Annual statement to payor: January 31

**Penalties:** Up to $250,000 for failure to file or criminal charges.

## Backup Withholding

### When Required
- Payee fails to provide TIN
- TIN is incorrect
- Payee subject to backup withholding order

**Rate:** 24% of payment

**Reporting:** Report withheld amount on Form 1099-NEC, Box 4

### Preventing Backup Withholding
- Provide TIN on Form W-9
- Verify TIN with IRS (optional)

## Filing Requirements

### Electronic Filing
**Required if:** Filing 10+ information returns total (all types combined)
- File through IRS Filing Information Returns Electronically (FIRE) system
- File Forms 1099 with IRS
- File Forms W-2 with Social Security Administration

### Paper Filing
If filing fewer than 10 returns:
- File with appropriate agency (IRS or SSA)
- Include Form 1096 for 1099s
- Include Form W-3 for W-2s

## Penalties

Per [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]]:

| Violation | Penalty (2025) |
|-----------|---------------|
| Failure to file | $60/form (intentional: $630) |
| Failure to furnish payee statement | $60/form |
| Incorrect information | $60/form |
| Intentional disregard | $630/form or 10% of amount |

**De Minimis Exception:** No penalty if errors are corrected by August 1 and affect less than the greater of 10 forms or 0.5% of total forms.

## Reasonable Cause Exception

Penalties may be waived if:
- Failure was due to reasonable cause
- Not willful neglect
- Must show ordinary business care and prudence

## State Reporting

Many states require separate information return filings:
- Check with your state tax agency
- Some states participate in combined federal/state filing

## Recordkeeping

Maintain records for:
- Payee names, addresses, TINs
- Payment amounts and dates
- Copies of information returns filed
- Form W-9 from payees

**Retention Period:** 3 years from filing date

## Common Questions

### Q: Do I need to file 1099s for corporations?
**A:** Generally no for payments to corporations (including LLCs taxed as C or S corporations). However, payments to attorneys and medical providers require 1099-MISC regardless of entity type.

### Q: What if I don't have the payee's TIN?
**A:** Request Form W-9. If not provided, begin backup withholding at 24%. You may still be subject to penalties for missing TINs.

### Q: Do I file 1099s for payments by credit card?
**A:** No. Payment settlement entities (credit card processors) file Form 1099-K. You do not file 1099 for those payments.
