# Distributions to Beneficiaries

## Overview

Estates and trusts make distributions to beneficiaries, which may carry out income that is taxable to the beneficiaries. The character of the income is preserved when distributed.

## Types of Distributions

### Income Required to Be Distributed Currently
- Required by will or local law
- Must be paid from estate income
- Beneficiary includes in income whether or not actually received
- Estate gets deduction in year required

### Other Amounts Distributed
- Discretionary distributions
- Paid, credited, or required to be distributed
- Includible by beneficiary when actually distributed
- Estate gets deduction when distributed

### Specific Bequests
- Gifts of specific property or amounts
- Not taxable income to beneficiary
- If paid in 3 or fewer installments
- Not considered distribution of income

## Distributable Net Income (DNI)

### Purpose
DNI determines:
- Maximum deduction for estate
- Maximum income includible by beneficiary
- Character of income distributed

### Calculation
```
Taxable income
+ Tax-exempt income
+ Income distribution deduction
+ Charitable deduction
- Capital gains allocable to corpus
- Expenses allocable to tax-exempt income
= Distributable Net Income
```

## Character of Distributions

### Flow-Through Nature
Distributions retain their character:
- Interest income stays interest
- Dividends stay dividends
- Capital gains stay capital gains
- Tax-exempt income stays tax-exempt

### Schedule K-1 Reporting
Each beneficiary receives Schedule K-1 showing:
- Type and amount of each income category
- Credits and deductions passed through
- Beneficiary reports on their individual return

## Separate Shares Rule

### When Applicable
- Estate has more than one beneficiary, AND
- Economic interests are independent (one doesn't affect the other)

### Effect
Each share treated as separate estate for DNI calculation:
- Separate DNI for each share
- Allocation based on will or local law
- Income in respect of decedent allocated proportionally

### Example
Pat's will creates two separate shares:
- Eli: ABC stock and all dividends
- Morgan: Residue of estate

Each share has separate DNI based on income allocable to that share.

## 65-Day Rule

### Election
Estates can elect to treat distributions made within 65 days after tax year ends as made on last day of tax year.

### Requirements
- Made on Form 1041, line 6
- Irrevocable for that tax year
- Only one election per year

### Benefit
Allows estate to take deduction in earlier year if income would otherwise be taxed at higher estate rate.

## Property Distributed in Kind

### Definition
Non-cash distributions other than:
- Specific bequests (3 or fewer installments)
- Real property passing directly under local law

### Deduction Amount
- **Ordinarily**: Lesser of estate's basis or FMV
- **If estate recognizes gain**: FMV

### Beneficiary's Basis
- Same as estate's basis at time of distribution
- Adjusted for gain recognized by estate

## Tax-Exempt Income

### Effect on Distribution
- Tax-exempt income distributed is still tax-exempt
- No deduction for estate for tax-exempt income
- Beneficiary receives tax-free

### Allocation
- Tax-exempt income part of DNI
- Must be allocated among beneficiaries
- Reduces taxable portion of distribution

## Double Deduction Prohibited

### Rule
Same amount cannot be deducted twice:
- Income required to be distributed: Deducted in year required
- Cannot deduct again when actually paid

### Charitable Contributions
- Amount allowed as charitable deduction
- Cannot also be claimed as distribution deduction

## Reporting to Beneficiaries

### Schedule K-1 (Form 1041)
Required information:
- Beneficiary's share of each type of income
- Deductions and credits
- Alternative minimum tax items
- Foreign tax information
- Beneficiary's share of IRD

### Due Date
- Same as Form 1041 due date
- Or extended due date if extension filed
