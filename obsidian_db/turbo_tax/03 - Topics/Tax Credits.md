# Tax Credits

Tax credits directly reduce your tax liability, dollar for dollar. They are more valuable than deductions of equal amounts.

## Credits vs. Deductions

| Type | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Credit** | Reduces tax directly ($1 credit = $1 tax saved) |
| **Deduction** | Reduces taxable income (value depends on tax bracket) |

## Types of Credits

### Refundable Credits
Can result in a refund even if you owe no tax:
- [[Earned Income Credit]] (fully refundable)
- Additional Child Tax Credit (up to $1,700 per child)
- Premium Tax Credit (may be refundable)
- American Opportunity Credit (40% refundable, up to $1,000)

### Nonrefundable Credits
Reduce tax to zero but cannot create a refund:
- Child Tax Credit (nonrefundable portion)
- [[Child and Dependent Care Credit]] - For childcare expenses
- Credit for Other Dependents
- Lifetime Learning Credit
- Saver's Credit
- Foreign Tax Credit

## Major Tax Credits

### Child Tax Credit (CTC)
- **Amount:** Up to $2,000 per qualifying child
- **Refundable portion:** Up to $1,700 (Additional Child Tax Credit)
- **Phaseout:** Begins at $200,000 ($400,000 MFJ)
- **Requirements:** Child under 17, U.S. citizen, lived with you over half year

See [[Child Tax Credit]] for details.

### Credit for Other Dependents
- **Amount:** $500 per qualifying dependent
- For dependents who don't qualify for CTC
- Same phaseout as CTC

### Earned Income Credit (EIC)
- **Amount:** Up to $7,830 (2025) depending on income and children
- **Fully refundable**
- Must have earned income
- Investment income limited to $11,600

See [[Earned Income Credit]] for details.

### Education Credits

**American Opportunity Credit:**
- Up to $2,500 per eligible student
- First 4 years of higher education
- 40% refundable (up to $1,000)
- Phaseout: $80,000-$90,000 single; $160,000-$180,000 MFJ

**Lifetime Learning Credit:**
- Up to $2,000 per tax return
- Any post-secondary education
- Nonrefundable
- Phaseout: $80,000-$90,000 single; $160,000-$180,000 MFJ

### Saver's Credit
- Credit for retirement contributions
- 10%, 20%, or 50% of contributions up to $2,000
- Income limits apply
- Must be 18+, not a student, not claimed as dependent

### Premium Tax Credit
- Helps pay for health insurance through Marketplace
- Based on household income vs. federal poverty line
- Can be paid in advance to insurer
- Reconciled on tax return with Form 8962

### Adoption Credit
- **2025 Amount:** Up to $17,280 per child
- **Refundable portion:** Up to $5,000 per child (NEW 2025)
- Special needs adoption: Full amount regardless of expenses
- Phaseout: $259,190 - $299,190 MAGI

### Elderly or Disabled Credit
- For taxpayers 65+ or retired on permanent disability
- Income limits apply
- Use Schedule R to calculate

## Credit Ordering

When claiming multiple credits, apply in this order:
1. Nonrefundable credits
2. Refundable credits

Nonrefundable credits are applied first to reduce tax liability before refundable credits.

## Eligibility Requirements

Most credits require:
- Valid SSN for you, spouse, and dependents
- U.S. citizen or resident alien status
- Meeting specific income tests
- Meeting relationship/residency tests (for dependent-related credits)

## 2025 Changes

- **Child Tax Credit:** Maximum refundable portion increased to $1,700
- **Adoption Credit:** Now partially refundable (up to $5,000)
- **Credit for Other Dependents:** SSN or ITIN required (NEW)
