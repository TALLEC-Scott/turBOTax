# Determination Letters

## Overview

A determination letter is an official IRS document that recognizes an organization's tax-exempt status. It is issued in response to an organization's application for recognition of exemption.

## Types of Determination Letters

### Favorable Determination Letters
- Recognizes tax-exempt status
- Specifies IRC section under which exempt
- Indicates public charity or private foundation status
- States effective date of exemption

### Adverse Determination Letters
- Denies exemption application
- Explains reasons for denial
- Provides appeal rights

### Modification Letters
- Changes foundation status
- Revokes or modifies prior determination
- Usually after examination

## Effective Date

### General Rule
- Within 27-month period: Effective from date of organization
- After 27-month period: Effective from date of application
- Material amendments: May affect effective date

### Discretionary Extension
Organizations may request retroactive recognition if:
1. Acted reasonably and in good faith
2. Granting extension won't prejudice government interests
3. Not automatically revoked before application date

## Application Process

### Submission
1. Complete appropriate form (1023, 1024, 1024-A)
2. Submit electronically via Pay.gov
3. Pay required user fee
4. Receive acknowledgment

### Processing
- IRS reviews application and supporting documents
- May request additional information
- Issues determination letter upon completion

### Timeline
- Simple applications: 2-4 months
- Complex applications: 6-12 months
- May vary based on IRS workload

## Public Inspection

### Available for Public Inspection
- Exemption application (Form 1023, 1024, etc.)
- Determination letter
- Supporting documents

### Requirements
- Must provide within 30 days of written request
- May charge reasonable copying fee
- Must post on website if applicable

## Revocation

### Material Change
Determination may be revoked if:
- Organization changes character, purpose, or method of operation
- Material change is inconsistent with exemption

### Failure to File Returns
- Automatic revocation after 3 years of non-filing
- Organization must reapply
- May be reinstated with or without retroactivity

### Appeal Before Revocation
Organization has right to:
- Receive written notice of proposed revocation
- Appeal to Independent Office of Appeals
- Seek judicial review

## Foundation Status Determinations

### Initial Determination
- Form 1023 includes foundation status questions
- May request specific ruling on public charity status

### Advance Rulings
- New organizations may receive advance ruling
- Subject to 5-year advance ruling period
- Must establish ongoing qualification

### Final Determination
- After advance ruling period
- Based on actual support records
- May change from advance ruling classification

## Administrative Remedies

Before court action, organization must:
1. File completed application
2. Submit all requested additional information
3. Exhaust all IRS administrative appeals
4. Wait reasonable time for IRS response (270-day rule)

## Court Appeals

### Available Courts
- U.S. Tax Court
- U.S. District Court
- U.S. Court of Federal Claims

### Declaratory Judgment
Available for Section 501(c)(3) organizations and certain others if IRS fails to act timely.
