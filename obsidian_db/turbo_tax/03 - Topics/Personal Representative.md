# Personal Representative

## Overview

A personal representative is an executor, administrator, or anyone in charge of a decedent's property (estate). They are responsible for managing the estate, paying debts and taxes, and distributing assets to beneficiaries.

## Types of Personal Representatives

### Executor/Executrix
- Named in the decedent's will to administer the estate
- Distributes properties as directed by the will
- Must be appointed, qualified, and acting

### Administrator/Administratrix
- Appointed by the court when no will exists
- Appointed when no executor named in the will
- Appointed when named executor cannot or will not serve

### Fiduciary Definition
For tax purposes, a fiduciary includes:
- Guardians
- Trustees
- Executors and administrators
- Receivers
- Conservators

## Primary Duties

1. **Apply for EIN** - Obtain Employer Identification Number for the estate immediately
2. **File Tax Returns** - File all income, estate, and gift tax returns when due
3. **Pay Taxes** - Pay tax determined up to the date of discharge from duties
4. **Collect Assets** - Gather all decedent's assets
5. **Pay Creditors** - Settle the decedent's debts
6. **Distribute Assets** - Distribute remaining assets to heirs and beneficiaries

## Identification Number Requirements

- Must apply for estate EIN as soon as possible
- EIN required on returns, statements, and other documents
- Must provide EIN to payers of interest and dividends
- Cannot use decedent's SSN for returns after the final return

## Penalties

- **Late Filing**: Penalty for failure to file returns when due unless reasonable cause
- **Reasonable Cause**: Reliance on an agent (attorney, accountant) is NOT reasonable cause
- **Missing TIN**: Penalty for not including required identification numbers

## Discharge from Liability

### Prompt Assessment Request (Form 4810)
- Reduces assessment period from 3 years to 18 months
- Can be made for any tax return except estate tax return
- Must be filed separately from other documents

### Discharge from Personal Liability (Form 5495)
- Executor can request discharge for income, gift, and estate taxes
- IRS must notify within 9 months of request
- Payment of notified amount discharges personal liability

## Insolvent Estate Rules

- Federal tax debts must be paid first
- Personal representative may be personally liable for:
  - Tax liabilities they had notice of
  - Tax obligations they failed to investigate before distribution
- Liability limited to amounts paid before federal debts
