# Form 2441

Child and Dependent Care Expenses. Used to claim the [[Child and Dependent Care Credit]] and report [[Dependent Care Benefits]]. Covered in [[Pub 503 - Child and Dependent Care Expenses]].

## Purpose

Use Form 2441 to:
1. Claim the Child and Dependent Care Credit
2. Report and exclude dependent care benefits from income
3. Report taxable dependent care benefits

## Parts of Form 2441

### Part I - Persons or Organizations Who Provided the Care

Report information about your care provider(s):
- Name
- Address
- Identification number (SSN, ITIN, or EIN)
- Amount paid
- Whether the provider was your household employee

**Special entries:**
- "Tax-Exempt" if provider is a tax-exempt organization
- "LAFCP" (Living Abroad Foreign Care Provider) if living abroad
- "See Attached Statement" if provider refused to give information

### Part II - Credit for Child and Dependent Care Expenses

Calculate the credit:

| Line | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Line 1 | Information from Part I about care providers |
| Line 2 | Qualifying persons (name, SSN, qualified expenses) |
| Line 3 | Dollar limit ($3,000 or $6,000) |
| Lines 4-6 | Earned income limitations |
| Lines 7-8 | Smallest of expenses, dollar limit, or earned income |
| Line 9 | AGI-based percentage |
| Line 10 | Credit amount |

### Part III - Dependent Care Benefits

Report employer-provided dependent care benefits:
- Line 12: Amount from Form W-2, Box 10
- Lines 13-26: Calculate exclusion limitation
- Line 27: Amount excludable from income
- Lines 28-31: Adjust dollar limit for credit

## Filing Requirements

- Attach Form 2441 to Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR
- Report the credit on Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 2
- The credit is limited to your tax liability (nonrefundable)

## Required Information

### For Qualifying Persons
- Name
- SSN (or ITIN/ATIN)
- Qualified expenses paid

### For Care Providers
- Name, address, and TIN
- Amount paid for care
- Whether they were your household employee

## Common Errors to Avoid

1. **Missing TIN** - Credit may be reduced or disallowed
2. **Wrong dollar limit** - Remember it's reduced by excluded benefits
3. **Earned income limit** - Must use lower of spouse's income if married
4. **Filing status** - Must file jointly if married (with exceptions)
