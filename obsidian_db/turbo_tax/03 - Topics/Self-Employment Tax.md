# Self-Employment Tax

## Overview

Self-employment tax consists of Social Security and Medicare taxes for individuals who work for themselves. Farmers pay self-employment tax on net farm earnings reported on Schedule F (Form 1040).

## 2025 Tax Rates and Limits

### Social Security Tax
- **Rate**: 12.4% (employer + employee combined)
- **Wage base limit**: $176,100 for 2025
- Only earnings up to this limit are subject to Social Security tax

### Medicare Tax
- **Rate**: 2.9% (employer + employee combined)
- **No wage base limit**: All earnings subject to Medicare tax
- **Additional Medicare Tax**: 0.9% on earnings over $200,000 (single) or $250,000 (married filing jointly)

## Farm Self-Employment Income

### Included Income
- Net profit from Schedule F (Form 1040)
- Crop share rent if landlord materially participates
- Gross income from conservation reserve program (CRP) payments if self-employed
- Income from custom hire work

### Excluded Income
- Cash rent from farmland (unless landlord materially participates)
- Government payments for cost-sharing conservation programs
- Income from land in government conservation programs (CRP) for certain recipients

## Optional Methods

Farmers with low net earnings may use optional methods to report self-employment income:

### Farm Optional Method
- Report two-thirds of gross farm income (up to $8,100) as net earnings
- Must have gross farm income under $11,640
- Useful for building Social Security credits

### Nonfarm Optional Method
- Available for other self-employment income
- Different calculation rules apply

## Deduction for Self-Employment Tax

- Deduct employer's half (50%) of self-employment tax
- Claim as adjustment to income on Schedule 1 (Form 1040)
- Reduces adjusted gross income

## Estimated Tax Payments

Self-employed farmers must make quarterly estimated tax payments if expected tax liability exceeds $1,000. Special rules for farmers:
- **One payment option**: File by March 1 and pay all tax due
- **Estimated tax**: Pay in four installments
