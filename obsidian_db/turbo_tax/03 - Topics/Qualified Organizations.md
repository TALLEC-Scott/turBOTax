# Qualified Organizations

Only contributions to qualified organizations are deductible as charitable contributions. A qualified organization is a nonprofit organization that meets specific IRS requirements.

## Types of Qualified Organizations

### 50% Limit Organizations (First Category)

These organizations qualify for the highest deduction limits:

1. **Churches and Religious Organizations**
   - Churches, conventions or associations of churches
   - Temples, synagogues, mosques
   - Organizations formed solely for religious purposes

2. **Educational Organizations**
   - Schools with regular faculty and curriculum
   - Normally have regularly enrolled student body
   - Colleges, universities, museums
   - Nonprofit daycare centers (if substantially all childcare enables parents to work)

3. **Hospitals and Medical Research Organizations**
   - Nonprofit hospitals
   - Medical research organizations associated with hospitals

4. **Government Entities**
   - United States or any state
   - District of Columbia, U.S. territories
   - Political subdivisions of states or territories
   - Indian tribal governments performing substantial government functions

5. **Publicly Supported Charities**
   - Organizations receiving substantial public support
   - American Red Cross, United Way, Salvation Army
   - Scouting organizations, Girl Scouts of the USA

6. **Private Operating Foundations**
   - Foundations that actively operate charitable programs

7. **Veteran Service Organizations**
   - Federally chartered organizations under section 501(c)(19)
   - Beginning 2025: Membership not required to be limited to wartime veterans

### 30% Limit Organizations (Second Category)

- Private nonoperating foundations
- Organizations not meeting public support tests
- Veterans' organizations (if not in first category)

## Verifying Qualified Status

### IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search

Use **IRS.gov/TEOS** to verify an organization's qualified status:
- Search by organization name or EIN
- Check if eligible to receive tax-deductible contributions
- View deduction limits that apply

### Asking the Organization

Most qualified organizations can tell you if they are qualified. Ask for:
- 501(c)(3) determination letter
- Public charity vs. private foundation status

## International Charities

### Canadian Charities

May be deductible if:
- Covered under U.S.-Canada income tax treaty
- You have income from Canadian sources
- Deduction calculated using Canadian-source income

See [[Pub 597 - Information on the United States-Canada Income Tax Treaty]].

### Mexican Charities

May be deductible if:
- Would qualify as U.S. public charity
- You have income from Mexican sources
- Subject to Mexican-source income limits

### Israeli Charities

May be deductible if:
- Would qualify as U.S. public charity
- You have income from Israeli sources
- Limited to 25% of Israeli-source AGI

## Organizations That Are NOT Qualified

- **Civic leagues and associations**
- **Social and sports clubs**
- **Labor unions**
- **Chambers of commerce**
- **Homeowners' associations**
- **Political organizations and candidates**
- **Foreign organizations** (except certain Canadian, Israeli, Mexican charities)
- **Groups run for personal profit**
- **Groups whose primary purpose is lobbying**

## Special Rules

### Contributions for Public Purposes

Contributions to government entities must be used **solely for public purposes** to be deductible.

**Example:** Contribution to city police department for crime reward fund = deductible.

### Contributions to Fraternal Organizations

Domestic fraternal societies operating under lodge system:
- Deductible only if used for charitable, religious, scientific, literary, or educational purposes
- Or for prevention of cruelty to children or animals

### Cemetery Companies

Nonprofit cemetery companies:
- Deductible only if not for care of specific lot or mausoleum crypt
