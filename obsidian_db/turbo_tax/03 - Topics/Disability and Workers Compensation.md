# Disability and Workers Compensation

## Overview

Disability and workers' compensation benefits have varying tax treatments depending on the source, who paid premiums, and the nature of the disability. Understanding these rules is essential for proper tax reporting.

## Workers' Compensation

### General Rule

Amounts received as workers' compensation for occupational sickness or injury are fully **exempt from tax** if paid under a workers' compensation act or similar statute.

### Exceptions

**Taxable workers' comp situations:**
- Benefits that reduce Social Security or railroad retirement benefits (treated as Social Security benefits)
- Salary payments for light duties while on workers' comp
- Pension benefits based on age or length of service, not disability

### Return to Work

If you return to work after qualifying for workers' compensation, salary payments for light duties are taxable wages.

## Disability Pensions

### Employer-Paid Plans

If you retired on disability, you must include disability pension income from employer-paid plans. Report on Form 1040, line 1h until reaching minimum retirement age.

### Minimum Retirement Age

The earliest age you could receive a pension if not disabled. After this age, payments are taxed as pension/annuity income (reported on lines 5a and 5b).

### Military and Government Disability Pensions

**Nontaxable if any condition applies:**
1. Entitled to receive payment before September 25, 1975
2. Member of armed forces, Public Health Service, NOAA, or Foreign Service on September 24, 1975
3. Payment for combat-related injury
4. Would be entitled to VA disability compensation

### Combat-Related Injury

Includes personal injury or sickness:
- Resulting directly from armed conflict
- During extra-hazardous service
- Under conditions simulating war (including training exercises)
- Caused by instrumentality of war

### Retroactive VA Determination

If you receive a retroactive service-connected disability rating from the VA:
- Retirement pay for retroactive period excluded up to VA benefit amount
- File Form 1040-X for each affected year
- Extended statute of limitations: 1 year from determination date

## Long-Term Care Insurance

### Tax Treatment

Generally treated as accident/health insurance. Benefits are excludable from income as amounts received for personal injury or sickness.

### Per Diem Limit

For 2025, exclusion limited to $420 per day. File Form 8853.

### Chronically Ill Individual

Must be certified as:
- Unable to perform 2+ activities of daily living for 90+ days, OR
- Requiring substantial supervision due to severe cognitive impairment

Activities of daily living: eating, toileting, transferring, bathing, dressing, continence.

## Other Sickness and Injury Benefits

### Taxable Benefits

- Railroad sick pay (except for on-the-job injury)
- Supplemental unemployment benefits from employer-financed fund
- Benefits from employer-paid policy if premiums weren't included in your income

### Nontaxable Benefits

- Black lung benefit payments
- FECA payments for personal injury/sickness (except continuation pay)
- Compensatory damages for physical injury
- Disability benefits from policy you paid premiums for
- Compensation for permanent loss/disfigurement

### Federal Employees' Compensation Act (FECA)

**Nontaxable:** Payments for personal injury or sickness

**Taxable:**
- Continuation of pay for up to 45 days while claim decided (report as wages)
- Sick leave pay while claim processed

### Qualified Indian Health Care Benefits

Nontaxable benefits include:
- Health services from Indian Health Service
- Medical care reimbursements from Indian tribe
- Coverage under accident/health insurance from tribe

## Terrorist or Military Action

Disability payments for injuries directly resulting from terrorist attacks or military action against the United States are excluded from income.

**Exception:** Must still include retirement plan payments you would have received regardless of disability.

## Reporting Requirements

## Common Questions

### Q: I paid premiums for disability insurance. Are benefits taxable?
**A:** No, if you paid the premiums with after-tax dollars, benefits are generally not taxable.

### Q: Is VA disability compensation taxable?
**A:** No, VA disability benefits are completely tax-free.

### Q: How do I report a retroactive VA disability determination?
**A:** File Form 1040-X for each affected year with a copy of the VA determination letter.

### Q: What if my workers' comp reduces my Social Security?
**A:** The reduced amount is treated as Social Security benefits and may be taxable under those rules.
