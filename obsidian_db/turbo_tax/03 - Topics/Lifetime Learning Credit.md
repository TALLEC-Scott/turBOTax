# Lifetime Learning Credit

## Overview

The Lifetime Learning Credit (LLC) is a tax credit for qualified education expenses paid for higher education. Unlike the [[American Opportunity Credit]], there is **no limit on the number of years** you can claim it, and it applies to courses to acquire or improve job skills.

**Maximum Credit:** Up to **$2,000 per tax return** (not per student)

**Authority:** [[Pub 970 - Tax Benefits for Education#Chapter 3]]

## Key Requirements

### Student Requirements
- Enrolled at an eligible educational institution
- Taking courses at undergraduate, graduate, or professional level
- OR taking courses to acquire or improve job skills
- No degree requirement
- No minimum enrollment (can be just one course)
- No felony drug conviction restrictions

### Taxpayer Requirements
- Paid qualified education expenses for eligible student
- Student is yourself, your spouse, or a dependent you claim
- MAGI at or below $90,000 ($180,000 if married filing jointly)
- Cannot file as Married Filing Separately

## Income Limits (2025)

| Filing Status | Phase-Out Begins | Phase-Out Ends |
|---------------|------------------|----------------|
| Single, HoH, QSS | $80,000 | $90,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $160,000 | $180,000 |

## Qualified Education Expenses

- Tuition and fees required for enrollment
- Course-related books, supplies, and equipment required to be paid to the institution
- Expenses paid in 2025 for academic periods beginning in 2025 or first 3 months of 2026

**Note:** Unlike AOTC, room and board are NOT qualified expenses.

## Credit Calculation

| Component | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Maximum Credit | $2,000 per return |
| Credit Rate | 20% of first $10,000 in expenses |
| Refundable | No |

## Form Required

- [[Form 8863]] - Education Credits
- Must provide educational institution's EIN

## Special Rules

### Form 1098-T Requirement
Taxpayers must receive [[Form 1098-T]] from the educational institution. Exceptions exist for certain students.

### Per-Return Limit
The $2,000 limit applies to your entire tax return, not per student. If you have multiple students, combine all expenses up to $10,000.

### Nonrefundable
The credit can reduce your tax to zero, but any excess is not refunded.

## Comparison with American Opportunity Credit

| Feature | Lifetime Learning Credit | American Opportunity Credit |
|---------|-------------------------|----------------------------|
| Maximum Credit | $2,000 per return | $2,500 per student |
| Refundable | No | 40% refundable |
| Years Allowed | Unlimited | 4 years per student |
| Degree Required | No | Yes |
| Half-time Enrollment | No | Yes |
| Felony Drug Conviction | No restriction | Disqualifies |
| Course Materials | Must be paid to institution | Required for all students |

## Coordination with Other Benefits

Cannot claim LLC for the same student in the same year as:
- [[American Opportunity Credit]]

Can coordinate with:
- [[Scholarships and Fellowship Grants]]
- [[Coverdell ESA]] distributions (different expenses)
- [[Qualified Tuition Program]] distributions (different expenses)

## Common Questions

### Q: Can I claim LLC for my child's graduate school?
**A:** Yes, LLC is available for graduate and professional degree courses.

### Q: Can I claim LLC for continuing education?
**A:** Yes, as long as the courses are at an eligible educational institution and help acquire or improve job skills.

### Q: What if I have two children in college?
**A:** Combine all qualified expenses for both children. The maximum credit is $2,000 per return regardless of number of students.
