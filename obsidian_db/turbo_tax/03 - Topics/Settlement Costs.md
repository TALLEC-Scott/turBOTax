# Settlement Costs

## Overview

Settlement or closing costs are fees and charges paid when buying a home or refinancing a mortgage. Understanding which costs are deductible, which are added to basis, and which are neither is essential for proper tax treatment.

## Tax Treatment Categories

| Category | Tax Treatment |
|----------|---------------|
| **Deductible** | Deduct in year paid (if itemizing) |
| **Added to Basis** | Increase [[Basis of Home]] |
| **Neither** | No tax benefit |

## Deductible Settlement Costs

The **only** settlement costs you can deduct in the year paid are:

1. **Home mortgage interest** paid at settlement
2. **Real estate taxes** paid at settlement (your share from date of purchase)

Report these on [[Schedule A (Form 1040)]]:
- Mortgage interest: Line 8a (or 8b if not on Form 1098)
- Real estate taxes: Line 5b

## Costs Added to Basis

Add to basis any fee you would have had to pay **even if you paid cash** for the home.

### Items Added to Basis

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Title fees** | Abstract fees, title search, owner's title insurance |
| **Legal fees** | Attorney fees, preparation of sales contract and deed |
| **Recording fees** | Government filing fees |
| **Transfer taxes** | Stamp taxes, transfer taxes (if paid by buyer) |
| **Surveys** | Property survey costs |
| **Utilities** | Charges for installing utility services |
| **Seller obligations** | Amounts seller owes that you agree to pay (back taxes, improvements) |

### Points Paid by Seller

If the seller paid points for your mortgage:
- **Add to basis**: No (actually reduce basis by this amount)
- See [[Points (Mortgage)]] for special treatment

## Costs Neither Deductible Nor Added to Basis

These costs provide no tax benefit:

1. **Fire insurance premiums**
2. **Charges for utilities** (using services before closing)
3. **Rent before closing** (if you occupied home before closing)
4. **Mortgage-related fees**:
   - Loan assumption fees
   - Credit report fees
   - Appraisal fees
   - Mortgage insurance premiums

## Real Estate Taxes at Settlement

### Division Between Buyer and Seller

For federal tax purposes:
- **Seller**: Treated as paying taxes up to (not including) date of sale
- **Buyer**: Treated as paying taxes from date of sale forward

This applies regardless of who actually paid the bill.

### If Seller Paid Your Share

If the seller paid your share (from date of sale) and you didn't reimburse them:
- **Reduce basis** by that amount
- You can still deduct your share as real estate taxes

### If You Paid Seller's Share

If you paid the seller's share (up to date of sale) and weren't reimbursed:
- **Add to basis** (cannot deduct as taxes)

## Refinancing Costs

Most refinancing costs are **not deductible**:
- Appraisal fees
- Credit report fees
- Loan origination fees (unless they qualify as [[Points (Mortgage)]])
- Title insurance

**Exception**: Points paid on refinancing may be deductible over the loan term.

## Form 1098

Your lender should provide Form 1098 showing:
- Total mortgage interest paid (including at settlement)
- Deductible points

If settlement interest isn't included, add it to the Form 1098 amount and attach an explanation.

## Example: Settlement Statement

| Item | Amount | Treatment |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Down payment | $50,000 | Part of basis (cost) |
| Points paid | $2,000 | Deductible (if tests met) |
| Real estate taxes (your share) | $500 | Deductible |
| Title insurance | $800 | Added to basis |
| Attorney fees | $400 | Added to basis |
| Recording fees | $150 | Added to basis |
| Appraisal fee | $300 | Neither |
| Credit report | $50 | Neither |
