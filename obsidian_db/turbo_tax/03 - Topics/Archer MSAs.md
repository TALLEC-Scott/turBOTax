# Archer MSAs

## Overview

An Archer Medical Savings Account (MSA) is a tax-exempt trust or custodial account established with a U.S. financial institution to save money for future medical expenses. Archer MSAs were designed for self-employed individuals and employees of certain small employers. See [[Pub 969 - Health Savings Accounts and Other Tax-Favored Health Plans]].

## Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for an Archer MSA, you must be:

1. An employee (or employee's spouse) of a small employer that maintains an HDHP for you, OR
2. A self-employed person (or spouse) who maintains an HDHP

### Small Employer Definition
An employer who had an average of **50 or fewer employees** during either of the last 2 calendar years.

### Growing Employer Exception
A small employer may grow beyond 50 employees and still qualify if:
- Had 50 or fewer employees when Archer MSAs began
- Made a contribution for the last year with 50 or fewer employees
- Has an average of 200 or fewer employees each year after 1996

### Active Participant Requirement (Post-2007)
After 2007, you can only be an eligible individual for Archer MSA purposes if:
- You were an active participant for any tax year ending before 2008, OR
- You became an active participant for a tax year ending after 2007 by reason of coverage under an HDHP of an Archer MSA participating employer

## Benefits of an Archer MSA

- Tax deduction for contributions (even if not itemizing)
- Tax-free earnings on assets
- Tax-free distributions for qualified medical expenses
- Contributions remain in account until used
- Portable - stays with you if you change employers

## HDHP Requirements

| 2025 Limits | Self-Only Coverage | Family Coverage |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Minimum Annual Deductible | $2,850 | $5,700 |
| Maximum Annual Deductible | $4,300 | $8,550 |
| Maximum Annual Out-of-Pocket | $5,700 | $10,500 |

Note: These limits differ from [[High Deductible Health Plans|HDHP limits for HSAs]].

## Contribution Rules

### Who Can Contribute
- You can contribute, OR
- Your employer can contribute, BUT NOT both in the same year

**Exception:** If your spouse is covered by your HDHP and your spouse's employer contributes to your spouse's Archer MSA, you cannot contribute.

### Contribution Limits

**Annual Deductible Limit:**
- Self-only plan: Up to 65% of annual deductible
- Family plan: Up to 75% of annual deductible

**Income Limit:**
- Cannot contribute more than earned from the employer through whom you have the HDHP
- Self-employed: Cannot contribute more than net self-employment income

### Changing Employers
If you change employers, your Archer MSA moves with you. However, you may not make additional contributions unless you remain otherwise eligible.

## Medicare Enrollment

Beginning with the first month enrolled in Medicare, you cannot contribute to an Archer MSA. However, you may be eligible for a [[Medicare Advantage MSAs|Medicare Advantage MSA]].

## Distributions

### Qualified Medical Expenses
Tax-free distributions for [[Qualified Medical Expenses]] incurred after establishing the Archer MSA.

### Covered Individuals
- You and your spouse
- All dependents claimed on your tax return
- Persons who could have been claimed as dependents except for certain exceptions

### Insurance Premiums
Generally cannot use Archer MSA for insurance premiums, EXCEPT:
- Long-term care coverage
- Health coverage while receiving unemployment benefits
- Health care continuation coverage (COBRA)

### Non-Qualified Distributions
Subject to:
- Income tax on distribution amount
- 20% additional tax (exceptions: disability, age 65+, death)

## Rollovers

- Can roll over to another Archer MSA or to an [[Health Savings Accounts|HSA]]
- Must complete within 60 days
- Only one rollover per 1-year period
- Trustee-to-trustee transfers are unlimited

## Excess Contributions

- Subject to 6% excise tax per year excess remains in account
- Can withdraw excess by due date (including extensions) to avoid tax
- Must withdraw earnings on excess and report as income

## Death of Archer MSA Holder

- **Spouse beneficiary:** Becomes spouse's Archer MSA
- **Non-spouse beneficiary:** Account ceases to be Archer MSA; FMV taxable in year of death
- **Estate beneficiary:** FMV included on final income tax return

## Employer Participation

For employers making Archer MSA contributions:
- Must make comparable contributions to comparable participating employees
- 35% excise tax on non-comparable contributions
- Contributions not subject to employment taxes
- Report on Form W-2, Box 12, Code R
