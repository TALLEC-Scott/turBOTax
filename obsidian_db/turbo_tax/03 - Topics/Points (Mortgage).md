# Points (Mortgage)

## Overview

"Points" are charges paid (or treated as paid) by a borrower to obtain a home mortgage. Points may also be called:
- Loan origination fees
- Maximum loan charges
- Loan discount
- Discount points

Points are a form of prepaid interest.

## General Rule

Points are **prepaid interest** and must generally be deducted over the life (term) of the mortgage, not in the year paid.

## Exception: Full Deduction in Year Paid

You can deduct the full amount of points in the year paid if you meet **ALL** of the following tests:

1. The loan is secured by your **main home**
2. Paying points is an **established business practice** in your area
3. The points paid were **not more than generally charged** in your area
4. You use the **cash method** of accounting (most individuals do)
5. The points were **not paid in place of** amounts ordinarily stated separately (appraisal fees, title fees, attorney fees, property taxes)
6. The funds you provided at closing **(plus seller-paid points)** were at least as much as the points charged
7. You use the loan to **buy or build** your main home
8. The points were **figured as a percentage** of the principal
9. The amount is **clearly shown** on the settlement statement as points

### Note

If you meet all tests, you can choose to either deduct points fully in year paid OR deduct them over the life of the loan.

## Home Improvement Loans

Points paid on a loan to substantially improve your main home can be fully deducted in the year paid if you meet tests 1-6 above.

## Refinanced Loans

### Partial Deduction

If you refinance and use part of the proceeds to substantially improve your main home:
- Points related to the improvement: Fully deductible in year paid (if tests 1-6 are met)
- Remaining points: Deduct over the life of the loan

### Same Lender Refinancing

If you refinance with the **same lender**, you cannot deduct remaining points from the old loan in the year of refinancing. Instead, deduct them over the term of the new loan.

## Points Paid by Seller

Seller-paid points are treated as if the buyer paid them:
- **Buyer** can deduct the points (if tests are met) or deduct over loan life
- **Seller** treats points as a selling expense (reduces amount realized)
- **Buyer** must reduce the [[Basis of Home]] by the seller-paid points

## Points Not Fully Deductible in Year Paid

If you don't qualify for the exception, see [[Pub 936 - Home Mortgage Interest Deduction]] for rules on deducting points over the loan term.

## Excess Points

If points paid exceed the amount generally charged in your area, you can only deduct the "normal" points in the year paid. Spread the excess over the mortgage life.

## Mortgage Ending Early

If you spread points over the loan life and the mortgage ends early (prepayment, refinancing, foreclosure), you can deduct any remaining balance in that year.

**Exception**: If you refinance with the same lender, remaining points are deducted over the new loan term.

## What Are NOT Points

The following charges are **not** points and are not deductible as interest:
- Appraisal fees
- Title insurance
- Notary fees
- Mortgage insurance premiums
- Preparation costs for mortgage note/deed of trust

These costs generally are added to the [[Basis of Home]].

## Examples

### Example 1: Insufficient Funds at Closing

You take a $100,000 mortgage and are charged 1 point ($1,000). You meet all tests except your down payment was only $750.

- **Deductible in year paid**: $750 (the funds you provided)
- **Spread over loan life**: $250 remaining

### Example 2: Seller-Paid Points

Same facts as Example 1, but the seller also paid 1 point ($1,000).

- **Deductible in year paid**: $1,750 ($750 from your funds + $1,000 seller-paid)
- **Spread over loan life**: $250 remaining
- **Reduce home basis by**: $1,000 (seller-paid points)
