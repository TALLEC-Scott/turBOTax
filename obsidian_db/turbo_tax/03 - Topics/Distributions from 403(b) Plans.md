# Distributions from 403(b) Plans

## Overview

This topic covers the rules for taking distributions (withdrawals) from [[403(b) Plans]]. Understanding when you can take distributions and the tax consequences is essential for retirement planning.

## Permissible Distribution Events

Generally, a distribution cannot be made from a 403(b) account until one of the following occurs:

| Event | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Age 59½ | Participant reaches age 59½ |
| Death | Participant dies |
| Severance from employment | Participant leaves employer |
| Disability | Participant becomes disabled |
| Financial hardship | For elective deferrals only |
| Qualified reservist distribution | Called to active duty |
| Lifetime income investment | Certain annuity distributions |
| Qualified birth or adoption distribution | New parent distribution |
| Emergency personal expense distribution | Emergency needs |
| Qualified disaster recovery distribution | Disaster victim distribution |
| Domestic abuse distribution | Victim of domestic abuse |

## Tax Treatment of Distributions

### Ordinary Income

Most distributions from 403(b) accounts are taxable in full as **ordinary income** in the year received.

### No 10-Year Tax Option

403(b) distributions do NOT qualify as lump-sum distributions, so you cannot use the special 10-year tax option. See [[Pub 575]] for more information.

## Early Distribution Tax

### 10% Additional Tax

Distributions before age 59½ may be subject to a 10% additional tax on early distributions.

### Exceptions to 10% Tax

The following distributions are NOT subject to the 10% additional tax:

- Distributions after age 59½
- Distributions due to disability
- Distributions after death
- Substantially equal periodic payments
- Distributions under a QDRO
- Qualified reservist distributions
- Qualified disaster recovery distributions
- Emergency personal expense distributions (after December 31, 2023)
- Domestic abuse distributions (after December 31, 2023)
- Distributions to terminally ill individuals

## Minimum Required Distributions (MRDs)

### When Must Distributions Begin?

You must begin receiving distributions by **April 1** of the calendar year following the later of:
- The year you reach age **73** (if you reached age 72 after December 31, 2022)
- The year you retire

### Annual Requirements

After the initial required beginning date, minimum distributions must be made by **December 31** of each year.

### Pre-1987 Accruals

Check with your employer or plan administrator about pre-1987 accruals. If not subject to current rules, distributions must begin by the later of:
- End of calendar year you reach age 75
- April 1 following retirement

### Failure to Take MRD

Subject to a **25% excise tax** on the difference between required and actual distribution.

**Reduced Penalty**: If corrected within specified time, tax is reduced to **10%**.

## Retired Public Safety Officers

If you're an eligible retired public safety officer, you can exclude up to **$3,000** from gross income for distributions used to pay premiums for:
- Accident or health plan coverage
- Long-term care insurance

**Coverage For**: You, your spouse, or your dependents

**Who Qualifies**: Law enforcement officers, firefighters, chaplains, rescue squad members, ambulance crew members

## Qualified Reservist Distributions

The 10% additional tax does not apply to qualified reservist distributions if:
- You're a reservist or National Guard member
- Called to active duty for more than 179 days or indefinite period
- Distribution made during active duty period
