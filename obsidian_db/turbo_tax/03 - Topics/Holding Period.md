# Holding Period

## Overview

The holding period determines whether a gain or loss is short-term or long-term. This classification affects the tax rate applied to capital gains.

## Short-Term vs. Long-Term

| Holding Period | Classification | Tax Rate |
|----------------|----------------|----------|
| 1 year or less | Short-term | Ordinary income rates |
| More than 1 year | Long-term | Preferential capital gains rates |

## How to Count

1. Start counting on the day **after** you acquire the property
2. The day you dispose of the property **is included**
3. Count all calendar days (including weekends, holidays)

### Example
- Purchased: June 17, 2024
- Start counting: June 18, 2024
- Short-term ends: June 17, 2025 (exactly 1 year)
- Long-term begins: June 18, 2025

## Holding Period by Acquisition Type

| Type of Acquisition | Holding Period Starts |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| Securities bought on market | Day after trading date |
| U.S. Treasury notes/bonds (auction) | Day after bid acceptance |
| U.S. Treasury notes/bonds (subscription) | Day after subscription submitted |
| Nontaxable exchange | Day after acquiring old property |
| Gift (donor's basis) | Same as donor's holding period |
| Gift (FMV basis) | Day after date of gift |
| Real property purchased | Day after receiving title or possession |
| Real property repossessed | Day after original title, excludes time between |
| Inheritance | Always treated as long-term |
| Patent property | Always treated as long-term |

## Special Rules

### Inherited Property
- **Always long-term** regardless of actual holding period
- Step-up (or step-down) in basis to FMV at date of death

### Nontaxable Exchanges
- Holding period includes old property's period
- "Tacks on" to new property

### Installment Sales
- Character determined in year of sale
- Maintains character in subsequent years
- **Tip:** Rate is determined by payment date, not sale date

### Corporate Liquidation
- Generally starts day after receipt
- If gain/loss recognized

### Profit-Sharing Plan Stock
- Starts day after trustee delivers to transfer agent
- For reissuance in your name
