# Schedule F (Form 1040)

## Overview

Schedule F (Form 1040), Profit or Loss From Farming, is the primary form used to report farm income and expenses. Individuals, trusts, partnerships, S corporations, and LLCs taxed as partnerships use this form to calculate net farm profit or loss.

## Who Files Schedule F

### Eligible Filers
- Individual farmers (sole proprietors)
- Partners in farming partnerships
- Shareholders in S corporations engaged in farming
- Single-member LLCs
- Trusts with farm income

### Corporations
- C corporations report farm income on Form 1120
- Do not use Schedule F

## Part I - Farm Income

### Sales of Products
- Line 1a: Sales of livestock, produce, grains, etc.
- Line 1b: Cost of items purchased for resale
- Line 1c: Profit from sales

### Other Income
- Government agricultural program payments
- Commodity Credit Corporation loans
- Crop insurance proceeds
- Custom hire income
- Other farm income

### Cooperative Distributions
- Report patronage dividends
- Per-unit retain allocations

## Part II - Farm Expenses

### Common Deductions
- Car and truck expenses
- Chemicals
- Conservation expenses
- Custom hire
- Depreciation
- Employee benefit programs
- Feed
- Fertilizer and lime
- Freight and trucking
- Gasoline and fuel
- Insurance
- Interest
- Labor hired
- Pension and profit-sharing plans
- Rent or lease
- Repairs and maintenance
- Seeds and plants
- Storage and warehousing
- Supplies
- Taxes
- Utilities
- Veterinary and breeding fees
- Other expenses

## Net Farm Profit or Loss

### Calculation
- Part I income minus Part II expenses
- Result flows to Form 1040, Schedule 1
- Also used to calculate self-employment tax

### Net Operating Loss
- If expenses exceed income, may have NOL
- Can carry forward to offset future income

## Filing Requirements

### Principal Agricultural Activity Codes
Must enter code on line B identifying principal farm activity:
- Oilseed and grain farming
- Vegetable and melon farming
- Fruit and tree nut farming
- Greenhouse and nursery production
- Cattle ranching and farming
- Hog and pig farming
- Poultry and egg production
- Sheep and goat farming
- Other crop farming
