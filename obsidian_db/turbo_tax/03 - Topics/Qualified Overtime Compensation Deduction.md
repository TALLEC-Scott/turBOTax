# Qualified Overtime Compensation Deduction

## Overview

For tax years 2025-2028, the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" (P.L. 119-21) allows employees to deduct qualified overtime compensation on their income tax returns. This provision helps workers keep more of their overtime earnings.

## Eligibility Period

- **Effective**: Tax years beginning after December 31, 2024
- **Expires**: Tax years beginning before January 1, 2029
- Applies to: Employees and other workers not treated as employees

## Deduction Limits

| Filing Status | Maximum Deduction |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Single | $12,500 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $25,000 |

## What is Qualified Overtime Compensation?

Qualified overtime is compensation that:
- Exceeds the regular rate of pay
- Is required to be paid under section 7 of the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) of 1938
- Typically the "half" portion of time-and-a-half compensation

### FLSA Overtime Rule
Employers must generally pay covered, nonexempt employees:
- At least one-and-a-half times their regular rate of pay
- For hours worked over 40 hours per week

## Tax Treatment

### Income Tax
- Deductible on individual income tax return
- Reduces taxable income

### Employment Taxes
- Still subject to Social Security tax (both employer and employee share)
- Still subject to Medicare tax (both shares)
- Not exempt from FICA

## Employer Withholding Responsibilities

### If Employee Submits Updated Form W-4:
1. Use the employee's updated Form W-4
2. Follow federal income tax withholding procedures in Pub 15-T
3. Allow employee to account for expected deduction
4. Employee receives more money in each paycheck

### Purpose:
Instead of waiting for a tax refund, employees can receive the benefit of the deduction throughout the year through reduced withholding.

## Information Return Requirements

Employers and payers must:
- File information returns (Forms W-2, 1099-MISC, 1099-NEC) with SSA or IRS
- Furnish statements to overtime recipients showing qualified overtime compensation paid during the year

### 2025 Transition Relief
The IRS provided transition relief for tax year 2025 reporting requirements. See Notice 2025-62, 2025-48 I.R.B. 740.

## Private Homes and Domestic Service

For more information about overtime requirements for:
- Private homes
- Domestic service employment

See: dol.gov/agencies/whd/fact-sheets/79-flsa-private-home-domestic-service

## Example

**Scenario**: Employee earns $20/hour regular rate
- Overtime rate: $30/hour (time-and-a-half)
- Regular portion: $20/hour
- **Qualified overtime portion: $10/hour** (the "half" portion)

If employee works 500 overtime hours at $10 qualified overtime portion:
- Qualified overtime compensation: $5,000
- Deductible on tax return (within limits)

## Household Employer Considerations

For household employers:
- This deduction affects income tax withholding (if requested by employee)
- Does NOT affect Social Security/Medicare tax obligations
- Be prepared to adjust withholding if employee provides updated Form W-4
