# Home Improvements

## Overview

Home improvements are additions or alterations that increase your home's value, prolong its useful life, or adapt it to new uses. The cost of improvements is added to the [[Basis of Home]] rather than deducted as an expense.

## Definition of Improvement

An improvement must meet at least ONE of these criteria:
1. **Materially adds to the value** of your home
2. **Considerably prolongs** its useful life
3. **Adapts it to new uses**

## Types of Improvements

### Additions
- Bedroom
- Bathroom
- Deck
- Garage
- Porch or patio
- Storage shed
- Fireplace

### Lawn and Grounds
- Landscaping
- Driveway
- Walkway
- Fences
- Retaining wall
- Sprinkler system
- Swimming pool
- Exterior lighting

### Exterior
- Roof replacement
- Storm windows and doors
- Siding

### Interior
- Built-in appliances
- Kitchen modernization
- Flooring (wall-to-wall carpet, hardwood, tile)
- Central vacuum system

### Systems
- Central air conditioning
- Heating system
- Plumbing
- Electrical wiring
- Security system

### Communications
- Satellite dish
- Intercom system

## What to Include in Cost

Add to the cost of an improvement:
- **Material costs**
- **Labor costs** (but not your own labor)
- **Architect fees**
- **Building permit fees**
- **Survey costs** related to the improvement

## Improvements No Longer Part of Home

When you replace an improvement, **remove its cost** from your adjusted basis.

**Example**: You installed wall-to-wall carpet 15 years ago for $3,000. This year, you replace it with new carpeting for $5,000. Remove the $3,000 from basis and add $5,000.

## Repairs vs. Improvements

| Repairs (Not Added to Basis) | Improvements (Added to Basis) |
|------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Repainting interior/exterior | Adding a room |
| Fixing gutters or floors | New roof |
| Patching leaks | Central A/C installation |
| Replacing broken windows | Rewiring the house |
| Fixing plaster | New plumbing system |

### Key Distinction

- **Repair**: Keeps home in ordinary operating condition
- **Improvement**: Adds value, extends life, or adapts to new uses

**Exception**: Repairs done as part of extensive remodeling or restoration are treated as improvements.

## Nondeductible Improvements

You **cannot deduct** improvement costs. Instead, add them to basis.

## Energy-Related Improvements

### Energy Conservation Subsidies

If a public utility gives you a subsidy for energy conservation measures:
- Don't include the subsidy in income
- **Reduce basis** by the subsidy amount

### Residential Energy Credits

If you claim a credit for qualifying energy-efficient improvements:
- **Reduce basis** by the credit amount claimed

See [[Residential Energy Credits]] for details.

## Assessments for Local Improvements

Assessments for local benefits that increase property value (streets, sidewalks, sewer systems):
- **Add to basis** (not deductible as taxes)
- If part is for maintenance/interest, that portion may be deductible

## Recordkeeping

Keep detailed records of all improvements, including:
- Description of the improvement
- Date completed
- Cost (materials, labor, permits, fees)
- Receipts and cancelled checks
- Before/after photos (helpful)

Use [[Pub 530 - Tax Information for Homeowners]] Table 4 as a tracking template.

## Why It Matters

Proper tracking of improvements:
- Reduces gain when you sell (higher basis = lower gain)
- May help qualify for full gain exclusion
- Provides documentation for IRS if audited
