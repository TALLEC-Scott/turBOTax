# Social Security and Medicare Taxes for Household Employers

## Overview

Household employers must withhold and pay Social Security and Medicare taxes (FICA) on wages paid to household employees who meet certain thresholds. These taxes fund retirement, disability, and hospital insurance programs.

## 2026 Thresholds and Rates

| Tax | Employer Rate | Employee Rate | Total | Wage Base |
|-----|---------------|---------------|-------|-----------|
| Social Security | 6.2% | 6.2% | 12.4% | $184,500 |
| Medicare | 1.45% | 1.45% | 2.9% | No limit |

**Filing Threshold**: Cash wages of $3,000 or more in 2026 to any one household employee.

## When Taxes Apply

### Social Security and Medicare Taxes Apply When:
- You pay cash wages of $3,000 or more in 2026 to any one household employee

### Social Security and Medicare Taxes Do NOT Apply When:
- You pay less than $3,000 in cash wages to the employee in 2026
- Wages are paid to exempt individuals (spouse, child under 21, parent, employee under 18)

## Calculating the Tax

### Employee's Share (7.65%)
- 6.2% Social Security tax (up to wage base)
- 1.45% Medicare tax (all wages)

### Employer's Share (7.65%)
- 6.2% Social Security tax (up to wage base)
- 1.45% Medicare tax (all wages)

### Example Calculation
| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Total cash wages | $3,360 |
| Employee SS tax (6.2%) | $208.32 |
| Employee Medicare tax (1.45%) | $48.72 |
| Employer SS tax (6.2%) | $208.32 |
| Employer Medicare tax (1.45%) | $48.72 |
| **Total tax due** | **$514.08** |

## Withholding Options

### Option 1: Withhold Employee's Share
- Withhold 7.65% from each wage payment
- Use withholding table (Table 3 in Pub 926) to calculate proper amount
- Stop withholding Social Security tax after wages reach $184,500

### Option 2: Pay Employee's Share Yourself
- Don't withhold from employee's wages
- Pay both shares from your own funds
- Include employee's share in their wages for income tax purposes
- Don't count the employer-paid amount as Social Security/Medicare wages or FUTA wages

## Additional Medicare Tax

For employees earning over $200,000 in a calendar year:
- **Additional rate**: 0.9% on wages over $200,000
- **Employee only**: No employer share
- Begin withholding in pay period when wages exceed $200,000
- Continue through end of calendar year

## Cash Wages Defined

Cash wages include:
- Payments by check, money order, electronic transfer
- Cash paid in lieu of food, lodging, clothing

Cash wages do NOT include:
- Value of food, lodging, clothing
- Transit passes (up to limits)
- Qualified parking reimbursements (up to limits)

## Exempt Workers (Wages Not Counted)

Don't count wages paid to:
1. **Your spouse** - All wages exempt
2. **Your child under age 21** - All wages exempt
3. **Your parent** - Generally exempt, EXCEPT if:
   - Parent cares for your child under 18 or with a qualifying condition, AND
   - You're divorced/widowed, OR living with a disabled spouse
4. **Employee under age 18** - Exempt unless household services is their principal occupation

## Reporting on Form W-2

| Box | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| Box 1 | Wages, tips, other compensation |
| Box 3 | Social Security wages (up to $184,500) |
| Box 4 | Social Security tax withheld |
| Box 5 | Medicare wages and tips |
| Box 6 | Medicare tax withheld |

If paying employee's share yourself, add the taxes paid to Box 1 wages and report in Boxes 4 and 6.
