# Additional Medicare Tax

## Overview

The Additional Medicare Tax is a 0.9% surtax on wages, compensation, and self-employment income above certain thresholds. It was enacted as part of the Affordable Care Act and took effect in 2013.

## Tax Rate

**0.9%** (in addition to the standard 1.45% Medicare tax)

## Income Thresholds (2025)

| Filing Status | Threshold |
|---------------|-----------|
| Single | $200,000 |
| Head of Household | $200,000 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | $200,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $250,000 |
| Married Filing Separately | $125,000 |

## What Income Is Subject to Additional Medicare Tax

### Wages and Compensation
- Wages from employment
- Tips reported to employer
- Bonuses and commissions
- Vacation allowances
- Sick pay

### Self-Employment Income
- Net earnings from self-employment
- Guaranteed payments to partners
- Income from pass-through entities

### Railroad Retirement Compensation
- Tier 1 and Tier 2 compensation

## How It Works

### Employee Withholding
- Employers must withhold 0.9% on wages **over $200,000** regardless of filing status
- Employer does NOT consider spouse's income
- Withholding begins when year-to-date wages exceed $200,000

### Example: Employee Withholding
- Employee earns $250,000 in wages
- Employer withholds Additional Medicare Tax on $50,000 ($250,000 - $200,000)
- Extra withholding: $450 ($50,000 × 0.9%)

### Self-Employed Individuals
- Must calculate and pay with estimated taxes
- Include in quarterly estimated payments
- Report on Form 1040 Schedule 2

## Married Filing Jointly Considerations

### Combined Income
- Threshold is $250,000 for joint returns
- If both spouses work, withholding may be insufficient
- Example: Each spouse earns $150,000 = $300,000 combined
- Each employer withholds only on amount over $200,000 individually
- Couple may owe additional tax at filing time

### Adjusting Withholding
- File Form W-4 to request additional withholding (Step 4(c))
- Use IRS Withholding Estimator at IRS.gov/W4App
- May need to make estimated tax payments

## Married Filing Separately

- Lower threshold: $125,000
- May result in higher total Additional Medicare Tax compared to MFJ
- Consider filing status implications

## Calculating the Tax

### Form 8959
Use Form 8959 (Additional Medicare Tax) to calculate:
1. Total wages, tips, and other compensation
2. Self-employment income
3. Railroad retirement compensation
4. Apply appropriate threshold
5. Calculate 0.9% on excess

### Integration with Other Taxes
Additional Medicare Tax is part of overall tax liability:
- Report on Form 1040 Schedule 2
- Include in estimated tax calculations
- May affect [[Underpayment Penalty]] calculations

## Withholding on Form W-4

### Requesting Additional Withholding
- Enter additional amount in Step 4(c)
- Calculate using worksheets in [[Pub 505 - Tax Withholding and Estimated Tax|Pub 505]]
- Consider combined income if married filing jointly

### Part-Year Employees
- Withholding begins when cumulative wages exceed $200,000
- No catch-up for earlier part of year

## Interaction with Medicare Tax

### Total Medicare Tax Rates

| Taxpayer | Employee Rate | Employer Rate | Total |
|----------|---------------|---------------|-------|
| Standard Medicare | 1.45% | 1.45% | 2.9% |
| Additional Medicare | 0.9% | 0% | 0.9% |
| **Total on excess wages** | **2.35%** | **1.45%** | **3.8%** |

### Employer Responsibility
- Employer pays standard 1.45% Medicare on all wages
- Employer does NOT pay Additional Medicare Tax
- Only employee pays the 0.9% additional amount

## Estimated Tax Considerations

### Self-Employed Taxpayers
Include Additional Medicare Tax when calculating:
- Quarterly estimated payments
- Annualized income calculations
- Safe harbor calculations

### Safe Harbor Impact
- Additional Medicare Tax is included in "total tax" for penalty calculations
- May need to adjust estimated payments to avoid underpayment

## Special Situations

### Nonresident Aliens
- Subject to Additional Medicare Tax on U.S.-source wages
- Same thresholds apply
- See [[Pub 519 - U.S. Tax Guide for Aliens|Pub 519]]

### Household Employees
- Subject to Additional Medicare Tax on wages over threshold
- Employer withholds on amount over $200,000

### Nonprofit Employees
- Subject to Additional Medicare Tax even if organization is exempt from FICA

## Reporting

### Forms and Documents

| Form | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Form 8959 | Calculate Additional Medicare Tax |

### Form W-2
- Box 5 shows Medicare wages and tips
- Box 6 shows Medicare tax withheld (includes Additional Medicare)
