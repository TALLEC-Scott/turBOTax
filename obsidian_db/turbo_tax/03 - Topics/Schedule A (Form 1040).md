# Schedule A (Form 1040)

## Overview

Schedule A is used to report itemized deductions as an alternative to taking the standard deduction. Itemizing is beneficial when your total deductible expenses exceed the standard deduction amount.

## When to Itemize

Itemize if your total deductions exceed the standard deduction:
- **Single or Married Filing Separately**: $15,000 (2025)
- **Married Filing Jointly**: $30,000 (2025)
- **Head of Household**: $22,500 (2025)

## Deduction Categories

### Medical and Dental Expenses (Lines 1-4)

- Deductible if exceeding 7.5% of AGI
- See [[Medical and Dental Expenses]] and [[Pub 502 - Medical and Dental Expenses]]

### State and Local Taxes (Lines 5a-5e)

- Subject to SALT limit ($40,000 for 2025)
- Includes income/sales taxes and real estate taxes
- See [[SALT Deduction]] and [[Real Estate Taxes]]

### Interest (Lines 8a-8c)

- Home mortgage interest on qualified loans
- [[Points (Mortgage)]]
- See [[Home Mortgage Interest]]

### Charitable Contributions (Lines 11-14)

- Cash and non-cash donations
- Subject to AGI limits
- See [[Charitable Contributions]] and [[Pub 526 - Charitable Contributions]]

### Casualty and Theft Losses (Line 15)

- Only for federally declared disasters
- See [[Pub 547 - Casualties, Disasters, and Thefts]]

### Other Itemized Deductions (Line 16)

- Gambling losses (to extent of winnings)
- Certain investment expenses
- See instructions for complete list

## SALT Deduction Limit

For 2025, the total SALT deduction is limited to:
- **$40,000** ($20,000 if married filing separately)
- Reduced if modified AGI exceeds $500,000 ($250,000 MFS)
- Not reduced below $10,000 ($5,000 MFS)
