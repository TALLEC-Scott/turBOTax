# Mortgage Debt Limits

## Overview

The amount of home mortgage interest you can deduct is limited based on when your mortgage debt was incurred and the type of debt. These limits apply to the combined mortgages on your main home and second home.

## Debt Limit Summary Table

| Debt Type | When Incurred | Limit (MFJ/QSS) | Limit (MFS) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| [[Grandfathered Debt]] | On or before October 13, 1987 | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| [[Home Acquisition Debt]] | After October 13, 1987 and before December 16, 2017 | $1,000,000 | $500,000 |
| [[Home Acquisition Debt]] | After December 15, 2017 | $750,000 | $375,000 |

## Important Notes

### Binding Contract Exception

A taxpayer who entered into a written binding contract before December 15, 2017, to close on the purchase of a principal residence before January 1, 2018, and who purchases such residence before April 1, 2018, is considered to have incurred the debt prior to December 16, 2017 (eligible for $1M limit).

### Grandfathered Debt Effect

The amount of your [[Grandfathered Debt]] reduces the limit for [[Home Acquisition Debt]] (but not below zero).

### Combined Limits

The limits apply to the **combined mortgages** on your main home and second home, not separately to each.

## When Interest Is Fully Deductible

Your home mortgage interest is fully deductible if ALL your mortgages fit into these categories at ALL times during the year:

1. All are [[Grandfathered Debt]]
2. Total mortgage balances are within the applicable limits

## When Limits Apply

If any mortgage doesn't fit into one of the three categories, or if total balances exceed the limits, use Table 1 in [[Pub 936 - Home Mortgage Interest Deduction]] to figure your deductible interest.

## Calculating Average Balance

To determine if you're within the limits, calculate your average mortgage balance using:

1. **Average of first and last balance** - For level-payment mortgages with no new borrowing
2. **Interest paid ÷ interest rate** - Divide annual interest by annual rate
3. **Lender statements** - Sum monthly balances and divide by 12

## Mixed-Use Mortgages

A mixed-use mortgage contains more than one category of debt. Principal payments are applied in this order:

1. First: Home equity debt (not used for home)
2. Second: Grandfathered debt
3. Third: Home acquisition debt

## Excess Interest Treatment

Interest on debt exceeding the limits is:

- **Not deductible** as home mortgage interest if used for personal purposes
- **Deductible elsewhere** if proceeds used for business, investment, or other deductible activities

| Activity | Deduct On |
|----------|-----------|
| Business (non-farm) | Schedule C |
| Farm business | Schedule F |
| Rental/royalty production | Schedule E |
| Investment interest | Schedule A, line 9 |

## Expired Provisions

- **Mortgage insurance premiums** - Deduction has expired
- **Home equity loan interest** - Only deductible if proceeds used to buy, build, or substantially improve the home
