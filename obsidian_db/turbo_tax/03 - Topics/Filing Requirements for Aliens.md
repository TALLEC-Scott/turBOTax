# Filing Requirements for Aliens

## Overview

Determining filing requirements for aliens depends on residency status, income type, and source of income. This topic covers the key filing obligations for both resident and nonresident aliens.

## Resident Aliens

### General Rule
Resident aliens file [[Form 1040]] or [[Form 1040-SR]] following the same rules as U.S. citizens.

### Filing Thresholds for 2025

| Filing Status | Gross Income Threshold |
|---------------|------------------------|
| Single | $15,000 (under 65) / $16,550 (65+) |
| Married Filing Jointly | $30,000 (both under 65) |
| Married Filing Separately | $5 |
| Head of Household | $22,500 (under 65) |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | $30,000 |

**Additional Requirements:** Self-employment income > $400, or other special situations.

### Due Date
**April 15** of the following year (or next business day if weekend/holiday).

### Extensions
- Automatic 6-month extension with [[Form 4868]]
- Automatic 2-month extension (June 15) if living outside U.S. on April 15
- Additional discretionary extensions available

## Nonresident Aliens

### Who Must File Form 1040-NR

You must file if you are a nonresident alien who:

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Engaged in U.S. trade or business | Even if no income from the business |
| U.S. income not fully withheld | Tax liability not satisfied by withholding |
| Want refund or deductions | To claim benefits you're entitled to |
| Representative of nonresident | Fiduciary or agent responsibility |

### Exceptions (No Filing Required)

You do NOT need to file Form 1040-NR if:

1. **Students/teachers on F, J, M, Q visas** with no taxable income
2. **India treaty students** (Article 21(2)) with gross income ≤ $15,000 (single)
3. **Partners** in U.S. partnership with only non-ECI income

**Caution:** The previous exception for personal services with wages ≤ personal exemption is NO LONGER AVAILABLE.

### Due Dates

| Situation | Due Date |
|-----------|----------|
| Received wages subject to withholding | April 15 |
| No wages subject to withholding | June 15 |
| Fiscal year | 15th day of 4th or 6th month |

### Extensions
File [[Form 4868]] by due date for automatic 6-month extension.

**Note:** Extension extends time to file, NOT time to pay.

## Dual-Status Aliens

### Resident at Year-End
- File [[Form 1040]] with "Dual Status Return" written at top
- Attach [[Form 1040-NR]] as statement for nonresident period
- **Cannot e-file** for 2025

### Nonresident at Year-End
- File [[Form 1040-NR]] with "Dual Status Return" written at top
- Attach [[Form 1040]] as statement for resident period
- **Cannot e-file** for 2025

### Restrictions
- Cannot claim standard deduction
- Cannot use head of household status
- Generally cannot file jointly (unless making resident election)

## When to File for Deductions and Credits

To claim deductions and credits on Form 1040-NR, you must file within **16 months** of the due date.

**Exception:** This requirement does NOT apply to:
- Credit for withheld taxes
- Credit for excise tax on gasoline/special fuels
- Credit for tax paid by mutual fund on undistributed capital gains

### Protective Return
If you believe you have no ECI but want to protect your right to deductions/credits, file a protective Form 1040-NR by the deadline. Report only a statement that you are filing to protect your rights.

## Where to File

### Resident Aliens
- Refer to Instructions for Form 1040 for current mailing addresses
- E-file available

### Nonresident Aliens
- See Instructions for Form 1040-NR for current addresses
- Mail to Austin, TX service center for most situations
- E-file available through approved providers

### Dual-Status Aliens
- See specific addresses in Instructions for Form 1040-NR
- Paper filing required (cannot e-file)

## Amended Returns

### Resident Aliens
File [[Form 1040-X]] within 3 years from original due date or 2 years from tax payment, whichever is later.

### Nonresident Aliens
File amended Form 1040-NR with "Amended" written at top, or use Form 1040-X.

## Foreign-Owned Domestic Disregarded Entities

If a foreign person wholly owns a domestic disregarded entity (DE):

- DE treated as domestic corporation for Form 5472 purposes
- Must file pro forma Form 1120 with Form 5472 attached
- Due date: Same as owner's return (including extensions)

## Records to Keep

Maintain records of:
- Days present in the U.S. (for substantial presence test)
- Visa status and changes
- Foreign income and taxes paid
- Documents supporting treaty claims
- Source of income determinations

**Duration:** Keep records for at least 3 years from filing date.

## Failure to File Penalties

| Penalty | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Failure to file | 5% per month, max 25% |
| Failure to pay | 0.5% per month, max 25% |
| Failure to file Form 8833 (treaty disclosure) | $1,000 per failure |
