# Installment Sales

## Overview

An **installment sale** is a sale of property where the seller receives at least one payment after the tax year of the sale. Under IRC Section 453, the installment method allows taxpayers to report gain proportionally as payments are received, rather than recognizing the entire gain in the year of sale.

## Key Rules

### Qualification Requirements

1. **Property sale** - Must involve the sale of property (not services)
2. **Payment after year-end** - At least one payment received after the tax year of sale
3. **Gain realized** - Must have a gain (losses cannot use installment method)
4. **Eligible property** - Not inventory, dealer property, or publicly traded securities

### Ineligible Sales

The installment method **cannot** be used for:
- **Inventory sales** - Regular sales of personal property held for sale to customers
- **Dealer sales** - Sales by persons who regularly sell the same type of property on installment plans
- **Stock/securities** - Stocks or securities traded on established securities markets
- **Losses** - Any sale at a loss (loss deducted in year of sale)

### Special Dealer Rules

Dealers in timeshares and residential lots may use the installment method if they elect to pay a special interest charge under Section 453(l).

## Figuring Installment Sale Income

### Payment Components

Each payment consists of three parts:
1. **Return of adjusted basis** (tax-free recovery of investment)
2. **Interest income** (ordinary income, reported separately)
3. **Gain on sale** (installment sale income)

### Gross Profit Percentage

The [[Gross Profit Percentage]] determines how much of each payment is taxable gain:

```
Gross Profit % = Gross Profit ÷ Contract Price
```

This percentage remains constant throughout the installment period unless the selling price is reduced.

## Contract Price Calculation

**Contract Price** = Selling Price − Mortgages/Debts Assumed + Excess of Liabilities Over Basis

When the buyer assumes or takes the property subject to a mortgage:
- **Mortgage ≤ Basis** - Not treated as payment; reduces contract price
- **Mortgage > Basis** - Excess treated as payment in year of sale

## Electing Out

Taxpayers may elect to recognize the entire gain in the year of sale by:
1. Not filing [[Form 6252]]
2. Reporting the sale on [[Form 8949]] and/or [[Form 4797]]
3. Making the election by the return due date (including extensions)

Once made, the election can only be revoked with IRS approval.

## Selling Price Reduction

If the selling price is reduced after the original sale:
1. Refigure gross profit using the new selling price
2. Calculate new gross profit percentage for remaining payments
3. Apply new percentage to all future payments

## Common Questions

### Q: Can I use the installment method for selling my home?
**A:** Yes, but first consider the home sale exclusion under Section 121. See [[Pub 523 - Selling Your Home]] for exclusion rules.

### Q: What if the buyer defaults and I repossess the property?
**A:** Special [[Repossession]] rules apply. The treatment differs for personal vs. real property.

### Q: Does the installment method apply to installment payments received before year-end?
**A:** All payments received in the year of sale are part of the initial reporting on [[Form 6252]]. The installment method applies to payments received in subsequent years.
