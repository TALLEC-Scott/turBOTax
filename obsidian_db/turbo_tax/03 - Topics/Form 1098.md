# Form 1098

## Overview

Form 1098 (Mortgage Interest Statement) is an information return provided by your mortgage lender showing the amount of mortgage interest you paid during the year. It's essential for claiming the home mortgage interest deduction.

## When You Receive It

You should receive Form 1098 by **January 31** of each year if you paid:
- **$600 or more** of mortgage interest during the year

The form is also sent to the IRS.

## What's Reported

| Box | Information |
|-----|-------------|
| 1 | Mortgage interest received from you |
| 2 | Outstanding mortgage principal |
| 3 | Mortgage origination date |
| 4 | Refund of overpaid interest |
| 5 | Mortgage insurance premiums |
| 6 | Points paid on purchase of principal residence |

## How to Use It

### Reporting Interest Deduction

On [[Schedule A (Form 1040)]]:
- **Line 8a**: Enter interest from Box 1
- If you have multiple mortgages, combine amounts from all Forms 1098

### Points

- Points shown in Box 6 may be deductible
- See [[Points (Mortgage)]] for deductibility rules

### Refunds

- Box 4 shows refunds of previously deducted interest
- Generally must be included in income

## What If You Don't Receive It

Not receiving Form 1098 doesn't mean you can't deduct your interest:
- Check year-end mortgage statements
- Contact your lender for a copy
- Deduct interest you can document

## Multiple Borrowers

If you and another person shared liability for the mortgage:
- The person who received Form 1098 reports interest on line 8a
- The other person reports on line 8b with an attached explanation

## What If Interest Is Missing

If settlement interest isn't included on Form 1098:
1. Add the settlement interest to the Form 1098 amount
2. Report the total on Schedule A, line 8a
3. Attach a statement explaining the difference
4. Write "See attached" on the dotted line next to line 8a

## Important Notes from Pub 936

### Points on Form 1098

Form 1098 generally includes only points that are fully deductible in the year paid. However:

- May report points that aren't deductible (married filing separately, multiple properties)
- You must verify deductibility using [[Points (Mortgage)]] rules
- Points not on Form 1098 may still be deductible (either in year paid or over loan life)

### Prepaid Interest

If you prepaid interest in 2025 that accrued in full by January 15, 2026, this prepaid interest may be included in Box 1. However:

- You can't deduct the prepaid amount for January 2026 in 2025
- Figure the interest that accrued for 2026 and subtract it from Box 1
- Include the January 2026 interest with other interest you pay for 2026

### Home Equity Loan Interest

Interest showing in Box 1 from a home equity loan, line of credit, or credit card loan secured by the property is **not deductible** if the proceeds were not used to buy, build, or substantially improve a qualified home.

### Government-Paid Interest

The form shouldn't show any interest that was paid for you by a government agency (such as mortgage assistance payments under section 235 of the National Housing Act).

### Refunded Interest in Box 4

If you received a refund of mortgage interest you overpaid in an earlier year, Box 4 will show the refund. See [[Home Mortgage Interest]] for treatment of refunded interest.
