# Chapter 11 Bankruptcy Tax

## Overview

Chapter 11 is reorganization bankruptcy. The debtor typically remains in control as a "debtor-in-possession" (DIP) and acts as the bankruptcy trustee. A separate trustee may be appointed if in the best interest of creditors.

Like [[Chapter 7 Bankruptcy Tax|Chapter 7]], the bankruptcy estate is treated as a **separate taxable entity** for individuals.

## Separate Taxable Entity

For individuals in Chapter 11:

| Entity | Files | Purpose |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Debtor | Form 1040/1040-SR | Individual return |
| Estate (DIP) | Form 1041 | Bankruptcy estate return |

The debtor-in-possession files **both** returns (unlike Chapter 7 where a trustee files the estate return).

## Key Difference from Chapter 7

**Post-petition earnings belong to the estate** under IRC Section 1398(e)(1) and 11 U.S.C. Section 1115.

This means:
- Wages earned after petition date are estate income
- Self-employment income after petition date is estate income
- DIP may be compensated by estate for managing business (reported on debtor's Form 1040)

## Income Allocation

### Estate Income

- Income from estate property
- **Post-petition earnings from debtor's services**
- Income from trade/business conducted before bankruptcy (managed by DIP)

### Debtor Income

- Pre-petition income
- DIP compensation for managing business (reported as miscellaneous income)

## W-2 Reporting and Allocation

When employer issues W-2 reporting all wages for calendar year:

1. **Allocate income** between debtor and estate based on pre/post petition split
2. **Allocate withholding** in same proportion
3. **Attach statement** to both debtor's and estate's returns per IRS Notice 2006-83

### Notice 2006-83 Statement Requirements

- Show allocations of income and income tax withheld
- Describe allocation method used
- List filing date, bankruptcy court, case number, and estate EIN

## Self-Employment Tax

The debtor (not the estate) reports self-employment income on Schedule SE, including post-petition self-employment earnings. The debtor pays SE tax under IRC Section 1401.

## Information Returns (Form 1099)

The DIP/trustee should notify payors required to file information returns (other than W-2) of the estate's EIN so post-petition income is reported to the estate.

**For W-2 employers:** Continue reporting all wages under debtor's name and SSN. The DIP/trustee should NOT provide the estate EIN to employers.

## Conversion or Dismissal

| Event | Tax Consequence |
|-------|-----------------|
| Convert to Chapter 13 | Estate ends; post-conversion income taxed to debtor |
| Convert to Chapter 7 | Estate continues; post-conversion services income taxed to debtor |
| Dismissed | Treated as if no bankruptcy filed; no estate created |

## Short Tax Year Election

Same rules as [[Chapter 7 Bankruptcy Tax|Chapter 7]]—debtor may elect to close tax year day before filing.

## Corporate Chapter 11

### Filing Requirements

No separate taxable entity created. File Form 1120 as usual.

### Tax-Free Reorganizations

IRC Sections 354, 355, and 356 allow tax-free transfers of assets in bankruptcy reorganizations:
- Shareholders exchange stock for acquiring company stock
- Creditors exchange securities for stock of equal principal amount
- Cash/other property received triggers gain recognition (not loss)

### Exemption from Filing

Trustee may request relief from filing if corporation:
- Ceased business operations
- Has no assets or income for the tax year

Submit request to local IRS Insolvency Office with signed statement under penalties of perjury.

## Discharge Rules

### Individuals

Same non-dischargeable taxes as Chapter 7 (eighth priority, late-filed, no return, fraud, evasion).

### Corporations

Broad discharge on plan confirmation, but:
- Secured and priority claims must be satisfied
- Exception for fraud/willful evasion taxes
