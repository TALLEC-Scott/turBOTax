# Gift Tax

## Overview

Federal gift tax applies to lifetime transfers of property from one person (donor) to another (donee) for less than full value. The tax is designed to prevent avoidance of estate tax through lifetime giving.

## What is a Gift

A gift occurs when property, money, or the use of property is transferred without receiving something of equal value in return.

### Examples of Taxable Gifts
- Cash or property transfers
- Interest-free or below-market loans
- Sales below fair market value
- Use of property without adequate rent

## Annual Exclusion

### Amount
Each person can give up to the annual exclusion amount per recipient per year without gift tax:

| Year(s) | Annual Exclusion |
|---------|------------------|
| 2018 - 2021 | $15,000 |
| 2022 | $16,000 |
| 2023 | $17,000 |
| 2024 | $18,000 |
| 2025 | $19,000 |

### Special Rules
- **Non-citizen spouse**: Higher exclusion ($190,000 for 2025)
- **Future interests**: Not eligible for annual exclusion
- **Per donee**: Applies separately to each recipient

### Gift Splitting
Married couples can elect to split gifts:
- Treated as one-half from each spouse
- Doubles effective exclusion per donee
- Both spouses must file Form 709
- Both must be U.S. citizens or residents

## Gifts Not Subject to Tax

### Excluded Transfers
- Gifts to U.S. citizen spouse (unlimited marital deduction)
- Tuition paid directly to educational institution
- Medical expenses paid directly to medical provider
- Gifts to qualified charities (charitable deduction)
- Gifts to political organizations
- Gifts to certain exempt organizations (501(c)(4), (5), (6))

## Gift Tax Return (Form 709)

### When to File
- Gifts exceeding annual exclusion to any one person
- Gift splitting elected
- Future interest gifts
- Gifts of present interests to spouse (certain situations)

### Due Date
- April 15 of year after the gift
- Automatic extension with Form 4868 or Form 8892

### Reporting Requirements
- Report total gifts for calendar year
- Subtract exclusions and deductions
- Calculate tax on taxable gifts

## Applicable Credit

### Credit Amount
- Reduces or eliminates gift tax
- Same credit used for estate tax
- Using credit for gifts reduces credit available at death

### 2025 Credit
- Credit: $5,541,800
- Exempts: $13,990,000 of lifetime gifts

### Example
If you make $100,000 in taxable gifts:
1. Gift tax on $100,000 = approximately $18,000
2. Apply $18,000 of credit
3. Remaining credit for future gifts/estate: $5,523,800

## Unified Credit Concept

Gift and estate taxes are unified:
- Same exclusion amount covers both
- Same credit applies to both
- Cumulative tracking across lifetime and death

## Basis of Gifted Property

Recipient's basis = Donor's adjusted basis (carryover basis)
- Different from inherited property (step-up in basis)
- Gift tax paid increases donee's basis
- Losses are not passed through

## Generation-Skipping Transfer Tax

### Additional Tax
Applies to gifts to skip persons (grandchildren, etc.):
- Same rate as estate tax
- Separate GST exemption available
- Annual exclusion applies before GST

## State Gift Taxes

- Most states do not have separate gift tax
- Connecticut has state gift tax
- Check state requirements
