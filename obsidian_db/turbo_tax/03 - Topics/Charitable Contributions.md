# Charitable Contributions

A charitable contribution is a donation or gift to, or for the use of, a qualified organization. It is voluntary and made without receiving anything of equal value in return.

## Requirements for Deduction

To deduct a charitable contribution, you must:

1. **Itemize deductions** on Schedule A (Form 1040)
2. **Contribute to a qualified organization** - See [[Qualified Organizations]]
3. **Have proper documentation** - Varies by amount and type
4. **Meet timing requirements** - Contribution made by December 31

## Types of Deductible Contributions

### Cash Contributions

**Includes:**
- Cash, checks, electronic transfers
- Credit card charges
- Payroll deductions
- Text message donations
- Gift cards redeemable for cash

**Limit:** 60% of AGI (to 50% limit organizations)

**Documentation Required:**
- Bank record (canceled check, statement)
- Written receipt from organization
- Payroll deduction records + pledge card

### Noncash Contributions

**Property Types:**
- Clothing and household items (must be in good condition)
- Vehicles (cars, boats, airplanes)
- Securities (stocks, bonds)
- Real estate
- Art and collectibles
- Inventory

**Limits Vary:**
- Ordinary income property: Lesser of FMV or basis
- Capital gain property: FMV (30% limit typically applies)

See [[Noncash Contributions]] for detailed rules.

### Out-of-Pocket Expenses for Volunteer Work

**Deductible:**
- Unreimbursed expenses directly related to services
- 14 cents per mile (2025) for charitable driving
- Uniforms not suitable for everyday use
- Travel expenses while away overnight

**Not Deductible:**
- Value of your time or services
- Childcare while volunteering

See [[Volunteer Expense Deductions]].

### Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs)

Direct IRA distribution to qualified organization:
- Must be age 70½ or older
- Up to $108,000 per year
- Not taxable; not also deductible as contribution

See [[Qualified Charitable Distributions]].

## Contributions You Cannot Deduct

| Type | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Contributions to individuals | Must be to qualified organization |
| Contributions to nonqualified organizations | Civic leagues, labor unions, social clubs |
| Value of your time or services | Only out-of-pocket expenses deductible |
| Raffle or lottery tickets | Considered personal expense |
| Tuition payments | Even if to religious school |
| Contributions for lobbying | Political activity not charitable |
| Partial interest in property | With specific exceptions |

## Contribution Limits

Your total charitable deduction cannot exceed certain percentages of AGI:

| Limit | Applies To |
|-------|------------|
| 60% | Cash contributions to 50% limit organizations |
| 50% | Noncash contributions to 50% limit organizations; Qualified conservation contributions |
| 30% | Capital gain property (FMV); Contributions to private foundations |
| 20% | Capital gain property to private foundations |

**Excess contributions** can be carried forward up to 5 years.

See [[Charitable Contribution Limits]] for detailed calculations.

## When to Deduct

### Timing Rules

- **Checks:** Deduct when mailed
- **Credit card:** Deduct when charged
- **Payroll deduction:** Deduct when withheld from paycheck
- **Text message:** Deduct when text sent
- **Stock certificate:** Deduct when mailed or delivered

### Conditional Gifts

Cannot deduct until:
- Condition is virtually certain to occur
- No chance of refund

**Example:** Contribution to build school gym with refund clause - deduct only when refund is impossible.

## Reporting

### Schedule A Lines

| Line | Type |
|------|------|
| 11 | Cash contributions and out-of-pocket expenses |
| 12 | Noncash contributions |
| 13 | Carryover from prior years |
| 14 | Total charitable contributions |

### Form 8283

Required for noncash contributions over $500:
- Section A: Deductions $500-$5,000
- Section B: Deductions over $5,000 (requires qualified appraisal)

See [[Form 8283]].
