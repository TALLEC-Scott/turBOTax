# General Rule

## Overview

The **General Rule** is one of two methods used to figure the tax-free part of each annuity payment. It calculates the tax-free portion based on the ratio of your investment in the contract to the total expected return.

## When to Use the General Rule

Use the General Rule if you receive pension or annuity payments from:

1. **Nonqualified plans**:
   - Private annuities
   - Purchased commercial annuities
   - Nonqualified employee plans

2. **Qualified plans** when:
   - Annuity starting date is before November 19, 1996 (and after July 1, 1986), and you don't qualify for or didn't choose the Simplified Method
   - Annuity starting date is after November 18, 1996, and you are age 75 or over with payments guaranteed for at least 5 years

## The Simplified Method Alternative

For qualified plans, the [[Simplified Method]] is typically used instead. The Simplified Method is covered in [[Pub 575 - Pension and Annuity Income]].

## Computation Steps

1. Figure your [[Investment in the Contract]] (including adjustments for [[Refund Feature]] and death benefit exclusion)
2. Figure your [[Expected Return]]
3. Divide Step 1 by Step 2 to get the [[Exclusion Percentage]]
4. Multiply exclusion percentage by the first regular payment to get the tax-free part
5. Multiply tax-free part by number of payments received during the year
6. Subtract tax-free part from total payment to get taxable amount

## Key Formula

```
Exclusion Percentage = Investment in Contract ÷ Expected Return
Tax-Free Part = Exclusion Percentage × Periodic Payment
```

## Exclusion Limits

- **After 1986**: Total tax-free amount over the years cannot exceed your net cost
- Any unrecovered net cost at death is allowed as an other itemized deduction on the final return

## Examples

**Example 1**: You purchased an annuity with an investment of $10,800. The annuity pays $100/month for life. Your expected return (based on age 65 multiple of 20.0) is $24,000. Your exclusion percentage is 45% ($10,800 ÷ $24,000). Each year, $540 (45% of $1,200) is tax-free.

**Example 2**: Joint and survivor annuity with investment of $62,712 and expected return of $121,200. The exclusion percentage is 51.7%. Annual payments of $6,000 result in $3,102 tax-free each year.
