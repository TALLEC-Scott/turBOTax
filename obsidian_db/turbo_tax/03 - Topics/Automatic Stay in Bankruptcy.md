# Automatic Stay in Bankruptcy

## Overview

The automatic stay arises by operation of law immediately upon filing a bankruptcy petition. It is a court-ordered injunction that suspends most collection activity, providing the debtor protection while the bankruptcy case proceeds.

The automatic stay applies to **all entities**, including governmental units like the IRS.

## What the Stay Prohibits

### Collection Actions

The automatic stay prohibits acts to collect taxes that arose **before** the bankruptcy filing:

| Prohibited Action | Description |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Notices of Federal Tax Lien | Serving new NFTLs |
| Levies | Seizing property |
| Civil actions | Commencing or continuing lawsuits |
| Tax Court cases | Commencing or continuing certain proceedings |

### Duration

The stay continues until:
- Bankruptcy court lifts the stay
- Case is closed or dismissed
- Debtor receives discharge

**Important:** The stay against property of the estate continues as long as property remains in the estate (even after discharge), unless stay is lifted.

## What the Stay Does NOT Prohibit

The automatic stay does **not** prohibit:

| Permitted Action | Description |
|------------------|-------------|
| Tax audits | Determining amount of tax owed |
| Demand for returns | Requiring tax return filings |
| Notice of Deficiency | Issuing statutory notices |
| Assessment | Recording tax liability |
| Notice and demand | Billing for payment |

## Collection Statute Expiration Date (CSED)

Bankruptcy affects the time the IRS can collect tax.

### General Rule

- IRS normally has 10 years from assessment to collect (CSED)
- Bankruptcy **suspends** the CSED during the case
- IRS adds 6 months to CSED after bankruptcy ends

### Calculation

```
CSED suspended from: Petition filing date
CSED resumes: Discharge, dismissal, or case closing
Additional time: + 6 months
```

## Offset of Refunds

### General Rule

The automatic stay **prevents** the IRS from offsetting a refund against a tax liability.

### Exception

The IRS **can** offset a pre-petition income tax refund against a pre-petition income tax liability while the stay is in effect.

The IRS may freeze a refund until the stay is lifted if offset is not permitted.

## Tax Court Proceedings

### Stay of Proceedings

The automatic stay stops commencement or continuation of Tax Court cases regarding:
- **Individual cases:** Tax liabilities for periods ending before order for relief
- **Corporate cases:** Liabilities for periods the bankruptcy court may determine

### Bankruptcy Court Authority

The bankruptcy court has sole authority to determine whether tax issues are decided by:
- Bankruptcy court, or
- Tax Court

The bankruptcy court can lift the stay to allow Tax Court proceedings.

### Suspension of Filing Period

For Statutory Notices of Deficiency issued before bankruptcy:
- 90-day period for filing Tax Court petition is **suspended**
- Suspension lasts while stay prevents filing
- Period resumes 60 days after stay ends

**Exception:** The 90-day period for filing after Notice of Determination in innocent spouse cases is **not suspended** by bankruptcy filing. Debtor must ask bankruptcy court to lift stay.

## Prompt Determination Requests

The trustee may request prompt determination of estate tax liability by:
1. Filing tax return with duplicate written request
2. Sending to IRS Centralized Insolvency Operation

**IRS Response Time:**
- 60 days to notify if return selected for examination
- 180 days to complete examination and notify of tax due

If IRS doesn't meet deadlines, estate is discharged upon payment of tax shown on return (unless fraud or material misrepresentation).

## Refund Requests

Trustee may request refund by filing:
- Form 1040-X (individual income tax)
- Form 1120-X (corporate income tax)
- Form 843 (other taxes)
- Form 8849 or 720-X (excise taxes)

Mark return "Request for Prompt Refund" and attach statement under Bankruptcy Code Section 505(a).

IRS must respond within 120 days. If disagreement or no response, trustee may seek bankruptcy court determination.
