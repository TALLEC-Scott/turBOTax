# Lump-Sum Election

## Overview

The lump-sum election method allows taxpayers who receive Social Security or Railroad Retirement benefits for prior years to potentially reduce their taxable benefits by using the prior year's income to calculate the taxable portion, rather than including the entire amount in the current year's income.

## When to Use

You may benefit from the lump-sum election if you received a lump-sum payment in 2025 that includes benefits for earlier years AND your income in the earlier year(s) was lower than your 2025 income.

### Indicators on Your Benefit Statement

- **Form SSA-1099**: Asterisk (*) after Box 3 amount indicates benefits received for earlier years
- **Form RRB-1099**: Boxes 7, 8, or 9 show amounts paid for 2024, 2023, or earlier years

## How the Election Works

### Regular Method (Default)
- Include full lump-sum amount in current year income
- Calculate taxability using current year's income

### Lump-Sum Election Method
- Calculate taxable portion using earlier year's income
- May result in lower taxable benefits if prior year income was lower
- Must complete worksheets in [[Pub 915 - Social Security and Equivalent Railroad Retirement Benefits]]

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Complete Worksheet 1 (Regular Method)
Figure your taxable benefits for 2025 under the regular method.

### 2. Complete Worksheet 2 or 3 (Lump-Sum Portion)
- **Worksheet 2**: For lump-sum payments for years after 1993
- **Worksheet 3**: For lump-sum payments for years before 1994

These worksheets figure the additional taxable benefits from the lump-sum payment using the earlier year's income.

### 3. Complete Worksheet 4 (Compare Methods)
Compare the results:
- Line 21: Taxable benefits under lump-sum election method
- Worksheet 1, Line 19: Taxable benefits under regular method

### 4. Make the Election (If Beneficial)

If Worksheet 4, Line 21 is smaller than Worksheet 1, Line 19:
1. Check the box on **Form 1040 or 1040-SR, Line 6c**
2. Enter total benefits on Line 6a
3. Enter the lower taxable amount from Worksheet 4, Line 21 on Line 6b

## Example

Terry Jackson received a lump-sum payment of $6,000 in 2025:
- $2,000 for 2024
- $4,000 for 2025
- Plus $5,000 in regular 2025 benefits

Terry's income was lower in 2024 ($23,000) than in 2025 ($25,500 + pension of $18,000). By using the lump-sum election method, Terry reduced taxable benefits from $4,230 to $2,500.

## Key Rules

### Do NOT File Amended Return
- The lump-sum election is made on your current year return only
- Do NOT file an amended return for the earlier year
- Complete a separate Worksheet 2 or 3 for each earlier year involved

### Worksheets Required
- Must use worksheets from [[Pub 915 - Social Security and Equivalent Railroad Retirement Benefits]]
- Cannot use the worksheet in Form 1040 instructions
- Keep worksheets for your records

### Prior Year Information Needed
To complete the worksheets, you need from the earlier year:
- Adjusted gross income (AGI)
- Taxable benefits reported
- Tax-exempt interest received
- Any exclusions claimed (foreign earned income, etc.)

## Situations Requiring Pub 915 Worksheets

You MUST use Pub 915 worksheets (not Form 1040 worksheet) if:
1. You received a lump-sum payment for an earlier year
2. You exclude qualified savings bond interest
3. You exclude adoption benefits
4. You exclude foreign earned income or housing
5. You exclude income from Puerto Rico or American Samoa

## Reporting on Tax Return

| Form 1040 Line | What to Enter |
|----------------|---------------|

| 6b | Taxable amount from Worksheet 4, Line 21 |

## Common Questions

### Q: Should I always use the lump-sum election?
**A:** Not necessarily. Use the worksheets to compare both methods. Only elect the lump-sum method if it results in lower taxable benefits.

### Q: What if I received lump-sum payments for multiple years?
**A:** Complete a separate Worksheet 2 or 3 for each earlier year, then combine the results on Worksheet 4.

### Q: Do I need to know my 2010 income if I received benefits for 2010?
**A:** Yes, you need your AGI, taxable benefits, and other income information from any year for which you received a lump-sum payment.
