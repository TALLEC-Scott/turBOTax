# Tax Forgiveness for Deceased Servicemembers

## Overview

Tax liability may be forgiven when a member of the U.S. Armed Forces dies while serving in a combat zone, from combat zone injuries, or from wounds incurred in a terrorist or military action.

## When Tax Forgiveness Applies

Tax forgiveness applies when a servicemember dies:
1. **While in active service in a combat zone**
2. **From wounds, disease, or injury received in a combat zone**
3. **From wounds or injury incurred in a terrorist or military action**

## What Tax Forgiveness Means

| Situation | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| Tax not yet paid | May not have to be paid |
| Tax already paid | Payment may be refunded |

## Combat Zone Related Forgiveness

### Eligibility
- Member of the U.S. Armed Forces at death
- Died while in active service in a combat zone, OR
- Died from wounds, disease, or injury incurred in active service in a combat zone

### Years Covered
- The tax year death occurred
- Any earlier tax year ending on or after the first day the member served in the combat zone
- Any unpaid taxes for years ending before the member began combat zone service

### Service Outside Combat Zone
Rules also apply if serving outside the combat zone when the service:
- Was in direct support of military operations in the zone
- Qualified the member for hostile fire/imminent danger pay

### Missing Status
For members in missing status (MIA or POW):
- Date of death = date name is removed from missing status
- Applies even if death actually occurred earlier

## Terrorist or Military Action Related Forgiveness

### Eligibility
- Member of the U.S. Armed Forces at death
- Died from wounds or injury incurred while in the Armed Forces in a terrorist or military action

### Years Covered
- The tax year death occurred
- Any earlier tax year in the period beginning with the year before the wounds or injury occurred

### Example
Army Private John Kane died in 2025 of wounds incurred in a terrorist attack in 2024. His income tax liability is forgiven for tax years 2023 through 2025.

## How to Claim Tax Forgiveness

### Step 1: File the Proper Form
| Situation | Form to File |
|-----------|--------------|
| No return filed for tax year | Paper Form 1040 or 1040-SR (with Form W-2) |
| Return already filed | Paper Form 1040-X (separate form for each year) |

### Step 2: Identify the Return
Write one of the following on page 1 in bold letters:

| Conflict | Write on Return |
|----------|-----------------|
| Iraq | "Iraqi Freedom-KIA" |
| Afghanistan | "Enduring Freedom-KIA" |
| Kosovo | "Kosovo Operation-KIA" |
| Persian Gulf | "Desert Storm-KIA" |
| Former Yugoslavia | "Former Yugoslavia-KIA" |
| Terrorist action | "KITA" |

Also write the phrase on the total tax line.

### Step 3: Include Computation
Attach a statement showing:
- Tax liability before forgiveness
- Amount to be forgiven

**Joint return computation:**
1. Figure tax if decedent filed separately
2. Figure tax if spouse filed separately
3. Multiply joint tax by: (1) ÷ ((1) + (2))

**Community property state:** Spouse may get refund of taxes paid on their share of military pay.

### Step 4: Provide Form 1310 (If Required)
Form 1310 is NOT required if:
- Surviving spouse filing original/amended joint return, OR
- Personal representative files original Form 1040 with court certificate attached

### Step 5: Provide Death Certification
| Personnel Type | Required Documentation |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Military/DoD civilian | DD Form 1300 (Report of Casualty) |
| Other agencies killed overseas | Letter from Director General of Foreign Service |

## Who Can File a Claim

- Surviving spouse
- Personal representative (executor, administrator, or anyone in charge of decedent's assets)

## Filing Deadline

| Situation | Deadline |
|-----------|----------|
| General rule | 3 years from filing or 2 years from payment (whichever is later) |
| Return filed before due date | Treated as filed on due date (usually April 15) |
| Combat zone death | Extended using combat zone extension rules |
