# Bad Debts

## Overview

A bad debt is an amount owed to you that becomes uncollectible. Per [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]], there are two types of bad debts with different tax treatments:

1. **Business Bad Debts** - Deductible as business expenses on [[Schedule C (Form 1040)]]
2. **Nonbusiness Bad Debts** - Treated as short-term capital losses on Schedule D

## Business Bad Debts

### Definition
A business bad debt is a loss from a debt that was:
1. Created or acquired in your business, OR
2. Closely related to your business when it became partly or totally worthless

**Closely Related Test:** Your primary motive for incurring the debt must be a business reason.

### Common Sources
- Credit sales to customers
- Loans to clients, suppliers, employees, or distributors
- Business loan guarantees

### Deductibility Requirements
You can only deduct a bad debt for:
- Amounts previously included in income (accrual method), OR
- Money actually loaned (cash method)

**Accrual Method:** Deduct when account becomes uncollectible
**Cash Method:** Cannot deduct unpaid receivables (never included in income)

## Types of Business Bad Debts

### Accounts and Notes Receivable
- Credit sales to customers
- Promissory notes from business transactions

### Loans to Clients and Suppliers
- Business-motivated loans
- Must have attempted collection

### Debts from Former Business
- If you sell your business but retain receivables
- Still treated as business debts

### Debt Acquired from Decedent
- Character determined same as debt acquired in business purchase
- Business bad debt if closely related to decedent's trade or business

### Business Loan Guarantee
Deductible as business bad debt if:
1. Made in course of your trade or business
2. Had legal duty to pay
3. Made guarantee before debt became worthless
4. Received reasonable consideration

### Insolvent Partner's Share
If a partnership dissolves and one partner is insolvent:
- Other partners may pay more than their share
- Excess payment = business bad debt deduction

## When Debt Becomes Worthless

A debt is worthless when there is no chance of collection:
- May occur before the due date
- Must demonstrate reasonable collection efforts

**No particular form required** for claiming deduction, but maintain documentation.

### Property Received for Debt
- Reduce debt by FMV of property received
- Property's basis = FMV
- Remaining debt may be deducted as worthless

## Methods of Claiming Deduction

### Specific Charge-Off Method (General Rule)

**Partly Worthless:**
- Deduct amount charged off on books during year
- Limited to amount written off

**Totally Worthless:**
- Deduct entire amount in year debt becomes worthless
- No charge-off required, but recommended

### Nonaccrual-Experience Method

May use this method if:
- You perform services
- Use accrual method
- Meet certain requirements

This method prevents accrual of amounts not expected to be collected.

## Recovery of Bad Debts

If you recover a bad debt after claiming a deduction:
1. Include recovery in gross income
2. Limited to amount that reduced your tax

**NOL Consideration:** If bad debt deduction increased an NOL carryover, treat as having reduced tax.

## Nonbusiness Bad Debts

### Definition
All bad debts that are NOT business bad debts:
- Personal loans to friends/family
- Investment loans not connected with business

### Tax Treatment
- Treated as short-term capital losses
- Report on Form 8949 and Schedule D
- Subject to capital loss limitations ($3,000 annual deduction against ordinary income)

### Requirements
- Must be a bona fide debtor-creditor relationship
- Must have actual basis in the debt (not gift)
- Must become totally worthless (not partly)

## Debts NOT Deductible as Bad Debts

Per [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]]:
- Unpaid wages (if cash method)
- Unpaid rents (if cash method)
- Loans treated as capital contributions
- Political party debts (special rules apply)

## Reporting

### Business Bad Debts
- Schedule C, Line 10 (if related to commissions/fees)
- Schedule C, Line 27a (other deductions)

### Nonbusiness Bad Debts
- Form 8949
- Schedule D (Form 1040)

## Common Questions

### Q: I use cash method. Can I deduct unpaid invoices?
**A:** No. Cash method taxpayers never included unpaid amounts in income, so there is no deduction when they become uncollectible.

### Q: How do I prove a debt is worthless?
**A:** Show that you took reasonable steps to collect and were unable to do so. Documentation of collection attempts is helpful.

### Q: Can I deduct a loan to a friend?
**A:** It would be a nonbusiness bad debt (short-term capital loss), not a business deduction, unless the loan had a business purpose.
