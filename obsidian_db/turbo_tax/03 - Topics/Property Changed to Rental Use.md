# Property Changed to Rental Use

## Overview

When you convert personal property (such as your main home) to rental use, special rules determine your basis for depreciation and how to handle expenses during the year of conversion.

## Basis for Depreciation

The basis for depreciation is the **lesser of**:

1. **Fair Market Value (FMV)** on the date of conversion, OR
2. **Adjusted Basis** on the date of conversion

### Why the Lesser Rule?
This prevents you from claiming depreciation on any decline in value that occurred while the property was personal use. Losses on personal-use property are not deductible.

### Example Calculation

**Facts**:
- Original cost of house: $140,000
- Improvements added: $28,000
- Casualty loss deducted: ($3,500)
- Energy credit claimed: ($500)
- **Adjusted basis**: $164,000

**FMV at conversion**:
- House: $147,000
- Land: $21,000
- Total: $168,000

**Basis for depreciation**: $147,000 (FMV is less than adjusted basis)

## Dividing Expenses in Year of Conversion

When converting mid-year, divide expenses between personal and rental periods:

### Prorated Expenses
- Real estate taxes
- Mortgage interest
- Insurance premiums

**Method**: Divide by 12 months, allocate based on months of each use.

### Monthly Expenses
- Utilities
- Other expenses billed monthly
- Deduct only amounts paid during rental period

### Depreciation Start Date
Treat property as **placed in service on the conversion date**. Use mid-month convention for residential rental property.

## Example: Year of Conversion

**Facts**:
- Moved out in May, started renting June 1
- Annual mortgage interest: $1,800
- Annual insurance: $100
- Annual real estate taxes: $1,200
- Repairs (after renting): $297

**Calculations**:
- Rental mortgage interest: $1,800 × 11/12 = $1,650
- Rental insurance: $100 × 11/12 = $92
- Rental taxes: $1,200 × 11/12 = $1,100
- Repairs: $297 (all deductible)
- Depreciation: Based on June 1 placed-in-service date

## What You CAN Deduct During Personal Period

On Schedule A (if you itemize):
- Mortgage interest for personal period
- Real estate taxes for personal period

On Schedule E:
- All expenses for rental period
- Depreciation starting from conversion date

## What You CAN'T Deduct

- Insurance for personal period (personal expense)
- Depreciation for personal period
- Repairs made during personal period (unless related to casualty)

## Records to Keep

Maintain separate records of:
- Date of conversion
- FMV at conversion (appraisal helpful)
- Adjusted basis calculations
- Improvements made before and after conversion
- Allocation of expenses by period

## Land vs. Building

Remember to:
- Separate land value from building value
- Only depreciate the building portion
- Land basis carries over at full amount (no depreciation)

## Sale of Former Main Home

When selling property previously used as a main home:

### Gain Calculation
- Use adjusted basis including depreciation claimed
- "Unrecaptured depreciation" (Section 1250 gain) taxed at 25% maximum

### Home Sale Exclusion
May qualify for exclusion if:
- Lived in home 2 of last 5 years before sale
- Up to $250,000 ($500,000 MFJ) gain may be excluded
- **Cannot exclude** gain attributable to depreciation

See [[Pub 523 - Selling Your Home]] for details.
