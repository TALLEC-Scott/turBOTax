# Exempt Individuals

## Overview

An "exempt individual" is someone whose days of presence in the United States do not count toward the substantial presence test. **Important:** This does NOT mean exempt from U.S. tax—it only affects residency determination.

## Categories of Exempt Individuals

| Category | Visa Types | Conditions |
|----------|------------|------------|
| Foreign government-related individuals | A, G (not A-3, G-5) | Full-time diplomatic/consular status |
| Teachers and trainees | J, Q | Temporarily present, complying with visa |
| Students | F, J, M, Q | Temporarily present, complying with visa |
| Professional athletes | N/A | Competing in charitable sports event |

## Foreign Government-Related Individuals

### Who Qualifies
- Full-time employees of international organizations
- Individuals with full-time diplomatic status
- Immediate family members (if visa derived from exempt individual)

### Immediate Family Definition
- Spouse
- Unmarried children under 21
- Must reside in exempt individual's household
- Visa derived from exempt individual's status

### Household Staff Exception
A-3 and G-5 visa holders (personal employees, attendants, domestic workers) are NOT exempt individuals and must count all days of presence.

## Teachers and Trainees

### Eligibility
- Temporarily present in U.S. under J or Q visa
- Substantially complying with visa requirements

### Time Limitation
You are NOT an exempt individual for 2025 if you were exempt as a teacher, trainee, or student for any part of **2 of the 6 preceding calendar years**.

### Exception (All Must Apply)
You remain exempt if:
1. Exempt 3 or fewer of the 6 preceding years
2. All compensation paid by foreign employer in 2025
3. Present as teacher/trainee in a prior year
4. Foreign employer paid all compensation in prior years

### Example
Carla was exempt as a teacher for the previous 2 years but compensation was not paid by a foreign employer. Carla is NOT exempt for the current year because she was exempt for at least 2 of the past 6 years without meeting the exception conditions.

## Students

### Eligibility
- Temporarily present in U.S. under F, J, M, or Q visa
- Substantially complying with visa requirements

### Time Limitation
You are NOT an exempt individual for 2025 if you have been exempt as a teacher, trainee, or student for **more than 5 calendar years**.

### Exception (Both Must Apply)
You remain exempt if:
1. You do not intend to reside permanently in the U.S.
2. You have substantially complied with visa requirements

### Intent Factors
Factors indicating intent to reside permanently:
- Applied for permanent residence
- Taken steps to change status to lawful permanent resident
- Maintain closer connection to U.S. than foreign country

### Immediate Family
Immediate family members of exempt students are also exempt individuals.

## Professional Athletes

### Eligibility
Temporarily present in U.S. to compete in a **charitable sports event**.

### Charitable Sports Event Requirements
- Main purpose benefits qualified charitable organization
- Entire net proceeds go to charity
- Volunteers perform substantially all work

### Days Excluded
Only days actually **competing** are excluded—not practice days, promotional activities, or travel days.

## Form 8843 Filing Requirement

You must file [[Form 8843]] if excluding days as:

- Teacher or trainee on J or Q visa
- Student on F, J, M, or Q visa
- Professional athlete in charitable event
- Unable to leave due to medical condition

### Filing Instructions

**With Tax Return:** Attach Form 8843 to your income tax return.

**Without Tax Return:** If not required to file a return, mail Form 8843 to:
```
Department of the Treasury
Internal Revenue Service Center
Austin, TX 73301-0215
```

**Due Date:** Same as Form 1040-NR (April 15 or June 15, depending on wages).

### Failure to File
If you fail to timely file Form 8843, you cannot exclude the days of presence, unless you show by clear and convincing evidence that you took reasonable actions to become aware of the filing requirements and significant steps to comply.

## Days Excluded for Medical Condition

Days unable to leave the U.S. due to a medical condition that arose while in the U.S. are excluded from the substantial presence test.

### Requirements
- Intended to leave the U.S. on the excluded days
- Medical condition arose while in the U.S.
- File Form 8843

### Cannot Exclude If
- Initially prevented, then able to leave but remained beyond reasonable time
- Returned to U.S. for treatment of prior condition
- Condition existed before arrival and you were aware of it
