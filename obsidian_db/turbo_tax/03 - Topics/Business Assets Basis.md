# Business Assets Basis

## Overview

Business assets are property used in the conduct of a trade or business. The basis of business property is usually its actual cost, but special rules apply when property is constructed, produced, or acquired as part of a business purchase.

## Determining Basis

### Purchased Property
Basis is the actual cost to you, including:
- Purchase price
- Sales tax
- Installation and testing
- Freight/delivery charges
- Legal and accounting fees (when capitalized)

### Constructed or Produced Property
Must capitalize all costs as basis, including:
- Direct costs (materials, labor)
- Indirect costs allocable to production
- Costs under uniform capitalization rules

## Uniform Capitalization Rules

The uniform capitalization rules (Section 263A) specify costs you must add to basis.

### Activities Subject to Rules
- Producing real or tangible personal property for use in business
- Producing real or tangible personal property for sale to customers
- Acquiring property for resale

### What "Produce" Means
Construct, build, install, manufacture, develop, improve, create, raise, or grow property.

### Exception for Small Businesses (2025)
Not subject to uniform capitalization rules if:
- Average annual gross receipts ≤ $31 million for 3 preceding tax years, AND
- Business isn't a tax shelter

### Other Exceptions
- Property not used in trade, business, or profit-seeking activity
- Research and experimental expenses under Section 174/174A
- Qualified creative expenses (freelance writers, photographers, artists)
- Property produced under long-term contracts (except certain home construction)

## Intangible Assets

### Types of Intangibles
- Goodwill
- Patents
- Copyrights
- Trademarks
- Trade names
- Franchises

### Patents
Basis = Cost of development:
- Research and experimental expenditures
- Drawings and working models
- Attorneys' and governmental fees

**Note:** Can't include research expenses deducted as current expenses.

### Copyrights
Basis = Cost of obtaining copyright + fees + clerical assistance + cost of plates

**Don't include:** Value of author's unpaid time

## Allocating Basis When Buying a Business

When you acquire a trade or business, allocate consideration to assets in this order:

1. Cash and general deposit accounts (reduce consideration)
2. Certificates of deposit, U.S. government securities, foreign currency, actively traded personal property
3. Accounts receivable, debt instruments, mark-to-market assets
4. Inventory and property held for sale
5. All other assets except Section 197 intangibles, goodwill, and going concern value
6. Section 197 intangibles (except goodwill and going concern value)
7. Goodwill and going concern value

**Reporting:** Both buyer and seller must file Form 8594 to report allocation.

## Section 197 Intangibles

Amortizable over 15 years:
- Goodwill
- Going concern value
- Workforce in place
- Information base
- Patents and copyrights
- Franchises and trademarks
- Customer-based intangibles
