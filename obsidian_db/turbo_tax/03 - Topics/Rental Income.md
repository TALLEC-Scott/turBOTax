# Rental Income

## Overview

Rental income is any payment received for the use or occupation of property. Most rental income must be included in gross income, but there are specific rules about when and how to report different types of rental payments.

## Types of Rental Income

### Advance Rent
Amounts received before the period they cover. Include in rental income in the year received regardless of the period covered or accounting method used.

**Example**: A 10-year lease with first year's rent ($9,600) and last year's rent ($9,600) paid in 2025. Report $19,200 in 2025 income.

### Lease Cancellation Payments
If a tenant pays to cancel a lease, the amount received is rent. Include in income in the year received.

### Expenses Paid by Tenant
Payments made by tenants for landlord expenses are rental income. You can also deduct these expenses if they are deductible rental expenses.

**Examples**:
- Tenant pays water/sewage bill and deducts from rent
- Tenant pays for repairs and deducts from rent

### Property or Services
Include the fair market value (FMV) of property or services received as rent.

**Example**: A painter tenant offers to paint rental property instead of 2 months' rent. Include the rent value in income and deduct same amount as rental expense.

### Security Deposits
- **Don't include** in income when received if you plan to return it
- **Include** in income in the year you keep part or all of it
- If a "security deposit" is actually final rent payment, treat as advance rent

### Lease with Option to Buy
Payments under a rental agreement with purchase option are generally rental income. Payments after the sale date are part of the selling price.

## When to Report

### Cash Method (Most Individual Taxpayers)
Report income in the year you actually or constructively receive it, regardless of when earned.

### Accrual Method
Report income when earned, deduct expenses when incurred.

## Special Situations

### Rental Less Than 15 Days
If you rent property used as your home for less than 15 days during the tax year:
- **Don't include** rent in income
- Expenses are **not** considered rental expenses
- See [[Vacation Home Rules]] for details

### Part Interest
Report your proportional share of rental income based on ownership percentage.
