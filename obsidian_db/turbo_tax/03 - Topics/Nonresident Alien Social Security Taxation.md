# Nonresident Alien Social Security Taxation

## Overview

Nonresident aliens (NRAs) who receive Social Security or Railroad Retirement benefits are subject to special tax rules. Generally, 85% of benefits are taxed at a 30% rate, unless a tax treaty provides an exemption or reduced rate.

## Who Is a Nonresident Alien

A nonresident alien is an individual who is NOT:
- A U.S. citizen
- A U.S. resident alien (green card holder or meets substantial presence test)

For more details on determining residency status, see [[Pub 519 - U.S. Tax Guide for Aliens]].

## Default Taxation Rule

### Standard Treatment
- **85%** of benefits are subject to U.S. tax
- Tax rate: **30%** (default withholding rate)
- Effective tax on benefits: 25.5% of gross benefits (85% × 30%)

### Forms Received
| Form | Issued By |
|------|-----------|
| SSA-1042S | Social Security Administration |
| RRB-1042S | Railroad Retirement Board |

These forms show the benefit amount, tax rate, and tax withheld.

## Treaty Country Exemptions

### Full Exemption from U.S. Tax

Residents of these countries are **exempt** from U.S. tax on Social Security/Railroad Retirement benefits:

| Country | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Canada | - |
| Germany | - |
| Egypt | - |
| Ireland | - |
| Italy | Must also be a citizen of Italy |
| Israel | - |
| Japan | - |
| Romania | - |
| United Kingdom | - |

### India Treaty (Special Rule)
Benefits paid to individuals who are BOTH residents AND nationals of India are exempt from U.S. tax IF the benefits are for services performed for:
- The United States government
- U.S. subdivisions
- Local government authorities

### Switzerland (Reduced Rate)
Residents of Switzerland are taxed at **15%** instead of 30%.

## How to Claim Treaty Benefits

### Social Security Benefits (SSA)
- SSA automatically applies treaty benefits
- No special form required
- Ensure SSA has correct country of residence

### Railroad Retirement Benefits (RRB)
You must file **Form RRB-1001** (Nonresident Questionnaire) with the Railroad Retirement Board to:
- Provide citizenship and residency information
- Claim treaty exemption
- Prevent 30% withholding

**If you don't file Form RRB-1001:**
- RRB will consider you a nonresident alien
- 30% tax will be withheld
- You must file Form 1040-NR to claim refund

## U.S. Citizens Residing Abroad

U.S. citizens who are residents of certain countries are **exempt** from U.S. tax on their benefits:

| Country | Country |
|---------|---------|
| Canada | Italy |
| Germany | Israel |
| Egypt | Romania |
| Ireland | United Kingdom |

**Note**: For Italy, you must ALSO be a citizen of Italy.

### SSA vs. RRB Treatment

**Social Security (SSA):**
- SSA does NOT withhold U.S. tax from benefits paid to U.S. citizens
- Treaty exemption applies automatically

**Railroad Retirement (RRB):**
- RRB WILL withhold tax unless you file Form RRB-1001
- Must file Form RRB-1001 to provide citizenship/residency information
- Otherwise, RRB treats you as a nonresident alien

## Lawful Permanent Residents (Green Card Holders)

### Tax Treatment
- Considered resident aliens for tax purposes
- Social Security benefits NOT subject to 30% withholding
- Benefits taxed under regular Social Security rules (see [[Social Security Benefits Taxation]])

### If Tax Withheld in Error
If tax was withheld because you have a foreign address:

1. **Same calendar year**: SSA can refund erroneously withheld tax
2. **After year ends**: File Form 1040 or 1040-SR with Austin, TX service center

**Required attachments:**
- Copy of Form SSA-1042S
- Copy of green card (unless bona fide resident of American Samoa)
- Signed declaration stating:
  - You are a U.S. lawful permanent resident
  - Green card has not been revoked or abandoned
  - You are filing as a resident alien reporting worldwide income
  - You have not claimed treaty benefits as a nonresident alien

### Filing Address
Internal Revenue Service Center
Austin, TX 73301

## Canadian and German Social Security Benefits

Under tax treaties with Canada and Germany, social security benefits paid by those countries to U.S. residents are treated as if paid under U.S. Social Security legislation.

### How to Report
- Include on Worksheet 1, Line 1 in [[Pub 915 - Social Security and Equivalent Railroad Retirement Benefits]]
- Follow same rules as U.S. Social Security benefits

## Reporting on Tax Return

### Form 1040-NR
Nonresident aliens file Form 1040-NR:
- Report benefits on appropriate line
- Claim treaty exemption if applicable
- Report tax withheld from SSA-1042S or RRB-1042S

### Multiple Forms
If you received multiple forms (different rates or countries):
- Add all amounts to determine totals
- Each form shows withholding at the rate for that period

## Common Questions

### Q: I'm a nonresident alien. Do I need to file a U.S. tax return?
**A:** Generally, yes, if you have U.S. source income including Social Security benefits. File Form 1040-NR to claim treaty benefits or refunds of excess withholding.

### Q: What if my country isn't listed in the treaty exemptions?
**A:** You'll be subject to the standard 30% withholding on 85% of your benefits. This is generally your final tax obligation, but you may still need to file Form 1040-NR.

### Q: I'm a U.S. citizen living abroad. Will my benefits be taxed?
**A:** If you reside in a treaty country (listed above), your benefits are exempt from U.S. tax. For Railroad Retirement, file Form RRB-1001 to prevent withholding.
