# Investment Income for EIC

To qualify for the [[Earned Income Credit|Earned Income Credit (EIC)]], your investment income must be $11,950 or less for tax year 2025.

## 2025 Investment Income Limit

**Maximum: $11,950**

If your investment income exceeds this limit, you cannot claim the EIC regardless of other eligibility factors.

## What Counts as Investment Income

### Interest and Dividends
- Taxable interest (Form 1040, line 2b)
- Tax-exempt interest (Form 1040, line 2a) plus Form 8814, line 1b
- Ordinary dividends (Form 1040, line 3b)
- Amount from Form 8814 used to report child's interest and dividends

### Capital Gains
- Capital gain net income (Form 1040, line 7a)
- Gain from Form 4797, line 7 (or line 9 if applicable)

### Royalties and Rental Income
- Royalty income from Schedule E, line 23b
- Income from rental of personal property (Schedule 1, line 8l)
- **Minus** related expenses (Schedule E, line 20; Schedule 1, line 24b)

### Passive Activities
- Net income from passive activities
- Examples: Schedule E, lines 26, 29a, 34a, 40; Form 4797, line 10

## What Does NOT Count as Investment Income

- Wages, salaries, tips ([[EIC Earned Income Rules|earned income]])
- Self-employment income
- Interest from trade or business
- Royalties from self-employment

## Calculating Your Investment Income

Use **Worksheet 1** in [[Pub 596 - Earned Income Credit|Publication 596]] to calculate investment income for EIC purposes.

### Quick Calculation Steps
1. Add interest income (taxable + tax-exempt)
2. Add dividend income
3. Add capital gains
4. Add net royalty and rental income (income minus expenses)
5. Add passive activity income
6. **Total** must be $11,950 or less

## Special Situations

### Alaska Permanent Fund Dividends
If using Form 8814 to report a child's income that includes Alaska Permanent Fund dividends, use Worksheet 2 in Publication 596 to calculate the proper allocation.

### Passive Activity Considerations
When determining passive activity income:
- Don't include royalty income already counted as earned income
- Check Schedule E instructions to determine if income is passive
- Mark "NPA" next to line 26 for non-passive rental real estate income

## Why This Matters

Investment income over $11,950 **completely disqualifies** you from the EIC, even if:
- Your earned income is low
- You have qualifying children
- You meet all other requirements

## Planning Considerations

If close to the investment income limit:
- Consider timing of investment sales
- Review tax-exempt interest reporting
- Calculate passive activity income carefully
