# Cash Wages for Household Employees

## Overview

Cash wages are the starting point for determining whether Social Security, Medicare, and FUTA taxes apply to household employees. Understanding what counts as cash wages is essential for proper tax compliance.

## Definition of Cash Wages

### Cash Wages Include:
- Payments by check or money order
- Electronic fund transfers
- Actual cash payments
- Cash given in lieu of food, lodging, or clothing

### Cash Wages Do NOT Include:
- Value of food, lodging, or clothing provided
- Qualified transit passes (up to monthly limits)
- Qualified parking reimbursements (up to monthly limits)
- Other noncash items

## 2026 Thresholds

| Tax | Threshold | What's Taxed |
|-----|-----------|--------------|
| Social Security & Medicare | $3,000+ to one employee | All cash wages to that employee |
| FUTA | $1,000+ total in any quarter | All cash wages to all household employees |

## Social Security and Medicare Wages

### If You Pay $3,000 or More to One Employee:
- ALL cash wages paid to that employee in 2026 count as Social Security and Medicare wages
- This includes wages paid before the $3,000 threshold was reached
- Social Security tax applies up to $184,500 wage base
- Medicare tax applies to ALL wages (no limit)

### If You Pay Less Than $3,000:
- None of the wages are Social Security or Medicare wages
- No FICA tax is due

## Noncash Wages

Noncash wages (food, lodging, clothing) are:
- NOT subject to Social Security or Medicare taxes
- Subject to federal income tax (unless specific exclusion applies)
- Reported in Box 1 of Form W-2 with cash wages
- NOT shown in Boxes 3 or 5 of Form W-2

## Transportation Benefits (Excluded from Cash Wages)

### 2026 Monthly Limits

| Benefit | Monthly Exclusion |
|---------|-------------------|
| Qualified parking | $340 |
| Commuter highway vehicle/transit passes | $340 |

### Qualified Parking
- Parking at or near YOUR home
- Parking at or near a location from which employee commutes to your home
- Does NOT include parking at employee's home

### Reporting
- Amounts up to limits: Not included in cash wages
- Amounts over limits: Include as wages subject to FICA

## State Disability Payments

Certain state disability plan payments received by household employees may be treated as Social Security and Medicare wages. Check:
- Instructions for Schedule H (Form 1040)
- Notice issued by the state

## Wages Not Counted

Even if wages are $3,000 or more, don't count wages paid to:

| Worker | Exception |
|--------|-----------|
| Your spouse | None - always exempt |
| Your child under 21 | None - always exempt |
| Your parent | Exempt unless specific childcare conditions apply |
| Employee under 18 | Exempt unless household work is principal occupation |

### Parent Exception Details
Count wages paid to your parent if BOTH conditions apply:
1. Parent cares for your child who is:
   - Under age 18, OR
   - Has a physical/mental condition requiring adult care for 4+ continuous weeks
2. Your marital status is:
   - Divorced and not remarried, OR
   - Widowed, OR
   - Living with a disabled spouse who can't care for the child

### Under-18 Exception Details
Count wages if:
- Employee is under 18 at any time during 2026, AND
- Providing household services is their principal occupation
- Student status = NOT principal occupation

## Recording Requirements

On each payday, record:
- Date and amount of cash wages
- Any noncash wages
- Employee Social Security tax withheld (or paid by employer)
- Employee Medicare tax withheld (or paid by employer)
- Federal income tax withheld
- State employment taxes withheld
