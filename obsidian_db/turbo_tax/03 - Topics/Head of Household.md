# Head of Household

Head of Household (HOH) is a filing status for unmarried taxpayers who pay more than half the cost of keeping up a home for a qualifying person. It offers a higher standard deduction and lower tax rates than filing as Single.

## 2025 Standard Deduction

**$23,625** (vs. $15,750 for Single)

## Requirements

To file as Head of Household, you must meet ALL of the following:

### 1. Unmarried on the Last Day of the Year

You must be considered unmarried on December 31. This means you:
- Were never married, OR
- Are legally separated under a divorce or separate maintenance decree, OR
- Were married but lived apart from your spouse for the LAST 6 MONTHS of the year

**Married Persons Living Apart:** If you live apart from your spouse and meet certain tests, you may be able to file as Head of Household even if not divorced or legally separated. You must:
- File a separate return
- Pay more than half the cost of keeping up your home
- Have your spouse not live in your home for the last 6 months of the year
- Have your home be the main home for your child, stepchild, or foster child for more than half the year
- Be able to claim the child as a dependent (or could claim except for joint return or gross income test)

### 2. Paid More Than Half the Cost of Keeping Up a Home

You must have paid more than 50% of the total cost of maintaining the household for the year.

#### What Counts as Cost of Keeping Up a Home

| Included | Not Included |
|----------|--------------|
| Property taxes | Clothing |
| Mortgage interest or rent | Education |
| Utilities | Medical expenses |
| Repairs and maintenance | Life insurance |
| Property insurance | Transportation |
| Food eaten in the home | Rental value of your home if you own it |

**TANF and Government Payments:** Count as support you provided if you received and used them to support another person.

**Calculation:** Compare your payments vs. what others paid (including government). If your total is more than others' total, you meet this test.

### 3. Have a Qualifying Person

Your home must be the main home of a qualifying person for more than half the year.

#### Who Is a Qualifying Person?

| If the person is your... | And... | Then they are a qualifying person |
|--------------------------|--------|----------------------------------|
| [[Qualifying Child]] (single) | Any relationship, age, residency, support, joint return tests met | Yes |
| [[Qualifying Child]] (married) | You can claim them as a dependent | Yes |
| [[Qualifying Child]] (married) | You can't claim them as a dependent | No |
| Parent (qualifying relative) | You can claim them as a dependent | Yes (even if they don't live with you) |
| Parent (qualifying relative) | You can't claim them as a dependent | No |
| Other qualifying relative | Lived with you more than half the year, related in a listed way, you can claim them as a dependent | Yes |
| Other qualifying relative | Didn't live with you more than half the year | No |
| Other qualifying relative | Only your qualifying relative because they lived with you all year (not related) | No |
| Any qualifying relative | You can't claim them as a dependent | No |

**Note:** A person claimed under a multiple support agreement is NOT a qualifying person for Head of Household.

#### Special Rule for Parent

If your qualifying person is your parent, you can file as Head of Household even if your parent doesn't live with you. However:
- You must be able to claim your parent as a dependent
- You must pay more than half the cost of keeping up a home that was your parent's main home for the entire year
- If your parent lives in a rest home, that counts as keeping up their home

## Exceptions to "Lived With You More Than Half the Year"

### Temporary Absences
You and the qualifying person are considered to live together during temporary absences for:
- Illness
- Education
- Business
- Vacation
- Military service
- Detention in a juvenile facility

Requirements:
- Must be reasonable to expect the person to return
- You must continue keeping up the home during the absence

### Death or Birth During the Year

The qualifying person qualifies you if:
- They lived with you for more than half the time they were alive during the year
- For a parent: You paid more than half the cost of keeping up their home for the entire time they were alive

**Example:** Your parent lived alone in an apartment and died on September 2. The upkeep cost $6,000 for the year until death. You paid $4,000 and your sibling paid $2,000. Since you paid more than half, you qualify for Head of Household.

### Adopted or Foster Child

A child adopted or placed with you during 2025 is considered to have lived with you for more than half the year if your main home was their main home for more than half the time since placement.

### Kidnapped Child

You can claim Head of Household if your qualifying child was kidnapped and:
1. Law enforcement presumes kidnapping by a non-family member
2. The child lived with you for more than half the year before the kidnapping
3. You would have qualified if the child hadn't been kidnapped

This treatment continues until the child is found dead or would have reached age 18.

## Benefits of Head of Household

| Benefit | Head of Household | Single |
|---------|-------------------|--------|
| Standard Deduction (2025) | $23,625 | $15,750 |
| Tax Brackets | More favorable | Less favorable |
| [[Earned Income Credit]] | Eligible | Limited eligibility |
| Child Tax Credit | Full | Full |

## How to File

On Form 1040 or 1040-SR:
1. Check the "Head of household" box on the Filing Status line
2. If your qualifying person is not claimed as your dependent, enter their name and SSN in the space provided
3. Use the Head of Household column of the Tax Table or Section C of the Tax Computation Worksheet
