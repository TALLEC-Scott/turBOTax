# Source of Income Rules

## Overview

Source of income rules determine whether income is from U.S. sources or foreign sources. This distinction is critical for:

- Nonresident alien taxation (only U.S. source income is taxable)
- Foreign tax credit calculations
- Treaty benefit applications

## Summary Table

| Income Item | Source Determination |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Salaries, wages | Where services performed |
| Business income (services) | Where services performed |
| Business income (inventory) | Where sold (purchased) or produced |
| Interest | Residence of payer |
| Dividends | U.S. or foreign corporation |
| Rents | Location of property |
| Royalties (natural resources) | Location of property |
| Royalties (patents, copyrights) | Where property is used |
| Real property sales | Location of property |
| Personal property sales | Seller's tax home |
| Pension distributions | Where services were performed |
| Transportation income | Where transportation begins/ends |

## Personal Services Income

### General Rule
Compensation for personal services is sourced where the services are **physically performed**, regardless of:
- Where payment is made
- Employer's location
- Where contract was signed

### Time Basis Allocation
For services performed both in and outside the U.S.:

```
U.S. Source Income = Total Compensation × (U.S. Days / Total Days)
```

### Geographical Basis (Fringe Benefits)
Certain fringe benefits are sourced by the location of the employee's **principal place of work**:
- Housing
- Local transportation
- Education
- Tax reimbursement
- Hazardous duty pay
- Moving expense reimbursement

## Interest Income

**General Rule:** Interest is sourced based on the residence of the payer.

| Payer | Source |
|-------|--------|
| U.S. individual | U.S. source |
| U.S. corporation | U.S. source |
| Nonresident alien | Foreign source |
| Foreign corporation | Foreign source |
| U.S. bank deposits | U.S. source |
| Foreign branch of U.S. bank | U.S. source (banking business) |

**Exception:** Interest from a foreign corporation may be U.S. source if 25%+ of its gross income is effectively connected with a U.S. trade or business.

## Dividend Income

| Corporation Type | Source |
|------------------|--------|
| Domestic corporation | U.S. source |
| Foreign corporation | Foreign source |

**Exception:** Foreign corporation dividends are partly U.S. source if 25%+ of gross income is ECI.

### Dividend Equivalents
U.S. source dividends include:
- Substitute dividends from securities lending
- Payments under notional principal contracts referencing U.S. dividends
- Equity-linked instrument payments referencing U.S. dividends

## Rental and Royalty Income

### Rents
Sourced to the **location of the property** generating the rent.

### Royalties
- Natural resources: Location of the property
- Patents, copyrights, etc.: Where the property is **used**

## Real Property

Gains from the sale of real property are sourced to the **location of the property**.

**Includes:**
- Land and buildings
- Natural deposits (oil, gas, minerals)
- Timber and crops
- Structural components

**U.S. Real Property Interest (USRPI):**
- Real property located in U.S. or U.S. Virgin Islands
- Stock in U.S. real property holding corporation

## Personal Property

**General Rule:** Sourced to seller's tax home at time of sale.

**Exceptions:**
- Inventory: Where sold or produced
- Depreciable property: Allocation rules apply
- Intangibles sold with payments contingent on productivity: Where used

## Transportation Income

| Transportation Type | U.S. Source Portion |
|--------------------|---------------------|
| Begins and ends in U.S. | 100% |
| Begins or ends in U.S. | 50% |
| Between U.S. and U.S. territory | 50% (aircraft only for NRAs) |

## Scholarships and Fellowships

**Source:** Residence of the payer, regardless of where funds are disbursed.

| Payer | Source |
|-------|--------|
| U.S. government | U.S. source |
| U.S. resident/corporation | U.S. source |
| Foreign government | Foreign source |
| Foreign corporation | Foreign source |
| International organization | Foreign source |

**Exception:** Payments for activities performed outside the U.S. are not U.S. source income.
