# Farm Property Dispositions

## Overview

When farm property is sold, exchanged, or otherwise disposed of, you may recognize a gain or loss. The tax treatment depends on the type of property, how long it was held, and whether it was used in the farming business.

## Types of Farm Property

### Section 1231 Property
Property used in farming business and held for more than one year:
- Land and buildings
- Farm machinery and equipment
- Livestock held for draft, breeding, dairy, or sporting purposes

### Ordinary Income Property
- Livestock held for sale
- Produce and crops
- Property held primarily for sale to customers

## Gain or Loss Calculation

### Basic Formula
- **Gain**: Amount realized minus adjusted basis
- **Loss**: Adjusted basis minus amount realized

### Adjusted Basis
- Original cost
- Plus: Improvements
- Minus: Depreciation, Section 179 deductions, casualty losses

## Section 1231 Treatment

### Net Gain
- Treated as long-term capital gain
- Taxed at lower capital gains rates

### Net Loss
- Treated as ordinary loss
- Fully deductible against ordinary income

## Depreciation Recapture

### Section 1245 Property
- Machinery, equipment, livestock
- Recapture gain as ordinary income up to depreciation taken
- Any remaining gain is Section 1231 gain

### Section 1250 Property
- Buildings and structures
- Limited recapture for farm buildings
- Unrecaptured Section 1250 gain may apply

## Livestock Sales

### Cattle and Horses
- Held 24+ months for draft, breeding, dairy, or sport: Section 1231 property
- Held less than 24 months: Ordinary income property

### Other Livestock
- Held 12+ months for breeding, dairy, or sport: Section 1231 property
- Raised livestock: Ordinary income property

## Special Sales

### Sale of Farm
- Allocate purchase price among assets
- May involve multiple property types
- Report on multiple forms

### Installment Sales
- Report gain as payments received
- File Form 6252
- Interest on deferred payments is taxable
