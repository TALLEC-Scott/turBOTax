# SLCSP (Second Lowest Cost Silver Plan)

## Overview

The Second Lowest Cost Silver Plan (SLCSP) is a benchmark plan used to calculate your Premium Tax Credit. It's not the plan you necessarily purchase—it's the reference point for determining your subsidy amount.

## Why SLCSP Matters

The SLCSP premium is crucial because:

1. **PTC Calculation**: Your monthly credit is based partly on the SLCSP premium
2. **Contribution Amount**: Your required contribution is calculated against the SLCSP
3. **Subsidy Caps**: The SLCSP sets the maximum premium the government will subsidize

## PTC Formula Using SLCSP

Your monthly PTC is the **lesser of**:

1. The enrollment premium for your chosen plan, OR
2. **Applicable SLCSP premium - Monthly contribution amount**

This means:
- If you choose a plan **cheaper** than the SLCSP, your PTC may cover most or all of the premium
- If you choose a plan **more expensive** than the SLCSP, you pay the difference
- If you choose **the SLCSP itself**, your PTC equals the SLCSP minus your contribution

## Determining Your Applicable SLCSP

The SLCSP that applies to you depends on:

1. **Your location**: Each rating area has different SLCSPs
2. **Your coverage family**: Who's enrolled in the qualified health plan
3. **Age**: Premiums vary by age in most states
4. **Tobacco use**: May affect premiums in some states

### Coverage Family Consideration

The SLCSP must cover the same people as your coverage family:
- If you're covering yourself only, use the self-only SLCSP
- If covering family members, use the appropriate family SLCSP

## Finding Your SLCSP Premium

### Form 1095-A, Column B

Your Form 1095-A reports the SLCSP premium in **Part III, Column B**.

**Important**: If Column B is:
- **Blank**: You need to find your SLCSP
- **Shows $0**: May need correction
- **Seems wrong**: Verify with Marketplace tools

### SLCSP Tools

If Form 1095-A doesn't show the correct SLCSP:

1. **HealthCare.gov**: Use the "See plans and prices" tool without logging in
2. **State Marketplaces**: Similar tools on state websites
3. **Don't request corrected Form 1095-A**: You can determine the correct SLCSP yourself

## Situations Requiring SLCSP Adjustment

### Missing or Incorrect SLCSP on Form 1095-A

Common causes:
- You enrolled off-Marketplace but received APTC
- Your coverage family changed during the year
- You moved to a different rating area
- The Marketplace has incorrect information

### Coverage Family Changes

If your coverage family changed during the year:
- Different SLCSPs may apply to different months
- Complete Form 8962 monthly (lines 12-23) instead of annually (line 11)

### Multiple SLCSPs During the Year

If you had different SLCSPs for different periods:
- Use Worksheet A in Pub 974 to determine reference months
- Report monthly amounts on Form 8962, lines 12-23

## Worksheet A - Determining Reference Months

Use Worksheet A when:
- An individual not lawfully present was enrolled
- Your coverage family composition changed
- You need to determine correct SLCSP for partial-year situations

### Reference Month Concept

A "reference month" is a month that can provide the correct SLCSP premium for another month when:
- The coverage family would have been the same except for a specific change

## Special Situations

### Individuals Not Lawfully Present

If someone in your coverage family is not lawfully present:
- Use SLCSP for the lawfully present family members only
- Different SLCSP applies for months when that person was enrolled vs. disenrolled

### Moving to a New Area

If you moved to a different rating area:
- Different SLCSPs apply before and after the move
- May need to report this change to Marketplace

### Plan Discontinuation

If the SLCSP for your area was discontinued mid-year:
- The replacement benchmark plan applies
- Check Marketplace tools for current SLCSP

## Example Calculations

### Example 1: Choosing a Cheaper Plan

- SLCSP premium: $500/month
- Your contribution amount: $150/month
- Your chosen Bronze plan: $350/month
- **PTC = $350** (lesser of $350 or $500-$150)
- **You pay**: $350 - $350 = $0

### Example 2: Choosing a More Expensive Plan

- SLCSP premium: $500/month
- Your contribution amount: $150/month
- Your chosen Gold plan: $600/month
- **PTC = $350** (lesser of $600 or $500-$150)
- **You pay**: $600 - $350 = $250

### Example 3: Choosing the SLCSP

- SLCSP premium: $500/month
- Your contribution amount: $150/month
- Your chosen plan: SLCSP ($500/month)
- **PTC = $350** ($500 - $150)
- **You pay**: $150 (your contribution amount)

## Impact on Tax Planning

1. **Plan Selection**: Understanding SLCSP helps choose the right metal level
2. **Income Projection**: Higher income = higher contribution = lower PTC
3. **APTC Management**: Report income changes to adjust advance payments
4. **Year-End Moves**: Moving may change your SLCSP
