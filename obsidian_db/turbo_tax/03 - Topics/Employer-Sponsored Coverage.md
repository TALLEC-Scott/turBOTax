# Employer-Sponsored Coverage

## Overview

Employer-sponsored coverage is health insurance offered by an employer to employees and their families. For Premium Tax Credit purposes, having access to affordable employer-sponsored coverage that provides minimum value generally disqualifies you from receiving PTC.

## Types of Employer-Sponsored Coverage

### MEC-Qualified Employer Plans

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Group health insurance | Insured plans through commercial insurers |
| Self-insured plans | Employer-funded health plans |
| Governmental plans | Federal Employees Health Benefits Program (FEHB) |
| Grandfathered plans | Plans existing before March 23, 2010 |
| Expatriate health plans | Coverage for employees working internationally |
| COBRA continuation | Coverage after employment ends |
| Retiree coverage | Health benefits after retirement |

### Not Considered MEC

The following are NOT minimum essential coverage:
- Stand-alone dental or vision plans
- Workers' compensation
- Accident-only coverage
- Coverage for a specified disease
- Disability income insurance

## PTC Eligibility Impact

### The General Rule

You cannot receive PTC for months when you are eligible for employer-sponsored coverage that is:
1. **Affordable**, AND
2. **Provides minimum value**

### Affordability Test (2025)

For the employee, coverage is affordable if the employee's share of the premium for self-only coverage does not exceed a percentage of household income (adjusted annually).

**Important for 2025**: For family members (spouse and dependents), affordability is now based on the premium to cover the employee AND family members, not just the employee-only premium.

### Minimum Value

A plan provides minimum value if it:
- Pays at least 60% of the total allowed costs of benefits
- Includes substantial coverage for physician and inpatient hospital services

## When You're NOT Considered Eligible

### Waiting Periods
You're not considered eligible during any waiting period before coverage becomes effective.

### Not Enrolled and Open Enrollment Passed
If you could have enrolled in affordable, minimum value coverage during an open enrollment period but didn't, you cannot get PTC for the remainder of that plan year.

### Coverage After Employment Ends
If you're offered employer coverage after employment ends (COBRA or retiree coverage), you're considered eligible only for months you're actually enrolled.

## Special Situations

### COBRA Coverage
- **Enrolled**: Considered eligible for MEC, no PTC
- **Not enrolled**: Not considered eligible, may qualify for PTC

### Retiree Coverage
- Same rules as COBRA - must be enrolled to be considered eligible

### Opt-Out Payments
If you decline employer coverage and receive opt-out payments:
- You may still be eligible for PTC
- The opt-out payment may be taxable compensation

### Health Reimbursement Arrangements

#### QSEHRA
See [[QSEHRA]] for special rules affecting PTC.

#### Other HRAs
May affect PTC eligibility depending on the type and structure.

## Determining Affordability for Family Members (2025+)

The "family glitch" fix: For tax years beginning after 2022:

| Before 2023 | 2023 and After |
|-------------|----------------|
| Family affordability based on employee-only premium | Family affordability based on actual family premium cost |

**Example**: If an employee's share for self-only is $100/month but family coverage is $600/month, family members may now qualify for PTC if the $600 exceeds the affordability threshold.

## Required Documentation

### Form 1095-C
Large employers (50+ full-time employees) must provide Form 1095-C showing:
- Whether employer coverage was offered
- Which months coverage was available
- Whether coverage was affordable and provided minimum value

**Note for 2025**: Employers may not automatically send Form 1095-C unless requested. If requested, must be provided by January 31 or 30 days after request.

## What to Do if Offered Employer Coverage

### If Coverage IS Affordable and Provides Minimum Value
- You cannot receive PTC for that coverage
- Family members may still qualify if family coverage is unaffordable (2025+)

### If Coverage IS NOT Affordable or Doesn't Provide Minimum Value
- You may be eligible for PTC
- Must decline the employer coverage
- Can purchase Marketplace coverage with PTC

### If You're Not Sure
- Ask your employer for the plan's Summary of Benefits
- Use the Marketplace's affordability estimator
- Consult a tax professional
