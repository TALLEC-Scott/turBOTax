# Charitable Contribution Limits

Your charitable contribution deduction may be limited based on the type of property donated, the type of organization receiving it, and your adjusted gross income (AGI).

## Overview

If your total contributions exceed 20% of your AGI, various percentage limits may apply. The limits determine:
- How much you can deduct in the current year
- How much carries forward to future years

## Organization Categories

### 50% Limit Organizations (First Category)

- Churches and religious organizations
- Educational organizations (schools, colleges, museums)
- Hospitals and medical research organizations
- Government entities (for public purposes)
- Publicly supported charities (Red Cross, United Way, etc.)
- Private operating foundations
- Veteran service organizations (501(c)(19))

### 30% Limit Organizations (Second Category)

- Private nonoperating foundations
- Organizations not qualifying as 50% limit organizations
- Veterans' organizations (if not in first category)

## Deduction Limits by Property Type

### Cash Contributions

| Organization Type | Deduction Limit |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| 50% limit organizations | 60% of AGI |
| 30% limit organizations | 30% of AGI |
| "For the use of" any organization | 30% of AGI |

### Noncash Contributions to 50% Limit Organizations

| Property Type | Deduction Limit |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Ordinary income property | 50% of AGI |
| Capital gain property (FMV) | 30% of AGI |
| Capital gain property (reduced by appreciation) | 50% of AGI |
| Qualified conservation contribution | 50% of AGI |

### Contributions to 30% Limit Organizations

| Property Type | Deduction Limit |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Cash | 30% of AGI |
| Ordinary income property | 30% of AGI |
| Capital gain property (FMV) | 20% of AGI |

### Conservation Contributions

| Taxpayer Type | Deduction Limit |
|---------------|-----------------|
| General | 50% of AGI |
| Qualified farmer/rancher | 100% of AGI |

## "For the Use Of" Rule

A contribution is "for the use of" a qualified organization when held in a legally enforceable trust or similar arrangement for the organization.

**Impact:**
- Cash contributions "for use of" organization: 30% limit
- Noncash contributions "for use of" organization: 30% or 20% limit
- Student living with you expenses: Considered "for use of" organization

## Calculating Your Deduction

### Step-by-Step Order

When multiple limits apply, determine deduction in this order:

1. **60% limit cash contributions** to 50% limit organizations
2. **50% limit noncash contributions** to 50% limit organizations
3. **30% limit contributions** (non-capital gain property) to 30% limit organizations
4. **30% limit capital gain property** contributions
5. **20% limit capital gain property** contributions
6. **50% limit qualified conservation contributions**
7. **100% limit qualified conservation contributions** (farmers/ranchers)

### Example Calculation

**Scenario:**
- AGI: $100,000
- Cash to church: $40,000
- Stock (capital gain property, FMV) to university: $25,000
- Cash to private foundation: $15,000

**Calculation:**
1. Cash to church (60% limit): $40,000 ≤ $60,000 → Deduct $40,000
2. Stock to university (30% limit): $25,000 ≤ $30,000 → Deduct $25,000
3. Cash to foundation (30% limit): Limited by 50% AGI minus contributions to 50% orgs
   - 50% AGI = $50,000
   - Contributions to 50% orgs = $40,000 + $25,000 = $65,000
   - No room remaining → Carry over $15,000

**Total deduction:** $65,000
**Carryover:** $15,000

## Capital Gain Property Election

You may elect to use the 50% limit for capital gain property contributions to 50% limit organizations (instead of 30%).

**Trade-off:**
- Higher percentage limit (50% vs. 30%)
- Must reduce FMV by appreciation

**Example:**
- Stock FMV: $10,000
- Basis: $4,000
- Appreciation: $6,000

| Election | Deduction Limit | Deduction Amount |
|----------|-----------------|------------------|
| No election | 30% of AGI | $10,000 (FMV) |
| Election | 50% of AGI | $4,000 (basis) |

Choose based on which provides larger total deduction.

## Carryovers

### General Rules

- Excess contributions carry forward up to **5 years**
- Conservation contribution excess carries forward **15 years**
- Carryovers retain their original character (limit category)

### Carryover Order

Apply carryovers in the order they were generated:
1. First-in, first-out basis
2. Same limits apply as original contribution
3. Must still meet substantiation requirements

### Reporting

Report carryovers on Schedule A, line 13:
- Total carryover from prior years
- Amount used in current year
- Remaining carryover to future years

## Total Contribution Limit

Your total charitable contributions cannot exceed 100% of your AGI (or 50% if not a qualified conservation contribution by a farmer/rancher).
