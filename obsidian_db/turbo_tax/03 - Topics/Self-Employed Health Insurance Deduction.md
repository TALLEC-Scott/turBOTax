# Self-Employed Health Insurance Deduction

## Overview

Self-employed individuals may be able to deduct health insurance premiums paid for themselves, their spouse, and their dependents as an adjustment to income (above-the-line deduction). This deduction can be taken in addition to the Premium Tax Credit, but special coordination rules apply.

## Eligibility

You may qualify for the self-employed health insurance deduction if you:

1. Are self-employed (sole proprietor, partner, or S corporation shareholder-employee)
2. Had a net profit from self-employment
3. Established a health insurance plan in your trade or business
4. The plan covers you, your spouse, and/or your dependents

### Eligible Coverage Types
- Individual health insurance (including Marketplace plans)
- Employer-sponsored coverage (if you're a partner or S corp shareholder)
- Medicare premiums
- Long-term care insurance (subject to age-based limits)

## The Coordination Problem with PTC

Self-employed taxpayers face a special challenge:

1. **PTC reduces** your health insurance premium
2. **Self-employed deduction** is limited to premiums actually paid
3. **But**: The deduction reduces AGI, which increases PTC

This creates a circular calculation that must be resolved iteratively.

## How to Coordinate PTC and the Deduction

### The Iterative Method

Use the worksheets in Publication 974 (Worksheets W and X) to figure both the PTC and deduction correctly.

**Step 1**: Estimate your self-employed health insurance deduction
**Step 2**: Calculate your PTC using this estimate
**Step 3**: Recalculate the deduction based on the PTC
**Step 4**: Repeat until the amounts stabilize

### Illustrated Example (from Pub 974)

Carla, a self-employed taxpayer, goes through this process:

1. **First Pass**: Uses estimated deduction to calculate AGI and PTC
2. **Second Pass**: Adjusts deduction based on PTC received
3. **Final**: Claims the coordinated amounts on her return

Key worksheets used:
- **Worksheet W**: Calculates maximum allowable deduction
- **Worksheet X**: Determines household income and repayment limitation

## Key Worksheets in Pub 974

### Worksheet W - Maximum Deduction

Calculates the maximum self-employed health insurance deduction considering:
- Net earnings from self-employment
- Premiums paid
- PTC received

### Worksheet X - Household Income

Determines modified AGI and household income for PTC purposes while accounting for the deduction.

## Specified Premiums

For PTC coordination, "specified premiums" are premiums for:
- Months you were self-employed
- Coverage of yourself, spouse, and dependents
- A qualified health plan through the Marketplace

**Note**: Premiums for months you weren't self-employed or for other family members don't count as specified premiums.

## Form 7206

For 2025, self-employed taxpayers use **Form 7206 (Self-Employed Insurance Deduction)** to calculate and claim this deduction.

The form calculates:
1. Maximum deduction based on net earnings
2. Amount allocable to specified premiums
3. Final deduction amount

## Step-by-Step Process

### If You Received APTC

1. Complete Worksheet 1-1 in Form 8962 instructions to find modified AGI
2. Complete Worksheet W using estimated deduction
3. Calculate tentative PTC on Form 8962
4. Complete Worksheet X to find household income and repayment limit
5. Use Step 3 Worksheet to find final deduction
6. Re-file Form 8962 with final AGI

### If You Did NOT Receive APTC

1. Calculate your maximum deduction (limited by net earnings)
2. Claim the deduction on Schedule 1, line 17
3. Claim any remaining PTC on Form 8962

## Important Limitations

### Net Earnings Limit
Your deduction cannot exceed your net earnings from self-employment (minus the deductible part of self-employment tax and qualified plan contributions).

### Specified Premiums Limit
The portion of your deduction allocable to specified premiums cannot exceed those premiums minus the PTC allocable to them.

## Tax Planning Considerations

1. **Lower AGI = Higher PTC**: The deduction reduces AGI, potentially increasing your PTC
2. **Don't Double-Dip**: You cannot deduct premiums paid with PTC (it's already tax-advantaged)
3. **Medicare Premiums**: May be deductible even for non-self-employed spouse
4. **Timing**: Report income changes to Marketplace to adjust APTC during the year
