# Foreign Currency and Exchange Rates

## Overview

Since U.S. income tax is imposed in U.S. dollars, foreign income and taxes must be translated into dollars. Exchange rate fluctuations can significantly affect the foreign tax credit calculation.

## Functional Currency

Your **functional currency** is generally the U.S. dollar unless you are required to use a foreign currency.

### Default Rule
- U.S. dollar is functional currency for most taxpayers
- Exception: Qualified Business Units (QBUs) meeting specific requirements

### Qualified Business Unit (QBU)
A QBU is a separate, clearly identified unit of a trade or business that maintains separate books and records.

**Functional currency is USD if:**
- Business conducted primarily in dollars
- Principal place of business in U.S.
- You choose or are required to use dollar
- Books not kept in currency of economic environment

## Translation Rules

### If Functional Currency Is USD
Translate into dollars **immediately**:
- All income, expense, etc. received/paid in foreign currency
- Use exchange rate that most properly reflects income

### If Functional Currency Is Foreign
- Make all income tax determinations in functional currency
- At year-end, translate results (income/loss) into USD for tax return

## Exchange Rates for Foreign Taxes

### Cash Method Taxpayers
Use the exchange rate **in effect on the date you paid** the foreign tax.

**Special rules:**
- **Withholding tax:** Rate on date tax was withheld
- **Estimated tax payments:** Rate on date payment made

### Accrual Method Taxpayers
Generally use the **average exchange rate** for the tax year to which taxes relate.

**Conditions for average rate:**
1. Taxes paid on/after first day of the tax year to which they relate
2. Taxes paid within 24 months after close of that tax year
3. Tax liability not denominated in inflationary currency

**Otherwise:** Use exchange rate on date paid.

### Election for Date-Paid Rate
You may elect to use the exchange rate on the date foreign taxes are paid if:
- Taxes denominated in nonfunctional foreign currency
- Election applies to all such taxes or only those attributable to QBUs

**Requirements:**
- Make election by due date (including extensions) for first tax year
- Attach statement to tax return identifying scope of election
- Once made, applies to all subsequent years unless revoked with IRS consent

## Foreign Tax Redetermination

Exchange rate fluctuations may trigger a foreign tax redetermination if:
- Dollar value of accrued tax differs from dollar value of tax paid
- Due to exchange rate fluctuation between accrual and payment

**De minimis exception:** No redetermination required if change is:
- Less than $10,000, AND
- Less than 2% of total foreign tax accrued for that country

## Effect on FTC

Exchange rate changes affect:

| Impact | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Credit amount** | Dollar value of foreign taxes claimed |
| **Limit calculation** | Foreign source income in dollars |
| **Carryovers** | Dollar value of unused taxes |

## Foreign Tax Redetermination Due to Exchange Rates

If exchange rate changes cause redetermination:
1. Adjust U.S. tax in year accrued taxes are paid
2. File Schedule C (Form 1116) to report
3. May need to file Form 1040-X if U.S. tax liability changes

## Getting Exchange Rates

Sources for exchange rates:
- Banks
- U.S. embassies
- Federal Reserve
- Commercial rate services
