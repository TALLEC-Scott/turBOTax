# Replacement Property

## Overview

If you receive insurance or other reimbursement that exceeds your adjusted basis in destroyed or stolen property, you have a **gain**. You may be able to **postpone reporting this gain** by purchasing replacement property.

## Requirements to Postpone Gain

1. Property was **involuntarily converted** (destroyed, stolen, condemned)
2. You receive reimbursement exceeding adjusted basis
3. You purchase **qualified replacement property**
4. Replacement is within the **replacement period**

## Qualified Replacement Property

Property that is:
- **Similar** to the converted property, OR
- **Related in service or use** to the converted property

### Main Home Special Rules

For main homes (or contents) in [[Federally Declared Disasters|federally declared disaster areas]]:

1. **No gain recognized** on insurance proceeds for unscheduled personal property
2. Other proceeds treated as for a **single item of property**
3. Any similar/related replacement property qualifies

## Replacement Period

### General Rule
Begins on date property was damaged, destroyed, or stolen.

Ends **2 years** after close of first tax year in which any part of gain is realized.

### Main Home in Disaster Area
Ends **4 years** after close of first tax year in which gain is realized.

### Condemnations
See [[Involuntary Conversions]] in Pub 544 for special rules.

### Extension
May request extension **before** the replacement period ends if construction cannot be completed in time. Contact IRS at:
- Fax: 877-477-9193
- Mail: IRS, 985 Michigan Ave., Stop 16, Detroit, MI 48226

## How to Postpone Gain

### Replacement Before Filing Return

Attach statement to return including:
- Date and details of casualty/theft
- Insurance or other reimbursement received
- How gain was figured
- Description of replacement property
- Postponed gain amount
- Basis adjustment to replacement property
- Any gain being reported as income

### Replacement After Filing Return

1. File statement with return for gain year indicating intent to replace property
2. File another statement when replacement property is acquired

### Required Statement Elements

1. Taxpayer name, address, TIN
2. Description of converted property
3. Date of conversion
4. Adjusted basis of converted property
5. Date(s) and amount(s) of payments received
6. Copy of return with involuntary conversion
7. Actions taken to replace property

## Basis of Replacement Property

> **Basis = Cost - Postponed Gain**

This postpones tax on the gain until you dispose of the replacement property.

## Example

Fire destroyed rental home:
- Insurance reimbursement: $67,000
- Adjusted basis: $62,000
- **Gain**: $5,000

New rental home constructed for $110,000:
- Basis = $110,000 - $5,000 postponed gain = **$105,000**

## Changing Your Mind

You can change your election about whether to report or postpone gain **at any time before the end of the replacement period**.

If you previously reported gain, file amended return (Form 1040-X) to postpone.

## Substituting Replacement Property

**Cannot substitute** different replacement property once you've designated property on your tax return.

**Exception**: If designated property turns out not to be qualified replacement property, you may substitute qualified property within the replacement period.

## Amended Return Required

File Form 1040-X for the gain year if:

1. You don't acquire replacement property within the replacement period, OR
2. Replacement property costs less than the reimbursement

Report the gain that cannot be postponed and pay any additional tax due.

## Three-Year Limit for Assessment

The period for assessing tax on gain ends **3 years** after you notify the IRS director that you:
- Replaced the property, OR
- Did not replace within the period, OR
- Do not intend to replace
