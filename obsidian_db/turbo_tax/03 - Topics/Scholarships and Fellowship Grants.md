# Scholarships and Fellowship Grants

## Overview

Scholarships and fellowship grants are amounts paid to help students pursue their education. In many cases, these amounts can be excluded from gross income if certain requirements are met.

**Authority:** [[Pub 970 - Tax Benefits for Education#Chapter 1]]

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Scholarship | Amount paid to aid an undergraduate or graduate student in pursuing studies |
| Fellowship Grant | Amount paid to aid an individual in pursuing study or research |
| Qualified Education Expenses | Tuition, fees, books, supplies, and equipment required for enrollment |

## Tax-Free Treatment Requirements

A scholarship or fellowship grant is **tax-free** only if:

1. **Candidate for a Degree**
   - Attending primary/secondary school, OR
   - Pursuing a degree at college/university, OR
   - At an eligible educational institution with acceptable program

2. **Eligible Educational Institution**
   - Maintains regular faculty and curriculum
   - Has regularly enrolled body of students
   - Carries on educational activities

3. **Expense Limitation**
   - Amount does not exceed qualified education expenses
   - Not designated for other purposes (room and board)
   - Not required to be used for non-qualified expenses

4. **No Service Requirement**
   - Does not represent payment for teaching, research, or other services

## Exceptions to Service Rule

The following are NOT treated as payment for services:
- National Health Service Corps Scholarship Program
- Armed Forces Health Professions Scholarship and Financial Assistance Program
- Comprehensive student work-learning-service programs at work colleges

## Taxable vs. Tax-Free

| Amount Type | Tax Treatment |
|-------------|---------------|
| Used for tuition, fees, books, supplies, equipment | Tax-free (if degree candidate) |
| Used for room and board | Taxable |
| Payment for required services | Taxable (with exceptions) |
| Not a degree candidate | Fully taxable |

## Reporting Requirements

### Form W-2 Received
- Include taxable amount in income on [[Form 1040]] Line 1a
- Taxable amount shown in Box 1

### No Form W-2 Received
- Include taxable amount on [[Schedule 1 (Form 1040)]] Line 8r
- Write "SCH" and the amount on the dotted line

## Special Types of Educational Assistance

### Pell Grants
- Need-based grants treated as scholarships
- Tax-free to extent used for qualified education expenses

### Fulbright Grants
- Generally treated as scholarship/fellowship
- Same rules apply for tax-free treatment

### Veterans' Benefits
- Payments for education under VA programs are **fully tax-free**
- Must reduce education expenses by VA payments for credit calculations

### Service Academy Appointments
- Not a scholarship - payment for services
- Reported on Form W-2 as wages
- Fully taxable

### Qualified Tuition Reduction
- Tax-free if received from eligible educational institution
- Available to employees, retirees, and their families
- Different rules for graduate vs. undergraduate level

## Coordination with Education Credits

May benefit from including otherwise tax-free scholarships in income:
- Increases eligible expenses for education credits
- Could result in larger credit than tax on scholarship income
- Requires careful calculation

## Common Questions

### Q: Is my athletic scholarship taxable?
**A:** Athletic scholarships follow the same rules - tax-free to the extent used for qualified education expenses and not payment for services.

### Q: Do I have to report my scholarship on my tax return?
**A:** Only if any part is taxable. If the entire amount is tax-free, no reporting is required.

### Q: Can I choose to include my scholarship in income to claim education credits?
**A:** Yes, sometimes this results in lower overall tax. Consult Chapter 2 and 3 of Pub 970 for coordination rules.
