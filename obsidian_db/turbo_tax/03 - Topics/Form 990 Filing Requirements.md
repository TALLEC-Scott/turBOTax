# Form 990 Filing Requirements

## Overview

Most tax-exempt organizations must file an annual information return with the IRS. The specific form required depends on the organization's size and type. Failure to file for three consecutive years results in automatic revocation of tax-exempt status.

## Form Requirements by Organization Type

| Organization Type | Gross Receipts | Form Required |
|-------------------|----------------|---------------|
| Most exempt organizations | ≥ $200,000 or total assets ≥ $500,000 | Form 990 |
| Most exempt organizations | < $200,000 and total assets < $500,000 | Form 990-EZ |
| Most exempt organizations | ≤ $50,000 | Form 990-N (e-postcard) |
| Private foundations | Any amount | Form 990-PF |
| Section 501(c)(3) organizations (alternate) | Any amount | Form 990 or 990-EZ |
| Black lung benefit trusts | Any amount | Form 990-BL |
| Political organizations | Any amount | Form 990 or 1120-POL |

## Filing Deadlines

### Regular Deadline
- **Due Date**: 15th day of the 5th month after end of tax year
- **Calendar year filers**: May 15
- **Fiscal year filers**: 15th day of 5th month after year-end

### Extensions
- Form 8868 can be filed for an automatic 6-month extension
- Additional extensions may be available in certain circumstances

## Automatic Revocation

### Three-Year Rule
Organizations that fail to file required returns for three consecutive years:
- Automatically lose tax-exempt status
- Must reapply for exemption
- Donor contributions no longer deductible (for 501(c)(3) organizations)

### Relief from Revocation
Organizations may request retroactive reinstatement:
- Within 15 months: Streamlined application process
- After 15 months: Must show reasonable cause

## Public Disclosure Requirements

### Returns Available for Public Inspection
- Form 990, 990-EZ, 990-PF
- Form 990-T (unrelated business income return)
- Exemption applications and supporting documents

### Inspection Rules
- Must provide copies within 30 days of request
- May charge reasonable copying fees
- Must make documents available at principal office

### Website Posting
Organizations with websites must post Forms 990 for most recent 3 years.

## Schedule A - Public Charity Status

Public charities must complete Schedule A to:
- Identify public charity classification
- Calculate public support percentage
- Document compliance with support tests

See [[Public Charities]] for support test details.

## Schedule B - Contributors

- Must list contributors giving $5,000 or more (or 2% of contributions)
- Public inspection version must exclude contributor names
- Required for most organizations except churches and government units

## Penalties for Non-Filing

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Failure to file | $20 per day, up to $10,500 |
| Failure to provide public inspection | $20 per day, up to $10,500 |
| Fraudulent returns | Criminal penalties may apply |
