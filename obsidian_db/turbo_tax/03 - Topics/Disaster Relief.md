# Disaster Relief

## Overview

Various types of disaster relief payments may be excluded from income. The tax treatment depends on the type of disaster, source of payment, and purpose of the funds.

## Qualified Disaster Relief Payments

### Definition

A qualified disaster relief payment is an amount paid to you:

1. To reimburse reasonable personal, family, living, or funeral expenses from a qualified disaster
2. To repair or rehabilitate your home or contents due to qualified disaster
3. Death or personal injury payments from common carrier due to qualified disaster
4. Government payments to promote general welfare in connection with qualified disaster

### Exclusion Rules

You can exclude these payments only to the extent expenses aren't covered by insurance or otherwise reimbursed.

### Qualified Disasters

- Federally declared disasters
- Terrorist or military action disasters
- Common carrier accidents
- Events determined catastrophic by Secretary of Treasury

## Disaster Relief Grants

### Nontaxable Grants

Under the Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act, post-disaster grants for necessary expenses are NOT taxable:
- Medical and dental expenses
- Housing expenses
- Personal property expenses
- Transportation expenses
- Funeral expenses

### Limitations

- Don't deduct casualty losses reimbursed by grants
- Don't deduct medical expenses reimbursed by grants
- If you deducted casualty loss and later receive grant, may need to include grant in income

## Special Disaster Programs

### Qualified Wildfire Relief Payments

Nontaxable payments for expenses or losses from qualified wildfire disasters (federally declared after December 31, 2014). See [[Pub 547 - Casualties, Disasters, and Thefts]].

### East Palestine Train Derailment

Payments from government agency, Norfolk Southern Railway, or related parties to individuals affected by February 3, 2023 derailment may be nontaxable "East Palestine train derailment payments."

### Homeowner Assistance Fund (HAF)

Payments under HAF program for mortgage assistance, utilities, and other housing expenses are NOT taxable income.

### Hardest Hit Fund

State Housing Finance Agency payments used for mortgage interest are NOT taxable.

### Emergency Homeowners' Loan Program (EHLP)

HUD or state-administered EHLP payments are NOT taxable.

## Disaster Mitigation Payments

Payments through state/local governments under the Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief Act or National Flood Insurance Act used to reduce future disaster damage are nontaxable.

**Important:** Can't increase basis of property for improvements made with nontaxable mitigation payments.

## Other Nontaxable Benefits

### Nutrition Program for the Elderly

Food benefits under this program are NOT taxable. Cash pay for preparing/serving meals is taxable wages.

### Winter Energy Assistance

State payments to reduce winter energy costs for qualified individuals are NOT taxable.

### Mortgage Assistance (Section 235)

Payments under National Housing Act section 235 for mortgage assistance are NOT taxable. Interest paid for homeowner under this program is NOT deductible.

### Replacement Housing Payments

Payments under Uniform Relocation Assistance Act are NOT included in income but ARE included in basis of newly acquired property.

### Indian Financing Grants

Nonreimbursable grants under Indian Financing Act for profit-making enterprises are NOT taxable.

### Indian General Welfare Benefits

Payments to tribal members under tribal government programs are NOT taxable if:
1. Program administered under specified guidelines without discrimination
2. Benefits available to members meeting guidelines, for general welfare, not lavish, and not compensation for services

## Disaster Unemployment Assistance

Unemployment assistance under Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act IS taxable unemployment compensation. See [[Unemployment Benefits]].

## Reporting Considerations

### Recoveries

If you deducted a casualty loss in a prior year and later receive disaster relief, you may need to include part of the relief in income. See [[Recoveries]] and Pub 525.

### Basis Adjustments

Nontaxable disaster relief generally:
- Does NOT increase basis in property
- May reduce casualty loss deduction

### Record Keeping

Keep records of:
- Disaster relief payments received
- How funds were used
- Insurance reimbursements
- Prior year casualty loss deductions

## Common Questions

### Q: Is FEMA assistance taxable?
**A:** Generally no, FEMA grants for necessary expenses and serious needs are not taxable.

### Q: Do I have to report disaster relief on my tax return?
**A:** Nontaxable disaster relief payments don't need to be reported. Taxable payments (like disaster unemployment) must be reported.

### Q: What if I already deducted a casualty loss?
**A:** If you receive disaster relief for a loss you previously deducted, you may need to include the relief in income under the tax benefit rule.

### Q: Are insurance proceeds taxable?
**A:** Generally not, but if proceeds exceed your basis in destroyed property, you may have a gain. Special deferral rules may apply for replacing property.
