# Passive Category Income

## Overview

Passive Category Income is one of the separate limit income categories used to calculate the foreign tax credit limitation. Income in this category is subject to a separate FTC limitation calculation, preventing taxpayers from using excess foreign taxes from high-tax passive income to offset U.S. tax on other foreign income.

## What Is Passive Income

Passive income generally includes:

| Income Type | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Dividends** | From foreign and domestic corporations |
| **Interest** | Including original issue discount |
| **Rents** | From real and personal property |
| **Royalties** | From patents, copyrights, etc. |
| **Annuities** | From insurance contracts |
| **Net gain from investment property** | Sale of non-income-producing property |
| **Commodities gains** | Except hedging and active business |
| **Section 1293 inclusions** | PFIC (Passive Foreign Investment Company) amounts |

### Mutual Fund/RIC Distributions
Foreign source distributions from mutual funds or other Regulated Investment Companies (RICs) that pass through foreign tax credits are generally treated as passive income.

## What Is NOT Passive Income

Passive income does **NOT** include:

- Gains/losses from sale of inventory property
- Export financing interest
- Active business rents and royalties
- **High-taxed income** (reclassified as general category)
- Income defined in another separate limit category
- Financial services income (for financial services entities)

### High-Taxed Income

Income is "high-taxed" if foreign taxes (after expense allocation) exceed the highest U.S. tax rate applicable to that income. High-taxed passive income is reclassified as **General Category Income**.

See Regulations section 1.904-4(c) for detailed rules.

### Export Financing Interest

Interest from financing sale of property for use outside the U.S. is NOT passive if:
- Property is manufactured/produced/grown/extracted in the U.S. by you or a related person, AND
- 50% or less of FMV is due to U.S. imports

## Specified Passive Category Income

A subset of passive income including:

1. **DISC Dividends** - From Domestic International Sales Corporation to extent treated as foreign source
2. **Former FSC Distributions** - From former Foreign Sales Corporation out of earnings attributable to:
   - Foreign trade income, OR
   - Interest and carrying charges from foreign trade income transactions

## Special Rules

### Income from CFCs

| Income Type | Treatment |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Interest, rents, royalties** | Passive if attributable to CFC's passive income |
| **Dividends** | Passive proportionate to CFC's passive E&P |
| **Subpart F income** | Same category as at CFC level |

### Partnership Distributive Share

- Partner's share of passive income from partnership is passive category
- Partners owning <10% partnership interest: income generally treated as passive
- See Regulations section 1.904-4(n) for details

## Form 1116 Reporting

- Report passive category income on **separate Form 1116**
- Check the "Passive category income" box
- Complete country-by-country reporting for each country
- Exception: RIC-passed income can be aggregated in single column

## Exemption from Form 1116

You may be exempt from filing Form 1116 if:

1. Only passive category income for the year
2. Qualified foreign taxes ≤ $300 ($600 if married filing jointly)
3. All foreign income and taxes on payee statement (e.g., Form 1099-DIV)
4. You elect this treatment

**Caution:** If you make this election, you cannot carry back or carry over unused foreign taxes.
