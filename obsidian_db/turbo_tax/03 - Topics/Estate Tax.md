# Estate Tax

## Overview

Federal estate tax is a tax on the transfer of property at death. The tax applies to the taxable estate, which is the gross estate minus allowable deductions.

## Filing Requirements

### When to File Form 706
File if gross estate plus adjusted taxable gifts exceeds the basic exclusion amount:

| Year of Death | Basic Exclusion Amount |
|---------------|------------------------|
| 2024 | $13,610,000 |
| 2025 | $13,990,000 |

### Portability Election
Form 706 must be filed to elect portability of Deceased Spousal Unused Exclusion (DSUE), even if estate is below filing threshold.

### Due Date
- 9 months after date of death
- Automatic 6-month extension available via Form 4768

## Gross Estate

### Property Included
- All property owned at death (real, personal, tangible, intangible)
- Life insurance proceeds payable to estate OR if decedent owned the policy
- Annuities payable to estate or heirs
- Property transferred within 3 years of death
- Community property (one-half)
- Joint property (proportionate share)

### Valuation
- Fair market value on date of death, OR
- Alternate valuation date (6 months after death) if elected
- Special-use valuation for qualifying farm/business real property

## Allowable Deductions

### From Gross Estate to Taxable Estate

| Deduction | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Funeral expenses | Paid from estate |
| Debts | Owed by decedent at death |
| Mortgages | On estate property |
| Medical expenses | Unpaid at death |
| Charitable deduction | Bequests to qualifying charities |
| Marital deduction | Property passing to surviving spouse |
| State death taxes | Estate, inheritance taxes paid |

## Tax Calculation

### Tax Rates
- Graduated rates up to 40%
- Tax calculated on taxable estate
- Credits applied to reduce tax

### Applicable Credit
- Credit equals tax on applicable exclusion amount
- For 2025: $5,541,800 credit (covers $13,990,000)
- Reduces credit previously used for gift tax

## Portability

### Deceased Spousal Unused Exclusion (DSUE)
- Surviving spouse can use decedent's unused exclusion
- Must elect on timely-filed Form 706
- Added to surviving spouse's basic exclusion

### Example
If decedent used $4,000,000 of $13,990,000 exclusion:
- DSUE = $9,990,000
- Surviving spouse's total exclusion = $13,990,000 + $9,990,000 = $23,980,000

### Extended Portability Election
Rev. Proc. 2022-32 allows filing Form 706 for portability up to 5 years after death (for estates without filing requirement).

## Consistent Basis Reporting

### Form 8971 Requirement
Executors must report to IRS and beneficiaries:
- Property transferred from estate
- Final estate tax value of property
- Basis for beneficiary's future use

### Timeline
- File within 30 days after Form 706 due date or filing, whichever is earlier
- Furnish Schedule A to each beneficiary

## Generation-Skipping Transfer Tax

Additional tax on transfers to "skip persons" (grandchildren and others two or more generations below):
- GST exemption = basic exclusion amount
- Rates same as estate tax
- Three types: Direct skip, taxable distribution, taxable termination

## Estate Tax Deduction for Income Tax

Beneficiaries receiving IRD can deduct estate tax paid on that income:
- Itemized deduction on Schedule A
- Must be claimed in same year IRD is reported
