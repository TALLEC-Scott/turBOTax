# Separate Limit Income Categories

## Overview

For foreign tax credit purposes, foreign income is divided into separate categories (often called "baskets"). The FTC limitation is calculated separately for each category, preventing taxpayers from using excess credits from high-tax income to offset U.S. tax on low-tax income.

## Why Separate Categories?

Without separate categories, taxpayers could:
- Use excess credits from high-tax passive income
- To offset U.S. tax on lower-tax active business income

Separate categories ensure credits match the income that generated them.

## The Categories

| Category | IRC Section | Description |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Section 951A Category** | 904(d)(2)(A)(i) | GILTI inclusions from CFCs |
| **Passive Category** | 904(d)(2)(A)(ii) | Dividends, interest, rents, royalties |
| **Foreign Branch Category** | 904(d)(2)(A)(v) | Business profits from foreign branches |
| **General Category** | 904(d)(2)(A)(iv) | All other foreign source income |
| **Section 901(j) Income** | 904(d)(2)(A)(iii) | Income from sanctioned countries |
| **Re-Sourced by Treaty** | 904(d)(2)(A)(vi) | Income re-sourced under treaty |
| **Lump-Sum Distributions** | 904(d)(2)(A)(vii) | Foreign retirement plan LSDs |

## 1. Section 951A Category Income

[[GILTI]] inclusions from [[Controlled Foreign Corporation|CFCs]]:

- U.S. shareholder's aggregate pro rata share of CFC tested income
- Less tested losses and net deemed tangible income return
- **No carrybacks or carryovers** allowed for this category
- Report on separate Form 1116 or Form 1118

## 2. Passive Category Income

See [[Passive Category Income]] for details.

Includes:
- Dividends, interest, rents, royalties
- Annuities
- Net gains from investment property
- Commodities gains (except hedging/active business)

**Does NOT include:**
- High-taxed income (reclassified as general category)
- Export financing interest
- Active business rents and royalties
- Financial services income (for financial services entities)

## 3. Foreign Branch Category Income

Business profits of a U.S. person attributable to one or more QBUs in foreign countries.

**Does NOT include:**
- Passive category income
- Section 951A category income

See IRC section 904(d)(2)(J) and Regulations section 1.904-4(f).

## 4. General Category Income

The "catch-all" category for income not in other categories:

- Active business income
- Wages, salaries, overseas allowances
- High-taxed income (that would otherwise be passive)
- Financial services income (for financial services entities)

### Financial Services Income
Treated as general category if derived by a financial services entity:
- Predominantly engaged in banking, insurance, financing, or similar business
- Income from active conduct of such business

## 5. Section 901(j) Income

Income from activities in sanctioned countries:
- Separate Form 1116 required for each sanctioned country
- No FTC allowed for taxes paid to sanctioned countries (must be deducted instead)
- See Taxes Imposed by Sanctioned Countries in Pub 514

## 6. Certain Income Re-Sourced by Treaty

When a treaty provides that certain U.S. source income is treated as foreign source:
- Separate limitation required
- Use separate Form 1116 for each treaty country's re-sourced income
- Exception: Relief from double taxation rules for U.S. citizens resident in treaty country

## 7. Lump-Sum Distributions (LSDs)

Foreign source LSDs from retirement plans:
- Special computation required
- Use worksheet in Form 1116 instructions
- Elect by filing Form 4972

## Special Allocation Rules

### Income from CFCs

| Income Type | Category Treatment |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Subpart F income | Same category as at CFC level |
| Interest/rents/royalties | Passive if attributable to CFC passive income |
| Dividends | Passive proportionate to passive E&P |

### Partnership Distributive Share

- Generally treated as same category as partnership's income
- Partners owning <10% interest: generally passive income
- See Regulations section 1.904-4(n)

## Allocating Foreign Taxes

If foreign tax relates to multiple categories:

```
Tax allocated to category = Total Foreign Tax × (Net Income in Category ÷ Total Net Income)
```

**Net Income** = Gross income minus expenses definitely related under foreign law.

## Form 1116 Requirements

File **separate Form 1116** for each category for which you claim FTC:
- Check appropriate box for category
- Report income and taxes by country
- Calculate separate limitation
