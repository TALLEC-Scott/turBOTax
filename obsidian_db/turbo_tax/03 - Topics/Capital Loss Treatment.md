# Capital Loss Treatment

## Overview

Capital losses can offset capital gains and, subject to limits, ordinary income. Understanding the rules for deducting and carrying over losses is essential for tax planning.

## Annual Deduction Limit

| Filing Status | Maximum Capital Loss Deduction |
|---------------|-------------------------------|
| Single, MFJ, QSS, HoH | $3,000 |
| Married Filing Separately | $1,500 |

## Calculation Process

### Step 1: Separate Short and Long-Term
- Combine all short-term gains and losses
- Combine all long-term gains and losses

### Step 2: Net Each Category
- Net short-term capital gain or loss
- Net long-term capital gain or loss

### Step 3: Combine Results
- If both gains: Both taxed at respective rates
- If both losses: Deduct up to limit, carry over remainder
- If one gain, one loss: Net them together

## Loss Ordering Rules

1. **Short-term losses used first** against the $3,000 limit
2. **Long-term losses used second**
3. Long-term losses reduce long-term gains before short-term gains

## Capital Loss Carryover

### When Carryover Occurs
- Net loss exceeds annual limit ($3,000/$1,500), OR
- Taxable income is less than zero

### Character Retention
- Short-term losses remain short-term
- Long-term losses remain long-term
- Carry forward indefinitely until used

### Example

2025 capital transactions:
- Net capital loss: $7,000

**2025 Deduction:** $3,000
**Carryover to 2026:** $4,000 (retains character)

## Wash Sale Rules

Losses are disallowed if you buy substantially identical securities:
- 30 days before the sale, OR
- 30 days after the sale

Disallowed loss adds to basis of replacement shares.

## Personal-Use Property

- Losses are **NOT deductible**
- Exception: Casualty or theft losses
- Gain IS taxable

## Nonbusiness Bad Debts

- Treated as short-term capital losses
- Must be completely worthless
- Deducted in year debt becomes worthless
