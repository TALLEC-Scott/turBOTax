# Standard Deduction for Seniors

## Overview

Taxpayers age 65 or older at the end of the tax year are entitled to a higher standard deduction. This additional amount is added to the regular standard deduction based on filing status. The higher standard deduction reduces taxable income for seniors who do not itemize deductions.

## Key Rules

### Age 65 Requirement
- You are considered age 65 on the day **before** your 65th birthday
- For 2025, you can take the higher standard deduction if born before January 2, 1961
- If preparing a return for a deceased taxpayer, they are considered age 65+ only if they were 65+ at time of death

### 2025 Standard Deduction Amounts

| Filing Status | Base Amount | Additional (65+ or Blind) |
|---------------|-------------|---------------------------|
| Single | $15,750 | $2,000 |
| Married filing jointly | $31,500 | $1,600 per spouse |
| Married filing separately | $15,750 | $1,600 |
| Head of household | $23,625 | $2,000 |
| Qualifying surviving spouse | $31,500 | $1,600 |

### Example Calculation
Jean and Terry file jointly for 2025. Both are over 65, neither is blind:
- Base standard deduction: $31,500
- Additional for both spouses over 65: $3,200 ($1,600 × 2)
- **Total standard deduction: $34,700**

## Higher Standard Deduction for Blindness

If you are blind on the last day of the year and don't itemize, you get an additional standard deduction amount (same as for age 65+).

### Blindness Definition (if not totally blind)
A certified statement from an eye doctor must confirm:
- You can't see better than 20/200 in the better eye with glasses/contacts, OR
- Your field of vision isn't more than 20 degrees

## Spouse Age 65+ or Blind

You can claim the higher standard deduction for your spouse if:
- You file a joint return, OR
- You file separately and your spouse had no gross income and can't be claimed as a dependent by another taxpayer

## Standard Deduction for Dependents

If you can be claimed as a dependent on another's return, your standard deduction is limited to the greater of:
- $1,350, OR
- Your earned income plus $450 (up to the regular standard deduction)

This limit may be higher if you are age 65+ or blind.

## Persons Not Eligible for Standard Deduction

You must itemize deductions if:
- Married filing separately and spouse itemizes
- Filing for a short tax year (change in accounting period)
- Nonresident or dual-status alien during the year

**Exception**: Nonresident aliens married to U.S. citizens/residents can choose to be treated as U.S. residents and take the standard deduction.
