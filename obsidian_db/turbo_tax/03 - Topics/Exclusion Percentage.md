# Exclusion Percentage

## Overview

The **exclusion percentage** is the ratio used under the [[General Rule]] to determine how much of each annuity payment is tax-free. It represents the proportion of your payments that represents a return of your investment.

## Formula

```
                      Investment in the Contract
Exclusion Percentage = ─────────────────────────
                           Expected Return
```

Round to three decimal places (e.g., 45.0% or 51.7%).

## Application

Once calculated, the exclusion percentage:

1. **Remains constant** - The same percentage applies to all payments until your net cost is recovered
2. **Applies to each payment** - Multiply by each periodic payment to find the tax-free portion
3. **Carries to survivors** - The same percentage applies to survivor annuity payments

## Calculating Tax-Free Amount

```
Tax-Free Amount = Exclusion Percentage × Periodic Payment
```

**Example**: Exclusion percentage of 45%, monthly payment of $100:
- Tax-free portion = $45 per payment
- Taxable portion = $55 per payment

## Annual Tax-Free Calculation

```
Annual Tax-Free = Exclusion Percentage × First Regular Payment × Number of Payments
```

**Part-year payments**: If you receive payments for only part of a year, apply the percentage to the first regular payment and multiply by the number of payments received.

## Exclusion Limits

### After 1986 Annuity Starting Date

The total amount of annuity income that you can exclude over the years **cannot exceed your net cost** (figured without reduction for refund feature).

If your annuity starting date is after July 1, 1986:
- Any unrecovered net cost at death is allowed as an **other itemized deduction** on the final return
- The deduction is not subject to the 2% floor on miscellaneous itemized deductions

### Cost-of-Living Increases

The tax-free amount remains the same even if payments increase due to cost-of-living adjustments. All increases are **fully taxable**.

**Example**: Original annuity $147/month, exclusion 22.5%. Tax-free = $33.08. If payment increases to $166/month due to COLA, the tax-free portion remains $33.08. The $19 increase is fully taxable.

## Variable Annuities

For [[Variable Annuities]], a different calculation applies:
- Divide investment in contract by total expected number of payments
- Each payment has a fixed tax-free dollar amount rather than a percentage

## Examples

**Example 1**: Investment = $10,800, Expected Return = $24,000. Exclusion percentage = 45%. Monthly payment $100 = $45 tax-free, $55 taxable each month.

**Example 2**: Investment = $62,712, Expected Return = $121,200. Exclusion percentage = 51.7%. Annual payment $6,000 = $3,102 tax-free, $2,898 taxable.

**Example 3**: Surviving spouse with same contract. The 51.7% exclusion percentage carries over. Spouse's annual payment $4,200 = $2,171.40 tax-free, $2,028.60 taxable.
