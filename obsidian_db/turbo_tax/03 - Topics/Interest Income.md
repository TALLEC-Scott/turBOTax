# Interest Income

Interest income is generally taxable unless specifically exempt. Understanding what types of interest are taxable and how to report them is essential for accurate tax filing.

## Types of Interest

### Taxable Interest
- Bank accounts (checking, savings, CDs)
- Money market accounts
- Bonds (corporate, some government)
- Loans you make to others
- Original Issue Discount (OID)
- Treasury bills, notes, and bonds

### Tax-Exempt Interest
- Municipal bonds
- State and local government bonds
- Certain U.S. territories bonds
- Interest on certain private activity bonds

*Note: Tax-exempt interest may still be subject to Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)*

## Reporting Interest Income

### Form 1099-INT
Received from each payer who paid $10 or more in interest.
- Box 1: Interest income
- Box 3: Interest on U.S. savings bonds and Treasury obligations
- Box 8: Tax-exempt interest

### Schedule B Required If:
- Taxable interest exceeds $1,500, OR
- Claiming education savings bond exclusion, OR
- Received interest as a nominee, OR
- Certain other special situations

### Where to Report
- Form 1040, Line 2a: Tax-exempt interest
- Form 1040, Line 2b: Taxable interest

## U.S. Savings Bonds

### Series EE and I Bonds
- Interest is taxable when bonds are cashed OR when they mature
- Can elect to report interest annually (file Form 8815)

### Education Savings Bond Program
Interest may be excluded if used for qualified higher education expenses:
- Must be Series EE or I bond issued after 1989
- Purchured by someone age 24+ before issue date
- Phaseout applies based on MAGI:
  - Single: Begins at $91,850 (2025)
  - MFJ: Begins at $137,350 (2025)

## U.S. Treasury Obligations

### Treasury Bills (T-bills)
- Short-term (4-52 weeks)
- Issued at discount; difference is interest
- Taxable at maturity
- **Exempt from state and local taxes**

### Treasury Notes and Bonds
- Longer maturities (2-30 years)
- Pay interest every 6 months
- **Exempt from state and local taxes**

## Original Issue Discount (OID)

OID occurs when a bond is issued for less than its face value.

### Rules
- Must include OID in income as it accrues
- Report annually even if no payment received
- De minimis rule: OID less than 0.25% × years to maturity × face value = ignore

### Form 1099-OID
Reports OID amounts for the year.

## Interest on State and Local Government Bonds

### Generally Tax-Exempt
Interest on state and municipal bonds is exempt from federal income tax.

### AMT Consideration
- Private activity bonds may be subject to AMT
- Reported on Form 6251

### State Tax
- May be taxable by your state
- Check state rules

## When to Report

### Cash Method Taxpayers (Most Individuals)
Report interest in the year actually or constructively received.

### Accrual Method Taxpayers
Report interest when earned, regardless of when received.

## Interest Income Limits for Dependents

If claimed as a dependent:
- Unearned income (including interest) over $1,350 may require filing
- Different thresholds for children under 19 or students under 24
- See "kiddie tax" rules (Form 8615)
