# Earned Income Credit for Clergy

## Overview

The Earned Income Credit (EIC) is a refundable tax credit for low-to-moderate income workers. Ministers and religious workers may qualify for EIC, but special rules apply when calculating earned income, especially for those with SE tax exemptions.

## 2025 Income Limits

You CANNOT claim EIC if earned income (or AGI) equals or exceeds:

| Filing Status | No Children | One Child | Two Children | Three+ Children |
|---------------|-------------|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Single/Head of Household | $19,104 | $50,434 | $57,310 | $61,555 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $26,214 | $57,554 | $64,430 | $68,675 |

## Definition of Earned Income for Clergy

### Wages and Salaries

Include even if exempt from FICA/SECA under approved [[Form 4029]] or [[Form 4361]]:
- Wages, salaries, tips
- Other taxable employee compensation

### Net Earnings from Self-Employment

Include if NOT exempt from SECA:
- Amount from [[Schedule SE]], line 3
- Subtract deductible part of SE tax ([[Schedule 1]], line 15)
- Add amounts from Schedule SE lines 4b and 5a

### Minister with Form 4361 Exemption

If you have an approved [[Form 4361]]:

| Include | Do NOT Include |
|---------|----------------|
| Wages earned as employee | Fees for marriages, baptisms |
| Salaries | Honoraria for speeches |
| | Other nonemployee ministerial duties |

**Note:** Nonemployee ministerial income is not counted as earned income for EIC purposes when you have a Form 4361 exemption.

## Calculating Earned Income

### For Ministers Without Exemption

1. Start with wages from Form W-2, box 1
2. Add net profit from [[Schedule C]], line 31
3. Add parsonage allowance (for SE purposes)
4. Subtract deductible SE tax portion
5. Make any Schedule SE adjustments

### For Ministers With Form 4361

1. Include wages and salaries as employee
2. Do NOT include nonemployee ministerial fees
3. Include any non-ministerial self-employment income

### For Members of Recognized Religious Sects (Form 4029)

1. Include wages (even though FICA-exempt)
2. Include any non-exempt self-employment income

## EIC and SE Tax Exemption Interaction

| Status | Earned Income Includes |
|--------|----------------------|
| No exemption | All ministerial income (wages + self-employment) |
| Form 4361 approved | Wages only; exclude nonemployee ministerial fees |
| Form 4029 approved | Wages (even FICA-exempt) + non-exempt SE income |

## Special Considerations

### Parsonage Allowance

For EIC purposes:
- Tax-free parsonage allowance is NOT earned income
- But may affect AGI calculations

### Foreign Earned Income

If excluding foreign earned income:
- EIC calculation becomes more complex
- May reduce or eliminate EIC eligibility

### Filing Requirements

To claim EIC:
- Must file Form 1040 or 1040-SR
- Cannot file Form 1040-SR if claiming EIC with qualifying child (must file Schedule EIC)

## Investment Income Limit

Cannot claim EIC if investment income exceeds $11,600 (2025).

Investment income includes:
- Taxable interest
- Tax-exempt interest
- Dividends
- Capital gains
- Passive income

## Qualifying Child Rules

To be a qualifying child for EIC, the child must meet:

1. **Relationship test**: Son, daughter, stepchild, foster child, sibling, or descendant
2. **Age test**: Under 19 (under 24 if student), or any age if disabled
3. **Residency test**: Lived with you in US for more than half the year
4. **Joint return test**: Cannot file joint return (except to claim refund)

## Filing for EIC

### With Qualifying Child
- File Schedule EIC with return
- Provide child's SSN

### Without Qualifying Child
- Claim directly on Form 1040
- Must be at least age 25 but under 65
- Cannot be dependent of another

## IRS Can Calculate EIC

You can ask IRS to calculate your EIC if:
1. You file Form 1040 or 1040-SR
2. You complete the EIC portion up through line for credit amount
3. You write "EIC" on the dotted line next to the credit line
4. You attach Schedule EIC if you have qualifying child
