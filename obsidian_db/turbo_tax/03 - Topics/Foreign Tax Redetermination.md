# Foreign Tax Redetermination

## Overview

A foreign tax redetermination is a change in your foreign tax liability that may require redetermination of your U.S. tax liability. Understanding these rules is essential for properly reporting foreign tax credit changes.

## What Triggers a Redetermination

A redetermination occurs when any of these conditions exist:

| Condition | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Accrued vs. Paid Difference** | Accrued taxes, when paid, differ from amount claimed |
| **Unpaid Accrued Taxes** | Accrued taxes not paid within 24 months after year-end |
| **Foreign Tax Refund** | Foreign taxes refunded in whole or part |
| **Election Change** | Changed from deduction to credit or vice versa |
| **Section 951/951A/1293 Changes** | Changes affecting distributions/inclusions |
| **Exchange Rate Fluctuation** | Dollar value differs between accrual and payment dates |

## When Redetermination Is Required

You **must** redetermine U.S. tax if the foreign tax redetermination changes the amount of U.S. tax due for any tax year.

### De Minimis Exchange Rate Exception
No redetermination required for exchange rate changes if:
- Change is solely attributable to exchange rate fluctuations, AND
- Change is less than the **smaller of**:
  - $10,000, OR
  - 2% of total foreign tax initially accrued for that country

## Notification Requirements

### If U.S. Tax Changes

**File:**
1. **Form 1040-X** (Amended Return) for each affected year
2. **Revised Form 1116** for each affected year
3. **Statement** with required information (see below)

**Additionally:**
- File **Schedule C (Form 1116)** with current-year return summarizing redeterminations

### If U.S. Tax Does NOT Change

You may simply attach **Schedule C (Form 1116)** to your original return for the year in which the redetermination occurs.

### Exceptions

No Form 1116 attachment required if:
1. Foreign taxes are ≤ $300 ($600 if married filing jointly) after redetermination
2. You meet exemption requirements from FTC limit

## Contents of Required Statement

Your statement must include:

1. Your name, address, and taxpayer identification number
2. Tax year(s) affected by redetermination
3. Date(s) foreign taxes were accrued (if applicable)
4. Date(s) foreign taxes were paid
5. Amount of foreign taxes paid/accrued (in foreign currency) and exchange rates used
6. Information for interest calculations:
   - Amount of any interest paid by foreign government
   - Dates received

**For unpaid accrued taxes:**
- Amount in foreign currency
- Exchange rate used when originally claimed

**For refunded taxes:**
- Date and amount (in foreign currency) of each refund
- Exchange rate when originally claimed
- Exchange rate on refund date (for currency gain/loss under section 988)

## Due Date for Notification

| Situation | Due Date |
|-----------|----------|
| **Paid less than claimed** | Due date (with extensions) of return for year redetermination occurred |
| **Paid more than claimed** | 10 years to file claim for refund |

### Multiple Redeterminations

If multiple redeterminations for same year occur within 2 consecutive years:
- File **one notification** reflecting all redeterminations
- Due date depends on whether total increases or decreases U.S. tax

### Carryover/Carryback Eliminates Tax

If additional U.S. tax would result but is eliminated by carryback/carryover of unused foreign tax:
- Don't amend affected year
- Attach Schedule C (Form 1116) to current-year return instead

## Failure-to-Notify Penalty

If you fail to notify the IRS without reasonable cause:
- **5% of tax due** per month (or part of month)
- Maximum: **25%** of tax due

## Foreign Tax Refunds

### Without Interest
If refund has no interest from foreign government:
- No interest owed to IRS for period before refund date

### With Interest
If refund includes interest:
- Pay interest to IRS up to amount received from foreign government
- Interest also owed from refund receipt until tax payment

### Foreign Tax on Refund
If foreign country taxes your refund:
- Cannot take separate credit/deduction for this tax
- Reduce refund amount by foreign tax paid when refiguring credit

**Example:**
- Paid foreign tax: $3,000 (2023)
- Foreign refund: $500 (2025)
- Foreign tax on refund: $100
- Reduce credit by $400 ($500 - $100)

## Time Limit on Refund Claims

You have **10 years** to file a refund claim based on:
- Paying more foreign tax than originally claimed
- Math errors in calculating foreign taxes
- Correcting foreign tax credit limit

The 10-year period begins the day after the regular due date (without extensions) for the year taxes were paid or accrued.
