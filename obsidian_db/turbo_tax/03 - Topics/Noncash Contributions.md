# Noncash Contributions

Noncash contributions are donations of property to qualified organizations. The deduction amount and substantiation requirements depend on the type and value of property donated.

## Determining Deduction Amount

### Ordinary Income Property

**Deduction:** Lesser of Fair Market Value (FMV) or your basis

**Includes:**
- Inventory from your business
- Property held primarily for sale
- Artwork, books, or other property created by you
- Capital assets held one year or less

### Capital Gain Property

**Deduction:** Fair Market Value (FMV)

**Requirements:**
- Held more than one year
- Would produce long-term capital gain if sold
- Not subject to recapture rules

**Examples:**
- Stocks and bonds held long-term
- Real estate held for investment
- Collectibles (special rules apply)

### Property That Has Decreased in Value

**Deduction:** Fair Market Value (FMV)

You cannot deduct a loss on donated property. If FMV is less than basis, your deduction is limited to FMV.

## Special Property Types

### Clothing and Household Items

**Requirements:**
- Must be in **good used condition or better**
- Deduction denied for items not in good condition (exception: items worth over $500 with qualified appraisal)

**Household items include:**
- Furniture, furnishings, electronics
- Appliances, linens
- Kitchenware, utensils

**Documentation:**
- Receipt from organization
- Description and condition of items
- FMV and method used to determine

### Vehicles (Cars, Boats, Airplanes)

**Deduction depends on what charity does with vehicle:**

| Charity Action | Your Deduction |
|----------------|----------------|
| Sells vehicle | Gross proceeds from sale (Form 1098-C) |
| Uses vehicle for exempt purpose | FMV (with written statement from charity) |
| Gives/sells to needy individual | FMV (with written statement) |
| Sells for $500 or less | Lesser of $500 or FMV |

See [[Vehicle Donations]] for comprehensive rules.

### Securities

**Publicly Traded Securities:**
- Deduction = FMV on date of contribution
- Use average of high and low prices
- No appraisal required

**Nonpublicly Traded Securities:**
- FMV determined by qualified appraisal (if over $5,000)
- Consider minority discounts, marketability issues

### Real Estate

**Bargain Sale:** If you sell property to charity for less than FMV:
1. Calculate FMV of contributed part (FMV - sale price)
2. Allocate basis proportionally
3. May recognize gain on sale portion

**Partial Interest:**
- Generally not deductible
- Exception: Remainder interest in personal home or farm
- Exception: Qualified conservation contribution

### Intellectual Property

**Includes:** Patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, software

**Initial Deduction:** Lesser of basis or FMV

**Additional Deduction:** May deduct percentage of income from property for up to 10 years:

| Years After Donation | Deductible % |
|---------------------|--------------|
| Years 1-2 | 100% |
| Year 3 | 90% |
| Year 4 | 80% |
| Year 5 | 70% |
| Year 6 | 60% |
| Year 7 | 50% |
| Year 8 | 40% |
| Year 9 | 30% |
| Year 10 | 20% |
| Years 11-12 | 10% |

### Food Inventory

**Special Rule for Businesses:**
- Deduct FMV even if exceeds basis
- Must be wholesome food
- From any trade or business

## Substantiation Requirements

### By Deduction Amount

| Deduction Amount | Required Documentation |
|------------------|----------------------|
| Under $250 | Receipt showing organization, date, description |
| $250 - $500 | Written acknowledgment (CWA) |
| $500 - $5,000 | Form 8283, Section A + CWA |
| Over $5,000 | Form 8283, Section B + CWA + Qualified Appraisal |
| Over $500,000 | Attach appraisal to return |

### Qualified Appraisal Requirements

For deductions over $5,000 (unless exception applies):

**Required:**
- Prepared by qualified appraiser
- Valuation date within 60 days before contribution to contribution date
- Includes appraiser declaration, signature, date
- Meets USPAP standards

**Exceptions (no appraisal needed):**
- Publicly traded securities
- Qualified vehicles with CWA from charity
- Certain inventory
- Intellectual property

See [[Form 8283]] for filing requirements.

## Deduction Limits

| Property Type | Organization Type | Limit |
|---------------|-------------------|-------|
| Noncash (ordinary income) | 50% limit org | 50% of AGI |
| Capital gain property (FMV) | 50% limit org | 30% of AGI |
| Capital gain property (basis) | 50% limit org | 50% of AGI |
| Capital gain property (FMV) | 30% limit org | 20% of AGI |

See [[Charitable Contribution Limits]] for detailed rules.
