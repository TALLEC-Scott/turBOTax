# Foreclosures and Repossessions

## Overview

If you do not make payments on a loan secured by property, the lender may foreclose or repossess the property. This is treated as a sale or exchange from which you may realize gain, loss, or ordinary income from cancellation of debt.

## Amount Realized

### Nonrecourse Debt
If you are NOT personally liable for the debt:
- Amount realized = Full debt canceled by transfer
- Includes debt even if FMV of property is less

### Recourse Debt
If you ARE personally liable for the debt:
- Amount realized = Lesser of:
  - Outstanding debt reduced by any remaining liability, OR
  - FMV of transferred property

## Cancellation of Debt Income

When debt exceeds FMV on foreclosure with recourse debt:
- Ordinary income = Canceled debt - FMV of property
- This is separate from any gain or loss on disposition

### Exceptions to COD Income
- Cancellation intended as a gift
- Qualified real property business debt
- Qualified farm debt
- Insolvency or bankruptcy
- Qualified principal residence indebtedness

## Calculating Gain or Loss

| Situation | Calculation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Gain | Amount realized - Adjusted basis |
| Loss | Adjusted basis - Amount realized |
| COD Income (recourse) | Debt canceled - FMV of property |
