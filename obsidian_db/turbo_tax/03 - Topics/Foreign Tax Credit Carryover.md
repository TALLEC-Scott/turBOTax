# Foreign Tax Credit Carryover

## Overview

When the foreign tax credit limit prevents you from claiming the full amount of qualified foreign taxes in a tax year, you may carry the unused foreign taxes to other years. This ensures you don't lose the benefit of foreign taxes paid in high-tax years.

## Carryback and Carryforward Rules

| Rule | Period | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Carryback** | 1 year | To first preceding tax year |
| **Carryforward** | 10 years | To the 10 succeeding tax years |

**Exception:** No carrybacks or carryovers allowed for Section 951A ([[GILTI]]) category income.

## Calculating Unused Foreign Tax

**Unused foreign tax** = Qualified foreign taxes paid or accrued − Credit limit for that category

**Excess limit** = Credit limit − Qualified foreign taxes paid or accrued

Both are calculated **separately for each separate limit income category**.

## Carryover Mechanics

### Order of Application
1. **First** carry back to the preceding tax year (if excess limit exists)
2. **Then** carry forward to the next 10 tax years (up to excess limit in each year)
3. Use on a **first-in, first-out (FIFO)** basis

### Example: Carryback and Carryforward

| Year | Limit | Tax Paid | Unused (+) / Excess (-) |
|------|-------|----------|-------------------------|
| 2024 | $200 | $100 | -$100 (excess limit) |
| 2025 | $300 | $500 | +$200 (unused) |

- 2025 unused foreign tax: $200
- Carry back to 2024: Up to $100 (excess limit)
- Carry forward remaining: $100

## Tracking Carryovers

### Schedule B (Form 1116)
Use **Schedule B (Form 1116)** to:
- Reconcile prior-year carryover with current-year carryover
- Track unused foreign taxes by category
- Report carryovers absorbed in current year

### Separate Accounts Required
Maintain separate accounts for:
- Each separate limit income category
- Each tax year's unused taxes
- Carryovers from different years

## Special Rules

### Pre-2018 Unused Foreign Taxes
Unused foreign taxes from pre-2018 general category income:
- Generally allocated to post-2017 general category income
- May alternatively allocate to foreign branch category income
- Simplified safe harbor available (Regulations section 1.904-2(j)(1)(iii))

### Bankruptcy or Insolvency
If debts are canceled due to bankruptcy or insolvency:
- Reduce unused FTC carryovers by 33⅓ cents per $1 of canceled debt excluded from income
- No carryback allowed to year before bankruptcy case began
- See [[Pub 908]] (Bankruptcy Tax Guide) for details

## Allocation Between Spouses

### Situations Requiring Allocation

1. **Joint → Separate Returns:** Separate returns in current year; joint return in carryover year
2. **Separate → Joint → Separate:** Separate returns, through a joint return year, to separate current year
3. **Joint → Separate → Joint:** Joint return in current year; separate returns in intervening year

### Allocation Method

1. **Calculate percentage** for each spouse by dividing their foreign source taxable income by joint foreign source taxable income
2. **Apply percentage** to joint foreign tax credit limit
3. **Determine unused tax or excess limit** for each spouse by comparing allocated limit with their foreign taxes

### Example: Allocation

| Year | Return | Spouse H | Spouse W |
|------|--------|----------|----------|
| 2021 | Joint | $50 unused | $30 unused |
| 2022 | Separate | $25 unused | $20 excess |
| 2023 | Joint | $65 excess | $20 excess |
| 2024 | Joint | $104 unused | $69 unused |
| 2025 | Separate | $50 excess | $10 excess |

## Credit vs. Deduction Interaction

### Taxes All Credited or All Deducted

| Rule | Description |
|------|-------------|
| No carryback/carryover from deduction year | Cannot carry from a year you deducted foreign taxes |
| No deduction of carryover taxes | Carryovers must be credited, not deducted |
| Credit election required | Must claim credit in carryover year |
| No exemption year carryover | Cannot carry to/from years exempt from FTC limit |

### Carryover to Deduction Year

If you carry unused taxes to a year you chose to deduct:
- Compute FTC limit as if you had chosen to credit
- Unused taxes are treated as absorbed to extent of excess limit
- No actual credit or deduction allowed for absorbed amount

## Claiming Carrybacks

To claim a carryback:
1. File **Form 1040-X** for the carryback year
2. Attach revised **Form 1116** for the affected year
3. File within 10 years of the regular due date of the return for the year taxes were paid

## Time Limit on Tax Assessment

When you carry back unused foreign tax:
- IRS gets additional time to assess resulting tax
- Assessment can be made up to 1 year after expiration of statutory period for the year in which carryback originated
