# Schedule EIC (Form 1040)

Schedule EIC is used to provide information about qualifying children for the [[Earned Income Credit|Earned Income Credit (EIC)]].

## Purpose

Schedule EIC is attached to Form 1040 or 1040-SR to report qualifying children when claiming the EIC with a qualifying child.

## When to File

**Required if:**
- You have at least one child who meets the conditions to be your qualifying child for EIC purposes
- You are claiming the EIC

**File even if:**
- Your qualifying child does not have a valid SSN (you may qualify for self-only EIC)

## Information Required

For each qualifying child, provide:

### Line 1: Child's Name
- First name and last name
- Must match Social Security records

### Line 2: Child's SSN
- Must be a valid SSN issued on or before the due date of the return
- ITIN or ATIN does NOT qualify for EIC

### Line 3: Child's Year of Birth
- Enter 4-digit year (e.g., 2015)

### Line 4: Child's Relationship to You
- Son, daughter, stepchild, foster child
- Brother, sister, half-brother, half-sister, stepbrother, stepsister
- Grandchild, niece, nephew

### Line 5: Residency
- Check box (a) if child lived with you in the U.S. for more than half the year
- Check box (b) if the residency test is met
- Also check the box on Form 1040, page 1, to the right of the address block

### Line 6: Months Child Lived with You
- Enter the number of months the child lived with you
- Enter "KC" if child was kidnapped (see special rules)

### Special Checkboxes
- **Full-time student**: Check if child was a full-time student for at least 5 months
- **Permanently and totally disabled**: Check if applicable

## Multiple Qualifying Children

Schedule EIC has space for up to three qualifying children. If you have more than three:
- List the three with the highest EIC benefit
- Attach a statement with information for additional children

## Special Situations

### Child Without Valid SSN
- Still complete Schedule EIC
- Note that child may not qualify for EIC calculation
- You may qualify for self-only EIC

### Kidnapped Child
- Enter "KC" instead of number of months on line 6
- Child is treated as living with you if:
  - Child lived with you for more than half the time before kidnapping
  - Kidnapper is not a family member
  - Law enforcement presumes kidnapping

### Foster Child
- Indicate "foster child" relationship on line 4
- Must have been placed by authorized agency

## Common Errors

- Not attaching Schedule EIC when required
- Incorrect SSN or name not matching SSA records
- Not checking both residency boxes
- Missing the full-time student or disabled checkboxes
- Not checking the Form 1040 box for U.S. residency

## Recordkeeping

Keep documentation to prove:
- Child lived with you (school records, medical records, childcare receipts)
- Child's age (birth certificate)
- Relationship (birth certificate, adoption papers)

The IRS may request these documents to verify EIC eligibility.
