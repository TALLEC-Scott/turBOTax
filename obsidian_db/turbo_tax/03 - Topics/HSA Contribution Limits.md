# HSA Contribution Limits

## Overview

The amount you can contribute to a Health Savings Account (HSA) depends on your coverage type, age, eligibility timing, and certain other factors. This page details the contribution limits and rules for calculating allowable contributions. See [[Pub 969 - Health Savings Accounts and Other Tax-Favored Health Plans]].

## 2025 Contribution Limits

| Coverage Type | Standard Limit | Age 55+ (with Catch-up) |
|--------------|----------------|------------------------|
| Self-Only | $4,300 | $5,300 |
| Family | $8,550 | $9,550 |

## 2026 Contribution Limits

| Coverage Type | Standard Limit | Age 55+ (with Catch-up) |
|--------------|----------------|------------------------|
| Self-Only | $4,400 | $5,400 |
| Family | $8,750 | $9,750 |

## Catch-Up Contributions

Individuals age 55 or older at the end of the tax year may contribute an additional **$1,000** annually.

### Rules for Catch-Up Contributions
- Each spouse must have their own HSA
- Each spouse 55+ can make the catch-up contribution
- Total for married couple (both 55+) with family coverage: **$10,550** (2025)
- Cannot combine with spouse's contribution limit if only one spouse is 55+

## Eligibility-Based Calculation

### Full Year Eligibility
If eligible for the entire year with same coverage type:
- Contribute the full annual limit based on coverage type

### Partial Year Eligibility
If not eligible for entire year or changed coverage during year, use the **greater of**:

1. **Monthly limitation method** - Use Line 3 Limitation Chart and Worksheet in Form 8889 Instructions, OR
2. **Last-month rule** - Maximum annual HSA contribution based on coverage on December 1

### Last-Month Rule Calculation
If you have family HDHP coverage on December 1:
- Your limit is $8,550 (for 2025) even if coverage changed during year

**Warning:** Subject to testing period requirements. See [[Last-Month Rule]].

## Reduction of Contribution Limit

### Archer MSA Contributions
Must reduce HSA contribution limit by any Archer MSA contributions (including employer contributions) for the year.

### Employer Contributions
Must reduce personal contribution limit by:
- Employer HSA contributions excludable from income
- Employer contributions through cafeteria plan (salary reduction)

### Qualified HSA Funding Distributions
Must reduce HSA contribution limit by any qualified HSA funding distribution from an IRA.

## Married Taxpayers - Special Rules

### Both Spouses with Family Coverage Under Separate Plans
If each spouse has family coverage under a separate HDHP:
- Combined limit is $8,550 (2025)
- Must reduce by both spouses' Archer MSA contributions
- Split equally unless spouses agree on different division

### Both Spouses 55+
If both are eligible individuals age 55+:
- Each can contribute catch-up to own HSA
- Total family limit: $10,550 (2025)
- Each spouse contributes catch-up to their own HSA (not combined)

### Example
Husband and wife, both 58, each have family HDHP coverage through separate employers:
- Total combined limit: $8,550 (family) + $2,000 (two catch-ups) = $10,550
- Each spouse can contribute $5,275 to their own HSA (unless different division agreed)

## Medicare Enrollment Impact

Beginning with the first month enrolled in Medicare:
- Contribution limit becomes **ZERO**
- Applies to periods of retroactive Medicare coverage

### Example
You turned 65 in July 2025 and enrolled in Medicare. You had self-only HDHP coverage and are eligible for catch-up contribution:
- Contribution limit: $5,300 × 6 ÷ 12 = **$2,650** (January-June only)

## Contribution Deadline

- Contributions can be made through April 15 of the following year
- For 2025: Contributions can be made through April 15, 2026
- Employer contributions can also be made January 1 - April 15 for prior year

## Qualified HSA Funding Distribution

One-time transfer from IRA to HSA:
- Not included in income
- Not deductible
- Reduces contribution limit
- Cannot be from ongoing SEP IRA or SIMPLE IRA
- Limited to contribution limit based on coverage type on first of month
- Multiple transfers allowed only if coverage changes from self-only to family

## Excess Contributions

### Definition
Contributions exceeding the allowable limit.

### Tax Consequences
- 6% excise tax per year excess remains in account
- Excess contributions not deductible

### Avoiding Excise Tax
Withdraw excess contributions by:
1. Due date (including extensions) of return
2. Withdraw earnings on excess
3. Report earnings as "Other income"

### Deducting Excess in Later Years
Can deduct prior excess contributions in current year:
- Lesser of: (Maximum limit - current contributions) OR (Prior excess remaining)
