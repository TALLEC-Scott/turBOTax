# Business Use of Home

## Overview

The home office deduction allows business owners to deduct expenses for the business use of their home. Per [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]] and [[Pub 587 - Business Use of Your Home]], you must meet specific tests to qualify.

The deduction is calculated on [[Form 8829]] and flows to [[Schedule C (Form 1040)]], Line 30.

## Qualification Tests

### Regular and Exclusive Use Test
The designated area must be used:
- **Regularly:** On a continuing basis, not occasional or incidental
- **Exclusively:** Solely for business purposes (no personal use)

### Place of Business Test
The home must be:
1. **Principal place of business**, OR
2. **Regular place of business** for patients, clients, or customers, OR
3. **Separate structure** not attached to the dwelling unit

## Principal Place of Business

Your home qualifies as principal place of business if:

### Primary Business Location
- You perform the most important business activities there
- Administrative/management activities occur there

### No Other Fixed Location
- You have no other fixed location where you conduct substantial administrative or management activities

**Qualifying Activities:**
- Billing customers
- Keeping books and records
- Ordering supplies
- Setting up appointments
- Writing reports

**Non-Qualifying Activities (if done elsewhere):**
- Meeting with patients/clients at hospitals, offices, etc.
- Minor administrative tasks

## Regular Meeting Place

Even if not your principal place, you may qualify if:
- You meet or deal with patients, clients, or customers at home
- Meetings are regular and continuous
- The use is substantial and integral to your business

## Separate Structure

A structure NOT attached to your dwelling may qualify if:
- Used exclusively for business
- Used regularly for business
- Example: Standalone garage, workshop, studio

## Deductible Expenses

### Direct Expenses (100% Deductible)
Expenses ONLY for the business portion:
- Painting or repairs to business area only
- Dedicated business phone line
- Security system for business area

### Indirect Expenses (Proportional Deduction)
Expenses for the ENTIRE home:
- Mortgage interest
- Real estate taxes
- Rent
- Utilities (electricity, gas, water)
- Homeowner's insurance
- Repairs to entire home
- Security system
- Trash removal

## Calculation Methods

### Regular Method (Form 8829)

#### Step 1: Determine Business Percentage
```
Square footage of business area ÷ Total square footage of home = Business Percentage
```

**Maximum deduction:** 300 square feet (simplified method limit)

#### Step 2: Calculate Deductible Amounts
Multiply indirect expenses by business percentage

#### Step 3: Gross Income Limitation
Home office deduction cannot exceed gross income from the business use of home after deducting:
- Business expenses not attributable to home use
- [[Business Expenses]] deductible regardless of home use

**Carryover:** Excess deduction carries forward to future years

### Simplified Method

- **Standard deduction:** $5 per square foot (max 300 sq ft)
- **Maximum deduction:** $1,500 per year
- No depreciation deduction (or recapture)
- No Form 8829 required
- Report directly on Schedule C, Line 30

**To elect:** Mark the checkbox on Schedule C, Line 30

## Depreciation

Using the regular method, you may depreciate:
- The business portion of your home's basis
- 39-year nonresidential real property (MACRS)
- Land is not depreciable

### Depreciation Recapture
When you sell your home:
- Depreciation allowed or allowable is "recaptured"
- Taxed as "unrecaptured Section 1250 gain" (max 25% rate)
- Cannot exclude from income

## Expenses NOT Deductible

Per [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]]:
- Lawn care and landscaping
- General home repairs not affecting business area
- Loss on sale of home
- Telephone line (first line to residence)

## Employees vs. Self-Employed

### Self-Employed (Schedule C)
- Claim deduction on Schedule C, Line 30
- File Form 8829 (regular method)

### Employees (Form 2106)
- Not deductible as "miscellaneous itemized deduction" after TCJA
- Deduction suspended 2018-2025

## Reporting on Schedule C

- Line 30: Business use of home deduction
- Attach Form 8829 if using regular method

## Common Questions

### Q: Can I deduct a home office if I also have an outside office?
**A:** Generally no, unless the home office is your principal place of business for administrative activities and you don't perform those activities at the outside office.

### Q: What if I use a room for business during the day and family room at night?
**A:** You do NOT qualify. The exclusive use test requires the space be used ONLY for business.

### Q: Should I use the regular or simplified method?
**A:** The simplified method is easier but capped at $1,500. The regular method may yield larger deductions if your business percentage is significant and home expenses are high—but requires depreciation recapture when you sell.
