# Ministers and Church Employees

## Overview

Self-employed ministers and church employees who participate in [[403(b) Plans]] generally follow the same rules as other participants, but with some important modifications for calculating limits and compensation.

## Who Qualifies?

### Ministers

The following ministers are eligible employees for 403(b) purposes:

1. **Ministers employed by section 501(c)(3) organizations** - Direct employment with qualifying tax-exempt organization

2. **Self-employed ministers** - Treated as employed by a tax-exempt organization that is an eligible employer

3. **Chaplains** - Ministers who:
   - Are employed by organizations that are NOT section 501(c)(3) organizations, AND
   - Function as ministers in their day-to-day professional responsibilities

**Examples of Eligible Chaplains**:
- Minister employed as chaplain by state-run prison
- Chaplain in U.S. Armed Forces

### Church Employees

A church employee is anyone who is an employee of:
- A church
- A convention or association of churches
- A tax-exempt organization controlled by or associated with a church

## Contribution Limits

### General Rule

For ministers and church employees, the Maximum Amount Contributable (MAC) is generally the lesser of:
- Your [[Annual Additions Limit]], OR
- Your [[Elective Deferrals|Limit on Elective Deferrals]]

### Annual Additions Limit for Ministers/Church Employees

| Year | Dollar Maximum |
|------|----------------|
| 2025 | $70,000 |
| 2026 | $72,000 |

Or 100% of includible compensation for most recent year of service.

## Alternative Limit for Church Employees

Church employees can choose to use **$10,000 per year** as the limit on annual additions, even if their regular limit would be less.

**Lifetime Limit**: Total contributions under this choice cannot exceed **$40,000**.

## Includible Compensation Special Rules

### Self-Employed Ministers

Your includible compensation equals:
- Net earnings from ministry
- **Minus**: Contributions made to retirement plan on your behalf
- **Minus**: Deductible portion of self-employment tax

### Foreign Missionaries

Your includible compensation **includes** foreign earned income that may otherwise be excludable under section 911.

**Eligibility as Foreign Missionary**:
- Layperson or ordained/commissioned/licensed minister of a church
- Employee of a church or convention/association of churches
- Performing services outside the United States

**Special Rule**: If AGI is $17,000 or less, contributions up to $3,000 won't be treated as exceeding annual additions limit.

## Years of Service Special Rules

### Church Employees

Treat all years of service with related church organizations as service with **one employer**.

### Self-Employed Ministers

Years of service include:
- Full years of self-employment
- Part years of self-employment

## Reporting Contributions

### Self-Employed Ministers

- Report total contributions as a **deduction** on your tax return
- Deduct on Schedule 1 (Form 1040), Line 16

### Chaplains

If your employer doesn't exclude contributions from earned income:
- May take a deduction on your tax return
- Report on Schedule 1 (Form 1040), Line 24g

If employer agrees to exclude contributions from earned income:
- No deduction allowed on tax return

## Who Can Set Up a 403(b) Account?

- Only employers can set up 403(b) accounts
- Self-employed ministers cannot set up their own accounts
- The denomination/organization associated with the minister must establish the account
