# American Opportunity Credit

## Overview

The American Opportunity Credit (AOTC) is a tax credit for qualified education expenses paid for an eligible student. The credit is worth up to **$2,500 per eligible student** per year. Up to **40% ($1,000) may be refundable**, meaning you can receive it even if you owe no tax.

**Authority:** [[Pub 970 - Tax Benefits for Education#Chapter 2]]

## Key Requirements

### Student Requirements
1. Must be pursuing a degree or recognized credential
2. Must be enrolled at least half-time for at least one academic period
3. Must not have completed the first 4 years of postsecondary education
4. Must not have claimed AOTC for this student for more than 4 tax years
5. Must not have a felony drug conviction as of end of tax year
6. Must have valid TIN by due date of return

### Taxpayer Requirements
- Paid qualified education expenses for eligible student
- Student is yourself, your spouse, or a dependent you claim
- MAGI at or below $90,000 ($180,000 if married filing jointly)

## Income Limits (2025)

| Filing Status | Phase-Out Begins | Phase-Out Ends |
|---------------|------------------|----------------|
| Single, HoH, QSS | $80,000 | $90,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $160,000 | $180,000 |

## Qualified Education Expenses

- Tuition and required fees
- Course materials (books, supplies, equipment) - even if not purchased from the institution
- Expenses paid in 2025 for academic periods beginning in 2025 or first 3 months of 2026

## Credit Calculation

| Component | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Maximum Credit | $2,500 per student |
| Nonrefundable Portion | 60% ($1,500) |
| Refundable Portion | 40% (up to $1,000) |

The credit is calculated as:
- 100% of first $2,000 of qualified expenses
- 25% of next $2,000 of qualified expenses

## Form Required

- [[Form 8863]] - Education Credits
- Must provide educational institution's EIN

## Special Rules

### Form 1098-T Requirement
Taxpayers must receive [[Form 1098-T]] from the educational institution. Exceptions exist for certain students.

### Form 8862 Required
If your AOTC was denied or reduced for any reason other than math/clerical error, you must file [[Form 8862]] to claim the credit again.

### Ban on Claiming Credit
- 2-year ban for reckless or intentional disregard of rules
- 10-year ban for fraud

## Coordination with Other Benefits

Cannot claim AOTC for the same student in the same year as:
- [[Lifetime Learning Credit]]

Can coordinate with:
- [[Scholarships and Fellowship Grants]] - May include some scholarship income to maximize credit
- [[Coverdell ESA]] distributions (different expenses)
- [[Qualified Tuition Program]] distributions (different expenses)

### Q: Can I claim AOTC for graduate school?
**A:** No, AOTC is only available for the first 4 years of postsecondary education.

### Q: What if my expenses exceed $4,000?
**A:** The maximum credit is $2,500 per student regardless of expenses above $4,000.

### Q: Can I claim both AOTC and Lifetime Learning Credit?
**A:** Not for the same student in the same year. However, you can claim AOTC for one student and LLC for another in the same year.
