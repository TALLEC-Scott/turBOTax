# Form 5695

## Overview

Form 5695 (Residential Energy Credits) is used to claim tax credits for energy-saving improvements to your home. The form covers both residential clean energy credits and energy efficient home improvement credits.

## Important Expiration

**These credits cannot be claimed for expenditures/property after December 31, 2025.**

## Two Parts of the Form

### Part I: Residential Clean Energy Credit

For qualifying property placed in service 2022-2025:
- Solar electric panels
- Solar water heaters
- Fuel cells
- Small wind energy property
- Geothermal heat pumps
- Battery storage technology

**Credit rate**: 30%
**No lifetime limit**

### Part II: Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit

For qualifying property placed in service through December 31, 2025:
- Insulation materials
- Exterior windows, skylights, doors
- Energy-efficient heating and cooling equipment
- Water heaters
- Biomass stoves

**No lifetime limit**, but annual caps apply per category.

## Home Energy Audit Credit

For 2024 and later, includes a credit for home energy audits performed by a Qualified Home Energy Auditor.

## Eligible Homes

The home must be located in the **United States** and can be:
- House
- Condominium
- Cooperative apartment
- Mobile home
- Houseboat

Must have sleeping, cooking, and toilet facilities.

## Documentation Required

Keep:
- Manufacturer's certification statement
- Receipts and invoices
- Home energy audit report (if applicable)

## Basis Reduction

If you claim a credit, you must **reduce the [[Basis of Home]]** by the credit amount.

## When to File

File Form 5695 with your Form 1040. The credit is part of the nonrefundable credits on Schedule 3.
