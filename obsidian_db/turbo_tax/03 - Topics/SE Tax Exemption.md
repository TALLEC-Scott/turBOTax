# SE Tax Exemption

## Overview

Members of the clergy and members of recognized religious sects may apply for exemption from self-employment (SE) tax and/or Social Security and Medicare taxes. The requirements and forms differ based on the applicant's status.

## Who Can Apply

### Members of the Clergy
- Ministers (ordained, commissioned, or licensed)
- Members of religious orders (without vow of poverty)
- Christian Science practitioners and readers

**Form:** [[Form 4361]]

### Members of Recognized Religious Sects
- Members of a recognized religious sect or division
- Must meet specific eligibility requirements

**Form:** [[Form 4029]]

## Form 4361 - Clergy Exemption

### Eligibility Requirements

To claim exemption, you must meet ALL of the following:

1. **Conscientious opposition** to public insurance based on:
   - Individual religious considerations (not general conscience), OR
   - Principles of your religious denomination

2. **Not for economic reasons** - Must file for non-economic reasons

3. **Tax-exempt organization** - Your ordaining/licensing body or religious order must be a tax-exempt religious organization

4. **Church or convention** - The organization must be a church or convention/association of churches

5. **Notification** - Inform your ordaining, commissioning, or licensing body of your opposition (not required for Christian Science practitioners/readers)

6. **Certification** - Sign and return the statement IRS mails to you

7. **No prior election** - Cannot have made irrevocable elections to be covered under Social Security

### When to File

File [[Form 4361]] by the due date (including extensions) of your income tax return for the **second tax year** in which:

1. You have net earnings from self-employment of at least $400, AND
2. Any part of those earnings was from [[Ministerial Services]]

The 2 years do NOT have to be consecutive.

### Effective Date

An approved exemption is effective for:
- All tax years after 1967
- Any year with $400+ net earnings from self-employment where any part is from ministerial services

**Important:** Once approved, the exemption is irrevocable.

### Example Filing Timeline

| Year | Net SE Earnings | Ministerial Portion | Action |
|------|-----------------|---------------------|--------|
| 2025 | $450 | $450 | First qualifying year |
| 2026 | $500 | $500 | Second qualifying year - File Form 4361 with 2026 return |

## Form 4029 - Religious Sect Exemption

### Eligibility Requirements

ALL of the following must be met:

1. File [[Form 4029]]

2. **Conscientious opposition** to accepting benefits of any private or public insurance for:
   - Death, disability, old age, retirement
   - Medical care or services

3. **Waiver of benefits** - Waive all rights to Social Security payments/benefits and agree no benefits paid to others based on your earnings

4. **Commissioner determination** that:
   - Your sect has the established teachings described above
   - It is the practice (for a substantial period) for members to provide for dependent members reasonably
   - The sect has existed since December 31, 1950

### Exception

Cannot apply if you (or anyone else) received Social Security benefits based on your earnings. May qualify if you pay back benefits received.

### When to File

File [[Form 4029]] at any time. File in triplicate with the Social Security Administration.

### Effective Date

- Effective from first year eligibility requirements are met
- Ends if you fail to meet requirements or sect no longer qualifies
- Must notify IRS within 60 days if no longer a member or following teachings

## Who Cannot Be Exempt

You cannot be exempt if you made irrevocable elections:

1. Elected before 1968 to be covered under Social Security for ministerial services
2. Elected coverage by filing Form 2031 for 1986, 1987, 2000, or 2001 tax year

## Exemption from FICA Taxes

Both employer and employee can be exempt from FICA taxes if both have approved [[Form 4029]].

### For Employers

- Don't report wages as Social Security/Medicare wages
- On Form W-2: Enter "Form 4029" in box 14, leave boxes 3-6 blank
- On Form 941: Check box on line 4, enter "Form 4029" below
- On Form 943: Enter "Form 4029" on dotted line next to lines 2 and 4
- On Form 944: Check box on line 3, enter "Form 4029" below

## Refunds of SE Tax

If you receive approval after paying SE tax, file [[Form 1040-X]] for refund.

**Deadline:** The later of:
- 3 years from date you filed the return, OR
- 2 years from date you paid the tax

## Death of Individual

The right to file for exemption ends with death. Surviving spouses, executors, or administrators cannot file for a deceased clergy member.
