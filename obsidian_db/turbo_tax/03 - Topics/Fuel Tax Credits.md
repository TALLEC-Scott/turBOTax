# Fuel Tax Credits

## Overview

Farmers may claim credits or refunds for federal excise taxes paid on fuel used in farming operations. This includes gasoline, diesel fuel, and other fuel used for farming purposes.

## Eligible Fuel Uses

### Farming Purposes
- Raising, feeding, caring for, or managing livestock, bees, poultry, or wildlife
- Cultivating, raising, or harvesting agricultural or horticultural crops
- Operating farm machinery or equipment
- Drying agricultural products
- Farming purposes include preparation for market

### Qualified Fuel Users
- Farmers
- Farm owners and operators
- Tenants
- Custom harvesters

## Fuel Types

### Gasoline
- Undyed gasoline used for farming
- Claim credit for tax paid

### Diesel Fuel and Kerosene
- Undyed diesel fuel and kerosene
- Dyed fuel is nontaxable and not eligible for credit

### Other Fuels
- Aviation fuel used in farming
- Alternative fuels used in farming operations

## Credit Amount

The credit equals the federal excise tax paid on the fuel:
- Gasoline tax rate (check current rate)
- Diesel fuel tax rate (check current rate)

## How to Claim

### Non-Claimant Method
- Buy undyed diesel or kerosene tax-free using Form 637 registration
- Use for farming purposes

### Claimant Method
- Purchase taxed fuel
- Claim credit on annual return

### Form 4136
File Form 4136 to claim the credit:
- Report gallons of fuel used for farming
- Calculate credit amount
- Attach to income tax return

## Recordkeeping Requirements

Maintain records showing:
- Number of gallons purchased
- Date of purchase
- Name and address of seller
- Price per gallon
- Use of fuel (farming purposes)

## Income Inclusion

If you deducted the full cost of fuel (including excise tax) as an expense, you must include the credit amount in gross income for the year you receive it.
