# Bartering Income

## Overview

Bartering is the exchange of property or services without using money. The fair market value (FMV) of property or services received through bartering is taxable income at the time received.

## General Rule

You must include in income the FMV of property or services you receive through bartering. If you and another person agree on the value of services beforehand, that value is accepted as FMV unless proven otherwise.

## Taxable Amount

### FMV Determination

- Agreed-upon value between parties (unless proven wrong)
- What you would pay a third party for the same property/services
- Value at time received

### Credit Units

Barter clubs often use credit units as currency. You must include in income the value of credits added to your account, even if you don't use them until a later year.

## Reporting Barter Income

### Schedule C (Form 1040)

Most barter income is reported on Schedule C for self-employed individuals.

| Transaction Type | Schedule |
|------------------|----------|
| Services exchanged | Schedule C |
| Goods exchanged | Schedule C |
| Rental property exchanged | Schedule E |
| Investment property exchanged | Schedule D |

### Examples

**Example 1: Services for Stock**
Attorney performs legal services for corporation, receives stock shares as payment. Report FMV of shares on Schedule C.

**Example 2: Barter Club**
Accountant and house painter exchange services through barter club. Each reports FMV of services received on Schedule C.

**Example 3: Credit Units**
Barter club member receives credits for goods/services provided. Must include credit value in income when credits are added to account, not when used.

**Example 4: Rent for Artwork**
Landlord exchanges 6 months rent-free apartment for artwork. Landlord reports FMV of art as rental income (Schedule E). Artist reports fair rental value as income (Schedule C).

## Form 1099-B

If you exchange through a barter exchange, you should receive Form 1099-B or similar statement by February 15 showing:
- Value of cash, property, services, credits received
- Tax withheld (if any)

## Backup Withholding

Barter income is not subject to regular withholding, but backup withholding may apply at 24% if:

- You don't provide your TIN to the barter exchange
- IRS notifies exchange that you provided incorrect TIN

### Avoiding Backup Withholding

Certify your TIN is correct and you're not subject to backup withholding when joining a barter exchange. Complete Form W-9.

## Tax Implications

### Self-Employment Tax

Barter income from services is subject to self-employment tax if you're self-employed.

### Business Expenses

If you provide goods or services in barter, you can deduct related business expenses just as if you received cash.

### Basis

Your basis in property received through barter is its FMV at time of receipt (the amount you included in income).

## Special Situations

### Personal vs. Business

- Business barter: Report on appropriate business schedule
- Personal barter: Generally not taxable to recipient if not in trade or business, but provider may still have income

### Different Tax Years

If you provide services in one year but receive property/services in another year, you may have income before actually receiving anything (for cash-method taxpayers, income when credits are added to account).

## Common Questions

### Q: Is bartering with friends taxable?
**A:** If you're not in a trade or business, personal exchanges are generally not taxable to the recipient. However, if one party is in business, they must report the FMV of what they receive.

### Q: How do I value services received?
**A:** Use what you would pay a third party for similar services. If parties agree on value beforehand, that's accepted as FMV.

### Q: What if I don't receive Form 1099-B?
**A:** You must still report barter income. Keep your own records of barter transactions.

### Q: Can I deduct expenses related to barter income?
**A:** Yes, ordinary and necessary business expenses related to providing goods or services in barter are deductible, just like cash transactions.
