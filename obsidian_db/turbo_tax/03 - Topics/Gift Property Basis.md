# Gift Property Basis

## Overview

Property received as a gift generally takes a "carryover basis" - the same basis the donor had. However, special rules apply when the FMV at the time of the gift is less than the donor's adjusted basis.

## General Rule: Carryover Basis

**If FMV at gift ≥ Donor's adjusted basis:**

Your basis = Donor's adjusted basis

This is called "carryover basis" because the basis carries over from the donor to you.

## Special Rule: Dual Basis

**If FMV at gift < Donor's adjusted basis:**

You have TWO different bases depending on whether you sell at a gain or loss:

| Sale Result | Your Basis |
|-------------|------------|
| **Gain** | Donor's adjusted basis |
| **Loss** | FMV at date of gift |
| **Neither gain nor loss** | Donor's adjusted basis |

### Example: Dual Basis

| | Amount |
|---|--------|
| Donor's adjusted basis | $30,000 |
| FMV at date of gift | $20,000 |
| Gift tax paid | $5,000 |

| If you sell for | Your basis | Result |
|------------------|------------|--------|
| $35,000 | $30,000 | $5,000 gain |
| $15,000 | $20,000 | $5,000 loss |
| $25,000 | $30,000 | $5,000 loss (can't use FMV basis for gain) |

## Gift Tax Adjustment

If gift tax was paid on the gift, you may increase your basis by the portion of gift tax attributable to the net appreciation in value.

### Formula

**Basis increase = Gift tax paid × (Net appreciation ÷ Total appreciation)**

Where:
- Net appreciation = FMV - Donor's adjusted basis
- Total appreciation = FMV - Donor's basis + Gift tax paid

### Example: Gift Tax Adjustment

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| FMV at gift | $50,000 |
| Donor's adjusted basis | $20,000 |
| Net increase in value | $30,000 |
| Gift tax paid | $6,220 |

**Calculation:**
- Gift tax due to net increase = $6,220 × ($30,000 ÷ $31,000) = $6,033
- Your basis = $20,000 + $6,033 = **$26,033**

## Property Transferred Between Spouses

### Transfers Before 1977
If property was transferred from one spouse to another before 1977 and the donee spouse still owns it at the time of the donor spouse's death, see the rules in Publication 551 for special basis adjustments.

### Transfers After 1976
No gain or loss is recognized on transfers between spouses or incident to divorce. The recipient takes the transferor's basis.

## Incomplete Gifts

If a gift is incomplete at the donor's death, the property is included in the donor's estate and receives stepped-up basis under the inherited property rules.

## Holding Period

For gift property:
- **Add donor's holding period** to your own
- This can affect whether gain is short-term or long-term

## Important Records to Keep

When receiving gifted property:

1. Donor's adjusted basis
2. FMV at date of gift
3. Any gift tax paid
4. Date donor acquired the property
5. Documentation of the gift
