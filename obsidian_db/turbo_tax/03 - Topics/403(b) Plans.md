# 403(b) Plans

## Overview

A 403(b) plan, also known as a tax-sheltered annuity (TSA) plan, is a retirement plan for certain employees of public schools, employees of certain tax-exempt organizations, and certain ministers. These plans allow participants to make pre-tax contributions and grow earnings tax-deferred until distribution.

## Types of 403(b) Accounts

Individual accounts in a 403(b) plan can be:

1. **Annuity contracts** - Provided through an insurance company
2. **Custodial accounts** - Invested in mutual funds or a group trust
3. **Retirement income accounts** - Set up for church employees; can invest in annuities, mutual funds, or other investments

## Eligible Employees

The following employees are eligible to participate:

- Employees of section 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organizations
- Employees of public school systems involved in day-to-day operations
- Employees of cooperative hospital service organizations
- Civilian faculty and staff of the Uniformed Services University of the Health Sciences
- Employees of public school systems organized by Indian tribal governments
- Certain ministers (including [[Ministers and Church Employees|self-employed ministers and chaplains]])

## Benefits of Contributing

1. **Tax-deferred growth** - Contributions aren't taxed until withdrawn
2. **Pre-tax contributions** - Reduce current taxable income (except Roth)
3. **Employer contributions** - May receive matching or nonelective contributions
4. **Tax credit eligibility** - May qualify for [[Retirement Savings Contributions Credit|Saver's Credit]]

## Types of Contributions

### Elective Deferrals
Contributions made under a salary reduction agreement. The employer withholds money from your paycheck to contribute directly to your 403(b) account. See [[Elective Deferrals]] for more details.

### Nonelective Contributions
Employer contributions not made under a salary reduction agreement, including matching contributions, discretionary contributions, and mandatory contributions.

### After-Tax Contributions
Contributions made with funds already included in income. Not excluded from income and not deductible.

### Roth Contributions
Contributions designated as Roth are included in income but qualified distributions are tax-free.

## Universal Availability

Generally, if an employer offers a 403(b) plan, all eligible employees must be permitted to make elective deferrals. Exceptions apply for certain collectively bargained employees and nonresident aliens.
