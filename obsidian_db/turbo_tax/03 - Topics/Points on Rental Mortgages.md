# Points on Rental Mortgages

## Overview

Points (also called loan origination fees, maximum loan charges, or premium charges) are charges paid to obtain a mortgage. Points that are solely for the use of money are interest. Because points are prepaid interest, they generally can't be deducted in full in the year paid but must be deducted over the loan term.

## What Are Points?

Points are the difference between:
- **Issue price** - Amount borrowed minus points paid
- **Stated redemption price at maturity** - The stated principal amount

This difference is Original Issue Discount (OID), which includes points.

## Deduction Methods

### De Minimis OID Test

Points are de minimis if less than:
```
0.0025 × Stated redemption price × Loan term (full years)
```

**Example**: $100,000 loan for 30 years
- De minimis threshold = $100,000 × 30 × 0.0025 = $7,500
- Points of $1,500 are de minimis (under $7,500)

### If De Minimis - Choose One Method

1. **Straight-line** - Equal deduction each year over loan term
2. **Entirety at maturity** - Deduct all at loan payoff
3. **Proportion to stated interest** - Deduct with interest payments
4. **Constant-yield method** - Apply OID rules

### If Not De Minimis - Must Use Constant-Yield Method

**Year 1 Calculation**:
1. Determine issue price (amount borrowed minus points)
2. Multiply by yield to maturity (YTM)
3. Subtract qualified stated interest (QSI)

**Subsequent Years**: Use adjusted issue price (add previously deducted OID).

## Example Calculation

**Loan Terms**:
- Amount borrowed: $100,000
- Points: $1,500
- Issue price: $98,500
- YTM: 10.2467%
- Annual stated interest: $10,000

**Year 1**:
- $98,500 × 0.102467 = $10,093
- $10,093 - $10,000 = $93 deductible

**Year 2**:
- Adjusted issue price: $98,500 + $93 = $98,593
- $98,593 × 0.102467 = $10,103
- $10,103 - $10,000 = $103 deductible

## Loan Payoff Rules

### When Loan Ends
May deduct remaining points in the year the loan ends due to:
- Refinancing
- Prepayment
- Foreclosure
- Similar event

### Same Lender Refinancing
If refinancing with the same lender, remaining points generally:
- **Not deductible** in the year of refinancing
- Deducted over the term of the new mortgage

### Cash-Out Refinancing
Points allocable to loan proceeds not related to rental use:
- Treated as nondeductible personal interest

**Example**: $100,000 loan refinanced for $120,000; extra $20,000 used for car purchase. Points on $20,000 are personal interest.
