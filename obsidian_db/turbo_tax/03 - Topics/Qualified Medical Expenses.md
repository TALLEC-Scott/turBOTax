# Qualified Medical Expenses

## Overview

Qualified medical expenses are amounts paid for medical care as defined in Internal Revenue Code Section 213(d). These expenses can be paid or reimbursed tax-free from HSAs, Archer MSAs, Medicare Advantage MSAs, Health FSAs, and HRAs. See [[Pub 969 - Health Savings Accounts and Other Tax-Favored Health Plans]] and [[Pub 502 - Medical and Dental Expenses]].

## Definition

Qualified medical expenses are amounts paid for:
- Diagnosis, cure, mitigation, treatment, or prevention of disease
- Amounts paid for the purpose of affecting any structure or function of the body
- Transportation primarily for and essential to medical care
- Insurance covering medical care

## Who Must Incur the Expense

Qualified medical expenses include those incurred by:

1. **You and your spouse**
2. **All dependents you claim on your tax return**
3. **Any person you could have claimed as a dependent except that:**
   - The person filed a joint return
   - The person had gross income equal to or more than the exemption amount
   - You or your spouse could be claimed as a dependent on someone else's return

**Special Rule for Divorced Parents:** A child of divorced, separated, or living-apart parents is treated as the dependent of both parents for this purpose.

## General Categories of Qualified Expenses

### Diagnostic and Treatment Services
- Doctor visits and exams
- Hospital services
- Nursing services
- Laboratory fees
- X-rays and diagnostic tests

### Preventive Care
- Annual physicals
- Immunizations
- Health screenings
- Well-child care
- Prenatal care

### Prescription Drugs
- Prescribed medications
- Insulin (even without prescription)

### Medical Equipment and Supplies
- Crutches, wheelchairs
- Hearing aids and batteries
- Eyeglasses and contact lenses
- Prosthetic devices

### Dental Care
- Exams, cleanings, fillings
- Orthodontics
- Oral surgery

### Vision Care
- Eye exams
- Eyeglasses, contact lenses
- LASIK surgery

### Mental Health
- Psychiatric care
- Psychological counseling
- Substance abuse treatment

## Special Items (Notice 2024-71 and 2024-75)

### Menstrual Care Products
Treated as paid for medical care under Section 213(d).

### Condoms
Amounts paid for condoms are treated as medical care expenses (Notice 2024-71).

### Over-the-Counter Items
- Over-the-counter medicines (whether or not prescribed)
- Over-the-counter oral contraceptives (including emergency contraceptives)
- Male condoms

## Insurance Premiums - HSA Rules

HSA funds can be used for insurance premiums ONLY for:

1. **Long-term care insurance** (subject to age-based limits)
2. **Health care continuation coverage** (COBRA)
3. **Health coverage while receiving unemployment compensation**
4. **Medicare and other health care coverage if age 65 or older**
   - Does NOT include Medigap (Medicare supplemental policies)

**Note:** For those under 65, Medicare premiums for a spouse or dependent who is 65+ are generally NOT qualified medical expenses.

## Insurance Premiums - FSA/HRA Rules

Health FSAs generally cannot reimburse insurance premiums. HRAs CAN reimburse:
- Health insurance premiums
- Long-term care coverage premiums

## Long-Term Care Premium Limits

Premiums for long-term care insurance are subject to annual limits based on age. See the Instructions for Schedule A (Form 1040) for current limits.

## Expenses NOT Qualified

### General Non-Qualifying Items
- Cosmetic surgery (unless for congenital abnormality or personal injury)
- Health club memberships (unless prescribed for specific condition)
- Non-prescription drugs (except insulin)
- Vitamins and supplements (unless prescribed)
- Funeral expenses
- Life insurance premiums

### From HSAs Specifically
- Health insurance premiums (except those listed above)
- Amounts covered by other health plans
- Expenses reimbursed elsewhere

### From Health FSAs
- Health insurance premiums
- Long-term care expenses

## Timing Rules

### HSA Timing
- Expenses must be incurred AFTER the HSA is established
- State law determines establishment date
- HSA funded by rollover is established on date the prior account was established

### Archer MSA Timing
- Expenses must be incurred after the MSA is established

### FSA Timing
- Expenses must be incurred during the period of coverage
- Entire annual election is available from start of plan year

## Documentation Requirements

You must maintain records showing:
- Distributions were exclusively for qualified medical expenses
- Expenses had not been previously paid or reimbursed
- Medical expenses had not been taken as an itemized deduction

**Do NOT send records with tax return.** Keep with tax records.

## Interaction with Itemized Deductions

You CANNOT deduct qualified medical expenses on Schedule A that are equal to:
- Tax-free distribution from HSA
- Tax-free reimbursement from Archer MSA
- Tax-free reimbursement from FSA
- Tax-free reimbursement from HRA

If expenses exceed reimbursement, the excess may be deductible on Schedule A (subject to 7.5% AGI floor).
