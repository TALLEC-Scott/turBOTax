# Social Security Coverage for Clergy

## Overview

Members of the clergy and religious workers have unique Social Security coverage rules. Unlike most employees who are covered under FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act), clergy are typically covered under SECA (Self-Employment Contributions Act) for their ministerial earnings.

## Two Tax Systems

### FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act)
- Applies to most employees
- Employer and employee each pay half (7.65% each)
- Total rate: 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare)

### SECA (Self-Employment Contributions Act)
- Applies to self-employed individuals and most clergy
- Individual pays both employer and employee portions
- Total rate: 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare)

## Coverage by Classification

### Ministers
- **FICA**: No - Ministerial earnings are exempt from FICA
- **SECA**: Yes, unless [[SE Tax Exemption]] is approved via [[Form 4361]]

Ministers are defined as individuals duly ordained, commissioned, or licensed by a religious body who have authority to conduct religious worship, perform sacerdotal functions, and administer sacraments.

### Members of Religious Orders

**Without Vow of Poverty:**
- **FICA**: No (exempt)
- **SECA**: Yes, unless exemption approved

**With Vow of Poverty:**
- **FICA**: Only if the order elected FICA coverage via [[Form SS-16]]
- **SECA**: No (exempt)

### Christian Science Practitioners and Readers
- **FICA**: No (exempt)
- **SECA**: Yes, unless exemption approved

### Religious Workers (Church Employees)
- **FICA**: Yes, unless employer elected exclusion via [[Form 8274]]
- **SECA**: Yes, if employer elected to exclude from FICA

### Members of Recognized Religious Sects
- **FICA**: Yes, unless exemption approved via [[Form 4029]]
- **SECA**: Yes, unless exemption approved

## Key Thresholds for 2025

| Threshold | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Maximum earnings subject to Social Security tax | $176,100 |
| Minimum earnings for SE tax filing | $400 |
| Minimum church wages for SE tax filing | $108.28 |

## Additional Medicare Tax

A 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax applies to earnings exceeding:
- $250,000 if married filing jointly
- $125,000 if married filing separately
- $200,000 for any other filing status

See [[Form 8959]] for calculation details.

## Employment Status Distinction

Even though ministerial earnings are subject to SE tax for Social Security purposes, a minister may still be a common-law employee for:
- Income tax withholding
- Retirement plan participation

The common-law test examines whether the church has the right to control both what the minister does and how they do it.
