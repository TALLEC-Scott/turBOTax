# IRA Deductions and Phaseouts

This topic covers the deduction rules for Traditional IRA contributions and the income phaseouts that affect both Traditional IRA deductions and Roth IRA contribution eligibility.

## Traditional IRA Deduction Rules

### If NOT Covered by an Employer Retirement Plan

**Full deduction** regardless of income level.

### If Covered by an Employer Retirement Plan

Your deduction is limited based on modified AGI (MAGI):

#### 2025 Phaseout Ranges

| Filing Status | Full Deduction | Partial Deduction | No Deduction |
|---------------|----------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Single/HOH | MAGI ≤ $79,000 | $79,001 - $89,000 | > $89,000 |
| MFJ/QSS | MAGI ≤ $126,000 | $126,001 - $146,000 | > $146,000 |
| MFS | N/A | $0 - $10,000 | > $10,000 |

#### 2026 Phaseout Ranges (Increased)

| Filing Status | Full Deduction | Partial Deduction | No Deduction |
|---------------|----------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Single/HOH | MAGI ≤ $81,000 | $81,001 - $91,000 | > $91,000 |
| MFJ/QSS | MAGI ≤ $129,000 | $129,001 - $149,000 | > $149,000 |

### If Not Covered, But Spouse IS Covered

#### 2025 Phaseout Range

| Filing Status | Full Deduction | Partial Deduction | No Deduction |
|---------------|----------------|-------------------|--------------|
| MFJ | MAGI ≤ $236,000 | $236,001 - $246,000 | > $246,000 |

#### 2026 Phaseout Range

| Filing Status | Full Deduction | Partial Deduction | No Deduction |
|---------------|----------------|-------------------|--------------|
| MFJ | MAGI ≤ $242,000 | $242,001 - $252,000 | > $252,000 |

## Calculating Modified AGI

Modified AGI for Traditional IRA purposes starts with AGI and adds back:

1. Traditional IRA deduction (if already taken)
2. Student loan interest deduction
3. Foreign earned income exclusion
4. Foreign housing exclusion/deduction
5. Excludable savings bond interest (Form 8815)
6. Excluded employer-provided adoption benefits (Form 8839)

### Worksheet 1-1 (Pub 590-A)

```
1. AGI from Form 1040, Line 11a                      _______
2. Student loan interest deduction                   _______
3. Foreign earned income exclusion                   _______
4. Foreign housing deduction                         _______
5. Excludable savings bond interest                  _______
6. Excluded adoption benefits                        _______
7. Modified AGI (add lines 1-6)                      _______
```

## Calculating Reduced Deduction

### Worksheet 1-2 (Pub 590-A)

Use when MAGI falls within the phaseout range:

**Step 1:** Enter the upper limit of your phaseout range (from table)
**Step 2:** Enter your MAGI
**Step 3:** Subtract line 2 from line 1 (this is your "phaseout space")
**Step 4:** Multiply by applicable percentage:
- MFJ/QSS covered by employer: 35% (40% if 50+)
- All others: 70% (80% if 50+)
**Step 5:** Enter compensation (minus self-employment deductions)
**Step 6:** Enter contribution made (max $7,000/$8,000)
**Step 7:** Enter the smallest of lines 4, 5, or 6 = **Your deduction**

### Example Calculation

**Scenario:** Single, age 40, covered by employer plan, MAGI $84,000

```
1. Upper limit: $89,000
2. MAGI: $84,000
3. Phaseout space: $5,000
4. Multiply by 70%: $3,500
5. Compensation: $60,000
6. Contribution made: $7,000
7. Deduction: $3,500 (smallest of $3,500, $60,000, $7,000)
```

**Result:** $3,500 deductible, $3,500 nondeductible contribution

## Roth IRA Contribution Phaseouts

Roth IRA contributions (not deductions) are phased out at higher income levels:

### 2025 Phaseout Ranges

| Filing Status | Full Contribution | Reduced Contribution | No Contribution |
|---------------|-------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| MFJ/QSS | MAGI < $236,000 | $236,000 - $246,000 | ≥ $246,000 |
| Single/HOH | MAGI < $150,000 | $150,000 - $165,000 | ≥ $165,000 |
| MFS (lived with spouse) | MAGI = $0 | $0 - $10,000 | ≥ $10,000 |

### 2026 Phaseout Ranges

| Filing Status | Full Contribution | Reduced Contribution | No Contribution |
|---------------|-------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| MFJ/QSS | MAGI < $242,000 | $242,000 - $252,000 | ≥ $252,000 |
| Single/HOH | MAGI < $153,000 | $153,000 - $168,000 | ≥ $168,000 |

### Calculating Reduced Roth Contribution

**Worksheet 2-2 (Pub 590-A):**

```
1. MAGI for Roth purposes                              _______
2. Lower limit of phaseout range                       _______
3. Subtract line 2 from line 1                         _______
```

## What Is "Covered by an Employer Plan"?

You're considered covered if you were an **active participant** at any time during the year:

### Types of Employer Plans
- 401(k) plans
- Pension/profit-sharing plans
- 403(b) tax-sheltered annuities
- 457(b) governmental plans
- SEP-IRA (if employer contributed)
- SIMPLE IRA

### How to Know If You're Covered
- **Form W-2, Box 13:** "Retirement plan" checkbox
- Check with your employer's HR department

### Special Rules
- Being eligible but not contributing = still "covered"
- Employer contributions count even if you're not vested

## Social Security Recipients

If you receive Social Security benefits and are covered by an employer plan, use the worksheets in Appendix B of Pub 590-A to:
1. Figure modified AGI
2. Figure your IRA deduction
3. Figure how much of your Social Security is taxable

## Nondeductible Contributions

When your deduction is limited or eliminated, you can still make **nondeductible contributions**:

### Requirements
- File Form 8606 each year you make nondeductible contributions
- Track your basis in nondeductible contributions

### Benefits
- Earnings grow tax-deferred
- Basis is recovered tax-free when distributed
- Can be converted to Roth IRA (pro-rata rule applies)

### Penalties
- $50 for not filing Form 8606
- $100 for overstating nondeductible contributions

## Planning Strategies

### Income Timing
- Defer income to stay below phaseout thresholds
- Accelerate income to maximize deductions in high-income years

### Spousal Strategies
- If one spouse not covered by employer plan, they may have higher deduction limits
- Coordinate contributions between spouses

### Roth Conversion Planning
- Make nondeductible contributions when deduction is limited
- Convert to Roth (consider pro-rata rule with other Traditional IRAs)

### Employer Plan Considerations
- Consider whether employer plan participation limits Traditional IRA deduction
- May prioritize Roth IRA contributions when Traditional deduction is limited
