# Federal Unemployment Tax (FUTA)

## Overview

The Federal Unemployment Tax Act (FUTA) imposes a payroll tax on employers to fund unemployment compensation programs. Unlike FICA taxes, FUTA tax is paid only by employers—employees do not contribute.

## Tax Rate and Wage Base

- **FUTA tax rate:** 6.0% on first $7,000 of each employee's wages
- **FUTA wage base:** $7,000 per employee per year
- **Maximum FUTA tax per employee:** $420 (if no state credit reduction)

## Credit for State Unemployment Tax

Employers can receive a credit of up to 5.4% for paying state unemployment taxes (SUTA) on time. This reduces the effective FUTA rate to:
- **Minimum effective rate:** 0.6% (after maximum 5.4% credit)
- **Minimum tax per employee:** $42

### Credit Reduction States
If a state has an outstanding federal loan for unemployment benefits, the credit may be reduced. Each year of loan outstanding reduces the credit by 0.3%.

## Who Must Pay FUTA Tax

Generally, you must pay FUTA tax if you meet **any** of these tests:

### General Test
- Paid wages of $1,500 or more in any calendar quarter

### Household Employment Test
- Paid cash wages of $1,000 or more in any calendar quarter for household work

### Farmworkers Test
- Paid cash wages of $20,000 or more in any calendar quarter, OR
- Employed 10 or more farmworkers during some part of a day in 20 or more weeks

## Exempt Employment

Some employment is exempt from FUTA tax:
- Services performed by your parent, spouse, or child under age 21
- Certain nonprofit organizations
- Government entities (federal, state, local)
- Indian tribal governments
- Certain religious organizations

## Deposit Requirements

### Quarterly Deposit Threshold
- Deposit FUTA tax **only if** undeposited amount exceeds $500
- If $500 or less, carry forward to next quarter
- Deposit by last day of month following end of quarter

### Deposit Schedule

| Quarter Ending | Deposit Due (if over $500) |
|----------------|----------------------------|
| March 31 | April 30 |
| June 30 | July 31 |
| September 30 | October 31 |
| December 31 | January 31 |

**Note:** Unlike income and FICA taxes, FUTA deposits are not made through EFTPS regular deposit schedules.

## Filing Requirements

- **Form 940:** Employer's Annual Federal Unemployment (FUTA) Tax Return
- **Due date:** January 31 (or February 10 if all taxes deposited on time)
- File annually even if no FUTA tax is due

## Calculating FUTA Tax

```
FUTA Tax = Gross FUTA wages (up to $7,000/employee) × 6.0%
            minus Credit for state unemployment tax (up to 5.4%)
            = Net FUTA Tax Due
```

## Recordkeeping Requirements

Keep records for at least 4 years, including:
- Total wages paid
- Wages subject to FUTA
- State unemployment contributions
- FUTA tax deposits made
