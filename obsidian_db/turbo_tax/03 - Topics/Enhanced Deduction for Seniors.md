# Enhanced Deduction for Seniors

## Overview

**New for 2025**: Taxpayers age 65 or older may be eligible for an enhanced deduction for seniors. This is a new deduction separate from and in addition to the higher standard deduction for taxpayers age 65+. Eligible taxpayers can claim this deduction whether they take the standard deduction or itemize.

## Key Rules

### Eligibility Requirements

To claim the enhanced deduction for seniors, you must meet ALL of the following:

1. **Age Requirement**: You (and/or spouse if filing jointly) were born before January 2, 1961 (considered age 65+ at end of 2025)
2. **Valid SSN**: You must have a valid Social Security Number issued by SSA before the due date of your 2025 return (including extensions)
3. **Filing Status**: If married, you must file a joint return with your spouse

### Maximum Deduction Amounts

| Filing Status | Maximum Deduction |
|---------------|------------------|
| Single, Head of Household | $6,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly (both 65+) | $12,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly (one spouse 65+) | $6,000 |

### Income Phase-Out

The deduction is reduced if your modified adjusted gross income exceeds:
- **$75,000** for Single, Head of Household
- **$150,000** for Married Filing Jointly

The deduction is limited/eliminated based on how much your income exceeds these thresholds.

## Claiming the Deduction

- Can be claimed whether you take the **standard deduction OR itemize**
- Report on Form 1040 or 1040-SR
- See Form 1040 Instructions for line details

## Important Notes

### Valid SSN Definition
- Must be valid for employment
- Must be issued by SSA before the due date of your 2025 return (including extensions)

### Special Rules Do NOT Apply
The special rules that apply to certain individuals do NOT apply to the enhanced deduction for seniors:
- U.S. nationals
- Residents of Canada, Mexico, and South Korea
- Residents of India who were students or business apprentices

See [[Pub 519]] for more information on these special rules.

## Comparison with Higher Standard Deduction

| Feature | Higher Standard Deduction (65+) | Enhanced Deduction for Seniors |
|---------|--------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Must itemize? | No (replaces itemizing) | No (can claim with either method) |
| Maximum amount | ~$2,000 (single) or $1,600/spouse (MFJ) | Up to $6,000/person |
| Income limit? | No | Yes ($75K/$150K) |
| New for 2025? | No | Yes |
