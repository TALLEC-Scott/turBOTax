# Form 8962 - Premium Tax Credit

## Overview

Form 8962 is used to figure your Premium Tax Credit (PTC) and reconcile it with any Advance Payments of the Premium Tax Credit (APTC) made on your behalf. This form must be attached to Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR.

## Who Must File

You must file Form 8962 if ANY of the following apply:

1. You are claiming the PTC
2. APTC was paid for you or another individual in your tax family
3. APTC was paid for an individual you told the Marketplace would be in your tax family, and neither you nor anyone else included that individual in a tax family

**Caution**: If you are filing Form 8962, you cannot file Form 1040-SS.

## Form Structure

### Part I - Annual and Monthly Contribution Amount

Lines 1-5: Calculate your household income and determine your contribution percentage

| Line | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Line 1 | Modified AGI (from Worksheet 1-1) |
| Line 2a | Your modified AGI |
| Line 2b | Spouse's modified AGI |
| Line 3 | Household income |
| Line 4 | Federal poverty line percentage |
| Line 5-8 | Monthly and annual contribution amounts |

### Part II - Premium Tax Credit Claim and Reconciliation

Lines 9-29: Calculate your PTC and reconcile with APTC

| Line | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Line 9 | Allocation situation indicator |
| Line 10 | Same SLCSP all year? |
| Line 11 | Annual calculation (if applicable) |
| Lines 12-23 | Monthly calculations |
| Line 24 | Total PTC |
| Line 25 | Total APTC from Form 1095-A |
| Line 26 | Net PTC (if PTC > APTC) |
| Line 27 | Excess APTC (if APTC > PTC) |
| Lines 28-29 | Repayment limitation application |

### Part III - Income Allocation

Used when allocating policy amounts with another taxpayer or using alternative calculation for year of marriage.

### Part IV - Coverage Family Members

List individuals in your coverage family and their coverage months.

## Required Documents

Before completing Form 8962, you need:

- **Form 1095-A** (Health Insurance Marketplace Statement) from your Marketplace
- Your tax return (Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR)
- Worksheet 1-1 and 1-2 from Form 8962 instructions

## Special Filing Situations

### Married Filing Separately Exception

If you're a victim of domestic abuse or spousal abandonment, check the box on line A and attach documentation to support your claim. Keep records such as:
- Protective/restraining orders
- Doctor's reports
- Police reports
- Witness statements

### Alternative Calculation for Year of Marriage

If you married during the tax year, you may use an alternative calculation that could lower your taxes. Complete Part III and follow specific instructions.

### Self-Employed Taxpayers

Must coordinate PTC with the self-employed health insurance deduction. See [[Self-Employed Health Insurance Deduction]] for details.

## Common Errors to Avoid

1. **Missing Form 1095-A**: Contact your Marketplace if you didn't receive it by early February
2. **Incorrect SLCSP**: Verify column B of Form 1095-A reflects your correct benchmark plan
3. **Income mismatch**: Report all income changes to the Marketplace during the year
4. **Filing status**: Married taxpayers must generally file jointly (with exceptions)
