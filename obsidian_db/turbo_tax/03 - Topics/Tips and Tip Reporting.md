# Tips and Tip Reporting

## Overview

Tips are taxable compensation subject to income tax withholding and FICA taxes. Employers have specific reporting and recordkeeping requirements for tipped employees.

## Employee Tip Reporting Requirements

### Monthly Threshold: $20
Employees must report tips to employer if they receive **$20 or more** in tips during a calendar month.

### What to Report
- Cash tips received directly from customers
- Credit card tips (charged tips)
- Tips received through tip-sharing arrangements
- Noncash tips (value must be reported, but not subject to withholding)

### Deadline
Report tips to employer by the **10th of the following month**.

## Employer Responsibilities

### When Tips Are Reported
1. Include reported tips in employee's wages
2. Withhold income tax on tips (from wages or cash)
3. Withhold employee's share of FICA on tips
4. Pay employer's share of FICA on tips
5. Report on Form 941

### Withholding Insufficient Funds
If employee's wages are insufficient to cover tax withholding on tips:
- You may withhold from later wages
- Report shortfall on Form W-2 (Box 8 - Allocated Tips)
- Employee pays with income tax return

## Credit Card Tips

When customers pay tips by credit card:
- Include in employee's wages when received
- Withhold and pay FICA taxes
- Employer may deduct processing fees from tip amount

## Tip Pooling and Tip Sharing

### Permissible Arrangements
- Tips may be shared among employees who customarily receive tips
- May include wait staff, bartenders, bus staff, etc.
- Back-of-house employees generally may not participate (except for tip credit employers)

### Mandatory Service Charges
- NOT considered tips
- Treated as wages subject to withholding
- Must be reported as regular wages

## Form 4070 and 4070-A

Employees use these forms to report tips:
- **Form 4070:** Monthly report to employer
- **Form 4070-A:** Daily tip record (employee keeps)

## Allocated Tips

If you operate a large food/beverage establishment:
- May need to allocate tips if reported tips fall below 8% of gross receipts
- Report allocated tips on Form W-2, Box 8
- Allocated tips are not subject to withholding

### Large Establishment Criteria
- More than 10 employees
- Food or beverages consumed on premises
- Tipping is customary

## Form 8027

**Employer's Annual Information Return of Tip Income and Allocated Tips**

### Who Must File
Large food/beverage establishments that:
- Serve food/drinks for consumption on premises
- Have more than 10 employees working more than 80 hours
- Tipping is customary

### Due Date
- Paper: February 28
- Electronic: March 31

## Qualified Tips Deduction (2025-2029)

Under P.L. 119-21, employees may deduct up to **$25,000** of qualified tips on their income tax returns.

### Qualified Tips Are:
- Cash tips (including charged tips)
- Tips in occupations that customarily received tips before December 31, 2024
- Received through tip-sharing arrangements

### NOT Qualified Tips:
- Mandatory service charges
- Noncash tips

### Employer Responsibility
- Accept updated Form W-4 to adjust withholding
- Use procedures in [[Pub 15-T]] to account for expected deduction
- Report tips on Form W-2

## Special Rules for Tipped Employees

### Tip Credit (Section 3(m))
- May pay reduced cash wage (at least $2.13/hour)
- Tips must make up difference to reach minimum wage
- Maximum tip credit is $5.12/hour
- See FLSA requirements

### Form 2210 (Employee Use)
Employees may owe estimated tax penalty if tip income is not fully withheld.
