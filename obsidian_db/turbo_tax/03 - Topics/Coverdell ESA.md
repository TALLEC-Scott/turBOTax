# Coverdell ESA

## Overview

A Coverdell Education Savings Account (ESA) is a tax-advantaged account designed to help pay for education expenses from kindergarten through college. Earnings grow tax-free and distributions are tax-free when used for qualified education expenses.

**Authority:** [[Pub 970 - Tax Benefits for Education#Chapter 6]]

## Contribution Rules

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Annual Limit | $2,000 per designated beneficiary |
| Contributor MAGI Limit | $110,000 (single), $220,000 (MFJ) |
| Contribution Deadline | Due date of tax return (no extensions) |
| Age Limit | Contributions must stop at age 18 (exception: special needs) |
| Cash Only | Contributions must be in cash |

## Contribution Phase-Out

| Filing Status | Full Contribution | Partial Contribution | No Contribution |
|---------------|-------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Single | Under $95,000 | $95,000 - $110,000 | $110,000+ |
| Married Filing Jointly | Under $190,000 | $190,000 - $220,000 | $220,000+ |

**Note:** Organizations can contribute regardless of income.

## Qualified Education Expenses

### Higher Education (Postsecondary)
1. Tuition and fees
2. Books, supplies, and equipment
3. Room and board (if enrolled at least half-time)
4. Computer equipment, software, and internet access
5. Expenses for special needs services

### K-12 Education
1. Tuition and fees
2. Books, supplies, and equipment
3. Academic tutoring
4. Special needs services
5. Room and board (if required/provided by school)
6. Uniforms
7. Transportation
8. Supplementary items and services (extended day programs)
9. Computer equipment and internet access

## Distribution Rules

### Tax-Free Distributions
- Used for qualified education expenses
- Must be used by the time beneficiary turns 30 (exceptions apply)

### Taxable Distributions
- Earnings portion is taxable income
- Subject to 10% additional tax

### Exceptions to 10% Tax
- Death of beneficiary
- Disability of beneficiary
- Distribution to surviving spouse/divorce

## Rollovers and Transfers

| Type | Rule |
|------|------|
| Same Beneficiary | Rollover within 60 days |
| Different Beneficiary | Must be family member under age 30 |
| Transfer upon Death | To spouse or family member |
| Transfer to QTP | Allowed for same beneficiary |

## Coordination with Other Benefits

### With QTP
- Can contribute to both in same year
- Same expenses cannot be used for both

### With Education Credits
- Can claim credits and use ESA distributions
- Cannot use same expenses for both
- Consider allocating to maximize benefits

## Forms Required

- [[Form 5329]] - Additional Taxes (for excess contributions or taxable distributions)
- No contribution deduction form (contributions are not deductible)

## Comparison with Qualified Tuition Program

| Feature | Coverdell ESA | Qualified Tuition Program |
|---------|---------------|---------------------------|
| Contribution Limit | $2,000/year | Varies by state |
| Income Limits | Yes | No |
| Investment Control | Full control | Limited options |
| K-12 Expenses | Full range | Tuition only ($10K) |
| Age Limit | Must distribute by 30 | No age limit |

## Common Questions

### Q: What happens if my child doesn't go to college?
**A:** The funds must be distributed by age 30 (earnings taxed + 10% penalty), or transferred to a family member.

### Q: Can I contribute to a Coverdell ESA and a 529 plan?
**A:** Yes, both can receive contributions in the same year for the same beneficiary.

### Q: Who can contribute?
**A:** Any individual (with MAGI limits) or organization. The $2,000 limit is per beneficiary, not per contributor.
