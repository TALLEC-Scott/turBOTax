# Catch-up Contributions

## Overview

Catch-up contributions allow participants age 50 or older to make additional contributions to their retirement plans beyond the standard contribution limits. This provision helps older workers accelerate their retirement savings as they approach retirement age.

## Eligibility

- Must be age 50 or older by the end of the calendar year
- Available for 401(k), 403(b), 457(b), SARSEP, SEP, and SIMPLE plans
- Also available for traditional and Roth IRAs

## Standard Catch-up Contribution Limits (2025-2026)

| Plan Type | 2025 Limit | 2026 Limit |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| 401(k), 403(b), 457(b), SARSEP | $7,500 | $8,000 |
| SIMPLE IRA, SIMPLE 401(k) | $3,500 | $4,000 |
| Traditional/Roth IRA | $1,000 | $1,000 |

## Enhanced Catch-up Contributions (Ages 60-63)

The SECURE 2.0 Act provides higher catch-up limits for participants attaining age 60, 61, 62, or 63 during the year.

| Plan Type | 2025-2026 Enhanced Limit |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| 401(k), 403(b), 457(b) | $11,250 |
| SIMPLE IRA, SIMPLE 401(k) | $5,250 |

## Total Contribution Limits with Catch-up (2025)

| Plan Type | Under 50 | Age 50-59 | Ages 60-63 | Age 64+ |
|-----------|----------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 401(k) Elective Deferral | $23,500 | $31,000 | $34,750 | $31,000 |
| Defined Contribution Annual Addition | $70,000 | $77,500 | $81,250 | $77,500 |
| SIMPLE Salary Reduction | $16,500 | $20,000 | $21,750 | $20,000 |

## Higher SIMPLE Plan Limits

Section 117 of SECURE 2.0 Act allows certain employers to elect higher limits for their SIMPLE plans.

| Limit Type | 2025 Standard | 2025 Higher Limit |
|------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Salary Reduction | $16,500 | $18,100 |
| Catch-up (age 50+) | $3,500 | $3,850 |

**Eligibility for Higher Limits**: Employers with no more than 25 employees, or employers with 26-50 employees with a 4% employer contribution.

## Limitations on Catch-up Contributions

The actual catch-up contribution cannot exceed the lesser of:
1. The catch-up contribution limit, OR
2. The excess of the participant's compensation over elective deferrals that are not catch-up contributions

**Example**: If a participant earns $25,000 and defers $20,000, their catch-up contribution is limited to $5,000 ($25,000 - $20,000), even though the catch-up limit is $7,500.

## SEP Plans

- SEP plans do not have separate catch-up contribution limits
- Participants can contribute to a personal IRA and use IRA catch-up limits
- SARSEPs (pre-1997 plans) can use catch-up contribution limits

## Reporting Requirements

- Reported separately on Form W-2
- Included in box 12 with appropriate code
- Used for nondiscrimination testing purposes

## Plan Document Requirements

- Plan must specifically permit catch-up contributions
- If plan allows, must be available to all eligible participants
- Cannot be limited to highly compensated employees only

## Key Points

- Catch-up contributions are not subject to the annual addition limit
- Must be elected by the employee (not automatic)
- Can be made even if participant has already maximized regular contributions
- Applies to both pre-tax and Roth contributions

## 403(b) Plan Specific Rules

For [[403(b) Plans]], catch-up contributions have special interaction with the [[15-Year Rule]]:

### Order of Application
If you're eligible for **both** the 15-year rule increase and age 50+ catch-up contributions:
1. First apply amounts under the 15-year rule
2. Then apply catch-up contributions

### Not Counted Against MAC
Catch-up contributions are **not** counted against your Maximum Amount Contributable (MAC). Therefore, the maximum amount you can have contributed is:
- Your MAC **plus** allowable catch-up contributions

### Combining Plans
When figuring allowable catch-up contributions, combine contributions to:
- Qualified retirement plans
- [[403(b) Plans]]
- SIMPLE plans
- SARSEP plans

The total catch-up contributions to all plans maintained by your employer cannot exceed the annual limit.
