# Qualifying Relative

A qualifying relative is a type of dependent who meets four specific tests. Unlike a [[Qualifying Child]], a qualifying relative can be any age but must meet income and support requirements.

## Four Tests for a Qualifying Relative

### 1. Not a Qualifying Child Test

The person must NOT be:
- Your [[Qualifying Child]], or
- The qualifying child of any other taxpayer

**Example:** Your 22-year-old child who is a student lives with you and meets all tests to be your qualifying child. This child cannot be your qualifying relative.

**Child of Non-Filing Parent:** A child isn't the qualifying child of any other taxpayer (and thus may qualify as your qualifying relative) if the child's parent isn't required to file a return and either doesn't file or files only for a refund.

### 2. Member of Household or Relationship Test

The person must meet **one** of the following:

#### Option A: Live with You All Year
- Must live with you all year as a member of your household
- Relationship must not violate local law

#### Option B: Be Related to You
The person can be related to you in any of these ways (doesn't need to live with you):

| Relationship | Includes |
|--------------|----------|
| **Lineal** | Child, stepchild, foster child, grandchild, parent, stepparent, grandparent |
| **Siblings** | Brother, sister, half-brother, half-sister, stepbrother, stepsister |
| **In-laws** | Son-in-law, daughter-in-law, father-in-law, mother-in-law, brother-in-law, sister-in-law |
| **Other** | Niece, nephew, aunt, uncle |

**Important Notes:**
- Relationships established by marriage continue even after death or divorce
- A foster child must live with you all year to qualify
- Cousins do NOT qualify as relatives who don't need to live with you
- Your spouse can never be your qualifying relative

### 3. Gross Income Test

The person's gross income for the year must be **less than $5,200** (2025).

**Gross Income Includes:**
- All taxable income
- Tax-exempt interest, Social Security benefits (if taxable portion counts)
- Capital gains and dividends

**Does NOT Include:**
- Nontaxable income
- Income that is excluded from gross income

### 4. Support Test

You must provide **more than half** of the person's total support for the year.

#### What Counts as Support

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Food** | Groceries, meals |
| **Housing** | Rent, utilities, repairs, insurance, fair rental value of home |
| **Clothing** | All clothing purchases |
| **Medical** | Doctor bills, medicine, health insurance premiums |
| **Education** | Tuition, books, fees |
| **Recreation** | Entertainment, hobbies |

#### Special Rules

- **Scholarships:** Not counted as support provided by the student (for full-time students)
- **Social Security/Disability:** Count as support provided by the person if used for their support
- **TANF payments:** Count as support you provided if you received the payments and used them to support another person
- **Housing:** Fair rental value of housing provided counts as support

#### Worksheet Calculation

1. Total household expenses ÷ Number of persons in household = Person's share
2. Add person's personal expenses (clothing, education, medical, recreation)
3. Total = Person's total support for the year
4. You must have paid more than 50% of this total

## Exceptions and Special Situations

### Multiple Support Agreement

When no single person provides more than half of a person's support, but two or more people together provide more than half:

1. One person can claim the dependent
2. Each provider must agree in writing (Form 2120)
3. The claimant must have provided at least 10% of support
4. Each provider must meet relationship or residency test

**Note:** A person claimed under a multiple support agreement cannot be a qualifying person for Head of Household.

### Children of Divorced or Separated Parents

Special support test rules apply when parents are divorced, separated, or lived apart for the last 6 months:

- The noncustodial parent can claim the child if the custodial parent signs Form 8332
- Child support payments count as support from the parent who paid them
- Pre-1985 child support agreements have special rules

### Kidnapped Child

A kidnapped child can be treated as your qualifying relative if:
1. Law enforcement presumes kidnapping by a non-family member
2. Child met qualifying relative tests before the kidnapping
3. This treatment continues until the child is found dead or would have reached age 18

### Child in Canada or Mexico

You may claim a child living in Canada or Mexico as a qualifying relative if:
- They are a U.S. citizen, U.S. national, or resident of Canada/Mexico
- They are not the qualifying child of any other U.S. taxpayer
- Gross income and support tests are met

## Tax Benefits from Claiming a Qualifying Relative

1. **[[Credit for Other Dependents]]** - $500 credit
2. **Head of Household** filing status (if the person is a qualifying person)
3. **Medical expense deduction** - Can deduct medical expenses you pay for them

**Note:** A qualifying relative does NOT qualify you for the [[Child Tax Credit]] or [[Earned Income Credit]].

## Qualifying Person for Head of Household

A qualifying relative can qualify you for Head of Household filing status if:

| If the person is your... | And... | Then they are a qualifying person |
|--------------------------|--------|----------------------------------|
| Parent | You can claim them as a dependent | Yes (even if they don't live with you) |
| Other qualifying relative | Lived with you more than half the year AND you can claim them as a dependent | Yes |
| Any qualifying relative | You can't claim them as a dependent | No |
| Qualifying relative only because they lived with you all year | Must be related in a listed way | No |
