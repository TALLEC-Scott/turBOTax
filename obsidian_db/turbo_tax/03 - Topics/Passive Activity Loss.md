# Passive Activity Loss

## Overview

A passive activity loss is generally **not allowed** as a deduction in the current tax year. Instead, it is suspended and carried forward to future years when you have passive income or dispose of your entire interest in the activity.

Passive activity losses are subject to a complex set of rules under IRC Section 469 designed to prevent taxpayers from using losses from activities in which they don't meaningfully participate to offset other income.

## Definition

Your passive activity loss for the tax year is generally the **excess of passive activity deductions over passive activity gross income**.

For closely held corporations, the passive activity loss is the excess of passive activity deductions over the sum of passive activity gross income and net active income.

## Types of Passive Activities

There are two kinds of passive activities:

1. **Rental activities** - Even if you materially participate (unless you're a [[Real Estate Professional]])
2. **Trade or business activities** - In which you don't [[Material Participation|materially participate]]

## Key Exceptions and Allowances

### Special $25,000 Allowance

If you [[Active Participation|actively participate]] in rental real estate activities, you may deduct up to $25,000 of loss against nonpassive income, subject to income phaseout:

| Modified AGI | Allowance |
|--------------|-----------|
| $100,000 or less | Full $25,000 |
| $100,001 - $150,000 | Reduced by 50¢ per dollar over $100,000 |
| $150,000 or more | $0 |

Married filing separately (lived apart all year): Maximum $12,500, phaseout begins at $50,000.

### Real Estate Professional Status

If you qualify as a [[Real Estate Professional]], rental real estate activities in which you materially participate are **not** passive activities. This allows you to deduct losses without limitation.

### Former Passive Activities

If an activity was passive in earlier years but isn't passive in the current year, you can:
- Deduct prior-year unallowed losses up to current-year net income
- Treat remaining unallowed losses as passive losses

## Allocation of Disallowed Losses

If your passive activity loss is disallowed, a **ratable portion** of the loss from each passive activity is disallowed. The ratable portion is computed by:

$$\text{Disallowed portion} = \text{Total disallowed loss} \times \frac{\text{Loss from activity}}{\text{Sum of all losses}}$$

## Carryover of Disallowed Deductions

Disallowed passive activity losses:
- Are allocated among activities for the next tax year
- Must reasonably reflect which activities continue
- Are treated as deductions from the activity in the following year

## Order of Loss Limitations

Apply limitations in this order:

1. **Basis limitations** - Cannot deduct more than your basis
2. **At-risk limitations** - [[Form 6198]] limits losses to amounts at risk
3. **Passive activity limitations** - [[Form 8582]] limits passive losses
4. **Excess business loss limitation** - [[Form 461]] may further limit losses
