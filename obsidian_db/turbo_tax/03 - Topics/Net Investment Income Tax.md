# Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)

## Overview

The Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT) is a 3.8% tax on the lesser of net investment income or the excess of modified adjusted gross income (MAGI) over certain thresholds. It was enacted as part of the Affordable Care Act.

## Tax Rate

**3.8%** on taxable amount

## Income Thresholds (2025)

| Filing Status | Threshold |
|---------------|-----------|
| Single | $200,000 |
| Head of Household | $200,000 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | $200,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $250,000 |
| Married Filing Separately | $125,000 |

## What Is Net Investment Income

### Included Income
- **Interest** - Taxable interest from investments
- **Dividends** - Qualified and non-qualified dividends
- **Capital gains** - Short-term and long-term gains
- **Rental income** - Net rental and royalty income
- **Passive income** - From partnerships, S corporations, LLCs
- **Annuities** - Income from nonqualified annuities
- **Royalties** - From intellectual property, oil/gas rights

### NOT Included
- Wages and self-employment income
- Tax-exempt interest (municipal bonds)
- Social Security benefits
- Distributions from qualified retirement plans (401(k), 403(b))
- IRA distributions
- Active trade or business income
- Tax-exempt gain on sale of principal residence

## Calculating NIIT

### Formula
NIIT = 3.8% × **Lesser of:**
1. Net Investment Income, OR
2. MAGI - Threshold

### Example 1: Single Taxpayer
- MAGI: $250,000
- Threshold: $200,000
- Net Investment Income: $40,000

**Calculation:**
- Excess MAGI: $250,000 - $200,000 = $50,000
- Taxable amount: Lesser of $40,000 or $50,000 = $40,000
- NIIT: $40,000 × 3.8% = **$1,520**

### Example 2: Married Filing Jointly
- MAGI: $400,000
- Threshold: $250,000
- Net Investment Income: $100,000

**Calculation:**
- Excess MAGI: $400,000 - $250,000 = $150,000
- Taxable amount: Lesser of $100,000 or $150,000 = $100,000
- NIIT: $100,000 × 3.8% = **$3,800**

### Example 3: No Investment Income
- MAGI: $300,000 (all wages)
- Threshold: $200,000
- Net Investment Income: $0

**Result:** NIIT = $0 (no investment income to tax)

## Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI)

For NIIT purposes, MAGI = AGI plus:
- Foreign earned income exclusion (Form 2555)
- Foreign housing exclusion/deduction
- Excluded income from American Samoa/Puerto Rico

## Special Situations

### Passive Activity Income
- Income from passive activities is subject to NIIT
- Passive income includes rental real estate (unless real estate professional)
- Material participation may exclude income from NIIT

### Trusts and Estates
- NIIT applies to trusts and estates
- Threshold is much lower (indexed annually)
- See Form 8960 for calculations

### Nonresident Aliens
- Generally not subject to NIIT
- Different rules apply

## Estimated Tax Considerations

### Include in Calculations
- NIIT should be included in estimated tax calculations
- May require quarterly estimated payments
- Include on Form 1040-ES worksheets

### Form W-4 Adjustments
- Request additional withholding on Form W-4 Step 4(c)
- Helps cover NIIT liability
- Reduces need for estimated payments

### Safe Harbor Impact
- NIIT increases total tax liability
- Must be considered for underpayment penalty safe harbor

## Reducing NIIT

### Strategies
1. **Maximize retirement contributions** - Reduces MAGI
2. **Tax-loss harvesting** - Reduces net investment income
3. **Charitable donations** - Reduces MAGI through deductions
4. **Municipal bonds** - Interest is exempt from NIIT
5. **Real estate professional status** - May convert passive to active income

### Timing Considerations
- Defer capital gains to lower-income years
- Spread gains across tax years if possible
- Consider installment sales

## Reporting

### Form 8960
Use Form 8960 (Net Investment Income Tax) to calculate:
- Part I: Investment income
- Part II: Investment expenses
- Part III: Net investment income
- Part IV: NIIT calculation

### Where to Report
- Report NIIT on Form 1040 Schedule 2
- Include in total tax liability

## Interaction with Other Taxes

### Additional Medicare Tax
Both taxes may apply:
- Additional Medicare Tax: 0.9% on earned income
- NIIT: 3.8% on investment income
- Different thresholds, different income bases

### Total Potential Surtax
For high-income taxpayers above both thresholds:
- Earned income: 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax
- Investment income: 3.8% NIIT
- Total surtax up to 4.7% on combined income
