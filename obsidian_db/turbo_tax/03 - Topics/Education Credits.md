# Education Credits

## Overview

Education credits reduce your income tax dollar-for-dollar for qualified education expenses paid for higher education. There are two main education credits: the [[American Opportunity Credit]] and the [[Lifetime Learning Credit]].

**Authority:** [[Pub 970 - Tax Benefits for Education]] - Chapters 2 & 3

## Available Education Credits

| Credit | Maximum Amount | Per Student/Return |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| [[American Opportunity Credit]] | $2,500 | Per eligible student |
| [[Lifetime Learning Credit]] | $2,000 | Per tax return |

## Key Differences

| Feature | American Opportunity Credit | Lifetime Learning Credit |
|---------|---------------------------|-------------------------|
| Refundable | Yes (40%) | No |
| Years Allowed | 4 years per student | Unlimited |
| Degree Required | Yes | No |
| Enrollment | At least half-time | Any enrollment |
| Felony Drug Conviction | Disqualifies | No restriction |
| Course Materials | All required materials | Must be paid to institution |

## Eligibility Requirements

### General Requirements (Both Credits)
- Paid qualified education expenses
- Expenses for eligible student at eligible institution
- Student is you, your spouse, or your dependent
- Listed on Form 1098-T

### Income Limits (2025)

| Filing Status | Phase-Out Begins | Phase-Out Ends |
|---------------|------------------|----------------|
| Single, HoH, QSS | $80,000 | $90,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $160,000 | $180,000 |

### Cannot Claim If:
- Married Filing Separately
- Claimed as dependent on another's return
- MAGI exceeds limits
- Nonresident alien (unless electing resident status)

## Choosing Between Credits

**Per-Student Election:**
- Must choose ONE credit per student per year
- Cannot claim both credits for same student

**Multiple Students:**
- Can claim AOTC for one student
- And LLC for another student
- In the same tax year

**AOTC Usually Better Because:**
- Higher maximum ($2,500 vs $2,000)
- 40% refundable
- But limited to 4 years per student

## Qualified Education Expenses

### American Opportunity Credit
- Tuition and required fees
- Books, supplies, equipment required for courses
- Even if not purchased from institution

### Lifetime Learning Credit
- Tuition and required fees
- Course-related books, supplies, equipment required to be paid to institution

### Timing of Payment
- Paid in 2025 for academic periods:
  - Beginning in 2025, OR
  - Beginning in first 3 months of 2026

## Form 1098-T Requirement

**Must receive Form 1098-T** from eligible educational institution unless:
- Institution not required to provide it
- You request it and institution refuses (must document)

### Information on Form 1098-T:
- Amounts paid for qualified tuition
- Scholarships/grants received
- Adjustments for prior years
- Whether enrolled half-time
- Whether graduate student

## Form Required

- [[Form 8863]] - Education Credits
- Must provide educational institution's EIN

## Coordination with Other Benefits

### Tax-Free Educational Assistance
Must reduce qualified expenses by:
- [[Scholarships and Fellowship Grants]] (tax-free portion)
- [[Coverdell ESA]] distributions (tax-free portion)
- [[Qualified Tuition Program]] distributions (tax-free portion)
- [[Employer-Provided Educational Assistance]]
- Veterans' educational assistance

### Multiple Benefits
- Cannot use same expenses for multiple benefits
- Consider which combination minimizes tax
- May benefit from including scholarship in income

### Q: Can I claim education credits for my child's college expenses?
**A:** Yes, if you claim your child as a dependent and paid qualified expenses. If your child is not your dependent, they may claim the credit.

### Q: What if my income exceeds the limits?
**A:** You cannot claim either education credit if your MAGI exceeds $90,000 ($180,000 MFJ).

### Q: Can I claim both credits in the same year?
**A:** Yes, but not for the same student. You can claim AOTC for one student and LLC for another.
