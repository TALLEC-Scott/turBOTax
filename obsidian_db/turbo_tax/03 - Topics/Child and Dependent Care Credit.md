# Child and Dependent Care Credit

A tax credit for expenses paid for the care of a [[Qualifying Person]] to allow the taxpayer (and spouse if filing jointly) to work or look for work. Covered in [[Pub 503 - Child and Dependent Care Expenses]].

## Overview

The credit can be worth **up to 35%** of your work-related expenses, subject to dollar limits and earned income limits. You must file Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR and complete [[Form 2441]].

## Eligibility Requirements

To claim the credit, you must meet ALL of these tests:

### 1. Qualifying Person Test
The care must be for one or more [[Qualifying Person|qualifying persons]]:
- Your dependent child under age 13
- Your spouse who is physically or mentally unable to care for themselves
- A dependent who is physically or mentally unable to care for themselves

### 2. Earned Income Test
You (and your spouse if filing jointly) must have earned income during the year.

**Special rule for students/disabled spouses:** A spouse who is a full-time student or not able to care for themselves is treated as having earned income of at least $250/month (one qualifying person) or $500/month (two or more).

### 3. Work-Related Expense Test
Expenses must be [[Work-Related Expenses|work-related]] - paid so you can work or look for work.

### 4. Payment Test
You cannot make payments to:
- Your spouse
- A person you can claim as a dependent
- Your child under age 19 (even if not your dependent)
- The other parent of your qualifying child (if child is under 13)

### 5. Joint Return Test
If married, you must file a joint return. **Exceptions:**
- Legally separated under decree of divorce or separate maintenance
- Living apart from spouse for last 6 months of year and meet other requirements

### 6. Provider Identification Test
You must identify the care provider on your return with:
- Name
- Address
- Taxpayer identification number (SSN, ITIN, or EIN)

## Dollar Limits

| Number of Qualifying Persons | Maximum Expenses |
|------------------------------|------------------|
| One | $3,000 |
| Two or more | $6,000 |

**Important:** The dollar limit is a yearly limit. It remains the same no matter how long you had a qualifying person during the year.

**Reduced Dollar Limit:** If you exclude or deduct [[Dependent Care Benefits]] from your income, you must subtract that amount from the dollar limit.

## Credit Percentage

The credit percentage is based on your AGI:

| AGI Range | Percentage |
|-----------|------------|
| $0 - $15,000 | 35% |
| $15,000 - $17,000 | 34% |
| $17,000 - $19,000 | 33% |
| $19,000 - $21,000 | 32% |
| $21,000 - $23,000 | 31% |
| $23,000 - $25,000 | 30% |
| $25,000 - $27,000 | 29% |
| $27,000 - $29,000 | 28% |
| $29,000 - $31,000 | 27% |
| $31,000 - $33,000 | 26% |
| $33,000 - $35,000 | 25% |
| $35,000 - $37,000 | 24% |
| $37,000 - $39,000 | 23% |
| $39,000 - $41,000 | 22% |
| $41,000 - $43,000 | 21% |
| Over $43,000 | 20% |

## Earned Income Limit

Your work-related expenses used for the credit cannot exceed:
- Your earned income (if single)
- The smaller of your or your spouse's earned income (if married)

## How to Claim

1. Complete [[Form 2441]], Child and Dependent Care Expenses
2. Attach to Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR
3. Enter credit on Schedule 3 (Form 1040), line 2

**Limitation:** The credit is limited to your tax liability. You cannot get a refund for any credit amount exceeding your tax.

## Examples

### Example 1: Basic Calculation
George is a widower with one child, earns $24,000/year. He pays $2,900 for childcare.
- Dollar limit: $3,000 (one qualifying person)
- Actual expenses: $2,900
- Credit percentage: 32% (AGI between $19,000-$21,000... wait, AGI is $24,000)
- Actually percentage would be ~30% based on the table
- Credit: $2,900 × applicable percentage

### Example 2: With Dependent Care Benefits
Randall has two children, pays $6,000 for daycare. His employer provides $5,000 in dependent care benefits (excluded from income).
- Original dollar limit: $6,000 (two qualifying persons)
- Minus excluded benefits: -$5,000
- Reduced dollar limit: $1,000
- Credit calculated on $1,000 maximum

## Coordination with Other Benefits

- **Medical expenses:** Expenses that don't qualify for this credit may qualify as [[Medical and Dental Expenses]]
- **Employer benefits:** See [[Dependent Care Benefits]] for exclusion rules
- **Earned Income Credit:** Combat pay election can differ between credits
