# Foreign Intermediaries

## Overview

A **foreign intermediary** is a person (custodian, broker, nominee, or other agent) that receives payments on behalf of another person. Foreign intermediaries play a critical role in the withholding tax system, as they can assume withholding responsibilities on behalf of withholding agents.

## Types of Intermediaries

### Qualified Intermediary (QI)
A QI is a foreign intermediary (or foreign branch of a U.S. intermediary) that has entered into a **QI agreement** with the IRS.

**Key Features:**
- Can assume primary Chapter 3 and Chapter 4 withholding responsibility
- Can assume primary Form 1099 reporting responsibility
- Must have a Global Intermediary Identification Number (GIIN)
- Can be an FFI or NFFE
- Can report on a pooled basis rather than per-account-holder basis

**QI Responsibilities:**
- Provide withholding statements to withholding agents
- Designate accounts for which it acts as QI
- Maintain documentation for account holders
- Withhold and report as required under the QI agreement

### Nonqualified Intermediary (NQI)
An NQI is a foreign intermediary that has **not** entered into a QI agreement with the IRS.

**Key Features:**
- Cannot assume primary withholding responsibility
- Must provide detailed withholding statements
- Must provide payee-specific allocation information
- Alternative procedures may apply for timing of information

**NQI Withholding Statement Requirements:**
- Name, address, and TIN of each payee
- Type of documentation provided for each payee
- Status of each payee (U.S. or foreign)
- Allocation of each payment by income type
- Applicable withholding rate for each foreign payee
- Country of residence for treaty purposes

### Withholding Foreign Partnership (WP)
A foreign partnership that has entered into a **WP agreement** with the IRS.

**Key Features:**
- Must assume primary Chapter 3 and Chapter 4 withholding responsibility
- Must assume reporting requirements for U.S. partners
- Uses WP-EIN for withholding purposes
- Must register for FATCA if an FFI

### Withholding Foreign Trust (WT)
A foreign simple or grantor trust that has entered into a **WT agreement** with the IRS.

**Key Features:**
- Similar to WP but for trust structures
- Uses WT-EIN for withholding purposes
- Must assume withholding responsibility for beneficiaries

## Documentation Requirements

### Form W-8IMY
All intermediaries must provide Form W-8IMY to withholding agents. The form:
- Certifies the intermediary's status
- Associates documentation for underlying payees
- Provides withholding statements

### Withholding Rate Pools
QIs and WPs/WTs can provide withholding rate pool information instead of payee-specific information:
- **Chapter 3 pools:** Same income type, same withholding rate
- **Chapter 4 pools:** Nonparticipating FFIs, recalcitrant account holders, U.S. payees

## Joint Account Treatment

A QI may apply joint account treatment to a partnership or trust if:
- It is a nonwithholding foreign partnership or trust
- It has appropriate chapter 4 status
- None of its owners are U.S. persons or flow-through entities
- It agrees to make records available upon request

## Agency Option

A QI may apply the agency option to a partnership or trust, allowing it to:
- Treat the partnership/trust as an agent of the QI
- Apply QI agreement provisions to partners/beneficiaries
- Treat partners/beneficiaries as indirect account holders
