# Tax Treaties for Withholding

## Overview

The United States has bilateral income tax treaties with numerous countries that may reduce or eliminate the 30% statutory withholding rate on U.S. source income paid to residents of treaty countries. Withholding agents must apply treaty benefits when properly claimed.

## How Treaty Benefits Apply

### Requirements
To claim treaty benefits, the foreign person must:
1. Be a **resident** of the treaty country (as defined in the treaty)
2. Meet any **limitation on benefits (LOB)** provisions
3. Provide valid **documentation** to the withholding agent
4. Meet any **specific requirements** in the treaty article

### Documentation
- **Individuals:** Form W-8BEN (with TIN and country of residence)
- **Entities:** Form W-8BEN-E (with certification of residency and LOB status)
- **Intermediaries/Flow-through entities:** Form W-8IMY with underlying documentation

## Common Treaty Withholding Rates

### Interest
| Type | Typical Treaty Rate |
|------|---------------------|
| Portfolio interest | 0% (statutory exemption) |
| Bank interest | 0% (many treaties) |
| Other interest | 0-15% |

### Dividends
| Type | Typical Treaty Rate |
|------|---------------------|
| Direct dividends | 5-15% |
| Portfolio dividends | 15% |
| Some treaties | 0% (for certain pension funds) |

### Royalties
| Type | Typical Treaty Rate |
|------|---------------------|
| Industrial royalties | 0-10% |
| Copyright royalties | 0% (many treaties) |
| Film/TV royalties | Varies widely |

### Personal Services
| Type | Typical Treatment |
|------|------------------|
| Independent services | Often 0% if no permanent establishment |
| Dependent services | Often exempt if ≤183 days and foreign employer |
| Teaching/Research | Often exempt for 2-3 years |
| Students/Trainees | Often exempt for limited period |

## Limitation on Benefits (LOB)

Many treaties include LOB provisions that require the claimant to meet additional tests:

### Common LOB Tests
- **Public company test:** Publicly traded on recognized stock exchange
- **Ownership-base erosion test:** >50% owned by residents, <50% base erosion
- **Active trade or business test:** Engaged in active trade or business
- **Derivative benefits test:** Owned by residents of other treaty countries

### LOB Documentation
Form W-8BEN-E requires entity to certify which LOB test is met.

## Treaty Suspension and Termination

### Current Suspensions (2025)
- **Russia:** Treaty partially suspended as of August 16, 2024
- **Belarus:** Partial suspension of USSR treaty as of December 17, 2024

### Terminated Treaties
- **Hungary:** Treaty terminated January 8, 2023; ceased effect for withholding January 1, 2024

**Effect:** Withholding agents must withhold at statutory 30% rate for affected payments.

## Special Treaty Provisions

### Artists and Athletes
- Many treaties exempt income ≤$10,000 (or $20,000 in newer treaties)
- Cannot claim exemption based on factors unknown at year-end
- May use Central Withholding Agreement (CWA) instead

### Gambling Winnings
Certain countries exempt gambling income:
- Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Japan, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Netherlands, Slovak Republic, Slovenia, South Africa, Spain, Sweden, Tunisia, Turkey, Ukraine, United Kingdom

### Government Services
Most treaties exempt payments to foreign governments and their employees.

## Claiming Treaty Benefits

### By Individuals
1. Complete Form W-8BEN
2. Provide U.S. or foreign TIN
3. Certify country of residence
4. State article and basis for reduced rate

### By Entities
1. Complete Form W-8BEN-E
2. Provide chapter 4 status and GIIN (if applicable)
3. Certify residency and meet LOB requirements
4. State article and basis for reduced rate

### By Intermediaries
- Form W-8IMY with withholding statement allocating to claimants
- Underlying Forms W-8BEN or W-8BEN-E for each claimant

## Fiscally Transparent Entities

For treaty purposes, an entity is fiscally transparent if:
- Interest holders must account for income currently
- Character and source determined as if realized directly

**Treatment:**
- Entity claims treaty benefits as resident if not fiscally transparent in treaty country
- Interest holders claim benefits if entity is fiscally transparent
