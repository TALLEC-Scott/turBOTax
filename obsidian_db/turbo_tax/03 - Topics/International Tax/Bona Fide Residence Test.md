# Bona Fide Residence Test

## Overview

The Bona Fide Residence Test determines whether an individual qualifies as a bona fide resident of a U.S. territory for tax purposes. Qualifying as a bona fide resident can provide significant tax benefits, including the ability to exclude territory-source income from U.S. federal income tax.

## Three-Part Test

To qualify as a bona fide resident of a U.S. territory, you must meet all three requirements during the tax year:

1. **Presence Test** - Physical presence in the territory
2. **Tax Home Test** - No tax home outside the territory
3. **Closer Connection Test** - Closer connection to the territory than to the U.S. or foreign country

## Presence Test Requirements

A U.S. citizen or resident alien meets the presence test if **any one** of the following conditions is met:

### Option 1: 183-Day Test
Present in the relevant territory for at least 183 days during the tax year.

### Option 2: 549-Day Test (3-Year Period)
Present in the territory for at least 549 days during the 3-year period including:
- Current tax year
- 2 immediately preceding tax years

**Additional requirement**: Must be present in the territory for at least 60 days during each year of the 3-year period.

### Option 3: 90-Day U.S. Presence Test
Present in the United States for no more than 90 days during the tax year.

### Option 4: $3,000 Earned Income Test
All of the following must be true:
- Earned income in the U.S. of $3,000 or less
- Present in the territory for more days than in the U.S.
- No significant connection to the U.S.

### Option 5: No Significant Connection Test
Had no significant connection to the United States during the tax year.

## Special Rules for Nonresident Aliens

Nonresident aliens of the United States must use the **substantial presence test** from Pub. 519, substituting the territory name for "United States."

## Days of Presence in Territory

You are considered present in the territory on:
- Any day physically present in the territory
- Days outside territory for qualifying medical treatment (for yourself or accompanying family)
- Days outside territory due to major disaster or mandatory evacuation
- Up to 30 days outside both the territory and U.S. for business/personal travel

## Days Not Counted as U.S. Presence

Days not counted as U.S. presence include:
- Days for qualifying medical treatment
- Days due to major disaster or mandatory evacuation in territory
- Days in U.S. for less than 24 hours while traveling between two places outside the U.S.
- Days as professional athlete competing in charitable sports event
- Days as a student
- Days serving as elected territory representative or government employee

## Significant Connection Factors

You have a significant connection to the U.S. if:
- Permanent home in the United States
- Registered to vote in any U.S. political subdivision
- Spouse or child under 18 with main home in the U.S. (with exceptions)

## Tax Home Test

Your tax home is your regular or main place of business, employment, or post of duty.

**Exceptions for:**
- Students temporarily in the U.S.
- Government officials serving territory
- Seafarers on vessels predominantly in local/international waters

## Closer Connection Test

Factors determining closer connection include:
- Location of family
- Location of permanent home
- Location of personal belongings
- Location of banking activities
- Location of social, political, cultural organizations
- Location of business activities
- Where you vote
- Where you hold a driver's license
- Charitable organizations you contribute to
