# Self-Employment Tax for Territory Residents

## Overview

Bona fide residents of U.S. territories who have self-employment income must generally pay self-employment tax to the United States, even if they have no income tax filing obligation with the United States. This includes both Social Security and Medicare taxes.

## General Rule

**All bona fide residents of U.S. territories are subject to U.S. self-employment tax on net earnings of $400 or more.**

This applies regardless of:
- Whether the income is excludable from U.S. income tax
- Whether a U.S. income tax return must otherwise be filed

## 2025 Thresholds and Rates

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Maximum income subject to Social Security tax | $176,100 |
| Maximum income for 2026 | $184,500 |
| Maximum for optional methods | $7,240 (2025); $7,560 (2026) |

## Which Form to File

| Situation | Form to File |
|-----------|--------------|
| Required to file Form 1040/1040-SR | Attach Schedule SE to Form 1040/1040-SR |
| No U.S. income tax filing requirement | File Form 1040-SS |
| Required to pay Additional Medicare Tax | Also attach Form 8959 |

## Important Notes

- **Do NOT file Form 1040-SS with Form 1040 or 1040-SR**
- Form 1040-SS is filed with the United States, not the territory

## Self-Employment Tax Deduction

### If Income Is Partially Excluded

When part of self-employment income is excluded from U.S. tax:

```
SE Tax on Non-Excluded Income = Total SE Tax × (Non-Excluded SE Income ÷ Total SE Income)
```

### If All Income Is Excluded

Bona fide residents of American Samoa or Puerto Rico who exclude ALL self-employment income:
- **Cannot** claim the SE tax deduction on Schedule 1, line 15
- The deduction is related to excluded income

### Deduction Calculation

The deductible part of SE tax is calculated on the non-excluded portion:

1. Calculate total SE tax on Schedule SE
2. Multiply by ratio of non-excluded to total SE income
3. Include deductible part on Schedule 1, line 15

## Additional Medicare Tax

In addition to self-employment tax, you may owe Additional Medicare Tax:

### Thresholds

| Filing Status | Threshold |
|---------------|-----------|
| Married Filing Separately | $125,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $250,000 |
| Single, Head of Household, Qualifying Surviving Spouse | $200,000 |

### Rate

0.9% on earned income above threshold

### Forms

- File Form 8959 with Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-SS

**Note**: Cannot include Additional Medicare Tax as deductible part of self-employment tax.

## Estimated Tax Payments

If you expect to owe $1,000 or more in self-employment tax:
1. Use Form 1040-ES to calculate and pay estimated tax
2. Make quarterly payments to avoid underpayment penalty
3. Pay to the jurisdiction where you would file your return

## Chapter 11 Bankruptcy Cases

If you are a debtor in a Chapter 11 bankruptcy case:
- Net profit/loss from self-employment is reported on Form 1041 (bankruptcy estate)
- **You** (not the estate) are responsible for SE tax on your net earnings

## Territory-Specific Notes

### American Samoa
- File Form 1040-SS for SE tax even if all AS income is excluded
- SE tax applies to income effectively connected with AS trade/business

### CNMI, Guam, USVI
- File Form 1040-SS if no U.S. income tax filing requirement
- SE tax applies to territory trade/business income

### Puerto Rico
- File Form 1040-SS for SE tax on PR self-employment income
- Even if income is excluded under Section 933
