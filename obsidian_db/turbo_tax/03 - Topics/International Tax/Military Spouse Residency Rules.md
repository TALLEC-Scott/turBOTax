# Military Spouse Residency Rules

## Overview

The Military Spouses Residency Relief Act (MSRRA) allows civilian spouses of active duty military members to maintain their prior tax residence when relocating with the service member under military orders. This has significant implications for income sourcing, tax filing, and withholding when military families are stationed in U.S. territories.

## MSRRA Provisions

### Election to Maintain Prior Tax Residence

A civilian spouse may choose to keep their prior residence or domicile for tax purposes when:
1. Accompanying a service member spouse relocating under military orders
2. Moving to a new duty station in one of the 50 states, D.C., or a U.S. territory
3. Both spouses had the same tax residence before relocating

### Key Tax Benefits

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Income sourcing | Services income sourced to prior tax residence |
| Withholding | May affect Form W-2 withholding claimed |
| Filing requirements | May change state/territory filing obligations |
| Same residence election | Spouse may elect same residence as service member regardless of marriage date |

## Income Source Rules Under MSRRA

### Civilian Spouse as Bona Fide Territory Resident

If the civilian spouse is a bona fide resident of a territory AND chooses to keep that territory as tax residence:
- Income for services is sourced to that territory
- Follow standard bona fide resident filing rules

### Civilian Spouse with 50-State/D.C. Tax Residence

If tax residence is in one of the 50 states or D.C.:
- Services income is U.S. source (specifically the state of residence)
- Only U.S. tax return required (not territory return) for wages/salaries/tips/self-employment
- May need Form 5074 or Form 8689 if territory income other than services

## Application by Territory

### CNMI, Guam, and USVI

If civilian spouse has 50-state/D.C. tax residence:
- File only U.S. tax return (Form 1040 or 1040-SR)
- File state/local return if required by state of residence
- File Form 5074 (CNMI/Guam) or Form 8689 (USVI) if territory income other than services

### American Samoa

- Different rules apply due to separate tax system
- Consult American Samoa Tax Office for guidance

### Puerto Rico

- Different rules apply
- Civilian spouse may need to file both U.S. and PR returns depending on circumstances

## Documentation Requirements

Maintain records showing:
- Service member's military orders
- Civilian spouse's prior tax residence
- Dates of relocation
- Election to maintain prior tax residence (if applicable)

## IRS Notices and Guidance

Refer to these IRS notices for detailed guidance:
- **Notice 2010-30**: Initial MSRRA guidance
- **Notice 2011-16**: Income sourcing rules
- **Notice 2012-41**: Additional clarifications

All notices available at IRS.gov/irb/

## State and Territory Consultation

In addition to IRS guidance, consult with:
- State tax authorities (for prior residence state)
- Territory tax authorities (for duty station territory)
- Military legal assistance offices
