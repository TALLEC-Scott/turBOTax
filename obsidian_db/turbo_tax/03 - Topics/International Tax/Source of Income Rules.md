# Source of Income Rules

## Overview

Determining the **source of income** is critical for withholding purposes because Chapter 3 and Chapter 4 withholding generally apply only to **U.S. source income** paid to foreign persons. The source rules vary by income type.

## Summary Table

| Income Type | Source Determination |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Personal services | Where services are performed |
| Dividends | Type of corporation (U.S. or foreign) |
| Interest | Residence of payer |
| Rents | Where property is located |
| Royalties (patents, copyrights) | Where property is used |
| Royalties (natural resources) | Where property is located |
| Pensions (contributions) | Where services were performed |
| Pensions (investment earnings) | Location of pension trust |
| Scholarships and grants | Residence of payer |
| Guarantee income | Residence of debtor or ECI connection |

## Personal Service Income

### General Rule
Income for personal services is sourced **where the services are performed**, regardless of:
- Where the contract was made
- Where payment is made
- Residence of the payer

### Partly Within/Without U.S.
Allocate income based on **time basis**:
```
U.S. Source Income = Total Pay × (Days in U.S. ÷ Total Days of Service)
```

### Exceptions
- **Crew members** of foreign vessels - Not U.S. source for temporary presence
- **Students/teachers** on F, J, M, Q visas - Special rules apply
- **90-day rule** - Compensation ≤$3,000 for ≤90 days from foreign employer may be foreign source

## Interest Income

### General Rule
Interest is sourced by the **residence of the payer**:
- Interest from U.S. persons = U.S. source
- Interest from foreign persons = Foreign source (with exceptions)

### Exceptions
- Interest from foreign corporations/partnerships engaged in U.S. trade or business may be U.S. source
- Interest paid by U.S. trade or business of foreign entity = U.S. source

## Dividend Income

### General Rule
Dividends are sourced by the **type of corporation**:
- Dividends from U.S. corporations = U.S. source
- Dividends from foreign corporations = Foreign source

### Exceptions
- Dividend equivalent payments - Special sourcing rules
- Certain distributions from foreign corporations with U.S. earnings

## Rents and Royalties

### Rents
Sourced by **location of property**:
- Real property in U.S. = U.S. source
- Personal property in U.S. = U.S. source

### Royalties
- **Patents, copyrights:** Sourced by where property is **used**
- **Natural resources:** Sourced by where property is **located**

## Guarantee Income

For guarantees of indebtedness issued after September 27, 2010:
- Paid by U.S. resident or corporation = U.S. source
- Paid by foreign person if ECI = U.S. source

## Pension Payments

### Compensation Element
Sourced the same as compensation for services performed:
- U.S. services = U.S. source
- Foreign services = Foreign source

### Investment Earnings Element
Sourced by **location of pension trust**:
- U.S. trust = U.S. source
- Foreign trust = Foreign source

## Scholarships and Fellowships

### General Rule
Sourced by **residence of payer**:
- U.S. entity = U.S. source
- Foreign entity = Foreign source

### Exception
Amounts for **activities outside the U.S.** = Foreign source

## Transportation Income

### U.S. Source Gross Transportation Income (USSGTI)
- 50% of income from transportation that begins **or ends** in the U.S.
- Subject to 4% tax (not 30% withholding)
- Chapter 4 withholding does not apply

## Special Situations

### Multiyear Compensation
Source determined over the period to which compensation is attributable.

### Fringe Benefits for Employees
Sourced on geographical basis:
- Housing - Employee's main job location
- Education - Employee's main job location
- Moving expenses - New job location

### Income from U.S. Possessions
Special rules apply for Guam, CNMI, USVI, and American Samoa.
