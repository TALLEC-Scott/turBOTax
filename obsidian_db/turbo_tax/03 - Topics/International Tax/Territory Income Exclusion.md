# Territory Income Exclusion

## Overview

Bona fide residents of certain U.S. territories may exclude territory-source income from their U.S. federal income tax return. This exclusion provides significant tax benefits for individuals who qualify as bona fide residents of American Samoa or Puerto Rico.

## Territories with Income Exclusion

| Territory | Exclusion Available | Legal Basis |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------|
| American Samoa | Yes | IRC Section 931 |
| Puerto Rico | Yes | IRC Section 933 |
| CNMI | No* | N/A |
| Guam | No* | N/A |
| USVI | No* | N/A |

*CNMI, Guam, and USVI bona fide residents generally file only with the territory, not the U.S., which provides similar practical benefit.

## American Samoa Exclusion (Section 931)

### Who Qualifies

U.S. citizens, U.S. nationals, and resident aliens who are bona fide residents of American Samoa for the entire tax year.

### What Can Be Excluded

- All American Samoa source income
- Does NOT include: U.S. Government wages for services performed as employee of the U.S. or its agencies

### How to Claim

1. File Form 1040 or 1040-SR with the United States
2. Complete and attach **Form 4563** to exclude American Samoa source income
3. Report worldwide income on American Samoa tax return

### Limitations

When income is excluded:
- Cannot claim deductions or credits related to excluded income
- Must allocate deductions between excluded and non-excluded income
- Self-employment tax still due on self-employment income

## Puerto Rico Exclusion (Section 933)

### Who Qualifies

Bona fide residents of Puerto Rico for the entire tax year.

### What Can Be Excluded

- Puerto Rico source income
- Does NOT include: U.S. Government wages

### U.S. Filing Requirement

Bona fide residents of Puerto Rico must file a U.S. return if:

1. Received U.S. source income, OR
2. Received income effectively connected with a U.S. trade or business, OR
3. Received income from sources outside Puerto Rico and the U.S.

### How to Claim

- Exclude Puerto Rico source income on U.S. return (no specific form required for Puerto Rico exclusion)
- File Puerto Rico tax return reporting worldwide income

## Deductions When Income Is Excluded

### General Rule

Deductions must be allocated between:
- Income subject to U.S. tax
- Excluded territory income

### Allocation Formula

```
Allowable Deduction = Total Deduction × (U.S. Taxable Income ÷ Total Gross Income)
```

### Types of Deductions

| Deduction Type | Treatment |
|----------------|-----------|
| Related to excluded income | Not deductible |
| Related to taxable income | Fully deductible |
| Not related to specific income | Must be allocated |

### Examples of Allocated Deductions

- Standard deduction (prorated)
- Medical expenses
- Charitable contributions
- Real estate taxes
- Home mortgage interest

## Special Deduction Rules (2025)

### Tips, Overtime, and Vehicle Loan Interest

For determining eligibility:
- **Must include** excluded territory income in modified AGI

For calculating deduction:
- Deduction allowed only for income **included** in U.S. gross income
- Cannot claim deduction for excluded tips or overtime

### Enhanced Deduction for Seniors

- Include excluded territory income in modified AGI for eligibility
- If eligible, claim full allowed deduction on U.S. return

## IRA Deductions

- **Do not** include excluded territory income when figuring IRA deduction limits
- Only consider compensation that is included in U.S. gross income

## Self-Employment Tax Deduction

If part of self-employment income is excluded:

```
SE Tax on Non-Excluded Income = Total SE Tax × (Non-Excluded SE Income ÷ Total SE Income)
```

## Standard Deduction Calculation

1. Calculate full standard deduction per Form 1040 instructions
2. Multiply by ratio of U.S. taxable income to total gross income
3. Note on return: "Standard deduction modified due to income excluded under section 931 (American Samoa) or section 933 (Puerto Rico)"

## Foreign Tax Credit with Exclusion

### General Rule

- Cannot claim foreign tax credit for taxes paid on **excluded** income
- Can claim credit for taxes paid on income reported on U.S. return

### Reduction Formula

For each income category on Form 1116:

```
Tax Reduction = (Excluded Income ÷ Total Income Subject to Territory Tax) × Territory Tax Paid
```
