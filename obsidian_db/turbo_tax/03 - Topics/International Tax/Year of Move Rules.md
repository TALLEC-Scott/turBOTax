# Year of Move Rules

## Overview

Special rules apply in the tax year you move to or from a U.S. territory. These rules help determine whether you qualify for bona fide residency status during a transition year, which affects your tax filing obligations and available exclusions.

## Moving TO a Territory

### Year of Move Requirements

You will satisfy the tax home and closer connection tests in the year of moving to a territory if:

1. **Not previously a bona fide resident** of the territory in any of the 3 immediately preceding tax years
2. **Last 183 days of year**: No tax home outside territory AND no closer connection to U.S. or foreign country
3. **Subsequent residency**: Bona fide resident of the territory for each of the 3 tax years following the move

### Example: Moving to USVI

- Lived in U.S. January 2019 through May 2025
- Moved to USVI in June 2025, purchased home, started job
- From July 1 - December 31, 2025 (183+ days): Principal place of business in USVI
- If bona fide resident of USVI for 2026, 2027, and 2028 → Qualify as bona fide resident for 2025

## Moving FROM a Territory

### General Rule (CNMI, Guam, USVI, American Samoa)

You will satisfy the tax home and closer connection tests for the year of moving from a territory if:

1. **Previous residency**: Bona fide resident for each of the 3 immediately preceding tax years
2. **First 183 days of year**: No tax home outside territory AND no closer connection to U.S. or foreign country
3. **No subsequent residency**: Not a bona fide resident for any of the 3 following tax years

### Example: Moving from American Samoa

- Bona fide resident 2022-2024
- Lived in American Samoa until September 6, 2025
- Principal place of business January 1 - September 5, 2025 (183+ days)
- Moved to Hawaii for new employment
- If not bona fide resident for 2026, 2027, 2028 → Qualify as bona fide resident for 2025

## Puerto Rico Special Rules

Puerto Rico has **different rules** for the year of moving FROM the territory.

### Puerto Rico Year-of-Move Requirements

You will be considered a bona fide resident for the part of the tax year **preceding** the date you move if:

1. U.S. citizen
2. Bona fide resident of Puerto Rico for at least **2 tax years** immediately preceding the move
3. Cease to be bona fide resident during the tax year
4. Cease to have tax home in Puerto Rico during the tax year
5. Had closer connection to Puerto Rico than to U.S./foreign country throughout the part of the year before ceasing tax home

### Example: Moving from Puerto Rico

- Bona fide resident 2023 and 2024
- January - May 4, 2025: Resided and worked in Puerto Rico
- May 5, 2025: Moved and changed tax home to Nevada
- Result: Bona fide resident January 1 - May 4, 2025

**Note**: This rule allows partial-year bona fide residency even if the presence test is not met for the full year.

## Reporting Requirements

### Form 8898 Requirement

You must file Form 8898 if:

1. Worldwide gross income > $75,000 for the year, AND
2. You take a position for U.S. tax purposes that you:
   - Became a bona fide resident of a territory (after filing as U.S. resident)
   - Ceased to be a bona fide resident of a territory
   - Became a bona fide resident of Puerto Rico or American Samoa (after being resident of CNMI, Guam, or USVI)

### Penalty for Not Filing

- $1,000 penalty for failure to file Form 8898
- Penalty may be waived for reasonable cause

## Tax Planning Considerations

### Timing of Move

Consider the impact on:
- Presence test calculations
- Tax home determination
- Closer connection factors

### Documentation

Maintain records of:
- Move date
- Change of tax home
- New employment/business location
- Sale/purchase of residence
- Voter registration changes
- Driver's license changes
