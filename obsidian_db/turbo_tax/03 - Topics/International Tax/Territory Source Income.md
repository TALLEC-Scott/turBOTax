# Territory Source Income

## Overview

Territory Source Income refers to income that is sourced within a U.S. territory (American Samoa, CNMI, Guam, Puerto Rico, or USVI). Determining the source of income is critical for figuring which tax returns to file and whether income can be excluded from U.S. taxation.

## General Rule

The same rules used to determine U.S. source income generally apply to determine territory source income, with some important exceptions.

## Source Rules by Income Type

### Compensation for Labor or Personal Services

**General Rule**: Income is sourced where services are performed.

| Situation | Source |
|-----------|--------|
| Services wholly in territory | Territory source |
| Services partly in/partly out | Allocated based on time or geography |
| Military active duty | State of legal residence |

**De Minimis Exception**: No territory income if:
- Not a bona fide resident of the territory
- U.S. citizen or resident
- Services for 90 days or less
- Earned $3,000 or less
- Not employed by entity engaged in trade/business in territory

### Investment Income

| Income Type | Source Determination |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Interest | Residence of payer |
| Dividends | Where corporation is organized |
| Rents | Location of property |
| Royalties (natural resources) | Location of property |
| Royalties (patents, etc.) | Where property is used |

### Sales of Property

| Property Type | Source Determination |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Real property | Location of property |
| Personal property | Seller's tax home |
| Purchased inventory | Where sold (title passes) |
| Produced inventory | Allocation based on production/sale locations |

## Special Rules for Certain Property Dispositions

### Investment Property Owned Before Becoming Bona Fide Resident

Special rules apply if:
- You are a bona fide resident of a territory
- You were a U.S. citizen/resident in any of the 10 preceding years

**Impact**: Gains on certain investment property (stocks, bonds, etc.) are NOT treated as territory source income.

### Special Election

You may elect to treat gain attributable to the territory holding period as territory source income:
- **Marketable securities**: Based on FMV change during territory holding period
- **Other personal property**: Time-based allocation

## Effectively Connected Income

Income from sources outside the territory may be treated as effectively connected with a territory trade or business if:
1. Office/fixed place of business in territory
2. Office is material factor in producing income
3. Income produced in ordinary course of business

### Income Types Subject to ECI Rules
- Rents/royalties for intangible property outside territory
- Dividends/interest from banking business
- Gains from sale of inventory through territory office

## U.S. Armed Forces Special Rules

Military pay during active duty is sourced based on state of legal residence, regardless of where services are performed.

## Civilian Spouse Rules (MSRRA)

If civilian spouse keeps prior tax residence under MSRRA:
- Income for services is sourced to the prior tax residence
- Affects withholding and filing requirements

## Compensation Allocation Methods

### Time Basis (Default)

```
Territory Source Income = Total Compensation × (Days in Territory ÷ Total Work Days)
```

### Geographical Basis (Fringe Benefits)

Certain fringe benefits are sourced geographically:
- Housing
- Local transportation
- Education
- Tax reimbursement
- Moving expense reimbursement
- Hazardous/hardship duty pay

### Alternative Basis

You may use an alternative basis if it more properly determines source of income under the facts and circumstances.
