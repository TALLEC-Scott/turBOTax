# Alimony

## Overview

Alimony is a payment to or for a spouse or former spouse under a divorce or separation instrument. The tax treatment of alimony depends on when the divorce or separation instrument was executed.

## Tax Treatment by Instrument Date

### Instruments Executed After December 31, 2018

For divorce or separation instruments executed after 2018:
- **Not deductible** by the payer
- **Not includible** in the recipient's income

This is the result of the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017.

### Instruments Executed On or Before December 31, 2018

For instruments executed on or before December 31, 2018:
- **Deductible** by the payer (above-the-line deduction)
- **Includible** in the recipient's income
- Must report recipient's SSN or ITIN on tax return (Schedule 1, line 19b)
- $50 penalty for failing to provide SSN/ITIN

### Modified Instruments

If a pre-2019 instrument is modified after 2018 to expressly apply post-2018 rules, the new treatment applies to payments after modification.

## Requirements for Alimony (Pre-2019 Instruments)

A payment qualifies as alimony only if ALL of the following requirements are met:

1. **Cash payment** - Must be cash, check, or money order (not property transfers)
2. **No designation as non-alimony** - The instrument doesn't state the payment isn't alimony
3. **Not same household** - Spouses can't be members of the same household when payment is made (exception for written separation agreements)
4. **No liability after death** - No obligation to continue payments after recipient's death
5. **Not child support** - Payment isn't specifically designated as child support

## What Is NOT Alimony

- [[Child Support]] payments
- Payments that are spouse's part of community income
- Noncash property settlements
- Payments to keep up the payer's property
- Use of the payer's property
- Voluntary payments not made under a divorce instrument

## Payments to Third Parties

Cash payments to third parties can qualify as alimony if:
- Made under terms of divorce or separation instrument, OR
- Made at written request of spouse (must state intent to treat as alimony)

Examples include payments for spouse's medical expenses, housing costs, tuition, and taxes.

## Recapture Rule

If alimony payments decrease significantly during the first 3 calendar years, you may be subject to recapture. You must include in income (third year) part of previously deducted alimony.

### Exceptions to Recapture
- Payments under temporary support orders
- Payments that vary because they're a fixed part of income from business or employment
- Decreases due to death of either spouse
- Decreases due to remarriage of recipient spouse

## Reporting Alimony

### Payer (Pre-2019 Instruments)
- Report on Schedule 1 (Form 1040), line 19a
- Enter recipient's SSN or ITIN on line 19b
- Enter month and year of original divorce/separation agreement on line 19c

### Recipient (Pre-2019 Instruments)
- Report on Schedule 1 (Form 1040), line 2a
- Enter month and year of original agreement on line 2b
