# Labor Organizations (501(c)(5))

## Overview

Labor organizations are exempt under Section 501(c)(5). They include associations of workers who have combined to protect and promote the interests of members by bargaining collectively with employers to secure better working conditions, wages, and similar benefits.

## Definition

A labor organization is an association of workers that:
- Has the purpose of bettering conditions of workers
- Improves the grade of their products
- Develops a higher degree of efficiency in their occupations
- Does not allow net earnings to inure to members

## Organizational Requirements

### Purpose Statement
Organizing document must establish:
- Betterment of conditions for workers
- Improvement of products or services
- Development of worker efficiency
- Collective bargaining purposes

### Inurement Prohibition
No part of net earnings can inure to benefit of any member.

## Membership Composition

### Typical Members
- Employees or employee representatives
- Collective bargaining agents
- Similar employee groups

### Important Note
Evidence that membership consists mainly of workers does NOT automatically indicate exempt purpose. Must show organization has proper purposes.

## Permitted Activities

### Collective Bargaining
- Negotiating with employers
- Securing better wages and benefits
- Improving working conditions

### Member Benefits
May pay death, sick, and accident benefits to members:
- Must be under plan to better member conditions
- Does not preclude exemption

### Educational Activities
- Training programs for workers
- Safety education
- Professional development

## Prohibited Activities

### Primary Activity Restriction
Cannot have primary activity of providing strike fund controlled by private individuals who:
- Control the organization
- Pay benefits to workers

### Inurement
No net earnings can benefit members individually.

## Application Process

### Form 1024
Must file Form 1024 for recognition of exemption:
- Not required for exemption (self-declaration possible)
- Required for IRS determination letter
- File within 27 months for retroactive recognition

## Contributions

### Not Deductible
Contributions to labor organizations are NOT deductible as charitable contributions.

### Business Expense Deduction
May be deductible as business expense if:
- Ordinary and necessary in trade or business
- Subject to political activity limits

### Political/Lobbying Limitation
Cannot deduct portion of dues used for:
- Influencing legislation
- Political campaign activity
- Grass roots lobbying
- Direct contact with executive branch officials

## Filing Requirements

### Form 990
Must file annual information return:
- Form 990 for larger organizations
- Form 990-EZ for mid-sized organizations
- Form 990-N for small organizations

### Form 990-T
Required if unrelated business income exceeds $1,000.

## Related Organizations

### Similar Organizations
- Agricultural organizations (501(c)(5))
- Horticultural organizations (501(c)(5))
- Business leagues (501(c)(6))

### Key Differences from 501(c)(6)
- Labor organizations benefit workers
- Business leagues benefit employers/businesses
- Different deductible dues limits
