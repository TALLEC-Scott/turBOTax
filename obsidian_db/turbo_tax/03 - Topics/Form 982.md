# Form 982

## Overview

Form 982 (Reduction of Tax Attributes Due to Discharge of Indebtedness) is used to exclude discharged debt from income and to reduce tax attributes accordingly.

## When to File

File Form 982 if you had:
- Discharge of qualified principal residence indebtedness
- Discharge of indebtedness in a Title 11 bankruptcy case
- Discharge of indebtedness to the extent insolvent
- Discharge of qualified farm indebtedness
- Discharge of qualified real property business indebtedness

## Qualified Principal Residence Indebtedness

For homeowners, the most common use is excluding discharged mortgage debt.

### Requirements

- Debt was secured by your principal residence
- Debt was used to buy, build, or substantially improve the home
- Discharge occurred after 2006 and before 2026

### Maximum Exclusion

- **$750,000** ($375,000 if married filing separately)

## Tax Attribute Reductions

When you exclude debt discharge from income, you must reduce certain tax attributes:

1. **Basis of property** (including your home)
2. Net operating losses
3. General business credit carryovers
4. Minimum tax credit
5. Net capital loss carryovers
6. Passive activity loss and credit carryovers
7. Foreign tax credit carryovers

## Basis Reduction

For homeowners, the most common attribute reduction is to the **basis of your home**. You must reduce the basis (but not below zero) by the amount excluded.

## How to Complete

### Part I

Report the total amount of debt discharged and the amount you're excluding.

### Part II

Reduce tax attributes in the required order. For homeowners, this is typically:
- Line 10a: Basis reduction amount
- Line 10b: Specify property (your home)
