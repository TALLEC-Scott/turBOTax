# Private Operating Foundations

## Overview

A private operating foundation is a private foundation that devotes most of its resources to the active conduct of its exempt activities. Operating foundations receive more favorable treatment than other private foundations, including higher contribution limits for donors.

## Advantages Over Regular Private Foundations

| Feature | Operating Foundation | Regular Private Foundation |
|---------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| Cash contribution limit | 50% of AGI | 30% of AGI |
| Distribution requirement | Met by direct operations | Must distribute 5% |
| Excise tax on investment income | Still applies | Still applies |
| Self-dealing rules | Still apply | Still apply |

## Qualification Requirements

A private foundation qualifies as an operating foundation if it meets the Income Test and one of three additional tests:

### Income Test (Required)
Must meet the 85% income test:
- Distribute substantially all (85%+) of adjusted net income OR
- Distribute 85% of minimum investment return

### Additional Tests (Must Meet One)

#### 1. Assets Test
- At least 65% of assets are devoted to active conduct of exempt activities
- Assets used directly in carrying out exempt purposes

#### 2. Endowment Test
- Normally makes qualifying distributions of 2/3 of minimum investment return
- Distributed directly for active conduct of exempt activities

#### 3. Support Test
- Receive at least 85% of support (other than gross investment income) from general public
- Receive 85% from 5 or more exempt organizations
- Not more than 25% from any one organization

## Adjusted Net Income

For purposes of the income test, adjusted net income includes:
- Gross income from interest, dividends, rents, and royalties
- Minus expenses directly connected with such income
- Does not include capital gains

## Qualifying Distributions

For operating foundations, qualifying distributions include:
- Direct charitable expenditures
- Amounts paid for active conduct of exempt activities
- Reasonable administrative expenses
- Grants to individuals for scholarships (with approved procedures)

## Application Process

### Initial Determination
- Request operating foundation status on Form 1023
- Provide narrative description of operations
- Document intended operating activities

### Ongoing Qualification
- Must meet tests annually
- Report on Schedule A of Form 990-PF
- Three-year averaging for some tests

## Conduit Treatment

Operating foundations that distribute all net investment income within 120 days after year-end may qualify for conduit treatment, providing additional benefits.

## Excise Taxes

Operating foundations are still subject to:
- Tax on net investment income (Section 4940)
- Self-dealing restrictions (Section 4941)
- Excess business holdings limitations (Section 4943)
- Jeopardizing investment rules (Section 4944)
- Taxable expenditure rules (Section 4945)

## Changes in Status

### Loss of Operating Foundation Status
If foundation fails to meet requirements:
- Reverts to regular private foundation status
- Donor contribution limits change
- Distribution requirements change

### Regaining Status
- Must meet requirements for consecutive years
- File for redetermination if status changed
