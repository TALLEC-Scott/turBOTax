# Section 1231 Gains and Losses

## Overview

Section 1231 provides favorable tax treatment for gains and losses from the sale of business property held longer than one year. The treatment depends on whether you have a net gain or net loss.

## Section 1231 Transactions

Property qualifying for Section 1231 treatment:

| Property Type | Holding Period |
|---------------|----------------|
| Real property used in business | > 1 year |
| Depreciable personal property | > 1 year |
| Leaseholds used in business | > 1 year |
| Cattle and horses (draft, breeding, dairy, sporting) | ≥ 2 years |
| Other livestock (draft, breeding, dairy, sporting) | ≥ 1 year |
| Unharvested crops sold with land | Land held > 1 year |
| Timber, coal, iron ore disposal | Treated as sale |
| Condemned business/investment property | > 1 year |
| Business casualty/theft | > 1 year |

## Treatment of Net Gain vs. Net Loss

### Net Section 1231 Gain
1. First, apply depreciation recapture rules (ordinary income)
2. Remaining gain is long-term capital gain
3. **Exception**: Nonrecaptured section 1231 losses may convert gain to ordinary

### Net Section 1231 Loss
- Entire amount is **ordinary loss**
- No capital loss treatment

## Nonrecaptured Section 1231 Losses

### Definition
Net section 1231 losses from the previous 5 years that haven't been applied against a net section 1231 gain.

### Effect
Current year net section 1231 gain is ordinary income to the extent of nonrecaptured losses from prior 5 years.

### Example
| Year | Section 1231 Gain/(Loss) |
|------|--------------------------|
| 2020 | $0 |
| 2021 | $0 |
| 2022 | ($2,500) |
| 2023 | $0 |
| 2024 | $1,800 |
| 2025 | $2,000 (current) |

**Calculation for 2025:**
- Nonrecaptured losses: ($2,500) + $1,800 = ($700) remaining
- Ordinary income: $700
- Long-term capital gain: $1,300

## What Is NOT Section 1231

- Property held for sale to customers (inventory)
- Patents and copyrights created by personal efforts
- Property deducted under de minimis safe harbor
- Personal-use property

## Caution: Depreciation Recapture First

Before Section 1231 treatment, determine if depreciation recapture applies:
- [[Section 1245 Property]] - Full depreciation recaptured as ordinary income
- [[Section 1250 Property]] - Additional depreciation recaptured as ordinary income

Only gain in excess of recapture is Section 1231 gain.
