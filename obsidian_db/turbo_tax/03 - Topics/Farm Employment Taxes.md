# Farm Employment Taxes

## Overview

Farm employers must withhold and pay federal income tax, Social Security, and Medicare taxes on wages paid to agricultural employees. These taxes are reported on Form 943, Employer's Annual Federal Tax Return for Agricultural Employees.

## Who Is a Farm Employee?

An agricultural employee performs services in:
- Raising, feeding, caring for, or managing livestock, bees, poultry, or wildlife
- Cultivating, raising, or harvesting agricultural or horticultural crops
- Operating farm machinery or equipment
- Handling agricultural commodities in unmanufactured state

## Social Security and Medicare Taxes (FICA)

### Requirements
Withhold and pay FICA taxes if during the year you pay:
- **Cash wages** of $150 or more to a farmworker, OR
- Total cash wages of $2,500 or more to all farmworkers

### 2025 Rates
- **Social Security**: 6.2% each for employer and employee (up to $176,100)
- **Medicare**: 1.45% each for employer and employee (no limit)
- **Additional Medicare Tax**: 0.9% employee-only on wages over $200,000

## Federal Income Tax Withholding

Required if:
- Cash wages are subject to FICA taxes, AND
- Employee requests withholding, OR
- Employee fails to certify exemption status

Use Form W-4 for withholding certificates.

## Federal Unemployment Tax (FUTA)

### Requirements
Pay FUTA tax if during current or prior year you:
- Paid cash wages of $20,000 or more to farmworkers in any calendar quarter, OR
- Employed 10 or more farmworkers for at least part of a day in 20 different weeks

### Rate
- 6.0% on first $7,000 of each employee's wages
- Credit up to 5.4% for state unemployment tax paid
- Net rate can be as low as 0.6%

## Reporting and Deposits

### Form 943
- Annual return filed by January 31
- Reports all wages, tips, and taxes for agricultural employees
- File electronically if required

### Federal Tax Deposits
- Must use EFTPS (Electronic Federal Tax Payment System)
- Deposit schedule based on lookback period
- Monthly or semi-weekly depositors

### Form W-2
- Furnish to employees by January 31
- File with Social Security Administration by January 31
- Electronic filing required for 10 or more forms
