# Social and Recreation Clubs (501(c)(7))

## Overview

Social and recreation clubs are exempt under Section 501(c)(7). They are organized for pleasure, recreation, and other nonprofitable purposes, with substantially all activities for these purposes.

## Definition

A social club is an organization:
- Organized for pleasure, recreation, or other nonprofitable purposes
- Substantially all activities are for these purposes
- Supported by membership fees, dues, and assessments
- Does not allow inurement to private benefit

## Examples of Social Clubs

- Country clubs
- College fraternities and sororities
- Dinner clubs
- Amateur sport clubs (hunting, fishing, tennis, swimming)
- Hobby clubs
- Garden clubs
- Variety clubs
- Alumni associations (not qualifying as educational)

## Organizational Requirements

### Fellowship Requirement
Must show evidence of:
- Personal contact among members
- Commingling of members
- Fellowship as material part of organization

### Limited Membership
- Membership must be limited
- Limits must be consistent with club character
- Cannot be open to general public

### Nonprofit Purpose
- Organized for pleasure, recreation, other nonprofitable purposes
- "Other nonprofitable purposes" means similar to pleasure and recreation

## Discrimination Prohibition

### Prohibited Discrimination
Organization cannot discriminate on basis of:
- Race
- Color
- Religion

### Exception for Religious Clubs
Club may limit membership to particular religion if:
- To further teachings or principles of that religion
- Not to exclude individuals of particular race or color

### Auxiliary Exception
Religious discrimination restriction does not apply to auxiliary of fraternal beneficiary society if:
- Society described in Section 501(c)(8)
- Limits membership to particular religion

## Financial Requirements

### Support Sources
Should be supported by:
- Membership fees
- Dues
- Assessments
- Revenue from member use of facilities

### Gross Receipts Test

#### Nonmembership Income Limits
Section 501(c)(7) organizations can receive up to 35% of gross receipts from nonmembership sources:
- Of this 35%, up to 15% can be from general public use of facilities
- Includes investment income in the 35% calculation

#### Traditional vs. Nontraditional Activities
| Activity Type | With Members | With Nonmembers |
|---------------|--------------|-----------------|
| Traditional (exempt function) | Exempt function income | Subject to 15% limit |
| Nontraditional | Prohibited | Prohibited |

#### Nontraditional Activities
Examples of prohibited nontraditional activities:
- Sale of package liquor
- Take-out food sales
- Long-term room rental

### Inurement Prohibition
No net earnings can inure to benefit of:
- Any person with personal interest in activities
- Members through decreased dues without fee increase
- Members through increased services without fee increase

### Exception for New Member Recruitment
Fixed-fee payments to members bringing new members NOT inurement if:
- Reasonable compensation for administrative service

## Business Activities

### Generally Prohibited
Club should not engage in business such as:
- Selling real estate
- Selling timber or other products
- Commercial services

### Exception for Member Services
Providing meals, refreshments, or services to:
- Own members
- Members' dependents
- Members' guests

This does not cause denial of exemption.

## Corporate Memberships

Corporate members create special issues:
- Corporate members are not "members" in traditional sense
- Gross receipts from corporate members treated as outside income
- Employees using corporate memberships count as nonmember receipts

## Application Process

### Form 1024
File Form 1024 for recognition of exemption:
- Not required for exemption (self-declaration possible)
- Required for IRS determination letter

## Contributions

### Not Deductible
Donations to social and recreation clubs are NOT deductible as charitable contributions.

## Filing Requirements

### Form 990
Must file annual information return:
- Form 990 for larger organizations
- Form 990-EZ for mid-sized organizations
- Form 990-N for small organizations

### Form 990-T
Required if unrelated business income exceeds $1,000. Note that nonmember income is generally taxable.

## Fraternity Foundations

A foundation formed to acquire and lease chapter house to fraternity/sorority may be:
- Title holding corporation (501(c)(2) or 501(c)(25))
- NOT a social club if no social activities conducted
