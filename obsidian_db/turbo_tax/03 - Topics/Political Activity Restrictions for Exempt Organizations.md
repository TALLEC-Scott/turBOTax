# Political Activity Restrictions for Exempt Organizations

## Overview

Tax-exempt organizations face various restrictions on political and legislative activities. The restrictions vary significantly based on the organization's exempt classification under Section 501(c).

## Section 501(c)(3) Organizations - Strictest Rules

### Political Campaign Activity - Absolute Prohibition
Section 501(c)(3) organizations CANNOT:
- Participate or intervene in political campaigns
- Endorse or oppose candidates for public office
- Make contributions to political campaigns
- Publish campaign statements
- Use organizational resources for campaign activities

### Consequences
- Loss of tax-exempt status
- Possible excise taxes on organization and managers
- Donor contributions may become nondeductible

### Lobbying Limitations
- Cannot have substantial part of activities be lobbying
- May make Section 501(h) election for measurable limits
- Private foundations: Generally cannot lobby

### Section 501(h) Election
If elected, lobbying limits are:
| Exempt Purpose Expenditures | Lobbying Limit |
|-----------------------------|----------------|
| First $500,000 | 20% |
| Next $500,000 | 15% |
| Next $500,000 | 10% |
| Over $1,500,000 | 5% |

Maximum grass roots lobbying: 25% of total lobbying limit.

## Section 501(c)(4) Social Welfare Organizations

### Political Campaign Activity
- Permitted if not primary purpose
- Less than 50% of activities can be political
- No contribution limits to candidates
- Must report political expenditures

### Lobbying
- Unlimited lobbying permitted
- Can be primary activity

## Section 501(c)(5) and (c)(6) Organizations

### Political Activity
- Permitted with limitations
- Must report political expenditures
- Members must be notified of nondeductibility

### Lobbying
- Permitted
- Dues used for lobbying not deductible by members

## Section 527 Political Organizations

### Purpose
- Primary purpose is influencing elections
- Exempt function income not taxed
- Subject to reporting requirements

### Types
- Campaign committees
- Political action committees (PACs)
- Political party committees

## Definitions

### Political Campaign Activity
Activity that participates or intervenes in a campaign for public office, including:
- Endorsements
- Campaign contributions
- Voter guides with candidate comparisons
- Candidate forums with unequal treatment

### Lobbying
Activity that attempts to influence legislation:
- Direct lobbying: Contacting legislators
- Grass roots lobbying: Mobilizing public opinion

### Exempt Function Income
Income used for political purposes under Section 527.

## Reporting Requirements

### Form 990 Reporting
- Schedule C: Political Campaign and Lobbying Activities
- Report lobbying expenditures
- Report political activity expenditures

### Form 8871
Notice of Section 527 Status for political organizations.

### Form 8872
Political organization report of contributions and expenditures.

## Penalties

### Section 501(c)(3) Violations
- Revocation of exempt status
- Excise taxes under Section 4955

### Section 4955 Taxes
| Taxpayer | Tax Rate |
|----------|----------|
| Organization | 10% of political expenditure |
| Manager | 2.5% (knowing/willful) |
| Manager | 5% (knowing and willful) |

### Failure to Report
- Penalties for failure to file Form 8871 or 8872
- Contribution and expenditure penalties
