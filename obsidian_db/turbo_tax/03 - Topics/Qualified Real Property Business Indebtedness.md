# Qualified Real Property Business Indebtedness

## Overview

Qualified real property business indebtedness (QRPBI) is debt incurred in connection with real property used in a trade or business. Taxpayers can elect to exclude canceled QRPBI from income, subject to limitations.

## Requirements

All of the following conditions must be met:

1. **Business Use**: Debt incurred or assumed in connection with real property used in a trade or business
   - Does NOT include property developed and held primarily for sale to customers
   - Residential rental property generally qualifies (unless used as a home)

2. **Secured by Property**: The debt must be secured by that real property
   - Includes debt secured by 100% ownership interest in a disregarded entity holding real property (see Rev. Proc. 2014-20)

3. **Timing Requirements**: Debt was incurred or assumed:
   - Before 1993, OR
   - After 1992 if it is qualified acquisition indebtedness, OR
   - After 1992 if refinancing pre-1993 debt (limited to amount refinanced)

4. **Election Made**: Must elect to apply these rules

## Qualified Acquisition Indebtedness

Debt incurred to acquire, construct, reconstruct, or substantially improve real property used in a trade or business that secures the debt. Also includes refinancing of such debt (limited to amount refinanced).

## Exclusion Limits

The exclusion amount cannot exceed **either** of:

1. **Loan-to-Value Excess**: Outstanding principal over FMV of the business real property securing the debt
   - Reduce FMV by other qualified debt secured by that property

2. **Basis Limit**: Total adjusted basis of depreciable real property held immediately before cancellation
   - Use basis after any prior reductions from bankruptcy, insolvency, or farm debt exclusions

## Ordering Rules

Other exclusions must be applied **before** the QRPBI exclusion:

1. **Bankruptcy exclusion** - Must apply if debt canceled in Title 11 case
2. **Insolvency exclusion** - Must apply first if insolvent immediately before cancellation

## Making the Election

1. File [[Form 982]] with a timely filed return (including extensions)
2. Check the box on line 1d
3. Enter the excluded amount on line 2
4. Complete Part II, using line 4 to reduce basis in depreciable real property

### Revoking the Election

The election can only be revoked with IRS consent. If you timely filed without making the election, you can still make it by filing an amended return within 6 months of the due date (excluding extensions). Enter "Filed pursuant to section 301.9100-2" on the amended return.

## Reporting

After excluding QRPBI, you must reduce basis in depreciable real property using line 4 of Form 982.

## Example

**Scenario**: Retail store with $185,000 loan balance, FMV $165,000, adjusted basis $210,000

- Exclusion limit 1: $20,000 ($185,000 debt minus $165,000 FMV)
- Exclusion limit 2: $210,000 (adjusted basis)
- Lower limit applies: $20,000

Result: Can exclude the entire $20,000 of canceled debt; reduce basis by $20,000.
