# Filing Requirements

Whether you must file a federal income tax return depends on your gross income, filing status, age, and whether you are a dependent. Even if not required to file, you may want to file to claim a refund or tax credits.

## 2025 Filing Requirements for Most Taxpayers

File a return if your gross income was at least the amount shown:

| Filing Status | Age at End of 2025 | File If Gross Income At Least |
|---------------|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| Single | Under 65 | $15,750 |
| Single | 65 or older | $17,750 |
| Head of Household | Under 65 | $23,625 |
| Head of Household | 65 or older | $25,625 |
| Married Filing Jointly | Both under 65 | $31,500 |
| Married Filing Jointly | One spouse 65+ | $33,100 |
| Married Filing Jointly | Both 65+ | $34,700 |
| Married Filing Separately | Any age | $5 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | Under 65 | $31,500 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | 65 or older | $33,100 |

**Age 65 Rule:** For 2025, you are considered 65 on the day before your 65th birthday (born before January 2, 1961).

**Married Filing Separately:** If you didn't live with your spouse at the end of 2025 (or on the date your spouse died) and your gross income was at least $5, you must file regardless of age.

## Gross Income Definition

Gross income includes:
- All income in the form of money, goods, property, and services that isn't exempt from tax
- Income from sources outside the United States
- Gains (but not losses) from property sales
- Business gross receipts (or sales minus cost of goods sold)

**Social Security Benefits:** Do NOT include unless:
- Married filing separately and lived with spouse at any time during 2025, OR
- Half of benefits plus other gross income and tax-exempt interest exceeds $25,000 ($32,000 if MFJ)

## Filing Requirements for Dependents

If you can be claimed as a dependent on another person's return, use these rules instead of the table above.

### Single Dependents

**Under 65 and Not Blind:** File if ANY apply:
1. Unearned income over $1,350
2. Earned income over $15,750
3. Gross income more than the larger of $1,350 OR earned income (up to $15,300) plus $450

**65 or Older OR Blind:** File if ANY apply:
1. Unearned income over $3,350 ($5,350 if 65+ and blind)
2. Earned income over $17,750 ($19,750 if 65+ and blind)
3. Gross income more than the larger of $3,350 ($5,350 if 65+ and blind) OR earned income (up to $15,300) plus $2,450 ($4,450 if 65+ and blind)

### Married Dependents

**Under 65 and Not Blind:** File if ANY apply:
1. Gross income at least $5 AND spouse files separately and itemizes
2. Unearned income over $1,350
3. Earned income over $15,750
4. Gross income more than the larger of $1,350 OR earned income (up to $15,300) plus $450

**65 or Older OR Blind:** File if ANY apply:
1. Gross income at least $5 AND spouse files separately and itemizes
2. Unearned income over $2,950 ($4,550 if 65+ and blind)
3. Earned income over $17,350 ($18,950 if 65+ and blind)
4. Gross income more than the larger of $2,950 ($4,550 if 65+ and blind) OR earned income (up to $15,300) plus $2,050 ($3,650 if 65+ and blind)

## Other Situations Requiring You to File

You MUST file a return even if your income is below the thresholds if ANY of these apply:

### 1. Special Taxes (Schedule 2)
- Alternative minimum tax
- Additional tax on IRAs or other tax-favored accounts
- Social Security or Medicare tax on unreported tips
- Uncollected Social Security, Medicare, or railroad retirement tax
- Household employment taxes
- Recapture taxes

### 2. Health Savings Accounts
You (or spouse if MFJ) received Archer MSA, Medicare Advantage MSA, or HSA distributions.

### 3. Self-Employment
Net earnings from self-employment of at least $400.

### 4. Church Employees
Wages of $108.28 or more from a church or qualified church-controlled organization exempt from employer Social Security and Medicare taxes.

### 5. Premium Tax Credit
Advance payments of premium tax credit were made for you, your spouse, or a dependent through the Health Insurance Marketplace.

### 6. Section 965
Required to include amounts in income under section 965 or have net tax liability under section 965.

### 7. Clean Vehicle Credit
Purchased a new or used clean vehicle and transferred the credit to the dealer.

## Who Should File (Even If Not Required)

You should file a return if you can get money back:

1. **Income tax withheld** from your pay
2. **Estimated tax payments** made or overpayment applied from prior year
3. **[[Earned Income Credit]]** - See [[Pub 596 - Earned Income Credit]]
4. **Additional Child Tax Credit** - See Schedule 8812
5. **Refundable American Opportunity Credit** - See Form 8863
6. **Premium Tax Credit** - See Form 8962

### Form 1099-B or 1099-DA Received

Consider filing if:
- The amount in box 1d (1099-B) or box 1f (1099-DA) means you must file
- You received Form 1099-B or 1099-DA
- Box 1e (1099-B) or box 1g (1099-DA) is blank

Filing may prevent an IRS notice.

## Special Filing Situations

### U.S. Citizens Living Abroad
Include all income earned or received abroad, including income excluded under foreign earned income exclusion. See [[Pub 54 - Tax Guide for U.S. Citizens and Resident Aliens Abroad]].

### Residents of Puerto Rico
- File U.S. return if you meet income requirements
- Puerto Rico source income generally excluded from U.S. gross income
- Standard deduction may be reduced
- See [[Pub 570 - Tax Guide for Individuals With Income From U.S. Possessions]]

### Deceased Persons
File a return for a decedent if:
- You are the spouse, executor, administrator, or legal representative
- The decedent met filing requirements at time of death

See [[Pub 559 - Survivors, Executors, and Administrators]].

## Failure to File

- You may owe a penalty if required to file but fail to do so
- Willful failure to file may result in criminal prosecution
