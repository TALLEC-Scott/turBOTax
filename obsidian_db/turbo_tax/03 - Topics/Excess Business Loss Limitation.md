# Excess Business Loss Limitation

## Overview

The excess business loss limitation is an **additional restriction** on business losses that applies AFTER the basis, at-risk, and passive activity loss limitations. It was introduced by the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017 and affects noncorporate taxpayers.

## Who Is Affected

The limitation applies to:
- Individuals
- Trusts and estates

It does NOT apply to C corporations.

## Order of Loss Limitations

Apply limitations in this order:

| Order | Limitation | Form |
|-------|------------|------|
| 1 | Basis limitations | Various |
| 2 | At-risk limitations | [[Form 6198]] |
| 3 | Passive activity limitations | [[Form 8582]] |
| 4 | **Excess business loss limitation** | [[Form 461]] |

## Definition

An **excess business loss** is the excess of your total business deductions over the sum of:
1. Your total business gross income or gain, PLUS
2. A threshold amount

### Threshold Amounts (2025)

| Taxpayer | Threshold |
|----------|-----------|
| Single | $305,000 |
| Joint | $610,000 |
| Head of Household | $457,500 |
| MFS | $305,000 |
| Trust/Estate | Varies |

*Note: Thresholds are adjusted annually for inflation.*

## Calculation

```
Total Business Deductions
Minus: Total Business Gross Income/Gain
Equals: Net Business Loss

If Net Business Loss exceeds Threshold:
  Excess Business Loss = Net Business Loss - Threshold
```

## Effect of Excess Business Loss

If you have an excess business loss:

1. The excess is **not deductible** in the current year
2. The excess is treated as a **net operating loss (NOL) carryforward**
3. Report on Form 461 and carry forward to future years

## What Constitutes Business Items

### Business Deductions Include
- Trade or business expenses
- Deductions attributable to rental real estate activities (if not passive)
- Section 199A qualified business income deduction (not included)

### Business Gross Income Includes
- Income from trades or businesses
- Gains from business property dispositions
- Rental income (if nonpassive)

### Not Included
- Capital losses (subject to separate limitation)
- Investment expenses
- Portfolio income and deductions

## Coordination with Passive Activity Rules

The excess business loss limitation applies to losses that have already passed through:

1. **Basis tests** - Loss allowed to extent of basis
2. **At-risk tests** - Loss allowed to extent at risk
3. **Passive tests** - Nonpassive losses only

So it only affects **nonpassive business losses**.

## Examples

### Example 1: Single Taxpayer
- Business deductions: $400,000
- Business income: $50,000
- Net business loss: $350,000
- Threshold: $305,000
- **Excess business loss: $45,000**
- Current deduction: $305,000
- NOL carryforward: $45,000

### Example 2: Joint Filers
- Business deductions: $500,000
- Business income: $100,000
- Net business loss: $400,000
- Threshold: $610,000
- **No excess business loss** (loss under threshold)
- Current deduction: $400,000

## Reporting

- [[Form 461]] - Limitation on Business Losses
- The form calculates:
  - Total business deductions and income
  - Excess business loss amount
  - NOL carryforward

## Planning Considerations

### Income Timing
- Consider timing of income recognition to avoid limitation
- Multi-year planning for significant business losses

### Entity Structure
- C corporations are not subject to this limitation
- Consider entity choice for significant loss activities

### NOL Carryforward
- Excess business losses become NOL carryforwards
- NOL rules determine deductibility in future years
- Current NOL rules: 80% of taxable income limitation, indefinite carryforward
