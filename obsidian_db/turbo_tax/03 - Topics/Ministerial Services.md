# Ministerial Services

## Overview

Ministerial services are services performed in the exercise of ministry, in the exercise of duties required by a religious order, or in the exercise of a profession as a Christian Science practitioner or reader. Income from ministerial services is subject to [[Self-Employment Tax for Clergy|SE tax]] unless an exemption applies.

## Services That Qualify as Ministerial

### For Ministers
1. Performing sacerdotal functions
2. Conducting religious worship
3. Controlling, conducting, and maintaining religious organizations under the authority of a religious body that is a church or denomination

A minister is considered to control, conduct, and maintain a religious organization if they direct, manage, or promote the organization's activities.

### For Members of Religious Orders
Services performed as required by the order as its agent.

### For Christian Science Practitioners and Readers
Services performed in the exercise of their profession.

## Assigned or Designated Services

Services for nonreligious organizations are ministerial services if:
- The services are assigned or designated by your church
- They qualify even if they don't involve sacerdotal functions or conducting religious worship

If services aren't assigned by your church, they are ministerial only if they involve sacerdotal functions or conducting religious worship.

## Services That Are NOT Ministerial

### Government Employment
Services performed as an employee of the United States, District of Columbia, foreign government, or political subdivisions are NOT ministerial services, even if performing sacerdotal functions or conducting religious worship.

**Example:** Military chaplains in the Armed Forces perform non-ministerial services.

### Government Hospitals
Services in government-owned and operated hospitals are NOT ministerial services.

**Exception:** Services at church-related hospitals, health and welfare institutions, or private nonprofit hospitals ARE ministerial services.

### Nonreligious Organizations
Services for nonreligious organizations not assigned by your church.

## Writing Religious Materials

Writing religious books or articles IS considered to be in the exercise of ministry and is a ministerial service. This rule applies to:
- Ministers
- Members of religious orders
- Christian Science practitioners and readers

## Royalty Income

Royalty income from religious books or articles is treated the same as other ministerial income.

## Impact on Tax Treatment

| Aspect | Treatment |
|--------|-----------|
| SE Tax | Subject to SE tax (unless exempt) |
| Income Tax | Taxable as self-employment or wage income |
| Housing Exclusion | May qualify for [[Parsonage Allowance]] exclusion |
| Retirement Plan | May affect eligibility to establish self-employed retirement plans |

## Employment Status

A minister can be:
- **Common-law employee**: If church controls what and how services are performed
- **Self-employed**: For nonemployee services like weddings, baptisms, honoraria

Even if classified as an employee for income tax purposes, ministerial earnings are subject to SE tax for Social Security purposes.
