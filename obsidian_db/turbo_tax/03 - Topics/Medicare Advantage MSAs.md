# Medicare Advantage MSAs

## Overview

A Medicare Advantage MSA is an [[Archer MSAs|Archer MSA]] designated by Medicare to pay qualified medical expenses of the account holder who is enrolled in Medicare. This combines a high-deductible health plan with a tax-advantaged savings account funded by Medicare. See [[Pub 969 - Health Savings Accounts and Other Tax-Favored Health Plans]].

## Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible for a Medicare Advantage MSA, you must:
1. Be enrolled in Medicare
2. Have an HDHP that meets Medicare guidelines
3. The policy must be approved by the Medicare program

## Key Features

### Funding
- Contributions can ONLY be made by Medicare (not by you or your employer)
- Contributions are NOT included in your income
- Medicare deposits money into your account for qualified medical expenses

### Tax Treatment
- Contributions are not taxable
- Earnings (interest/dividends) are tax-free
- Distributions for qualified medical expenses are tax-free

### HDHP Requirements
You must choose an HDHP policy that:
- Meets Medicare guidelines for Medicare Advantage MSA plans
- Is approved by Medicare

## How It Works

1. **Enrollment:** Enroll in a Medicare Advantage MSA plan through Medicare
2. **Medicare Contribution:** Medicare deposits a set amount into your MSA
3. **Pay Medical Expenses:** Use MSA funds to pay for qualified medical expenses
4. **HDHP Coverage:** Your HDHP provides coverage after you meet the deductible

## Qualified Medical Expenses

Same as for [[Health Savings Accounts|HSAs]] and [[Archer MSAs]] - see [[Qualified Medical Expenses]].

Distributions used for qualified medical expenses are:
- Tax-free
- Not counted as income

## Distributions for Non-Qualified Expenses

If distributions are not used for qualified medical expenses:
- Amount is included in gross income
- May be subject to additional tax (similar to HSA rules)

## Account Ownership

- You own the account
- Funds can be used for any purpose, but only qualified medical expenses are tax-free
- Unused funds carry over from year to year

## Benefits

- Medicare-funded savings account
- Tax-free growth and distributions for medical expenses
- Choice of HDHP policy (Medicare-approved)
- Funds roll over if not used

## Important Considerations

### Not Both HSA and Medicare MSA
If you enroll in Medicare, you can no longer contribute to an [[Health Savings Accounts|HSA]]. However, you may be eligible for a Medicare Advantage MSA.

### Account Continues After Death
Upon death, the account is treated similar to Archer MSAs:
- **Spouse beneficiary:** Becomes spouse's account
- **Non-spouse beneficiary:** FMV taxable in year of death

## Comparison with Other Tax-Favored Health Plans

| Feature | Medicare Advantage MSA | HSA | Archer MSA |
|---------|------------------------|-----|------------|
| Who can contribute | Medicare only | Individual, employer, others | Individual OR employer |
| Must be enrolled in Medicare | Yes | No (disqualifies) | No (disqualifies) |
| Requires HDHP | Yes (Medicare-approved) | Yes | Yes |
| Earnings tax-free | Yes | Yes | Yes |
