# Moving Expenses (Military)

## Overview

Active duty members of the Armed Forces who move due to a permanent change of station (PCS) may deduct unreimbursed moving expenses. This is an exception to the general suspension of the moving expense deduction for civilian taxpayers.

## Who Can Deduct Moving Expenses

To deduct moving expenses, you must:
1. Be a member of the Armed Forces **on active duty**
2. Move due to a **military order**
3. Move as the result of a **permanent change of station (PCS)**

## What Qualifies as a Permanent Change of Station

A PCS includes:
- Move from your home to your first post of active duty
- Move from one permanent post of duty to another
- Move from your last post of duty to your home (or nearer point in the U.S.) within 1 year of ending active duty

### Spouse/Dependent Moves
If you're the spouse or dependent of a member who deserts, is imprisoned, or dies on active duty, a PCS includes moves to:
- The member's place of enlistment or induction
- Your or the member's home of record
- A nearer point in the United States

## Excludable Government Benefits

**Don't include in income:**
- Value of moving and storage services provided by the government
- Dislocation allowance
- Temporary lodging expense allowance
- Temporary lodging allowance
- Move-in housing allowance

### Excess Reimbursements
If total reimbursements exceed actual expenses:
- Excess is generally included in wages on Form W-2
- If not on W-2, report excess on Form 1040, line 1h

## Deductible Moving Expenses

### Moving Household Goods and Personal Effects
- Packing, crating, and hauling
- In-transit storage (up to 30 consecutive days)
- Insurance
- Hauling a trailer

**Not deductible:** Cost of moving furniture or goods purchased en route

### Travel Expenses
- Transportation from old home to new home (airfare, car expenses)
- Lodging during the move
- Parking fees and tolls

**Standard mileage rate (2025):** 21 cents per mile

**Not deductible:**
- Meals
- Unnecessary side trips
- Lavish or extravagant lodging

## Foreign Moves

A foreign move is:
- From the U.S. or its territories to a foreign country, OR
- From one foreign country to another

**Note:** Moving from a foreign country to the U.S. is NOT a foreign move.

### Additional Deductions for Foreign Moves
- Moving household goods to/from storage
- Storage costs while stationed abroad

## How to Report Moving Expenses

1. Complete **Form 3903** (Moving Expenses)
2. Carry deduction to **Schedule 1 (Form 1040), line 14**

Use separate Form 3903 for each qualifying move.

## Reservist Travel Expenses

Members of reserve components traveling **more than 100 miles** from home for reserve duty can deduct unreimbursed travel expenses:

| Expense Type | Deductible Amount |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Mileage | 70 cents/mile (2025) |
| Lodging | Actual cost |
| Meals | 50% of actual cost |

**Report on:** Schedule 1 (Form 1040), line 12
