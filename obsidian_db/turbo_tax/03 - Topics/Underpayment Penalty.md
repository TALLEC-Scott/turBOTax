# Underpayment Penalty

## Overview

The underpayment penalty applies when taxpayers do not pay enough tax throughout the year through withholding or estimated tax payments. The U.S. tax system is pay-as-you-go, meaning taxes must be paid as income is earned.

## When Penalty Applies

### General Rule
Penalty applies if required payments (withholding + estimated) do not equal the **lesser of:**
1. **90% of current year's tax**, OR
2. **100% of prior year's tax** (110% if AGI > $150,000)

### Safe Harbor Rules
No penalty if payments meet safe harbor requirements:
- **Regular taxpayers:** 100% of prior year tax
- **Higher income (AGI > $150,000):** 110% of prior year tax
- **Farmers/fishermen:** 66.67% of current year tax

## Exceptions to Penalty

### Automatic Exceptions (No Penalty)

#### Small Balance Exception
- Tax due is **less than $1,000** after withholding and credits

#### No Tax Liability Prior Year
- Had zero tax liability in prior year
- Was U.S. citizen or resident for entire prior year
- Prior year covered full 12 months

### Reasonable Cause Exceptions

#### Casualty, Disaster, or Unusual Circumstance
- Natural disaster, casualty, or similar event
- IRS may waive penalty on case-by-case basis

#### Retirement or Disability
- Retired after reaching age 62 during tax year or prior year, OR
- Became disabled during tax year or prior year
- Underpayment due to reasonable cause, not willful neglect

## Penalty Calculation

### How Penalty Is Determined
- Penalty is essentially **interest** on the underpaid amount
- Rate is the federal short-term rate plus 3%
- Compounded daily
- Calculated separately for each payment period

### Form 2210
Use Form 2210 to calculate penalty:
- **Part I:** Calculate underpayment for each period
- **Part II:** Apply exceptions
- **Part III:** Annualized income method (optional)
- **Part IV:** Final penalty calculation

### IRS Calculation
- IRS will calculate penalty and send bill
- Taxpayer can calculate on Form 2210 if preferred
- File Form 2210 to claim exceptions

## Payment Periods for Penalty

### 2025 Periods

| Period | Dates | Due Date |
|--------|-------|----------|
| 1 | January 1 - March 31 | April 15, 2025 |
| 2 | April 1 - May 31 | June 16, 2025 |
| 3 | June 1 - August 31 | September 15, 2025 |
| 4 | September 1 - December 31 | January 15, 2026 |

### Important: Payments Are Period-Specific
- Penalty calculated for each period separately
- Late payment in one period may cause penalty even if caught up later
- Withholding is treated as paid equally throughout year (unless actual dates proven)

## Avoiding the Penalty

### Methods to Meet Safe Harbor

#### Withholding (Preferred)
- Withholding is treated as paid evenly throughout the year
- Can increase withholding late in year to catch up
- Use [[Form W-4]] to adjust

#### Estimated Tax Payments
- Pay by quarterly due dates
- Use Form 1040-ES vouchers
- Can annualize if income varies

### Annualized Income Method
For variable income (seasonal, self-employment):
- Calculate tax based on actual income each period
- Use Form 2210 Schedule AI
- May reduce or eliminate penalty for uneven income

## Special Rules by Taxpayer Type

### Farmers and Fishermen
- Only one estimated payment due January 15
- Lower safe harbor: 66.67% of current year tax
- Must have at least 2/3 of gross income from farming/fishing
- Or file return and pay full tax by March 1

### Higher Income Taxpayers
If AGI exceeded $150,000 on prior year return:
- Must pay **110% of prior year tax** for safe harbor
- Higher threshold applies to MFJ and MFS

### Household Employers
- Include household employment taxes in estimated payments
- Use Schedule H (Form 1040) calculations

## Penalty Rates

### 2025 Rates (Quarterly)
Determined quarterly based on federal short-term rate + 3%:
- Q1: Announced by IRS
- Q2: Announced by IRS
- Q3: Announced by IRS
- Q4: Announced by IRS

Check IRS.gov for current rates.

## Filing Form 2210

### When to File
- With your tax return (by due date including extensions)
- IRS will calculate if you leave blank or write "RFC" (Request for Computation)

### Who Must File
- Required if using annualized income method
- Required if requesting waiver for casualty/disaster/retirement
- Optional if letting IRS calculate

### Required Schedules
- **Schedule AI:** Annualized Income Installment Method
- **Schedule AI (Form 2210-F):** Farmers and fishermen
