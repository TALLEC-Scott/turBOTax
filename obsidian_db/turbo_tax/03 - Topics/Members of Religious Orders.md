# Members of Religious Orders

## Overview

Members of religious orders have unique tax treatment that varies significantly based on whether they have taken a vow of poverty. Their Social Security coverage, income tax obligations, and exemption options differ from other clergy.

## Definition

A religious order is a organization where members:
- Live together as a community
- Follow established religious rules and discipline
- Are bound by vows to the order

Members may or may not take a vow of poverty, which significantly affects their tax treatment.

## Social Security Coverage by Status

### Without Vow of Poverty

| FICA | SECA |
|------|------|
| No (exempt) | Yes, unless exemption approved via [[Form 4361]] |

Treatment is similar to ministers:
- Earnings from [[Ministerial Services]] subject to SECA
- Can apply for [[SE Tax Exemption]] using [[Form 4361]]

### With Vow of Poverty

| FICA | SECA |
|------|------|
| Only if order elected coverage | No (exempt) |

**Default status:**
- Automatically exempt from SE tax on ministerial earnings
- No need to request separate exemption
- Earnings are considered income of the religious order

**FICA coverage applies if:**
- Order elected coverage via [[Form SS-16]], OR
- Member works outside the order (not required by or on behalf of the order)

## Vow of Poverty Effects

### For Income Tax Purposes
- Earnings for ministerial services as agent of order are tax-free
- You renounce claims to earnings
- Earnings belong to the order

### For Social Security Purposes
- Generally exempt from SE tax
- May be covered under FICA if order elected coverage

### Working Outside the Order

If you work outside the order AND:
- Work is not required by the order
- Work is not done on behalf of the order
- You are self-employed or employee of outside organization

Then:
- Income is taxable to you directly
- Subject to either FICA or SECA (not both)
- May claim charitable deduction for amounts given to order

## Order Election of FICA Coverage

A religious order may elect FICA coverage for vow-of-poverty members:

**Process:**
- File [[Form SS-16]]
- May cover members retroactively up to 20 calendar quarters

**Effect:**
- Order pays both employer and employee portions
- Member pays none of the FICA tax

## Services as Agent of the Order

### Qualifying Services
Services performed as agent of the order are ministerial services:
- Services required by the order
- Services for supervising church or associated institution
- Services directed by the order

### Non-Qualifying Services
Services are NOT considered duties of the order unless:
1. Services are part of duties for/on behalf of the order as its agent
2. Services are kind ordinarily performed by members of the order

### Example

**Chris (Secretary):**
- Instructed to work at church business office that supervises the order
- Services ARE duties required by the order
- Acting as agent of the order
- Earnings not included in gross income
- Not subject to income tax withholding or FICA/SECA

**Pat (Attorney):**
- Instructed to get job with law firm
- Services are NOT duties required by the order
- Earnings subject to FICA and income tax
- May claim charitable deduction for amounts given to order

## Requesting IRS Ruling

Organizations and individuals may request IRS rulings on:
- Whether organization qualifies as a religious order
- Whether individual qualifies as a member of a religious order

**Process:** Follow Revenue Procedure 2025-1

## Filing Requirements

| Status | Filing Requirement |
|--------|-------------------|
| Vow of poverty, working for order | Generally none (earnings belong to order) |
| Vow of poverty, working outside order | File if income exceeds thresholds |
| No vow of poverty | Same as ministers - file if SE income $400+ or standard thresholds |
| SE tax exemption approved | File if other SE income $400+ |

## Retirement Plan Eligibility

Members of religious orders can generally participate in:
- Tax-sheltered annuity (403(b)) plans
- Church retirement plans

See [[Retirement Plans for Clergy]] for details.
