# Qualified Farm Indebtedness

## Overview

Qualified farm indebtedness is debt incurred directly in connection with the operation of a farming business that can be excluded from income if canceled, provided certain requirements are met.

## Requirements for Exclusion

All of the following must apply:

1. **Gross Receipts Test**: 50% or more of total gross receipts for the 3 preceding tax years (2022, 2023, 2024 for 2025 returns) must be from the trade or business of farming

2. **Debt Connection**: The debt was incurred directly in connection with operating the farming business

3. **Qualified Person**: The cancellation was made by a qualified person:
   - Individual or organization actively and regularly engaged in lending money
   - Federal, state, or local government or agency (e.g., U.S. Department of Agriculture)
   - Cannot be related to you
   - Cannot be the person from whom you acquired the property
   - Cannot receive a fee due to your investment in the property

## Exclusion Limit

The amount you can exclude is limited to the sum of:

1. Your adjusted tax attributes (NOLs, capital losses, passive losses, credits)
2. The total adjusted basis of qualified property held at the beginning of the year following the cancellation

Any canceled farm debt exceeding this limit must be included in income.

## Adjusted Tax Attributes

For purposes of this exclusion, adjusted tax attributes means the sum of:

- Net operating loss (NOL) for the current year and any NOL carryover
- Net capital loss and any capital loss carryover
- Passive activity loss carryover
- Three times the sum of:
  - General business credit carryover
  - Minimum tax credit available
  - Foreign tax credit carryover
  - Passive activity credit carryover

## Ordering Rules

Other exclusions must be applied **before** the qualified farm indebtedness exclusion:

1. **Bankruptcy exclusion** must be applied if debt is canceled in a Title 11 case
2. **Insolvency exclusion** must be applied first if you were insolvent immediately before cancellation

## Reporting Requirements

1. File [[Form 982]] with your tax return
2. Check the box on line 1c
3. Enter the excluded amount on line 2 (not more than the exclusion limit)
4. Complete Part II to reduce tax attributes

## Example

**Scenario**: Farmer has $10,000 of farm debt canceled
- Adjusted tax attributes: $5,000
- Qualified property basis at start of 2026: $3,000
- Exclusion limit: $8,000 ($5,000 + $3,000)
- **Result**: Can exclude $8,000; must include $2,000 in income on Schedule F
