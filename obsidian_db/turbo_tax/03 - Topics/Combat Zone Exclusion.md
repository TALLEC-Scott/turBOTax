# Combat Zone Exclusion

## Overview

The combat zone exclusion allows eligible members of the U.S. Armed Forces to exclude certain military pay from gross income when serving in a designated combat zone. This exclusion provides significant tax benefits for servicemembers deployed to hazardous areas.

## Who Qualifies

The exclusion applies to:
- Members of the U.S. Armed Forces (including commissioned officers, warrant officers, and enlisted personnel)
- Reservists called to active duty
- Coast Guard and Space Force members
- Support personnel (Red Cross, accredited correspondents, civilian personnel under Armed Forces direction)

## Combat Zone Defined

Designated combat zones include:

| Combat Zone | Beginning Date |
|-------------|----------------|
| Afghanistan Area | September 19, 2001 |
| Kosovo Area | March 24, 1999 |
| Arabian Peninsula | January 17, 1991 |
| Sinai Peninsula | Sinai Peninsula operations |
| Former Yugoslavia | Various dates (terminated) |

**Qualifying service outside combat zone:** Service in direct support of military operations in a combat zone that qualifies the member for hostile fire/imminent danger pay.

## Exclusion Amount

### Enlisted Personnel
- **Full exclusion** of military pay for service in a combat zone
- No dollar limit on excluded amount

### Commissioned Officers
- Exclusion limited to the highest rate of enlisted pay plus hostile fire/imminent danger pay
- For 2025, officer exclusion is capped (see current pay tables)

### Warrant Officers
- Treated as enlisted personnel for exclusion purposes
- Full exclusion available

## What Pay Can Be Excluded

| Type of Pay | Excludable? |
|-------------|-------------|
| Basic pay | Yes (subject to officer limits) |
| Hostile fire/imminent danger pay | Yes |
| Hardship duty pay | Yes |
| Foreign duty pay | Yes |
| Reenlistment bonuses | Yes (if reenlistment occurs in combat zone) |
| Accrued leave | Yes |
| BAH/BAS | Already excluded from income |

## Hospitalization Rule

If hospitalized for injuries, illness, or disease incurred in a combat zone:
- Exclusion continues during hospitalization
- Hospitalization outside the U.S. has no time limit
- Hospitalization inside the U.S. limited to 5 years

## Nonqualifying Presence

The exclusion does **not** apply to:
- Presence in a combat zone while on leave from a duty station outside the zone
- Passage through a combat zone between two points outside the zone
- Presence in a combat zone solely for personal convenience

## Reporting the Exclusion

### Form W-2
- Nontaxable combat pay appears in Box 12 with code Q
- This amount is not included in taxable wages (Box 1)

### Form 1040
- Beginning 2024, nontaxable combat pay is reported on Form 1040, line 1i
- This is for informational purposes only

## Earned Income Credit Election

You may elect to include nontaxable combat pay in earned income for [[Earned Income Credit|EIC]] calculation purposes. Consider calculating EIC both ways to determine if the election benefits you.

**Thresholds for beneficial election (2025):**
- $8,490 if no qualifying children
- $12,730 if one qualifying child
- $17,880 if two or more qualifying children

## Deadline Extensions

Combat zone service triggers automatic filing and payment extensions:
- **180 days** after the last day in the combat zone (or hospitalization)
- **Plus** the number of days remaining in the original filing period

See [[Pub 3 - Armed Forces Tax Guide]] for detailed extension calculations.
