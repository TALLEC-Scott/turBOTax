# Innocent Spouse Relief

## Overview

Innocent spouse relief provides relief from joint and several liability for tax, interest, and penalties on a joint tax return. When you file a joint return, both spouses are generally responsible for the entire tax liability, even if one spouse earned all the income.

## Types of Relief Available

### 1. Innocent Spouse Relief

Relief from liability for tax, interest, and penalties arising from erroneous items of the other spouse on a joint return.

**Requirements:**
- You filed a joint return with an understatement of tax
- The understatement is due to erroneous items of your spouse
- You didn't know and had no reason to know of the understatement
- It would be unfair to hold you liable

### 2. Separation of Liability

Allocates tax, interest, and penalties between you and your spouse based on each spouse's share of the deficiency.

**Eligibility:**
- You filed a joint return
- You are divorced, legally separated, OR
- You haven't lived together for the 12 months before filing the relief request, OR
- Your spouse is deceased

### 3. Equitable Relief

Available when the other two types don't apply but it would be unfair to hold you liable.

**Covers:**
- Understated tax on a joint return
- Underpaid tax on a joint return
- Tax on items not properly reported on separate returns in community property states

## How to Request Relief

File [[Form 8857]] - Request for Innocent Spouse Relief

**Deadline:** Within 2 years after the date of the IRS's first attempt to collect the tax from you.

## Factors IRS Considers

For equitable relief, the IRS considers:
- Whether you knew or had reason to know of the understatement
- Whether you received significant benefit from the understatement
- Whether you've been abused by your spouse
- Your mental or physical health
- Whether you've made a good faith effort to comply with tax laws
- Whether you've been abandoned by your spouse

## Divorced Taxpayers

If you're divorced, you remain jointly and individually responsible for any tax, interest, and penalties due on a joint return for a tax year ending before your divorce.

**Important:** This responsibility applies even if your divorce decree states that your former spouse will be responsible for amounts due on previously filed joint returns.

## Difference from Injured Spouse

| Innocent Spouse Relief | [[Injured Spouse]] |
|------------------------|-------------------|
| Relief from joint tax liability | Recovery of your share of a refund |
| Use Form 8857 | Use [[Form 8379]] |
| Involves erroneous items on return | Involves spouse's past-due debts |
| No time limit to file (2 years for collection) | File with return or after offset |
