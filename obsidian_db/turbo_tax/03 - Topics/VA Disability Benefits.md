# VA Disability Benefits

## Overview

Disability benefits received from the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) are **not taxable** and should not be included in gross income. This exclusion applies to a wide range of veterans' benefits administered by the VA.

## Tax-Exempt VA Benefits

The following veterans' benefits are excluded from gross income:

| Benefit Type | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Disability Compensation** | Payments for service-connected disabilities |
| **Disability Pension** | Needs-based payments for wartime veterans with limited income |
| **Education & Training** | GI Bill benefits, vocational rehabilitation |
| **Subsistence Allowances** | Payments for living expenses during training |
| **Home Grants** | Grants for wheelchair-accessible housing (Specially Adapted Housing) |
| **Vehicle Grants** | Grants for veterans who lost sight or use of limbs |
| **Insurance Proceeds** | Veterans' Group Life Insurance proceeds and dividends |
| **Insurance Dividends** | Interest on dividends left on deposit with VA |
| **Dependent Care** | Benefits under VA dependent-care assistance programs |
| **Compensated Work Therapy** | Payments from VA work therapy programs |
| **Death Gratuity** | Survivor payments for service members who died after September 10, 2001 |

## Who Qualifies

### Disability Compensation
- Veterans with service-connected disabilities
- Paid at different rates based on disability rating (0% to 100%)
- Additional allowances for dependents
- No income limit

### Disability Pension
- Wartime veterans with limited income
- Must have honorable discharge
- Age 65+ OR permanently and totally disabled
- Income and net worth limits apply

## Reporting Requirements

**Do NOT report VA disability benefits on your tax return.** They are:
- Not included in gross income
- Not reported on Form 1040
- Not subject to federal income tax withholding

### Form 1099-R Consideration
If a payer incorrectly reports VA disability payments as taxable income on Form 1099-R:
1. Contact the payer to request a corrected form
2. Request they report payments as nontaxable in box 12 (code J) of Form W-2, or
3. Report only the correct nontaxable amount on your return

## Interaction with Other Tax Benefits

### Earned Income Credit
VA disability benefits are **not earned income** for EIC purposes. However, if you retired on disability from an employer's plan, those payments may count as earned income until you reach minimum retirement age.

### Credit for the Elderly or Disabled
VA disability benefits count toward the "nontaxable benefits" limit when determining eligibility for this credit. See [[Credit for the Elderly or Disabled]] for income limits.

### Social Security Benefits
VA disability benefits do not affect Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) or Supplemental Security Income (SSI) payments.

## Military Retirees

If you are a military retiree receiving disability benefits from the VA rather than from military retirement:
- VA disability payments are tax-free
- Military retirement pay is generally taxable
- You may be eligible for Combat-Related Special Compensation (CRSC) or Concurrent Retirement and Disability Pay (CRDP)

See [[Pub 3 - Armed Forces Tax Guide]] for military-specific rules.
