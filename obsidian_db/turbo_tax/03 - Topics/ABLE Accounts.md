# ABLE Accounts

## Overview

An ABLE (Achieving a Better Life Experience) account is a tax-favored savings account for eligible individuals with disabilities or who are blind. Established under the Stephen Beck, Jr., Achieving a Better Life Experience Act of 2014, these accounts help maintain health, independence, and quality of life without jeopardizing eligibility for means-tested federal benefits.

## Eligibility Requirements

An individual may establish an ABLE account if their blindness or disability occurred before age 26. Eligibility can be established through:

1. **Entitlement to benefits** under title II or XVI of the Social Security Act based on blindness or disability
2. **Disability certification** filed with the ABLE program, including:
   - Physician-signed diagnosis of relevant impairment
   - Certification of blindness within meaning of section 1614(a)(2) of Social Security Act, OR
   - Medically determinable physical/mental impairment resulting in marked and severe functional limitations expected to result in death or last at least 12 months

## Contribution Limits (2025)

| Limit Type | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| Annual contribution limit | $19,000 (gift tax exclusion amount) |
| Additional contribution (employed beneficiaries) | Lesser of: compensation OR poverty line amount |
| Poverty line - Continental US | $15,650 |
| Poverty line - Hawaii | $17,990 |
| Poverty line - Alaska | $19,550 |

**Note:** Additional contribution not available if employer contributes to qualified defined contribution plan, 403(b), or 457(b) on behalf of beneficiary.

## Tax Treatment

### Contributions
- **Not tax deductible** - Contributions are made with after-tax dollars
- Must be in cash or cash equivalents
- Anyone can contribute, including the designated beneficiary

### Earnings and Distributions
- Earnings grow **tax-free** within the account
- Distributions for **qualified disability expenses** are **tax-free**
- Non-qualified distributions: Earnings portion is taxable plus 10% penalty

### SSI and Federal Benefits
- ABLE accounts are generally **disregarded** for SSI and certain means-tested federal program eligibility
- First $100,000 in ABLE account does not affect SSI eligibility

## Qualified Disability Expenses

Expenses that relate to blindness or disability, including:

- Education
- Housing
- Transportation
- Employment training and support
- Assistive technology
- Personal support services
- Health, prevention, and wellness
- Financial management
- Administrative services
- Legal fees
- Oversight and monitoring
- Funeral and burial expenses

## Rollovers and Transfers

| Type | Rules |
|------|-------|
| **Rollover** | Tax-free if completed within 60 days to ABLE account for self or eligible family member (sibling). Only one rollover per 12 months. |
| **Program-to-program transfer** | Direct transfer between ABLE programs; not a distribution |
| **Section 529 to ABLE** | Rollovers permitted without penalty from 529 tuition account to ABLE account for same beneficiary or eligible family member |
| **Beneficiary change** | May change beneficiary to a sibling if they are an eligible individual |

## Tax Forms

- **Form 1099-QA** - Reports distributions from ABLE accounts
- **Form 5498-QA** - Reports contributions, FMV, account opening, certification
- **Form 5329** - Report excess contributions and 10% additional tax on non-qualified distributions
- **Form 8880** - Saver's credit for ABLE contributions

## Medicaid Payback

Upon death of designated beneficiary, the state may file a claim for total medical assistance paid under Medicaid after ABLE account establishment. This payback:
- Is not a taxable distribution
- Is paid only after all qualified disability expenses are satisfied
- Is reduced by premiums paid to Medicaid Buy-In program
