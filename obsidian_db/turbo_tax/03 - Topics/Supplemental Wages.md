# Supplemental Wages

## Overview

Supplemental wages are compensation paid in addition to regular wages. They include bonuses, commissions, overtime pay, severance payments, and other similar payments.

## Types of Supplemental Wages

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Bonuses | One-time or annual performance payments |
| Commissions | Sales-based compensation |
| Overtime | Pay for hours worked over 40/week |
| Severance | Payments upon termination |
| Back pay | Retroactive wage adjustments |
| Sick pay | Third-party sickness payments |
| Awards | Achievement awards (cash) |
| Vacation buyouts | Payment for unused vacation |

## Withholding Methods

### Option 1: Flat Rate Method (22%)
**Use when:**
- Supplemental wages are paid separately from regular wages
- Income tax was withheld from regular wages in same calendar year

**Rate:** 22% (or 37% for amounts over $1 million)

### Option 2: Aggregate Method
**Use when:**
- Supplemental wages are combined with regular wages
- Regular wages paid in same payment

**Procedure:**
1. Add supplemental wages to regular wages
2. Calculate withholding as if single payment
3. Subtract tax already withheld on regular wages
4. Withhold remaining amount from supplemental wages

### Option 3: Optional Flat Rate (No Income Tax Withheld)
If no income tax was withheld from regular wages:
- Must use aggregate method, OR
- Withhold at 37% flat rate if supplemental wages exceed $1 million

## $1 Million Threshold

If supplemental wages to an employee exceed **$1 million** during the calendar year:
- Withhold at **37%** on the excess
- The 22% rate may apply to amounts under $1 million

## Overtime Deduction (2025-2029)

### Qualified Overtime Compensation
Under P.L. 119-21, employees may deduct up to:
- **$12,500** (Single, Head of Household, MFS)
- **$25,000** (Married Filing Jointly)

### What Qualifies
- Compensation that exceeds regular rate of pay
- The "half" portion of time-and-a-half
- Required by FLSA Section 7

### Employer Responsibilities
- Accept updated Form W-4 to account for expected deduction
- Use [[Pub 15-T]] procedures for withholding
- Overtime is still subject to FICA taxes

### Reporting Requirements
- Report qualified overtime on Form W-2
- See Notice 2025-62 for transition relief

## Severance Pay

- **Subject to:** Income tax withholding, FICA, and FUTA
- **Withholding method:** Use regular supplemental wage rules
- **FICA exemption:** None (supreme Court decision in US v. Quality Stores)

## Sick Pay

### Third-Party Sick Pay
- Subject to income tax withholding (if paid by employer or agent)
- May be exempt from FICA after 6 months

### Employer-Paid Sick Pay
- Subject to all employment taxes
- Report on Form 941

## Differential Wage Payments

Payments to employees serving in U.S. Armed Forces:
- **Subject to:** Income tax withholding
- **Exempt from:** FICA and FUTA
- Employer may claim credit for payments made

## Moving Expense Reimbursements

**No longer excluded** from income (P.L. 119-21 made permanent).

**Exceptions:**
- Active duty military moving due to PCS
- Intelligence community employees relocating
