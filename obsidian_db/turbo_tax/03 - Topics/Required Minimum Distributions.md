# Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)

## Overview

Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) are mandatory withdrawals that must be taken from retirement accounts starting at a certain age. The purpose is to ensure that tax-deferred retirement savings are eventually distributed and taxed.

## RMD Start Age

| Birth Date | RMD Start Age |
|------------|---------------|
| Before 1951 | 72 |
| 1951-1959 | 73 |
| 1960 or later | 75 |

**Note**: The SECURE 2.0 Act increased the starting age from 72 to 73 (2023) and 75 (2033).

## Required Beginning Date

The required beginning date is April 1 of the year after the calendar year in which the participant:
- Reaches the applicable age (73 for most), OR
- Retires from employment with the employer maintaining the plan (if less than 5% owner)

**Exception**: 5% or greater owners must begin RMDs by April 1 after reaching the applicable age, regardless of retirement status.

## Distribution Schedule

| Year | Deadline |
|------|----------|
| First RMD Year | April 1 of following year |
| Second RMD Year | December 31 |
| Subsequent Years | December 31 each year |

**Note**: If the first RMD is taken in the following year (by April 1), two RMDs will be due that year.

## Calculating the RMD

### Formula
```
RMD = Account Balance (December 31 of prior year) / Applicable Life Expectancy
```

### Life Expectancy Tables
- **Uniform Lifetime Table**: Most commonly used (for unmarried owners, or married with spouse not more than 10 years younger)
- **Joint Life Expectancy Table**: For married participants with spouse more than 10 years younger and sole beneficiary
- **Single Life Expectancy Table**: For beneficiaries after account owner's death

## RMDs by Plan Type

| Plan Type | RMD Required? |
|-----------|---------------|
| Traditional IRA | Yes, starting at applicable age |
| Roth IRA | No lifetime RMDs (beneficiary RMDs after death) |
| 401(k), 403(b), 457(b) | Yes, starting at applicable age or retirement |
| Designated Roth Account (in qualified plan) | No lifetime RMDs (beneficiary RMDs after death) |
| SEP IRA | Yes, same as traditional IRA |
| SIMPLE IRA | Yes, same as traditional IRA |

## Roth Account Exception

Designated Roth accounts in qualified plans are NOT subject to lifetime RMDs:
- No RMDs required during owner's lifetime
- RMDs required after owner's death
- Includes designated Roth accounts in 401(k), 403(b), and 457(b) plans

## Penalties for Missing RMDs

- **Excise Tax**: 25% of the amount not distributed (reduced from 50% by SECURE 2.0)
- **Further Reduction**: 10% if corrected timely
- **Waiver**: May request waiver for reasonable error

## Requesting a Waiver

File Form 5329 and attach a statement explaining:
- Why the RMD was missed
- Steps taken to correct the shortfall
- Request for waiver of the additional tax

## Multiple Plans

- IRAs: Calculate RMD separately, but can take total from one or more IRAs
- Qualified Plans: Must calculate and take RMD separately from each plan
- 403(b) Plans: Can aggregate similar to IRAs

## Inherited Retirement Accounts

Different rules apply to beneficiaries:
- **Eligible Designated Beneficiaries** (spouse, minor child, disabled/chronically ill): Life expectancy method available
- **Non-Eligible Beneficiaries**: 10-year rule (entire account must be distributed within 10 years)
- **Spouse Beneficiaries**: Can roll over to own account or use life expectancy method

## Section 402(f) Notice

Before an eligible rollover distribution, the plan must provide written notice explaining:
- Direct transfer options
- Tax withholding requirements
- 60-day rollover rules
- Other applicable rules
