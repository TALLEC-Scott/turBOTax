# Federal Income Tax Withholding for Household Employers

## Overview

Unlike Social Security and Medicare taxes, household employers are NOT required to withhold federal income tax from household employees' wages. However, you may do so if your employee requests it and you agree.

## Key Rule

**Withholding is VOLUNTARY for household employers.**

You should withhold federal income tax only if:
1. Your household employee asks you to withhold it, AND
2. You agree to do so

## How to Withhold

### Step 1: Get Form W-4
- Ask employee to complete Form W-4, Employee's Withholding Certificate
- Newly hired employees must use the redesigned Form W-4
- Use the most recently submitted Form W-4

### Step 2: Use Pub 15-T
- Follow federal income tax withholding procedures in Pub 15-T
- Publication 15-T provides withholding tables and methods
- Consider any expected deductions (like qualified overtime)

### Step 3: Withhold from Each Payment
- Withhold based on W-4 information and Pub 15-T tables
- Add federal income tax withholding to Social Security/Medicare withholding

## 2026 Withholding Tables

The federal income tax withholding tables are included in:
- **Pub 15-T, Federal Income Tax Withholding Methods**
- Available at IRS.gov/Pub15T

## Qualified Overtime Compensation Deduction

For tax years 2025-2028, employees may deduct up to:
- $12,500 (single)
- $25,000 (married filing jointly)

### Employer Requirements:
- Use employee's updated Form W-4 if submitted
- Follow Pub 15-T withholding procedures
- Allow employee to account for expected deduction
- Employee receives more money per paycheck instead of waiting for refund

### Note:
- Overtime compensation is still subject to Social Security and Medicare taxes (both shares)
- This is a deduction on the employee's income tax return, not an exclusion from wages

## Reporting Withheld Income Tax

### On Form W-2
| Box | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| Box 1 | Wages (include all wages) |
| Box 2 | Federal income tax withheld |

### On Schedule H
- Report total federal income tax withheld
- Add to Social Security/Medicare taxes due

## Making Tax Payments

### Option 1: Pay with Income Tax Return
- Include with your Form 1040 when filing
- May need to make estimated tax payments to avoid penalties

### Option 2: Increase Your Own Withholding
- Submit new Form W-4 to your employer, OR
- Submit Form W-4P to your pension payer
- Increase withholding to cover household employment taxes

### Option 3: Estimated Tax Payments
- Use Form 1040-ES payment vouchers
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, 2026; January 15, 2027
- Pay electronically via IRS.gov/Pay

## When No Return Is Required

If you're not required to file a tax return:
- You can still file Schedule H by itself
- File by April 15, 2027
- Include any federal income tax withheld

## Example Scenario

**Situation**: Employee Jane Oak works for household employer Mary Brown
- Weekly wages: $70
- Total annual wages: $3,360
- Jane did NOT submit Form W-4 for income tax withholding

**Result**: Mary withholds only Social Security and Medicare taxes (not federal income tax)

If Jane submits Form W-4 requesting additional withholding:
- Mary would follow Pub 15-T to calculate withholding
- Include federal income tax with FICA withholding
- Report on Form W-2 and Schedule H
