# Tax Attribute Reduction

## Overview

When debt is excluded from income under bankruptcy or insolvency rules, the debtor must reduce certain "tax attributes" by the excluded amount. This prevents excessive tax benefit from the debt cancellation by postponing the tax rather than eliminating it entirely.

## Order of Reduction

Reduce tax attributes in the following order:

| Order | Attribute | Reduction Amount |
|-------|-----------|------------------|
| 1 | Net operating loss (NOL) for year of cancellation | $1 per $1 excluded |
| 2 | NOL carryovers to year of cancellation | $1 per $1 excluded |
| 3 | General business credit carryovers | 33⅓¢ per $1 excluded |
| 4 | Minimum tax credit | $1 per $1 excluded |
| 5 | Net capital loss for year of cancellation | $1 per $1 excluded |
| 6 | Capital loss carryovers to year of cancellation | $1 per $1 excluded |
| 7 | Basis of debtor's property | $1 per $1 excluded |
| 8 | Passive activity loss and credit carryovers | $1 per $1 excluded |
| 9 | Foreign tax credit carryovers | 33⅓¢ per $1 excluded |

**Exception:** The debtor may elect to reduce basis of depreciable property first (see below).

## Making the Reduction

### Timing

- Make reductions **after figuring tax** for the year of debt cancellation
- For NOLs and capital losses: reduce current year loss first, then carryovers in order of tax years (earliest first)
- For credits: reduce in order taken into account for the tax year

### Who Makes the Reduction

| Situation | Who Reduces |
|-----------|-------------|
| Individual Chapter 7/11 | Trustee reduces estate's attributes |
| Individual (no bankruptcy estate) | Debtor reduces own attributes |
| Partnership | Individual partners reduce their attributes |
| S Corporation | Corporate-level reduction |

## Basis Reduction Rules

### When to Reduce

Basis reductions are made at the **beginning of the tax year following** the debt cancellation. Applies to property held at that time.

### Limit on Reduction

The reduction in basis for bankruptcy or insolvency cannot exceed:
> Total basis of property + Money held immediately after cancellation − Total liabilities immediately after cancellation

This limit doesn't apply if election is made to reduce basis before other attributes.

### Exempt Property

Don't reduce basis in property the debtor treats as exempt under Bankruptcy Code Section 522.

### Recapture of Basis Reductions

If reduced-basis property is later sold at a gain:
- The portion corresponding to basis reduction is taxable as ordinary income
- Treat basis reduction as depreciation deduction for recapture purposes
- Treat basis-reduced property as Section 1245 property (if not already 1245/1250 property)

## Election to Reduce Basis First

The debtor may elect to reduce the basis of depreciable property **before** reducing other tax attributes.

### Requirements

- Election must be made on Form 982 for the year of debt cancellation
- Reduction cannot exceed total basis of depreciable property held at beginning of following year
- "Depreciable property" = property subject to depreciation where basis reduction reduces future depreciation
- May elect to treat real property inventory as depreciable property

### Revocation

Once made, election can only be revoked with IRS approval. May make election with amended return if reasonable cause exists.

## Example: Tax Attribute Reduction

Charlie Jones has $10,000 debt canceled while insolvent:
- $2,000 NOL for current year
- $3,000 NOL carryover from 2022
- $5,000 capital loss carryover
- $40,000 depreciable basis in rental condo

**Standard order:** Reduce NOL ($2,000), NOL carryover ($3,000), capital loss carryover ($5,000)

**With basis election:** Reduce depreciable basis by $10,000, preserving loss carryovers. Future depreciation reduced; gain on sale recaptured as ordinary income.

## Reporting

File [[Form 982]] with the tax return for the year of debt discharge. Attach statement describing the debt cancellation and identifying property for basis reduction.
