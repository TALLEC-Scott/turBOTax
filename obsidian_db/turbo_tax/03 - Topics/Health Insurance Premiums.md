# Health Insurance Premiums

## Overview

Health insurance premiums you pay for medical, dental, and vision coverage may be deductible as [[Medical and Dental Expenses]] on [[Schedule A (Form 1040)|Schedule A]]. Self-employed individuals may be able to deduct premiums as an adjustment to income, avoiding the 7.5% AGI floor.

## Deductible Premiums

### Types of Deductible Insurance

- **Medical insurance** - Hospital, surgical, and physician services
- **Dental insurance** - Dental care coverage
- **Vision insurance** - Eye care coverage
- **Medicare Part A** - If voluntarily enrolled (not covered by Social Security)
- **Medicare Part B** - Supplemental medical insurance
- **Medicare Part D** - Prescription drug coverage
- **Long-term care insurance** - Subject to age-based limits (see [[Long-Term Care]])
- **HMO premiums** - Health maintenance organization fees

### Premiums Must Be for Medical Care

Policies must cover:
- Hospitalization, surgical services, X-rays
- Dental care
- Prescription drugs and insulin
- Replacement of lost or damaged contact lenses
- Long-term care (with limitations)

## Self-Employed Health Insurance Deduction

If you are self-employed, you may deduct health insurance premiums as an **adjustment to income** on Form 1040, not as an itemized deduction. This avoids the 7.5% AGI limitation.

### Who Qualifies

- Self-employed individuals with net profit
- General partners (or limited partners receiving guaranteed payments)
- More-than-2% shareholders of S corporations

### Requirements

1. **Net profit requirement** - Must have earned income from the trade/business
2. **No employer-subsidized plan** - Cannot be eligible for employer-subsidized health plan (yours, spouse's, or dependent's)
3. **Plan established under business** - Insurance must be under your trade or business

### Coverage Included

- Yourself
- Spouse
- Dependents
- Children under age 27 (even if not dependents)

### Reporting

- File Form 7206 to calculate the deduction
- Deduct on Form 1040 as adjustment to income
- Any excess can be claimed on Schedule A subject to 7.5% AGI limit

## Premiums NOT Deductible

### Excluded Insurance Types

| Premium Type | Reason |
|--------------|--------|
| Life insurance | Not medical care |
| Loss of limb/sight policies | Not medical care |
| Loss of earnings policies | Not medical care |
| Guaranteed hospital payments | Not medical insurance |
| Car insurance (medical portion) | Not separately stated |

### Pre-tax Premiums

Cannot deduct premiums paid with pre-tax dollars:
- Employer-sponsored plans with pre-tax contributions
- Premium conversion plans (FEHB)
- Cafeteria plan contributions

### Medicare Taxes

Payroll taxes for Medicare Part A are not insurance premiums and are not deductible.

## Special Rules

### Employer-Sponsored Plans

- **Premiums paid by employer**: Not deductible by employee
- **Premiums paid pre-tax**: Not deductible
- **Premiums included in W-2**: May be deductible as medical expenses
- **Retired public safety officers**: Cannot deduct premiums paid with tax-free retirement plan distributions

### Coverage for Nondependents

Generally cannot deduct additional premium for including a nondependent on your policy. Exceptions:
- Child under divorced parent rules
- Person who would be dependent except for gross income ($5,200+) or joint return
- Person who would be dependent except you can be claimed as dependent by someone else

### Prepaid Insurance Premiums

Premiums paid before age 65 for coverage after 65 are deductible if:
- Payable in equal yearly installments (or more often)
- Payable for at least 10 years or until age 65 (minimum 5 years)

## Premium Tax Credit Interaction

If you receive premium tax credits through the Health Insurance Marketplace:
- Cannot deduct premiums paid by the credit
- Can deduct premiums you actually paid out-of-pocket
- Can deduct excess advance credit payments you had to repay

## Long-Term Care Insurance Limits

Annual deduction limits for 2025 based on age at end of year:

| Age | Maximum Premium |
|-----|-----------------|
| 40 or under | $480 |
| 41-50 | $900 |
| 51-60 | $1,800 |
| 61-70 | $4,810 |
| 71 or over | $6,020 |

See [[Long-Term Care]] for more details.
