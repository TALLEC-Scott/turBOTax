# Insolvency Exclusion

## Overview

The insolvency exclusion allows taxpayers to exclude canceled debt from gross income to the extent they were insolvent immediately before the cancellation. A taxpayer is insolvent when their total liabilities exceed the fair market value (FMV) of their total assets.

## Key Rules

### Definition of Insolvency

You are insolvent when the total of all liabilities exceeds the FMV of all assets immediately before the debt cancellation.

**Assets include:**
- Everything you own (including collateral for debt)
- Exempt assets beyond reach of creditors (e.g., pension plan interests, retirement accounts)
- Cash and bank accounts
- Real property (main home, additional homes, investment property)
- Vehicles, household goods, jewelry
- Stocks, bonds, investments
- Business interests

**Liabilities include:**
- The entire amount of recourse debt
- Nonrecourse debt up to the FMV of property securing it
- Nonrecourse debt in excess of FMV, to the extent forgiven
- Credit card debt, mortgages, car loans, student loans
- Medical bills, judgments, business debts

### Important Limitations

1. **Does not apply to bankruptcy**: The insolvency exclusion does not apply to debt canceled in a Title 11 bankruptcy case
2. **Applies before other exclusions**: Must apply insolvency exclusion before qualified farm indebtedness or qualified real property business indebtedness exclusions
3. **Election for qualified principal residence debt**: You can elect to apply insolvency exclusion instead of qualified principal residence indebtedness exclusion

## How to Calculate

Use the Insolvency Worksheet (found in [[Pub 4681 - Canceled Debts, Foreclosures, Repossessions, and Abandonments|Publication 4681]]) to determine the extent of insolvency:

1. List all liabilities immediately before cancellation
2. List FMV of all assets immediately before cancellation
3. Subtract total assets from total liabilities
4. The result is the amount of insolvency

**Exclusion Amount**: The smaller of:
- The amount of canceled debt, OR
- The amount by which you were insolvent

## Reporting Requirements

To claim the insolvency exclusion:

1. File [[Form 982]] with your tax return
2. Check the box on line 1b
3. Enter the excluded amount on line 2
4. Complete Part II to reduce tax attributes

## Example

**Scenario**: Credit card debt of $5,000 canceled in 2025

| Assets | Amount | Liabilities | Amount |
|--------|--------|-------------|--------|
| Cash | $2,000 | Credit card debt | $5,000 |
| Car | $5,000 | Car loan | $8,000 |
| **Total** | **$7,000** | Medical bills | $2,000 |
| | | **Total** | **$15,000** |

**Insolvency Amount**: $15,000 - $7,000 = $8,000

Since insolvency ($8,000) exceeds canceled debt ($5,000), the entire $5,000 can be excluded from income.
