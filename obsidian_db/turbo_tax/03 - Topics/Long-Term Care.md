# Long-Term Care

## Overview

Long-term care expenses and insurance premiums may be deductible as [[Medical and Dental Expenses]] on [[Schedule A (Form 1040)|Schedule A]]. Qualified long-term care services and insurance contracts have specific requirements under the tax code.

## Qualified Long-Term Care Services

### Definition

Qualified long-term care services are necessary:
- Diagnostic, preventive, therapeutic, curing, treating, or rehabilitative services
- Maintenance and personal care services

These services must be:
1. **Required by a chronically ill individual**, and
2. **Provided pursuant to a plan of care prescribed by a licensed health care practitioner**

### Chronically Ill Individual

An individual is chronically ill if, within the previous 12 months, a licensed health care practitioner certifies that the individual meets either test:

| Test | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Activities of Daily Living (ADL)** | Unable to perform at least 2 of 6 ADLs without substantial assistance for at least 90 days |
| **Cognitive Impairment** | Requires substantial supervision due to severe cognitive impairment |

### Activities of Daily Living (ADLs)

The six ADLs are:
1. Eating
2. Toileting
3. Transferring
4. Bathing
5. Dressing
6. Continence

### Maintenance and Personal Care Services

Care with the primary purpose of providing a chronically ill individual with needed assistance with their disabilities, including protection from threats to health and safety due to severe cognitive impairment.

## Qualified Long-Term Care Insurance Contracts

### Requirements

A qualified long-term care insurance contract must:
1. Be **guaranteed renewable**
2. **Not provide** for a cash surrender value or other money that can be paid, assigned, pledged, or borrowed
3. **Provide** that refunds and dividends must be used only to reduce future premiums or increase future benefits
4. Generally **not pay** expenses that would be reimbursed under Medicare

### Deductible Premium Limits (2025)

The amount of long-term care insurance premiums you can deduct is limited based on the insured's age at year-end:

| Age at End of 2025 | Maximum Deductible Premium |
|--------------------|----------------------------|
| 40 or under | $480 |
| 41-50 | $900 |
| 51-60 | $1,800 |
| 61-70 | $4,810 |
| 71 or over | $6,020 |

**Note:** These limits are per person. You can also deduct unreimbursed expenses for qualified long-term care services.

### Per Diem Limitation

For per diem or similar periodic payments under a qualified long-term care insurance contract, amounts received in excess of $420 per day ($153,300 for 2025) for qualified long-term care services must be included in gross income.

## Nursing Home Care

### Fully Deductible

If the principal reason for being in a nursing home is to receive medical care, the **entire cost** (including meals and lodging) is deductible as a medical expense.

### Partially Deductible

If the principal reason is personal, you can still deduct:
- The portion of costs for medical or nursing care
- Not the costs of meals and lodging

## Home Care Services

### Nursing Services

Wages paid for nursing services in the home are deductible as medical expenses. Services need not be performed by a nurse if they are of a kind generally performed by a nurse.

### Attendant Care

If an attendant provides both medical and personal/household services:
- Divide costs between medical and personal services
- Only the medical portion is deductible
- Can include attendant's meals (allocated) and extra housing costs

### Employment Taxes

You can include as a medical expense:
- Social Security tax
- FUTA
- Medicare tax
- State employment taxes

Paid for an attendant providing medical care. See Pub. 926 for household employer responsibilities.

## What Is NOT Deductible

- Life care fees or "founder's fees" not allocable to medical care
- Future medical care payments (exceptions for lifetime care contracts)
- Long-term care insurance premiums paid with tax-free retirement plan distributions (retired public safety officers)

## Special Situations

### Lifetime Care - Advance Payments

You can include part of a life-care fee or founder's fee paid to a retirement home if:
- Required as condition for lifetime care promise
- Amount is properly allocable to medical care
- Use statement from retirement home to determine allocation

### Dependents with Disabilities

Advance payments to a private institution for lifetime care of a physically or mentally impaired child are deductible if:
- Payments are condition for future acceptance
- Payments are not refundable
