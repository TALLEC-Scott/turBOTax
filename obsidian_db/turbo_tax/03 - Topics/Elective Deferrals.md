# Elective Deferrals

## Overview

Elective deferrals are contributions made by employees to a retirement plan through a salary reduction agreement. Instead of receiving the money as taxable wages, employees elect to have the employer contribute it directly to a retirement plan, deferring the tax on those amounts until distribution.

## Types of Elective Deferrals

### Pre-tax Deferrals
- Contributed before income tax
- Not included in gross income in year of contribution
- Tax-deferred growth until distribution

### Designated Roth Contributions
- Contributed after income tax
- Included in gross income in year of contribution
- Qualified distributions are tax-free

## Elective Deferral Limits (2025-2026)

| Year | Base Limit | Age 50+ | Ages 60-63 (Enhanced) |
|------|------------|---------|----------------------|
| 2025 | $23,500 | $31,000 | $34,750 |
| 2026 | $24,500 | $32,500 | $35,750 |

## Plans That Allow Elective Deferrals

- [[401(k) Plans]]
- [[403(b) Plans]] - Tax-sheltered annuities
- 457(b) governmental plans
- SARSEPs (pre-1997 SEP plans)
- SIMPLE plans (separate limits apply)

## 403(b) Plan Elective Deferrals

For [[403(b) Plans]], elective deferrals are made under a salary reduction agreement between you and your employer. The employer withholds money from your paycheck to be contributed directly into a 403(b) account.

### 403(b) Deferral Limits (2025-2026)

| Year | Base Limit | With 15-Year Rule |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| 2025 | $23,500 | Up to $26,500 |
| 2026 | $24,500 | Up to $27,500 |

### Reporting 403(b) Deferrals

- Pre-tax deferrals: Form W-2, Box 12, Code E
- Roth deferrals: Form W-2, Box 12, Code BB
- Retirement plan checkbox (Box 13) must be checked

### Roth Contribution Programs

Your 403(b) plan may allow designation of all or part of elective deferrals as Roth contributions:
- Must be maintained in a separate Roth account
- Not excludable from gross income
- Qualified distributions are tax-free

See [[Roth Contributions]] for more information.

## Aggregation Rules

The elective deferral limit applies to the total deferrals under ALL plans maintained by the employer. Deferrals to plans of unrelated employers are not aggregated.

**Example**: If an employee participates in both a 401(k) and a 403(b) plan sponsored by the same employer, the total deferrals to both plans cannot exceed $23,500 (2025).

## SIMPLE Plan Elective Deferrals

SIMPLE plans have separate limits that do not count toward the $23,500 aggregate limit:

| Year | SIMPLE Limit | With Catch-up (Age 50+) | Enhanced (Ages 60-63) |
|------|--------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| 2025 | $16,500 | $20,000 | $21,750 |
| 2026 | $17,000 | $21,000 | $22,250 |

## Excess Deferrals

### Definition
Deferrals exceeding the applicable limit are "excess deferrals."

### Treatment
- Included in employee's gross income for the year
- Taxed twice if not withdrawn timely (once when contributed, once when distributed)

### Correction Period
- Must be withdrawn by April 15 of the following year
- Plan must be notified by April 15
- Plan must distribute excess plus earnings

## Nondiscrimination Testing

### Actual Deferral Percentage (ADP) Test
- Compares deferral rates of HCEs to NHCEs
- HCE deferral percentage cannot exceed 125% of NHCE percentage
- Safe harbor plans exempt from ADP test

### Actual Contribution Percentage (ACP) Test
- Applies to matching contributions and after-tax employee contributions
- Similar structure to ADP test

## Salary Reduction Agreement

- Must be in writing
- Employee elects specific dollar amount or percentage
- Can be changed during the year (if plan permits)
- Automatic enrollment permitted

## Timing Requirements

### Non-Owner Employees
- Must be elected by end of tax year
- Must be deposited within 7 business days (safe harbor), no later than 15 days

### Owner/Employees (Self-Employed)
- Must be elected by end of tax year
- Can be made by tax filing deadline (including extensions)

## Tax Treatment

### Income Tax
- Pre-tax deferrals excluded from gross income
- Roth deferrals included in gross income

### Payroll Taxes
- Both pre-tax and Roth deferrals subject to:
  - Social Security tax
  - Medicare tax
  - FUTA tax

## Vesting

- Elective deferrals are always 100% immediately vested
- Employee has nonforfeitable right at all times

## Reporting

- Reported on Form W-2, box 12
- Code D for 401(k) deferrals
- Code S for SIMPLE deferrals
- Code F for SARSEP deferrals
- Retirement plan checkbox must be marked
