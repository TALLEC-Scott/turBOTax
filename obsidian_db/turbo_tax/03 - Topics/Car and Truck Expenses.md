# Car and Truck Expenses

## Overview

If you use your car, van, pickup, or panel truck for business purposes, you may deduct the costs of operating and maintaining the vehicle. Two methods are available per [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]] and [[Pub 463 - Travel, Gift, and Car Expenses]]:

1. **Standard Mileage Rate** - Fixed rate per business mile
2. **Actual Expenses** - Deduct actual costs proportional to business use

## Standard Mileage Rate Method

### 2025 Rate
**70 cents per mile** for business use

### Requirements
- Must choose this method in the **first year** the vehicle is available for business use
- If owned: Can switch to actual expenses in later years
- If leased: Must use standard mileage for entire lease term

### Cannot Use Standard Mileage Rate If:
1. Operate five or more vehicles simultaneously
2. Claimed depreciation other than straight-line (ACRS/MACRS)
3. Claimed [[Section 179 Deduction]] on the vehicle
4. Claimed special depreciation allowance
5. Claimed actual expenses for a leased vehicle
6. Are a rural mail carrier with qualified reimbursement

### What's Included
The standard rate covers:
- Depreciation
- Lease payments
- Maintenance and repairs
- Gas and oil
- Insurance
- Registration fees

### Additional Deductions
Even using standard mileage, you can still deduct:
- Business-related parking fees
- Tolls
- Vehicle loan interest (portion for business use)

## Actual Expense Method

Deduct actual costs proportional to business use percentage:

| Expense Category | Examples |
|-----------------|----------|
| Depreciation | MACRS or Section 179 deduction |
| Lease payments | Monthly lease costs |
| Gas and oil | Fuel expenses |
| Insurance | Vehicle insurance premiums |
| Repairs | Maintenance and repair costs |
| Registration | License and registration fees |
| Tires | Replacement tires |
| Garage rent | Parking/storage costs |
| Tolls | Highway tolls |
| Parking fees | Business parking |

### Calculating Business Percentage
```
Business Miles ÷ Total Miles = Business Use Percentage
```

**Example:** 16,000 business miles ÷ 20,000 total miles = 80% business use
Deduct 80% of actual vehicle expenses.

## Local Transportation vs. Travel

### Local Transportation (Deductible)
- Travel between business locations
- Visiting clients or customers
- Business meetings away from regular workplace
- Home to temporary workplace (when you have a regular workplace)

### Commuting (NOT Deductible)
- Travel between home and your **regular** place of business
- Personal commuting expenses are never deductible

### Travel Away From Home (Separate Category)
- Overnight business travel
- Deduct travel expenses (lodging, meals 50%, transportation)
- Report on Schedule C, Line 24

## Home Office Exception

If your home qualifies as your **principal place of business**:
- Travel from home to clients/customers = deductible business travel
- Your home becomes your "regular workplace"

See [[Business Use of Home]] for home office requirements.

## Depreciation Limits for Passenger Vehicles

### 2025 Passenger Automobile Limits
| Type | First Year | Subsequent Years |
|------|-----------|------------------|
| Standard MACRS | $12,200 | Varies by year |
| With bonus depreciation | $20,200 | Varies by year |

### SUV Limit
Section 179 deduction for SUVs and certain vehicles is limited to **$31,300** for 2025.

See [[Pub 946 - How to Depreciate Property]] for complete limits.

## Employee Reimbursement

If you reimburse employees for vehicle expenses:
- **Accountable plan:** No W-2 reporting, deductible business expense
- **Nonaccountable plan:** Reported on W-2 as wages

See [[Pub 15 - Employer's Tax Guide]] for details.

## Recordkeeping Requirements

Per [[Pub 463 - Travel, Gift, and Car Expenses]]:

### Required Records
- Total miles driven during the year
- Business miles driven (with purpose and destination)
- Commuting miles
- Personal miles
- Date of each business trip

### Documentation
- Keep a mileage log or use a tracking app
- Record odometer readings at start and end of year
- Keep receipts for actual expenses (if using actual method)
- Maintain records for at least 3 years

## Vehicle Loan Interest

### For Self-Employed
- Deduct business portion on Schedule C (Line 16)
- **New for 2025:** Personal portion may be deductible on Schedule 1-A as qualified passenger vehicle loan interest

**Example:** 60% business use × $600 interest = $360 deductible on Schedule C

## Reporting on Schedule C

- Line 9: Car and truck expenses
- Line 24: Travel and meals (overnight travel)
- Line 16: Vehicle loan interest (business portion)

## Common Questions

### Q: Which method gives a larger deduction?
**A:** Calculate both methods and compare. The actual expense method may be better for expensive vehicles with low mileage, while standard mileage is simpler for high-mileage situations.

### Q: Can I switch methods?
**A:** If you own the vehicle, you can switch from standard mileage to actual expenses in later years (but not vice versa). If leased, you must use the same method for the entire lease.

### Q: Is commuting ever deductible?
**A:** Generally no. The only exception is if your home is your principal place of business—then travel to other work locations may be deductible.
