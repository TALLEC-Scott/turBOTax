# Noncapital Assets

## Overview

Noncapital assets are property that is NOT treated as capital assets. Dispositions of noncapital assets result in ordinary income or loss, or Section 1231 treatment.

## Categories of Noncapital Assets

### 1. Inventory and Stock in Trade
- Property held mainly for sale to customers
- See [[Pub 538 - Accounting Periods and Methods]]

### 2. Accounts/Notes Receivable
- Acquired in ordinary course of trade or business
- From services rendered or sale of inventory

### 3. Depreciable Business Property
- Property used in trade or business
- Rental property
- Section 197 intangibles
- See [[Section 1245 Property]] and [[Section 1250 Property]]

### 4. Real Business Property
- Real property used in trade or business
- Rental property

### 5. Self-Created Intellectual Property
- Patents, inventions, models, designs
- Copyrights, literary/musical/artistic compositions
- Letters, memoranda, similar property
- Created by personal efforts OR prepared for you

### 6. U.S. Government Publications
- Obtained free or below normal price

### 7. Commodities Derivative Instruments
- Held by commodities derivatives dealer
- Unless no connection to dealer activities

### 8. Hedging Transactions
- Identified as hedging transaction by end of day
- Used to manage risk in trade or business

### 9. Supplies
- Regularly used or consumed in business

### 10. De Minimis Safe Harbor Property
- Costs deducted under de minimis safe harbor
- Gain treated as ordinary income

## Tax Treatment

| Property Type | Gain Treatment | Loss Treatment |
|---------------|----------------|----------------|
| Inventory | Ordinary income | Ordinary loss |
| Business property (held > 1 yr) | Section 1231 | Section 1231 |
| Business property (held ≤ 1 yr) | Ordinary income | Ordinary loss |
| Accounts receivable | Ordinary income | Ordinary loss |
| Self-created IP | Ordinary income | Ordinary loss |

## Self-Created Musical Works Exception

You may elect to treat self-created musical compositions or copyrights as capital assets. See [[Pub 550 - Investment Income and Expenses]], Chapter 4.
