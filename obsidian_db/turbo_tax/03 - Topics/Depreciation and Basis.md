# Depreciation and Basis

## Overview

Depreciation reduces the basis of property over its useful life. Understanding how depreciation affects basis is essential for calculating gain or loss on disposition and for other tax purposes.

## General Rule

**Decrease basis by the depreciation you deducted OR could have deducted** under the method of depreciation you chose.

### Key Points

- You must reduce basis even if you didn't claim depreciation
- Use the amount you were entitled to deduct, not just what you claimed
- Applies to regular depreciation and special depreciation allowance

## Depreciation Methods

### MACRS (Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System)
- Required for most property placed in service after 1986
- Recovery periods based on property class
- Accelerated methods available (200% DB, 150% DB)

### Alternative Depreciation System (ADS)
- Required for certain property
- Slower depreciation (straight-line)
- Longer recovery periods

## Special Depreciation Allowance

### Bonus Depreciation
For qualified property placed in service after September 10, 2001:
- Must decrease basis by the special allowance you deducted or could have deducted
- Can elect not to claim bonus depreciation

### Making the Election
If you make a timely election not to deduct the special depreciation allowance:
- Don't reduce basis for bonus depreciation
- File statement with return

## Excess Depreciation

If you deducted more depreciation than you should have:

**Reduce basis by:**
1. The depreciation you should have deducted, PLUS
2. The part of excess depreciation that actually reduced your tax liability

### Example
| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Correct depreciation | $5,000 |
| Depreciation claimed | $7,000 |
| Excess depreciation | $2,000 |
| Portion that reduced tax | $1,500 |
| **Basis reduction** | $5,000 + $1,500 = $6,500 |

## Depreciation Recapture

When you sell depreciated property, you may have to recapture depreciation as ordinary income:

### Section 1245 Property
- Recapture ALL depreciation as ordinary income
- Applies to personal property and certain real property

### Section 1250 Property
- Recapture "additional depreciation" (excess over straight-line)
- Residential rental: No recapture if straight-line used
- Nonresidential real: May have recapture

## Listed Property

For vehicles and other listed property:
- If business use drops to 50% or less, recapture excess depreciation
- Include excess depreciation in gross income
- Add recaptured amount to basis

### Business Vehicle Example
If car not used more than 50% for business during tax year:
1. Recalculate depreciation using straight-line ADS
2. Include excess MACRS depreciation in income
3. Add excess to basis
4. Use ADS for remaining recovery period

## Capitalized Depreciation

Under uniform capitalization rules, depreciation on equipment used to produce property:
- Must be capitalized into the cost of the property produced
- Becomes part of the basis of the produced property
- Is then depreciated as part of that property

## Section 179 Deduction

The Section 179 deduction immediately reduces basis:

- Must reduce basis by the Section 179 deduction taken
- Even if the deduction is limited or disallowed
- Affects basis for later depreciation calculations

## Recording Requirements

Maintain records of:
- Original unadjusted basis
- Accumulated depreciation
- Adjusted basis
- Depreciation method used
- Recovery period
- Convention used (half-year, mid-quarter, mid-month)
