# Physical Presence Test

## Overview

The Physical Presence Test is one of two tests that can qualify you for the [[Foreign Earned Income Exclusion]] and [[Foreign Housing Exclusion and Deduction]]. Unlike the [[Bona Fide Residence Test]], this test is purely mathematical—it only counts days present in a foreign country.

## The 330-Day Rule

You qualify if you are physically present in a foreign country (or countries) for **330 full days** during any **12 consecutive months**.

### Key Points

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Days Required** | 330 full days |
| **Period** | Any 12 consecutive months |
| **Days Need Not Be** | Consecutive |
| **Intentions** | Irrelevant |
| **Type of Residence** | Irrelevant |

## Who Can Use This Test

Both U.S. citizens AND resident aliens can use this test, regardless of treaty country citizenship.

**This is an advantage over the Bona Fide Residence Test**, which requires resident aliens to be citizens of treaty countries.

## What Counts as a Full Day

### A Full Day Is:
- A full 24-hour period from midnight to midnight
- Time spent in one or more foreign countries
- Days when you are physically in the foreign country at midnight

### NOT a Full Day:
- Day of arrival (if not present at midnight)
- Day of departure (if not present at midnight)
- Days spent traveling over international waters
- Days in U.S. territories (Puerto Rico, Guam, USVI, etc.)—these are not "foreign countries"

## The 12-Month Period

### Flexibility
- Can be ANY 12 consecutive months, not just calendar year
- Can start on any day
- Multiple overlapping periods can qualify

### Example
You are in Japan from March 1, 2025, through February 28, 2026. During this period you were present 340 full days. You qualify for the physical presence test for tax year 2025.

## Calculating Days in the U.S.

Brief trips to the United States do NOT disqualify you, but those days don't count toward the 330-day total.

**Common situations:**
- Vacation in the U.S. - those days don't count
- Business trip to U.S. - those days don't count
- Family emergency in U.S. - those days don't count

### Maximum U.S. Days
In a 12-month period, you can spend up to **35 days** in the U.S. and still meet the 330-day requirement.

## Special Rules and Exceptions

### Adverse Conditions
If you must leave a foreign country due to war, civil unrest, or similar adverse conditions, the time requirements may be waived. The IRS publishes a list of qualifying countries annually.

### Combat Zone/Qualifying Hazardous Duty Area
Special rules apply to military personnel. See [[Pub 3 - Armed Forces Tax Guide]].

### Travel Over International Waters
Days spent traveling over international waters (on ships, planes) don't count as days in a foreign country.

## When to Use This Test

**Best for:**
- Fixed-term assignments abroad
- Frequent travelers between countries
- Resident aliens without treaty country citizenship
- Those who cannot commit to a full tax year in one country
- Short-term overseas contracts

**Advantages over Bona Fide Residence:**
- No intent requirement
- Any 12-month period works
- Available to all U.S. persons
- Easier to document

## Documentation

Keep records of:
- Passport stamps
- Travel itineraries
- Hotel receipts
- Foreign residence documentation
- Employer letters confirming foreign assignment

## Common Mistakes

1. **Counting partial days** (arrival/departure days)
2. **Including U.S. travel days** in the 330-day count
3. **Counting days in U.S. territories** as foreign days
4. **Using calendar year only** instead of any 12-month period
5. **Not accounting for international travel days**

## Example Calculation

**Scenario:** You move to Singapore on January 15, 2025. You take a 2-week vacation in the U.S. in July. You return to the U.S. on January 10, 2026.

**Calculation:**
- January 15 - December 31, 2025: 350 days in Singapore
- Minus 14 vacation days in U.S.: -14 days
- Minus arrival/departure days as applicable: varies
- **Qualifying days:** ~336 days

**Result:** You qualify under the physical presence test for 2025.
