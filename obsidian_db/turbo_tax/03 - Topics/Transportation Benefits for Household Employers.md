# Transportation Benefits for Household Employers

## Overview

Household employers may provide transportation (commuting) benefits to employees. Certain reimbursements for qualified parking, transit passes, and commuter highway vehicle transportation may be excluded from cash wages for Social Security and Medicare tax purposes.

## 2026 Monthly Exclusion Limits

| Benefit Type | Monthly Exclusion |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Qualified Parking | $340 |
| Commuter Highway Vehicle/Transit Passes | $340 (combined) |

## Qualified Parking

### Definition
Qualified parking is parking:
- At or near YOUR home (the employer's residence)
- At or near a location from which the employee commutes to your home

### What is NOT Qualified Parking
- Parking at or near the employee's home

### Tax Treatment
- Reimbursements up to $340/month: Excluded from cash wages (no FICA)
- Reimbursements over $340/month: Included as wages subject to FICA

## Commuter Highway Vehicle Transportation

### Definition
A commuter highway vehicle is:
- A highway vehicle with a seating capacity of at least 6 adults (excluding driver)
- At least 80% of mileage is for transporting employees to/from work
- At least 50% of seating capacity occupied during these trips

### Tax Treatment
- Reimbursements up to $340/month combined with transit passes: Excluded from cash wages
- Over the limit: Included as wages

## Transit Passes

### Definition
Transit passes include:
- Any pass, token, farecard, voucher, or similar item
- For transportation on:
  - Public transit systems
  - Private transit systems funded by public entities
  - Qualified highway vehicle transit

### Tax Treatment
- Combined with commuter highway vehicle reimbursements
- Monthly exclusion: $340 total

## Bicycle Commuting Reimbursements

### Important Change (P.L. 119-21)

**Before 2026**: Section 11047 of P.L. 115-97 suspended the exclusion for tax years 2018-2025

**Starting 2026**: P.L. 119-21 **permanently eliminates** the exclusion of qualified bicycle commuting reimbursements from employee income

### Result
- Bicycle commuting reimbursements are now **taxable income**
- Must be included in employee's wages
- Subject to income tax withholding and FICA

## How Reimbursements Affect Wages

### For Social Security and Medicare Taxes:
- Reimbursements within limits: NOT counted as cash wages
- Reimbursements over limits: Counted as cash wages

### For Federal Income Tax:
- All reimbursements (within limits) are still excludable from income
- Amounts over limits must be included in Box 1 of Form W-2

## Reporting Requirements

### Form W-2 Reporting

| Box | Treatment |
|-----|-----------|
| Box 1 (Wages) | Include amounts over limits |
| Box 3 (SS Wages) | Include amounts over limits |
| Box 5 (Medicare Wages) | Include amounts over limits |

### Recordkeeping
Keep records of:
- Date and amount of reimbursements
- Type of transportation benefit
- Monthly calculations

## Employer Considerations

### Cash Reimbursement Method
- Employee submits receipts for parking or transit costs
- Employer reimburses up to monthly limit
- Amounts within limit excluded from FICA wages

### Direct Payment Method
- Employer pays parking facility or transit provider directly
- Same exclusion limits apply

## Example Calculation

**Scenario**: Household employer reimburses employee for:
- Monthly parking at transit station: $300
- Monthly transit pass: $100
- **Total**: $400

**Tax Treatment**:
- Qualified parking: $300 (within $340 limit) - Excluded
- Transit pass: $100 (within $340 limit) - Excluded
- **Total excluded**: $400 (both under separate limits)

If transit pass were $350:
- Transit pass over limit by $10
- $10 included in wages subject to FICA

## State Tax Considerations

Some states may have different treatment for transportation benefits:
- Check state tax laws for conformity
- Some states may tax benefits that are federally excluded
