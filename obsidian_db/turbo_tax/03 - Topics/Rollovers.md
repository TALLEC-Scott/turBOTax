# Rollovers

## Overview

A rollover is a tax-free transfer of retirement funds from one qualified plan to another. Rollovers allow you to move money between retirement accounts without paying taxes or penalties, provided you follow the rules.

## Types of Rollovers

### Direct Rollover
- Funds transferred directly between plans
- No tax withholding
- Full amount is transferred
- Recommended method to avoid tax complications

### 60-Day Rollover
- Distribution paid to you first
- Must deposit into new plan within 60 days
- 20% mandatory withholding applies
- You must make up withheld amount from other funds

### Trustee-to-Trustee Transfer
- Direct transfer between IRAs
- Not subject to 60-day rule or withholding
- No limit on number of transfers per year

## Eligible Rollover Distributions

Most distributions from qualified plans can be rolled over, including distributions from:
- [[401(k) Plans]]
- [[403(b) Plans]]
- [[Section 457 Plans]]
- [[IRAs]]
- Qualified pension and profit-sharing plans

## Distributions That Cannot Be Rolled Over

- Required minimum distributions (RMDs)
- Corrective distributions of excess contributions
- Loans treated as distributions
- Certain periodic payments for 10+ years
- Hardship distributions

## Rollover Rules

### 60-Day Rule
You must complete the rollover within 60 days of receiving the distribution. The IRS may waive this deadline in certain hardship situations.

### One-Rollover-Per-Year Rule (IRAs)
- Only one IRA-to-IRA rollover per 12-month period
- Does not apply to direct transfers between IRAs
- Does not apply to rollovers between different types of plans

### Withholding Rules
| Rollover Type | Withholding |
|---------------|-------------|
| Direct rollover | 0% |
| Distribution to you | 20% mandatory |

### Same-Property Rule
You must roll over the same property received. You cannot substitute other property (except cash for cash).

## Special Situations

### Rollovers to Roth Accounts
- Must include in income (taxable conversion)
- No early distribution penalty
- Future qualified distributions are tax-free

### In-Plan Roth Rollovers
- Transfer from traditional to Roth account within same plan
- Amount is taxable in year of rollover
- Separate 5-year period for each conversion

### Qualified Plan Loan Offsets
- Loan offset due to plan termination or severance
- Extended deadline to end of tax year following distribution
- Must show code 'M' in box 7 of Form 1099-R

## Reporting Rollovers

Report on Form 1040:
- Line 5a: Total distribution amount
- Line 5b: Taxable amount (0 if full rollover)
- Write "rollover" next to line 5b

## Key Rules

1. **Complete rollover within 60 days**
2. **Direct rollovers avoid withholding issues**
3. **Only one IRA-to-IRA rollover per 12 months**
4. **Rollovers to Roth accounts are taxable conversions**
5. **RMDs cannot be rolled over**
