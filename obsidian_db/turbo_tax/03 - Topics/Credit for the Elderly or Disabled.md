# Credit for the Elderly or Disabled

## Overview

The Credit for the Elderly or Disabled is a nonrefundable tax credit available to taxpayers who are either age 65 or older OR under 65 and retired on permanent and total disability. The credit can reduce your tax liability but cannot result in a refund.

## Eligibility Requirements

### You Must Be a Qualified Individual

**Option 1: Age 65 or Older**
- Age 65 by end of tax year (considered 65 on day before 65th birthday)
- For 2025: Born before January 2, 1961
- Must be U.S. citizen or resident alien

**Option 2: Under Age 65 with Disability**
All three must be true:
1. Retired on permanent and total disability
2. Received taxable disability income in 2025
3. Had not reached mandatory retirement age by January 1, 2025

### Income Limits

Even if you qualify, you CANNOT take the credit if your income exceeds:

| Filing Status | AGI Limit | Nontaxable Benefits Limit |
|---------------|-----------|---------------------------|
| Single, Head of Household, QSS | $17,500 | $5,000 |
| MFJ - One spouse qualifies | $20,000 | $5,000 |
| MFJ - Both spouses qualify | $25,000 | $7,500 |
| MFS (lived apart all year) | $12,500 | $3,750 |

**Nontaxable benefits include**: Social Security, pensions, annuities, disability benefits

## Disability Definitions

### Permanent and Total Disability
Unable to engage in any substantial gainful activity due to physical or mental condition that:
- Has lasted or will last continuously for 12+ months, OR
- Can be expected to result in death

### Substantial Gainful Activity
- Work for pay or profit
- Full-time work at minimum wage = presumed able to engage in substantial gainful activity

### Disability Income Requirements
- Paid under employer's accident/health plan or pension plan
- Included in income as wages for time absent due to disability

### NOT Disability Income
- Payments from plans without disability retirement provisions
- Lump-sum payments for accrued annual leave
- Payments after reaching mandatory retirement age

## Physician's Statement

Taxpayers under 65 must keep (not file) a physician's statement certifying permanent and total disability. Veterans can substitute VA Form 21-0172.

### When New Statement Not Needed
If you had a statement in an earlier year and continued disability prevents substantial gainful activity, you may not need a new statement. Check Schedule R instructions.

## Figuring the Credit

### Maximum Credit
- Generally 15% of base amount
- Base amounts vary by filing status and number of qualifying individuals

### Calculation
Complete **Schedule R (Form 1040)** to calculate credit.

### IRS Can Figure It
If you want the IRS to calculate the credit, follow instructions in Schedule R or Pub 17, Chapter 13.

## Filing Requirements

### Must File Form 1040 or 1040-SR
- Cannot claim credit on Form 1040-NR
- Must file Schedule R with Form 1040/1040-SR

### Married Taxpayers
Generally must file jointly to claim credit.

**Exception**: If didn't live together at any time during the year, can file either jointly or separately.
