# Sales and Exchanges

## Overview

A sale is a transfer of property for money or a promise to pay. An exchange is a transfer of property for other property or services. Understanding when a disposition occurs is essential for determining gain or loss.

## Key Concepts

### Sale vs. Lease
- Agreements that appear to be leases may be conditional sales contracts
- Intention of parties determines the distinction
- Consider facts and circumstances of each agreement

### Transactions Treated as Sales or Exchanges

| Transaction | Treatment |
|-------------|-----------|
| Cancellation of lease (tenant) | Amount realized from sale of property |
| Cancellation of lease (landlord) | Ordinary income (substitute for rent) |
| Copyright grant (exclusive, life) | Sale of property |
| Perpetual easement for consideration | Sale of property |
| Property transferred to satisfy debt | Exchange |
| Note maturity extension | May be exchange if materially different |

### Easements
- Grant of easement reduces basis of affected property
- Perpetual easement with no retained interest = sale
- Qualified conservation easement = charitable contribution

## Amount Realized

The amount realized from a sale or exchange includes:
- Cash received
- Fair market value of property or services received
- Liabilities assumed by buyer
- Liabilities to which property is subject
- Minus: Selling expenses

## Gain or Loss Calculation

**Gain** = Amount realized - Adjusted basis (when amount realized > basis)

**Loss** = Adjusted basis - Amount realized (when basis > amount realized)

### Adjusted Basis
- Original cost or other basis
- Plus: Improvements, additions
- Minus: Depreciation, casualty losses, other deductions

## Special Rules

### Bargain Sales
- Sale below FMV with donative intent
- Part sale, part gift
- Gain recognized if amount realized > adjusted basis
- No loss deduction if amount realized < basis
- Special allocation rules for sales to charity

### Canceling Sale of Real Property
- Same tax year: No gain or loss recognized
- Later tax year: Gain recognized in year of sale
- New basis = amount paid to buyer on return
