# At-Risk Rules

## Overview

The at-risk rules limit your deductible loss from an activity to the amount you have "at risk" in that activity. These rules prevent taxpayers from claiming losses that exceed their actual economic investment.

**Critical:** Apply at-risk rules **BEFORE** passive activity rules when figuring allowable losses.

## Activities Covered by At-Risk Rules

The at-risk rules apply to activities conducted:
- As a trade or business
- For the production of income
- Through partnerships or S corporations
- As lessors of certain property

### Specific Activities

1. Holding, producing, or distributing motion picture films or videotapes
2. Farming
3. Leasing any Section 1245 property
4. Exploring for or exploiting oil and gas resources
5. Exploring for or exploiting geothermal deposits
6. **Any other activity** not specifically exempted

## What Is Considered "At Risk"

You are generally considered at risk for:

### 1. Cash Contributions
Money contributed to the activity from your personal funds.

### 2. Adjusted Basis of Property Contributed
The adjusted basis of property you contributed to the activity.

### 3. Amounts Borrowed
Money borrowed to use in the activity IF:
- You are **personally liable** for repayment, OR
- You pledge property (other than property used in the activity) as security
  - Amount at risk = Net fair market value of your interest in the pledged property

## What Is NOT Considered "At Risk"

### Nonrecourse Financing
Amounts borrowed for which you are **not personally liable** (the lender's only recourse is to the activity or property used in the activity).

**Exception:** [[Qualified Nonrecourse Financing]] secured by real property used in holding real property.

### Certain Borrowed Amounts
Even if you are personally liable, amounts borrowed from:
- A person with an interest in the activity
- Someone related to a person (other than you) with an interest in the activity

**Exceptions:**
- Amounts borrowed by a corporation from a person whose only interest is as a shareholder
- Amounts borrowed from a person having an interest only as a creditor
- Certain amounts secured by real property in holding real property activities

### Other Loss Limiting Arrangements
Capital contributed to an activity isn't at risk if you're protected against economic loss by:
- Guarantees
- Stop-loss agreements
- Other arrangements for compensation or reimbursement

## Related Persons

For purposes of the borrowed amounts exclusion, related persons include:

| Relationship Type | Examples |
|-------------------|----------|
| Family | Brothers, sisters, half-siblings, spouse, ancestors, lineal descendants |
| Corporations | Two corporations in same controlled group (10% test) |
| Trusts | Fiduciaries of different trusts with same grantor |
| Organizations | Tax-exempt organization and controlling person |
| Corporation & Individual | Individual owning >10% of corporation stock |
| Trust & Corporation | Trust owning >10% of corporation stock |
| Grantor & Fiduciary | Of any trust |
| Corporation & Partnership | Same persons own >10% of each |
| S Corporations | Two S corps with >10% common ownership |
| S Corp & C Corp | Same persons own >10% of each |
| Partnership & Person | Person owning >10% of partnership |
| Partnerships | Two partnerships with >10% common ownership |
| Common Control | Persons engaged in business under common control |

## Effect of Losses on At-Risk Amount

Any loss allowable in a particular year:
- Reduces your at-risk investment (but not below zero)
- Affects the beginning balance for the next tax year
- Suspended losses may be deducted when at-risk amount increases

## Recapture Rule

If your at-risk amount goes **below zero** at year-end, you must recapture previously allowed losses:

1. Add to your income the **lesser of**:
   - The negative at-risk amount (as positive), OR
   - Total losses deducted in previous years (after 1978) minus any prior recaptures

2. Don't use recapture income to reduce current-year net loss
3. Treat recaptured amount as a deduction for the activity next year

### Pre-1979 Activities
Special rules apply for activities that existed before 1979.

## Special Exceptions

### Qualified Corporations
Closely held corporations may qualify for an exception if:
- Stock is held by individuals (directly or indirectly)
- At least one shareholder is an individual who is not a corporation
- Other requirements are met

### Real Estate Activities
Special rules apply for amounts borrowed after May 3, 2004, secured by real property in holding real property activities.

## Coordination with Passive Activity Rules

The order of loss limitations is:

1. **Basis limitations**
2. **At-risk rules** - [[Form 6198]]
3. **Passive activity rules** - [[Form 8582]]
4. **Excess business loss limitation** - [[Form 461]]
