# Chapter 7 Bankruptcy Tax

## Overview

Chapter 7 is liquidation bankruptcy. A trustee is appointed to administer the estate and liquidate nonexempt assets to pay creditors. For individuals, the bankruptcy estate is treated as a **separate taxable entity** from the debtor.

Only individuals may receive a discharge in Chapter 7—corporations and other entities cannot.

## Separate Taxable Entity

When an individual files Chapter 7:

| Entity | Files | Purpose |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Debtor | Form 1040/1040-SR | Individual income tax return |
| Estate (Trustee) | Form 1041 | Bankruptcy estate return |

The trustee is responsible for preparing and filing all estate tax returns.

## Income Allocation

### Estate Income

- Gross income of debtor to which estate is entitled under Bankruptcy Code
- Income generated from estate property after petition date

### Not Estate Income

- Amounts received by debtor before petition date
- Income debtor earns **after** petition date (post-petition wages are debtor's income)

## Short Tax Year Election

The debtor may elect under IRC Section 1398 to close the tax year on the day before filing bankruptcy.

### Effect of Election

| Period | Tax Year | Tax Liability |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| Jan 1 - Day before filing | First short year | Claim against estate; not dischargeable |
| Filing date - Dec 31 | Second short year | Debtor's responsibility |

### Requirements

- File Form 1040 for first short year by 15th day of 4th month after year ends
- Write "Section 1398 Election" at top of return
- Can't make election if estate has no nonexempt assets

## Estate Tax Computation

The estate calculates tax using:
- **Tax rates:** Married Filing Separately schedule (not estate/trust rates)
- **Standard deduction:** $15,750 for 2025 (MFS amount)
- No higher standard deduction for age 65+ or blind

## Discharge Rules

### Non-Dischargeable Taxes

- Eighth priority taxes
- Late-filed returns (filed within 2 years of petition)
- No return filed
- Fraudulent return
- Willful evasion

### Dischargeable

- Penalties (unless event occurred within 3 years of bankruptcy and relates to non-dischargeable tax)
- Most other debts

## Case Dismissal

If Chapter 7 case is dismissed:
- Estate is no longer a separate taxable entity
- Treat as if no estate was created
- File Form 1040-X to replace all returns filed during bankruptcy
- Report all estate income/deductions on debtor's amended returns
