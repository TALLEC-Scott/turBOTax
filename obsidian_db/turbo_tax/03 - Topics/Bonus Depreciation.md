# Bonus Depreciation

## Overview

Bonus depreciation (also called the special depreciation allowance) allows taxpayers to deduct a percentage of the cost of qualified property in the year it is placed in service, before computing regular MACRS depreciation.

## 2025 Bonus Depreciation Rules

### 100% Bonus Depreciation Restored

For qualified property **acquired and placed in service after January 19, 2025**, the 100% special depreciation allowance is restored.

### Transition Rules

Property placed in service between January 1, 2025, and January 19, 2025, OR acquired before January 20, 2025, and placed in service later:
- Remain subject to **phase-down rules under prior law**
- Prior law phased down bonus depreciation rates

## Qualified Property

Bonus depreciation generally applies to:
- Qualified property with a recovery period of 20 years or less
- Qualified improvement property
- Certain property used in connection with residential real property activities

## How It Works

1. **Calculate bonus depreciation first** - Before regular MACRS depreciation
2. **Reduce basis** by the bonus depreciation amount
3. **Calculate regular depreciation** on the reduced basis

## Electing Out of Bonus Depreciation

If you qualify but choose NOT to take bonus depreciation:
- Must attach a **statement to your return**
- Election applies to all property in the same class placed in service during the year

**Why elect out?**
- Preserve depreciation deductions for future years
- Manage taxable income levels
- Avoid alternative minimum tax implications

## Relationship to Section 179

Bonus depreciation differs from [[Section 179 Deduction]]:

| Feature | Bonus Depreciation | Section 179 |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| Dollar limit | None | $2,500,000 (2025) |
| Investment limit | None | $4,000,000 (2025) |
| Taxable income limit | No | Yes |
| Choice per asset | No (class-wide) | Yes |
| Order of application | First | After bonus |

## Order of Deductions

1. **Bonus depreciation** (if applicable and not elected out)
2. **Section 179 deduction** (if elected)
3. **Regular MACRS depreciation** on remaining basis
