# SIMPLE Plans

## Overview

A SIMPLE (Savings Incentive Match Plan for Employees) plan is a written arrangement that provides employers and employees with a simplified way to make contributions to provide retirement income. SIMPLE plans are available to employers with 100 or fewer employees and can be set up as either a SIMPLE IRA plan or a SIMPLE 401(k) plan.

## Types of SIMPLE Plans

### SIMPLE IRA Plan
- Contributions deposited to SIMPLE IRAs (traditional or Roth)
- Easier administration than qualified plans
- No annual filing requirements for most plans

### SIMPLE 401(k) Plan
- Part of a 401(k) qualified plan
- Not subject to nondiscrimination and top-heavy rules
- Must satisfy qualified plan distribution rules

## Eligibility Requirements

### Employer Requirements
- Had 100 or fewer employees who received $5,000 or more in compensation in the preceding year
- Cannot maintain another qualified plan (exception: collective bargaining employees)
- Grace period: Treated as meeting 100-employee limit for 2 years after ceasing to meet it

### Employee Eligibility
- Received at least $5,000 in compensation during any 2 preceding years
- Reasonably expected to receive at least $5,000 during current year
- Employers can use less restrictive requirements

## Contribution Limits (2025)

| Contribution Type | Limit |
|-------------------|-------|
| Salary Reduction | $16,500 |
| Catch-up (age 50+) | $3,500 additional |
| Higher limit (ages 60-63) | $5,250 catch-up |
| Higher limit (certain employers) | $18,100 salary reduction |

## Contribution Limits (2026)

| Contribution Type | Limit |
|-------------------|-------|
| Salary Reduction | $17,000 |
| Catch-up (age 50+) | $4,000 additional |
| Higher limit (ages 60-63) | $5,250 catch-up |

## Employer Contributions

### Matching Contributions
- Dollar-for-dollar match up to 3% of employee's compensation
- Can reduce to as low as 1% (limited to 2 out of 5 years)
- Higher matching applies for certain employers electing higher salary reduction limits

### Nonelective Contributions
- 2% of compensation for all eligible employees
- Must be made whether or not employee makes salary reduction contributions
- Compensation limited to $350,000 (2025) / $360,000 (2026)

## Setting Up a SIMPLE Plan

1. Complete Form 5304-SIMPLE or Form 5305-SIMPLE
2. Set up SIMPLE IRA for each eligible employee
3. Provide employee notification before each election period

**Deadline**: Between January 1 and October 1 (or as soon as administratively feasible for new employers)

## Time Limits for Contributions

- **Salary Reduction**: Within 30 days after the end of the month in which amounts would have been payable
- **Employer Contributions**: By due date (including extensions) of employer's tax return

## Tax Treatment

### Traditional SIMPLE IRA
- Salary reduction contributions excluded from income tax
- Subject to Social Security, Medicare, and FUTA taxes
- Employer contributions not subject to payroll taxes

### Roth SIMPLE IRA
- Salary reduction contributions includible in gross income
- Subject to income tax withholding and payroll taxes
- Qualified distributions are tax-free

## Distributions

- Subject to IRA rules
- Required minimum distributions begin at age 73
- **Early withdrawal penalty**: 25% within first 2 years of participation (instead of usual 10%)
- After 2 years, standard 10% early withdrawal penalty applies

## Employee Notification Requirements

Must provide before the beginning of each election period:
1. Employee's opportunity to make or change salary reduction elections
2. Employer's decision to make matching or nonelective contributions
3. Summary description from financial institution
4. Notice of transfer rights if using designated financial institution

## Forms and Documents

- **Form 5304-SIMPLE**: For plans allowing employee to select financial institution
- **Form 5305-SIMPLE**: For plans using designated financial institution
- Contributions reported on Form W-2 (box 12, code S)
