# SEP Plans (Simplified Employee Pension)

## Overview

A SEP (Simplified Employee Pension) plan provides a simplified method for employers to make contributions toward their own retirement and their employees' retirement without getting involved in a more complex qualified plan. Under a SEP, contributions are made to an individual retirement arrangement (SEP IRA) set up for each eligible employee.

## Types of SEP IRAs

- **Traditional SEP IRA**: Pre-tax contributions, tax-deferred growth
- **Roth SEP IRA**: After-tax contributions (available for tax years beginning after 2022 per SECURE 2.0 Act)

## Eligibility Requirements

An eligible employee must meet all of the following:
- Has reached age 21
- Has received at least $750 in compensation (2025) / $800 (2026)
- Has worked for the employer in at least 3 of the last 5 years

**Tip**: Employers can use less restrictive participation requirements, but not more restrictive ones.

## Excludable Employees

The following employees can be excluded from coverage:
- Employees covered by a union agreement with bargained retirement benefits
- Nonresident alien employees with no U.S. source wages

## Contribution Limits (2025)

| Limit Type | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| Maximum Contribution | Lesser of $70,000 or 25% of compensation |
| Compensation Limit | $350,000 |
| Deduction Limit | 25% of all participants' compensation |

## Contribution Limits (2026)

| Limit Type | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| Maximum Contribution | Lesser of $72,000 or 25% of compensation |
| Compensation Limit | $360,000 |
| Deduction Limit | 25% of all participants' compensation |

## Setting Up a SEP

1. Execute a formal written agreement (Form 5305-SEP or prototype plan)
2. Give each eligible employee information about the SEP
3. Set up a SEP IRA for each eligible employee

**Deadline**: Can be set up as late as the due date (including extensions) of the employer's income tax return for that year.

## Deducting Contributions

- Contributions must be made by the due date (including extensions) of the employer's tax return
- Self-employed individuals must use special computation for maximum deduction
- Use the Rate Table for Self-Employed or Rate Worksheet for Self-Employed in Pub 560 Chapter 5

## Salary Reduction SEPs (SARSEPs)

- Only available for plans established before 1997
- Employees can choose to have employer contribute part of their pay to SEP IRA
- Must meet ADP (Actual Deferral Percentage) test requirements
- Elective deferral limit: $23,500 (2025) / $24,500 (2026)

## Tax Treatment

- Contributions to traditional SEP IRAs are excluded from employee's wages for income tax
- Contributions are subject to Social Security, Medicare, and FUTA taxes
- Earnings grow tax-deferred until distribution

## Distributions

- Subject to IRA rules
- Required minimum distributions (RMDs) begin at age 73
- Early withdrawal penalty: 10% additional tax before age 59½

## Forms and Documents

- **Form 5305-SEP**: IRS model SEP agreement
- **Form 5305A-SEP**: SARSEP agreement (pre-1997 plans)
- Contributions reported on Form W-2 for SARSEPs
