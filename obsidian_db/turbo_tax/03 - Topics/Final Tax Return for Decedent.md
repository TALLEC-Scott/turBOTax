# Final Tax Return for Decedent

## Overview

The final income tax return (Form 1040 or 1040-SR) must be filed for the year of a decedent's death by the personal representative. This return covers income from January 1 through the date of death.

## Filing Requirements

### When to File
- Due by April 15 of the year following death
- Covers period from January 1 to date of death
- Same due date as if the person were still alive

### Who Must File
- Personal representative (executor or administrator)
- Surviving spouse if filing joint return
- Person in charge of decedent's property if no personal representative

## Return Preparation

### Required Notations
- Write "DECEASED" across the top
- Include decedent's name and date of death
- Enter name and address as follows:
  - **Joint return**: Both names and addresses
  - **Non-joint return**: Decedent's name, personal representative's address

### Signature Requirements
- **Personal representative appointed**: Must sign the return
- **Joint return**: Surviving spouse must also sign
- **No personal representative, joint return**: Surviving spouse signs "Filing as surviving spouse"
- **No personal representative, no surviving spouse**: Person in charge signs as "personal representative"

## Income to Include

### Cash Method Taxpayers
- Only income actually or constructively received before death
- Constructive receipt examples:
  - Bond coupons that matured but weren't cashed
  - Dividends available but not yet received

### Accrual Method Taxpayers
- Only income items normally accrued before death
- Income reported when earned, not when received

### Special Income Types

| Income Type | Treatment |
|-------------|-----------|
| Wages | Through date of death |
| Interest/Dividends | Through date of death (see constructive receipt) |
| Partnership Income | Through date of death (closes partnership year for partner) |
| S Corporation Income | Through date of death |
| Self-Employment Income | Through date of death (based on accounting method) |
| Community Income | Half included if in community property state |

## Deductions

### Standard Deduction
- Full standard deduction allowed regardless of date of death

### Itemized Deductions
- Deduct expenses paid (or accrued) before death
- Medical expenses paid before death are deductible
- **Election for medical expenses paid by estate**: Can deduct on final return if paid within 1 year after death

### Losses
- NOL deduction from prior year deductible on final return
- Capital losses and carryovers deductible on final return
- Cannot carry over unused losses to estate

## Credits and Payments

### Available Credits
- Child tax credit (for qualifying dependents)
- Earned income credit (if otherwise eligible)
- Education credits (for qualified expenses)

### Tax Payments
- Withholding from wages, pensions, annuities
- Estimated tax payments made by decedent
- Any overpayment results in refund

## Refund Claims

### Form 1310 Requirements
Not required if:
- Surviving spouse filing joint return, OR
- Court-appointed representative with certificate attached

Required for all other refund claims.

## Joint Return Rules

### Eligibility
- Personal representative and surviving spouse can file jointly
- Surviving spouse alone can file if no personal representative appointed by due date
- Includes decedent's income through death and spouse's income for full year

### Restrictions
- Cannot file joint return if surviving spouse remarried before end of year of death
- Court-appointed personal representative can revoke joint return election within 1 year

## Nonresident Aliens

If decedent was a nonresident alien:
- File Form 1040-NR for final tax year
- See Form 1040-NR instructions for requirements
