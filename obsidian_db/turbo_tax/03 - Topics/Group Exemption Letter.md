# Group Exemption Letter

## Overview

A group exemption letter is a determination letter issued to a central organization recognizing on a group basis the exemption under Section 501(c) of subordinate organizations on whose behalf the central organization has applied for recognition of exemption.

## Key Definitions

### Central Organization
An organization that has one or more subordinates under its general supervision or control.

### Subordinate Organization
A chapter, local, post, or unit of a central organization:
- May or may not be incorporated
- Must have an organizing document
- Must have its own EIN
- Cannot be organized in a foreign country
- Private foundations cannot be included

## Benefits of Group Exemption

- Subordinates need not file individual applications
- Centralized compliance management
- Consistent treatment across subordinates
- Reduced administrative burden

## Application Process (Currently Suspended)

As of Notice 2020-36, the IRS is not accepting new group exemption requests until further guidance is published.

### Historical Requirements
1. Central organization must have recognition of its own exemption
2. Must submit letter request (not a specific form)
3. Must provide detailed subordinate information
4. Must pay appropriate user fee

### Information Required

1. Verification that subordinates:
   - Are affiliated with central organization
   - Are subject to general supervision or control
   - Qualify under the same IRC paragraph
   - Are not private foundations (if 501(c)(3))

2. Detailed description of purposes and activities

3. Sample governing instrument or representative copies

4. Written authorization from each subordinate

5. Names, addresses, and EINs of all subordinates

## Annual Requirements

### Information Required Annually
Must submit at least 90 days before end of accounting period:
1. Changes in purposes, character, or method of operation
2. Separate lists for:
   - Subordinates with name/address changes
   - Subordinates no longer to be included
   - New subordinates to be added
3. Information about new subordinates

### Where to File
Internal Revenue Service Center
Ogden, UT 84201-0027

## Maintaining Group Exemption

### Conditions for Continued Effectiveness
1. Continued existence of central organization
2. Continued qualification of central organization
3. Annual submission of subordinate information
4. Annual filing of required Form 990 by central organization

### Individual Subordinate Requirements
Each subordinate must:
- Conform to requirements for inclusion
- Maintain authorization for inclusion
- File required information returns

## Loss of Group Exemption

### When Exemption No Longer Has Effect
1. Central organization notifies IRS it is going out of existence
2. Central organization notifies IRS subordinates no longer meet requirements
3. IRS notifies central organization or subordinate that requirements not met
4. Central organization automatically revoked for failure to file returns

### Effect on Subordinates
- IRS no longer recognizes exempt status
- Subordinates must file separate applications
- May be retroactive revocation in some cases

## Automatic Revocation Impact

If central organization is automatically revoked for failure to file returns for 3 consecutive years, all subordinates under the group exemption lose their recognized exempt status.

## New Subordinate Organizations

A new 501(c)(3) organization must submit authorization to the central organization before the end of the 15th month after formation to satisfy Section 508(a) requirements for retroactive recognition.
