# Form 4797 - Sales of Business Property

## Overview

**Form 4797** reports sales, exchanges, and involuntary conversions of business property. It is used alongside or instead of [[Form 6252]] when business property is sold on [[Installment Sales|installment]].

## When to Use Form 4797

Use Form 4797 for:
- Sales of business or rental property
- [[Depreciation Recapture]] calculations
- Section 1231 gains and losses
- Installment sales of business property (in addition to Form 6252)

## Parts of Form 4797

### Part I - Sales or Exchanges of Property Used in Trade or Business

Section 1231 property held more than 1 year:
- Ordinary income treatment if gains exceed losses
- Capital loss treatment if losses exceed gains

### Part II - Ordinary Gains and Losses

Includes:
- [[Depreciation Recapture]] (from Part III)
- Noncapital assets held 1 year or less
- Ordinary income from Section 1231 netting

### Part III - Gain from Disposition of Property Under Sections 1245, 1250, and 1313

Calculate depreciation recapture:
- Section 1245 property (personal property)
- Section 1250 property (real property)
- Section 179 deduction recapture

## Relation to Installment Sales

For installment sales of business property:

1. **Depreciation recapture** - Report in Part III, recognized in year of sale
2. **Installment income** - Report on [[Form 6252]] for deferred gain
3. **Both forms required** - Form 4797 for recapture, Form 6252 for installment income

### Example Flow

1. Complete Form 4797 Part III for depreciation recapture
2. Report recapture on Form 4797 Part II
3. Include recapture in Form 6252 Line 4
4. File Form 6252 annually for installment income
5. Capital gain portion flows to [[Schedule D (Form 1040)]]

## Section 1231 Treatment

Business property held more than 1 year receives favorable treatment:
- **Net gain** - Taxed as long-term capital gain
- **Net loss** - Deducted as ordinary loss

This "best of both worlds" treatment makes Section 1231 property desirable.

## Common Questions

### Q: Do I need Form 4797 if I'm using the installment method?
**A:** Yes, for business property. Form 4797 reports depreciation recapture, while Form 6252 reports installment income.

### Q: What happens to depreciation recapture in an installment sale?
**A:** It's recognized immediately in the year of sale—it cannot be deferred under the installment method.

### Q: Can Section 1231 losses offset ordinary income?
**A:** Yes. If Section 1231 losses exceed gains, the net loss is ordinary and can offset ordinary income without limits.
