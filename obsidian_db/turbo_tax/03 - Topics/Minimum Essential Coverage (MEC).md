# Minimum Essential Coverage (MEC)

## Overview

Minimum Essential Coverage (MEC) is a term used under the Affordable Care Act (ACA) to describe types of health coverage that meet the requirement for having comprehensive health insurance. Your eligibility for MEC affects your ability to claim the Premium Tax Credit.

## Key Rule

You generally **cannot** take the Premium Tax Credit (PTC) for any month that you or a family member is eligible for MEC, except for individual market coverage (including qualified health plans).

## Types of MEC

### 1. Individual Market Coverage
- Qualified health plans purchased through the Marketplace
- Individual health insurance policies
- Certain expatriate health plans for students and missionaries

**Note**: Eligibility for individual market coverage does NOT prevent PTC eligibility for Marketplace plans.

### 2. Government-Sponsored Programs

| Program | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Medicare Part A | Hospital insurance |
| Medicare Advantage | Part C plans |
| Medicaid | With exceptions (see below) |
| CHIP | Children's Health Insurance Program |
| TRICARE | Military health coverage |
| VA Health Care | Veterans benefits |
| CHAMPVA | Civilian Health and Medical Program of VA |
| Peace Corps | Volunteer coverage |
| Refugee Medical Assistance | Refugee health coverage |
| Basic Health Program | State BHP coverage |

**Medicaid Exceptions** (NOT considered MEC):
- Optional family planning services
- Tuberculosis-related services only
- Pregnancy-related services in states without full Medicaid
- Emergency medical services only
- Medically needy coverage (unless designated as MEC by HHS)
- Section 1115 waiver programs (unless designated as MEC by HHS)

### 3. Employer-Sponsored Plans

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Group health insurance | Insured plans in small/large group markets |
| Self-insured plans | Employer-funded health plans |
| Governmental plans | Federal Employees Health Benefits Program |
| Grandfathered plans | Plans existing before March 2010 |
| Expatriate health plans | For employees working internationally |
| COBRA coverage | Continuation coverage after employment ends |
| Retiree coverage | Employer coverage after retirement |

### 4. Other Coverage Designated by HHS
- Certain CHIP buy-in programs
- Coverage under certain Medicaid demonstration programs

## What is NOT MEC

The following are **not** MEC:

- Stand-alone dental plans
- Stand-alone vision plans
- Workers' compensation
- Coverage limited to a specified disease
- Accident-only coverage
- Disability income insurance
- Liability insurance

## MEC Eligibility Rules

### When Are You Considered Eligible?

You are considered eligible for MEC if you:

1. Meet the criteria for coverage under the program
2. Can enroll and receive benefits (with some exceptions)

### Waiting Periods

You are NOT considered eligible for employer coverage during:
- Waiting periods before coverage becomes effective
- Any period when you cannot receive benefits

### Government Programs - Special Rules

For certain programs, you must be **enrolled** to be considered eligible:
- Veteran's health care programs
- Certain TRICARE programs (CHCBP, Retired Reserve, Young Adult, Reserve Select)
- Medicaid for pregnancy-related services (if enrolled in qualified health plan)
- Medicare Part A requiring premium payment

### Retroactive Coverage

If you become eligible for government coverage retroactively (like Medicaid or CHIP):
- You will NOT lose PTC for months before approval
- PTC stops the first day of the first calendar month AFTER approval

## Impact on PTC

### Employer Coverage Affordability

Even if offered employer coverage, you may still be eligible for PTC if the employer coverage is:

1. **Not Affordable**: Employee's share of premium exceeds a threshold percentage of household income
2. **Doesn't Provide Minimum Value**: Plan pays less than 60% of total allowed costs

For 2025, family member affordability is based on the premium to cover the employee AND family members, not just employee-only.

### Reporting

MEC may be reported on:
- Form 1095-A (Marketplace coverage)
- Form 1095-B (Other coverage)
- Form 1095-C (Employer coverage)

**Note**: Reporting entities may not automatically send Forms 1095-B/1095-C unless requested (2025 change).
