# Sale of Business

## Overview

The sale of a business is treated as a sale of each individual asset, not a single asset. Each asset's gain or loss is determined separately using its classification.

## Asset Classification

| Asset Type | Gain/Loss Treatment |
|------------|---------------------|
| Capital assets | Capital gain/loss |
| Business real/depreciable property (held > 1 yr) | Section 1231 |
| Inventory | Ordinary income/loss |
| Accounts receivable | Ordinary income |

## Residual Method

Both buyer and seller must use the residual method to allocate consideration among assets transferred.

### When Required
- Transfer of group of assets constituting a trade or business
- Buyer's basis determined by amount paid

### What Constitutes a Trade or Business
- Goodwill or going concern value could attach, OR
- Use of assets would constitute an active trade or business

## Classes of Assets

Allocate consideration in this order (cannot exceed FMV except Class VII):

### Class I
- Cash and general deposit accounts

### Class II
- Certificates of deposit
- U.S. Government securities
- Foreign currency
- Actively traded personal property (stocks, securities)

### Class III
- Accounts receivable
- Other debt instruments
- Other assets that are marked to market

### Class IV
- Inventory
- Other property held for sale to customers

### Class V
- All assets not in other classes
- Office furniture, fixtures, equipment
- Buildings (except where in Class VI)

### Class VI
- Section 197 intangibles (except goodwill, going concern value)
- Customer lists, patents, copyrights, franchises

### Class VII
- Goodwill
- Going concern value
- **Can exceed FMV**

## Form 8594

Both buyer and seller must file Form 8594 to report allocation of purchase price.

## Partnership Interests

- Treated as capital asset
- Part of gain/loss from unrealized receivables or inventory = ordinary
- See [[Pub 541 - Partnerships]]

## Corporate Stock

- Sale of stock = capital gain/loss
- Different from asset sale

## Corporate Liquidations

- Generally treated as sale or exchange
- Gain/loss recognized by corporation
- Gain/loss recognized by distributee at FMV
