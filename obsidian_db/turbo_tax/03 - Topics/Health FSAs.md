# Health Flexible Spending Arrangements (Health FSAs)

## Overview

A Health Flexible Spending Arrangement (FSA) is an employer-established benefit plan that allows employees to be reimbursed for qualified medical expenses. FSAs are typically funded through voluntary salary reduction agreements with an employer. See [[Pub 969 - Health Savings Accounts and Other Tax-Favored Health Plans]].

## Benefits of an FSA

- Contributions excluded from gross income
- No employment or federal income taxes on contributions
- Tax-free reimbursements for qualified medical expenses
- Can use funds before fully contributed (entire annual election available at start of year)
- Employer may also contribute

## Eligibility

- Must be offered through employer
- Self-employed individuals are NOT eligible
- May have limitations for highly compensated participants or key employees
- Often offered as part of a cafeteria plan

## Contribution Limits (2025)

| Limit Type | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| Maximum Salary Reduction | $3,300 |
| Maximum Carryover (if plan permits) | $660 |

**Source:** Revenue Procedure 2024-40

## Making Contributions

1. **Election:** At the beginning of the plan year, designate how much to contribute
2. **Salary Reduction:** Employer deducts amounts periodically (generally each payday)
3. **Employer Contributions:** Employer may also contribute if specified in plan

**Changing Elections:** Can only change or revoke election if allowed by law and the plan.

## Distributions

### Qualified Medical Expenses
Tax-free reimbursements for [[Qualified Medical Expenses]] incurred during the coverage period.

### Covered Individuals
- You and your spouse
- All dependents on your tax return
- Persons who could have been dependents except for exceptions
- Your child under age 27 at end of tax year

### Substantiation Requirements
- Written statement from independent third party confirming expense incurred and amount
- Written statement that expense not paid or reimbursed under other health plan coverage
- No advance reimbursements of future/projected expenses

### Debit/Credit Cards
May use employer-provided cards if substantiation methods are met. See:
- Revenue Ruling 2003-43
- Notice 2006-69
- Notice 2007-2

### Expenses NOT Covered
- Health insurance premiums
- Amounts covered under another health plan
- Long-term care expenses

## Use-It-or-Lose-It Rule

Amounts remaining at end of plan year are generally forfeited. However, the plan may provide:

### Grace Period Option
- Up to 2½ months after plan year ends
- Medical expenses incurred during grace period can be paid from prior year balance
- Cannot receive cash refund of balance

### Carryover Option
- Up to $660 (for 2025) can carry over to following plan year
- Plan may specify lower carryover amount
- Amounts over carryover are forfeited
- Cannot have both grace period AND carryover

## Interaction with HSA

General Rule: An employee covered by both an HDHP and a general purpose health FSA cannot contribute to an HSA.

**Exceptions that allow HSA contributions:**

### Limited-Purpose FSA or HRA
- Can pay for items permitted as other health coverage (except long-term care)
- Can pay preventive care expenses

### Suspended HRA
- Elect to suspend HRA before coverage period begins
- HRA doesn't pay expenses during suspension except preventive care and permitted items

### Post-Deductible FSA or HRA
- Doesn't pay expenses before minimum annual deductible is met
- Doesn't have to be same deductible as HDHP

### FSA Grace Period with HSA
Coverage during grace period by general purpose health FSA is allowed if:
- Balance in health FSA at end of prior-year plan is zero

## Qualified Reservist Distribution

Special rule allows distributions from health FSA to reservists:
- Ordered or called to active duty for more than 179 days or indefinite period
- Distribution made during period from order date through last date for reimbursements
- Employer reports as wages on Form W-2
- Subject to employment taxes and included in gross income

## Employer Requirements

For tax-qualified status:
- Must comply with cafeteria plan requirements
- Restrictions for highly compensated employees and key employees
- Must comply with accident and health plan rules
- See [[Pub 15-B - Employer's Tax Guide to Fringe Benefits]]

## Comparison with Other Health Plans

| Feature | Health FSA | HSA | HRA |
|---------|-----------|-----|-----|
| Who contributes | Employee, employer | Individual, employer, others | Employer only |
| Self-employed eligible | No | Yes | No |
| Annual limit | $3,300 (2025) | $4,300/$8,550 (2025) | No limit |
| Carryover | Up to $660 or grace period | Unlimited | Yes |
| Portable | No | Yes | No |
