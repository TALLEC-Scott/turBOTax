# Schedule C (Form 1040)

## Overview

Schedule C (Form 1040) is the primary tax form used by sole proprietors, independent contractors, and statutory employees to report profit or loss from a business. It calculates net earnings from self-employment, which flows to [[Form 1040]] via Schedule 1.

**Who Must File:**
- Sole proprietors operating a trade or business
- [[Independent Contractor|Independent contractors]]
- Statutory employees (box 13 checked on Form W-2)
- Single-member LLC owners (unless electing corporate treatment)
- [[Qualified Joint Venture]] spouses (each files separate Schedule C)

**Who Does NOT File Schedule C:**
- Partnerships (file [[Form 1065]])
- Corporations (file Form 1120 or 1120-S)
- Rental activities not connected with a trade or business (use Schedule E)
- Farming businesses (use [[Schedule F (Form 1040)]])

## Key Lines

| Part | Lines | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| I | 1-7 | [[Business Income|Income]] - Gross receipts, returns/allowances, cost of goods sold, gross profit |
| II | 8-27 | Expenses - Advertising, car/truck, commissions, contract labor, depletion |
| III | 28-30 | Cost of Goods Sold (from lines 35-42) |
| IV | 31 | Net profit or loss |
| V | 32 | Investment in the business (at-risk amount) |

## Cost of Goods Sold (Lines 35-42)

If the business produces, purchases, or sells merchandise, calculate [[Cost of Goods Sold]]:

| Line | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 35 | Inventory at beginning of year |
| 36 | Purchases less cost of items withdrawn for personal use |
| 37 | Cost of labor (not paid to yourself) |
| 38 | Materials and supplies |
| 39 | Other costs |
| 40 | Total (sum of lines 35-39) |
| 41 | Inventory at end of year |
| 42 | Cost of goods sold (line 40 minus line 41) |

## Business Expenses

Deductible expenses must be both:
- **Ordinary:** Common and accepted in your trade or business
- **Necessary:** Helpful and appropriate for your business

Common deductible expenses on Schedule C:
- [[Car and Truck Expenses]] (line 9)
- [[Depreciation for Farmers|Depreciation]] and Section 179 (line 13)
- Insurance (line 15)
- Interest (line 16)
- Legal and professional fees (line 17)
- Office expense (line 18)
- Rent or lease (line 20)
- Supplies (line 22)
- Travel and meals (line 24)
- Wages (line 26)

## Reporting Flow

```
Schedule C (Form 1040)
        ↓
   Net Profit/Loss (Line 31)
        ↓
   Schedule 1 (Form 1040), Line 3
        ↓
   Form 1040 (Adjusted Gross Income)
        ↓
   Schedule SE (Form 1040) → [[Self-Employment Tax]]
```

## Multiple Businesses

If operating more than one business as a sole proprietorship:
- File a separate Schedule C for each business
- File only one Schedule SE combining all net profits/losses
- A loss from one business reduces profit from another for SE tax purposes

## Recordkeeping Requirements

Per [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]], maintain records that:
- Show all income and expenses
- Support deductions claimed
- Track inventory and assets
- Substantiate business purpose of expenses

**Retention Period:** Keep records for at least 3 years from the date the return was filed

## Common Questions

### Q: Can I deduct expenses if I don't make a profit?
**A:** Generally yes, if the activity is engaged in for profit. If not, deductions may be limited under hobby loss rules. See [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]] Chapter 9.

### Q: What if I have a net loss?
**A:** Net losses may reduce other income on your tax return. However, excess business loss limitations may apply. Use [[Form 461]] to determine limitations.

### Q: Can I deduct my own salary?
**A:** No. As a sole proprietor, you are not an employee of your business. You report net profit and pay income and self-employment tax on that amount.
