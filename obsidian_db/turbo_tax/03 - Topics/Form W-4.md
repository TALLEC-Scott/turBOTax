# Form W-4 - Employee's Withholding Certificate

## Overview

Form W-4 tells employers how much federal income tax to withhold from employee wages. The redesigned form (2020 and later) uses a four-step process to calculate withholding based on personal circumstances.

## Purpose

- Determines federal income tax withholding from wages
- Accounts for multiple jobs, dependents, and other income
- Can be updated anytime during the year
- Must be completed when starting a new job

## Four Steps of Form W-4

### Step 1: Personal Information
- Enter name, address, SSN
- Select anticipated **filing status**:
  - Single or Married filing separately
  - Married filing jointly (or Qualifying surviving spouse)
  - Head of household

### Step 2: Multiple Jobs or Working Spouse
Complete if you:
1. Hold more than one job at a time, OR
2. Are married filing jointly and spouse also works

**Options:**
- **Option (a):** Use IRS Tax Withholding Estimator (IRS.gov/W4App)
- **Option (b):** Use Multiple Jobs Worksheet
- **Option (c):** Check box for highest-paying job (simpler but less accurate)

**Important:** Complete Steps 3-4(b) on only ONE Form W-4 (preferably highest-paying job).

### Step 3: Claim Dependents and Tax Credits
- Calculate [[Child Tax Credit]] amount
- Calculate [[Credit for Other Dependents]] amount
- Include other tax credits:
  - Education credits ([[Pub 970 - Tax Benefits for Education|Pub 970]])
  - [[Foreign Tax Credit]] ([[Pub 514 - Foreign Tax Credit for Individuals|Pub 514]])
  - [[Child and Dependent Care Credit]] ([[Pub 503 - Child and Dependent Care Expenses|Pub 503]])
  - [[Retirement Savings Contributions Credit]] (Saver's credit)

### Step 4: Other Adjustments
- **4(a):** Other income (not from jobs) - interest, dividends, retirement income
- **4(b):** Deductions (itemized or above-the-line deductions exceeding standard)
- **4(c):** Extra withholding amount per pay period

## When to Submit a New Form W-4

### Required Updates (within 10 days)
- You or spouse start another job (if used multiple jobs worksheet)
- Filing status changes (MFJ→Single, HOH→Single, etc.)
- No longer expect to claim Child Tax Credit taken on prior W-4
- Other credits decrease by more than $500
- No longer qualify for exemption from withholding
- Deductions decrease by more than $2,300

### Recommended Updates
- Marriage or divorce
- Birth or adoption of child
- Home purchase
- Large refund or balance due on prior return
- Significant income changes

## Exemption From Withholding

### Eligibility Requirements
Can claim exemption if BOTH apply:
1. Expected refund of ALL federal income tax withheld (no tax liability for 2025)
2. Had right to refund of ALL federal tax withheld in 2024 (no tax liability)

### How to Claim
- Write "Exempt" below Step 4(c)
- Complete only Steps 1(a), 1(b), and 5
- Do NOT complete Steps 2-4

### Exemption Rules
- Good for **one year only**
- Must submit new Form W-4 by **February 15** each year
- Does NOT exempt from Social Security or Medicare taxes
- May still need to file a tax return

### Students
- Not automatically exempt
- May qualify if working part-time or summer only
- Use Figure 1-A in [[Pub 505 - Tax Withholding and Estimated Tax|Pub 505]] to determine eligibility

## Special Situations

### Nonresident Aliens
- Cannot claim "Exempt" status
- Must follow special instructions in Notice 1392
- See [[Pub 519 - U.S. Tax Guide for Aliens|Pub 519]] Chapter 8

### Multiple Jobs
- Withholding may be too low if same W-4 used for all jobs
- Use Step 2(c) checkbox on highest-paying job's W-4
- Or use Multiple Jobs Worksheet/IRS Estimator for accuracy

### Part-Year Employment
- May request employer use **part-year method** if employed ≤245 days
- Reduces withholding compared to full-year calculation
- Must request in writing

### Cumulative Wage Method
- Available when changing withholding mid-year
- Compensates for earlier over/under-withholding
- Must request in writing from employer

## Employer Responsibilities

### Implementation Timeline
- New Form W-4 must be implemented by start of first payroll period ending 30+ days after receipt
- Changes for next year take effect January 1

### No Form W-4 Received
- Withhold as **Single** with no adjustments in Steps 2-4

### IRS Lock-In Letters
- IRS may issue lock-in letter if employee claims incorrect withholding
- Employer must follow IRS-specified withholding
- Employee can increase but not decrease withholding

## Penalties

### Civil Penalty
- **$500** for making false statements that reduce withholding
- Must have no reasonable basis for statements

### Criminal Penalty
- Fine up to **$1,000**, imprisonment up to **1 year**, or both
- For willfully supplying false/fraudulent information

## Tools and Resources

### IRS Tax Withholding Estimator
- Available at **IRS.gov/W4App**
- Helps determine correct withholding
- Accounts for multiple jobs, dependents, credits

### Worksheets in Pub 505
- Worksheet 1-3: Project total tax liability
- Worksheet 1-5: Compare withholding to projected tax
- Worksheet 1-6: Calculate tax credits for Step 3

## 2025 Standard Deduction Reference

| Filing Status | Amount |
|---------------|--------|
| Married Filing Jointly | $30,000 |
| Single or MFS | $15,000 |
| Head of Household | $22,500 |
