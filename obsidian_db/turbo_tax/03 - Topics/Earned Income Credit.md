# Earned Income Credit (EIC)

The Earned Income Credit (EIC), also called the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC), is a refundable tax credit for low-to-moderate income working individuals and families. It reduces the amount of tax owed and may result in a refund.

## 2025 Income Limits

| Qualifying Children | Single/Head of Household | Married Filing Jointly |
|---------------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| 0 | $19,104 | $26,214 |
| 1 | $50,434 | $57,554 |
| 2 | $57,310 | $64,430 |
| 3 or more | $61,555 | $68,675 |

**Both earned income AND AGI must be below these limits.**

## 2025 Maximum Credit Amounts

| Qualifying Children | Maximum Credit |
|---------------------|----------------|
| 0 | $649 |
| 1 | $4,328 |
| 2 | $7,152 |
| 3 or more | $8,046 |

## Eligibility Requirements

### Basic Requirements (Everyone)
1. **AGI Limit**: Must be below the applicable limit (see table above)
2. **Valid SSN**: You (and spouse if filing jointly) must have valid SSN by return due date
3. **Filing Status**: Cannot file as [[Married Filing Separately|Married Filing Separately]]. See [[Separated Spouse EIC Rules|separated spouse exceptions]].
4. **Citizenship**: Must be U.S. citizen or resident alien all year
5. **No Form 2555**: Cannot file [[Foreign Earned Income Exclusion|Form 2555]] (foreign earned income exclusion)
6. **Investment Income**: Must be $11,950 or less (see [[Investment Income for EIC|investment income rules]])
7. **Earned Income**: Must have earned income from work (see [[EIC Earned Income Rules|earned income definition]])

### With Qualifying Child
If you have a [[Qualifying Child|qualifying child]], additional rules in [[Pub 596 - Earned Income Credit|Publication 596, Chapter 2]] apply:
- Child must meet relationship, age, residency, and joint return tests
- [[EIC Tiebreaker Rules|Tiebreaker rules]] apply if child qualifies multiple persons
- Cannot be qualifying child of another taxpayer

### Without Qualifying Child
If you have no qualifying child, see [[Pub 596 - Earned Income Credit|Publication 596, Chapter 3]]:
- Must be at least age 25 but under age 65 at end of year
- Cannot be dependent of another person
- Cannot be qualifying child of another taxpayer
- Must have lived in U.S. more than half the year

## Qualifying Child for EIC

| Test | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Relationship** | Son, daughter, stepchild, foster child, sibling, or descendant of any of them |
| **Age** | Under 19 (under 24 if full-time student) or any age if permanently and totally disabled |
| **Residency** | Lived with you in the U.S. for more than half the year |
| **Joint Return** | Must not file joint return (unless only to claim refund) |
| **SSN** | Must have valid SSN issued before return due date |

See [[Qualifying Child]] for detailed rules.

## Special Situations

### Separated Spouses
Married persons who lived apart for the last 6 months of the year may qualify for EIC even if not filing jointly. See [[Separated Spouse EIC Rules]] for details.

### Members of the Military
- Nontaxable combat pay can be elected to be included in earned income
- Extended active duty outside U.S. counts as U.S. residency
- See [[Pub 3 - Armed Forces Tax Guide]] for military-specific rules

### Members of the Clergy
- Housing allowances have special EIC treatment
- See [[Ministers and Church Employees]] and Form 4361

### Self-Employed
- Calculate earned income on EIC Worksheet B
- See [[Self-Employment Tax]] and [[Schedule C (Form 1040)|Schedule C]]

## Earned Income Definition

### Includes
- Wages, salaries, tips (Form W-2, box 1)
- Net earnings from [[Self-Employment Tax|self-employment]]
- Disability benefits (if under minimum retirement age)
- Nontaxable combat pay (optional election)

### Does NOT Include
- Interest and dividends
- Pensions and annuities
- [[Social Security Benefits]]
- Alimony and child support
- Unemployment compensation

See [[EIC Earned Income Rules]] for comprehensive details.

## Claiming the Credit

1. File [[Form 1040]] or 1040-SR
2. Complete [[Schedule EIC]] if claiming with qualifying child
3. Use EIC Table or IRS EITC Assistant to calculate credit
4. File Form 8862 if EIC was previously disallowed

## Disallowance and Recertification

EIC may be disallowed for:
- **Fraud**: 10-year ban
- **Reckless/disregard of rules**: 2-year ban
- **Intentional disregard**: 2-year ban

File Form 8862 to recertify eligibility after disallowance.

## Impact on Welfare Benefits

EIC refunds cannot be counted as income for:
- TANF (Temporary Assistance for Needy Families)
- SSI (Supplemental Security Income)
- Medicaid
- SNAP (food stamps)
- Low-income housing

## State EIC

Many states offer their own earned income credit. Check your state's tax form if you qualify for federal EIC.

## Common Errors to Avoid

- Claiming child who doesn't meet qualifying child tests
- Using incorrect SSN
- Reporting incorrect income
- Filing as [[Head of Household]] when not eligible
- Not attaching [[Schedule EIC]] when required
