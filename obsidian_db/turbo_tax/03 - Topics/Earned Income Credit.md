# Earned Income Credit (EIC)

The Earned Income Credit (EIC) is a refundable tax credit for low-to-moderate income working individuals and families. It reduces the amount of tax owed and may result in a refund.

## 2025 Eligibility Requirements

### Basic Requirements
- Have earned income from employment or self-employment
- Have investment income of $11,600 or less
- Have a valid Social Security Number (for you, your spouse if filing jointly, and any qualifying children)
- Be a U.S. citizen or resident alien for the entire year
- Not file Form 2555 (Foreign Earned Income) or Form 2555-EZ

### Filing Status
- Cannot file as **Married Filing Separately**
- If married, must file jointly (with exceptions for separated spouses)

### Income Limits (2025)

| Filing Status | No Children | One Child | Two Children | Three+ Children |
|---------------|-------------|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Single/Head of Household | $18,600 | $46,650 | $52,850 | $56,600 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $24,450 | $52,500 | $58,700 | $62,450 |

### Maximum Credit Amounts (2025)

| Children | Maximum Credit |
|----------|----------------|
| 0 | $649 |
| 1 | $4,328 |
| 2 | $7,152 |
| 3 or more | $8,046 |

## Qualifying Child for EIC

A qualifying child for EIC must meet:

| Test | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Relationship** | Son, daughter, stepchild, foster child, sibling, or descendant |
| **Age** | Under 19 (under 24 if full-time student) or any age if disabled |
| **Residency** | Lived with you in the U.S. for more than half the year |
| **Joint Return** | Must not file a joint return (unless only for refund) |
| **SSN** | Must have valid SSN issued before due date of return |

## Special Rules

### Married Persons Living Apart
If married but lived apart from spouse for the last 6 months of the year, you may be able to claim EIC if:
- Your home was the main home of your child for more than half the year
- You paid more than half the cost of keeping up the home for the year

### Members of the Military
- Nontaxable combat pay can be included in earned income (may increase EIC)
- Combat zone extensions apply for filing deadlines
- See [[Pub 3 - Armed Forces Tax Guide]] for military-specific rules

### Puerto Rico Residents
Bona fide residents of Puerto Rico may now claim ACTC with one or more qualifying children (previously required three or more).

## What Counts as Earned Income?

**Includes:**
- Wages, salaries, tips
- Union strike benefits
- Disability pensions (if under minimum retirement age)
- Self-employment income
- Nontaxable combat pay (optional)

**Does NOT include:**
- Interest and dividends
- Pensions or annuities
- Social Security benefits
- Alimony
- Unemployment compensation

## Preventing Errors and Fraud

Common errors to avoid:
- Claiming a child who doesn't meet the tests
- Using an incorrect SSN
- Reporting incorrect income
- Filing as head of household when not eligible

## Claiming the Credit

1. File Form 1040 or 1040-SR
2. Complete Schedule EIC if you have a qualifying child
3. Answer EIC questions on your return
4. Attach Form 8862 if required (after credit was previously denied)
