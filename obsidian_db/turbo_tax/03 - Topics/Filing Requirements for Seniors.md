# Filing Requirements for Seniors

## Overview

Taxpayers age 65 or older have higher gross income thresholds for determining whether they must file a federal income tax return. These higher thresholds recognize that many seniors live on fixed incomes and may have less taxable income than working-age taxpayers.

## Key Rules

### Age 65 Definition
- Considered age 65 on the day **before** your 65th birthday
- For 2025 returns: Born before January 2, 1961 = age 65+ for entire year

### 2025 Filing Thresholds

| Filing Status | Age at End of 2025 | File if Gross Income ≥ |
|---------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Single | Under 65 | $15,750 |
| Single | 65 or older | $17,750 |
| Head of Household | Under 65 | $23,625 |
| Head of Household | 65 or older | $25,625 |
| Married Filing Jointly | Both under 65 | $31,500 |
| Married Filing Jointly | One spouse 65+ | $33,100 |
| Married Filing Jointly | Both 65+ | $34,700 |
| Married Filing Separately | Any age | $5 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | Under 65 | $31,500 |
| Qualifying Surviving Spouse | 65 or older | $33,100 |

## When to File Even If Below Threshold

File a return if any of these apply:

1. **Tax was withheld** and you want a refund
2. **Refundable credits** you may qualify for:
   - Earned Income Credit (EIC)
   - Additional Child Tax Credit
   - American Opportunity Credit
   - Premium Tax Credit
3. **Estimated tax payments** made and want refund
4. **Self-employment income** of $400 or more

## Gross Income Definition

Gross income includes all income received as:
- Money
- Goods
- Property
- Services

**Not included** (nontaxable):
- Social Security benefits (usually - see below)
- SSI payments
- Veterans' benefits
- Welfare benefits
- Gifts and inheritances

### Self-Employed Persons
- Service businesses: Gross receipts = gross income
- Sales businesses: Total sales minus cost of goods sold

## Special Situations

### Dependents
If you can be claimed as a dependent by another taxpayer, different filing requirements apply. See [[Pub 501]].

### Decedents
- Final return required if deceased would have been required to file
- Mark return "DECEASED" with name and date of death
- Surviving spouse can file joint return for year of death
- File Form 56 to notify IRS of fiduciary relationship

### Surviving Spouse Filing Status
- Year of spouse's death: Can file joint return
- Next 2 years: May qualify as Qualifying Surviving Spouse (same as MFJ tax rates)
- After that: Single or Head of Household if qualifying

### Community Property States
Half of community income may be considered yours. Community property states:
- Arizona, California, Idaho, Louisiana, Nevada, New Mexico, Texas, Washington, Wisconsin
