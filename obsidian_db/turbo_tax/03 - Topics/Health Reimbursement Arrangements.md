# Health Reimbursement Arrangements (HRAs)

## Overview

A Health Reimbursement Arrangement (HRA) is an employer-funded plan that reimburses employees for qualified medical expenses. Unlike FSAs, HRAs are funded solely by employers and employees cannot contribute. See [[Pub 969 - Health Savings Accounts and Other Tax-Favored Health Plans]].

## Benefits of an HRA

- Employer contributions excluded from gross income
- No federal income or employment taxes on contributions
- Tax-free reimbursements for qualified medical expenses
- Unused amounts can carry forward to future years
- Can be offered with other health plans, including FSAs

## Eligibility

- Must be offered through employer
- Self-employed individuals are NOT eligible
- May have limitations for highly compensated participants
- Can be offered with other employer-provided health benefits

## Contributions

### Who Can Contribute
- **Employer ONLY** - Employees cannot contribute
- Cannot be funded through salary reduction under a cafeteria plan

### Contribution Limits
- No statutory limit on employer contributions
- Employer has flexibility in setting maximum reimbursement amounts

## Distributions

### Qualified Medical Expenses
Tax-free reimbursements for [[Qualified Medical Expenses]] incurred on or after enrollment date.

### Covered Individuals
1. Current and former employees
2. Spouses and dependents of employees
3. Persons who could have been dependents except for exceptions
4. Child under age 27 at end of tax year
5. Spouses and dependents of deceased employees

### Expenses Covered
- Same qualified medical expenses as itemized deduction
- Over-the-counter medicine (whether or not prescribed)
- Menstrual care products
- **Health insurance premiums** (unlike HSAs and FSAs)
- **Long-term care coverage** (unlike HSAs and FSAs)

### Substantiation
Debit/credit cards may be used if substantiation methods are met. See:
- Revenue Ruling 2003-43
- Notice 2006-69
- Notice 2007-2

### Taxable Distributions
Any distribution becomes taxable if:
- Unused reimbursement payable in cash at year-end or employment termination
- Unused amount upon death payable in cash to beneficiary/estate
- Option to transfer unused amount to retirement plan
- Payments made to non-spouse/non-dependent beneficiaries

**Caution:** If ANY distribution can be made for non-qualified purposes, ALL distributions become taxable.

## Balance and Carryover

- Plan may allow unused amounts to carry to next year
- Employer cannot refund any balance to employee
- Amounts must be used only for qualified medical expenses
- No "use-it-or-lose-it" rule like FSAs

## Interaction with HSA

General Rule: An employee covered by an HDHP and an HRA that pays medical expenses cannot contribute to an HSA.

**Exception Types that Preserve HSA Eligibility:**

### Limited-Purpose HRA
- Pays only dental, vision, preventive care, or other permitted coverage
- Does not pay general medical expenses

### Suspended HRA
- Employee elects to suspend HRA before coverage period
- HRA doesn't pay expenses during suspension (except preventive care/permitted items)
- Contributions resume after suspension period

### Post-Deductible HRA
- Pays only after minimum annual deductible met
- Does not pay expenses before deductible

### Retiree-Only HRA
- Pays only medical expenses incurred after retirement
- Does not cover current employees

## Qualified Small Employer HRA (QSEHRA)

Special rules apply to QSEHRA under Section 18036. See separate guidance for:
- Eligibility requirements
- Contribution limits
- Reporting requirements

## Individual Coverage HRA (ICHRA)

Special rules under Section 54.9831-1 for HRAs that reimburse individual health insurance premiums. Consult current guidance for requirements.

## Employer Requirements

For tax-qualified status:
- Must comply with applicable cafeteria plan requirements
- Must comply with accident and health plan rules
- See [[Pub 15-B - Employer's Tax Guide to Fringe Benefits]]

## Comparison with Other Health Plans

| Feature | HRA | HSA | Health FSA |
|---------|-----|-----|------------|
| Who contributes | Employer only | Individual, employer, others | Employee, employer |
| Self-employed eligible | No | Yes | No |
| Contribution limit | No limit | $4,300/$8,550 (2025) | $3,300 (2025) |
| Carryover | Yes | Yes | Limited |
| Portable | No | Yes | No |
| Can reimburse premiums | Yes | Limited | No |
| Can reimburse LTC | Yes | Yes (with limits) | No |
