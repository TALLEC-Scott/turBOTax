# Installment Sales for Farmers

## Overview

An installment sale is a sale of property where at least one payment is received after the tax year of sale. This method allows farmers to spread gain recognition over multiple years as payments are received.

## Requirements

### Eligible Property
- Real property used in farming
- Farm machinery and equipment
- Livestock (except inventory animals)
- Must not be property held for sale to customers in ordinary course of business

### Ineligible Sales
- Inventory items (raised livestock, crops)
- Dealers in installment obligations
- Losses (deduct in year of sale)

## How Installment Sales Work

### Gross Profit Percentage
- Gross profit ÷ Contract price = Gross profit percentage
- Apply to each payment received

### Payments Received
- Cash payments
- Fair market value of property received
- Liabilities assumed by buyer

## Reporting

### Year of Sale
- File Form 6252 for each installment sale
- Report gross profit percentage
- Calculate and report gain on payments received

### Subsequent Years
- File Form 6252 for each year payments received
- Apply gross profit percentage to payments

## Farm Sales

### Multiple Assets
- Allocate selling price among assets
- Figure separate gross profit percentage for each
- May need multiple Form 6252 filings

### Home on Farm
- Personal residence portion excluded from installment reporting
- May qualify for home sale exclusion
- See [[Sale of Home Exclusion]]

## Special Rules

### Related Parties
- May need to report all gain immediately
- Anti-abuse rules apply
- Second disposition rules

### Depreciation Recapture
- Report recapture as ordinary income in year of sale
- Recapture reported before installment gain
- Cannot use installment method for recapture portion

### Interest
- Charge adequate interest or imputed interest applies
- Interest is ordinary income when received

## Electing Out

### When to Elect Out
- Report entire gain in year of sale
- May be beneficial if:
  - Tax rates expected to increase
  - Have losses to offset gains
  - Prefer certainty

### How to Elect Out
- Report entire sale on Form 4797 or Schedule D
- Do not file Form 6252
