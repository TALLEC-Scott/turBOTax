# Form 6252 - Installment Sale Income

## Overview

**Form 6252** is used to report income from an [[Installment Sales|installment sale]] of property. It is filed for the year of sale and for each subsequent year in which a payment is received.

## When to Use Form 6252

Use Form 6252 when:
- You sold property on the installment method
- You are receiving payments after the tax year of sale
- You have not elected out of the installment method

Do NOT use Form 6252 if:
- You elect to report the entire gain in the year of sale (use [[Form 8949]] instead)
- The sale resulted in a loss
- The property was inventory or dealer property

## Parts of Form 6252

### Part I - Gross Profit and Contract Price

Completed for the year of sale and each subsequent year:
- Description of property sold
- Date of sale
- Selling price
- Adjusted basis
- Selling expenses
- Depreciation recapture
- Gross profit
- Contract price
- Gross profit percentage

### Part II - Installment Sale Income

Completed for each year of the installment agreement:
- Payments received during the year
- Payments applied to:
  - Return of basis
  - Gain (installment sale income)

### Part III - Related Party Information

Required when:
- You sold property to a related person
- Must be completed for year of sale and 2 years after
- Reports second disposition information if applicable

## Filing Requirements

File Form 6252:
- For the year of the sale
- For each year you receive a payment
- For the year of final payment (even if no payment received)
- Each year if you sold to a related person (until obligation is paid)

## Common Questions

### Q: Do I need to file Form 6252 if I receive no payment this year?
**A:** If you sold to a related person, yes—file Form 6252 each year until the debt is paid. Otherwise, file only for years you receive payments.

### Q: Can I use tax software to file Form 6252?
**A:** Yes. Most tax software supports installment sale reporting and will calculate the gross profit percentage and annual income.

### Q: What if I made multiple installment sales?
**A:** File a separate Form 6252 for each installment sale.
