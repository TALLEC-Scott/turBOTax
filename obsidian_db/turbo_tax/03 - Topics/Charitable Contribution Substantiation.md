# Charitable Contribution Substantiation

## Overview

Tax-exempt organizations must provide donors with written acknowledgments for charitable contributions. The substantiation requirements ensure donors can claim appropriate deductions while protecting organizations from penalties.

## Contributions of $250 or More

### Written Acknowledgment Required
Donors cannot deduct contributions of $250 or more without written acknowledgment from the organization.

### Required Information
Acknowledgment must include:
1. Amount of cash contributed
2. Description (but not value) of noncash property
3. Statement whether goods or services were provided
4. Description and good faith estimate of value of goods/services
5. Statement that only the contribution exceeding the value is deductible

### Timing
Must be received by earlier of:
- Date donor files tax return for year of contribution
- Due date (including extensions) of that return

### Donor Responsibility
- Donor must request and obtain acknowledgment
- Organization not required to send automatically (but should)

## Quid Pro Quo Contributions Over $75

### Definition
A quid pro quo contribution is a payment:
- Made partly as contribution
- Partly for goods or services

### Disclosure Required
Organization must provide written statement:
1. Informing donor deductible amount is limited
2. Providing good faith estimate of value received

### Exception
No disclosure required if:
- Goods/services have insubstantial value
- No donative element involved
- Only intangible religious benefit provided
- Payment $75 or less with annual membership benefits

### Penalty
- $10 per contribution failure
- Maximum $5,000 per fundraising event or mailing
- Reasonable cause exception available

## Cash Contributions

### Documentation Required
Donor must have:
- Bank record (cancelled check, bank statement)
- Written communication from organization
- Payroll deduction records

### Required Information
- Name of organization
- Date of contribution
- Amount of contribution

## Noncash Contributions

### C-Corporation Inventory Gifts
Qualified contributions of inventory for care of ill, needy, or infants.

### Vehicle Contributions
If claimed value exceeds $500:
- Organization must provide Form 1098-C
- Donee acknowledgment within 30 days of sale
- Report to IRS by February 28 (March 31 if electronic)

### Noncash Over $5,000
Donor needs:
- Qualified appraisal
- Form 8283, Section B signed by donee
- Organization acknowledgment

## Donee Information Return (Form 8282)

### When Required
If donated property is sold, exchanged, or disposed of within 3 years:
- Value was over $500
- Donor signed Form 8283
- Organization must file Form 8282

### Exceptions
- Property consumed for charitable purposes
- Property distributed to charitable beneficiaries
- Property value $500 or less

### Timing
- File within 125 days of disposition
- Provide copy to donor

## Good Faith Estimate of Value

### Methods
Organization may use any reasonable method applied in good faith:
- Comparable sales prices
- Commercial prices for similar services
- $0 for items not commercially available

### Examples
- Tennis lesson provided by professional: FMV = commercial lesson price
- Museum event: FMV = comparable hotel rental
- Private museum tour by artist: FMV = $0 (no commercial equivalent)

## Penalties

### Failure to Disclose Quid Pro Quo
- $10 per contribution
- Maximum $5,000 per event
- Reasonable cause exception

### Fraudulent Acknowledgment (Vehicle)
- Penalty per occurrence
- May affect organization's exemption

## Recordkeeping for Organizations

### Best Practices
- Send acknowledgments for all contributions of $250+
- Include all required information
- Maintain copies of acknowledgments
- Track noncash contributions separately

### Sample Acknowledgment Language
"Thank you for your contribution of $[amount] on [date]. No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution."

For quid pro quo: "You received [goods/services] with a fair market value of $[amount]. The deductible portion of your contribution is $[amount]."
