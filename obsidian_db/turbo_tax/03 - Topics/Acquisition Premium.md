# Acquisition Premium

## Overview

Acquisition premium is the excess of a debt instrument's adjusted basis immediately after purchase over its adjusted issue price at that time. When you purchase a debt instrument at acquisition premium, you reduce the OID you report each year.

## Definition

**Acquisition Premium** = Adjusted Basis after Purchase - Adjusted Issue Price at Purchase

Where:
- **Adjusted Basis** = Your purchase price
- **Adjusted Issue Price** = Issue Price + OID accrued before purchase

## When It Occurs

Acquisition premium occurs when you purchase a debt instrument for MORE than its adjusted issue price but LESS than the total of all amounts payable (otherwise you have premium, not acquisition premium).

## Effect on OID Reporting

### Reduction Method
Reduce the daily OID by the daily acquisition premium:

**Daily Acquisition Premium** = Daily OID × (Acquisition Premium ÷ Total OID Remaining)

### Example Calculation
- Daily OID: $1.01965
- Acquisition premium: $590.72
- Total OID remaining after purchase: $13,590.72
- Daily acquisition premium: $1.01965 × ($590.72 ÷ $13,590.72) = $0.04432
- Reduced daily OID: $1.01965 - $0.04432 = $0.97533

## Reporting on Form 1099-OID

For covered securities, brokers may either:
- Report gross OID in Box 1/8 AND acquisition premium amortization in Box 6
- Report net OID amount in Box 1/8 (reflecting the adjustment), leaving Box 6 blank

## Key Rules by Purchase Date

### Purchased Before July 19, 1984
Divide total acquisition premium by days from purchase to maturity.

### Purchased After July 18, 1984
Use the fraction method: Daily OID × (Acquisition Premium ÷ Total OID Remaining)

## Distinction from Premium

| Situation | Condition | Effect |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Acquisition Premium | Basis > Adjusted Issue Price but < Total Payable | Reduces OID |
| Premium | Basis > Total Payable Amounts | Eliminates OID entirely |
