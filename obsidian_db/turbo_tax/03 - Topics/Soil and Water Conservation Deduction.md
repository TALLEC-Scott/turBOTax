# Soil and Water Conservation Deduction

## Overview

Farmers can deduct expenses for soil and water conservation on land used in farming or for the prevention of erosion. This deduction is subject to limitations and may require capitalization in certain circumstances.

## Eligible Expenses

### Deductible Conservation Expenses
- Leveling, grading, and terracing farmland
- Contour farming and strip cropping
- Irrigation ditches and canals
- Erosion control structures
- Windbreaks and water conservation plants
- Drainage facilities and ditches

### Requirements
- Land must be used for farming
- Expenses must be for conservation purposes
- Must be consistent with conservation plan (if applicable)

## Deduction Limit

The deduction is limited to **25% of gross farm income** for the tax year.

### Order of Application
1. First, deduct soil and water conservation expenses up to 25% of gross farm income
2. Any excess carries forward to future years
3. Excess is treated as conservation expense in carryforward year

### Example
- Gross farm income: $200,000
- 25% limit: $50,000
- If conservation expenses are $70,000, deduct $50,000 and carry forward $20,000

## Land Requirement

The land must be used in farming at the time the expenses are paid or incurred. If land is later sold or converted to nonfarm use, recapture rules may apply.

## Conservation Plan

Expenses must be consistent with a soil conservation plan approved by the Natural Resources Conservation Service (NRCS) or a comparable state agency. However, having a plan is not required to deduct expenses.

## Non-Deductible Items

- Expenses for land not used in farming
- Landscaping for aesthetic purposes
- Drainage facilities that permanently drain wetlands
- Expenses for producing sod or nursery stock
