# Student Loan Cancellations

## Overview

Generally, when a loan is canceled or forgiven, the amount is taxable income. However, there are important exceptions for student loans, particularly for discharges in 2021-2025 under the American Rescue Plan Act.

**Authority:** [[Pub 970 - Tax Benefits for Education#Chapter 5]]

## General Rule

Loan cancellation = taxable income to the borrower

**Exception:** Certain student loan cancellations can be excluded from gross income.

## Tax-Free Cancellation Programs

### 1. Loans for Postsecondary Educational Expenses
Cancellation is tax-free if loan was made, insured, or guaranteed by:
- United States or agency
- State, territory, or political subdivision
- Eligible educational institution

**Condition:** Cancellation must NOT be for services performed for lender or funding organization.

### 2. Private Education Loans
Cancellation is tax-free if:
- Loan provided by private educational lender
- Issued expressly for postsecondary education
- Cancellation NOT for services performed for lender

### 3. Educational Organization Loans (Section 170(b)(1)(A)(ii))
Tax-free if:
- Loan from educational organization
- Made under program to encourage service in underserved areas
- Services for government unit or 501(c)(3) organization

### 4. Tax-Exempt Organization Refinancing
Tax-free if:
- Original loan was qualified student loan
- Refinanced by tax-exempt organization
- Program designed to encourage service in underserved areas

## Student Loan Repayment Assistance (Tax-Free)

Amounts received from these programs are tax-free:

1. **National Health Service Corps (NHSC) Loan Repayment Program**

2. **State Education Loan Repayment Programs**
   - Eligible for funds under Public Health Service Act

3. **Other State Programs**
   - Intended to increase health services in underserved areas

**Note:** Cannot deduct student loan interest paid through these programs.

## American Rescue Plan Act (2021-2025)

**Student loan forgiveness is tax-FREE for discharges in 2021 through 2025.**

This includes:
- Federal student loan forgiveness programs
- Income-driven repayment forgiveness
- Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)
- Other federal discharges

## Coordination with Other Benefits

When loan is forgiven:
- Reduces qualified education expenses for credits
- Cannot claim interest deduction for forgiven interest

## Reporting

| Situation | Tax Treatment |
|-----------|---------------|
| Tax-free forgiveness | No reporting required |
| Taxable forgiveness | Report as "Other Income" on Schedule 1 |

## Common Questions

### Q: Is student loan forgiveness always tax-free?
**A:** For federal loans discharged in 2021-2025, yes. Otherwise, depends on the type of loan and reason for discharge.

### Q: What about state taxes on forgiveness?
**A:** Some states may tax federal loan forgiveness. Check your state's rules.

### Q: Does employer student loan payment affect this?
**A:** Employer payments up to $5,250/year are separately excluded under educational assistance programs.
