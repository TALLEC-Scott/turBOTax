# Tax Withholding and Estimated Tax

The U.S. tax system is pay-as-you-go. You must pay tax throughout the year through withholding, estimated payments, or both.

## Pay-As-You-Go System

Two ways to pay tax during the year:
1. **Withholding** - Automatically taken from wages, pensions, etc.
2. **Estimated Tax** - Quarterly payments for income not subject to withholding

## Withholding

### Wage Withholding (Form W-4)
- Employer withholds based on Form W-4 information
- Adjust W-4 when life changes (marriage, children, second job)
- Use IRS Withholding Estimator: IRS.gov/W4app

### Pension/Annuity Withholding (Form W-4P)
- Can elect withholding from pension, annuity, IRA distributions
- Choose percentage or specific amount

### Social Security Withholding (Form W-4V)
- Voluntary withholding from Social Security benefits
- Choose: 7%, 10%, 12%, or 22%

### Other Withholding
- Gambling winnings (Form W-2G)
- Unemployment compensation (Form 1099-G)

## Estimated Tax Payments

### Who Must Pay Estimated Tax
Generally required if you expect to owe $1,000+ at tax time AND:
- Self-employment income
- Interest, dividends, alimony
- Rental income
- Gains from asset sales
- Income not subject to withholding

### 2025 Quarterly Due Dates

| Quarter | Period Covered | Due Date |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| 1 | Jan 1 - Mar 31 | April 15, 2025 |
| 2 | Apr 1 - May 31 | June 16, 2025 |
| 3 | Jun 1 - Aug 31 | September 15, 2025 |
| 4 | Sep 1 - Dec 31 | January 15, 2026 |

### How Much to Pay

**Safe Harbor Rules (avoid penalty):**
Pay the lesser of:
1. **90%** of current year's tax, OR
2. **100%** of prior year's tax (110% if AGI over $150,000)

### How to Pay
- IRS Direct Pay: IRS.gov/Payments
- Electronic Federal Tax Payment System (EFTPS): EFTPS.gov
- Debit/credit card (fees apply)
- Check/money order with Form 1040-ES

## Underpayment Penalty

### Penalty Avoided If:
1. Total tax less than $1,000, OR
2. Withholding + estimated payments equal:
   - 90% of current year's tax, OR
   - 100% of prior year's tax (110% if AGI > $150,000)

### Penalty Calculation
- Based on amount underpaid and time unpaid
- IRS calculates on Form 2210
- Interest compounds daily

### Penalty Exceptions
- Casualty, disaster, or other unusual circumstance
- Retired (after age 62) or became disabled
- Reasonable cause, not willful neglect

## Adjusting Withholding

### When to Adjust Form W-4
- Marriage or divorce
- Birth or adoption of child
- Home purchase
- Second job or side income
- Large refund or amount owed previous year

### Using Form W-4
- Step 1: Personal information
- Step 2: Multiple jobs or working spouse
- Step 3: Claim dependents
- Step 4: Other adjustments
- Step 5: Sign

## Self-Employment Tax

Self-employed individuals must pay:
- Income tax (via estimated payments)
- Self-employment tax (Social Security + Medicare)

### SE Tax Rate (2025)
- Social Security: 12.4% on first $176,100
- Medicare: 2.9% on all earnings
- Additional Medicare: 0.9% on earnings over $200,000 (single)

**Deduction:** Half of SE tax is deductible on Schedule 1.

## Farmers and Fishermen

Special rules apply:
- Only one estimated payment (January 15) if:
  - Farming/fishing is primary income source, AND
  - Filed prior year return by March 1
- Lower safe harbor: 66.67% of current year tax

## Higher Income Earners

If AGI exceeded $150,000 on prior year return:
- Must pay 110% of prior year tax for safe harbor
- Applies to both MFJ and MFS
