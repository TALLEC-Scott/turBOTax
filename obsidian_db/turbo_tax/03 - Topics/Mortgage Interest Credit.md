# Mortgage Interest Credit

## Overview

The mortgage interest credit helps lower-income individuals afford home ownership. If you qualify, you can claim a credit for part of the home mortgage interest you pay.

## Qualification Requirements

You may be eligible for the credit if you were issued a **Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC)** from your state or local government.

### Key Points About MCCs

- Generally issued only in connection with a **new mortgage** for purchasing your main home
- Shows the **certificate credit rate** you use to figure your credit
- Shows the **certified indebtedness amount** (only interest on this amount qualifies for the credit)
- You must apply through your state or local housing finance agency **before** getting a mortgage

## How to Claim the Credit

Complete [[Form 8396]] and attach it to your Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR.

## Figuring the Credit

### If Mortgage ≤ Certified Indebtedness

Enter all interest paid on your mortgage during the year on Form 8396, line 1.

### If Mortgage > Certified Indebtedness

Multiply total interest paid by:

```
Certified indebtedness amount on MCC
───────────────────────────────────── = Fraction
Original amount of your mortgage
```

**Example**:
- Mortgage: $125,000
- Certified indebtedness: $100,000
- Interest paid: $7,500
- Fraction: $100,000 ÷ $125,000 = 80%
- Enter on line 1: $7,500 × 0.80 = $6,000

## Credit Limits

### Limit Based on Credit Rate

If the certificate credit rate is **higher than 20%**, your credit cannot exceed **$2,000**.

### Limit Based on Tax

Your credit generally cannot exceed your tax liability. Use the Credit Limit Worksheet in Form 8396 instructions.

## Dividing the Credit

If two or more persons (other than a married couple filing jointly) hold an interest in the home with an MCC:

- The credit must be divided based on ownership interest
- The $2,000 limit is also divided proportionally

**Example**: You own 60%, sibling owns 40%. The $2,000 limit is split: $1,200 for you, $800 for sibling.

## Carryforward

If your credit is reduced due to the tax limit, you can carry forward the unused portion for **up to 3 years** or until used.

**Exception**: Amounts over the $2,000 rate-based limit cannot be carried forward.

## Reducing Mortgage Interest Deduction

If you itemize deductions, you must reduce your [[Home Mortgage Interest]] deduction by the amount of the mortgage interest credit claimed on Form 8396, line 3.

## Refinancing

If you refinance, you must get a **new MCC** to claim the credit on the new loan.

| If new mortgage is... | Then interest claimed on Form 8396, line 1 is... |
|-----------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| ≤ certified indebtedness on new MCC | All interest paid on new mortgage |
| > certified indebtedness on new MCC | Interest × (certified amount ÷ original mortgage) |

### Key Refinancing Rules

- The new MCC cannot increase your credit beyond what the old MCC would have provided
- In the year of refinancing, add applicable interest from both old and new mortgages
- If credit rates differ, attach a statement with separate calculations

## Selling Your Home

If you purchase a home using an MCC and sell within **9 years**, you may have to **recapture** (repay) part of the benefit received.

See [[Sale of Home]] and [[Pub 523 - Selling Your Home]] for details.
