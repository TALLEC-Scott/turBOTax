# Retirement Plan Distributions

## Overview

Retirement plan distributions are payments you receive from qualified retirement plans such as 401(k)s, pensions, profit-sharing plans, and IRAs. The tax treatment depends on the type of distribution, your age, and whether you roll over the distribution to another qualified plan.

## Types of Distributions

### Periodic Payments
- Regular payments over time (annuity)
- May be fully or partly taxable depending on your investment in the contract
- Reported on Form 1040 lines 5a and 5b

### Nonperiodic Payments
- Lump-sum distributions
- Partial withdrawals
- May be eligible for rollover treatment
- Reported on Form 1040 lines 5a and 5b

### Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)
- Must begin after age 73
- Failure to take RMDs results in 25% excise tax
- See [[Required Minimum Distributions]] for details

| Plan Type | Description |
|-----------|-------------|

| [[IRAs]] | Individual retirement arrangements |

## Taxation Rules

### Qualified Plans
- Distributions are taxed as ordinary income
- Pre-tax contributions and earnings are taxable
- After-tax contributions are recovered tax-free

### Nonqualified Plans
- Earnings are taxed first (taxable)
- Then return of investment (tax-free)
- May be subject to Net Investment Income Tax

### Designated Roth Accounts
- Qualified distributions are tax-free
- Must be made after 5-year period and after age 59½, death, or disability
- See [[Roth Contributions]] for details

## Reporting Distributions

### Form 1099-R
Reports all distributions from retirement plans:
- Box 1: Gross distribution
- Box 2a: Taxable amount
- Box 4: Federal tax withheld
- Box 7: Distribution code

### Distribution Codes
| Code | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| 1 | Early distribution, no known exception |
| 2 | Early distribution, exception applies |
| 3 | Disability |
| 4 | Death |
| 7 | Normal distribution |

## Key Rules

1. **Distributions are generally taxed as ordinary income**
2. **Early distributions before age 59½ may be subject to 10% penalty**
3. **Rollovers must be completed within 60 days**
4. **Direct rollovers avoid mandatory 20% withholding**
5. **RMDs must begin by April 1 after the year you turn 73**
