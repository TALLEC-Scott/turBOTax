# Education Savings Bond Program

## Overview

The Education Savings Bond Program allows you to exclude from income some or all of the interest earned on qualified U.S. savings bonds when used to pay qualified education expenses.

**Authority:** [[Pub 970 - Tax Benefits for Education#Chapter 9]]

## Qualified U.S. Savings Bonds

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Bond Type | Series EE (issued after 1989) or Series I |
| Ownership | Must be in your name (sole owner) or jointly with spouse |
| Age Requirement | Owner must be at least 24 years old before issue date |
| Issue Date | EE bonds: after 1989 |

## Eligibility Requirements

To exclude interest, you must meet ALL of the following:

1. **Pay qualified education expenses** for yourself, spouse, or dependent
2. **MAGI below limit** - $114,500 ($179,250 if MFJ) for 2025
3. **Filing status** - Cannot be married filing separately

## Income Limits (2025)

| Filing Status | Phase-Out Begins | Phase-Out Ends |
|---------------|------------------|----------------|
| Single, HoH, QSS | $99,500 | $114,500 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $149,250 | $179,250 |

## Qualified Education Expenses

1. Tuition and fees (required for enrollment)
   - Does NOT include room and board
   - Does NOT include courses for sports, games, hobbies (unless degree program)

2. Contributions to [[Qualified Tuition Program]]

3. Contributions to [[Coverdell ESA]]

## Adjusted Qualified Education Expenses (AQEE)

Must reduce qualified expenses by:

1. Tax-free scholarships and fellowship grants
2. Distributions from Coverdell ESA used tax-free
3. Distributions from QTP used tax-free
4. Veterans' educational assistance
5. Qualified tuition reductions
6. Employer-provided educational assistance
7. Expenses used for education credits

## Calculating the Exclusion

If total bond proceeds ≤ AQEE: All interest is tax-free

If total bond proceeds > AQEE: Only part of interest is tax-free

**Formula:**
```
Tax-Free Interest = (AQEE ÷ Total Proceeds) × Total Interest
```

### Example
- Bond proceeds: $9,000 (Principal: $6,000, Interest: $3,000)
- AQEE: $7,650
- Tax-Free Interest: ($7,650 ÷ $9,000) × $3,000 = $2,550
- Taxable Interest: $450

## Form Required

- [[Form 8815]] - Exclusion of Interest From Series EE and I U.S. Savings Bonds
- [[Schedule B (Form 1040)]] - Report exclusion on Line 3
- Attach Form 8815 to return

## Coordination with Other Benefits

Cannot use same expenses for:
- Education credits (American Opportunity or Lifetime Learning)
- Coverdell ESA distributions
- QTP distributions

Consider which benefit provides greatest tax savings.

## Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI)

MAGI is AGI with add-backs for:
1. Foreign earned income exclusion
2. Foreign housing exclusion/deduction
3. American Samoa income exclusion
4. Puerto Rico income exclusion
5. Adoption benefits exclusion
6. Student loan interest deduction

## Common Questions

### Q: Can I buy bonds in my child's name?
**A:** No, the bond must be issued in your name (or jointly with spouse) to qualify for the exclusion. The owner must be at least 24 before the issue date.

### Q: What if I cash bonds for expenses not related to education?
**A:** The interest is fully taxable. The exclusion only applies when proceeds are used for qualified education expenses.

### Q: Can I claim both the exclusion and an education credit?
**A:** Not for the same expenses. You must allocate expenses between benefits.
