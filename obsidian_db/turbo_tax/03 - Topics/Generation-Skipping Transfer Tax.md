# Generation-Skipping Transfer Tax

## Overview

The Generation-Skipping Transfer (GST) tax is an additional tax on transfers to "skip persons" – individuals two or more generations below the transferor. The tax prevents avoidance of estate tax by skipping a generation.

## What is a Skip Person

### Definition
A skip person is someone at least two generations below the transferor:
- Grandchildren
- Great-grandchildren
- More remote descendants
- Certain trusts benefiting skip persons

### Assigning Generations
- Lineal descendants: Based on family tree
- Non-lineal persons: Use younger of donor or donor's spouse as reference
- Spouse of lineal descendant: Same generation as descendant

## GST Exemption

### Amount
- Same as basic exclusion amount for estate/gift tax
- For 2025: $13,990,000
- Inflation-adjusted annually

### Allocation
- Allocate exemption to specific transfers
- Once allocated, cannot be changed
- Use Form 709 for lifetime gifts
- Use Form 706 for transfers at death

## Types of GST Transfers

### 1. Direct Skip
A transfer that:
- Is subject to gift or estate tax
- Is made to a skip person
- Skips the intervening generation entirely

**Examples**:
- Gift to grandchild during life
- Bequest to grandchild at death
- Payment directly to skip person

### 2. Taxable Distribution
A distribution from a trust to a skip person:
- Not a direct skip (trust is involved)
- Taxed to the recipient
- Distribution amount reduced by GST exemption allocation

### 3. Taxable Termination
Occurs when:
- A trust interest terminates
- Next interest passes to skip person
- No non-skip person has an interest after termination

**Example**: Trust pays income to child for life, then remainder to grandchild. Taxable termination occurs when child dies.

## Tax Rate

- Flat rate equal to maximum estate tax rate
- Currently 40%
- Applied to amount transferred after exemption

## Calculating GST Tax

### Inclusion Ratio
Determines how much GST exemption applies:
- Inclusion ratio = 1 if no exemption allocated
- Inclusion ratio = 0 if fully exempt
- Partial exemption: 0 < inclusion ratio < 1

### Tax Calculation
```
GST Tax = Transfer Amount × Inclusion Ratio × GST Tax Rate
```

## Exceptions

### De Minimis Exception
No GST tax if:
- Trust would have qualified for gift tax annual exclusion
- Only distributions of present interests allowed

### Educational and Medical Exclusions
Same exclusions as gift tax:
- Tuition paid directly to institution
- Medical expenses paid directly to provider

### Beneficiary Deemed Owner
No GST tax if:
- Beneficiary is treated as owner of trust
- Distribution is from beneficiary's own trust

## Reporting Requirements

### Forms

| Form | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Form 709 | Report lifetime GST transfers |
| Form 706 | Report GST transfers at death |
| Form 706-GS(D) | Taxable distributions to skip persons |
| Form 706-GS(D-1) | Notification of distribution from GST trust |
| Form 706-GS(T) | Taxable terminations |

### Due Dates
- Generally April 15 of year after transfer
- Extensions available with Form 4868 or Form 8892

## Planning Considerations

### Maximizing Exemption
- Allocate exemption strategically
- Consider appreciation potential
- Use exemption on high-value transfers first

### Dynasty Trusts
- Long-term trusts designed to benefit multiple generations
- GST exemption can make trust GST-tax-free
- State perpetuity laws affect duration

### Grandchild Exclusions
- Use gift tax annual exclusion for grandchildren
- Combined with GST exemption for larger transfers
- Education and medical exclusions are GST-free

## Coordination with Gift/Estate Tax

### Unified System
GST tax works alongside:
- Gift tax on lifetime transfers
- Estate tax on transfers at death

### Example
Transfer $100,000 to grandchild:
1. May be subject to gift tax
2. May use gift tax annual exclusion
3. May use GST exemption
4. GST tax applies if not exempt
