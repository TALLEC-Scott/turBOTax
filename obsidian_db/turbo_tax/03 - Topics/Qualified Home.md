# Qualified Home

## Overview

A qualified home is your main home or second home. For home mortgage interest to be deductible, your debt must be secured by a qualified home.

## What Counts as a Home

A home includes:
- House
- Condominium
- Cooperative
- Mobile home
- House trailer
- Boat
- Similar property with **sleeping, cooking, and toilet facilities**

## Main Home

You can have only **one main home** at any time. This is where you ordinarily live most of the time.

## Second Home

A second home is any home you choose to treat as your second home.

### Second Home Not Rented Out

If you don't hold your second home for rent or resale at any time during the year, you can treat it as a qualified home. You don't have to use the home during the year.

### Second Home Rented Out

If you rent out your second home part of the year, you must also use it as a home for it to qualify. You must use it:

- **More than 14 days**, OR
- **More than 10%** of the days rented at fair rental

Use the **longer** of these two periods.

If you don't meet this requirement, the property is considered rental property, not a second home. See [[Pub 527 - Residential Rental Property]].

### More Than One Second Home

If you have more than one second home, you can treat **only one** as the qualified second home during any year. However, you can change which home is treated as your second home:

- When you buy a new home during the year
- When your main home no longer qualifies as your main home
- When your second home is sold or becomes your main home

## Special Situations

### Divided Use of Your Home

If you use part of your home for non-residential purposes (like a home office), only the residential living portion is a qualified home.

You must:
- Divide both the cost and fair market value between qualified and non-qualified portions
- This may affect your [[Home Acquisition Debt]] limit

### Renting Out Part of Your Home

You can treat rented part as used by you for residential living if ALL apply:

- Tenant uses the rented part primarily for residential living
- Rented part isn't a self-contained unit with separate sleeping, cooking, and toilet facilities
- You don't rent to more than two tenants at any time during the year

### Home Under Construction

You can treat a home under construction as a qualified home for up to **24 months** if it becomes your qualified home when ready for occupancy.

The 24-month period can start any time on or after construction begins.

### Home Destroyed

You can continue treating a destroyed home as a qualified home if, within a reasonable time, you:

- Rebuild the home and move into it, OR
- Sell the land where the home was located

### Time-Sharing Arrangements

You can treat a home owned under a time-sharing plan as a qualified home if it meets all requirements.

**Rental of time-share**: Qualifies as a second home only if you also use it as a home during the year. Count use and rental days only during the time you have a right to use it.

## Married Taxpayers

### Joint Return

Your qualified home(s) can be owned either jointly or by only one spouse.

### Separate Returns

If married filing separately and you own more than one home:
- Each spouse can take into account only one home as a qualified home
- If both consent in writing, one spouse can take both the main home and a second home
