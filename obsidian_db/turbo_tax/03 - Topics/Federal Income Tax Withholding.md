# Federal Income Tax Withholding

## Overview

Employers must withhold federal income tax from employees' wages based on the information provided on Form W-4. The withheld amounts are trust fund taxes that must be deposited with the U.S. Treasury.

## How Withholding Works

### Form W-4 Basis
Withholding is calculated based on:
- Filing status (Step 1)
- Multiple jobs or working spouse (Step 2)
- Dependents claimed (Step 3)
- Other income and deductions (Step 4)

### Withholding Methods
Two primary methods from [[Pub 15-T]]:
1. **Percentage Method** - Most commonly used for payroll software
2. **Wage Bracket Method** - Tables based on wage ranges and pay periods

## Withholding Requirements

### What Must Be Withheld
- Regular wages (salaries, hourly pay)
- Supplemental wages (bonuses, commissions)
- Supplemental unemployment benefits
- Certain fringe benefits (when paid in cash)

### Payroll Periods

| Period | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Daily | Payment each day |
| Weekly | Payment each week |
| Biweekly | Payment every 2 weeks |
| Semimonthly | Payment twice monthly |
| Monthly | Payment once monthly |
| Quarterly | Payment once per quarter |
| Semiannual | Payment twice yearly |
| Annual | Payment once yearly |
| Miscellaneous | No regular schedule |

## Special Withholding Situations

### Nonresident Alien Employees
- Add specific amount to wages before calculating withholding
- Use special tables in [[Pub 15-T]]
- Use Form W-4 but cannot claim "Exempt" status

### Exempt Employees
- Employee claims exemption on Form W-4
- **Expires February 15** each year
- Must file new Form W-4 annually by February 15
- Withhold as "Single" if no new form received

### Multiple Jobs
- Employee should check Step 2(c) on Form W-4, OR
- Use Step 2(b) worksheet, OR
- Use IRS Tax Withholding Estimator

## Form W-4 Processing

### New Employees
- Must complete Form W-4 by first payday
- If no form submitted, withhold as "Single" with no adjustments

### Form Changes
- Implement new W-4 by the start of the **first payroll period** ending on or after the 30th day from receipt
- Keep forms in records for 4 years

### Invalid Forms
- If form is invalid, continue withholding based on prior valid form
- Notify employee to submit valid form

## Supplemental Wage Withholding

Supplemental wages include bonuses, commissions, overtime, etc.

| Scenario | Withholding Rate |
|----------|------------------|
| Regular wages paid separately | 22% flat rate |
| Regular and supplemental combined | Use aggregate method |
| Supplemental wages exceed $1 million/year | 37% rate |

## 2026 Withholding Changes

### Qualified Tips Deduction
- Employees may deduct up to $25,000 of qualified tips
- Use updated Form W-4 to account for expected deduction
- Deduction applies for tax years 2025-2029

### Qualified Overtime Deduction
- Employees may deduct up to $12,500 ($25,000 MFJ) of qualified overtime
- Use updated Form W-4 to account for expected deduction
- Deduction applies for tax years 2025-2029

## Backup Withholding

### Rate: 24%
Applies when payee:
- Fails to provide correct TIN
- Certifies TIN is incorrect
- Fails to certify they're not subject to backup withholding

### Exempt Payments
Backup withholding does NOT apply to:
- Wages (regular income tax withholding applies)
- Pensions and annuities
- IRAs (including SEP and SIMPLE)
- HSA distributions
- Real estate transactions
