# Recourse vs Nonrecourse Debt

## Overview

The distinction between recourse and nonrecourse debt is critical in determining tax consequences when property is foreclosed, repossessed, or abandoned. The classification affects how gain, loss, and cancellation of debt income are calculated.

## Definitions

### Recourse Debt

A debt for which you are **personally liable**. If the collateral does not satisfy the debt, the lender can pursue your other assets to collect the balance.

### Nonrecourse Debt

A debt for which you are **not personally liable**. The lender's only remedy is to take the collateral property; they cannot pursue your other assets if the debt exceeds the property value.

## Impact on Foreclosures and Repossessions

### Recourse Debt Foreclosure

**Amount Realized** = The smaller of:
1. Outstanding debt minus any remaining personal liability, OR
2. FMV of the transferred property

**Cancellation of Debt Income** = If FMV is less than the debt (minus remaining liability), the difference is COD income

**Gain/Loss** = Amount realized minus adjusted basis

### Nonrecourse Debt Foreclosure

**Amount Realized** = The entire outstanding debt immediately before transfer
- This applies even if FMV is less than the debt

**No COD Income** = There is no separate cancellation of debt income

**Gain/Loss** = Outstanding debt minus adjusted basis

## Impact on Abandonments

### Recourse Debt Abandonment

- **No gain or loss** until the later foreclosure is completed
- Must wait for foreclosure to calculate any gain, loss, or COD income

### Nonrecourse Debt Abandonment

- **Treated as a sale or exchange** immediately upon abandonment
- Amount realized = the nonrecourse debt amount
- Calculate gain or loss by comparing debt to adjusted basis
- No separate COD income

## Examples

### Example 1: Recourse Debt Foreclosure

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Outstanding debt | $180,000 |
| FMV of property | $170,000 |
| Adjusted basis | $175,000 |
| Remaining liability after foreclosure | $8,000 |

- Amount realized: $170,000 (smaller of $172,000 or $170,000)
- COD income: $2,000 ($172,000 - $170,000)
- Loss: $5,000 nondeductible (personal-use property)

### Example 2: Nonrecourse Debt Foreclosure

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Outstanding debt | $180,000 |
| FMV of property | $170,000 |
| Adjusted basis | $175,000 |

- Amount realized: $180,000 (full debt)
- Gain: $5,000 ($180,000 - $175,000)
- No separate COD income

## Tax Reporting

### Recourse Debt
- Report COD income on Schedule 1 (Form 1040), line 8c (unless exclusion applies)
- Report gain/loss on Form 8949 and Schedule D

### Nonrecourse Debt
- Report the transaction as a sale
- No separate COD income to report
- Report gain/loss on Form 8949 and Schedule D
