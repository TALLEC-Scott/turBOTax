# Residential Energy Credits

## Overview

Residential energy credits provide tax incentives for making energy-saving improvements to your home. These credits are claimed on [[Form 5695]] and have specific rules for qualifying property and credit amounts.

## Important: Expiration After 2025

**Caution**: You cannot claim these credits for expenditures made after December 31, 2025:
- Residential clean energy credits
- Energy efficient home improvement credits

## Types of Credits

### Residential Clean Energy Credit

For property placed in service 2022-2025:
- **Credit rate**: 30%
- **No lifetime limit**

#### Qualifying Property
- Solar electric panels
- Solar water heaters
- Fuel cells
- Small wind energy property
- Geothermal heat pumps
- **Battery storage technology** (added for 2022-2025)

### Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit

For property placed in service through December 31, 2025:
- **No lifetime limit** on credit amount
- Divided into two sections:
  1. Qualified energy efficiency improvements
  2. Residential energy property expenditures

#### Qualified Energy Efficiency Improvements
- Insulation materials
- Exterior windows and skylights
- Exterior doors
- Metal or asphalt roofs

#### Residential Energy Property Expenditures
- Energy-efficient heating and cooling equipment
- Water heaters
- Biomass stoves

## Home Energy Audits (Beginning 2024)

Home energy audits must be performed by a **Qualified Home Energy Auditor** or under their supervision. Requirements:
- Auditor certified by a Qualified Certification Program
- Must provide written report identifying:
  - Most significant and cost-effective improvements
  - Estimated energy and cost savings for each improvement

## Claiming the Credit

### How to Claim

1. Complete [[Form 5695]]
2. Attach to your Form 1040
3. Maintain documentation of qualifying improvements

### Documentation Required

- Manufacturer's certification statement
- Receipts and invoices
- Home energy audit report (if applicable)

## Basis Reduction

**Important**: If you claim a residential energy credit, you must **reduce the basis of your home** by the credit amount.

This applies to both:
- Residential clean energy credits
- Energy efficient home improvement credits

## Limits and Caps

The energy efficient home improvement credit has annual caps for different categories of improvements. See Form 5695 instructions for current limits.

## Eligible Homes

The home must be located in the **United States** and can include:
- House
- Condominium
- Cooperative apartment
- Mobile home
- Houseboat

The home must have sleeping, cooking, and toilet facilities.

## Former Names

These credits were previously known as:
- **Residential energy efficient property credit** → Now "residential clean energy credit"
- **Nonbusiness energy property credit** → Now "energy efficient home improvement credit"
