# Qualified Joint Venture

## Overview

A Qualified Joint Venture (QJV) is an election available to married couples who jointly own and operate an unincorporated business. Per [[Pub 334 - Tax Guide for Small Business]], this election allows spouses to avoid partnership filing requirements while each receiving Social Security credit.

## Eligibility Requirements

To qualify as a QJV, ALL of the following must be met:

1. **Married Filing Jointly** - File a joint return for the tax year
2. **Only Members Are Spouses** - No other owners
3. **Both Materially Participate** - Each spouse must materially participate in the business
4. **Not in State Law Entity** - Cannot be operated through an LLC or other state law entity
5. **Co-Owners** - Own and operate as co-owners

## Material Participation

Each spouse must materially participate in the business. Per Schedule C instructions:

**Material participation means:**
1. Participate more than 500 hours during the year, OR
2. Participation is substantially all participation in the activity, OR
3. Participate more than 100 hours and more than anyone else, OR
4. Participate in any significant participating activity for more than 100 hours, OR
5. Materially participated in 5 of the 10 preceding years, OR
6. Personal services activity and participated more than 100 hours for 3 consecutive years, OR
7. Participated on a regular, continuous, and substantial basis

## How to Make the Election

To elect QJV treatment:

1. **Divide all business items** between spouses according to respective ownership interests
2. **Each spouse files:**
   - Separate [[Schedule C (Form 1040)]]
   - Separate [[Schedule SE (Form 1040)]]
3. **Do NOT file:**
   - [[Form 1065]] (Partnership return)
   - Schedule K-1

No formal statement or specific form is required to make the election.

## Why Elect QJV Status?

### Advantages

| Advantage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Simpler filing | No Form 1065 or K-1s required |
| Social Security credits | Each spouse earns SS credits on their share |
| Single tax return | Still file joint Form 1040 |
| No partnership rules | Avoid complex partnership accounting |
| Self-employment income | Each spouse reports SE income separately |

### Comparison: QJV vs. Partnership

| Factor | QJV | Partnership |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| Form 1065 required | No | Yes |
| Schedule K-1 | No | Yes |
| Schedule C (each spouse) | Yes | No |
| Social Security credit | Each spouse | Only partners with SE income |
| Filing complexity | Simpler | More complex |

## Who Cannot Elect QJV

### LLCs
A business owned by spouses through an LLC does **NOT qualify** for QJV election. Must file as:
- Partnership (Form 1065) if multi-member
- Sole proprietorship (Schedule C) if single-member (one spouse only)
- Elect corporate treatment

### State Law Entities
Any business operated through a state law entity (other than general partnership) cannot elect QJV.

### Non-Spouse Owners
If anyone other than the spouses owns any interest in the business, QJV is not available.

## Division of Income and Expenses

All business items must be divided according to ownership interests:
- Gross receipts
- Cost of goods sold
- Business expenses
- Assets
- Liabilities

**Example:** If spouses own 50/50:
- Each reports 50% of gross income
- Each deducts 50% of expenses
- Each claims 50% of depreciation

## Self-Employment Tax Benefit

The QJV election ensures each spouse:
- Reports their share of net earnings
- Pays SE tax on their share
- Earns Social Security credits

**Without QJV:** If treated as sole proprietorship with one spouse as owner, only that spouse gets Social Security credit.

## Community Property States

In community property states (AZ, CA, ID, LA, NV, NM, TX, WA, WI):
- Spouses may treat community business as either sole proprietorship or partnership
- QJV election still available if requirements met
- See [[Pub 555 - Community Property]] for special rules

## Alternative: Spousal Partnership

If QJV requirements are not met, spouses in business together must file as a partnership:
- File Form 1065
- Issue Schedule K-1 to each spouse
- Each spouse reports K-1 income on Schedule E
- Both may have self-employment income

## Alternative: Sole Proprietorship

One spouse may be treated as sole proprietor if:
- Only one spouse operates the business
- Other spouse is not materially involved
- Report on single Schedule C

**Disadvantage:** Only the operating spouse earns Social Security credits.

## Reporting Example

**Scenario:** Married couple operates consulting business 50/50
- Gross income: $100,000
- Expenses: $40,000
- Net profit: $60,000

**QJV Reporting:**
| Spouse | Schedule C Net Profit | SE Tax |
|--------|----------------------|--------|
| Spouse A | $30,000 | $4,590 |
| Spouse B | $30,000 | $4,590 |

Each spouse earns Social Security credits on $30,000.

## Common Questions

### Q: Do we need to file anything to elect QJV?
**A:** No formal election statement is required. Simply file separate Schedule C and Schedule SE forms for each spouse with your joint Form 1040.

### Q: What if we live in a community property state?
**A:** Community property rules may affect how you divide income. Consult [[Pub 555 - Community Property]] and consider whether QJV or another approach is better.

### Q: Can we change from partnership to QJV?
**A:** Yes, if you meet the requirements. This is treated as a partnership termination. No longer have to file Form 1065.
