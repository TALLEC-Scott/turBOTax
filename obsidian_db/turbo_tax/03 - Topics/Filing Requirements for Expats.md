# Filing Requirements for Expats

## Overview

U.S. citizens and resident aliens living abroad must generally follow the same filing requirements as those living in the United States. However, there are special provisions for extensions and filing locations.

**Key Principle:** Your worldwide income is subject to U.S. taxation regardless of where you live. You must file if your gross income meets the threshold for your filing status.

## Who Must File

### Income Thresholds
You must file a U.S. income tax return if your worldwide gross income is at least the amount shown for your filing status in the Form 1040 instructions.

**Gross Income Includes:**
- All income from U.S. and foreign sources
- Income you exclude under [[Foreign Earned Income Exclusion]]
- Income you exclude under [[Foreign Housing Exclusion and Deduction]]

### Self-Employed Individuals
Must file if net earnings from self-employment are **$400 or more**.

**Note:** SE tax applies even if you exclude all income under FEIE. See [[Self-Employment Tax Abroad]].

### Special Filing Requirements
- **Form 8898:** If you begin or end bona fide residence in a U.S. territory
- **FBAR (FinCEN Form 114):** If foreign financial accounts exceed $10,000 at any time during the year
- **Form 8938:** If specified foreign financial assets exceed thresholds
- **Form 5471:** If you own interests in foreign corporations
- **Form 8865:** If you own interests in foreign partnerships

## Filing Deadlines

### Regular Due Date
**April 15** for calendar year taxpayers.

### Extended Due Date (Automatic 2-Month Extension)
**June 15** if on April 15 you are:
- Living outside the U.S. and Puerto Rico, AND
- Your main place of business or post of duty is outside the U.S., OR
- You are in military or naval service on duty outside the U.S.

**Caution:** Interest still accrues from April 15 on any unpaid tax.

## Extensions

### Automatic 2-Month Extension
No form required. Attach a statement to your return explaining which situation qualified you.

### Additional 4-Month Extension (Form 4868)
Extends filing to October 15. File Form 4868 by June 15.

**Note:** This extends time to FILE, not time to PAY. Interest accrues from April 15.

### Extension for Meeting Residency Tests (Form 2350)
If you need more time to meet the [[Bona Fide Residence Test]] or [[Physical Presence Test]] for the [[Foreign Earned Income Exclusion]], file Form 2350.

**Extension granted until 30 days after you expect to qualify.**

### Discretionary 2-Month Extension
Taxpayers out of the country can request an additional 2 months (to December 15) by sending a letter to the IRS explaining the reasons.

## How to File

### E-File
- Returns with foreign addresses can be e-filed
- Use IRS-approved software
- Most tax professionals can e-file

### Mail
International returns have specific mailing addresses. See IRS.gov/Filing/International-Where-To-File-Form-1040-Addresses-For-Taxpayers-And-Tax-Professionals

### Designated Delivery Services
Returns postmarked by approved private delivery services are considered timely filed.

## Payment Options

### Electronic Payments
- Direct Pay from U.S. bank account
- Debit or credit card
- Digital wallet
- IRS Online Account

**Foreign Electronic Payments:** See IRS.gov/Individuals/International-Taxpayers/Foreign-Electronic-Payments-Tax-Type-Codes

### Check or Money Order
Payable to "United States Treasury"

## Estimated Tax Payments

### Who Must Pay
You must pay estimated tax if you expect to owe at least $1,000 in tax after withholding and credits.

### Due Dates
| Quarter | Due Date |
|---------|----------|
| 1 (Jan-Mar) | April 15 |
| 2 (Apr-Jun) | June 15 |
| 3 (Jul-Sep) | September 15 |
| 4 (Oct-Dec) | January 15 (next year) |

**Note:** If you qualify for the 2-month extension, your Q1 and Q2 payments are due June 15.

### Safe Harbor
No penalty if you pay:
- 90% of current year tax, OR
- 100% of prior year tax (110% if AGI exceeds $150,000)

## Foreign Currency

You must report amounts in U.S. dollars. Translate foreign currency at the exchange rate on the date of receipt, or use yearly average rates.

**Resources:**
- IRS.gov/Individuals/International-Taxpayers/Foreign-Currency-And-Currency-Exchange-Rates
- IRS.gov/Individuals/International-Taxpayers/Yearly-Average-Currency-Exchange-Rates

## U.S. Territories

### U.S. Virgin Islands (USVI)
- **Bona fide residents:** File with USVI only (not U.S.)
- **Non-residents with USVI income:** File with both U.S. and USVI

### Guam, CNMI
- **Bona fide residents:** File with territory
- **Non-residents with territory income:** File with U.S.

### Puerto Rico, American Samoa
- Separate tax systems
- See Pub 570 for details

## Nonresident Alien Spouse

You may elect to treat a nonresident alien spouse as a U.S. resident by:
1. Filing a joint return
2. Attaching a signed statement with required information

**Consequences:**
- Must report worldwide income for both spouses
- Election remains in effect until suspended or terminated

## Withholding Adjustments

If you expect to qualify for the [[Foreign Earned Income Exclusion]], you may reduce or eliminate withholding by:
- Providing Form 673 to your employer
- Adjusting Form W-4

**Caution:** If you don't qualify for FEIE, you may owe substantial tax plus penalties.

## Penalties and Interest

### Late Filing Penalty
5% of unpaid tax per month, up to 25%

### Late Payment Penalty
0.5% of unpaid tax per month, up to 25%

### Interest
Accrues from due date until paid. Interest compounds daily.

### Reasonable Cause
Penalties may be waived if you can show reasonable cause for late filing/payment.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Missing the April 15 interest start date** even with extension
2. **Not filing FBAR** for foreign accounts
3. **Assuming foreign income is exempt** from filing requirements
4. **Not making estimated tax payments**
5. **Filing late without requesting extension**
