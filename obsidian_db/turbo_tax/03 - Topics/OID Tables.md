# OID Tables

## Overview

The OID Tables are annual IRS publications listing publicly offered Original Issue Discount debt instruments with their key characteristics and calculated OID amounts. Available at IRS.gov/Pub1212.

## Purpose

1. Help brokers and middlemen prepare information returns (Forms 1099-OID, 1099-INT)
2. Provide OID amounts for owners of publicly offered OID instruments
3. Identify which debt instruments have OID

## Table Structure

### Section I - Long-Term Debt Instruments

#### Section I-A: Corporate Debt Instruments Issued Before 1985
- Historical instruments still outstanding
- Uses annual accrual periods

#### Section I-B: Corporate Debt Instruments Issued After 1984
- Most commonly referenced section
- Uses 6-month accrual periods
- Includes YTM for bonds issued after December 31, 2006

#### Section I-C: Inflation-Indexed Debt Instruments
- TIPS and similar instruments
- Adjusted for inflation

### Section II - Stripped Coupons and Principal
- U.S. Treasury STRIPS program components
- Government-Sponsored Enterprise stripped components
- Arranged by maturity date

### Section III - Short-Term Discount Obligations
- Section III-A: Short-Term U.S. Treasury Bills
- Section III-B: Federal Home Loan Banks
- Section III-C: Federal National Mortgage Association (Fannie Mae)
- Section III-D: Federal Farm Credit Banks
- Section III-E: Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation (Freddie Mac)
- Section III-F: Federal Agricultural Mortgage Corporation

## Information Provided per Instrument

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Issuer Name | Entity issuing the debt |
| CUSIP Number | Unique security identifier |
| Issue Date | Original issue date |
| Maturity Date | When principal is repaid |
| Issue Price | As percent of principal |
| Coupon Rate | Annual stated interest rate |
| YTM | Yield to maturity (post-2006 issues) |
| Total OID to January 1 | Cumulative OID accrued |
| Daily OID | Per accrual period |
| Annual OID | Per $1,000 of principal |

## Using the Tables

### For Full-Year Holdings
Use the annual OID shown in the tables, adjusted for principal amount.

### For Partial-Year Holdings
1. Look up daily OID for each accrual period
2. Multiply by days held in each period
3. Sum the results
4. Adjust for actual principal amount

## Important Limitations

### Should NOT Use Tables For:
- Determining exact OID on your tax return (use Form 1099-OID as starting point)
- Instruments not publicly offered
- Foreign obligations not traded in U.S.

### Tax Matters Subject to Change:
- OID reported on income tax returns
- Issuer's classification as debt
- Adjusted basis of debt instrument

## Debt Instruments NOT in OID Tables

- U.S. savings bonds
- Certificates of deposit
- Tax-exempt organization obligations
- Matured or called instruments
- Mortgage-backed securities
- REMIC regular interests and CDOs
- Commercial paper
- Foreign obligations not traded in U.S.
