# Real Estate Taxes

## Overview

Real estate taxes (also called property taxes) are annual taxes charged by state and local governments on the value of real property. These taxes are deductible on your federal income tax return if you itemize deductions, subject to the SALT deduction limit.

## Deductibility Rules

### Requirements for Deduction

To deduct real estate taxes, the tax must be:
- **Imposed on you** - You must be legally liable for the tax
- **Actually paid** - Paid to the taxing authority during the tax year
- **Uniformly assessed** - Assessed at a like rate on all real property throughout the community
- **For general purposes** - Used for general community/governmental purposes, not special privileges

### Where to Deduct

Report deductible real estate taxes on [[Schedule A (Form 1040)]], line 5b.

## Special Situations

### Taxes Paid at Settlement or Closing

Real estate taxes are divided between buyer and seller based on the ownership period:
- **Seller** pays taxes up to (but not including) the date of sale
- **Buyer** pays taxes from the date of sale forward

This division applies regardless of who actually paid the bill. Each party can deduct their respective share.

### Division Example

| Step | Calculation |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | Enter total real estate taxes for the year |
| 2 | Enter days you owned the property |
| 3 | Divide line 2 by 365 |
| 4 | Multiply line 1 by line 3 = Your deduction |

### Delinquent Taxes

If you agree to pay the seller's delinquent taxes when buying a home, you **cannot deduct them**. Instead, add them to the [[Basis of Home]].

### Escrow Accounts

You can only deduct the taxes actually **paid from escrow** to the taxing authority, not the total amount deposited into escrow.

## Nondeductible Items

The following are **not deductible** as real estate taxes:

- **Charges for services** (trash collection, water fees, lawn mowing charges)
- **Assessments for local benefits** that increase property value (streets, sidewalks, sewer systems) - add these to basis instead
- **Transfer taxes/stamp taxes** on sale of home
- **Homeowners' association assessments**
- **Foreign real estate taxes**

## SALT Deduction Limit

For 2025, the overall limit on state and local tax deductions is **$40,000** ($20,000 if married filing separately). This limit applies to the combined total of:
- State and local income or sales taxes, AND
- State and local real estate taxes

The limit is reduced if modified AGI exceeds $500,000 ($250,000 MFS) but not below $10,000 ($5,000 MFS).

See [[SALT Deduction]] for more details.

## Cooperatives

Owners of cooperative apartments have special rules:
- Deduct your share of the corporation's real estate taxes
- The cooperative housing corporation must meet specific requirements
- Your share is figured based on your stock ownership percentage

See [[Cooperative Housing]] for details.

## Refunds and Rebates

- **Refund of current year taxes**: Reduce your deduction by the refund amount
- **Refund of prior year taxes**: May need to include in income (see [[Itemized Deductions]] recoveries rules)
