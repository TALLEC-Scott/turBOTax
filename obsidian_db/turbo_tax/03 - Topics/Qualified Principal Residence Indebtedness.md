# Qualified Principal Residence Indebtedness

## Overview

Qualified principal residence indebtedness (QPRI) is mortgage debt used to buy, build, or substantially improve your main home and secured by that home. Taxpayers may be able to exclude canceled QPRI from gross income, subject to limitations.

## Definition

Qualified principal residence indebtedness is:

1. **Acquisition Debt**: Any mortgage taken out to buy, build, or substantially improve your main home
2. **Secured by Main Home**: The debt must be secured by the main home
3. **Refinanced Debt**: Debt used to refinance qualifying debt, but only up to the amount of the old mortgage principal just before refinancing

### Main Home

Your main home is the one in which you live most of the time. You can have only one main home at any one time.

## Exclusion Limit

The maximum amount treated as QPRI is **$750,000** ($375,000 if married filing separately).

## Expiration

**Important**: Qualified principal residence indebtedness cannot be excluded from income for discharges completed or discharge agreements entered into after December 31, 2025.

## Key Rules

### Ordering Rule

If only part of a loan is QPRI, the exclusion applies only to the extent the canceled amount exceeds the non-QPRI portion of the loan. The remaining canceled debt may qualify for another exclusion.

### Exclusions Must Be Applied in Order

The QPRI exclusion does NOT apply if:
- The cancellation occurred in a Title 11 bankruptcy case (use bankruptcy exclusion instead)
- You were insolvent immediately before cancellation (you may elect insolvency exclusion instead)

### Reporting Requirements

1. File [[Form 982]] with your tax return
2. Check the box on line 1e
3. Enter the excluded amount on line 2
4. Complete line 10b to reduce basis in the home if you continue to own it

## Examples

### Example 1: Qualifying Mortgage

You bought a main home for $315,000 with a $300,000 mortgage and later took out a $50,000 second mortgage to add a garage. Both loans are QPRI totaling $350,000.

### Example 2: Refinance with Cash Out

You refinance $325,000 of qualifying mortgages into a $400,000 loan, using the extra $75,000 for personal expenses. Only $325,000 qualifies as QPRI.

### Example 3: Ordering Rule

You have an $800,000 loan where only $750,000 is QPRI. If $115,000 is canceled, only the amount above the non-qualifying portion can be excluded as QPRI.

## Basis Reduction

If you exclude canceled QPRI from income and continue to own your home, you **must reduce your basis** in the home by the amount excluded. Report this on line 10b of Form 982.
