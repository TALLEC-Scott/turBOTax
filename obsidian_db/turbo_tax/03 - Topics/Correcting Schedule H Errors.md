# Correcting Schedule H Errors

## Overview

If you discover an error on a previously filed Schedule H (Form 1040), you can correct it using specific procedures. The correction method depends on whether Schedule H was attached to another form or filed by itself.

## Correction Methods

### Schedule H Attached to Another Form

| Original Filing | Correction Method |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Form 1040, 1040-SR, or 1040-NR | File Form 1040-X with corrected Schedule H |
| Form 1040-SS | File new Form 1040-SS with corrected Schedule H |
| Form 1041 | File amended Form 1041 with corrected Schedule H |

### Schedule H Filed by Itself (Stand-Alone)

1. File another stand-alone Schedule H with corrected information
2. Write "CORRECTED" in bold letters in the top margin
3. Include the date you discovered the error
4. Attach a statement explaining:
   - Reason for correction
   - Date error was discovered
5. If overpayment, write "ADJUSTED" or "REFUND" in top margin

## When to File Corrected Schedule H

### Underpayment Correction
- File by due date of next tax return (generally April 15 of following year)
- Pay underpayment when filing corrected return

### Overpayment Correction
- File within 3 years of original filing date, OR
- Within 2 years of paying the tax
- Whichever is later

**Note**: If filing within 90 days of expiration of period of limitations, you can only claim a refund (not adjust).

## Underpayment of Tax

### Requirements:
- Pay the underpayment by the time you file corrected Schedule H
- If filed on time and paid by filing, generally no interest or penalties
- FUTA underpayments are subject to interest

### Penalties Avoided by Timely Filing:
- Failure-to-pay penalty
- Estimated tax penalties

## Overpayment of Tax

### Two Options:

#### 1. Adjust the Overpayment
**Form 1040-X**: Indicate on line 23 to apply overpayment to estimated taxes
**Form 1041**: Indicate on line 30a
**Stand-Alone**: Write "ADJUSTED" in bold in top margin

**Pros/Cons**:
- No interest received on overpayment
- Applied to next year's estimated taxes
- Cannot adjust FUTA overpayments

#### 2. Claim a Refund
**Form 1040-X**: Indicate on line 22 for refund
**Form 1041**: Indicate on line 30b
**Stand-Alone**: Write "REFUND" in bold in top margin

**Pros/Cons**:
- Interest received on overpayment (except certain FUTA credits)
- Cash payment to you

## Required Repayment or Consent

### For Overreported Social Security/Medicare Taxes:

**To Adjust:**
- Must first repay or reimburse employees the overcollected amount
- Reimburse by applying against future withholding

**To Claim Refund:**
- Must repay/reimburse employees, OR
- Obtain written consents from employees

### Documentation Required:
Include statement with corrected Schedule H showing:
- Repayment/reimbursement to employees, OR
- Written consents obtained

## Form W-2 and W-2c Requirements

Whether underreported or overreported tax, you generally must file:
- Form W-2 (if none previously filed), OR
- Form W-2c (corrected Wage and Tax Statement)
- Include territorial equivalents if applicable

## Additional Medicare Tax Corrections

### Administrative Errors (Can Correct)
- Amount on Schedule H differs from amount actually withheld
- Mathematical or transposition errors
- Can correct both wages and withholding

### Non-Administrative Errors (Limited Correction)
- Can only correct wages subject to Additional Medicare Tax
- Cannot correct withholding amount

### Timing Requirements:
- **Underwithheld**: Must recover from employee by end of calendar year
- **Excess withholding**: Must repay/reimburse employee by end of calendar year
