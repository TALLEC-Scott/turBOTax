# Qualified Nonrecourse Financing

## Overview

Qualified nonrecourse financing is a special category of debt that is treated as at-risk for purposes of the [[At-Risk Rules]], even though it is nonrecourse. This exception allows real estate investors to increase their at-risk amount with certain loans.

## Definition

Qualified nonrecourse financing is financing for which:
1. **No one is personally liable** for repayment, AND
2. All of the following requirements are met:

### Requirements

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Borrowed for real property | Loan is borrowed in connection with holding real property |
| Secured by real property | Loan is secured by real property used in the activity |
| Non-convertible | Loan is not convertible from debt to ownership interest |
| Qualified lender | Loaned from or guaranteed by government, or borrowed from qualified person |

## Qualified Person

A **qualified person** is someone who:
- Actively and regularly engages in the business of lending money
- Most common example: **Banks**

### Persons Who Are NOT Qualified

1. **Related persons** (with exception for commercially reasonable terms)
   - Family members
   - Related corporations, partnerships, trusts
   - Persons under common control
   - See [[At-Risk Rules]] for full list of related persons

2. **Seller or related to seller**
   - Person from whom you acquired the property
   - Person related to that seller

3. **Fee recipient or related person**
   - Person receiving a fee due to your investment in the real property
   - Person related to that fee recipient

### Exception for Related Persons

A related person MAY be a qualified person if:
- The nonrecourse financing is **commercially reasonable**, AND
- The terms are the **same as loans involving unrelated persons**

## Security Requirements

### What Counts as Secured Only by Real Property

In determining whether qualified nonrecourse financing is secured only by real property:

1. **Disregard incidental property**
   - Property incidental to holding real property

2. **Disregard other property if value < 10%**
   - If total gross FMV of other property is less than 10% of total gross FMV of all property securing the financing

### Rules for Financing After August 3, 1998

The above security rules apply to financing incurred after August 3, 1998. You may elect to apply these rules to earlier financing, but must reduce at-risk amounts for years before August 4, 1998.

## Activities Where This Applies

Qualified nonrecourse financing is treated as at-risk for activities involving:
- **Holding real property** (other than mineral property)
- The real property must be used in the activity

### Does NOT Apply To
- Mineral property activities
- Activities not involving real property holding
- Personal property rentals

## Effect on At-Risk Amount

If you have qualified nonrecourse financing:
- The amount is **included in your at-risk amount**
- This can significantly increase your ability to deduct losses
- Losses may still be limited by passive activity rules

## Example

You purchase a rental property:
- Cash down payment: $50,000
- Qualified nonrecourse financing: $450,000

| Item | At-Risk Treatment |
|------|-------------------|
| Cash contribution | $50,000 at risk |
| Qualified nonrecourse financing | $450,000 at risk |
| **Total at risk** | **$500,000** |

Compare with regular nonrecourse:
| Item | At-Risk Treatment |
|------|-------------------|
| Cash contribution | $50,000 at risk |
| Regular nonrecourse financing | $0 at risk |
| **Total at risk** | **$50,000** |

## Partnership Interests

For purposes of these rules:
- Treat yourself as owning directly your proportional share of partnership assets
- This affects determination of whether financing is qualified nonrecourse

## Coordination with Other Rules

| Rule | Treatment of Qualified Nonrecourse |
|------|-----------------------------------|
| At-risk rules | INCLUDED in at-risk amount |
| Basis rules | INCLUDED in basis |
| Passive activity rules | Does not affect; applies separately |
