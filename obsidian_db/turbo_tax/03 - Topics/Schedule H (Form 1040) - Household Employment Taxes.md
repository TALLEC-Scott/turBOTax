# Schedule H (Form 1040) - Household Employment Taxes

## Overview

Schedule H (Form 1040) is used to report household employment taxes, including Social Security and Medicare taxes (FICA), Federal Unemployment Tax (FUTA), and withheld federal income taxes for household employees.

## When to File Schedule H

File Schedule H if you pay any of the following wages to a household employee:
- Social Security and Medicare wages of $3,000 or more (2026)
- FUTA wages (total cash wages of $1,000+ in any calendar quarter)
- Wages from which you withhold federal income tax

## Filing Requirements

### With Income Tax Return
File Schedule H with your 2026 federal income tax return (Form 1040, 1040-SR, 1040-SS, 1040-NR, or 1041) by April 15, 2027. Extensions to file your return also apply to Schedule H.

### Stand-Alone Filing
If you're not required to file a tax return, you can file Schedule H by itself. This applies to certain tax-exempt organizations (e.g., churches paying a household worker for a minister's home).

### Business Employer Option
If you own a business or farm, you may include household employment taxes with your business employment tax deposits on Form 941, 943, or 944, and FUTA on Form 940 instead of using Schedule H.

## What to Report on Schedule H

### Social Security and Medicare Taxes
- Total cash wages paid to household employees
- Employee's share of taxes (7.65%)
- Employer's share of taxes (7.65%)
- Total Social Security and Medicare taxes due

### FUTA Tax
- Total cash wages subject to FUTA
- FUTA tax due (6% of first $7,000 per employee)
- State unemployment tax credits

### Federal Income Tax Withholding
- Wages from which federal income tax was withheld
- Total federal income tax withheld

## Key Deadlines for 2026

| Deadline | Action |

| February 1, 2027 | Provide Form W-2 to employee |

## Correcting Errors on Schedule H

### Schedule H Attached to Another Form
- Filed with Form 1040/1040-SR/1040-NR: File Form 1040-X with corrected Schedule H
- Filed with Form 1040-SS: File new Form 1040-SS with corrected Schedule H
- Filed with Form 1041: File amended Form 1041 with corrected Schedule H

### Stand-Alone Schedule H
File another stand-alone Schedule H with "CORRECTED" written in bold in the top margin. Include statement explaining the correction and date error was discovered.

## Underpayment vs Overpayment

### Underpayment
- Pay by the time you file the corrected Schedule H to avoid interest and penalties
- FUTA underpayments are subject to interest

### Overpayment
- **Adjust**: Apply to estimated taxes (no interest received)
- **Refund**: Claim refund (interest received, except for certain FUTA credits)
- Must file within 3 years of original filing or 2 years from payment date

## Payment Methods

1. **With tax return**: Include payment with Form 1040
2. **Estimated tax**: Use Form 1040-ES quarterly payments
3. **Increased withholding**: Adjust Form W-4 or W-4P to cover additional tax
