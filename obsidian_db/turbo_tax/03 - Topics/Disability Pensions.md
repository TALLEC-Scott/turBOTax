# Disability Pensions

## Overview

Disability pensions are payments you receive because you retired on disability before reaching minimum retirement age. The tax treatment depends on the type of disability, who pays for the coverage, and your age.

## General Tax Treatment

### Before Minimum Retirement Age
- Report as **wages** on Form 1040, line 1h
- Taxed the same as regular wage income
- Subject to income tax withholding

### After Minimum Retirement Age
- Report as **pension or annuity** on Form 1040, lines 5a and 5b
- Can recover your investment in the contract tax-free
- Use the Simplified Method to figure tax-free portion

### Minimum Retirement Age
Minimum retirement age is generally the age at which you could first receive a pension if you weren't disabled. Check with your employer or plan administrator.

## Tax-Exempt Disability Pensions

### Service-Connected Disability
You may be able to exclude disability payments for personal injury or sickness resulting from active service in:
- Armed forces of any country
- National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration
- Public Health Service
- Foreign Service

### Military and Government Disability Pensions
Certain military and government disability pensions are not taxable:
- VA disability benefits are generally tax-free
- Military retirement based on disability may be partially or fully excluded
- See [[Pub 3 - Armed Forces Tax Guide]] for military-specific rules

### Terrorist Attack Injuries
Disability payments for injuries incurred as a direct result of a terrorist attack directed against the United States (or its allies) are not taxable. See [[Pub 3920 - Tax Relief for Victims of Terrorist Attacks]].

## Disability Tax Credit

You may be entitled to a tax credit if you were permanently and totally disabled when you retired. Use Schedule R (Form 1040) to claim this credit.

### Permanently and Totally Disabled
You are considered permanently and totally disabled if you cannot engage in any substantial gainful activity because of a physical or mental condition that:
- Has lasted or is expected to last at least 12 months, OR
- Is expected to result in death

## Workers' Compensation

Workers' compensation for occupational injuries is generally not taxable. However, if you receive a disability pension that is based on age, length of service, or prior contributions, it may be taxable even if it was paid under a workers' compensation act.

## Reporting Requirements

| Age Status | Where to Report | Form Line |
|------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Before minimum retirement age | Wages | Form 1040, line 1h |
| After minimum retirement age | Pension/Annuity | Form 1040, lines 5a, 5b |
| Tax-exempt disability | Not reported | N/A |

## Public Safety Officer Disability

If you are a retired public safety officer receiving a disability pension and paying health insurance premiums, see [[Public Safety Officer Insurance Exclusion]] for a potential income exclusion.

## Key Rules

1. **Report as wages before minimum retirement age**
2. **Report as pension after minimum retirement age**
3. **Service-connected disability may be tax-exempt**
4. **VA disability benefits are generally tax-free**
5. **Credit for elderly/disabled may be available on Schedule R**

## Special Rules for Persons with Disabilities

### VA Disability Benefits
Veterans Affairs disability benefits are **not taxable**. This includes:
- Education, training, and subsistence allowances
- Disability compensation and pension payments
- Grants for wheelchair-accessible homes and vehicles
- Veterans' insurance proceeds and dividends
- Interest on insurance dividends left on deposit with VA
- VA compensated work therapy program payments
- Death gratuity for survivors of service members who died after September 10, 2001

### Terrorist Attack Injuries
Disability payments for injuries incurred as a direct result of terrorist attacks directed against the United States are not taxable. This includes injuries covered by the September 11 Victim Compensation Fund.

**Exception:** Must still include retirement plan distributions (401(k), pension, etc.) that would have been received regardless of disability.

### Workers' Compensation vs. Disability Pension
- Workers' compensation for occupational injury: **Not taxable**
- Disability pension based on age, service, or contributions: **Taxable** (even if paid under workers' comp act)
