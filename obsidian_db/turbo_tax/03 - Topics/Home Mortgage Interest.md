# Home Mortgage Interest

## Overview

Home mortgage interest is interest paid on a loan secured by your main home or second home. It is one of the most significant tax deductions available to homeowners who itemize deductions.

## Requirements for Deduction

To be deductible, the interest must be on a loan:
- **Secured by** your main home or second home
- Used to **buy, build, or substantially improve** a qualified home

The loan can be a:
- First mortgage
- Second mortgage
- Home improvement loan
- Home equity loan
- Refinanced mortgage

## Where to Deduct

| Type of Interest | Schedule A Line |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Interest reported on Form 1098 | Line 8a |
| Interest not reported on Form 1098 | Line 8b |
| Points not reported on Form 1098 | Line 8c |

## Mortgage Debt Limits

### Loans After December 15, 2017

Maximum debt for interest deduction: **$750,000** ($375,000 if married filing separately)

### Loans On or Before December 15, 2017

Maximum debt for interest deduction: **$1,000,000** ($500,000 if married filing separately)

### Grandfathered Debt

Loans taken out on or before October 13, 1987 have different rules - see [[Pub 936 - Home Mortgage Interest Deduction]].

## Home Acquisition Debt

Home acquisition debt is a mortgage taken out after October 13, 1987, to:
- Buy a qualified home
- Build a qualified home
- Substantially improve a qualified home

The debt must be secured by that home.

## Home Equity Loan Interest

**Important**: Interest on home equity loans is only deductible if the loan proceeds were used to buy, build, or substantially improve the home securing the loan. This rule applies regardless of when the indebtedness was incurred.

## Special Situations

### Late Payment Charges

Late payment charges on mortgage payments are deductible as mortgage interest if not for a specific service.

### Mortgage Prepayment Penalty

Penalties for paying off your mortgage early are deductible as mortgage interest.

### Ground Rent

In some states (like Maryland), you may buy your home subject to ground rent. Payments on **redeemable ground rent** are deductible as mortgage interest if specific requirements are met:
- Lease (including renewals) exceeds 15 years
- You can freely assign the lease
- You have the right to end the lease and buy the lessor's interest
- The lessor's interest is primarily a security interest

### Cooperative Apartments

Tenant-stockholders can deduct:
- Interest on loans to buy cooperative shares
- Their share of the corporation's deductible mortgage interest

See [[Cooperative Housing]] for details.

### SBA Disaster Home Loans

Interest on SBA disaster home loans is deductible as mortgage interest if the requirements are met.

## Points

[[Points (Mortgage)]] are charges paid to obtain a mortgage. They may be deductible in the year paid or over the life of the loan, depending on circumstances.

## Form 1098

You should receive Form 1098 (Mortgage Interest Statement) if you paid $600 or more of mortgage interest. The form shows:
- Total interest paid during the year
- Deductible points paid
- Any points paid by the seller

## Refund of Mortgage Interest

If you receive a refund of mortgage interest deducted in an earlier year, you generally must include it in income in the year received.

## Mortgage Debt Categories

### Grandfathered Debt
[[Grandfathered Debt]] - Mortgages taken out on or before October 13, 1987. All interest is fully deductible with no limit, but the debt amount reduces the home acquisition debt limit.

### Home Acquisition Debt
[[Home Acquisition Debt]] - Mortgages taken out after October 13, 1987, to buy, build, or substantially improve a qualified home. Subject to $1M or $750K limits based on when debt was incurred.

### Binding Contract Exception
A taxpayer who entered into a written binding contract before December 15, 2017, to close on the purchase of a principal residence before January 1, 2018, and who purchases such residence before April 1, 2018, may use the pre-2017 $1M limit.

## Calculating Average Mortgage Balance

To determine if you're within the debt limits, calculate your average mortgage balance using one of these methods:

1. **Average of first and last balance method** - For mortgages with level payments and no new borrowing
2. **Interest paid divided by interest rate method** - Divide interest paid by annual rate
3. **Lender statements** - Use monthly closing or average balances from lender statements

## Mortgage Interest Credit

If you were issued a [[Mortgage Interest Credit|Mortgage Credit Certificate (MCC)]] by a state or local government, you may be able to claim a mortgage interest credit on [[Form 8396]]. If you take this credit, you must reduce your mortgage interest deduction by the amount of the credit.

## Refunds of Interest

If you receive a refund of mortgage interest:
- **Same year as paid** - Reduce your interest expense by the refund amount
- **Earlier year** - Generally include in income (up to the amount of deduction that reduced your tax)
- Report on Schedule 1 (Form 1040), line 8z
