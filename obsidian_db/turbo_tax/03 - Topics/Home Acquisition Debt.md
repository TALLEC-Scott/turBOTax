# Home Acquisition Debt

## Overview

Home acquisition debt is a mortgage you took out after October 13, 1987, to buy, build, or substantially improve a qualified home (your main home or second home). The debt must be secured by that home.

## Debt Limits by Date

| When Debt Was Secured | Limit (MFJ/QSS) | Limit (MFS) |
|-----------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| After October 13, 1987 and prior to December 16, 2017 | $1,000,000 | $500,000 |
| After December 15, 2017 | $750,000 | $375,000 |

### Binding Contract Exception

A taxpayer who entered into a written binding contract before December 15, 2017, to close on the purchase of a principal residence before January 1, 2018, and who purchases such residence before April 1, 2018, is considered to have incurred the home acquisition debt prior to December 16, 2017 (eligible for $1M limit).

## What Qualifies as Home Acquisition Debt

### Buying a Home

- Debt used to purchase your main home or second home
- Limited to the cost of the home plus substantial improvements
- Must be secured by that home

### Building a Home

- Debt used to construct a qualified home
- Includes costs for real property, building materials, architect fees, design plans, and building permits

### Substantially Improving a Home

An improvement is substantial if it:
- Adds to the value of your home
- Adapts your home to new uses
- Prolongs your home's useful life

Repairs that maintain your home in good condition (like repainting) are NOT substantial improvements.

## Timing Rules for Treatment as Home Acquisition Debt

A mortgage may be treated as home acquisition debt even if you don't use the proceeds directly for the home:

### Purchase Within 90 Days

You buy your home within 90 days before or after taking out the mortgage. The debt is limited to the home's cost plus improvements.

### Building/Improvement - Mortgage Before Completion

You build or substantially improve your home and take out the mortgage before work is completed. The debt is limited to expenses incurred within 24 months before the mortgage date.

### Building/Improvement - Mortgage After Completion

You build or substantially improve your home and take out the mortgage within 90 days after work is completed. The debt is limited to expenses incurred within 24 months before work was completed.

## Refinanced Home Acquisition Debt

A refinanced mortgage is treated as home acquisition debt up to the amount of the balance of the old mortgage principal just before refinancing.

**Additional debt** not used to buy, build, or substantially improve a qualified home is NOT home acquisition debt.

## When Debt May Qualify Later

A mortgage that doesn't currently qualify may become home acquisition debt later:

- **Not originally secured by home**: If later secured by the home, may qualify after that time
- **Property wasn't a qualified home**: If property later becomes a qualified home, debt may qualify after that time

## Divorce Situations

If you incur debt to acquire the interest of a spouse or former spouse in a home because of a divorce or legal separation, you can treat that debt as home acquisition debt.

## Part of Home Not a Qualified Home

If you use part of your home for non-residential purposes (like a home office), you must divide the cost between the qualified home portion and the non-qualified portion. Only the qualified portion counts toward home acquisition debt.
