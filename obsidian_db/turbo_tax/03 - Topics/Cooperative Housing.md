# Cooperative Housing

## Overview

Owners of cooperative apartments have special tax rules that differ from other homeowners. As a cooperative owner, you own shares of stock in a corporation that owns or leases the housing facilities, rather than owning real estate directly.

## Tax Treatment

Despite the different ownership structure, cooperative owners generally receive the **same tax treatment** as other homeowners for:
- Real estate tax deductions
- Mortgage interest deductions

## Requirements for Deductions

To deduct your share of the cooperative corporation's real estate taxes and mortgage interest, the corporation must meet ALL of these conditions:

1. **Single class of stock**: Only one class of stock outstanding
2. **Right to occupy**: Each stockholder can live in a house, apartment, or house trailer owned or leased by the corporation (solely due to stock ownership)
3. **No capital distributions**: No stockholder can receive any distribution out of capital (except on partial or complete liquidation)
4. **Income or usage test** (at least ONE must be met):
   - At least 80% of corporation's gross income is from tenant-stockholders, OR
   - At least 80% of total square footage is available for tenant-stockholder use, OR
   - At least 90% of expenditures are for acquisition, construction, management, maintenance, or care of property for tenant-stockholders

## Tenant-Stockholders

A tenant-stockholder can be:
- An individual
- A company or corporation
- A trust or estate
- A partnership or association

The tenant-stockholder does NOT have to live in the cooperative's dwelling units.

## Real Estate Taxes

### Figuring Your Share

1. Divide your shares by total shares outstanding (including shares held by the corporation)
2. Multiply the corporation's deductible real estate taxes by that percentage

The corporation will typically tell you your share. This amount is deductible if it reasonably reflects the cost of real estate taxes for your unit.

### Refund of Taxes

If the corporation receives a refund of real estate taxes from an earlier year:
- Your deduction for the current year is reduced by your share of the refund

## Mortgage Interest

### What You Can Dededuct

1. **Interest on your loan**: Interest on a loan you took out to buy your cooperative shares
2. **Your share of corporation's mortgage interest**: Figured the same way as real estate taxes

### Refund of Cooperative's Interest

If the corporation receives a cash patronage dividend (partial refund of prior year mortgage interest):
- Reduce your mortgage interest deduction by your share

### Form 1098

If you receive Form 1098 from the cooperative housing corporation, it should show only the amount you can deduct.

## Small Business Administration Disaster Loans

Interest on SBA disaster home loans is deductible as mortgage interest if the requirements are met.

## Basis of Cooperative Shares

Your basis in cooperative shares is:
- Amount paid for the shares
- Plus: Any purchase commissions or acquisition costs

This is different from the [[Basis of Home]] rules for direct homeownership.

## Selling Cooperative Shares

When you sell your cooperative shares:
- Calculate gain or loss like other property sales
- May qualify for the [[Sale of Home]] exclusion if the unit was your main home
- Meet the ownership and use tests
