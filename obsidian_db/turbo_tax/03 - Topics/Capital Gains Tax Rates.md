# Capital Gains Tax Rates

## Overview

Net capital gains may be taxed at lower rates than ordinary income. The rate depends on the type of asset, holding period, and your taxable income.

## Short-Term vs. Long-Term

| Holding Period | Tax Treatment |
|----------------|---------------|
| 1 year or less | Ordinary income rates (short-term) |
| More than 1 year | Preferential capital gains rates (long-term) |

## Long-Term Capital Gains Rates (2025)

### Regular Capital Gains
| Tax Bracket | Rate |
|-------------|------|
| 10% or 12% | 0% |
| 22% or 24% | 15% |
| 32%, 35%, or 37% | 20% |

### Collectibles (28% Rate)
- Coins and stamps
- Artwork
- Antiques
- Gems and jewelry
- Precious metals (gold, silver)
- Alcoholic beverages held as collectibles

### Unrecaptured Section 1250 Gain (25% Rate)
- Depreciation on real property
- Accumulated straight-line depreciation
- Limited to total gain on sale
- Maximum rate: 25%

## Qualified Small Business Stock

- Section 1202 exclusion may apply
- Up to 100% exclusion for certain stock
- See Pub. 550 for details

## Holding Period Rules

### When Period Starts
| Acquisition Type | Holding Period Begins |
|------------------|----------------------|
| Purchase | Day after acquisition |
| Gift (donor's basis) | Donor's holding period |
| Gift (FMV basis) | Day after gift |
| Inheritance | Always long-term |
| Nontaxable exchange | Includes old property period |
| Stock on securities market | Day after trading date |

### Inherited Property
- Always treated as held more than 1 year
- Regardless of actual holding period

## Net Capital Gain Calculation

1. Combine all short-term gains and losses
2. Combine all long-term gains and losses
3. Net short-term gain/loss
4. Net long-term gain/loss
5. Apply loss limitations if applicable
