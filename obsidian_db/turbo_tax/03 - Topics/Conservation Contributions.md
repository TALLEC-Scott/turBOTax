# Conservation Contributions

A qualified conservation contribution is a donation of a qualified real property interest to a qualified organization for conservation purposes. These contributions have special rules and enhanced deduction limits.

## Definition

A qualified conservation contribution requires three elements:

1. **Qualified real property interest** - Your entire interest, remainder interest, or perpetual conservation restriction (easement)
2. **Qualified organization** - Governmental unit or publicly supported charity with resources to enforce restriction
3. **Conservation purpose** - Must be preserved for specific purposes

## Conservation Purposes

A contribution qualifies only if made for one or more of these purposes:

### 1. Outdoor Recreation or Education
- Land preserved for public outdoor recreation
- Education of the general public
- Public access generally required

### 2. Wildlife Habitat Protection
- Protecting relatively natural habitat
- Fish, wildlife, plants, or ecosystems
- Does not require public access

### 3. Open Space Preservation
- Preserving open space including farmland and forest land
- Must yield significant public benefit
- For scenic enjoyment OR pursuant to government conservation policy

### 4. Historic Preservation
- Preserving historically important land area
- Certified historic structure
- Building listed in National Register of Historic Places

## Qualified Real Property Interests

| Interest Type | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Entire interest | All rights to property (except mineral interest) |
| Remainder interest | Interest that passes after earlier interest ends |
| Perpetual restriction | Conservation easement limiting use forever |

### Remainder Interest in Personal Residence or Farm

You can deduct the value of a remainder interest in your personal residence or farm while retaining the right to live there for life.

**Example:** You keep the right to live in your home during your lifetime and give your church a remainder interest that begins upon your death.

## Qualified Organizations

For conservation contributions, qualified organizations include:

- **Governmental units** (federal, state, local)
- **Publicly supported charities** with conservation mission
- Organizations controlled by governmental unit or public charity

**Additional Requirements:**
- Organization must have resources to monitor and enforce easement
- Must have documentation (maps, photographs) of property condition at donation

## Deduction Limits

| Taxpayer Type | Deduction Limit |
|---------------|-----------------|
| General | 50% of AGI minus other charitable contributions |
| Qualified Farmer/Rancher | 100% of AGI minus other contributions |

### Qualified Farmer or Rancher

You qualify if gross income from farming/ranching exceeds 50% of your total gross income for the year.

**Additional Requirement for 100% Limit:**
- Donated property must be available for agriculture/livestock production
- Restriction must require property remain available for such production

## Carryover Period

Excess conservation contributions can be carried forward **up to 15 years** (vs. 5 years for other charitable contributions).

## Substantiation Requirements

### Documentation Must Include

1. **Form 8283** - Section B (over $5,000)
2. **Qualified appraisal** - From qualified appraiser
3. **FMV before and after** - Property value before and after easement
4. **Conservation purpose** - Description of purpose furthered
5. **National Park Service number** (if applicable for historic structures)

### Historic Structure Requirements

For certified historic structures, additional requirements:

**National Register Building:**
- No additional certification if listed as single building
- Certification required if multiple buildings on listing

**Historic District Building:**
- Must be separately certified by Department of the Interior
- Mere listing as contributing to district is not sufficient

**Exterior Restriction Requirements:**
- Preserve entire exterior (front, sides, rear, height)
- Prohibit changes inconsistent with historical character
- Written agreement with organization
- Photographs of entire exterior
- $500 filing fee if donation exceeds $10,000

## Partnership/S Corporation Disallowance Rule

For contributions made by partnerships or S corporations, the deduction may be disallowed if:

**The Rule:**
Contribution exceeds 2.5 times the sum of each ultimate member's relevant basis.

**Exceptions:**
1. **3-year holding period** - Property and interests held 3+ years before contribution
2. **Family pass-through entity** - 90%+ held by individual and family members
3. **Certified historic structure** - Contribution for historic preservation

## Valuation Considerations

### Before and After Method

1. Determine FMV of property before easement
2. Determine FMV of property after easement
3. Difference is the contribution value

### Factors Affecting Value

- Development potential lost
- Public access requirements
- Restrictions on future use
- Adjacent property values

## Penalties for Overvaluation

| Overvaluation | Penalty Rate |
|---------------|--------------|
| 150%+ of correct value | 20% of underpayment |
| 200%+ of correct value | 40% of underpayment |

Minimum underpayment: $5,000
