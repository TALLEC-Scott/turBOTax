# Depreciation Recapture

## Overview

When you dispose of depreciable or amortizable property at a gain, you may have to treat part or all of the gain as ordinary income. This is called depreciation recapture.

## Purpose

Recapture prevents taxpayers from converting ordinary income (depreciation deductions) into capital gains when property is sold.

## Types of Recapture

| Section | Property Type | Amount Recaptured |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| [[Section 1245 Property]] | Personal property, certain real property | All depreciation allowed or allowable |
| [[Section 1250 Property]] | Real property (buildings) | Additional depreciation (above straight-line) |

## Section 1245 Recapture

### Property Includes
- Personal property (tangible and intangible)
- Certain storage facilities
- Single-purpose agricultural structures
- Railroad grading and tunnel bores
- Qualified production property (after July 4, 2025)

### Recapture Amount
**Lesser of:**
1. Depreciation allowed or allowable, OR
2. Gain realized on disposition

### Result
- All gain is ordinary income up to depreciation taken
- Only excess gain (if any) qualifies as Section 1231

## Section 1250 Recapture

### Property Includes
- Real property that is NOT Section 1245 property
- Buildings and structural components
- Leaseholds of land or Section 1250 property

### Recapture Amount
**Additional depreciation** = Actual depreciation - Straight-line depreciation

### For Property Held > 1 Year
- Only depreciation in excess of straight-line is recaptured
- MACRS residential and nonresidential real property generally has no additional depreciation (straight-line)

### For Property Held ≤ 1 Year
- All depreciation is additional depreciation
- All recaptured as ordinary income

## What Counts as Depreciation

- Ordinary depreciation deductions
- Special depreciation allowance (bonus depreciation)
- Section 179 deduction
- Amortization of:
  - Lease acquisition costs
  - Lessee improvements
  - Certified pollution control facilities
  - Reforestation expenses
  - Section 197 intangibles
- Basis reduction for investment credit
- Basis reduction for qualified electric vehicle credit

## Exceptions and Special Rules

### Like-Kind Exchanges and Involuntary Conversions
- Recapture may be limited
- Carryover basis rules apply

### Gifts and Transfers at Death
- Recapture rules apply to recipient
- Depreciation taken by donor counts

### Corporate Distributions
- See Pub. 542 for special rules

## Recordkeeping Requirements

Keep permanent records of:
- Date and manner of acquisition
- Cost or other basis
- Depreciation/amortization claimed
- All adjustments affecting basis
