# Form 8949 - Sales and Other Dispositions of Capital Assets

## Overview

**Form 8949** reports sales and dispositions of capital assets. It is used to calculate capital gains and losses, which then flow to [[Schedule D (Form 1040)]]. In the context of [[Installment Sales]], Form 8949 is used when electing out of the installment method.

## When to Use Form 8949

Use Form 8949 for:
- Sales of capital assets (stocks, bonds, real estate held for investment)
- Dispositions not reported on other forms
- Electing out of the installment method

## Relation to Installment Sales

When you elect **out of the installment method**:
1. Report the entire sale on Form 8949 in the year of sale
2. Do NOT file [[Form 6252]]
3. Include the full gain (not spread over time)

## Parts of Form 8949

### Part I - Short-Term Transactions

Assets held one year or less:
- Reported on separate copies based on how basis was reported to IRS
- Box A: Basis reported to IRS on Form 1099-B
- Box B: Basis NOT reported to IRS
- Box C: Transaction not reported on Form 1099-B

### Part II - Long-Term Transactions

Assets held more than one year:
- Same Box A/B/C structure as Part I
- Most favorable capital gains rates apply

## Required Information

For each transaction:
| Column | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| (a) | Description of property |
| (b) | Date acquired |
| (c) | Date sold |
| (d) | Proceeds (sales price) |
| (e) | Cost or other basis |
| (f) | Adjustments |
| (g) | Gain or loss |

## Common Questions

### Q: When should I elect out of installment treatment?
**A:** Consider electing out when you have losses to offset, expect lower tax rates this year, or want to simplify reporting.

### Q: How do I elect out of the installment method?
**A:** Simply report the sale on Form 8949 (and Form 4797 if business property) instead of Form 6252. The election is automatic when you file this way.

### Q: Can I revoke the election later?
**A:** Generally no. Once you elect out by filing Form 8949, you can only revoke with IRS approval.
